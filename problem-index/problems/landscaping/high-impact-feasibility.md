# Feasibility — Turf Disease and Nutrient Deficiency Identification from Site Photos

**Industry:** [[landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Problem:** [[problems/landscaping/high-impact|Turf Disease and Nutrient Deficiency Identification from Site Photos]]
**Problem type:** High Impact

**Problem summary:** Experienced lawn care technicians who diagnose turf disease and nutrient deficiency by reading grass color patterns, lesion shapes, and disease progression signs — visual pattern recognition that determines which pesticide or fertilizer product to apply — get that diagnostic expertise replicated by a computer vision model, enabling technicians with 6 months of experience to diagnose correctly instead of guessing.

## Solution Approach
Stand up a focused pilot that integrates current systems, captures the missing signals, and adds analytics/ML once the data path is stable. Deliver mobile-first jobsite workflows with photo/checklist capture and scheduling tied to field realities.

## Open Source Data
Building codes, weather, and some permit data are open; job cost, photos, and schedule data are shop-specific.

## Worth Solving?
High — core outcome/financial impact justifies investment if change management is handled.
