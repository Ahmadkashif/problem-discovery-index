# Church Management for Family Units & Small Groups

**Industry:** [[faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic CRMs track individuals — churches need to track households, spiritual milestones, group affiliations, and giving as a family unit.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #recommendation #tabular-ml #data-integration

## The Problem
Churches operate around family units, not individual contacts. A household might include two parents, three children in different age-based ministries, a grandmother in the seniors' group, and a college student home for summers. Tracking engagement, communication preferences, giving history, and volunteer roles at the household level — while still managing individual participation — requires a data model that generic CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot don't natively support. Staff waste hours manually linking records, merging duplicates after marriages or divorces, and cross-referencing attendance across ministries to understand a family's total involvement.

## What Already Exists
Planning Center and Church Community Builder offer church-specific CRM features with household grouping, but their recommendation and insight layers are thin. They can tell you the Smith family attended 3 of 4 Sundays this month, but they can't recommend which small group would best fit their life stage, suggest the right volunteer role for a newly retired member, or flag that a family's participation pattern matches others who eventually left. Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud is too expensive and complex for most congregations.

## The Customisation Gap
The system needs a church-native data model: households with age-stratified members, spiritual milestones (baptism, membership class, first-time guest), small-group lifecycle tracking (joined, active, facilitating, dormant), and giving patterns normalized against household income estimates. On top of this, a recommendation engine should suggest small-group placements based on life stage, geography, and affinity; flag families ready for deeper involvement; and auto-generate pastoral care lists segmented by need type (grief, new baby, job loss, health crisis). Integration must work with existing giving platforms (Tithely, Pushpay) and check-in systems (Planning Center) rather than replacing them.

## Impact If Solved
Administrative staff reclaim 5-8 hours per week currently spent on manual data reconciliation. Pastors get a single-pane view of family engagement that would otherwise require querying three separate systems and cross-referencing in their heads.
