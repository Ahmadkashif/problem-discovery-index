# Project Manager Emergency Response Burden

**Industry:** [[insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Restoration project managers stop being on-call 24/7/365 because an intelligent triage system handles after-hours emergency calls, dispatches the right crew, and only wakes the PM when a loss genuinely requires senior decision-making.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Water losses, fires, and storm damage don't happen during business hours. Restoration companies promise 24/7 emergency response — carrier programs require it, and the competitive landscape demands it. The burden falls on project managers who rotate on-call duties, fielding 5-15 after-hours calls per week. Each call requires triage: Is this an emergency requiring immediate dispatch (active water flow, structure fire aftermath, sewage backup) or can it wait until morning (slow leak discovered, minor smoke damage, old stain)? The PM must assess urgency from a panicked homeowner's description, decide on crew size and equipment, coordinate dispatch, and often drive to the site personally for large losses. Sleep disruption is chronic — PMs report averaging 4-5 hours of sleep during on-call rotations, which typically last a full week.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
PM burnout is the #1 retention problem in restoration. The combination of on-call stress, long hours during catastrophic events (storms can trigger 50+ losses in 24 hours), and the emotional weight of working with distressed homeowners creates turnover rates above 40% annually. Many PMs leave restoration entirely for general construction where after-hours work is rare. The on-call rotation means even "off" weeks are colored by anticipation of the next rotation. Family relationships suffer from unpredictable night disruptions. The inability to truly disconnect — even on vacation, someone has to cover the phone — makes career longevity in restoration PM roles exceptionally rare beyond 5-7 years.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An AI-powered triage system that handles incoming after-hours calls and web submissions. The system collects structured information from the caller (water source, duration, affected area, property type, insurance carrier), classifies urgency (immediate dispatch, morning dispatch, or self-service guidance), and for immediate dispatches, selects the appropriate crew based on loss type, crew proximity, certifications, and current workload. The PM receives a notification with the triage assessment and dispatch recommendation — approving with one tap for routine losses and only engaging directly for complex or large-loss situations.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces PM after-hours engagement from 5-15 calls per week to 2-3 that genuinely require senior judgment. Improves average sleep quality during on-call weeks from 4-5 hours to 6-7 hours. Reduces PM turnover from 40%+ to 20-25% by making on-call rotations manageable. Maintains carrier-required response time SLAs through faster automated triage and dispatch.
