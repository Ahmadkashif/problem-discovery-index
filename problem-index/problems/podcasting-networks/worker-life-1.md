# Producer Editing & Show Notes Tedium

**Industry:** [[podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Producers get back 15-20 hours per week currently spent on mechanical editing, transcription cleanup, and show notes writing — time they can redirect to creative development and talent coaching.
**Tags:** #llm #speech-recognition #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing

## The Problem
A podcast producer managing 3-5 weekly shows spends 3-5 hours per episode on post-production tasks that are skilled but repetitive: trimming dead air and filler words, leveling audio between speakers, generating accurate transcripts, writing SEO-optimized show notes with timestamps, pulling quotable clips for social media, and drafting episode descriptions. For a producer handling 4 shows, that is 12-20 hours per week on tasks that follow predictable patterns but still require human judgment on what to cut, what to highlight, and how to frame the episode for discovery.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Producers enter the industry to develop shows and coach talent, not to manually scrub "um"s from hour-long recordings. The repetitive nature of post-production drives burnout — particularly at smaller networks where one producer handles an entire catalog. The cognitive load of switching between creative work (giving episode feedback, planning season arcs) and mechanical work (trimming silences, writing timestamps) fragments attention and reduces the quality of both. Producers who fall behind on post-production delay episode releases, which directly hurts download numbers and creates a stress cycle.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An integrated post-production assistant that ingests raw episode audio and produces: (1) an edited audio file with filler words removed, silence trimmed, and audio levels normalized based on the producer's learned preferences, (2) a timestamped transcript with speaker labels, (3) a draft show notes document with key topics, guest bio, and chapter markers, and (4) 3-5 suggested clip segments for social promotion with auto-generated captions. The producer reviews and approves rather than creates from scratch — reducing per-episode post-production from 3-5 hours to 30-60 minutes.

## Impact If Solved
Producers reclaim 10-15 hours per week, eliminating the primary driver of burnout in the role and allowing them to manage more shows or invest deeper in creative development for existing ones.
