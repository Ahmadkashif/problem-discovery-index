# Field Technician Sample Collection Documentation Burden

**Industry:** [[environmental-consultants|Environmental Consultants]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Field technicians get back 30-45 minutes per site visit currently lost to handwriting chain-of-custody forms, field notes, and sample logs in adverse conditions — rain, heat, PPE gloves, confined spaces.
**Tags:** #llm #speech-recognition #nlp #worker-facing

## The Problem
An environmental field technician collecting groundwater or soil samples must document every action in real time: well purge parameters (pH, conductivity, turbidity, dissolved oxygen readings at timed intervals), sample container types and preservatives, collection times to the minute, field observations (odors, sheens, discoloration), weather conditions, and chain-of-custody details for each sample bottle. This documentation happens while wearing nitrile gloves (often doubled), sometimes in Tyvek suits, frequently in rain or 100-degree heat, and occasionally in confined spaces or on active construction sites. Technicians fill out paper forms or struggle with tablet touchscreens through gloves, generating 3-8 pages of handwritten documentation per site visit that must later be transcribed into digital format. Illegible handwriting, smudged ink from wet conditions, and transcription errors are constant quality issues that can invalidate sample results.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Field technicians are the lowest-paid professionals in environmental consulting ($18-$28/hour) but bear the highest physical burden — early mornings, outdoor exposure, chemical handling, and repetitive documentation that extends already long field days. The documentation burden means a 4-well groundwater sampling event that takes 3 hours of physical work requires 4.5-5 hours total on-site. Technicians frequently skip lunch to finish paperwork before samples exceed hold times. The tedium of documentation is cited as the top reason field technicians leave the profession within 3 years, creating chronic hiring problems for firms.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A voice-first mobile app that the technician speaks to while working: "Well MW-3, purge start 10:42 AM, initial readings pH 6.8, conductivity 450 microsiemens, turbidity 22 NTU." The app uses speech recognition trained on environmental terminology (analyte names, well IDs, unit abbreviations) to populate structured forms in real time. It auto-generates chain-of-custody documents, flags missing required fields before the technician leaves the site, and syncs to the project database. The technician never removes their gloves to write or type.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 30-45 minutes per site visit across 3-5 visits per week per technician, totaling 6-10 hours monthly. Eliminates transcription errors that currently invalidate 2-5% of sample results. Reduces field technician burnout and turnover, addressing the industry's most persistent staffing problem.
