# Pipeline and Client Relationship Intelligence for Brokers

**Industry:** [[commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** CRE brokers who manage 20-40 active client and deal relationships across 12-24 month deal cycles — without a CRM discipline — and routinely lose deals because they didn't follow up at the right moment get an automated relationship intelligence layer that surfaces which clients need attention this week and why.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #time-series-forecasting #probability-distributions #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem

CRE deal cycles are long — 12-24 months from first contact to transaction close on investment sales, 6-12 months on major leasing transactions. Brokers who manage 20-40 simultaneous relationships without systematic follow-up cadence lose deals to competitors who stayed in front of the client during the silence periods. The problem isn't lack of intent — brokers know they should follow up — it's lack of a systematic trigger that tells them who needs attention today.

CRE CRMs (Buildout, ClientLook) record contact history but don't generate proactive relationship intelligence. A broker who hasn't called a prospect in 60 days doesn't know it unless they manually review every contact record.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

A 12-18 month deal that goes to a competitor because the broker went silent for 3 months at the wrong time costs $50,000-$200,000 in commission. Long-cycle sales require consistent relationship maintenance — the broker who is "top of mind" when the principal makes their disposition or acquisition decision wins the assignment. Brokers who do this consistently earn 50-100% more than equally skilled brokers who don't.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A Buildout or ClientLook integration that: (1) tracks last contact date per client and computes "days since last touch" automatically; (2) scores each relationship on dormancy risk (combination of days since contact, deal stage, and relationship warmth signals from email response rate); (3) generates a weekly priority list of 5-10 relationships that need touchpoint this week with the recommended action (call, send market update, invite to property tour); (4) provides a brief talking point for each relationship (last discussed X, their current situation is Y based on notes). The broker spends 30 minutes on Monday morning reviewing the list and making the prioritized outreach calls.

## Impact If Solved

Converts passive relationship management into active. Reduces deal losses from relationship dormancy. Enables brokers to maintain larger, more active pipelines without proportional time investment.
