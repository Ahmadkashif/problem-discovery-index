# Offering Memorandum Generation from Property Data

**Industry:** [[commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** CRE brokers who spend 15-25 hours assembling an investment property offering memorandum — compiling financial summaries, market comparables, property description, and investment highlights into a formatted PDF — get the OM generated from the property data inputs in 2-3 hours of review rather than 15-25 hours of assembly.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #attention-mechanisms #transformers #transfer-learning #word-embeddings #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem

An investment sales offering memorandum (OM) is the primary marketing document for a commercial property for sale. A standard OM includes: executive summary, investment highlights, property description and photographs, financial summary (rent roll, operating statement, NOI), market overview (submarket demographics and comparable transactions), and offering terms. Producing a professional OM takes a junior analyst 15-25 hours of assembly work: writing narrative sections, formatting financials from the operating statement, selecting and formatting comparable transactions, and producing the designed PDF layout.

For boutique investment sales firms without dedicated analysts, the OM preparation burden falls on the broker — who bills at $300-$500/hour equivalent and should be spending that time on client relationships, not desktop publishing.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Time spent on OM production is time not spent on client development, property tours, and offer negotiation. A broker who can produce an OM in a 2-hour review session rather than a 20-hour production session can carry 2-3× more listings simultaneously. The OM is a template-driven document — the structure is standard, the content is specific to the property. It is the prototypical high-effort, low-judgment task that should be automated.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A Buildout or CRE CRM integration where the broker enters the property data (address, property type, rent roll, operating statement, asking price, cap rate), and the system generates: the financial section (formatted rent roll, NOI calculation, returns analysis at multiple price points), the market section (automatically pulled comparable transactions and submarket statistics), the narrative sections (investment highlights, property description from the data inputs and photos, market overview from submarket data), and the designed PDF layout. The broker reviews and edits the generated draft — making it accurate and adding their market perspective — in 2-3 hours. Total time saved: 12-20 hours per OM.

## Impact If Solved

Returns 12-20 hours per listing to the broker. Enables small investment sales firms to market listings with institutional-quality OMs that would previously have required a junior analyst. At $350/hour equivalent, 15 hours per OM × 12 listings per year = $63,000 in recovered productive time annually per broker.
