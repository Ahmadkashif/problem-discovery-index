# Tenant Communication Drafting and Response Automation

**Industry:** [[property-management|Property Management]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Property managers who receive 50-100 emails and texts per day from tenants — questions about lease terms, maintenance status, payment plans, renewal inquiries — and spend 3-5 hours per day drafting individual responses get an AI drafting assistant that generates appropriate responses from the tenant's message and the property record, requiring only review and send.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #attention-mechanisms #transfer-learning #loss-functions #evaluation-metrics #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem

Property manager communication volume is relentless. A manager handling 200 units receives maintenance status questions, payment inquiries, lease renewal requests, neighbor complaints, and general questions continuously. Each response requires: looking up the tenant's account, checking the relevant record (maintenance work order status, payment history, lease terms), and drafting a professional response. This process takes 3-5 minutes per message — at 40-60 messages per day, it consumes 2-3 hours of the manager's time.

Most of these messages follow predictable patterns: maintenance status requests ("any update on my work order?"), rent payment confirmation, late fee dispute, renewal inquiry. An LLM with access to the tenant's account data can generate appropriate responses for the majority of these patterns.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Property managers cite communication volume as the single greatest source of job stress and the primary barrier to handling larger portfolios. A manager who handles 200 units is limited not by the management complexity but by the communication overhead. Reducing communication time from 2-3 hours to 45 minutes per day enables the same manager to handle 350-400 units — a 75% portfolio expansion with the same headcount.

## What a Solution Looks Like

An AppFolio or Buildium integration where incoming tenant messages are analyzed for intent classification (maintenance status request, payment inquiry, renewal question, complaint) and the system drafts a response from the relevant account data. The manager reviews the draft — which is already correct for routine cases — and sends with one click. For unusual cases (escalated complaints, legal matters), the message is flagged for careful manual response. 80% of routine messages are handled in 10-15 seconds of review instead of 3-5 minutes of lookup and writing.

## Impact If Solved

Returns 1.5-2 hours per day to property managers. Enables portfolio scaling without proportional headcount increase. Improves response time to tenant inquiries — same-hour responses vs. end-of-day responses improve tenant satisfaction and retention.
