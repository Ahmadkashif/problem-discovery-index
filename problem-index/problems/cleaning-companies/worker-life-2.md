# Supervisor Multi-Site Quality Control Travel

**Industry:** [[cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Supervisors stop driving 200+ miles per week visiting sites to verify cleaning quality and start reviewing AI-assessed post-cleaning photos from every site, every night.
**Tags:** #cnn #binary-classification #computer-vision #worker-facing

## The Problem
Quality control supervisors at commercial cleaning companies physically visit 5-15 sites per week to verify cleaning quality — driving between locations after hours, often until midnight, to inspect work performed by evening crews. A supervisor covering 40 sites can physically inspect each one monthly at best, leaving 75% of cleaning events unmonitored. The driving is unpaid or underpaid, the hours are brutal (6pm-midnight, 5 nights a week), and the supervisor can only catch problems after the fact.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Supervisor burnout is the #2 cause of turnover in commercial cleaning (after cleaner turnover). The combination of late-night driving, confronting underperforming crews, and client complaint management creates a role that most people leave within 18 months. The physical inspection requirement means supervisors can't have a normal evening life — every weeknight is spent driving between job sites.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A remote quality verification system where cleaners take standardized post-cleaning photos at each site, AI analyzes the photos for cleanliness standards (floor condition, trash removal, restroom cleanliness, surface dusting), and supervisors review flagged exceptions rather than visiting every site. On-site visits become targeted (responding to AI-flagged quality issues) rather than routine.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces supervisor driving from 200+ miles/week to 50 miles for targeted visits. Enables 100% cleaning event monitoring instead of 25%. Converts supervisor role from "roving inspector" to "quality manager" — a more sustainable, higher-value position.
