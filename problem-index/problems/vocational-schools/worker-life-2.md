# Career Services Coordinator Employer Outreach Overload

**Industry:** [[vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Career services coordinators stop cold-calling 50-100 employers per week and instead focus on the relationships most likely to place students.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #ranking #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
A typical vocational school career services coordinator is responsible for placing 100-300 graduates per year across 5-10 trade programs. Their primary tool is the phone: calling employers to ask about openings, following up on placed graduates to verify retention (required for accreditation), and chasing alumni who haven't reported employment status. A single coordinator makes 50-100 outbound calls per week, maintains a personal spreadsheet of 200-500 employer contacts, and tracks placement status for each graduate manually. Hit rates on cold outreach are 10-15% — most calls go to voicemail or reach employers who aren't currently hiring. The coordinator has no systematic way to know which employers are most likely to be hiring right now or which students are the best fit for which employers.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Career services coordinators are typically empathetic, relationship-oriented people who chose the role because they want to help students succeed. Instead, they spend 70% of their time on low-yield phone calls and data entry. The emotional toll is significant: every unplaced graduate feels like a personal failure, and the accreditation pressure (maintain 70%+ placement or risk the school's existence) adds constant anxiety. Coordinators burn out within 2-3 years, and schools struggle to recruit replacements at the $35-50K salaries typical for the role. The ones who stay develop deep employer relationship knowledge that lives entirely in their heads — when they leave, the school's placement network walks out the door.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A prioritization engine ranks employer outreach targets by likelihood of having relevant openings (based on historical hiring patterns, seasonality, company growth signals, and time since last placement). The coordinator opens their dashboard each morning and sees: "Call these 10 employers first — 3 have hired your welding grads in Q1 the last two years, 2 posted HVAC jobs this week, and 5 haven't been contacted in 90 days and historically respond well to re-engagement." Placement tracking is automated through employer portal check-ins and graduate text surveys, cutting verification calls by 60%.

## Impact If Solved
Converts 50-100 weekly cold calls into 15-25 warm, prioritized calls with 3x the hit rate. Reclaims 15-20 hours per week for actual career counseling, resume coaching, and interview preparation — the high-touch work that actually moves placement rates. Institutional employer knowledge is captured in a system rather than a coordinator's head, surviving turnover.
