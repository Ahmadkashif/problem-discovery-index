# Employer Advisory Board & Curriculum Labor Market Alignment

**Industry:** [[vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Curriculum committees make program changes based on anecdotal employer feedback from poorly attended annual meetings — they should be working from real-time labor market intelligence matched to their specific trade programs.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #data-integration

## The Problem
Accreditors require vocational schools to maintain employer advisory boards that review curriculum relevance. In practice, these boards meet once or twice a year, attendance is spotty (3-5 of 12 invited employers show up), and feedback is anecdotal: "We're seeing more solar on commercial roofs" or "Your graduates don't know BIM software." Program directors then translate these vague signals into curriculum changes with a 6-18 month lag. Meanwhile, actual labor market demand shifts faster — new certifications emerge, specific tools become industry standard, and geographic demand for certain trades spikes or collapses. Schools are making multi-year curriculum bets on thin, delayed signal.

## What Already Exists
Labor market analytics platforms (Lightcast/EMSI, Burning Glass/Lightcast, ONET OnLine) aggregate job posting data, wage trends, and skill demand signals. CRM tools track employer engagement. Generic survey tools (SurveyMonkey, Google Forms) collect advisory board feedback. None of these are integrated — a program director can't see "the top 10 skills appearing in HVAC job postings in our metro that our curriculum doesn't cover" without manually cross-referencing multiple platforms.

## The Customisation Gap
A vocational-specific tool must map the school's exact curriculum (course-level learning outcomes and skill competencies) against live job posting data filtered by geography, trade, and employer size. It needs NLP to extract skill requirements from unstructured job postings and classify them against the school's existing competency framework. It must also structure and analyze advisory board feedback — transcribing meeting notes, extracting actionable recommendations, and tracking which suggestions were implemented. The output is a curriculum gap report showing: skills in demand that the program doesn't teach, skills the program teaches that employers no longer need, and trending certifications the school should offer.

## Impact If Solved
Programs that align curriculum to actual employer demand see 10-15% higher placement rates and can justify higher tuition based on demonstrated market relevance. Advisory board engagement improves when members see their feedback systematically tracked and acted upon, reducing the annual attendance problem.
