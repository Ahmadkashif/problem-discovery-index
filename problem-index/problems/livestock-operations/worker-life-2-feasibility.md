# Feasibility — Manual Pen Riding and Health Checking

**Industry:** [[livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Problem:** [[problems/livestock-operations/worker-life-2|Manual Pen Riding and Health Checking]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Feedlot pen riders spend 4-6 hours daily on horseback scanning pens of 150-300 cattle each for illness signs — a cognitively exhausting task where detection accuracy drops below 40% by mid-shift — and an AI-assisted detection system would transform their role from brute-force surveillance to targeted intervention, reducing cognitive load while improving outcomes.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Blend field/plant logs with weather/soil inputs and deliver simple decision-support that works offline.

## Open Source Data
NOAA/USDA weather and agronomic data are open; field logs, inputs, and yield histories come from growers/processors.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
