## 1. Herd Health Alert Agent
#ai-agent #anomaly-detection #data-integration

**Concept:** An autonomous monitoring agent that fuses data from pen cameras, ear-tag sensors, weather stations, and treatment records to generate real-time illness risk scores for every animal on the operation. The agent doesn't just flag sick animals — it identifies emerging disease events at the pen and lot level, detects when morbidity rates are deviating from seasonal baselines, and autonomously escalates to the consulting veterinarian when patterns suggest a systemic issue (new BRD strain, mycotoxin-contaminated feed, water quality problem) rather than routine individual illness. It learns the operation's specific disease patterns over time, adjusting thresholds for breed types, arrival source (auction-origin cattle have 3-5x the BRD risk of ranch-direct cattle), and season.

**Inputs:** Ear-tag accelerometer data streams (activity, rumination, resting), camera-derived behavioral features (bunk visits, water visits, gait scores, social positioning), daily weather data, treatment records entered by pen riders via voice or text, arrival/processing records (vaccination protocol, source, weight, risk score), and feed delivery logs from the feed truck's scale system.
**Outputs / Actions:** (1) Morning pull list ranked by illness probability delivered to pen rider's phone by 5 AM; (2) automatic text/call escalation to the consulting DVM when pen-level morbidity exceeds the operation's historical 95th percentile for that week of year; (3) weekly health dashboard showing treatment rates, case fatality rates, retreatment rates, and cost-per-head by arrival cohort; (4) automatic flagging of animals approaching antibiotic withdrawal deadlines for the hospital pen manager.
**Why now:** Low-cost IoT ear tags have dropped below $15/unit (Ceres Tag, HerdDogg), cellular-connected edge computing devices can run lightweight inference models without cloud connectivity (critical for rural feedlots with poor internet), and transformer-based time-series models can learn individual behavioral baselines from 7 days of data rather than the 21-30 days required by earlier statistical approaches.
**Market:** 26 million cattle placed in US feedlots annually across ~2,100 feedlots with 1,000+ head capacity. Primary buyers are feedlot managers and cattle feeding companies. At $3-5/head/turn, the addressable market is $80-130M annually for feedlots alone, expanding to $200M+ including backgrounding yards, dairy operations, and international markets (Australia, Brazil, Argentina).

## 2. Calving Watch Agent
#ai-agent #computer-vision #worker-facing

**Concept:** A dedicated calving surveillance agent that monitors camera feeds from calving pastures and barns 24/7 during the calving season, autonomously classifying labor stages and dispatching alerts only when human intervention is needed. The agent distinguishes between normal unassisted calving (85-95% of events in mature cows) and dystocia requiring assistance, and learns the specific operation's herd characteristics over successive calving seasons — adjusting alert thresholds for heifer vs. mature cow calvings, updating its model with the outcome of every birth (assisted/unassisted, calf vigor score, cow recovery).

**Inputs:** Thermal and visible-spectrum camera feeds from calving areas (pasture poles, barn overhead cameras), animal identification via visual tag reading or proximity to RFID readers, pre-calving risk scores from the dystocia prediction model (sire calving ease EPD, heifer pelvic measurements, days since breeding), weather conditions (wind chill is critical — a healthy calf born in -20°F wind chill can die of hypothermia within 30 minutes if not dried and sheltered).
**Outputs / Actions:** (1) Push notification with photo/video clip when an animal enters Stage 2 labor, classified as "normal progress — monitoring" or "possible dystocia — check immediately"; (2) hypothermia risk alert when a calf is born in extreme cold and hasn't stood within 15 minutes; (3) post-calving bonding assessment — alert if a cow hasn't cleaned/nursed her calf within 2 hours (indicating possible rejection or illness); (4) end-of-season calving report with timestamps, intervention rates, and calf vigor scores linked to sire breeding data for genetic selection decisions.
**Why now:** Thermal cameras capable of detecting calving events in darkness have dropped below $500/unit (FLIR Lepton modules). Edge AI processors (NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, Google Coral) can run real-time pose estimation and activity classification models at 15 fps for under $200 in hardware. Starlink has solved the rural connectivity problem that previously made cloud-dependent systems impractical for remote calving pastures.
**Market:** Approximately 30 million beef cows calve annually in the US, primarily on 727,000 cow-calf operations. The target market is operations with 50-500 cows where the owner-operator or a single hired hand handles calving — roughly 400,000 operations. At $1,500-3,000/system (cameras + edge processor + subscription), the hardware market is $600M-1.2B with annual monitoring subscription revenue of $200-500/year per operation ($80-200M recurring).

## 3. Market Timing Agent
#ai-agent #ai-platform #time-series-forecasting #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

**Concept:** An autonomous marketing decision agent for cattle producers that integrates live cattle futures prices, regional basis levels, seasonal price patterns, individual animal performance data (weight, rate of gain, projected quality grade), feed costs, and packer grid premiums/discounts to recommend optimal marketing timing and sorting for every pen or group of cattle. The agent runs continuous what-if simulations: "If you hold Pen 12 another 14 days, projected weight gain adds $38/head but increased feed cost is $25/head and futures curve suggests $15/head price decline — net: sell now at $2/head advantage over holding." It accounts for the non-linear economics that most producers miss — the interaction between yield grade discounts (overweight carcasses), quality grade premiums (more days on feed = more marbling), and declining feed efficiency in the final feeding period.

**Inputs:** CME live cattle and feeder cattle futures prices (real-time), USDA-AMS regional cash cattle trade reports (weekly), packer-specific grid pricing sheets (updated weekly, obtained via OCR/parsing of PDF grids from Tyson, JBS, Cargill, National Beef), individual animal or pen-average weights from walk-over scales, rate of gain calculations, projected carcass quality from ultrasound or predictive model, current and projected feed costs (corn futures, local hay prices, supplement costs), and the operation's specific cost of gain (variable by pen based on ration and health history).
**Outputs / Actions:** (1) Weekly marketing recommendation for each pen: sell this week / hold N more days / sort and sell top-end now while holding tail-enders; (2) automated price alerts when cash-to-futures basis narrows to historically favorable levels; (3) hedging recommendations tied to specific pens ("Pen 8 breakeven is $183/cwt, current December futures at $186 — consider selling 2 contracts to lock margin"); (4) end-of-closeout profitability analysis comparing actual outcomes to agent recommendations for continuous learning.
**Why now:** Real-time market data APIs (CME DataMine, USDA-AMS LMR), combined with LLM-powered parsing of unstructured packer grid PDFs and regional market reports, make it possible to automate the data aggregation that previously required a cattle marketing consultant ($5,000-15,000/year) or a commodity broker. Transformer-based price forecasting models trained on 20+ years of cattle market data can capture the complex seasonal, cyclical, and supply-driven price patterns that drive short-term basis movements.
**Market:** 26 million fed cattle marketed annually, with a $2,000-2,500 per head gross value. Even a 1-2% improvement in marketing timing represents $500M-1.3B in aggregate value. Primary buyers: feedlot operators (2,100 feedlots >1,000 head), cattle feeding investors, and order buyers/cattle brokers. SaaS pricing at $1-3/head marketed yields a $26-78M addressable market for the software alone, with additional revenue from integrated hedging/brokerage services.
