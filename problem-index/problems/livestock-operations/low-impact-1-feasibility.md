# Feasibility — Livestock Record Keeping for USDA/State Compliance

**Industry:** [[livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Problem:** [[problems/livestock-operations/low-impact-1|Livestock Record Keeping for USDA/State Compliance]]
**Problem type:** Low Impact

**Problem summary:** USDA's Animal Disease Traceability (ADT) program requires individual animal identification and movement documentation for interstate commerce, but generic farm management software doesn't handle the specific interplay of official ear tag numbers, brand inspection certificates, veterinary health certificates (CVIs), withdrawal period tracking for antibiotics, and the Beef Quality Assurance (BQA) treatment protocols that auditors actually check during on-farm inspections.

## Solution Approach
Adapt a horizontal tool with industry-specific templates, rules, and integrations so teams stop working around generic software. Blend field/plant logs with weather/soil inputs and deliver simple decision-support that works offline.

## Open Source Data
NOAA/USDA weather and agronomic data are open; field logs, inputs, and yield histories come from growers/processors.

## Worth Solving?
Medium — worthwhile as a customization/efficiency lever once a clear sponsor and data owner are identified.
