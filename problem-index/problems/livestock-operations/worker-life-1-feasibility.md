# Feasibility — Ranch Hand Night Calving Watch

**Industry:** [[livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Problem:** [[problems/livestock-operations/worker-life-1|Ranch Hand Night Calving Watch]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** During calving season, ranch hands and owner-operators drag themselves out of bed every 2-4 hours for 4-8 weeks straight to check heifers in freezing dark pastures, and a camera-based calving detection system would eliminate 80% of these checks while catching the 3-5% of births that actually need intervention — giving workers back 200+ hours of broken sleep per season.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Blend field/plant logs with weather/soil inputs and deliver simple decision-support that works offline.

## Open Source Data
NOAA/USDA weather and agronomic data are open; field logs, inputs, and yield histories come from growers/processors.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
