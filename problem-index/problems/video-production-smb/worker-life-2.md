# Freelancer & Crew Scheduling and Availability

**Industry:** [[video-production-smb|SMB Video Production]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Freelance crew members stop losing gigs to scheduling chaos and producers stop scrambling for last-minute replacements, because real-time availability and skill matching replace the frantic group-text-at-10-PM workflow.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #linear-regression #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #cross-validation #time-series-forecasting #probability-distributions #worker-facing

## The Problem
SMB production companies rely on a rotating pool of 15-40 freelancers (camera operators, gaffers, sound recordists, PAs, makeup artists) booked on a per-project basis. The producer texts or calls their preferred people, waits for responses, gets a mix of "yes," "maybe," and silence. Double-bookings happen constantly because freelancers juggle 3-5 ongoing relationships with different production companies, each using a different communication channel. A freelancer confirms a Tuesday shoot on Monday morning, then gets a higher-paying gig and cancels at 6 AM on shoot day. The producer's panic call tree begins, burning 2-3 hours and sometimes resulting in a shoot with a suboptimal crew member who has never worked with the team.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Freelancers lose income because they weren't aware of a gig that perfectly fit their schedule — the producer texted their second choice first. Crew members who are reliable but less socially connected to the producer get systematically underbooked despite strong skills. The uncertainty of "will I work next week?" creates chronic financial anxiety that drives talented crew out of production entirely. On the producer side, the person responsible for crewing up shoots — often the owner — spends 6-10 hours per week on scheduling logistics that feel like herding cats, time stolen from creative work and business development.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A scheduling platform where freelancers maintain real-time availability calendars, skill profiles, and rate cards. Producers post crew needs with dates, roles, rates, and location; the system matches available crew, weighted by past performance ratings, team chemistry (who has worked well together before), proximity to the shoot location, and rate fit. Confirmations are binding with a cancellation penalty visible to both parties. A demand forecasting layer predicts busy periods (Q4 corporate video rush, spring wedding season) so freelancers can block time strategically and producers can book critical crew 2-3 weeks earlier than their current last-minute pattern.

## Impact If Solved
Freelancers see a 15-25% increase in booked days per month from reduced information asymmetry. Producers reclaim 5-8 hours per week in scheduling overhead. Day-of cancellation rates drop from ~12% to under 3%.
