# Stock Footage & Music Licensing Management

**Industry:** [[video-production-smb|SMB Video Production]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Production companies stop risking copyright strikes and surprise licensing fees because every asset in every project is tracked from purchase through final delivery.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #compliance

## The Problem
A typical corporate video uses 5-15 stock footage clips, 1-3 music tracks, and assorted sound effects — each with different license terms (single-use, multi-use, platform-restricted, duration-limited, territory-restricted). Producers purchase assets from Shutterstock, Artgrid, Epidemic Sound, Musicbed, and a dozen other sources, then lose track of which license covers which deliverable. Six months later, the client wants to repurpose the video for a trade show booth, and nobody can confirm whether the music license permits public exhibition. One copyright claim on a client's YouTube channel can destroy the relationship.

## What Already Exists
Digital asset management platforms (Brandfolder, Bynder, Canto) handle file storage and metadata. License management tools exist for enterprise software. Stock footage platforms provide download history and license PDFs.

## The Customisation Gap
No existing tool connects the dots between "this specific clip from Artgrid, purchased under this specific license tier, was used in this specific project, delivered to this specific client, for these specific distribution channels." The gap is an NLP-driven system that ingests license agreements (which vary wildly in format and terminology across vendors), classifies the usage rights into standardized permission categories (web, broadcast, social, theatrical, duration, territory, exclusivity), and automatically flags conflicts when a project's deliverable specs exceed the purchased license scope. It must understand production-specific licensing language — "in-perpetuity web use" vs. "1-year social media license" vs. "unlimited internal screening rights" — and map these to actual client distribution plans.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates the 1-2 hours per project currently spent manually cross-referencing licenses. Prevents the $5K-$50K+ exposure from a single copyright infringement claim. Gives producers confidence to quote re-licensing fees instantly when clients request new distribution channels.
