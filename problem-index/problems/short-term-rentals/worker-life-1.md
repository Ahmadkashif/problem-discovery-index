# Turnover Coordination and Cleaner Dispatch Automation

**Industry:** [[short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** STR property managers who manually text cleaners every morning with that day's checkout/checkin schedule — a task that takes 20-40 minutes of coordination per day across a multi-property portfolio — get automated turnover dispatch based on the booking calendar, with linen logistics and issue reporting built in.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #llm

## The Problem

STR turnover is the operational heartbeat of short-term rental management. For every checkout, a cleaner must be dispatched within a window between checkout time and the next check-in. The property manager must: identify the day's turnovers from the booking system, match each turnover to an available cleaner with the right property access, communicate checkout time and check-in deadline, confirm the cleaner is en route, receive the post-cleaning report and any issue flags, and approve check-in readiness.

Across a 20-property portfolio, this coordination occupies 45-90 minutes every morning. It requires the property manager's active involvement because the cleaner assignment depends on availability, property familiarity, and the specific day's time constraints — all of which change daily.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Turnover coordination is the task property managers most want to eliminate. It's the reason they wake up at 6am instead of 8am and why they can't take a day off without risk of a failed turnover. It requires no judgment — the information is all in the booking calendar — but it's consumed manually by the manager every day.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A Breezeway or Properly integration (or standalone app) that: (1) ingests the booking calendar nightly and generates next-day's turnover task list automatically; (2) assigns each turnover to the preferred or available cleaner based on a configured priority list; (3) sends the cleaner their task (property address, checkout window, check-in deadline, linen requirements) automatically at 7am; (4) receives the cleaner's post-clean confirmation with photos and any issue flags; (5) notifies the property manager only when there's an issue or a turnover is at risk. Manager involvement drops to exception handling only — 5-10 minutes per day instead of 45-90.

## Impact If Solved

Returns 30-60 minutes per day to the property manager. Eliminates the early-morning coordination burden that caps portfolio size at ~15 properties per manager. Enables portfolio expansion without proportional management headcount increase.
