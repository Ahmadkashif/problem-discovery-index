# Guest Inquiry Response and Automated Messaging

**Industry:** [[short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** STR property managers who respond to 30-80 guest messages per day — pre-booking inquiries, check-in instructions, mid-stay questions, checkout reminders — and spend 1-2 hours daily on routine guest communication get that communication handled automatically, with only escalations and unusual requests requiring human response.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem

Guest communication in STR follows highly predictable patterns. 80% of messages are one of a small set of templates: "Is early check-in available?" (answer: depends on the day's turnover schedule, automated check possible), "What's the wifi password?" (in the house manual), "Where do I park?" (in the welcome message), "Can I get a late checkout?" (check calendar, approve or decline), "How do I operate the hot tub?" (walkthrough in the house manual). These questions repeat across every property, every booking, every week.

Responding to these messages manually consumes 1-2 hours per day for a manager running a 20+ property portfolio. The responses require no real judgment — they're information retrieval from the property's existing documentation. Yet the messages arrive continuously from 7am to 11pm.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Guest communication is the second most time-consuming task in STR management after turnover coordination. It's also the most boundary-violating — guests message at all hours and expect quick responses (Airbnb penalizes response times > 1 hour). Automating routine responses restores the manager's schedule and response rate simultaneously.

## What a Solution Looks Like

An LLM-powered messaging layer integrated with Guesty or Hostaway that: (1) receives incoming guest messages; (2) classifies the intent (check-in question, amenity question, early/late request, issue report, complaint, booking inquiry); (3) for routine intents: generates a response from the property's house manual and booking calendar data, sends automatically or with one-tap approval; (4) for non-routine intents (damage reports, complaints, special requests): flags for human response with context. The property manager reviews the automated message log once per day instead of responding in real time.

## Impact If Solved

Returns 1-2 hours per day. Improves response time metrics on Airbnb (automatic responses are faster than human responses, protecting response rate scores). Enables managers to handle larger portfolios with the same attentiveness to guest communication.
