# Condition Clearing Automation for Loan Processors

**Industry:** [[mortgage-brokers|Mortgage Brokers]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Processors get their evenings back instead of spending them chasing borrowers for the same missing documents across 30 open files.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transfer-learning #attention-mechanisms #transformers #entropy-cross-entropy-kl-divergence #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
After initial underwriting, every loan receives a list of 8-25 conditions that must be cleared before closing — items like updated bank statements, letters of explanation for large deposits, proof of insurance, HOA certifications, or corrected documents. Loan processors spend 40-60% of their day tracking which conditions are outstanding on which files, drafting borrower-specific requests explaining what's needed and why, following up when borrowers don't respond, and verifying that submitted documents actually satisfy the condition. A processor managing 25-40 active files sends 50-100 condition-related communications per day, many of them near-identical but requiring enough customization that templates don't work cleanly.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Loan processors are the most burned-out role in mortgage brokerage. The condition clearing loop is emotionally draining — borrowers are frustrated by repeated requests, loan officers pressure processors to close faster, and a single missing document can delay a closing and trigger a cascade of rate lock extensions and rescheduled signings. Processors report that the repetitive communication drafting and follow-up tracking is the primary driver of after-hours work and weekend catch-up. Turnover in the processor role exceeds 30% annually at many brokerages.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An LLM-powered system reads each condition from the underwriting response, generates borrower-facing messages in plain language explaining exactly what's needed with examples, tracks submission status across files, and auto-verifies basic conditions (e.g., confirming a bank statement covers the required date range and matches the borrower name). The processor reviews and sends rather than drafts from scratch, and the system escalates only the genuinely complex conditions that need human judgment.

## Impact If Solved
Saves processors 2-3 hours per day on communication drafting and tracking. Reduces average condition clearing time from 7-10 days to 3-5 days, directly shortening close timelines. Materially reduces processor burnout and turnover.
