# Billing Reconciliation and Accounts Receivable Management

**Industry:** [[small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Solo attorneys and small firm office managers who manually review unbilled time, send invoices, and follow up on overdue accounts — spending 4-8 hours per month on billing administration that generates its own awkwardness in client relationships — get an automated billing cycle that handles invoicing, follow-up, and AR aging without them having to initiate each step.
**Tags:** #automation #tabular-ml #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Billing is the least favorite task of most attorneys, and in small firms there is no billing manager to delegate it to. The workflow involves: reviewing unbilled time to ensure accuracy, preparing and sending invoices on the billing cycle, tracking which invoices have been paid, following up on overdue accounts at the right time and in the right tone, and writing off balances that are uncollectable. The follow-up step is particularly fraught — an attorney must contact a client about an overdue invoice without damaging the relationship or appearing to prioritize money over the matter. Many attorneys delay follow-up out of discomfort, allowing AR to age further. The resulting cash flow unpredictability is a significant operational stress for firm owners.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
The billing cycle is a perpetual overhead that attorneys perform poorly because it conflicts with their primary identity as legal advisors rather than collections agents. The relationship friction of billing follow-up — particularly with long-term clients who pay slowly — is a real psychological cost that solo attorneys bear personally. Many sole practitioners report that billing administration, combined with practice management overhead, consumes 8-12 hours per week that could otherwise be billable. This overhead is a structural disadvantage of small firm practice relative to large firms where billing is handled by dedicated staff.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated billing workflow that: runs at the billing cycle, prepares draft invoices from approved time entries for attorney review; sends approved invoices automatically on the scheduled date; sends a first reminder at configurable days past due (e.g., day 15) in a neutral, professional tone; escalates to a second reminder (day 30) with a slightly more direct tone; and surfaces overdue accounts beyond 45 days for attorney review and decision on next action (third party collections, write-off, payment plan). The attorney's interaction with billing is reduced to: approve invoices and make decisions on 45+ day accounts. Everything else happens automatically.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 4-8 hours per month of attorney/office manager time. More importantly, reduces average AR aging — firms that switch to automated billing typically see 15-25% improvement in days-to-payment — because consistent, timely follow-up (which the automated system performs without discomfort) is the primary driver of collections performance.
