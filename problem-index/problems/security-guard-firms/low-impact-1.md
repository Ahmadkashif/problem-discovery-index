# Incident Reporting and Client Intelligence

**Industry:** [[security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Incident reporting tools capture what happened, but they can't analyze patterns across hundreds of reports to surface the security intelligence that clients actually need — trend analysis, risk zone identification, and proactive recommendations.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #compliance

## The Problem
Security guards submit incident reports for every notable event — trespasser contacts, property damage, safety hazards, medical emergencies, alarm responses. These reports are filed but rarely analyzed. Clients receive a stack of incident reports monthly with no synthesis, trend analysis, or actionable recommendations. A pattern of repeated trespassing at the same loading dock at the same time suggests a security gap — but nobody connects these dots. Guard-written reports vary wildly in quality, from detailed narratives to "person removed from property."

## What Already Exists
TrackTik and Silvertrac capture incident reports with location, time, type, and narrative fields. Some generate basic frequency counts by type. Client portals display individual reports chronologically. None analyze report content for patterns or generate synthesized intelligence.

## The Customisation Gap
A security intelligence layer needs to: (1) standardize and enrich guard-written narratives (extracting key details from inconsistent prose), (2) analyze patterns across incidents (time, location, type, suspect descriptions), (3) identify emerging trends and risk concentrations, (4) generate client-facing intelligence briefs with proactive recommendations, and (5) benchmark site security metrics against similar properties.

## Impact If Solved
Transforms the client relationship from "body on a post" to "security intelligence partner." Justifies premium pricing through demonstrated analytical value. Identifies security gaps proactively rather than reactively, reducing incidents by 15-25%.
