# Association-Specific Event and Conference Management

**Industry:** [[trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic event platforms handle registration and logistics but fail at the association-specific workflows of speaker recruitment, sponsor fulfillment, CE credit tracking, and multi-track agenda optimization.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Trade association conferences are not corporate events — they serve as the primary venue for member networking, continuing education credit delivery, sponsor/exhibitor revenue generation, and policy discussion. Event planners spend 6-9 months per annual conference managing speaker recruitment pipelines (often from the membership itself), sponsor fulfillment obligations (booth placement, speaking slots, logo placement tiers), CE/CLE credit submission to accrediting bodies, and multi-track agenda construction that balances topical diversity against room capacity and attendee preferences. These workflows are deeply interconnected: moving a session to a different time slot cascades into sponsor contract obligations, CE credit hour calculations, and speaker availability conflicts.

## What Already Exists
Cvent, Eventbrite, and Whova handle registration, check-in, mobile apps, and basic agenda display. Higher Logic and MemberClicks offer event modules integrated with AMS membership records. These tools are built for generic corporate events or simple meetups and treat the agenda as a static schedule rather than an optimization problem with constraints.

## The Customisation Gap
Association events need a planning layer that understands sponsor tier contracts (Platinum gets a keynote slot, Gold gets a breakout, Silver gets expo-only), CE credit requirements per session (contact hours, learning objectives mapped to accreditation standards), speaker recruitment from the member database with conflict-of-interest screening, and attendee preference data from prior years to optimize track scheduling. The system should generate speaker invitation drafts referencing the invitee's past presentations and expertise areas, auto-calculate CE credit submissions from session metadata, and flag sponsor fulfillment gaps against contract terms. None of the existing platforms model these association-specific constraint relationships.

## Impact If Solved
Event planners reclaim 10-15 hours per week during the 6-month planning cycle — time currently spent on spreadsheet-based constraint juggling, manual sponsor fulfillment tracking, and CE paperwork. Sponsor satisfaction improves because fulfillment obligations are systematically tracked rather than remembered.
