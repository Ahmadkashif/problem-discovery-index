# Rights & Royalty Tracking Across Formats and Territories

**Industry:** [[independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Royalty accounting software exists for large publishers, but independent presses need a system that handles the chaotic reality of 15+ royalty structures per title across print, ebook, audiobook, foreign, and subsidiary rights without requiring a dedicated finance team.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
A single title at an independent publisher may have separate royalty terms for hardcover, paperback, ebook, audiobook, book club, foreign translation (by territory), serial rights, film/TV option, and merchandise licensing. Each format reports sales on different schedules, in different currencies, through different distributors. Rights managers track grant dates, reversion clauses, option windows, and territory restrictions in spreadsheets that become unmaintainable past 50 titles. Quarterly royalty statements take weeks to produce and frequently contain errors that damage author relationships.

## What Already Exists
MetaComet's Royalty Tracker and Westchester Publishing Services handle royalty accounting for large publishers. QuickBooks and Xero manage general accounting. But these tools either price out small presses ($20K+/year) or lack publishing-specific constructs like escalating royalty tiers, reserve against returns, foreign-currency conversion at statement-date rates, and cross-format rights dependency tracking.

## The Customisation Gap
The system must model publishing-specific royalty structures (advance earn-out with basket accounting, escalating percentage tiers tied to format-specific unit thresholds, reserves against returns with release schedules) and ingest sales data from Ingram, Amazon KDP, ACX, and foreign co-publishers in their native report formats. It needs to flag approaching reversion dates, expiring option windows, and territory conflicts automatically, and produce author-facing royalty statements that comply with Authors Guild recommended standards.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 20-30 hours of quarterly manual reconciliation per publisher, reduces royalty statement errors that cause author disputes and occasionally litigation, and surfaces revenue leakage from unexploited subsidiary rights or missed reversion deadlines worth $5K-$50K per title over its lifecycle.
