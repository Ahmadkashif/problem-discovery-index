# Feasibility — Defect Severity Calibration and Pricing Impact Estimation

**Industry:** [[home-inspection|Home Inspection]]
**Problem:** [[problems/home-inspection/worker-life-1|Defect Severity Calibration and Pricing Impact Estimation]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Home inspectors who can identify defects but aren't sure how to communicate their financial significance to an anxious buyer — is a failed water heater a $800 item or a $1,500 item? does this foundation crack need a $500 patch or a $15,000 underpinning? — get a repair cost estimate range for each deficiency that the inspector can include in the report.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Deliver mobile-first jobsite workflows with photo/checklist capture and scheduling tied to field realities.

## Open Source Data
Building codes, weather, and some permit data are open; job cost, photos, and schedule data are shop-specific.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
