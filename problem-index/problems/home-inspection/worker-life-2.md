# Agent Referral Relationship Management and Report Quality Scoring

**Industry:** [[home-inspection|Home Inspection]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Home inspectors who depend on real estate agent referrals for 70-80% of their bookings but have no systematic way to track which agent relationships are active vs. dormant, or to measure how their report quality compares to what agents say clients expect, get a referral relationship dashboard and report quality feedback loop.
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #automation #recommendation #worker-facing

## The Problem

Home inspectors are B2B service providers to real estate agents, even though the end client is the buyer. An inspector's business success depends on being on 5-15 agents' preferred vendor lists — the shortlists agents give to buyers when asked "who do you recommend?" An inspector who doesn't track their agent relationships actively loses those relationships by attrition — an agent who used them once and had a great experience stops referring when they haven't been contacted in 6 months and a new inspector introduced themselves at the broker meeting.

Inspectors manage these relationships entirely by memory and personal initiative. They have no CRM for agent contacts, no tracking of referral frequency per agent, and no feedback mechanism to understand what agents and buyers thought of their reports.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

A well-managed agent referral network of 15 active referral partners generates 300-500 inspections per year for a full-time inspector. Losing 3 agents from the network and replacing none reduces annual revenue by $15,000-$30,000. The difference between inspectors who maintain their networks and those who don't is primarily the discipline of systematic relationship follow-up — a task that inspectors consistently deprioritize in favor of the actual inspection work.

## What a Solution Looks Like

An ISN (Inspection Support Network) integration that: (1) tracks referral source per inspection booking; (2) alerts the inspector when a previously-active referring agent has gone 60+ days without generating a booking; (3) auto-generates a "reconnect" task with the agent's contact information and the last inspection they referred; (4) sends a brief post-inspection report quality survey to the buyer and the referring agent (2-3 questions, NPS-style); (5) surfaces the feedback as a quarterly report quality score by report section. The inspector reviews the referral health dashboard weekly and makes reconnect calls in 20 minutes instead of never.

## Impact If Solved

Active agent relationship management is the highest-leverage marketing activity for an independent inspector. Systematic reconnect outreach to dormant referral partners recovers 20-30% of relationships that would otherwise decay to zero. Buyer and agent feedback enables report quality improvement — identifying which report sections or communication styles are generating complaints or compliments.
