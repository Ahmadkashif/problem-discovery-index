# RFP/RFQ Response Assembly for Engineering Proposals

**Industry:** [[engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Proposal tools built for sales teams cannot assemble SF330 forms, project experience matrices, or discipline-specific resumes that engineering RFPs demand.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation

## The Problem
Engineering firms pursue 50-200 RFPs per year, and each response requires assembling project descriptions from past work, formatting staff resumes with PE licensure and relevant project lists, building organization charts with discipline leads, and writing technical approach narratives tailored to the specific scope. For federal work, the SF330 form (Architect-Engineer Qualifications) has rigid section requirements that must be followed exactly. A typical RFP response takes 40-80 person-hours, with marketing coordinators and principals spending nights and weekends before deadlines reconstructing information that exists somewhere across old proposals, project files, and staff records.

## What Already Exists
Proposify, PandaDoc, and Qvidian handle proposal workflows for sales-oriented organizations. Some AEC-specific tools like Cosential (now Unanet) and Deltek Vision offer CRM modules with project experience databases. These cover basic contact management and pipeline tracking but fall short on the content assembly problem — they store data but do not generate compliant proposal sections from it.

## The Customisation Gap
Engineering proposals are not sales decks. They require precise mapping of past project experience to RFP evaluation criteria, staff qualification matrices showing PE/SE licensure by state, technical approach narratives that reference specific methodologies (FEA, CFD, LRFD), and compliance with format requirements that vary by client and procurement type. The gap is a system that maintains a structured knowledge base of completed projects (scope, budget, client, disciplines, outcomes) and staff qualifications, then assembles draft proposal sections that match the specific RFP's evaluation criteria, page limits, and format requirements — especially SF330 Section E (project experience) and Section H (additional information).

## Impact If Solved
Cutting proposal assembly time by 50% saves a 15-person firm 1,000-2,000 hours annually — equivalent to a full-time marketing coordinator. More importantly, it allows firms to pursue more opportunities without burning out senior staff on repetitive writing.
