# Project Manager Timesheet Chasing and Invoicing

**Industry:** [[engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** PMs stop spending Friday afternoons hunting down timesheets and manually assembling invoices so they can focus on managing projects.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
Engineering project managers spend 3-5 hours every week chasing engineers for timesheet submissions, verifying that hours are coded to the correct project phase and billing rate, reconciling timesheet data with contract fee schedules, and assembling invoices that match client-specific formatting requirements. Many clients require detailed backup — hours by person, by task, by date — formatted in their proprietary templates. A PM managing 8-12 active projects may spend an entire Friday afternoon on this administrative cycle, with the added stress that late or inaccurate invoices delay payment by 30-60 days, directly impacting the firm's cash flow.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
PMs are promoted from senior engineering roles for their technical judgment and client relationship skills, then immediately buried in administrative work that uses neither. The constant timesheet nagging damages PM relationships with their project teams — nobody wants to be the person sending the fourth reminder email. Invoice assembly is tedious and error-prone, and every mistake (wrong billing rate, hours on the wrong phase, client template not followed) triggers a rejection cycle that adds weeks to payment. PMs routinely cite administrative burden as the primary reason they either burn out or refuse promotion into management.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated system that monitors timesheet submissions, sends escalating reminders through the channels engineers actually check (Slack, Teams, not email), validates entries against project phase budgets and flags anomalies (engineer billing to a closed phase, hours exceeding remaining budget), and auto-generates draft invoices in client-specific formats by pulling timesheet data, contract rates, and billing templates. The PM reviews a pre-assembled invoice package rather than building it from scratch, reducing the weekly cycle from hours to minutes.

## Impact If Solved
PMs reclaim 3-5 hours weekly — 150-250 hours annually — from administrative tasks and redirect that time to client communication, scope management, and mentoring junior staff. Faster, more accurate invoicing reduces average days-to-payment by 15-20 days, improving firm cash flow by tens of thousands of dollars annually.
