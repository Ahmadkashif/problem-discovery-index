# Fleet Coordinator Vehicle Assignment Juggling

**Industry:** [[fleet-managers|Fleet Managers]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Fleet coordinators stop spending their mornings in a frantic puzzle of matching vehicles to drivers to routes, and start managing exceptions to an optimized plan.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #feature-engineering #optimization-fundamentals #evaluation-metrics #worker-facing #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Fleet coordinators start each day with a constraint-satisfaction problem: assign available vehicles to available drivers for scheduled routes, considering vehicle type requirements (box truck vs. cargo van vs. pickup), driver certifications (CDL class, hazmat, endorsements), customer-specific requirements, vehicle maintenance status, and driver-vehicle familiarity. When a vehicle is down for maintenance or a driver calls in sick, the cascade of reassignments can take 30-60 minutes and results in suboptimal matches that increase risk and cost.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Fleet coordinators describe their job as "solving a puzzle that changes every morning before you're done solving it." The stress of last-minute changes, driver complaints about assignments, and responsibility for decisions that affect safety and service creates chronic anxiety. The role has high turnover because the mental load is relentless — every decision has downstream consequences for compliance, safety, and customer satisfaction.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated assignment optimizer that generates daily vehicle-driver-route assignments considering all constraints, with one-click reassignment when changes occur. The system suggests optimal swaps when vehicles or drivers become unavailable, quantifies the impact of each alternative, and maintains compliance audit trails automatically.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces daily assignment time from 45-90 minutes to 10-15 minutes of exception review. Decreases assignment-related driver complaints by providing transparent, data-backed assignments. Lowers coordinator turnover by removing the most stressful aspect of the role.
