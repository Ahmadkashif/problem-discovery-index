# Regulatory Monitoring and Change Digest

**Industry:** [[compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Compliance consultants who spend 5-10 hours per week reading Federal Register notices, agency guidance updates, and industry association newsletters — trying to stay current across 4-6 regulatory frameworks — get a daily digest that surfaces only the changes relevant to their specific client portfolio, with a plain-language summary of each change's significance.

## The Problem
Staying current with regulatory changes is an existential professional requirement for compliance consultants — giving a client outdated guidance is a career-ending mistake. But the volume of regulatory publications across even a single domain is daunting: the FDA publishes dozens of guidance documents, warning letters, and Federal Register notices monthly; OSHA updates standards and enforcement policies regularly; state healthcare regulatory boards add jurisdiction-specific requirements; international standards bodies (ISO, IEC) revise standards on multi-year cycles. A consultant covering healthcare compliance, for example, must monitor FDA, CMS, OIG, OCR (HIPAA), DEA, and state departments of health simultaneously. Current practice is subscribing to agency email lists, scanning regulatory news aggregators, and reading everything — including documents not relevant to any active client.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Regulatory monitoring is a form of professional overhead that has no ceiling — there is always more to read, and the cost of missing something is high. Consultants report reading regulatory publications during evenings and weekends to stay current, blurring the boundary between professional development and personal time. The anxiety of potentially missing a significant regulatory change that affects a client is a persistent background stress in the profession. Additionally, reading unfiltered regulatory publications is cognitively demanding even when the material is familiar — the technical language of regulatory drafting requires careful reading that can't be skimmed.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A regulatory monitoring service that: subscribes to all relevant agency publication feeds for the consultant's configured regulatory frameworks; processes each new publication with an NLP classifier trained to identify the regulatory section affected, the type of change (new requirement, modification, enforcement guidance, safe harbor), and the affected industry; filters and summarizes only the publications relevant to the consultant's active client industry configuration; and delivers a morning digest of new regulatory developments with a 3-5 sentence summary per item and a flag for items that likely require active client communication. The consultant reads the digest (10-15 minutes) rather than the primary publications.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces regulatory monitoring time from 5-10 hours per week to 30-60 minutes. Eliminates the anxiety of potentially missing a significant change — the digest surfaces everything relevant with high recall. Allows consultants to add more client engagements with the same monitoring burden because the monitoring scales with the tool, not with the consultant's reading time.
