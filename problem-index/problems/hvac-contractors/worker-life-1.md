# Load Calculation Assistant for Equipment Sizing

**Industry:** [[hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** HVAC technicians sizing replacement equipment who should perform a Manual J load calculation — the ACCA standard for determining correct system tonnage — but instead use rule-of-thumb methods (400-500 sq ft per ton) that produce oversized equipment, poor dehumidification, and comfort complaints get a mobile load calculation tool that produces a defensible Manual J result from house characteristics in 10 minutes.
**Tags:** #arithmetic-and-basic-algebra #automation #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem

Manual J load calculation determines the correct HVAC system size for a given home — accounting for square footage, ceiling height, insulation R-values, window area and orientation, climate zone, and occupancy. The correct calculation prevents both undersizing (system runs continuously, poor comfort) and oversizing (short cycles, poor humidity control, increased wear). Oversized equipment is endemic in the residential HVAC industry because technicians default to rule-of-thumb sizing rather than performing the full calculation.

A proper Manual J calculation using ACCA-approved software (Wrightsoft, Elite RHVAC) takes 30-45 minutes to input all the variables and produces a defensible equipment sizing report. Most residential HVAC technicians skip this calculation for straightforward replacements, using only square footage as a guide. The result: industry estimates suggest 50-70% of residential HVAC equipment is oversized by at least one ton.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

A technician who installs oversized equipment gets callbacks — the oversized unit short cycles, doesn't dehumidify properly in humid climates, and creates comfort complaints the technician has to diagnose and explain. For the rare customer who knows what Manual J is, a technician without a load calculation is immediately at a credibility disadvantage. Technicians want to do the right thing but the full Manual J workflow is genuinely cumbersome for a replacement call.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A mobile load calculation tool that: accepts inputs available at the service call (square footage from county records, ceiling height, rough window count and orientation, climate zone from address, existing insulation type from attic inspection); applies simplified but ACCA-defensible load calculation methods; outputs recommended equipment tonnage and a simplified load report. The technician adjusts for unusual conditions (unusually poor insulation, south-facing glass walls) and confirms the size before ordering equipment. Total time: 10-12 minutes instead of 30-45.

## Impact If Solved

Reduces oversized equipment installations. Protects technicians from comfort callbacks caused by their own equipment sizing decisions. Provides a documented sizing rationale for customer presentations — increases credibility of the replacement recommendation.
