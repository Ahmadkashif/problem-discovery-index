# Sales Consultant Lot Knowledge Overload

**Industry:** [[rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Sales consultants stop bluffing through walkthroughs of 150 unique units and start having instant access to every feature, spec, and known issue for the unit they're standing in front of.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #attention-mechanisms #transfer-learning #word-embeddings #worker-facing

## The Problem
An RV dealership lot holds 50-200 units, each unique — different floor plans, different option packages, different brands with different feature implementations. A customer walks onto the lot and points at a unit. The sales consultant must walk through that specific RV explaining features, demonstrating systems, answering questions about tow capacity, sleeping capacity, tank sizes, and comparing it to the unit three rows over. The consultant has seen this specific unit once during lot orientation, if at all. Faking knowledge destroys trust; admitting ignorance loses the sale.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
RV sales consultants describe the "fear of the walkaround" — the anxiety of walking into a unit with a customer and not knowing whether the water heater is gas-only or gas/electric, whether the awning is manual or power, or what the fresh water tank capacity is. Unlike car sales where every 2024 Camry is the same, every RV on the lot is functionally unique. The knowledge load creates impostor syndrome and reduces confidence, especially for newer consultants.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A mobile app that takes a stock number or VIN scan and instantly surfaces everything the consultant needs: floor plan, feature list, spec sheet, known issues for this model year, competitive comparisons to similar units on the lot, and customer-facing talking points. The app answers natural-language questions: "what's the tow rating for this unit?" or "how does this compare to the Jayco unit out front?"

## Impact If Solved
Enables new sales consultants to match the product knowledge of 10-year veterans within weeks. Increases average customer walkaround satisfaction by providing accurate, specific information. Reduces the "I'll have to check on that" moments that erode customer confidence and extend the sales cycle.
