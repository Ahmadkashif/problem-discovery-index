# Clinical Documentation Burden for Therapists

**Industry:** [[behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Therapists get back the 60-90 minutes per day they currently spend writing progress notes after sessions, reclaiming that time for direct patient care or simply going home on time.
**Tags:** #worker-facing #ml-nlp #ai-agent

## The Problem
After every 45-55 minute therapy session, the clinician must write a progress note — typically in DAP (Data, Assessment, Plan) or BIRP (Behavior, Intervention, Response, Plan) format — that documents what was discussed, clinical observations, interventions used, and the plan going forward. Most therapists see 6-8 patients per day, and each note takes 10-15 minutes. This means 60-90 minutes of documentation after their last patient, often done at home in the evening. The notes must be detailed enough to meet medical necessity requirements if audited, but therapists receive minimal training on documentation and live in constant anxiety about writing too much (privacy risk) or too little (audit risk).

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Therapists entered the field to help people, not to write notes. The documentation burden is the #1 cited reason for burnout in behavioral health, ahead of caseload size and emotional toll. Therapists lose their evenings to charting, experience decision fatigue choosing what to include vs. exclude, and carry chronic stress about whether their notes would survive a payer audit. Over time, this drives clinicians to reduce caseloads, switch to cash-pay-only practices (eliminating insurance documentation requirements), or leave the profession entirely.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A session-aware documentation assistant that the therapist briefly dictates to after each session — capturing key clinical observations, interventions used, and plan changes — and generates a properly structured note in the clinic's required format. The therapist reviews, edits, and signs in under 2 minutes. The tool understands behavioral health note structures, flags when medical necessity language is insufficient, and never records or stores raw session audio (only the therapist's post-session summary dictation).

## Impact If Solved
Reclaims 5-8 hours per week per clinician. Reduces documentation-related burnout — the leading driver of the 30%+ annual turnover rate in behavioral health clinicians.
