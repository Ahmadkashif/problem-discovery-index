# Automated Outcome Measurement Administration

**Industry:** [[behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Standardized outcome measures (PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5) are well-established tools, but administering them at the right intervals and connecting scores to treatment decisions is still a manual clipboard exercise in most behavioral health clinics.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #quick-win #ml-time-series

## The Problem
Payers and accrediting bodies increasingly require outcome measurement — administering validated screening tools at intake, regular intervals, and discharge. In practice, front-desk staff hand patients a paper form or iPad with a generic survey app, someone manually enters scores into the EHR, and clinicians check scores inconsistently. The result: outcome data exists but isn't reliably tied to treatment plan reviews or used to flag patients who are deteriorating.

## What Already Exists
Generic survey tools (Google Forms, Qualtrics, even SurveyMonkey) can administer any questionnaire. EHRs like SimplePractice and TherapyNotes have built-in PHQ-9/GAD-7 modules. Measurement-based care platforms like Greenspace and Owl Insights exist as standalone products.

## The Customisation Gap
Behavioral health clinics need measure selection tied to diagnosis (PCL-5 for PTSD, AUDIT for alcohol use, CSSRS for suicide risk), automated scheduling based on treatment plan review dates, score-change alerts that trigger clinical workflows (e.g., a 5-point PHQ-9 increase flags the case for supervisor review), and integration with payer-specific reporting requirements. The generic tools don't know which measure to give which patient or when.

## Impact If Solved
Saves clinicians 5-10 minutes per patient per measurement cycle in manual tracking. More importantly, ensures deteriorating patients are flagged within days rather than discovered at the next scheduled session.
