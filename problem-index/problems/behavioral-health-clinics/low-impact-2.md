# Insurance Credentialing Workflow Management

**Industry:** [[behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Provider credentialing with insurance payers is a solved workflow in general healthcare, but the behavioral health-specific nuances — license types, supervision requirements, telehealth-specific credentials — make generic credentialing tools fall short.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Every clinician at a behavioral health clinic must be individually credentialed with each insurance payer the clinic accepts. For a clinic with 15 therapists and 10 payers, that's 150 credentialing applications, each with different forms, required documents, and renewal timelines. Until a clinician is credentialed, the clinic cannot bill for their services — meaning a new hire can sit idle for 3-6 months while applications process. Tracking where each application stands across payers is a full-time job.

## What Already Exists
Credentialing platforms like CAQH (the industry standard provider data repository), Medallion, and Modio Health handle credentialing workflows for medical providers. They manage document collection, application submission, and renewal tracking.

## The Customisation Gap
Behavioral health has unique credentialing complexity: LCSWs, LMFTs, LPCs, and psychologists each have different scope-of-practice rules by state; pre-licensed clinicians (associates) require supervision documentation that must be submitted with credentialing packets; telehealth credentialing often requires separate applications; and many payers have behavioral health-specific carve-out networks (e.g., Optum Behavioral Health, Magellan) with their own credentialing processes separate from the medical payer. Generic platforms don't model these distinctions.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces new clinician revenue ramp-up from 3-6 months to 4-8 weeks. Eliminates the 10-15 hours per week a practice manager spends chasing credentialing status across payer portals.
