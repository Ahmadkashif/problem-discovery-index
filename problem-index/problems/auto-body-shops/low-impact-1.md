# Insurance DRP Compliance and Cycle Time Management

**Industry:** [[auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic project management tools can track timelines, but they can't navigate the insurer-specific DRP scorecards that determine whether a shop keeps its most valuable referral relationships.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml #compliance

## The Problem
Direct Repair Program relationships with major insurers (State Farm, GEICO, Progressive, Allstate) drive 50-70% of volume for most collision shops. Each insurer tracks different KPIs — cycle time, supplement ratio, customer satisfaction scores, touch time, parts utilization ratios — with different thresholds that determine whether a shop stays in the program. A shop in 4 DRP programs is simultaneously optimizing for 4 different scorecard systems, often with conflicting incentives.

## What Already Exists
CCC ONE and Mitchell provide DRP performance dashboards. Body shop management systems (BodyShop Booster) track cycle time and touch time. These tools report what happened but don't predict outcomes or recommend actions. No tool synthesizes across multiple insurer scorecards to optimize trade-offs.

## The Customisation Gap
A DRP optimization system needs to: (1) ingest each insurer's scorecard methodology and weighting, (2) predict how each operational decision affects scores across all programs simultaneously, (3) alert when a metric is trending toward a threshold, (4) recommend scheduling and workflow adjustments that optimize composite DRP performance, and (5) model the revenue impact of losing vs. maintaining each DRP relationship.

## Impact If Solved
Shops maintain DRP relationships that represent $500K-$2M in annual revenue per insurer. Reduces the management overhead of multi-DRP compliance from 5-10 hours/week of manual scorecard monitoring to automated alerts and recommendations.
