# Feasibility — Medical Records Review and Chronology Compilation

**Industry:** [[personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law]]
**Problem:** [[problems/personal-injury-law/worker-life-1|Medical Records Review and Chronology Compilation]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Legal staff who currently spend weeks reading hundreds of pages of medical records to produce injury chronologies — the most cognitively demanding and time-consuming task in a PI office — get an AI-generated first draft in hours, focusing their effort on review and strategic interpretation rather than extraction.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Integrate with case management, add retrieval over statutes/precedents, and ship automation with human oversight.

## Open Source Data
Public statutes and filings are open; matter notes, client data, and outcomes are proprietary, so training/grounding must use firm data.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
