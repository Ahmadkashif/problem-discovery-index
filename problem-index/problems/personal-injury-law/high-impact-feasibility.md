# Feasibility — Medical Record AI — Automatic Chronology, Injury Extraction, and Causation Linking

**Industry:** [[personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law]]
**Problem:** [[problems/personal-injury-law/high-impact|Medical Record AI — Automatic Chronology, Injury Extraction, and Causation Linking]]
**Problem type:** High Impact

**Problem summary:** A paralegal who currently spends 20-40 hours reading, paginating, and chronologizing medical records for a complex PI case gets a structured injury timeline, pre-existing condition flags, and causation gap analysis in under an hour — then spends their time evaluating the output rather than producing it.

## Solution Approach
Stand up a focused pilot that integrates current systems, captures the missing signals, and adds analytics/ML once the data path is stable. Integrate with case management, add retrieval over statutes/precedents, and ship automation with human oversight.

## Open Source Data
Public statutes and filings are open; matter notes, client data, and outcomes are proprietary, so training/grounding must use firm data.

## Worth Solving?
High — core outcome/financial impact justifies investment if change management is handled.
