# Medical Records Review and Chronology Compilation

**Industry:** [[personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Legal staff who currently spend weeks reading hundreds of pages of medical records to produce injury chronologies — the most cognitively demanding and time-consuming task in a PI office — get an AI-generated first draft in hours, focusing their effort on review and strategic interpretation rather than extraction.
**Tags:** #bert #named-entity-recognition #nlp #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
Medical records review falls almost entirely on paralegals and legal assistants in most PI practices. Reading 500-2,000 pages of records per case — including handwritten physician notes, hospital discharge summaries, physical therapy progress notes, and radiology reports — is exhausting work that requires sustained concentration over many hours. The task must be done carefully because missed findings (a pre-existing condition reference on page 847 of a hospital record) have real consequences for the case. Experienced medical records staff develop speed and pattern recognition over years, but the work never becomes truly efficient — every record set is different, every provider documents differently, and the key facts can be buried anywhere.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Medical records review is the kind of high-volume, attention-intensive task that burns out good legal staff over time. Paralegals who entered PI law for its human dimension — helping injured clients — find that their day is dominated by document reading rather than case strategy or client communication. Firms with high records review volume often have high paralegal turnover precisely because the work becomes monotonous and unsustainable. When a paralegal who has processed hundreds of cases leaves, the pattern recognition they built for specific injury types, treatment trajectories, and pre-existing condition flags leaves with them.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An AI records review tool that processes the full medical record set, extracts key clinical events (with source citation: page number and provider), builds a chronological timeline, flags treatment gaps, identifies pre-existing condition references, and produces a structured draft chronology in the firm's preferred format. The paralegal opens the chronology, reads the flagged highlights, reviews questionable extractions, and adds interpretive analysis (why a specific treatment gap may or may not be significant legally). What was 30 hours becomes 3 hours of skilled review.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 20-40 hours per complex case of paralegal time. Paralegals redirect effort toward case strategy, client communication, and deposition preparation — the work they were trained for. Firms can handle more active cases with the same staff, increasing revenue per employee.
