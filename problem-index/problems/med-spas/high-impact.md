# Treatment Recommendation Personalization

**Industry:** [[med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** High Impact
**One-liner:** Med spas stop leaving $200-$500 per patient visit on the table because injectors can recommend optimal treatment combinations based on facial analysis and outcome data from thousands of similar patients — not just the 3-4 treatments they personally prefer.
**Tags:** #cnn #gradient-boosting #recommendation #computer-vision #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a patient walks into a med spa consultation, the injector must assess their facial anatomy (bone structure, fat pad distribution, skin quality, muscle dynamics), understand their aesthetic goals ("I want to look refreshed" vs. "I want to look younger" vs. "I want to look like this Instagram photo"), consider their treatment history, budget, and pain tolerance, and recommend a treatment plan that may include neurotoxins (Botox, Dysport, Xeomin), dermal fillers (multiple types with different viscosities for different facial zones), laser treatments (BBL, Halo, Fraxel), radiofrequency (Morpheus8, Thermage), and topical treatments (chemical peels, microneedling, PRP).

An experienced injector with 10+ years of practice has seen thousands of faces and outcomes. They know that this 45-year-old with deep nasolabial folds, midface volume loss, and early jowling needs a combination of cheek filler (to restore the midface triangle), a small amount of chin filler (to improve jawline definition), Botox in the masseters (to slim the lower face), and a series of Morpheus8 treatments (to tighten the skin envelope). They know that filler alone won't achieve the desired result because the skin laxity needs to be addressed simultaneously. A newer injector might recommend only filler — addressing the volume loss but missing the skin quality and structural components — resulting in an outcome that looks "filled" rather than natural.

This treatment planning expertise directly drives both clinical outcomes and revenue. The experienced injector's comprehensive treatment plan generates $2,000-$4,000 per patient visit; the novice's single-modality recommendation generates $600-$800. The difference isn't upselling — it's clinical accuracy that produces better results AND higher revenue.

## Why It's High Impact
The average med spa has 3-5 injectors with widely varying experience levels. Revenue per patient visit can differ by 2-3x between the most and least experienced provider. If every injector could recommend treatment combinations at the level of the practice's best injector, the revenue impact would be $300K-$800K annually for a mid-size med spa. Patient outcomes would also improve: comprehensive treatment plans produce more natural results with higher patient satisfaction, leading to better retention and referrals. Current patient lifetime value (LTV) averages $2,500-$4,000; optimal treatment planning could increase LTV to $5,000-$8,000 through better outcomes driving more consistent return visits.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A consultation support system that combines facial analysis with treatment outcome data. During the consultation, standardized photos are captured (front, 45-degree angles, profile, with consistent lighting). The system analyzes facial proportions, volume distribution, skin quality indicators (texture, pore size, pigmentation, laxity), and dynamic muscle patterns. Based on the patient's facial assessment, stated goals, age, skin type, treatment history, and budget parameters, the system recommends a prioritized treatment plan — specifying which treatments address which concerns, the expected outcome, and a suggested sequencing timeline. The recommendation is informed by outcome data from similar patients (matched by facial characteristics, age, and concern profile) who received various treatment combinations.

## Implementation Path
Phase 1: Standardized facial analysis — anatomical landmark detection, facial proportion measurement, skin quality scoring from calibrated photos. Builds on existing facial recognition technology with aesthetic-specific training. 1,000-2,000 patient photos with expert annotation, 3-4 months. Phase 2: Treatment outcome correlation — pairing before/after photos with treatment records to build a dataset of "what works for faces like this." Requires 5,000-10,000 before/after pairs with complete treatment records (products used, quantities, injection sites, concurrent treatments). 6-9 months. Phase 3: Recommendation engine — generating personalized treatment plans based on facial analysis matched to outcome data. Requires integration with practice management systems for treatment history and scheduling. 4-6 months. Total timeline: 15-20 months to full deployment with 3-4 person team.
