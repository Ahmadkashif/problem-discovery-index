# Client Reporting Automation with Business Context

**Industry:** [[marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Reporting tools already aggregate the numbers — but no tool explains what the numbers mean for this specific client's business, so AMs spend 4-6 hours per client per month writing narrative around dashboards.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation

## The Problem
Every month, account managers pull data from 3-5 platforms (Google Ads, Meta, Google Analytics, email, SEO tools), paste metrics into a reporting template, and then write 1-2 pages of narrative context explaining what happened, why it happened, and what the agency recommends doing next. For an AM managing 12 clients, this consumes 50-70 hours per month — nearly half their available time. The narrative is the part the client actually reads, but it's the part no tool generates. Clients who receive numbers-only reports ask more questions, schedule more "catch-up" calls, and rate agency communication lower in satisfaction surveys.

## What Already Exists
Agency Analytics, Whatagraph, and Google Looker Studio automate metric aggregation and dashboard generation. They pull data from ad platforms, GA4, social channels, and email tools into branded templates with charts and KPI cards. Some offer automated email delivery of PDF reports on a schedule. DashThis and Supermetrics handle similar aggregation with slightly different connector sets.

## The Customisation Gap
None of these tools generate the narrative layer — the "Your cost-per-lead increased 18% this month because we shifted budget to top-of-funnel awareness campaigns that won't convert for another 4-6 weeks, which is expected and on-strategy" paragraph that prevents client panic. The narrative requires understanding the client's specific goals, the agency's strategic rationale for recent changes, seasonal patterns in the client's industry, and competitive context. An agency-specific reporting layer would ingest the raw metrics, cross-reference them with the client's goal document and the agency's campaign change log, and generate a first-draft narrative with recommendations that the AM reviews and personalizes in 15 minutes instead of writing from scratch in 90 minutes.

## Impact If Solved
AMs reclaim 3-4 hours per client per month on reporting, freeing 36-48 hours monthly for strategy work and client relationship building. Agencies can increase AM client loads from 12 to 15 without burnout, improving revenue per employee by 20-25%.
