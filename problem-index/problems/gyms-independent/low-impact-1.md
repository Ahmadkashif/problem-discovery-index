# Class Schedule Optimization

**Industry:** [[gyms-independent|Independent Gyms]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic scheduling tools treat yoga and HIIT the same way — independent gyms need demand-aware scheduling that accounts for seasonal swings, instructor draw, and room constraints unique to fitness.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #linear-regression #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #cross-validation #probability-distributions #bias-variance-tradeoff #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Independent gym owners spend 3-5 hours per week manually adjusting group fitness schedules, trying to balance instructor availability, room capacity, equipment needs, and fluctuating member demand. A spin class that fills 25 bikes in January drops to 8 riders by March; a new barre instructor draws a crowd that outgrows the studio within weeks. Owners rely on gut feel and stale attendance spreadsheets to decide when to add, move, or cut classes, leading to either empty rooms that cost instructor wages or overbooked sessions that turn members away.

## What Already Exists
Mindbody and Zen Planner offer basic class scheduling with waitlist management and attendance tracking. Google Sheets and simple dashboards let owners view historical attendance counts. Generic workforce scheduling tools (When I Work, Deputy) handle staff shift assignment but have zero awareness of fitness-specific demand patterns.

## The Customisation Gap
Fitness class demand is driven by factors that generic schedulers ignore: seasonal enrollment surges (New Year, pre-summer), instructor-specific draw (members follow a favorite teacher, not a time slot), equipment constraints (only 20 reformers, only 30 bikes), competing local studio schedules, and weather-dependent attendance for gyms without climate control. A gym-specific optimizer needs to forecast per-class attendance using these features, then recommend schedule changes that maximize total attendance while respecting instructor contract minimums and room turnover times.

## Impact If Solved
Properly optimized schedules can increase average class fill rate from 55-60% to 75-80%, translating to 15-25% more class revenue per week with the same instructor payroll, while reducing the owner's weekly scheduling burden from hours to minutes.
