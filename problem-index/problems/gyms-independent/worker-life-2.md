# Group Fitness Instructor Schedule Instability

**Industry:** [[gyms-independent|Independent Gyms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Instructors get predictable teaching hours and fair class assignments instead of a schedule that shifts weekly based on owner guesswork.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Group fitness instructors at independent gyms — overwhelmingly 1099 contractors — face chronic schedule instability. Classes get added, moved, or cancelled with days of notice based on the owner's perception of demand. An instructor teaching six classes per week can drop to three if a new format is tested in their time slot or if summer attendance dips. Because pay is per-class ($25-75/session), income swings 30-50% month-to-month. Instructors who teach at multiple studios to hedge their income end up with fragmented schedules, long commutes between locations, and zero benefits.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Most group fitness instructors chose the work because they love teaching and community — not for the financial stability, which is nearly nonexistent. Unpredictable scheduling makes it impossible to plan childcare, take on other part-time work, or commit to personal training clients. Instructors who build loyal followings at a gym can still lose prime-time slots to a new hire the owner wants to trial. The lack of transparency in how schedule decisions are made — attendance data is rarely shared with instructors — creates resentment and a feeling of disposability despite being the primary reason many members stay.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A demand forecasting system that projects per-class attendance 4-6 weeks out, shared transparently with instructors, and used to set stable minimum-class guarantees. Instructors would see projected attendance for their classes, understand why schedule changes are happening (data-driven, not owner whim), and receive advance notice calibrated to forecast confidence. The system would also recommend instructor-class pairings that maximize attendance based on historical draw patterns, giving popular instructors priority for high-demand slots rather than relying on seniority or favoritism.

## Impact If Solved
Instructors gain 4-6 weeks of schedule visibility instead of 1, income variance drops from 30-50% to under 15%, and instructor retention at independent gyms improves — reducing the constant recruiting cycle that costs owners 10-15 hours per month.
