# Sales Inspector Upsell Pressure

**Industry:** [[pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Sales inspectors stop being forced to choose between their commission and their conscience — an objective diagnostic system validates what the property actually needs.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #linear-regression #feature-engineering #cross-validation #evaluation-metrics #worker-facing

## The Problem
The "sales inspector" role combines pest diagnosis with sales — the person who identifies the problem also sells the treatment. Commission structures incentivize upselling: recommending a $1,500 termite treatment when monitoring would suffice, or selling a full-home fumigation when targeted treatment would resolve the issue. Ethical inspectors face constant tension between doing right by the customer and earning a living. The result is either customer exploitation (upselling) or inspector income loss (honest recommendations that leave money on the table).

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Sales inspectors report moral distress from the structural conflict of interest in their role. Many entered pest control because they enjoy problem-solving and helping people, not sales. The commission pressure creates ethical compromises that accumulate into job dissatisfaction and burnout. Inspectors who refuse to upsell earn less and face management pressure; those who comply lose self-respect and customer trust. The role has 35-40% annual turnover, among the highest in the company.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An objective inspection diagnostic tool that generates a treatment recommendation based on evidence — species identification, severity assessment, structural risk factors — before the sales conversation begins. The recommendation includes "minimum effective treatment" and "comprehensive treatment" tiers with clear justification for each. The inspector presents evidence-based options rather than subjective recommendations, removing the perceived conflict of interest and aligning the sales process with customer trust.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces inspector turnover by 20-30% by removing the core source of role dissatisfaction. Increases customer trust and close rates by providing evidence-based recommendations. Aligns company incentives with customer outcomes — a recurring customer relationship is worth more than a single upsell.
