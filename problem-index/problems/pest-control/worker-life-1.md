# Technician Chemical Exposure Risk

**Industry:** [[pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Technicians stop relying on "I'll be careful" and start getting real-time exposure tracking that keeps cumulative chemical contact within safe limits across their entire workday.
**Tags:** #cnn #binary-classification #computer-vision #worker-facing

## The Problem
Pest control technicians handle concentrated pesticides daily — mixing, loading, and applying products that range from low-toxicity baits to restricted-use organophosphates and carbamates. Acute exposure incidents (spills, spray-back, improper mixing) are visible, but chronic low-level exposure through skin absorption, inhalation of mist or dust, and contaminated clothing is the greater long-term risk. Technicians performing 8-15 applications per day accumulate exposure across products with different active ingredients, and no system tracks their cumulative daily load against safety thresholds.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Long-term health effects of chronic pesticide exposure include neurological damage, endocrine disruption, and elevated cancer risk — effects that develop over 10-20 year careers and are difficult to attribute to workplace exposure after the fact. Technicians are aware of acute risks but underestimate chronic exposure because they "feel fine" after each individual application. Younger technicians especially rush through PPE protocols under time pressure from the route schedule.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A cumulative exposure tracking system that logs every product used, application method, duration, and PPE worn throughout the day. The system calculates running exposure estimates by active ingredient against established daily limits (EPA worker protection standards), alerts when approaching thresholds, and recommends protective measures or service reassignment when limits are near. Camera-based PPE compliance monitoring at the truck-to-site transition ensures gloves, respirator, and protective clothing are worn correctly.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces chronic exposure incidents by 40-60% through cumulative tracking and threshold alerting. Provides documented exposure history for workers' compensation and health claims. Enables companies to demonstrate duty-of-care compliance. Extends healthy career longevity for technicians by 5-10 years.
