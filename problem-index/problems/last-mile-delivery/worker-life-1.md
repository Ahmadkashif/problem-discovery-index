# Address Intelligence and Access Note Capture for Drivers

**Industry:** [[last-mile-delivery|Last-Mile Delivery]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Delivery drivers who waste 2-4 minutes per problem address searching for apartment unit doors, intercom codes, or back entrance routes — knowledge that experienced drivers on that route have but new drivers lack — get that address-specific knowledge surfaced in their routing app before they arrive.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #word-embeddings #feature-engineering #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem

Address complexity is the primary driver of per-stop time variability in residential delivery. A driver delivering to 300 Riverside Drive, Apt 4B faces: finding the building entrance, identifying the intercom system, finding apartment 4B's buzzer, waiting for a response, gaining entry, finding the elevator or stairwell, navigating to 4B. For an experienced driver who delivers this route daily, this sequence takes 2-3 minutes. For a new driver or a driver on an unfamiliar route, it takes 5-8 minutes — a 3-5 minute per-stop premium on every access-complex address.

This knowledge exists in the heads of experienced drivers but isn't captured or transferred systematically. When a driver leaves, their route knowledge leaves with them.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

New drivers experience significantly more stress on complex routes — they feel they're falling behind schedule, worry about missing delivery windows, and encounter more frustrated recipients who've been waiting. The address intelligence gap is a primary contributor to new driver burnout. Capturing and systematizing route knowledge makes new drivers competent faster and reduces the experience gap.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A driver app feature (Onfleet or similar) where: (1) drivers can add access notes at any stop ("back entrance, gate code 4521, leave at door B"); (2) notes are shared with all drivers on the same route; (3) crowdsourced notes from multiple drivers are surfaced before arriving at the stop; (4) customer-provided access instructions (from e-commerce platform preferences or a pre-delivery form) are displayed automatically. The driver sees "Apt complex — enter via Oak St gate (code 4521), take elevator to 4th floor, unit 4B is on the right" before getting out of the vehicle.

## Impact If Solved

Reduces per-stop time on access-complex addresses by 3-5 minutes. On a 40-stop route with 15% access-complex stops, this is 18-30 minutes of recovered daily time — enough to add 3-5 additional stops to the route.
