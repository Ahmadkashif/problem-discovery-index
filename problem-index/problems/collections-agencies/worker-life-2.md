# Non-Contact Time Reduction

**Industry:** [[collections-agencies|Collections Agencies]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Agents reclaim productive work time by eliminating the 70% of their shift currently spent on wrong numbers, voicemails, and disconnected lines that yield zero meaningful conversations.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #logistic-regression #feature-engineering #cross-validation #evaluation-metrics #conditional-probability-and-bayes-theorem #bias-variance-tradeoff #worker-facing

## The Problem
A typical collection agent's shift looks like this: the predictive dialer connects a call, the agent hears ringing, then a voicemail, then a disconnected number, then a wrong party, then another voicemail — for hours on end. Industry data shows agents spend only 25-30% of their shift in actual conversations with debtors. The remaining time is dead air, wrong numbers, and voicemail boxes. This is not just an efficiency problem; it is profoundly demoralizing. Agents describe the experience as "calling into a void" — repetitive non-contact that creates a sense of futility and helplessness that compounds the emotional toll of the hostile calls they do connect.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
The psychological impact of sustained non-productivity is underestimated. Agents are measured on "right-party contacts per hour" and "promises to pay per hour," but the inputs they control (effort, skill, persistence) are overwhelmed by the system feeding them bad numbers. High performers and low performers get the same ratio of garbage contacts, creating learned helplessness. Agents who actually enjoy the problem-solving aspect of negotiating payment arrangements never get to exercise that skill because 70% of their shift is wasted on mechanical non-contacts.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A pre-call filtering layer validates phone numbers against real-time carrier data (disconnected, ported, wireless vs. landline), recent contact attempt outcomes across the agency's entire portfolio (not just the current campaign), and time-of-day reachability patterns. Numbers with less than 10% probability of reaching a live person are automatically routed to voicemail-drop or digital channels, preserving agent time for high-probability contacts. The agent's queue is restructured so that productive conversations are clustered, reducing the demoralizing stretches of repeated non-contacts.

## Impact If Solved
Agent productive talk time increases from 25-30% to 45-55% of shift. Right-party contacts per agent per hour double from 2-3 to 4-6. Agents experience a fundamentally different workday — one where their skills are actually utilized — reducing burnout-driven turnover by 10-15%.
