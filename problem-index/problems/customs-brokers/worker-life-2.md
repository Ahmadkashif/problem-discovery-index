# Client Duty Spend Analysis and Trade Program Opportunity Reporting

**Industry:** [[customs-brokers|Customs Brokers]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Customs brokers who have years of entry data for their major clients but never generate a comprehensive duty spend analysis — showing the client their total duty spend by HTS chapter, their top 10 duty-intensive products, and the specific programs (USMCA, CAFTA, GSP) they're eligible for but not using — get that analysis auto-generated quarterly.
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #automation #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem

Customs brokers hold years of entry data for their clients — every imported shipment, HTS code, country of origin, and duty paid. This data contains actionable intelligence: which products carry the highest duty burden, which countries of origin are qualifying for trade program eligibility the client isn't claiming, which Section 301 tariffs could be avoided by reclassification or country-of-origin engineering. Brokers who systematically analyze this data and present recommendations to clients become strategic advisors; brokers who don't are transactional filing services.

Most brokers never generate this analysis because it requires pulling data from the ACE portal and their entry management system, reformatting it for analysis, and writing a client-facing report — a 4-6 hour project per client per year. The 90% who skip it leave significant advisory fee and client retention opportunity untapped.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Trade compliance advisory fees ($150-$350/hour) are 3-5× the margin of transaction brokerage fees. A broker who converts 10 transactional clients to advisory engagements by delivering proactive duty optimization analysis adds $50,000-$200,000 in annual revenue. The barrier is the time required to generate the analysis, not the knowledge to do it — brokers know what to look for but can't justify the time at transaction brokerage rates.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A quarterly auto-generated client report from the broker's entry management system: total duty spend by HTS chapter (which product categories generate the most duty cost), top 10 duty-paying shipments, trade program eligibility analysis (does the country of origin qualify for GSP/USMCA/CAFTA? is the client claiming it?), and Section 301 tariff exposure summary. The report is generated in 10 minutes instead of 4-6 hours and delivered to the client with the broker's cover note.

## Impact If Solved

Enables brokers to deliver annual value reviews to every client without dedicated analysis time. Increases client retention by making the broker's value visible beyond transaction filing. Creates natural advisory upsell opportunities from identified duty optimization opportunities.
