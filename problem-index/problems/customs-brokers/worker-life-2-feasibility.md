# Feasibility — Client Duty Spend Analysis and Trade Program Opportunity Reporting

**Industry:** [[customs-brokers|Customs Brokers]]
**Problem:** [[problems/customs-brokers/worker-life-2|Client Duty Spend Analysis and Trade Program Opportunity Reporting]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Customs brokers who have years of entry data for their major clients but never generate a comprehensive duty spend analysis — showing the client their total duty spend by HTS chapter, their top 10 duty-intensive products, and the specific programs (USMCA, CAFTA, GSP) they're eligible for but not using — get that analysis auto-generated quarterly.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that aggregates ACE entry data to produce duty spend breakdowns by HTS chapter/product family and flags trade program eligibility (USMCA, CAFTA, GSP) with supporting rules. Keep brokers in control with transparent calculations and exportable client-ready summaries.

## Open Source Data
Trade agreement texts, HTS schedules, and CROSS rulings are public; the opportunity analysis depends on proprietary entry histories and client product catalogs.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
