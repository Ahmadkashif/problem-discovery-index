# OTA Commission Optimization & Direct Booking Conversion

**Industry:** [[hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic direct-booking tools exist but don't account for the boutique hotel's unique position — they need channel-mix optimization that tells them exactly when an OTA listing is worth the 18-22% commission and when to redirect spend toward direct acquisition.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #causal-inference #decision-trees #feature-engineering #hypothesis-testing #conditional-probability-and-bayes-theorem #evaluation-metrics #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Boutique hotels pay Booking.com and Expedia 15-25% commission per reservation, with the blended OTA share typically reaching 60-70% of total bookings. The operator knows they should drive more direct bookings but lacks the analytical framework to determine which OTA spend is truly incremental (guests who would never have found the hotel otherwise) versus cannibalistic (guests who would have booked direct but found the OTA listing first). Blanket strategies — "lower your OTA allocation" or "invest in Google Ads" — ignore the property-specific dynamics: a beachfront boutique in Savannah has fundamentally different channel economics than a design hotel in Portland.

## What Already Exists
Direct booking platforms (Triptease, The Hotels Network) offer price-match widgets and "book direct" messaging overlays. Metasearch management tools (Koddi, Derbysoft) automate Google Hotel Ads bidding. Channel managers like SiteMinder synchronize inventory across OTAs. These tools optimize within a single channel but none perform cross-channel attribution that answers the core question: "If I removed this OTA listing, how many of these bookings would I lose entirely vs. recapture through other channels?"

## The Customisation Gap
Boutique hotels need a causal inference model trained on their specific booking data — examining what happens to direct booking volume when OTA visibility changes, measuring the billboard effect (guests who discover on an OTA but book direct) per market and season, and recommending a property-specific channel mix that maximizes net revenue after commissions. This requires integrating PMS reservation data, OTA performance reports, Google Analytics referral paths, and advertising spend into a unified attribution model calibrated to properties with 20-80 rooms — not the 200+ room datasets these tools were built for. The model must also account for the boutique segment's higher repeat-guest rates and the lifetime value of a guest acquired direct vs. OTA.

## Impact If Solved
Shifting 10-15 percentage points of booking mix from OTA to direct channels saves $40K-$120K annually in commission costs for a typical 40-room boutique property, with the added benefit of owning the guest relationship for future marketing.
