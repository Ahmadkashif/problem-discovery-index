# Crew Productivity Tracking and Job Profitability Feedback

**Industry:** [[painting-contractors|Painting Contractors]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Painting company owners who don't know whether their jobs are profitable until the invoice is sent — and who have no visibility into which crew members or job types consistently run over budget — get a real-time profitability dashboard from clock-in/clock-out data and material usage records.
**Tags:** #linear-regression #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #descriptive-statistics #automation #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem

Painting company profitability is determined by two variables: labor hours per job and material cost per job. Most painting firm owners track total revenue but don't have job-level profitability visibility — they don't know which job types (interior vs. exterior, new construction vs. repaint) are profitable at their current pricing, or which crew lead consistently completes jobs within the estimated hours. Without this visibility, underpriced job types continue to be underpriced and underperforming crews continue to underperform without correction.

The data exists — clock-in/clock-out from field apps, material receipts per job, invoiced revenue per job — but it's not assembled into actionable profitability reporting. Owners who attempt to analyze it manually spend hours in spreadsheets for limited insight.

## Why It Matters to the Worker

Crew leads who complete jobs within estimated hours and with minimal material waste are the most valuable employees in a painting firm. They rarely get explicit recognition because the owner doesn't have the data to identify them. Conversely, crew leads who consistently run 20% over hours aren't corrected because the pattern isn't visible until it accumulates over months. Job-level profitability data enables performance conversations grounded in numbers rather than impressions.

## What a Solution Looks Like

A weekly profitability report in Jobber or PaintScout that shows: (1) per-job profitability (revenue minus estimated labor cost from hours logged minus material cost); (2) job type profitability breakdown (which categories are positive-margin vs. negative-margin at current pricing?); (3) crew performance ranking by hours-to-estimate ratio; (4) red flag jobs (>15% over estimated hours) with the deviation flagged for owner review. The report requires no manual data entry — it's generated automatically from the data already captured in the field service platform.

## Impact If Solved

Enables owners to identify and reprice unprofitable job types before another quarter of losses. Enables crew performance management grounded in data. Creates a feedback loop for estimating improvement — jobs that consistently run over budget indicate systematic estimating errors that can be corrected.
