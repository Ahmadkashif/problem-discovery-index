# Feasibility — Production Line Operator Ergonomic Strain from Repetitive Manual Inspection

**Industry:** [[food-manufacturing|Food Manufacturing]]
**Problem:** [[problems/food-manufacturing/worker-life-2|Production Line Operator Ergonomic Strain from Repetitive Manual Inspection]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Line inspectors who stand at a fixed station for 8-hour shifts performing continuous visual assessment of product passing at high speed develop chronic neck, shoulder, and eye strain from the static posture and repetitive focal-distance locking that the job demands.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Layer on MES/SCADA/quality logs, apply SPC/ML for anomalies, and pilot on one line before scaling.

## Open Source Data
Safety standards and some process benchmarks are public; yields, scrap, and line telemetry stay proprietary.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
