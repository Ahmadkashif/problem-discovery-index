# AM Contract Renewal Tracking

**Industry:** [[it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Account managers stop losing renewals and conversion-to-hire fees because contract end dates were buried in email threads and CRM notes they forgot to check.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #logistic-regression #feature-engineering #cross-validation #evaluation-metrics #conditional-probability-and-bayes-theorem #bias-variance-tradeoff #worker-facing

## The Problem
Account managers at mid-size IT staffing firms each manage 30-80 active contractor placements simultaneously. Each placement has a contract end date, optional extension windows (often requiring 30-60 day advance notice), conversion-to-hire triggers with fee schedules, and client satisfaction signals scattered across emails, Bullhorn notes, and Slack messages. AMs manually track these dates in spreadsheets or calendar reminders, but with dozens of overlapping timelines, they routinely miss renewal windows — discovering a contract expired only when the contractor asks about their next assignment. Each missed renewal costs the firm $15K-$40K in lost billing and risks the client relationship.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Missed renewals directly hit the AM's commission and bonus targets. But beyond money, the constant low-grade anxiety of "what am I forgetting?" is the real burden. AMs spend Sunday evenings scrolling through CRM records and email chains trying to reconstruct which contracts need attention this week. The mental load of tracking dozens of concurrent timelines with different terms, different client contacts, and different contractor satisfaction levels creates chronic stress that pushes experienced AMs toward burnout or career changes.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A predictive dashboard that ingests contract terms from the ATS/CRM, monitors email and Slack for sentiment signals (client complaints, contractor dissatisfaction, scope changes), and surfaces a priority-ranked list of contracts requiring action this week. The system predicts renewal likelihood using historical patterns — client renewal rates, contractor tenure, billing consistency — and flags at-risk placements 45-60 days before expiration. AMs receive a daily briefing with three categories: renew now, monitor closely, and on-track.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 5-10 hours per week of manual tracking time per AM. Reduces missed renewal windows by 70-80%, translating to $100K-$300K in preserved annual revenue per firm. Lowers AM stress by replacing reactive scrambling with proactive, system-driven prioritization.
