# Repetitive Client Status Inquiries During Filing Season

**Industry:** [[tax-prep-firms|Tax Prep Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Preparers and admin staff stop answering the same "where's my return?" question 40-60 times per day so they can focus on the work that actually requires their expertise.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #attention-mechanisms #word-embeddings #transfer-learning #evaluation-metrics #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
From February through April, the front desk and every preparer's phone/email become flooded with client inquiries that follow a predictable pattern: "Did you receive my documents?", "When will my return be ready?", "Has it been filed yet?", "Where's my refund?" A mid-size firm (1,000-2,000 returns) fields 40-60 of these calls and emails per day during peak weeks. Each interaction takes 3-5 minutes — checking the client's status in the system, composing a response, and managing the client's anxiety. Admin coordinators spend 3-4 hours daily on status communications alone, and preparers lose an additional 30-60 minutes of focused prep time to interruptions.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
For admin staff, the repetitive nature of answering identical questions with slightly different client names for 10 weeks straight is genuinely demoralizing. For preparers, every phone interruption during a complex return breaks concentration and adds 5-10 minutes of re-orientation time. The emotional labor is significant: clients are anxious about refunds, and some are aggressive about timelines, creating stress that compounds over the season. Seasonal admin staff, who are often the least trained, bear the brunt of client frustration and have the highest turnover as a result.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated client communication system that maintains real-time status for every engagement (documents received, documents missing, in preparation, in review, filed, accepted/rejected by IRS, refund issued), proactively sends status updates at each milestone via the client's preferred channel (text, email, portal notification), answers inbound inquiries with accurate, personalized responses ("Hi Sarah, your 2025 return is currently in review — we expect to file by March 12"), and escalates only genuine questions that require preparer judgment. The system integrates with the firm's tax software and client portal to pull status without manual updates.

## Impact If Solved
Admin staff recover 3-4 hours per day during peak season — equivalent to hiring one additional seasonal coordinator. Preparers eliminate 30-60 minutes of daily interruptions, recovering 2-3 returns worth of preparation time per week. Client satisfaction improves because they receive proactive updates rather than having to chase the firm.
