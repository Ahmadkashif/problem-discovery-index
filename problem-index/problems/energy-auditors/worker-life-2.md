# Scheduling and No-Show Management

**Industry:** [[energy-auditors|Energy Auditors]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Auditors stop losing 3-5 hours per week to no-shows and poorly routed schedules that burn fuel and daylight.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Energy auditors travel to homes and small commercial buildings across wide service territories — a single day might cover 50+ miles between appointments. No-show rates run 15-25% for residential audits because homeowners forget, lose interest, or do not understand they need to be present. When a homeowner no-shows, the auditor has a dead hour in their schedule plus wasted drive time, and the open slot cannot easily be filled because audits require homeowner presence and access to mechanical systems. Scheduling is typically handled through generic CRM tools (Housecall Pro, Jobber) that optimize for calendar density but not for route efficiency or no-show risk.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Auditors paid per-audit or on commission lose direct income on every no-show. Even salaried auditors face productivity pressure — utility program contracts often specify minimum audit volumes per month. The frustration of driving 30 minutes to a dark house compounds over weeks into genuine job dissatisfaction. Poor route planning means auditors spend more time driving and less time in buildings, stretching the workday and cutting into personal time during peak seasons (fall and spring, when weatherization programs ramp up).

## What a Solution Looks Like
A scheduling system that scores each appointment for no-show probability based on lead source (utility referral vs. self-scheduled), time since booking, homeowner engagement signals (opened confirmation email, responded to text), day of week, and weather. High-risk appointments get additional confirmation touchpoints or are double-booked with a nearby backup. Route optimization factors in drive time, appointment duration estimates (larger homes take longer), and real-time rescheduling when cancellations occur — automatically offering the open slot to nearby waitlisted homeowners.

## Impact If Solved
Reducing no-show waste by 50% recovers 1.5-2.5 hours per auditor per week. Route optimization saves an additional 30-60 minutes daily in drive time. Combined, an auditor gains the equivalent of one additional audit day per week without working longer hours.
