# Automated Treatment Compliance Follow-Up and Recall Management

**Industry:** [[veterinary-practices|Veterinary Practices]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Veterinary technicians who currently spend 30-60 minutes per day manually calling clients for follow-ups, prescription refills, lab result communications, and overdue vaccination recalls get that time back — with AI handling the routine communication loop and escalating only when a human response is required.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem
A vet tech at a busy practice manages a continuous stream of client communication that has nothing to do with patient care: calling owners to report that their pet's lab results are back and normal, reminding clients that their dog is overdue for a rabies booster, following up on whether the cat's antibiotic course improved the presenting symptoms, asking if the post-surgical patient is eating and mobile. This communication is important — it drives patient outcomes, compliance, and client retention — but it is almost entirely templated and doesn't require a clinical decision. Yet it's done manually by the most clinical members of the support team because the practice management system has no automation layer.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Veterinary technicians have significant clinical training and professional ambition, but in many practices they spend a meaningful fraction of their day on phone and messaging tasks that require no clinical judgment. This is a skill and satisfaction mismatch — a tech who trained to handle anesthetic monitoring, blood draws, and wound management is spending their afternoon calling people to say "your pet's bloodwork looks great." It's a waste of their training and a source of career dissatisfaction. In the context of a severe national vet tech shortage, this misalignment contributes to burnout and turnover.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An AI communication layer integrated with the practice management system that automatically drafts and sends (via text/email based on owner preference) routine messages: lab result notifications (normal results auto-sent, abnormal results flagged to vet before sending), vaccination recall reminders with online booking link, post-visit follow-up questionnaires (how is your pet doing? any concerns?), and prescription refill reminders. Responses that indicate a clinical concern (owner replies with new symptoms, question about medication) are flagged and routed to the appropriate staff member. The tech reviews a morning inbox of escalations rather than spending the afternoon on routine calls.

## Impact If Solved
Saves 30-60 minutes per tech per day on routine client communication, redistributing that time to clinical tasks. Improves client compliance (automated reminders are more consistent than manual calling) and recall rates — practices that implement recall automation typically see 15-25% improvement in annual exam completion rates. Reduces tech burnout by aligning daily work with clinical training.
