# Instructor Lesson Planning for Heterogeneous Groups

**Industry:** [[language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Instructors get back evenings and weekends currently consumed by building differentiated materials for classes where proficiency levels span two or more CEFR bands.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing

## The Problem
A typical language school instructor teaches 4-6 class sections per week, each containing 8-15 students whose actual proficiency ranges from strong A2 to weak B2 despite being nominally placed in a "B1" group. For every lesson, the instructor must prepare tiered versions of reading passages (simplified for lower students, extended for higher), scaffold writing prompts at multiple complexity levels, select listening materials with appropriate speech rate and vocabulary density, and design pair/group activities that create productive interaction across levels rather than frustration. This differentiation work is done manually — copying, editing, reformatting — and adds 1-2 hours of prep per teaching hour. Most instructors are part-time hourly employees; this prep time is often unpaid or compensated at a fraction of the teaching rate.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Instructor burnout in language schools is acute: the median tenure at independent language programs is under two years. The primary driver is not classroom difficulty but the unseen prep burden. An instructor teaching 20 contact hours per week may spend an additional 15-25 hours on differentiated planning, grading, and material adaptation — effectively working full-time-plus for part-time pay. The cognitive load of holding multiple proficiency profiles in mind while sequencing a 90-minute lesson is exhausting, and the work product (a set of leveled handouts) is discarded after a single use because the next cohort's level spread will be different.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An LLM-powered lesson planning tool that takes the instructor's topic, target grammar/vocabulary, and the class's proficiency distribution (pulled from the school's placement or assessment data) and generates a complete differentiated lesson package: a core text with two leveled variants, scaffolded discussion questions, a tiered writing prompt, and a suggested pairing matrix for group activities. The instructor reviews, edits, and approves — the tool handles the 80% of mechanical adaptation work. Output is formatted for the school's LMS or as printable PDFs. The instructor's expertise shifts from material production to material curation and in-class facilitation.

## Impact If Solved
Instructors reclaim 8-12 hours per week of unpaid or underpaid prep time. Lesson quality becomes more consistent across sections and across instructors with varying experience levels. Reduced burnout extends average instructor tenure, cutting the school's recurring recruitment and training costs ($3,000-$5,000 per replacement cycle).
