# Student Advisor Enrollment & Visa Paperwork Overload

**Industry:** [[language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Student advisors and DSOs stop drowning in repetitive enrollment forms, I-20 requests, and SEVIS data entry so they can actually advise students.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #compliance

## The Problem
Student advisors at language schools — many of whom also serve as DSOs (Designated School Officials) — spend the majority of their working hours on document processing rather than student support. During a typical enrollment cycle, each new international student requires: an application review, financial documentation verification, I-20 creation in SEVIS, an admissions letter, a pre-arrival orientation packet, and a housing questionnaire follow-up. Each continuing student requires: session-to-session re-enrollment paperwork, attendance compliance checks, program extension requests (if applicable), and any change-of-status filings. A school with 150 students and 6 session starts per year processes over 1,000 individual document transactions annually, almost entirely by hand. Advisors toggle between email, SEVIS, the school's SIS, Excel spreadsheets, and physical file folders — re-entering the same student data across 3-5 systems.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Advisors entered this role to help international students navigate a complex educational and immigration system — not to be data-entry clerks. The paperwork volume means that actual advising (academic planning, cultural adjustment support, career guidance, crisis intervention) is compressed into whatever time is left over, which is often zero. During peak enrollment weeks, advisors regularly work 50-60 hour weeks to clear the document backlog. The stress of knowing that a single data-entry error in SEVIS could terminate a student's immigration status adds a constant low-grade anxiety to every keystroke. Turnover in DSO/advisor roles at small language schools is high; replacements require SEVP certification training that takes weeks.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An LLM-powered document workflow that ingests a student's application packet (scan or PDF), extracts structured data (name, passport number, program dates, financial sponsor details), pre-populates enrollment forms and I-20 fields, generates the admissions letter and orientation materials from templates with student-specific details inserted, and queues all SEVIS-required data entries for the DSO's one-click review and submission. The advisor's role shifts from data transcription to exception review — flagging cases where documents are inconsistent, financials are borderline, or a student's situation requires a judgment call that only a human can make.

## Impact If Solved
Advisors reclaim 15-20 hours per enrollment cycle for actual student advising. Document-processing errors — currently running at 3-8% per cycle at small schools — drop below 1%. Advisor job satisfaction improves measurably, reducing turnover in a role where institutional knowledge and SEVP certification are expensive to replace.
