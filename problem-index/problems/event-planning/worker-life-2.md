# Weekend and Evening Work Destroying Personal Life

**Industry:** [[event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Planners stop sacrificing every weekend and most evenings to event execution, and start having the capacity and scheduling flexibility to have a personal life.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Events happen on weekends and evenings — that's when people celebrate. A full-time planner managing 20-40 events per year works 35-45 weekends annually, plus evening site visits, vendor meetings, and client consultations during the week. The work is front-loaded before each event (12-16 hour days in the week leading up) and concentrated during execution (14-18 hour event days). The result is a professional life that's structurally incompatible with personal relationships, family time, and rest.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Planners describe missing every family event, children's activities, and friend gatherings because their weekends belong to clients. Romantic relationships suffer — partners grow resentful of perpetual absence. Physical health declines from chronically inadequate sleep during peak season. The industry attracts creative, social people who ironically can't maintain their own social lives. Many talented planners leave to become corporate meeting planners (Monday-Friday) at lower pay, simply to reclaim their weekends.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A workload optimization tool that helps planners schedule events to maintain sustainable rhythms: no more than 2 events per weekend, at least 1 free weekend per month, buffer days built into the calendar before and after events. Revenue modeling shows the planner what their income looks like at different capacity levels, enabling conscious tradeoffs rather than defaulting to "take everything." Team coordination tools enable task delegation to assistants and day-of coordinators, reducing the planner's required hours per event.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces planner average working weekends from 40+ to 25-30 per year through better capacity management. Enables conscious work-life tradeoff decisions based on real revenue data. Improves planner retention by demonstrating that a sustainable career in event planning is achievable.
