# Claims Adjudication Speed and Accuracy

**Industry:** [[insurance-tpa|Insurance Third-Party Administrators (TPAs)]]
**Type:** High Impact
**One-liner:** TPAs cut claims processing time from 15-30 days to 3-5 days for routine claims by automating document extraction, coverage matching, and initial decision recommendations — while maintaining the accuracy that keeps carriers from pulling their book of business.

## The Problem
A TPA claims examiner processes 150-200 claims per month across workers' compensation, group health, or property/casualty lines. Each claim requires: (1) intake and documentation assembly — collecting the claim form, supporting medical records, employer reports, police reports, or property damage assessments, (2) coverage verification — matching the claim against the specific policy's terms, exclusions, waiting periods, and benefit schedules, (3) medical review — for health and workers' comp claims, evaluating whether the treatment is medically necessary, whether the diagnosis matches the mechanism of injury, and whether the provider's billing is appropriate, (4) reserve estimation — setting aside the predicted total cost of the claim based on diagnosis, treatment trajectory, and historical outcomes, and (5) adjudication decision — approve, deny, or pend for additional information, with written justification that can withstand regulatory audit and legal challenge.

The bottleneck is documentation review. A single workers' comp claim may include 50-200 pages of medical records, employer incident reports, witness statements, and prior claim history. The examiner must extract relevant facts from unstructured documents, cross-reference them against policy provisions, and make a coverage determination. This process takes 45-90 minutes per claim for routine cases and 4-8 hours for complex cases. Experienced examiners develop pattern recognition that allows them to scan documents faster — they know where to look for red flags, which diagnosis-treatment combinations are suspicious, and which providers consistently over-bill — but this expertise takes 3-5 years to develop and leaves when the examiner turns over (annual turnover in claims roles averages 25-30%).

## Why It's Unsolved
Claims documents are unstructured and highly variable — medical records from different providers use different formats, different terminologies, and different levels of detail. Policy language is complex and varies by client. Coverage determination requires matching unstructured claim facts against structured-but-nuanced policy terms, which rules engines handle poorly for edge cases. Auto-adjudication works for the simplest 30-40% of claims but fails on the remaining 60-70% that require judgment. The regulatory environment demands auditable decision trails, making "black box" AI unacceptable to carriers and regulators.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A three-stage AI pipeline: (1) Document extraction — NLP models extract structured facts from unstructured claim documents (diagnosis codes, treatment dates, providers, billing amounts, mechanism of injury, employer information) and assemble a claim summary. (2) Coverage matching — the extracted facts are matched against the specific policy terms to identify applicable coverages, exclusions, and benefit limits, flagging ambiguous cases for examiner review. (3) Decision recommendation — a classification model recommends approve/deny/pend with confidence score and supporting rationale, presenting the examiner with a pre-populated decision that requires review and approval rather than generation from scratch. The examiner remains the decision-maker but spends 10-15 minutes reviewing and approving rather than 45-90 minutes building the decision from raw documents.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces average claims processing time by 60-70% for routine claims, enabling TPAs to meet aggressive SLAs with smaller examiner teams. Increases examiner throughput from 150-200 claims/month to 350-500 claims/month. Reduces decision error rates by 30-40% through consistent policy application. Enables TPAs to compete for larger carrier accounts that demand faster turnaround. For a mid-size TPA processing 50,000 claims annually, this represents $2-5M in labor cost savings and $1-3M in new business from improved SLA competitiveness.
