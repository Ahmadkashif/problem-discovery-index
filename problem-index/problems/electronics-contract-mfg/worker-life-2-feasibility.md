# Feasibility — Rework Technician Micro-Soldering Strain

**Industry:** [[electronics-contract-mfg|Electronics Contract Manufacturing]]
**Problem:** [[problems/electronics-contract-mfg/worker-life-2|Rework Technician Micro-Soldering Strain]]
**Problem type:** Worker Life

**Problem summary:** Rework technicians who spend 6-8 hours per shift hand-soldering BGA, QFN, and fine-pitch components under stereo microscopes — removing and replacing components with hot air stations, soldering irons, and flux at 0.4mm pitch — develop chronic eye strain, hand tremor, and neck pain that limits their career span in the role to 5-10 years before physical degradation forces a role change.

## Solution Approach
Ship a lightweight assistant that removes repetitive steps for frontline staff, with fast feedback loops and clear handoff to humans. Layer on MES/SCADA/quality logs, apply SPC/ML for anomalies, and pilot on one line before scaling.

## Open Source Data
Safety standards and some process benchmarks are public; yields, scrap, and line telemetry stay proprietary.

## Worth Solving?
High for workers — improves retention and quality; commercial ROI is moderate but defensible as part of the platform.
