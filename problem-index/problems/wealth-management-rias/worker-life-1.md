# Advisor Meeting Prep Automation

**Industry:** [[wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Advisors get back 30-45 minutes per client meeting that currently goes to toggling between five different software systems to assemble a complete client picture.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transfer-learning #attention-mechanisms #transformers #entropy-cross-entropy-kl-divergence #worker-facing #data-integration

## The Problem
Before every client meeting — and a typical advisor has 6-10 per week — the advisor or their paraplanner must manually pull data from multiple disconnected systems: current portfolio holdings and performance from Orion or Black Diamond, financial plan status and projections from MoneyGuidePro or eMoney, recent communications and notes from Redtail or Wealthbox CRM, pending service requests or account paperwork from the custodian portal, and any recent life events or planning triggers noted in scattered email threads. This assembly process takes 30-45 minutes per client and involves logging into 3-5 separate applications, copying data, and mentally synthesizing it into a coherent briefing.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Advisors enter meetings either underprepared (because they ran out of prep time between back-to-back meetings) or exhausted from the mechanical data gathering that preceded the conversation. The actual advisory skill — reading the client, connecting financial decisions to life goals, building trust — requires mental energy that gets depleted by administrative friction. Associate advisors and paraplanners, who often handle the prep, describe it as the most tedious part of their job and a primary reason for turnover in operations roles.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A single interface that auto-generates a structured meeting brief 30 minutes before each calendar event: portfolio snapshot with notable changes since last meeting, financial plan progress against milestones, recent communication summary with sentiment flags, open action items and pending paperwork, and suggested talking points based on market conditions relevant to this client's holdings. The advisor opens one document and walks into the meeting fully briefed.

## Impact If Solved
Advisors reclaim 3-7 hours per week of prep time, equivalent to freeing up capacity for 2-3 additional client meetings. Paraplanner/operations staff turnover decreases as the most hated repetitive task is eliminated. Meeting quality improves because advisors enter every conversation with complete context rather than partial recall.
