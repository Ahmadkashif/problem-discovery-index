# Feasibility — Automated Document Assembly from Structured Client Intake

**Industry:** [[estate-planning|Estate Planning]]
**Problem:** [[problems/estate-planning/low-impact-1|Automated Document Assembly from Structured Client Intake]]
**Problem type:** Low Impact

**Problem summary:** Document assembly tools like HotDocs and Wealth Counsel generate standard estate planning documents from templates, but the intake process that feeds them — gathering asset information, family structure, and planning objectives — is still a manual conversation requiring the attorney to translate unstructured client input into structured form fields.

## Solution Approach
Adapt a horizontal tool with industry-specific templates, rules, and integrations so teams stop working around generic software. Integrate with case management, add retrieval over statutes/precedents, and ship automation with human oversight.

## Open Source Data
Public statutes and filings are open; matter notes, client data, and outcomes are proprietary, so training/grounding must use firm data.

## Worth Solving?
Medium — worthwhile as a customization/efficiency lever once a clear sponsor and data owner are identified.
