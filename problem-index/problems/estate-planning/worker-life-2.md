# Estate Administration Coordination and Task Tracking

**Industry:** [[estate-planning|Estate Planning Law Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Estate administration paralegals who manually track 40-60 open tasks per estate — contacting financial institutions, recording assets, filing tax returns, distributing to beneficiaries — across multiple simultaneous administrations get a structured workflow system that surfaces the right task at the right time and tracks every correspondence automatically.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #data-integration

## The Problem
Estate administration is a multi-month (often multi-year) process involving dozens of coordinated steps: obtaining death certificates, notifying beneficiaries, collecting financial institution letters and account statements, inventorying and appraising assets, filing estate tax returns, paying creditors, and distributing remaining assets. Each estate involves correspondence with 5-15 financial institutions — each with its own requirements, forms, and response times. A paralegal managing 10-15 simultaneous estate administrations is tracking 500+ open items, each at a different stage, with different deadlines, and requiring different follow-up. The current tracking tool is typically a combination of spreadsheet and email folder — chronically inadequate for the coordination complexity.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Estate administration paralegals work in a context of high consequence (beneficiaries waiting for distributions, tax filing deadlines with penalties for lateness) and high complexity (no two estates are the same; financial institutions have different requirements). The coordination overhead consumes a disproportionate share of their time — calling the same bank for the fourth time to ask for a copy of the account statement, or failing to notice that the estate tax filing deadline is 6 months from a death date that just passed 5.5 months ago. Missed deadlines create real harm (penalties, personal liability for the executor, professional liability for the attorney), but the current tracking systems are not sophisticated enough to prevent them reliably.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A purpose-built estate administration workflow system that: generates the complete task checklist for a new estate based on asset types and jurisdiction; tracks each task to completion with automated reminders at configurable intervals; logs every correspondence (email sent, letter received, phone call noted) against the relevant task; surfaces the daily priority list for each paralegal — which tasks are at risk of deadline, which institutions haven't responded in 30 days, which beneficiary distributions are approved and ready to process; and generates status reports for the executor at regular intervals. The paralegal reviews exceptions and exceptions only.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces the coordination overhead of estate administration by 30-40%. More importantly, eliminates missed deadlines and lost correspondence as a source of professional liability exposure. Allows paralegals to manage 20-25 simultaneous estates rather than 10-15 — significantly increasing firm capacity without additional headcount.
