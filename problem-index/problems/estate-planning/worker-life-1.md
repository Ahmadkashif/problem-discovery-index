# Client Meeting Preparation Brief Generation

**Industry:** [[estate-planning|Estate Planning Law Firms]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Estate planning attorneys who spend 20-40 minutes before each client meeting reviewing the file, refreshing their memory on the estate structure, and identifying what has changed since the last meeting get a structured two-page briefing generated automatically from the file — so they walk in ready, not rusty.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
An estate planning attorney with 200+ active clients cannot hold every client's family structure, asset configuration, and plan details in memory. Before a client meeting — whether for a plan update, a trust administration question, or an annual review — the attorney needs to re-read the file: the original plan documents, any amendments, the prior meeting notes, the asset summary, and the named parties. For a client with a complex plan last reviewed 3 years ago, this prep takes 20-40 minutes even for an experienced attorney. The prep quality also degrades under scheduling pressure — an attorney back-to-back with four client meetings in a day may arrive at the fourth meeting insufficiently refreshed on the file.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Meeting preparation is a cognitive overhead that doesn't create client value — the attorney is refreshing context, not applying expertise. It happens at the margins of the workday (early morning, between meetings) and is often rushed. The quality of the meeting suffers when prep is inadequate: the attorney may not recall a specific trust provision that is directly relevant to the client's question, may not remember that the client mentioned a pending business sale 18 months ago, or may not notice that a named beneficiary has died since the plan was drafted. These lapses erode client confidence and miss cross-selling opportunities (the pending business sale might warrant a business succession planning engagement).

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated meeting preparation brief generated 24 hours before each scheduled client appointment: family structure summary (spouse, children, key relationships), current plan structure (type of trust, key provisions, named executor/trustee/POA), asset categories documented (real property, financial accounts, business interests, estimated values if known), flagged issues since last review (death of a named party, relevant tax law change, anniversary of plan execution suggesting review), prior meeting notes summary, and suggested topics for the current meeting. The attorney reads a 2-page brief instead of a full file review.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 20-40 minutes per client meeting for the attorney. More importantly, improves meeting quality — the attorney arrives with full context and can focus on applying legal expertise rather than remembering case facts. Meeting preparation becomes a quality floor, not a variable quality ceiling.
