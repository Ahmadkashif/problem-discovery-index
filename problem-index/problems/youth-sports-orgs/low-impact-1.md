# Team Formation and Competitive Balance

**Industry:** [[youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Generic scheduling tools can assign players to teams, but they can't balance competitive fairness using the subjective skill assessments that actually determine game quality.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml

## The Problem
House leagues need balanced teams so games are competitive and fun. Current practice: a few board members sit in a room with paper cards, sorting 150 kids into 12 teams based on memory, coach evaluations, and friend requests. Travel clubs need tryout-based tiering that's defensible to angry parents. Both processes are manual, politically fraught, and produce inconsistent results.

## What Already Exists
TeamSnap and SportsEngine can randomly assign players to teams. Some leagues use basic rating systems (1-5 scales from coaches). Neither accounts for the multi-dimensional nature of player skill — a kid rated "3" in soccer could be a fast striker or a smart midfielder, and those aren't interchangeable for team balance.

## The Customisation Gap
Team formation needs sport-specific skill taxonomies (speed, positioning, ball control for soccer vs. shooting, rebounding, court vision for basketball), constraint satisfaction (sibling requests, carpool groups, coach conflicts), and fairness metrics that go beyond average rating to consider positional balance and skill-distribution variance.

## Impact If Solved
Games become more competitive, reducing the #1 cause of player dropout: blowout losses. Coach satisfaction improves when they receive balanced rosters instead of inheriting the "leftover" team.
