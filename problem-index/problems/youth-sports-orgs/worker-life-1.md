# Volunteer Coach Administrative Burnout

**Industry:** [[youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Volunteer coaches get back 5-8 hours per week currently lost to roster management, parent communication, and paperwork — letting them actually coach.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
A volunteer coach signs up to teach kids soccer. Instead, they spend 5-8 hours per week managing a group chat of 30 parents, tracking attendance, filing game reports, coordinating snack schedules, handling equipment distribution, and responding to individual parent concerns about playing time. Most coaches have full-time jobs and families — this administrative load is unsustainable.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Coach burnout is the #1 operational crisis in youth sports. When coaches quit mid-season, teams fold or merge, disrupting 15-20 families. More commonly, coaches just don't return the next year, forcing organizations into increasingly desperate recruitment. The coaches who remain are the ones willing to ignore the admin burden, which selects for less organized programs.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An AI-powered coaching assistant that handles parent communication (auto-responds to routine questions, sends practice reminders, manages RSVPs), generates game reports from simple voice input, tracks attendance automatically via check-in, and escalates only genuine issues to the coach. The coach opens one app, sees what needs their human judgment, and ignores everything else.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces coach administrative time from 5-8 hours to under 1 hour per week. Improves coach retention by removing the primary driver of burnout. Enables more parents to volunteer as coaches by lowering the perceived commitment.
