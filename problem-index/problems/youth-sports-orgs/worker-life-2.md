# Board Member Liability and Compliance Anxiety

**Industry:** [[youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Volunteer board members stop lying awake worrying about whether their organization is one missed background check or unsigned waiver away from a lawsuit.

## The Problem
Youth sports board members are personally liable for organizational failures — SafeSport compliance, background checks for all volunteers, insurance coverage, field safety inspections, concussion protocols, and state-specific nonprofit reporting. Most boards are 5-8 parents with no legal or compliance training, managing obligations they don't fully understand. A single missed background check on a volunteer can create catastrophic liability.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Board members report compliance anxiety as the #1 reason for not volunteering for leadership roles. Those who do serve spend disproportionate time on compliance tasks rather than program improvement. The personal liability exposure is real — board members have been named individually in lawsuits over youth sports injuries where safety protocols were incomplete.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A compliance dashboard that tracks every requirement by state and sport — background check status for all volunteers, waiver completion rates, insurance renewal dates, safety inspection schedules, and mandatory reporting deadlines. Automated alerts when any item falls out of compliance. Pre-built document templates for the most common requirements.

## Impact If Solved
Board member compliance time drops from 10+ hours/month to 2-3 hours/month of review. Organizations achieve and maintain full compliance instead of discovering gaps after incidents. Board recruitment improves as the perceived risk and burden of service decreases.
