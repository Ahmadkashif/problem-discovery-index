# Customer Expectation Management Burden

**Industry:** [[alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Tailors stop having five difficult conversations per day about why their $30 H&M dress can't be made to fit like a $3,000 bespoke gown — because visual AI-assisted intake shows the customer exactly what's possible, what's not, and why, before the tailor has to be the bearer of bad news.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing

## The Problem
Alterations customers frequently arrive with expectations that are structurally impossible or economically irrational. Common scenarios: a customer brings a $40 polyester dress and requests alterations that would cost $120 and still not achieve the desired result because the garment's construction doesn't support the changes. A bride wants her mother's 1985 wedding dress converted to a 2025 silhouette — requiring the dress to be essentially rebuilt. A customer wants a suit jacket "made smaller" without understanding that reducing more than one size requires pattern reconstruction, not just seam adjustment. The tailor must navigate these conversations with diplomacy, explaining garment construction limitations to people who have no frame of reference for why "just make it tighter" is a 4-hour job on a lined blazer but a 20-minute job on a cotton sundress.

These conversations are emotionally draining for tailors, who report them as the most unpleasant part of the job. Customers perceive the tailor as being difficult or overcharging when the tailor is simply explaining physical reality. Many tailors accept impossible jobs to avoid the confrontation, then absorb the extra time at below-minimum-wage effective rates. Others refuse work brusquely, losing customers and developing a reputation for being "unfriendly."

## What a Solution Looks Like
An intake system that captures the customer's garment photos and requested changes, then generates a visual explanation of what's involved: annotated images showing which seams will be altered, a complexity assessment explaining why the specific garment's construction affects the work (e.g., "Your jacket has pick-stitched lapels — adjusting the chest requires re-stitching these by hand, which adds 2 hours"), realistic before/after previews showing expected results, and a transparent price breakdown tied to specific tasks. The system serves as a diplomatic intermediary — the customer reads the explanation on a screen rather than hearing "no" from the tailor's mouth.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces confrontational customer conversations from 3-5 per day to 0-1 by providing objective, visual explanations that customers can process without feeling personally rejected. Decreases accepted-but-unprofitable jobs by 40-50% because customers self-select out when they see the true cost relative to the garment's value. Improves tailor job satisfaction by removing the emotional burden of being the constant bearer of disappointing news. Increases trust — customers who understand what they're paying for are more satisfied with results and become repeat clients.
