# Digital Vehicle Inspection Intelligence

**Industry:** [[auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** DVI tools capture photos and checkmarks, but they don't tell the service advisor which findings actually matter to this customer on this vehicle — leaving repair prioritization to guesswork.
**Tags:** #cnn #multiclass-classification #computer-vision #automation

## The Problem
Digital Vehicle Inspections generate 20-40 photos and 50+ checkpoints per vehicle. Service advisors must review this data, determine urgency, estimate costs, and present recommendations — all while the customer is waiting. They lack the technical depth to prioritize intelligently and the time to research each finding. Result: either everything is presented as urgent (destroying trust) or critical items are buried in a long list (creating safety risk).

## What Already Exists
AutoVitals, Bolt On Technology, and Tekmetric offer DVI platforms that let technicians photograph issues and mark items red/yellow/green. Some include canned descriptions and stock images. None analyze the photos to confirm the technician's assessment, correlate findings with vehicle-specific failure patterns, or prioritize by safety and cost-effectiveness.

## The Customisation Gap
An intelligent DVI layer needs to: (1) analyze inspection photos to confirm and grade severity (is that tire at 3/32 or 5/32?), (2) cross-reference findings with vehicle-specific TSBs and common failure patterns, (3) rank recommendations by safety urgency, cost-effectiveness, and customer vehicle lifecycle stage, and (4) generate customer-friendly explanations tailored to the vehicle owner's communication preferences.

## Impact If Solved
Increases average repair order value 15-25% by presenting findings in a trust-building, prioritized format. Reduces service advisor preparation time per vehicle from 15 minutes to 3 minutes. Improves customer trust through visual, data-backed recommendations.
