# Instructional Designer Content Update Treadmill

**Industry:** [[corporate-training|Corporate Training]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Instructional designers get their creative time back instead of spending 60% of their week updating existing courses every time a product feature changes, a policy is revised, or a regulation is amended.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transfer-learning #attention-mechanisms #transformers #word-embeddings #entropy-cross-entropy-kl-divergence #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
Instructional designers at mid-market companies maintain catalogs of 50–200 active courses. Every product release, policy update, or regulatory change triggers a cascade of updates across multiple courses — a single product UI change might affect 8 different courses that reference that screen. Designers spend Monday mornings reviewing change logs from product, legal, and HR teams, then spend the rest of the week hunting through SCORM packages and video scripts to find every reference that needs updating. They re-record narration, re-screenshot interfaces, re-export modules, and re-upload to the LMS. By the time they finish one update cycle, the next batch of changes has arrived. Original course creation — the work they were hired to do and trained for — gets pushed to evenings and weekends.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Instructional designers entered the field because they enjoy the craft of turning complex knowledge into clear, engaging learning experiences. Instead, they spend the majority of their time on mechanical find-and-replace work across multimedia assets, which is tedious, error-prone, and completely uncreative. The constant backlog of updates creates chronic stress — they know courses in the catalog contain outdated information, but they cannot get to them fast enough. Several IDs describe the feeling as "never being caught up," and burnout-driven turnover in corporate ID roles averages 18–24 months.

## What a Solution Looks Like
A content monitoring and auto-update system that watches source-of-truth documents (product docs, policy repos, regulatory feeds), detects changes relevant to specific courses, identifies exactly which slides, scripts, and assessment items need updating, generates draft updates with tracked changes for designer review, and handles the re-export and LMS re-upload after approval. The designer shifts from "find and fix" to "review and approve," spending 2 hours instead of 20 on a typical update cycle.

## Impact If Solved
Reclaims 20–25 hours per week per instructional designer for original course development. Reduces catalog staleness from an industry average of 35% of courses containing outdated content to under 5%. Directly addresses the primary driver of ID burnout and turnover.
