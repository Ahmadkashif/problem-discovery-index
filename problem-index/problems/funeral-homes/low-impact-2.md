# FTC Funeral Rule Compliance and Price List Management

**Industry:** [[funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Low Impact (Customisation Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Compliance management tools exist for every regulated industry, but none handle the FTC Funeral Rule's unique requirements — itemized General Price Lists, mandatory telephone price disclosure, casket handling rules, and embalming authorization disclosures — which carry $50,000+ fines per violation.

## The Problem
The FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453) mandates that funeral homes provide itemized General Price Lists (GPLs) to every family who inquires in person, disclose prices by telephone on request, offer specific casket and outer burial container price lists, and include mandated disclosures about embalming requirements, third-party casket acceptance, and cremation alternatives. State regulations add further requirements — some states mandate specific cooling and embalming timeframes, cremation authorization procedures, and preneed contract disclosures. A single GPL may contain 80-150 line items with prices that change annually. Most funeral homes update their GPL once per year on a Word document, with manual calculation of package prices that bundle individual items. Errors in GPL math, missing mandated disclosures, or failure to present the GPL trigger FTC enforcement actions with fines starting at $50,000 per violation.

## What Already Exists
Generic compliance platforms (Compli, NAVEX) track regulatory requirements across industries. SRS Computing and Passare include GPL templates. The NFDA (National Funeral Directors Association) publishes GPL guidance documents. No system actively monitors regulatory changes across all 50 states, validates GPL completeness against current requirements, or ensures that package pricing accurately reflects bundled item prices.

## The Customisation Gap
A funeral compliance system needs to: (1) maintain a current database of FTC Funeral Rule requirements plus state-specific regulations for all jurisdictions where the funeral home operates, (2) validate GPL documents against requirements — checking for mandated disclosures, accurate package-to-item-price reconciliation, and proper formatting, (3) generate telephone price disclosure scripts that comply with FTC requirements, (4) track GPL distribution (documenting that every inquiring family received the GPL as required), (5) alert when regulatory changes require GPL updates, and (6) manage preneed contract compliance with state-specific trust and insurance requirements.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates GPL compliance risk — the average FTC Funeral Rule enforcement action costs $50,000-$100,000 in fines plus legal fees. Reduces annual GPL update process from 8-15 hours of manual editing to 1-2 hours of review. Ensures telephone price disclosure compliance, which is the most commonly violated provision. Provides audit-ready documentation of GPL distribution for every family interaction.
