# PM Status Reporting Across Multiple Projects

**Industry:** [[software-dev-agencies|Software Development Agencies]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Project managers stop spending Friday afternoons manually assembling status updates for 5-10 clients and get that time back for actual project leadership.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
Agency PMs manage 5-10 concurrent client projects and each client expects a weekly status update — different formats, different levels of detail, different stakeholder audiences. A PM spends 3-5 hours every week pulling data from Jira, cross-referencing Slack conversations, checking GitHub for merge activity, reviewing time logs, and writing personalized status emails or slide decks. The work is pure synthesis: the information already exists across 6 tools, but no single view assembles it. PMs resort to copy-pasting from dashboards and manually translating developer-speak ("merged the auth refactor PR") into client-speak ("user login system upgrade completed").

## Why It Matters to the Worker
Status reporting is the most dreaded recurring task in agency project management. It is high-effort, low-creativity, and high-stakes — a missed update or an inaccurate one damages client trust. PMs who manage more projects get disproportionately punished because the reporting burden scales linearly while the actual management work scales worse. The result is that senior PMs cap their project load to protect reporting time, reducing agency throughput, or they burn out trying to manage 10 projects with reporting overhead designed for 5.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An automated status report generator that pulls from Jira/Linear (task completion, sprint progress), GitHub/GitLab (commits, PRs, deployments), Slack (key decisions, flagged blockers), and time tracking (hours burned vs. budget). It drafts a client-appropriate status update per project, in the client's preferred format (email, Notion page, slide deck), using language calibrated to the audience (technical CTO vs. non-technical founder). The PM reviews and edits in 5 minutes instead of writing from scratch in 30.

## Impact If Solved
PMs reclaim 3-5 hours per week — nearly a full half-day — and redirect that time to proactive risk management and client relationship building. Client satisfaction improves because updates become more consistent and timely, eliminating the "we haven't heard from you in two weeks" problem.
