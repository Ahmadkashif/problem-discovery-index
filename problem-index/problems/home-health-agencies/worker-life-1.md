# OASIS Assessment Documentation Burden

**Industry:** [[home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Type:** Worker Life Changing
**One-liner:** Home health nurses reclaim the 60-90 minutes per patient they spend completing OASIS assessments — the most dreaded documentation task in all of home health — and spend that time on actual patient care.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #attention-mechanisms #transfer-learning #bert #worker-facing #ai-agent

## The Problem
The Outcome and Assessment Information Set (OASIS) is a federally mandated comprehensive assessment that home health clinicians must complete at admission, every 60 days (recertification), and at discharge. It contains 100+ questions covering functional status, clinical status, service utilization, and social determinants. Clinicians complete it on a laptop or tablet in the patient's home, often while also performing clinical care, educating the patient and family, and managing medications. A single OASIS assessment takes 45-90 minutes to complete accurately. Errors trigger claim denials, incorrect case-mix scoring (which determines reimbursement), and potential compliance violations.

## Why It Matters to the Worker
OASIS is universally despised by home health clinicians. It's the documentation task they do on weekends, the reason they stay up until midnight, and the thing that makes experienced nurses leave home health for hospital jobs where documentation — while still heavy — doesn't follow them home. The cognitive burden of answering 100+ interrelated clinical questions while simultaneously providing care in a home environment (with family members asking questions, pets underfoot, and environmental distractions) leads to documentation fatigue, scoring errors, and deep professional dissatisfaction.

## What a Solution Looks Like
An intelligent OASIS assistant that pre-populates answers from existing patient data (prior assessments, recent visit notes, medication lists), guides the clinician through only the questions that need fresh clinical judgment, flags internally inconsistent answers in real-time, and validates scoring against CMS guidelines before submission. The clinician interacts with a streamlined interface that surfaces the 20-30 questions requiring genuine clinical observation rather than the full 100+ item set.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces OASIS completion time from 60-90 minutes to 20-30 minutes per assessment. For a clinician handling 3-4 OASIS assessments per week, that's 2-4 hours reclaimed. Directly reduces the #1 driver of home health nursing turnover.
