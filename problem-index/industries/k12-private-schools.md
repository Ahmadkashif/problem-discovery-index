# K-12 Private Schools

## Profile
**Category:** Education
**Market Size:** ~$70B US private K-12 education; ~30,000 private schools ranging from single-campus parish schools to multi-campus independent day schools
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Blackbaud (FACTS, Smart Tuition), Veracross, and Finalsite dominate SIS/CRM/billing; most schools have a website and an LMS (Google Classroom, Canvas), but AI adoption is near-zero and data sits in disconnected silos
**Workforce:** Teachers, administrators, admissions directors, development/advancement staff, business office staff, school counselors, learning specialists

## Key Pain Themes
Enrollment is existential: a private school that misses its enrollment target by 5% can face a six-figure budget gap with no public funding backstop. Admissions directors manually review hundreds of applications in a compressed January-March window, relying on gut feel and committee meetings to predict which families will actually enroll (yield) and which will attrite after year one. The business office juggles tuition billing across financial aid awards, sibling discounts, monthly vs. semester payment plans, and mid-year withdrawals — often in spreadsheets alongside FACTS. Teachers spend 8-12 hours per week on non-instructional tasks: attendance, grade entry, parent email threads, IEP/504 documentation, and report card narratives. Parent communication is fragmented across email, SMS, app notifications, and printed flyers, with no unified thread per family.

## Current Tech Landscape
Blackbaud's suite (FACTS SIS, Smart Tuition, NetCommunity) is the closest thing to an integrated platform, but schools frequently bolt on Finalsite for web/CMS, Google Classroom or Canvas for LMS, and a separate donor CRM. Data rarely flows between systems without manual CSV exports. AI-powered tools are almost nonexistent in the private K-12 space — enrollment analytics is done in Excel, and "personalized learning" usually means a teacher manually differentiating lesson plans.
