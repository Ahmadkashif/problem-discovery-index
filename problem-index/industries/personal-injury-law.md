# Personal Injury Law Firms

## Profile
**Category:** Legal
**Market Size:** ~$53B annually in plaintiff's attorney contingency fees; PI attorneys represent roughly 40% of all US attorneys
**Tech Maturity:** Low-Medium — case management software (Filevine, Clio, MyCase, SmartAdvocate) is widely adopted; analytics and AI are minimal despite enormous data assets locked in case files
**Workforce:** PI attorneys, paralegals, medical records staff, case managers, legal assistants, intake specialists — heavily leveraged toward paralegal and case management staff given contingency fee economics

## Key Pain Themes
Personal injury practice runs on three fundamental variables that are all poorly systematized: medical record review, case valuation, and settlement timing. Medical records for a typical auto accident or premises liability case arrive in stacks of 200-2,000+ pages covering emergency records, hospitalizations, specialist visits, physical therapy, and follow-up care — and each page must be read and chronologized to establish the injury timeline, link injuries to the accident, and identify pre-existing conditions the defense will exploit. This is the single highest labor cost in a PI practice and is entirely manual. Case valuation is the second broken pillar: experienced PI attorneys develop intuition about what a case is worth — integrating injury severity, jurisdiction, judge and jury composition, opposing counsel, the specific insurer and their known negotiating patterns, liability clarity, and client presentation factors — but this judgment is not systematized, varies between attorneys in the same firm, and is difficult to transfer to associates. The result is inconsistent settlement offers, cases settled too early or too late, and no firm-wide intelligence about which case types generate the best outcomes.

## Current Tech Landscape
Filevine and SmartAdvocate are the dominant PI-focused case management platforms, offering intake workflows, deadline tracking, and document management. Neither offers AI-assisted medical record review, case value prediction, or settlement timing optimization. Medical record retrieval and organization services (MedRetrieve, Record Retrieval Solutions) exist but are manual outsourcing, not AI automation. Some PI firms are experimenting with general-purpose LLMs for demand letter drafting, but with no systematic integration. The data assets in a mature PI practice — thousands of settled cases with documented outcomes, opposing counsel patterns, and insurer behavior — are entirely unmined.
