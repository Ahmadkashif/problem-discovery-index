# Corporate Training

## Profile
**Category:** Education
**Market Size:** ~$100B US corporate training / L&D market
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — LMS platforms like Cornerstone, Docebo, and Absorb dominate delivery; content libraries from LinkedIn Learning, Coursera for Business, and Udemy Business are standard; but measurement, personalization, and live-session intelligence remain primitive
**Workforce:** L&D managers, instructional designers, facilitators/trainers, LMS administrators

## Key Pain Themes
Corporate training teams operate under constant pressure to prove business value while managing an ever-growing catalog of mandatory compliance courses, skills development programs, and leadership tracks. Instructional designers spend weeks converting SME knowledge into structured curricula, only to have content go stale within months when products, policies, or regulations change. Facilitators running live sessions — whether in-person or virtual — rely entirely on gut feel to gauge whether a room of 25 managers is actually absorbing material or mentally checked out. Meanwhile, L&D leadership faces quarterly budget justification cycles where they cannot draw a credible line from a training investment to a measurable performance outcome, making the department perpetually vulnerable to cuts.

## Current Tech Landscape
LMS platforms handle enrollment, delivery tracking, and completion certificates well, but they are fundamentally content-delivery systems with minimal intelligence about learning effectiveness. Authoring tools like Articulate Rise and Adobe Captivate produce polished e-learning modules but require significant manual effort and offer no automated content refresh when source material changes. Analytics capabilities are limited to completion rates and quiz scores — no platform in common use connects training participation to downstream business metrics like quota attainment, error rates, or promotion velocity.
