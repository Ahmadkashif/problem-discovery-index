# Insurance Restoration

## Profile
**Category:** Insurance / Construction
**Market Size:** ~$60B US restoration market
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Xactimate dominates estimating, job management platforms (PSA, DASH, MICA, Next Gear Solutions) handle workflow, but field documentation and moisture mapping remain largely manual with handheld meters and paper logs
**Workforce:** Project managers, water/fire/mold technicians (IICRC-certified), estimators, contents specialists, subcontractors, equipment managers, and administrative/billing staff

## Key Pain Themes
Damage scope accuracy determines profitability — underscoping means the contractor absorbs costs, overscoping triggers carrier audits and preferred vendor program penalties. Moisture migration behind walls, under floors, and into adjacent rooms is invisible to the naked eye, requiring experienced technicians to predict where water traveled based on construction type, elapsed time, and visible indicators. Carrier program compliance (response time SLAs, documentation completeness, Xactimate line-item accuracy, customer satisfaction scores) determines whether a company keeps its preferred vendor status — which can represent 60-80% of revenue. Equipment logistics across concurrent jobs (dehumidifiers, air movers, air scrubbers, moisture meters) create constant allocation conflicts where pulling equipment from one job to rush another delays both.

## Current Tech Landscape
Xactimate is the universal estimating tool — carriers require it, and line-item accuracy directly affects payment. PSA (by Encircle), DASH, MICA, and Next Gear Solutions provide job management, but none integrates field moisture data with scope estimation in real time. Encircle and magicplan handle photo documentation. Moisture meters (Tramex, Delmhorst, Protimeter) are handheld and readings are recorded manually on grid paper. No platform connects moisture readings to drying predictions or scope expansion alerts.
