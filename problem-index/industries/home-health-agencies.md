# Home Health Agencies

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare & Clinical
**Market Size:** $130B annually in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Low — field-based care delivery creates unique tech challenges; most agencies cobble together scheduling, clinical documentation, and billing across disconnected tools
**Workforce:** Registered nurses, licensed practical nurses, home health aides, physical/occupational/speech therapists, scheduling coordinators, and compliance officers

## Key Pain Themes
Scheduling and routing field clinicians across a geographic territory is a daily logistical puzzle — coordinators manually match patient acuity, clinician certifications, visit frequency orders, and drive time, often using spreadsheets or whiteboards alongside a scheduling module that doesn't optimize routes. OASIS assessments (the federally mandated comprehensive patient evaluation) take clinicians 45-90 minutes per patient and must be completed with precise timing relative to admission, recertification, and discharge, with errors triggering claim denials. EVV (Electronic Visit Verification) compliance — required by the 21st Century Cures Act — adds another documentation layer that must reconcile with clinical visit notes and payroll. Clinician turnover exceeds 60% annually because of documentation burden, unpredictable schedules, and isolation from peers.

## Current Tech Landscape
Major platforms include WellSky (Kinnser), Homecare Homebase, and Axxess, which bundle scheduling, clinical documentation, billing, and OASIS into one system. These platforms handle the basics but struggle with intelligent route optimization, real-time schedule adjustments when patients cancel or clinicians call out, and predictive analytics for patient deterioration. Integration with hospital discharge systems and referring physicians remains largely fax-based.
