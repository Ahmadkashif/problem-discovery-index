# Security Guard Firms

## Profile
**Category:** Specialty Services
**Market Size:** ~$30B US security guard services market
**Tech Maturity:** Low — TrackTik, Silvertrac, and GuardsPro handle basic operations (guard tracking, incident reports, scheduling), but workforce management, site assessment, and client reporting remain largely manual
**Workforce:** Security guards (armed and unarmed), mobile patrol officers, site supervisors, operations managers, account managers, human resources specialists, and dispatchers

## Key Pain Themes
Guard scheduling is the operational nightmare — matching 100-1,000 guards across 20-200 client sites with varying shift patterns, certification requirements (armed, CPR, fire watch), client-specific preferences, and chronic no-show rates of 10-20%. A single no-show cascades into emergency callouts, overtime costs, and client dissatisfaction. Guard quality and reliability vary enormously — the industry has 100-300% annual turnover, meaning half the workforce at any given time has been on the job less than 6 months. Incident reporting is inconsistent and often uninformative, providing clients with minimal actionable intelligence. New site assessments require experienced operations managers to evaluate coverage gaps, patrol routes, and staffing levels — knowledge that doesn't transfer to junior staff.

## Current Tech Landscape
TrackTik (now part of Trackforce Valiant) leads in guard management (scheduling, GPS tracking, incident reporting, client portals). Silvertrac provides guard tour management and reporting. Belfry and ShiftBoard handle scheduling for guard firms. None offer intelligent scheduling optimization, predictive no-show management, or AI-assisted site assessment. Most firms use a combination of spreadsheets, text messages, and phone calls for daily operations alongside their management platform.
