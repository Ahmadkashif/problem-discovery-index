# Electrical Contractors

## Profile
**Category:** Construction & Trades
**Market Size:** ~$220B annually in the US electrical contracting industry; 70,000+ electrical contracting firms, 80% of which have fewer than 10 employees
**Tech Maturity:** Low — ServiceTitan and FieldEdge handle scheduling and dispatching; Procore is used by larger contractors for project management; but zero AI tooling exists for the technical work of electrical diagnostics, code compliance, or panel assessment
**Workforce:** Master electricians (licensed), journeymen electricians, apprentices, estimators, dispatchers — a typical small electrical firm has 1-3 master electricians, 3-8 journeymen, and 2-4 apprentices

## Key Pain Themes
Electrical contracting is a profession where the knowledge gap between a master electrician with 20 years of experience and a first-year apprentice is enormous — and almost entirely tacit. The master electrician walks into a panel room and within seconds identifies problems that a journeyman would miss: double-tapped breakers, wire gauge mismatches, Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panels that are fire risks, signs of arc flash damage on a terminal, a neutral conductor sized incorrectly for the load. This is pattern recognition built from thousands of panel inspections, service calls, and code enforcement encounters — knowledge that is almost impossible to document or transfer efficiently through apprenticeship. The NEC (National Electrical Code) is updated on a 3-year cycle, and understanding which code edition applies to which installation requires experience with both the code history and local adoption timelines — another tacit knowledge domain. Estimating labor hours is the second major pain: experienced electricians know how long it takes to wire a circuit in a wood-frame house vs. a concrete structure vs. a finished renovation, but this knowledge is internalized and not systematized, leading to estimating errors when junior staff prepare bids unsupervised. The electrical industry also has a severe knowledge transfer problem: the average master electrician in the US is 55+ years old, and the apprenticeship pipeline is not replacing retirements fast enough — the tacit knowledge walking out the door with retiring masters is a genuine industry crisis.

## Current Tech Landscape
ServiceTitan dominates field service management for electrical contractors, handling scheduling, dispatching, customer management, and invoicing. Field-edge and Jobber serve smaller firms. Procore is used by electrical contractors doing commercial work. Estimating software (ConEst, Accubid) handles quantity takeoffs for commercial projects but requires experienced electricians to input unit costs. The NEC codebook and NFPA resources exist in digital form. No tool exists for: AI-assisted panel inspection, fault diagnosis decision support, voice-to-structured-form field documentation, or apprentice training augmentation. The industry is entirely unaddressed by AI.
