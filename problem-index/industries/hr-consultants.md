# HR Consultants

## Profile
**Category:** Professional Services
**Market Size:** ~$25B in US HR consulting and outsourcing for SMBs; ~10,000 firms
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — BambooHR, Gusto, Rippling exist but HR consultants serve clients who can't configure these tools themselves and need hands-on advisory
**Workforce:** HR consultants/generalists, benefits specialists, compliance advisors, talent acquisition specialists, payroll administrators

## Key Pain Themes
HR consultants operate as fractional HR departments for dozens of SMB clients simultaneously, each with different state jurisdictions, HRIS platforms, and policy needs. Compliance monitoring is a constant treadmill — employment law changes at the federal, state, and local level pile up faster than any single consultant can track across their full client roster. Handbook creation, benefits benchmarking, and payroll processing are repeated for every client but require enough customization that templating breaks down. The multi-client model creates extreme context-switching overhead, where a consultant must mentally reload an entire client's regulatory environment, org chart, and cultural norms between every call.

## Current Tech Landscape
HRIS platforms (BambooHR, Gusto, Rippling, Zenefits) handle core HR operations but assume a dedicated in-house HR admin — not a consultant managing 30 accounts. Compliance tracking tools like SixFifty and Mineral (formerly ThinkHR) provide legal update feeds but don't map changes to specific client configurations. Benefits brokerages use proprietary quoting tools that don't integrate with the consultant's workflow, forcing manual data re-entry for every renewal cycle.
