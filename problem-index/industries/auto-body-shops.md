# Auto Body Shops

## Profile
**Category:** Automotive
**Market Size:** ~$50B US collision repair market
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — CCC ONE, Mitchell, and Audatex dominate estimating; body shop management systems (BodyShop Booster, Collision Hub) handle workflow, but damage assessment and repair planning remain heavily experience-dependent
**Workforce:** Collision estimators, body technicians, painters, frame technicians, parts managers, customer service representatives, and shop managers

## Key Pain Themes
The estimating-to-repair gap is the industry's central problem: initial estimates miss 30-50% of actual damage because hidden damage behind panels isn't visible until disassembly. This creates a supplement cycle where shops must repeatedly request additional funds from insurance companies, delaying repairs and frustrating customers. Insurance DRP (Direct Repair Program) relationships create pressure to keep supplements low, but underbidding destroys margins. Paint matching across OEM finishes, aftermarket panels, and aging existing paint is an art form that takes years to master. Cycle time — the days from vehicle drop-off to delivery — is the key performance metric, and it's driven by parts availability, supplement approval delays, and production scheduling.

## Current Tech Landscape
CCC ONE dominates the insurance-estimating ecosystem, connecting shops, insurers, and parts suppliers. Mitchell and Audatex provide alternative estimating platforms. Photo-based estimating (CCC Quick Estimate, Tractable AI) enables remote preliminary estimates but miss hidden damage by design. Body shop management systems track workflow and cycle time. OEM certification programs dictate repair procedures for specific manufacturers. I-CAR provides training standards.
