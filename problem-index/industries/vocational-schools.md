# Vocational Schools

## Profile
**Category:** Education
**Market Size:** ~$20B US vocational/trade school market; ~8,000 institutions including community college CTE programs, private trade schools, and registered apprenticeship programs
**Tech Maturity:** Low — Student information systems (Campus Cafe, SONIS, Populi) handle enrollment and billing, but placement tracking is spreadsheet-driven, competency assessments are paper rubrics or instructor gut calls, and accreditation reporting requires months of manual document assembly each cycle
**Workforce:** Trade instructors (often former journeymen), program directors, career services coordinators, admissions representatives, financial aid officers, compliance/accreditation staff

## Key Pain Themes
Vocational schools live and die by placement rates — accreditors like ACCSC and COE require documented employment outcomes, and prospective students choose programs based on "will I get a job?" Yet most schools track placements in spreadsheets updated by career services coordinators making 50-100 cold calls per week to employers and alumni. Instructors spend as much time documenting hands-on competency assessments as they do teaching, using subjective rubrics that vary instructor-to-instructor. Accreditation cycles consume 3-6 months of administrative effort assembling self-study documents from siloed data sources. Curriculum alignment with actual employer needs happens through annual advisory board meetings that are poorly attended and produce vague feedback.

## Current Tech Landscape
SIS platforms (Campus Cafe, SONIS, Populi) manage enrollment, grades, and financial aid but lack integrated placement tracking or competency assessment modules. Schools bolt on CRM tools like Salesforce or HubSpot for admissions funnels, but career services outreach is manual. Accreditation document management is mostly shared drives and Word templates. LMS adoption (Canvas, Moodle) exists for theory courses but rarely captures hands-on lab performance, leaving a critical gap for trade programs where the shop floor is the classroom.
