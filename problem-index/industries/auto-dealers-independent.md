# Independent Auto Dealers

## Profile
**Category:** Retail & E-Commerce
**Market Size:** ~$120B in independent (non-franchise) used car dealerships; ~38,000 independent dealers in US
**Tech Maturity:** Low — DealerSocket, VinSolutions, and DealerCenter exist primarily for franchise dealers; most independents run on spreadsheets, Craigslist postings, and dealer management systems from the 2000s that still require manual double-entry
**Workforce:** Dealer principal/owner, sales associates, finance managers, title clerks, lot attendants, reconditioning technicians

## Key Pain Themes
Independent dealers live and die by acquisition — buying the right car at the right price at auction is a margin game measured in hundreds of dollars per unit, and most owners rely on gut feel developed over years of buying. Once a car is on the lot, the clock starts: every day unsold costs $30-50 in floorplan interest, depreciation, and lot rent. Sales follow-up is chaotic because leads arrive from six different sources (web forms, Facebook Marketplace, phone calls, walk-ins, third-party listing sites, referrals) with no unified system to prioritize them. Title and registration paperwork varies wildly by state and municipality, creating compliance exposure and deal-funding delays that can stretch cash cycles by weeks.

## Current Tech Landscape
Franchise-oriented DMS platforms (DealerSocket, CDK Global, Reynolds & Reynolds) price independents out or lack features for auction-heavy workflows. Budget DMS tools like Frazer and DealerCenter handle basic inventory and forms but offer no analytics, no lead scoring, and minimal integration with auction platforms like Manheim or ADESA. Most independents stitch together a spreadsheet for inventory, a CRM trial they never fully adopt, and manual posting to Cars.com, Autotrader, and Facebook Marketplace — each requiring separate photo uploads and description entry.
