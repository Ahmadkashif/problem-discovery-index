# Oil & Gas Field Services

## Profile
**Category:** Energy
**Market Size:** ~$150B US oilfield services
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Fieldware and FieldFX handle field ticketing and service orders; SCADA systems monitor wellsite pressure, flow, and temperature in real time; but AI/ML adoption remains minimal, and most diagnostic decisions still depend on experienced field techs making judgment calls on-site.
**Workforce:** Field technicians, pumpers, well service operators, wireline technicians, frac crews, coiled tubing operators, field supervisors, and HSE (health, safety, environment) coordinators.

## Key Pain Themes
Wellsite equipment — rod pumps, ESPs, compressors, separators — fails unpredictably, and the cost of a single well going offline can exceed $10,000/day in lost production. Field technicians diagnose problems through sensory observation (sound, vibration, fluid color, smell) accumulated over years, but this expertise walks out when they retire. Service documentation is still dominated by handwritten field tickets transcribed hours later, leading to billing disputes, lost revenue, and incomplete maintenance histories. Regulatory compliance spans state oil and gas commissions, EPA emissions rules, and BLM lease terms, each with different reporting cadences that field supervisors track manually across dozens of scattered wellsites.

## Current Tech Landscape
SCADA and RTU systems provide real-time wellsite telemetry (pressure, temperature, flow rates), but alerts are threshold-based and generate excessive false alarms. Field service management platforms like FieldFX, ServiceMax, and Wellsite Report handle ticketing and dispatch, but none integrate sensory diagnostics or predictive analytics. Production accounting software (Avocet, ARIES) tracks decline curves historically but does not forecast equipment-driven production loss.
