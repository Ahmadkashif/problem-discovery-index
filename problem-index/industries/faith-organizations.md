# Faith Organizations

## Profile
**Category:** Nonprofit & Government
**Market Size:** ~$130B annual US religious giving; ~380,000 congregations
**Tech Maturity:** Low — Planning Center, Tithely, Pushpay, and Church Community Builder exist but adoption is spotty; most congregations still rely on spreadsheets, paper sign-up sheets, and tribal knowledge held by long-tenured staff.
**Workforce:** Pastors/clergy, worship leaders, children's ministry directors, administrative staff, volunteer coordinators

## Key Pain Themes
Congregations struggle with invisible attrition: families drift away over weeks or months and no one notices until they're gone, because attendance and giving data live in separate silos — or aren't tracked at all. Volunteer recruitment runs on guilt and personal asks, burning out the same 20% of members who say yes to everything. Facility scheduling across worship services, youth programs, recovery groups, and community events is managed via shared Google Calendars or whiteboards, producing double-bookings and last-minute scrambles. Pastoral care demand far exceeds capacity — a single senior pastor may carry 50+ active counseling relationships while also preparing sermons, managing staff, and fundraising.

## Current Tech Landscape
Planning Center dominates mid-size churches with check-in, giving, and volunteer modules, but its analytics are shallow — it tells you who came last Sunday, not who is trending toward disengagement. Tithely and Pushpay handle digital giving well but don't connect to attendance or small-group participation. Most churches under 300 members use no integrated system at all, relying on QuickBooks for finances and hand-written attendance rolls.
