# Owner-Operator Trucking

## Profile
**Category:** Transportation
**Market Size:** ~$250B US trucking industry; ~350,000 active owner-operators running as single-truck businesses or small fleets of 2-5 trucks
**Tech Maturity:** Low — DAT and Truckstop.com dominate load boards; ELD mandates forced adoption of KeepTruckin/Motive and Samsara for hours-of-service logging, but most operators still run their businesses on spreadsheets, paper receipts, and phone calls to brokers
**Workforce:** Owner-operators who simultaneously serve as driver, mechanic, dispatcher, accountant, compliance officer, and sales department — a one-person business hauling 80,000 lb loads at 70 mph

## Key Pain Themes
The core business decision — which load to accept — is made dozens of times per week under incomplete information: posted rates don't account for deadhead miles, fuel costs along the route, toll structures, detention risk at the shipper/receiver, or whether the destination market will have favorable outbound freight. Experienced operators develop an intuitive feel for "good loads" that newer drivers lack, and this tacit profitability sense is the difference between $80K/year and $180K/year net income. Compliance burden is relentless: IFTA fuel tax reporting across 48 states, quarterly filings, ELD hours-of-service management, DOT inspections, insurance renewals, and FMCSA authority maintenance all fall on one person who is also driving 2,500+ miles per week. Maintenance is existential — a blown turbo or failed injector means zero revenue and a $5,000-$15,000 repair bill, so operators develop deep mechanical intuition about their truck's sounds, vibrations, and fluid consumption patterns.

## Current Tech Landscape
Load boards (DAT, Truckstop.com, Uber Freight, Convoy) provide rate data and load matching but do not calculate true per-mile profitability after all costs. ELD providers (Motive, Samsara) capture driving data but don't optimize remaining hours against available loads. Accounting is typically done annually by a trucking-specialized CPA; few operators track per-load P&L in real time. TruckSmarter and CloudTrucks are emerging as operator-focused platforms but remain early-stage.
