# IT Staffing Firms

## Profile
**Category:** Technology
**Market Size:** ~$45B (US IT staffing and consulting)
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Bullhorn and JobDiva dominate ATS/CRM; HackerRank and Codility handle automated coding assessments; but recruiter judgment on candidate quality remains manual and experience-dependent.
**Workforce:** Technical recruiters sourcing and screening candidates, account managers maintaining client relationships and negotiating rates, delivery managers matching bench consultants to open reqs, and bench consultants awaiting or cycling between engagements.

## Key Pain Themes
Technical recruiters spend 60-70% of their day evaluating whether candidates can actually perform at the level their resumes claim — parsing GitHub activity, assessing project authenticity, and distinguishing genuine full-stack experience from keyword-stuffed profiles. Rate card management is largely spreadsheet-driven, with margins eroding when recruiters underprice niche skills or fail to track market rate shifts in real time. Contractor onboarding across different client IT environments (VPN configs, security clearances, toolchain access) creates a 1-3 week dead zone between placement and productivity. Account managers juggle dozens of active contracts with renewal dates, extension windows, and conversion-to-hire triggers scattered across email threads and CRM notes.

## Current Tech Landscape
Bullhorn and JobDiva provide core ATS/CRM functionality but lack intelligent candidate-skill matching beyond keyword search. HackerRank and Codility automate coding assessments but cannot evaluate soft skills, architectural thinking, or whether a candidate's claimed experience is genuine. Rate benchmarking tools like Staffing Industry Analysts exist at the macro level but do not provide the real-time, technology-specific, geography-specific rate intelligence that individual firms need for competitive pricing.
