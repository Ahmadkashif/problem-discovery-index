# Dental Practices

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare & Clinical
**Market Size:** $165B annually in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — practice management software is mature but imaging, treatment planning, and insurance verification remain poorly integrated
**Workforce:** Dentists (general and specialists), dental hygienists, dental assistants, office managers, front-desk/insurance coordinators

## Key Pain Themes
Insurance verification and benefit breakdown is the single biggest administrative time sink — front-desk staff spend 10-15 minutes per patient calling payers or navigating portals to confirm coverage, remaining benefits, and frequency limitations before the patient even sits down. Treatment plan acceptance rates hover around 50% because case presentation tools are disconnected from financial options (insurance estimates, payment plans, third-party financing), leaving patients confused about true out-of-pocket costs. Dental imaging exists in a silo — cone beam CTs, intraoral scans, and panoramic X-rays each have their own software, and none feeds cleanly into the treatment planning module of the practice management system. Multi-location DSO (Dental Service Organization) groups face an additional layer: standardizing clinical protocols and tracking provider productivity across locations with no unified analytics.

## Current Tech Landscape
Dentrix (Henry Schein), Eaglesoft (Patterson), and Open Dental dominate practice management. Cloud-native newcomers like Curve Dental and tab32 are gaining ground. Imaging software (Dexis, Planmeca Romexis, Carestream) remains separate. Insurance verification tools like Vyne Dental (formerly NEA) and DentalXChange automate some eligibility checks but rarely return full benefit breakdowns with frequency limitations.
