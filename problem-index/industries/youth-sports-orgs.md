# Youth Sports Organizations

## Profile
**Category:** Sports & Fitness
**Market Size:** ~$20B US youth sports market
**Tech Maturity:** Low — TeamSnap, SportsEngine, and LeagueApps handle registration but leave scheduling, volunteer coordination, and development tracking largely manual
**Workforce:** Volunteer coaches, board members, league administrators, paid club directors, referees, and parent coordinators

## Key Pain Themes
Registration season creates annual chaos as organizations juggle waitlists, age-group placements, and fee collection across multiple programs simultaneously. Scheduling fields, referees, and teams across weather cancellations and facility conflicts consumes 10-20 hours per week of volunteer time. Volunteer coach recruitment and retention is a perpetual crisis — 40% of youth coaches quit after one season due to administrative burden rather than coaching difficulty. Communication with hundreds of families across multiple teams requires constant attention with no single channel that reaches everyone.

## Current Tech Landscape
TeamSnap and SportsEngine dominate registration and basic team communication but offer minimal scheduling intelligence and no volunteer management. Most organizations supplement with Google Sheets for scheduling, Venmo for ad-hoc payments, and group texts for communication. Player development tracking is essentially nonexistent outside elite travel clubs.
