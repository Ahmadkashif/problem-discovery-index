# Childcare Centers

## Profile
**Category:** Education
**Market Size:** ~$60B US childcare/early childhood education; ~500,000 licensed providers (centers + family childcare homes)
**Tech Maturity:** Low — Procare, brightwheel, and HiMama dominate childcare management software with check-in/check-out, billing, and parent communication features, but AI adoption is near-zero and most operational decisions are manual
**Workforce:** Lead teachers, assistant teachers, center directors, administrative staff, floater/substitute teachers

## Key Pain Themes
The core operational constraint is maintaining state-mandated staff-to-child ratios (typically 1:4 for infants, 1:10 for preschoolers) across every room at every moment — during transitions, breaks, and callouts — while running on razor-thin margins (~1-3% net for most centers). Teacher turnover exceeds 30% annually due to wages that average $13-15/hr, creating a constant cycle of recruiting, onboarding, and quality loss. Billing complexity is extreme: a single family may combine state childcare subsidies, employer-sponsored dependent care FSA, sibling discounts, part-time/drop-in schedules, and late pickup fees, all with different payment cadences. Licensing compliance requires meticulous record-keeping — immunization logs, incident reports, fire drill documentation, allergen tracking, background check renewals — with unannounced inspections that can shut a center down for paperwork gaps.

## Current Tech Landscape
Procare (~35% market share), brightwheel, and HiMama handle check-in/out, basic billing, parent messaging, and digital daily reports. These platforms have improved parent communication significantly but do almost nothing for the two hardest operational problems: real-time ratio compliance during staff transitions and developmental assessment documentation. Scheduling is still done in spreadsheets or on whiteboards; developmental observations are handwritten or entered into clunky portfolio tools that teachers fill out after hours unpaid.
