# Independent Coffee Shops

## Profile
**Category:** Hospitality & Food Service
**Market Size:** ~$48B US coffee shop market; ~40,000 independent shops competing against chain dominance
**Tech Maturity:** Low-Medium — Most shops run Square or Toast POS with basic reporting; few use integrated inventory, scheduling, or loyalty tools beyond spreadsheets and punch cards
**Workforce:** Owner-operators (often working the bar themselves), baristas, shift leads, bakers/prep cooks, occasional delivery drivers

## Key Pain Themes
Labor scheduling is the central operational headache: demand swings wildly by hour, day of week, weather, and local events, but most owners build schedules manually from gut feel and last week's pattern. Perishable inventory — milk, pastries, fresh produce for food menus — creates daily waste-or-stockout decisions with thin margins that punish mistakes in either direction. Barista skill variance directly impacts drink quality and speed, but training is informal and turnover runs 60-80% annually. Customer retention beyond the morning-commute habit is poorly understood because most shops lack any data layer between the POS transaction and the human relationship the owner maintains in their head.

## Current Tech Landscape
Square and Toast dominate POS, offering basic sales reporting and some inventory tracking, but neither handles perishable waste forecasting or labor optimization well. Scheduling tools like 7shifts and Homebase exist but require manual demand inputs that owners rarely update. Loyalty is typically a physical punch card or a basic digital stamp (Square Loyalty), with no personalization, no LTV modeling, and no re-engagement capability. Espresso machine telemetry exists on high-end equipment (La Marzocca, Decent) but is not connected to quality feedback loops.
