# Small Law Firms (Solo and 2-10 Attorney Practices)

## Profile
**Category:** Legal
**Market Size:** ~$100B+ revenue segment; 360,000+ solo practitioners and 60,000+ small firms (2-10 attorneys) in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Low-Medium — Clio, PracticePanther, and MyCase have strong adoption for billing and matter management; AI tooling is minimal and adoption is inconsistent; most small firms have no data strategy
**Workforce:** Solo attorneys, associates, paralegals, legal assistants, part-time office managers — small firms are extremely lean, with attorneys often performing administrative tasks

## Key Pain Themes
Small law firms face the same competitive pressures as large firms — client expectations for responsiveness, documentation quality, and transparent billing — with a fraction of the administrative and analytical support. Time tracking is the most pervasive operational failure: small firm attorneys consistently under-bill by 20-35% because they don't capture time in real-time and reconstruct it from memory at day's end. Studies consistently show attorneys bill 1.5-2 hours per day less than they actually work because of missed entries — representing $50,000-150,000 in annual revenue loss per attorney at typical billing rates. Conflict checking is a second chronic pain: before accepting a new client, the attorney must ensure the new representation doesn't conflict with any existing or former client — a manual search process that is prone to error, especially as the firm's client history grows. Legal research, which small firm practitioners must do without the associate depth of BigLaw, is time-consuming and produces inconsistent quality when junior attorneys are involved. Cash flow unpredictability — particularly in contingency and flat-fee matters mixed with hourly billing — makes financial planning difficult for owners.

## Current Tech Landscape
Clio is the dominant small firm practice management platform, followed by PracticePanther and MyCase. All three handle matter management, billing, and document storage competently. Clio has introduced some AI features (AI-assisted time entries, document summarization) but adoption is nascent. Legal research tools (Westlaw, LexisNexis, Fastcase) provide the content; small firms often use only Fastcase or Clio's bundled free tools due to cost. Conflict checking is a manual search within the practice management system — adequate for the firm's direct client list but unable to capture business affiliates, parent companies, related parties, and former client conflicts that require legal judgment.
