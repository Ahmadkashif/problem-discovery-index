# Fleet Managers

## Profile
**Category:** Automotive
**Market Size:** ~$30B US fleet management services market (vehicles, telematics, maintenance management)
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Samsara, Geotab, Verizon Connect, and Fleetio provide telematics, GPS tracking, and maintenance scheduling, but predictive capabilities remain basic
**Workforce:** Fleet managers, fleet coordinators, maintenance technicians, dispatchers, compliance officers, and drivers

## Key Pain Themes
The central challenge is the reactive-to-preventive maintenance gap: breakdowns cost 3-5x more than scheduled maintenance, but determining optimal maintenance intervals for each vehicle requires analyzing telematics, operating conditions, and historical patterns that vary wildly across a mixed fleet. Driver behavior impacts fuel costs by 15-25% and maintenance costs by 20-30%, but coaching programs are generic rather than driver-specific. Compliance with DOT, FMCSA, and state regulations generates enormous documentation burden — hours-of-service, DVIR, drug testing, CDL verification — with severe penalties for violations. Vehicle assignment optimization across varying job requirements, driver certifications, and route characteristics is managed by gut feel rather than data.

## Current Tech Landscape
Samsara and Geotab dominate telematics with GPS, engine diagnostics, and driver behavior scoring. Fleetio provides maintenance management and parts inventory. RTA Fleet Management handles government fleets. Compliance is managed through a mix of ELD platforms (KeepTruckin/Motive), drug testing services, and manual tracking. No platform synthesizes telematics, maintenance history, driver behavior, and compliance data into a unified predictive system.
