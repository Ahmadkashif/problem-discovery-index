# Cleaning Companies

## Profile
**Category:** Specialty Services
**Market Size:** ~$60B US commercial and residential cleaning market
**Tech Maturity:** Low — Jobber, ZenMaid, and Swept handle scheduling and basic operations, but job bidding, quality management, and workforce optimization remain largely manual
**Workforce:** Cleaners (residential and commercial), team leads, supervisors, estimators, operations managers, and business owners

## Key Pain Themes
Commercial contract bidding is the make-or-break skill — underbid by 10% and you're working for free, overbid by 10% and you lose the contract. Experienced estimators walk a facility and know the labor hours needed by reading floor type, traffic patterns, fixture density, and window count, but this knowledge doesn't transfer to new estimators. Employee turnover exceeds 200% annually in residential cleaning, creating constant recruitment, training, and quality control costs. Multi-site commercial operations require route optimization across geographically dispersed locations with varying schedules (daily, 3x/week, weekly). Quality consistency is the core competitive differentiator but is nearly impossible to maintain without on-site supervision.

## Current Tech Landscape
Jobber and Housecall Pro provide scheduling, invoicing, and basic CRM for residential operations. Swept and CleanTelligent focus on commercial janitorial operations with inspection tools and work verification. ZenMaid targets maid services specifically. None offer intelligent bidding tools, route optimization beyond basic mapping, or computer vision-based quality verification. The industry is fragmented with 1M+ operators, most using spreadsheets or paper.
