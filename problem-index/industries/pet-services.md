# Pet Services

## Profile
**Category:** Specialty Services
**Market Size:** ~$12B US pet grooming, boarding, daycare, and walking services
**Tech Maturity:** Low-Medium — Gingr and PetExec dominate boarding/daycare management, Time To Pet handles dog walking scheduling, PetDesk provides client communication. Most small operators use paper intake forms and text messages for scheduling
**Workforce:** Pet groomers, daycare attendants, kennel technicians, dog walkers, pet sitters, facility managers, and front desk staff

## Key Pain Themes
Group play safety is the industry's highest-stakes operational challenge — mixing 20-40 dogs of varying sizes, temperaments, and play styles in daycare requires constant human monitoring, and a single dog fight can cause $5,000-$20,000 in veterinary liability plus permanent reputation damage from a single social media post. Grooming operations face extreme scheduling variability because a "standard bath and trim" takes 45 minutes for a cooperative golden retriever but 2.5 hours for a matted, aggressive doodle — yet both are booked for the same slot. Staff turnover exceeds 40% annually because the work is physically demanding, the pay is low ($12-$18/hour for attendants), and one bad bite can end a career. Seasonal demand swings — holiday boarding at 150% capacity, January at 50% — make staffing and cash flow management brutal for facility owners.

## Current Tech Landscape
Gingr dominates the boarding/daycare management space with check-in, feeding schedules, and report cards. PetExec is the primary competitor for multi-service facilities. Time To Pet handles dog walking route optimization and GPS tracking. PetDesk manages client communication and vaccination record tracking. None of these platforms address the core safety challenge — real-time behavioral monitoring of dogs in group settings — or provide meaningful analytics on grooming time estimation, pack compatibility, or injury prevention.
