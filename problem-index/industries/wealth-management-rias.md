# Wealth Management RIAs

## Profile
**Category:** Financial Services
**Market Size:** ~$5T AUM managed by ~15,000 RIA firms in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Orion, Black Diamond, and Tamarac handle portfolio management and reporting; Redtail and Wealthbox serve as CRM; MoneyGuidePro and eMoney power financial planning. Integration between these systems remains fragile and manual.
**Workforce:** Lead advisors, associate advisors, paraplanners, operations/trading staff, client service associates

## Key Pain Themes
RIA advisors spend 60-70% of their time on non-advisory tasks: pulling account data from custodian portals (Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing), assembling meeting prep packets across CRM, planning software, and portfolio tools, and formatting compliance documentation for SEC or state exams. Client communication is high-touch but unscalable — advisors develop deep intuition about which clients are about to make emotionally-driven decisions, but this pattern recognition lives entirely in individual heads and disappears when an advisor leaves the firm. Portfolio reporting exists but is generic; clients receive the same charts regardless of whether they understand duration risk or just want to know if they can retire on time.

## Current Tech Landscape
The RIA tech stack is fragmented across 5-8 applications with limited native integration. Orion and Black Diamond provide portfolio accounting and performance reporting, but narrative context is manual. CRM systems like Redtail capture contact logs but don't surface behavioral patterns. Financial planning tools produce projections but don't feed back into portfolio management automatically. Custodian portals require manual login and navigation for routine operations like money movements, account openings, and tax-lot harvesting.
