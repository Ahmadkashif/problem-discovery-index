# Auto Repair Shops

## Profile
**Category:** Automotive
**Market Size:** ~$75B US independent auto repair market
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Mitchell 1, AllData, and ShopWare/Tekmetric provide shop management, parts lookup, and labor guides, but diagnostic workflows remain heavily experience-dependent
**Workforce:** ASE-certified technicians, service advisors, shop owners/managers, parts specialists, and apprentices

## Key Pain Themes
Diagnostic accuracy is the central bottleneck — experienced technicians diagnose from sound, feel, vibration, and smell before connecting a scan tool, while newer techs over-rely on OBD codes that identify symptoms rather than root causes. The flat-rate pay model creates perverse incentives where technicians are paid per job, not per hour, rewarding speed over thoroughness and discouraging time-consuming diagnostic work. Parts sourcing across multiple distributors (NAPA, O'Reilly, AutoZone, dealer) is a daily time sink — the same part varies 40-60% in price, availability, and quality across suppliers. Customer communication is a constant friction point: translating technical findings into language that builds trust rather than suspicion.

## Current Tech Landscape
Mitchell 1 ProDemand and AllData provide repair procedures and TSBs but don't learn from shop-specific diagnostic patterns. Shop management systems (Tekmetric, Shop-Ware, AutoFluent) handle workflow and invoicing but lack predictive capabilities. Digital vehicle inspection (DVI) tools like AutoVitals and Bolt On generate visual reports but don't prioritize findings by urgency or predict related failures. OBD-II scan tools provide codes but not root-cause analysis.
