# E-Commerce Sellers

## Profile
**Category:** Retail & E-Commerce
**Market Size:** ~$600B in US e-commerce (excluding Amazon 1P); ~2.5M active sellers on Amazon, Shopify, Walmart, eBay
**Tech Maturity:** High on infrastructure (Shopify, Amazon Seller Central), Low on profit analytics and operational intelligence
**Workforce:** Founders/operators, listing specialists, PPC managers, supply chain/logistics coordinators, customer service reps

## Key Pain Themes
Sellers operate across multiple marketplaces — Amazon, Shopify, Walmart, eBay — each with different fee structures, advertising platforms, listing requirements, and customer communication channels. True per-SKU profitability is nearly impossible to calculate because costs are fragmented across ad spend, marketplace fees (which change quarterly), return shipping, FBA storage fees, and COGS that fluctuate with supplier pricing and freight rates. Advertising management is a full-time job: Amazon PPC alone requires daily bid adjustments across hundreds of campaigns, while Shopify sellers juggle Meta, Google Shopping, and TikTok ads simultaneously. Account health on Amazon is an existential risk — a single unresolved policy violation or IP complaint can freeze revenue overnight with no human support available.

## Current Tech Landscape
Shopify and Amazon Seller Central provide robust storefront and fulfillment infrastructure, but neither offers meaningful profit analytics beyond gross revenue. Tools like Helium 10, Jungle Scout, and Sellbrite address specific slices (keyword research, inventory sync, product research) but don't integrate into a unified operational view. The gap is widest in cross-channel profitability, intelligent ad optimization, and proactive compliance monitoring — sellers stitch together 5-10 SaaS tools and spreadsheets to approximate what should be a single dashboard.
