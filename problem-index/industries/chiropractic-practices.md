# Chiropractic Practices

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare
**Market Size:** ~$18B US chiropractic industry, ~70,000 practicing chiropractors
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — ChiroTouch dominates practice management, Jane App and ECLIPSE provide EHR, but clinical assessment remains hands-on with minimal digital instrumentation. X-ray analysis is still primarily visual interpretation by the DC
**Workforce:** Doctors of Chiropractic (DCs), chiropractic assistants (CAs), X-ray technicians, front desk/scheduling staff, massage therapists, billing specialists

## Key Pain Themes
Postural and spinal assessment is the core clinical skill — experienced chiropractors see compensatory patterns across the entire kinetic chain (foot pronation driving knee valgus driving pelvic tilt driving lumbar hyperlordosis driving thoracic kyphosis driving forward head carriage) that new graduates miss entirely. Treatment plans must be justified to insurance carriers with objective findings, but most clinical assessment relies on visual observation and palpation that are inherently subjective. Documentation burden is extreme — 20-30 SOAP notes per day, each requiring specific findings, treatment rendered, and response to care. Insurance reimbursement is adversarial: visit limits (12-24 visits per year), pre-authorization requirements, and frequent denials on medical necessity create a constant billing battle. The physical toll of performing 20-30 manual adjustments daily causes chiropractor burnout and career-ending shoulder/wrist injuries.

## Current Tech Landscape
ChiroTouch holds the largest EHR market share with integrated scheduling, billing, and SOAP notes. Jane App is gaining share with a more modern interface. ECLIPSE provides comprehensive practice management. PostureScreen and PostureRay offer basic posture analysis from photos but are limited to static measurement (degrees of deviation) without kinetic chain analysis or outcome correlation. No platform connects postural assessment findings to treatment protocols to patient outcomes in a way that would enable evidence-based treatment planning.
