# Plumbing Contractors

## Profile
**Category:** Construction & Trades
**Market Size:** ~$130B annually in the US plumbing contracting industry; 480,000+ plumbing establishments, majority with fewer than 10 employees
**Tech Maturity:** Low — ServiceTitan and FieldEdge handle scheduling and invoicing; sewer inspection cameras (RIDGID, Spartan Tool) produce video but review is manual; AI tooling for diagnostics and inspection is essentially absent
**Workforce:** Master plumbers (licensed), journeymen plumbers, apprentices, drain technicians, service dispatchers — most residential plumbing firms run 2-8 plumbers with 1-2 support staff

## Key Pain Themes
Plumbing is among the highest tacit-knowledge trades in the skilled trades category. Experienced plumbers have developed a diagnostic sensory palette that involves sound (listening to pipe flow and identifying turbulence, cavitation, or water hammer), touch (feeling pipe vibration signatures that indicate partial blockage or impending failure), smell (identifying sewer gas infiltration and localizing its source by concentration gradient), and sight (reading water stain patterns on ceilings and walls to map the pipe run above). This multi-sensory pattern recognition cannot be taught in a classroom — it accumulates from thousands of service calls and is the primary differentiator between an apprentice who parts the water and a master who diagnoses in minutes. The sewer camera inspection workflow is a specific, high-value example of this tacit knowledge: experienced drain technicians watch inspection camera footage and immediately identify root intrusion, pipe offset, cracking, bellied sections, and grease accumulation — assessments that junior techs miss or misclassify. The documentation and quoting overhead is a second major pain: residential service calls require scope assessment, job quoting, permit identification, and job write-up, all of which currently happen verbally or on paper and must be transcribed. The skilled plumber shortage is severe and worsening — average journeyman plumber age is rising, and apprenticeship pipelines are insufficient to replace retirements.

## Current Tech Landscape
ServiceTitan is the dominant field service management platform for plumbing contractors doing $1M+ revenue. RIDGID, Spartan Tool, and Envirosight produce sewer inspection cameras with basic video recording. Pipeline assessment software (WinCan, PACP) exists for municipal sewer inspection but is designed for infrastructure departments, not residential/commercial plumbing contractors. No AI tools exist for residential plumbing diagnosis, pipe condition assessment, or service call documentation. The gap between what technology does (schedule and invoice) and what it could do (diagnose, document, and predict) is enormous.
