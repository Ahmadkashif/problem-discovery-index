# Local News Media

## Profile
**Category:** Media & Publishing
**Market Size:** ~$20B US local news media (newspapers, digital-only local outlets, local TV station web operations); ~6,000 local news organizations
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — WordPress or Arc Publishing for CMS; Mailchimp/Sailthru for newsletter distribution; Google Analytics/Chartbeat for traffic and engagement metrics. Most newsrooms lack data science capability entirely.
**Workforce:** Reporters, editors, photographers, ad sales reps, digital producers, copy editors. Typical local newsroom has 10–40 editorial staff, down 60%+ from 2008 peaks.

## Key Pain Themes
Newsrooms operate on razor-thin margins with headcounts that have been halved or worse since the print advertising collapse. Editors must allocate scarce reporter-hours across beats with almost no quantitative signal about which stories will drive subscriptions, engagement, or community impact — relying instead on gut instinct honed over decades. Ad sales teams pitch local businesses with generic rate cards and no audience intelligence. Multi-platform publishing (web, app, newsletter, social, Apple News) requires reformatting every story manually, burning digital producer time that could go toward original reporting.

## Current Tech Landscape
CMS platforms (WordPress, Arc, Jeeves/TownNews) handle publishing but offer minimal predictive analytics. Newsletter tools like Sailthru provide open-rate data but no feedback loop into editorial planning. Google Analytics tracks pageviews but cannot connect upstream story-selection decisions to downstream subscription conversion. Most local outlets have zero ML infrastructure and no one on staff who could build or maintain a model.
