# Landscaping

**Category:** Trades & Home Services
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #computer-vision #automation #revenue-impact

## Industry Overview

The US landscaping services industry generates approximately $105 billion annually. The market includes lawn care (mowing, fertilization, weed control, aeration), landscape installation (design and planting), irrigation installation and service, tree care, and snow removal. Most firms are small — a single owner with 2-10 employees — competing on reliability, quality, and local reputation. Regional franchise networks (TruGreen, BrightView) hold a meaningful share of the commercial and lawn care segments.

Recurring service contracts (weekly mowing, seasonal fertilization programs, irrigation maintenance agreements) are the economic foundation of successful landscaping businesses — they create predictable revenue and reduce the cost of customer acquisition by retaining existing clients across multiple service seasons.

## Dominant Platforms

- **Jobber** — the leading field service management platform for small landscaping firms; scheduling, invoicing, client management
- **ServiceTitan / FieldEdge** — adopted by larger landscaping firms, particularly those managing commercial contracts
- **LMN (Landscape Management Network)** — landscaping-specific CRM and job costing platform popular with growth-focused firms
- **DPR (Desco Pro Routing)** — route optimization for multi-stop daily routing common in lawn maintenance
- **Real Green Systems / Service Autopilot** — lawn care-specific platforms with fertilization program tracking and route management

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Experienced lawn care technicians develop visual pattern recognition for turf and plant health assessment: reading grass color, density, and growth patterns to identify nutrient deficiencies (nitrogen deficiency shows as yellowing from tips, potassium deficiency shows as brown margins), disease identification (brown patch, dollar spot, pythium blight all have distinct visual signatures at different moisture and temperature conditions), weed species identification for selective herbicide application, and irrigation system fault identification from surface evidence (dry spots, wet spots, ponding patterns).

Irrigation technicians develop diagnostic expertise for controller programming issues, valve coil failures, and head spacing problems — multi-system pattern recognition that takes years to develop for complex commercial systems.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Labor turnover is extremely high (40-60% annual turnover for field crews); quality is highly variable across crews
- Route efficiency determines profitability — a well-routed 20-stop day at 8 minutes per yard requires tight geographic clustering and accurate time estimates
- Weather variability (drought conditions, excess rain) disrupts service schedules and creates unpredictable demand spikes for irrigation service and disease treatment
- Customer retention — residential customers cancel lawn care service at 20-30% annually; the cost of reacquiring a customer who canceled last season is high
