# Behavioral Health Clinics

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare & Clinical
**Market Size:** $110B annually in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Low — most clinics rely on fragmented EHR systems not designed for behavioral health workflows, with heavy manual workarounds
**Workforce:** Licensed therapists (LCSWs, LMFTs, psychologists), psychiatric nurse practitioners, intake coordinators, billing specialists, and front-desk staff

## Key Pain Themes
Intake and assessment workflows are brutally manual — new patients fill out lengthy paper or PDF forms that staff re-key into the EHR, often across multiple systems for clinical notes, billing, and outcome tracking. Treatment planning requires clinicians to reconcile information from referring providers, insurance pre-authorizations, and patient self-reports, with no single view of the patient's behavioral health history. Progress note documentation consumes 30-60 minutes per clinician per day because templates in general-purpose EHRs don't match behavioral health note structures (DAP, BIRP, SOAP-BH). Insurance credentialing and re-credentialing for each clinician across multiple payers is a months-long administrative nightmare that directly limits revenue.

## Current Tech Landscape
Dedicated behavioral health EHRs like TherapyNotes, SimplePractice, and Valant exist but serve solo practitioners better than multi-clinician clinics. Larger clinics often end up on enterprise systems like Netsmart or Qualifacts that are powerful but expensive and require significant configuration. Gaps remain in automated outcome measurement, payer-specific pre-authorization workflows, and cross-provider care coordination for patients seeing both a therapist and a prescriber.
