# Acupuncture Practices

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare / Alternative Medicine
**Market Size:** ~$4B US acupuncture market, ~38,000 licensed acupuncturists
**Tech Maturity:** Low — most practices use generic scheduling tools or pen-and-paper systems. Jane App and Unified Practice are the leading practice management options, but adoption is uneven. Clinical assessment (tongue diagnosis, pulse diagnosis) is entirely manual with no digital instrumentation
**Workforce:** Licensed Acupuncturists (L.Ac.), Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) herbalists, front desk staff, billing specialists (often outsourced), massage therapists and bodyworkers in integrated practices

## Key Pain Themes
Acupuncture clinical assessment relies on diagnostic methods (tongue observation, pulse palpation, patient inquiry) that have been transmitted through apprenticeship for millennia but resist standardization. Two experienced practitioners examining the same patient may identify different TCM pattern diagnoses (e.g., Liver Qi Stagnation vs. Spleen Qi Deficiency with Liver Overacting on Spleen) and select different point prescriptions — both may be clinically effective. Insurance billing is a growing but painful frontier: coverage has expanded (most states now mandate some acupuncture coverage), but the CPT codes (97810-97814) and documentation requirements were designed for a biomedical framework that maps poorly onto TCM diagnostic reasoning. Solo practitioners (75%+ of the profession) carry the full burden of clinical care, business management, marketing, billing, and continuing education with no support staff. Herbal dispensary management adds inventory complexity for practices that prescribe traditional formulas.

## Current Tech Landscape
Jane App provides the most popular practice management platform with scheduling, charting, and billing. Unified Practice offers TCM-specific features including herbal formula management. Jasmine and AcuGraph provide some digital diagnostic tools (AcuGraph measures electrical skin conductance at acupoints). No platform integrates TCM diagnostic assessment (tongue, pulse, pattern diagnosis) with treatment planning and outcome tracking in a way that supports both clinical decision-making and insurance documentation.
