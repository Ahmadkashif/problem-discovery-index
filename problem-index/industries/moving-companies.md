# Moving Companies

## Profile
**Category:** Transportation
**Market Size:** ~$20B US moving and relocation services
**Tech Maturity:** Low — MoverBase, SmartMoving, and MoveitPro exist but the vast majority of operators run on spreadsheets, whiteboards, and phone calls. Digital adoption is concentrated in large van lines; independent movers rarely use anything beyond basic accounting software.
**Workforce:** Estimators (in-home surveyors), CDL and non-CDL drivers, movers/laborers, dispatchers, sales and customer service representatives

## Key Pain Themes
Move estimation is the central bottleneck: an estimator walks a home, eyeballs cubic footage, mentally calculates crew size and truck space, and produces a binding or not-to-exceed quote — often within 10% accuracy through pure experience. When estimates miss, the company either eats the cost on a binding quote or triggers a customer dispute on a non-binding one. Downstream, dispatch is a daily puzzle of matching crew availability, truck capacity, drive time, and customer time windows across jobs that vary wildly in scope. Damage claims are a persistent margin drain; items get scratched, dented, or broken on every third long-distance move, and the documentation process (before photos, claim forms, carrier liability calculations) is almost entirely manual. Finally, physical injury rates among movers are among the highest in any occupation — repetitive heavy lifting, narrow staircases, and time pressure create chronic back, knee, and shoulder problems that drive turnover above 80% annually at many firms.

## Current Tech Landscape
SmartMoving and MoveitPro offer CRM, dispatch, and invoicing in one package, but their estimation modules are little more than digital inventory checklists — they don't predict labor hours or flag risk factors. Virtual survey tools (e.g., Yembo, MoveSnap) use video walkthroughs to generate cube sheets, but adoption is low because estimators don't trust the output and customers find the process awkward. Route optimization is handled ad hoc by dispatchers using Google Maps; no mainstream moving-specific tool integrates route planning with crew scheduling and truck loading constraints.
