# Specialty Food Retail

## Profile
**Category:** Retail & E-Commerce
**Market Size:** ~$175B in US specialty food retail including natural/organic grocers, gourmet shops, cheese shops, wine shops, butcher shops, bakery-cafes
**Tech Maturity:** Low — POS systems exist (Square, Clover, Revel) but inventory, ordering, and spoilage management are largely manual spreadsheet or clipboard processes
**Workforce:** Store owners, specialty counter staff (cheesemongers, butchers, bakers), cashiers, stock clerks, buyers

## Key Pain Themes
Perishable inventory management dominates operator stress: specialty stores carry 1,000–5,000 SKUs with shelf lives ranging from 2 days (fresh bread, cut fish) to 14 days (aged cheeses, cured meats), and ordering decisions are made by owners using intuition rather than data. Vendor management is unusually fragmented — a single cheese shop may source from 30–50 small artisan producers, each with different minimums, lead times, and invoicing quirks, turning purchasing into a full-time coordination job. Prepared food labeling compliance (allergen declarations, nutritional panels) is a growing regulatory burden that most stores handle with handwritten signs or generic templates. Staff knowledge is a competitive moat but also a bottleneck: counter staff are expected to discuss provenance, flavor profiles, and pairings for hundreds of products, and replacing a knowledgeable cheesemonger or wine specialist takes months.

## Current Tech Landscape
POS systems (Square, Clover, Revel, Lightspeed) handle transactions and basic inventory counts but lack perishable-aware reorder logic, expiration tracking, or vendor-specific ordering workflows. Distributors like UNFI and KeHE offer online ordering portals, but most artisan vendors still accept orders via email, text, or phone. There is no vertical SaaS platform purpose-built for small-format specialty food retail that ties together demand forecasting, multi-vendor ordering, spoilage tracking, and staff product knowledge.
