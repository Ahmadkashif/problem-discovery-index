# HOA Management

**Category:** Real Estate & Property
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #nlp #automation #llm #compliance

## Industry Overview

The US HOA (Homeowners Association) management industry generates approximately $11 billion annually. Professional HOA management companies manage the administrative, financial, and maintenance functions of residential communities — single-family subdivisions, condominium associations, and planned developments — in exchange for a per-unit monthly management fee ($15-$50/unit/month). The market is highly fragmented: thousands of local and regional management companies, plus a handful of national platforms (FirstService Residential, Associa).

A typical HOA management company manages 20-80 communities with 50-500 units each. The work involves: collections and delinquency management, CC&R (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions) violation enforcement, vendor coordination, board meeting support, financial reporting, and maintenance work order management.

## Dominant Platforms

- **Vantaca / AppFolio HOA / Buildium HOA / TOPS** — HOA-specific management platforms for delinquency tracking, work orders, and financial reporting
- **Cinc Systems** — HOA management platform with portal and communication tools
- **HOAleader / Community Associations Institute (CAI)** — industry association and compliance resource

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Experienced HOA managers develop judgment in CC&R violation enforcement (distinguishing a resident who needs one warning from one who requires formal notice and hearing), delinquency management (reading payment patterns to identify when a resident needs a payment plan vs. when to accelerate to collections), and vendor management (knowing which contractors reliably serve HOA work without excessive callbacks).

The regulatory complexity is high: HOA managers must know their state's Davis-Stirling Act (California), Condominium Act, or equivalent; the specific CC&Rs for each community they manage; and fair enforcement principles that protect against discrimination claims. Enforcement decisions that aren't documented carefully and applied consistently create legal liability for the association.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Delinquency management is the largest financial risk — high delinquency rates impair the association's ability to fund reserves and operations
- Vendor coordination volume is high — every maintenance work order, inspection, and capital project requires vendor sourcing, approval routing, and invoice processing
- Compliance documentation — CC&R enforcement must be documented per a defined escalation procedure to withstand challenge; inconsistent enforcement creates fair housing and discrimination liability
- Resident communication volume mirrors property management — dozens of inquiries daily across multiple communities
