# SMB Video Production

## Profile
**Category:** Media & Publishing
**Market Size:** ~$20B US video production services (corporate, commercial, event, social media content); ~25,000 production companies
**Tech Maturity:** Medium on creative tools (Adobe Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, Frame.io), Low on business operations — most shops run projects off spreadsheets, email threads, and informal verbal agreements
**Workforce:** Producers, directors/DPs, editors, motion graphics artists, sound designers, freelance crew

## Key Pain Themes
Projects routinely blow past budget because initial scopes are verbal or captured in vague one-page proposals, then the client adds "just one more round" of revisions indefinitely. Editors spend 40-60% of post-production time logging and reviewing raw footage to find usable takes, relying on instinct honed over years to judge performance energy, focus sharpness, and audio quality in seconds. Client feedback arrives as timestamped emails, Slack messages, or phone calls — rarely frame-accurate — forcing editors to interpret phrases like "make it punchier around the middle" into concrete cuts. Freelancer availability is tracked in the producer's head or a shared Google Sheet that's perpetually out of date, leading to double-bookings and last-minute scrambles for crew.

## Current Tech Landscape
Adobe Creative Cloud and DaVinci Resolve dominate editing/color/audio. Frame.io handles review workflows for larger shops but most SMBs use Vimeo links with comment threads or Dropbox shares. Project management lives in Asana, Monday.com, or Notion — none built for production-specific workflows like shot lists, call sheets, or talent releases. Business operations (quoting, invoicing, contracts) are almost entirely manual or run through generic tools like QuickBooks and HoneyBook.
