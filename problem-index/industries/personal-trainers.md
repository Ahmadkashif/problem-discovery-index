# Personal Trainers

## Profile
**Category:** Sports & Fitness
**Market Size:** ~$12B US personal training market; ~350,000 certified personal trainers (NASM, ACE, NSCA)
**Tech Maturity:** Low — Trainerize, TrueCoach, and My PT Hub handle basic program delivery; most trainers still rely on spreadsheets, Google Docs, or paper notebooks for programming and client tracking.
**Workforce:** Independent trainers (1099 contractors), gym-employed trainers, online coaches, boutique studio owners

## Key Pain Themes
Trainers spend 30-60 minutes per client per week on program design, progress notes, and check-in messages — time that is unbillable and competes directly with session hours. Client retention hinges on visible progress, but tracking load progression, movement quality, and recovery readiness across 15-30 active clients is a manual cognitive burden that degrades as caseload grows. Cancellation and no-show rates of 15-25% create unpredictable income, and trainers lack tools to forecast or mitigate revenue gaps. The most valuable skill a trainer develops — the ability to watch a client move and instantly identify compensatory patterns, mobility restrictions, and injury risk — lives entirely in their head and cannot be transferred, scaled, or preserved when the trainer burns out or changes careers.

## Current Tech Landscape
Trainerize and TrueCoach dominate program delivery but offer no movement analysis, no intelligent progression logic, and no predictive features. Wearable integrations (Apple Watch, Whoop, Garmin) provide heart rate and recovery scores but do not connect to programming decisions. Video review tools exist (Coach's Eye, Hudl Technique) but require manual annotation and provide no automated assessment — the gap between raw video capture and actionable movement feedback remains entirely unfilled.
