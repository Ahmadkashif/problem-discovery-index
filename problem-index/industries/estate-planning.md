# Estate Planning Law Firms

## Profile
**Category:** Legal
**Market Size:** ~$4B annually in estate planning legal fees; estate administration and trust services add another $3-5B
**Tech Maturity:** Low — document assembly tools (HotDocs, WealthDocx, Wealth Counsel) handle template drafting; practice management (Clio, Tabs3) handles billing and matter tracking; but intelligence — which clients need updates, which estates are being mismanaged, which plans are becoming tax-inefficient — is entirely absent
**Workforce:** Estate planning attorneys, paralegals, trust officers, estate administration staff — typically 1-3 attorneys at a boutique firm with 1-2 paralegals each

## Key Pain Themes
Estate planning is a practice where the attorney's most valuable work happens twice — once when the plan is drafted and once when it must be administered at death or incapacity. Between those events, the plan silently becomes outdated as life circumstances change: marriages, divorces, births, deaths of named beneficiaries, major asset acquisitions or disposals, tax law changes, state of domicile changes. Most clients do not update their estate plans after signing, and most estate planning attorneys have no systematic mechanism to identify which of their clients need updates and what those updates should be. This creates both a risk (clients die with plans that no longer reflect their wishes or minimize taxes efficiently) and a missed revenue opportunity (update engagements are high-margin, routine matters). Document assembly is a second major pain — while templates exist, each plan requires significant customization, and the intake process for gathering asset and family information is largely manual conversation and note-taking. Trust administration is the third chronic pain: administering an estate after a death involves inventorying assets across institutions, filing tax returns, communicating with beneficiaries, and managing distributions — a process that requires coordination across financial institutions that have no API interfaces and communicate by mail and fax.

## Current Tech Landscape
Wealth Counsel and Wealth Docx provide template libraries for estate planning documents — they handle the drafting automation for standard plan types (revocable living trusts, wills, POAs, health care directives). Clio and Tabs3 handle matter management and billing. Trust accounting software (Fiduciary Accounting System, Sitgo) handles trust administration accounting. No tool monitors the existing client base for plan staleness, proactively identifies update triggers, or integrates financial data for tax optimization analysis. The AI gap is entirely in the intelligence and proactive management layer.
