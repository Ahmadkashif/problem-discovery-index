# Independent Gyms

## Profile
**Category:** Sports & Fitness
**Market Size:** ~$35B US fitness industry; ~40,000 gyms and studios, ~60% independently owned (non-franchise)
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — Mindbody, Zen Planner, and Pike13 handle scheduling and billing; access control via key fobs or mobile apps; but member behavior analytics and retention tooling remain primitive or absent
**Workforce:** Owner-operators (often still coaching), front desk staff, personal trainers (frequently 1099 contractors), group fitness instructors, and part-time maintenance staff

## Key Pain Themes
Member churn is the defining financial problem: 30-50% of members cancel within a year, and most owners discover attrition only when a credit card declines or a member simply stops showing up. Class scheduling is a constant balancing act between instructor availability, room capacity, and shifting member demand — a Wednesday 6pm yoga class can swing from 30 attendees to 8 in a single month with no obvious cause. Trial-to-member conversion hovers around 20-30%, with front desk staff expected to close sales despite having no training in consultative selling. Equipment maintenance is reactive rather than preventive, leading to out-of-service cardio machines during peak hours and frustrated members who leave without complaining.

## Current Tech Landscape
Mindbody dominates scheduling and POS but offers weak retention analytics — its reporting is backward-looking and requires manual CSV exports for anything beyond canned dashboards. Zen Planner and Pike13 serve smaller operators with tighter budgets but similarly lack predictive capabilities. Access control systems (key fobs, QR codes, facial recognition kiosks) generate rich check-in data that almost no gym actually analyzes beyond headcounts.
