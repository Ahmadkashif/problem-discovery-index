# Funeral Homes

## Profile
**Category:** Specialty Services
**Market Size:** ~$20B US funeral services industry
**Tech Maturity:** Low — most funeral homes operate on legacy management systems (SRS, Passare), paper-heavy preneed files, and word-of-mouth marketing. FrontRunner Professional provides websites and basic CRM but adoption is uneven across the 19,000+ US funeral homes
**Workforce:** Licensed funeral directors, embalmers, crematory operators, preneed counselors, administrative staff, livery/transport drivers, and facility maintenance personnel

## Key Pain Themes
Families arrive in acute grief and must make $8,000-$15,000 in purchasing decisions within 24-48 hours — arrangement conferences are emotionally charged and time-pressured, with directors balancing empathy against business reality. Preneed (pre-planning) sales are the industry's most profitable channel but require sustained outreach to a population that actively avoids thinking about death. FTC Funeral Rule compliance demands itemized price lists presented in specific formats, and state-level regulations on embalming disclosure, casket sourcing, and cremation authorization add layers of documentation. Obituary writing, death certificate filing, and coordination with cemeteries, clergy, florists, and caterers create a 50-100 task coordination burden per case that directors manage from memory and handwritten checklists.

## Current Tech Landscape
SRS Computing (now part of SCI) and Passare dominate case management. FrontRunner Professional handles websites, aftercare, and basic CRM. Batesville and Matthews International control casket/urn supply chains with proprietary ordering systems. Cremation management uses separate tracking (e.g., CANA-compliant systems). Preneed funding is handled through third-party insurance products (Homesteaders, NGL). No platform integrates the full workflow from first call through aftercare, and AI adoption is virtually nonexistent.
