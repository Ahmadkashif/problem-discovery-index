# Physical Therapy

## Profile
**Category:** Healthcare & Clinical
**Market Size:** $58B annually in the US
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — EMR systems exist for PT but outcome tracking, home exercise compliance, and authorization management remain manual
**Workforce:** Physical therapists (PTs), physical therapist assistants (PTAs), front-desk coordinators, billing specialists, and clinic directors

## Key Pain Themes
Prior authorization and concurrent review management is a constant battle — most payers require authorization every 10-12 visits, and tracking where each patient stands relative to their authorization limit while simultaneously requesting extensions is a manual, error-prone process that directly causes revenue leakage when visits are delivered without valid authorization. Outcome measurement is theoretically required by most payers and accrediting bodies, but PTs find it burdensome to administer standardized tests (Oswestry, DASH, LEFS) at the right intervals and correlate scores with treatment progression. Home exercise program (HEP) adherence is a black hole — PTs prescribe exercises, hand over a printed sheet or app link, and have no visibility into whether patients actually do them between visits. Documentation requires capturing detailed functional measurements, manual muscle testing grades, and treatment interventions in formats that satisfy both clinical best practice and payer-specific documentation requirements for medical necessity.

## Current Tech Landscape
WebPT, Clinicient (now part of Prompt), TheraOffice, and Net Health dominate the PT-specific EMR space. Most include scheduling, documentation, billing, and basic outcome tracking. Home exercise platforms (MedBridge, PT Wired, PhysiTrack) exist as separate tools with limited EMR integration. Authorization tracking is typically a spreadsheet or a basic field in the EMR that doesn't proactively alert staff when limits approach.
