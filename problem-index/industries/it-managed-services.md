# IT Managed Services

## Profile
**Category:** Professional Services
**Market Size:** ~$300B globally, ~$100B in North America; approximately 40,000 MSPs in the US alone, ranging from 2-person shops to 500+ employee regional players
**Tech Maturity:** High — ConnectWise, Datto/Kaseya, and NinjaRMM dominate the RMM/PSA stack; most MSPs run deeply integrated toolchains but still rely on manual processes for triage, reporting, and billing reconciliation
**Workforce:** L1/L2/L3 technicians, NOC engineers, vCIOs, service managers, account managers, with L1 techs handling the highest volume of repetitive tickets

## Key Pain Themes
The core operational bottleneck is ticket volume: 60-70% of inbound tickets are L1 issues — password resets, printer problems, basic connectivity failures — that consume technician hours without requiring real skill. MSPs struggle to accurately reconcile billing across PSA and RMM platforms, leading to revenue leakage on per-device and per-user contracts. vCIOs lack automated, MSP-specific client health scoring for quarterly business reviews, forcing manual spreadsheet work. Alert fatigue in NOCs degrades response quality: technicians see hundreds of monitoring alerts daily, most of which are noise, burying the genuine incidents that require action.

## Current Tech Landscape
ConnectWise Manage/Automate and Datto/Kaseya are the dominant PSA/RMM platforms, with NinjaRMM and Syncro gaining share among smaller MSPs. AI-assisted ticketing is emerging (e.g., Rewst for automation, Gradient MSP for billing reconciliation) but adoption is early and most MSPs still route tickets manually or with basic keyword rules. Reporting and QBR preparation remain largely manual, cobbled together from PSA exports and spreadsheets.
