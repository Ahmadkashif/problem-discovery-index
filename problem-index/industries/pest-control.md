# Pest Control

## Profile
**Category:** Specialty Services
**Market Size:** ~$22B US pest control market
**Tech Maturity:** Medium — PestPac, FieldRoutes (ServiceTitan), and ServSuite provide operations management, but inspection, diagnosis, and treatment planning remain entirely experience-dependent
**Workforce:** Pest control technicians, inspectors/sales inspectors, branch managers, operations managers, customer service representatives, and certified applicators

## Key Pain Themes
Infestation diagnosis is the core skill — an experienced technician reads the signs (droppings shape and distribution, damage patterns, entry points, harborage indicators, species-specific behavior) and knows both what they're dealing with and how to treat it. A new technician sees droppings and sprays a baseboard. The difference determines whether the problem is resolved in one visit or five. Regulatory compliance is heavy — EPA registration of pesticides, state agricultural department licensing, application record-keeping, restricted-use product handling, and notification requirements vary by state and municipality. Route optimization matters because recurring service visits (monthly/quarterly) span large territories with variable stop durations. The sales inspector role creates a conflict of interest: the person who diagnoses the problem also sells the treatment, incentivizing upselling that erodes customer trust.

## Current Tech Landscape
PestPac (WorkWave) is the dominant operations platform, handling scheduling, routing, billing, and chemical tracking. FieldRoutes (now ServiceTitan) is growing quickly with a more modern interface. ServSuite provides similar capabilities. These platforms manage the business but don't assist with the core technical work — inspection, diagnosis, and treatment planning are entirely in the technician's head. No platform offers visual pest identification, infestation severity assessment, or treatment protocol recommendation.
