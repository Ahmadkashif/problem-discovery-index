# Cold Chain Logistics

## Industry Overview

The US cold chain logistics industry generates approximately $100 billion annually. Cold chain operators move temperature-sensitive cargo — fresh produce, pharmaceuticals, frozen food, chemicals, and biologics — through a connected system of refrigerated trucks, cold storage warehouses, and air freight that must maintain product within strict temperature bands throughout transit. The FDA's FSMA rule and the pharmaceutical industry's GDP (Good Distribution Practice) standards impose documented temperature compliance requirements on every shipment.

The market includes dedicated cold chain carriers (Lineage Logistics, Americold, US Foods Logistics), third-party temperature-controlled warehouses (3PLs with cold storage), and asset-based refrigerated trucking fleets. Temperature excursion events — cargo going out of range — cost the industry an estimated $35 billion annually in product loss, recall costs, and regulatory penalties.

## Dominant Platforms

- **Sensitech / Emerson GoTrack** — Temperature data loggers and monitoring hardware standard in pharma cold chain; Sensitech TempTale and ColdTrak are the monitoring devices in FDA-regulated shipments
- **Controlant / Monnit** — IoT-based temperature monitoring platforms with real-time cellular/cloud alerts and dashboard reporting
- **TMW Systems / McLeod (TMS)** — Temperature-controlled carrier TMS platforms for load management and customer reporting
- **Lineage Link / Americold Dimension** — Proprietary warehouse management systems at the two largest cold storage REITs
- **VeriShip / project44** — Shipment visibility platforms with temperature-controlled carrier integrations

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Cold chain logistics requires a combination of standard logistics dispatch expertise and specialized knowledge of temperature management. Experienced cold chain dispatchers know which refrigerated units run cold (over-cooling product), which lanes have predictable pre-cool challenges at origin, and which receiver docks create extended door-open exposure. Drivers monitor reefer unit performance and recognize early indicators of refrigeration unit failure — unusual fuel consumption, abnormal cycling patterns, and airflow irregularities — before they manifest as temperature excursions.

Warehouse cold storage operators manage complex zoning: blast freeze cells, frozen storage, chill rooms, and banana rooms each with different temperature requirements and product velocity patterns. Experienced operators know which product positions in a rack system receive insufficient airflow in a given rack configuration.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Temperature excursion claims — product loss, recall costs, and FDA enforcement create existential financial exposure
- Fuel costs — refrigerated transport burns 20-30% more fuel than dry van; reefer unit diesel consumption is a major cost center
- Regulatory documentation burden — FDA FSMA Rule 204 and pharma GDP require chain-of-custody temperature documentation for every shipment
- Driver shortage — refrigerated driving requires additional endorsements and operational knowledge; driver retention is more expensive than dry van
- Equipment maintenance — reefer unit maintenance costs are 2-3× dry van trailer maintenance; unplanned breakdowns create both load loss and customer penalty exposure
