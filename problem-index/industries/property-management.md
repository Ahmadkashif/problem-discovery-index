# Property Management

**Category:** Real Estate & Property
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #nlp #automation #revenue-impact #llm

## Industry Overview

The US property management industry generates approximately $88 billion annually. Property managers operate on behalf of property owners — collecting rent, managing maintenance, screening tenants, and handling compliance — in exchange for a percentage of collected rent (typically 8-12% for residential, 4-8% for commercial). The market spans residential rentals (single-family, multifamily), commercial properties (office, retail, industrial), and mixed-use portfolios.

The industry is highly fragmented at the small end (thousands of independent property managers handling 20-200 units) with national platforms (Greystar, CBRE Property Management, Cushman & Wakefield) managing large institutional portfolios.

## Dominant Platforms

- **AppFolio / Buildium / Propertyware** — leading property management platforms for small-to-mid-sized residential portfolios; tenant portal, maintenance management, accounting
- **Yardi / MRI Software** — enterprise platforms for large portfolios and commercial properties
- **Cozy / RentSpree** — tenant screening and application platforms
- **Thumbtack / Angi** — vendor sourcing platforms used by property managers who need on-demand maintenance contractors

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Property managers develop experience-based judgment in tenant screening (reading between the lines on rental applications — identifying red flags in income documentation, rental history gaps, and reference patterns), maintenance triage (deciding which maintenance requests can wait vs. require emergency dispatch), and vendor management (knowing which contractors deliver reliable quality at reasonable cost in the local market).

The most economically significant tacit knowledge is tenant quality assessment — an experienced property manager can identify a high-risk tenant application that would result in non-payment or property damage from patterns in the application that formal screening scores miss. This judgment is built from seeing the outcomes of hundreds of placement decisions.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Vacancy cost is the primary revenue driver; reducing days-to-fill on vacant units is worth $50-$150 per day per unit
- Maintenance cost management — a property manager who efficiently triages and dispatches maintenance controls a major expense category on behalf of owners
- Tenant turnover — a non-renewing tenant costs $1,500-$4,000 in turnover costs (cleaning, repairs, re-leasing); retention is high-value
- Regulatory compliance — fair housing, rent control, habitability requirements, security deposit law — all vary by state and municipality
