# Home Inspection

**Category:** Trades & Home Services
**Tags:** #tacit-knowledge-ml #computer-vision #nlp #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

## Industry Overview

The US home inspection industry generates approximately $5 billion annually. Home inspectors examine residential properties at the point of sale, providing buyers with a written assessment of the property's condition. The industry is populated by approximately 25,000 licensed inspectors, primarily sole proprietors or small two-to-five-person firms. Inspection fees range from $300 to $700 for a residential inspection, with additional fees for specialized inspections (radon, sewer, mold, thermal imaging).

Home inspections are primarily demand-driven by real estate transactions — inspection volume closely tracks housing market activity. In competitive markets, inspectors who produce clear, well-organized reports quickly, and who can articulate defect severity intelligently to anxious buyers, build referral networks through real estate agents and command premium pricing.

## Dominant Platforms

- **Spectora** — the leading modern home inspection report writing software; mobile-first, photo-integrated, fast report delivery
- **Home Inspector Pro / HomeGauge / Horizon** — legacy report writing platforms with large installed bases
- **ISN (Inspection Support Network)** — scheduling and business management platform for inspection firms
- **InterNACHI / ASHI** — the two primary certification bodies whose standards define inspection scope and report requirements

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Experienced home inspectors develop multi-system, multi-sensory diagnostic knowledge that spans the entire built environment: electrical (identifying double-tapped breakers, improper wire gauge for breaker size, aluminum wiring indicators, panel installation quality); structural (reading crack patterns in foundations and walls to distinguish settling from active movement, identifying roof truss cut modifications, reading deflection in floor systems); roofing (identifying shingle condition, flashing failures, ridge and soffit ventilation adequacy); plumbing (identifying cross-connections, active vs. inactive leaks from staining patterns, pressure issues from observation of fixtures).

This cross-domain visual pattern recognition — developed over hundreds of inspections and often thousands of hours of continuing education — allows experienced inspectors to identify subtle defects that newer inspectors miss. The quality gap between a 2-year inspector and a 15-year inspector is primarily accumulated pattern recognition across dozens of defect categories.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Referral from real estate agents is the dominant marketing channel; agent relationships are maintained through report quality and reliability
- Report turnaround time: buyers and agents expect reports within 24 hours of inspection; inspectors who deliver in 4-6 hours stand out
- Liability exposure: inspectors who miss significant defects face E&O claims; insurance premiums are a significant operating cost
- Competition from discount inspection services (lower price, less experience) creates price pressure on independent inspectors
- Ancillary services (radon, sewer camera, thermal imaging, mold sampling) provide revenue diversification
