# Engineering Consultants

## Profile
**Category:** Professional Services
**Market Size:** ~$330B in US engineering services; ~60,000 firms, 80% have <20 employees
**Tech Maturity:** High on design tools (AutoCAD, Revit, Civil 3D), Low on project management and business operations
**Workforce:** PEs (Professional Engineers), EITs (Engineers-in-Training), CAD technicians, project managers, admin staff

## Key Pain Themes
Engineering firms price work on fixed-fee proposals but deliver it on hourly labor, creating a structural margin erosion problem that compounds across dozens of active projects. Scope creep is endemic — clients add "small" requests mid-project that never get formalized as change orders, and PMs lack real-time visibility into budget burn until the damage is done. On the business development side, firms spend 40-80 hours per RFP response, rewriting qualifications and project descriptions from scratch because no structured repository of past project data exists. Compliance checking against jurisdiction-specific building and civil codes remains almost entirely manual, with engineers cross-referencing PDFs of local amendments against national standards.

## Current Tech Landscape
Design tools are mature — most firms run Autodesk suites (AutoCAD, Revit, Civil 3D) or Bentley products, with BIM adoption growing in structural and MEP disciplines. Project management and accounting rely on Deltek Vantagepoint, Ajera, or BST Global, but these systems are poorly adopted at small firms and rarely configured to provide real-time budget alerts. Proposal management is largely Word/InDesign plus shared drives; timesheet entry is manual and often weeks behind.
