# Last-Mile Delivery

**Category:** Logistics & Transportation
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #time-series-forecasting #computer-vision #automation #revenue-impact

## Industry Overview

The US last-mile delivery market generates approximately $100 billion annually, serving e-commerce, grocery delivery, restaurant delivery, and local courier needs. The market includes large national carriers (FedEx, UPS, USPS), regional carriers, and thousands of local independent delivery services and DSPs (Delivery Service Partners) operating under Amazon, FedEx, or UPS contracting arrangements.

Last-mile delivery is economically critical — it represents 50-60% of total supply chain cost but is also the customer experience touchpoint that determines brand loyalty. Failed or late deliveries generate customer service costs, re-delivery expenses, and return processing costs that quickly erode the unit economics of e-commerce.

## Dominant Platforms

- **Route4Me / OptimoRoute / Circuit** — route optimization platforms for small and mid-sized delivery operations
- **Amazon Relay / FedEx Compatible** — DSP management platforms for contracted delivery operations
- **Onfleet / Tookan** — delivery management platforms with driver dispatch and tracking
- **Samsara / Verizon Connect** — fleet telematics platforms used for vehicle tracking and driver performance

## Workforce and Knowledge Structure

Experienced delivery drivers develop local route knowledge — which addresses have access issues, which apartment complexes require buzzer codes not in the system, which customers prefer packages left at the back door vs. front. This local knowledge, accumulated over months of delivering the same routes, enables experienced drivers to deliver 15-20% more stops per hour than new drivers on unfamiliar territory.

Delivery operations managers develop dispatch intuition: understanding which driver-route pairings work, which customers generate excessive delivery attempts, and how to load and sequence stops for efficient delivery from the vehicle's cargo organization.

## Key Economic Pressures

- Failed delivery attempts — 5-10% of residential deliveries fail on first attempt, requiring re-delivery at full per-stop cost with no additional revenue
- Driver retention — turnover in last-mile delivery is 50-70% annually; lost local route knowledge with each departing driver is a significant operational cost
- Fuel and vehicle cost — route efficiency and driver behavior directly determine fuel spend
- Customer experience — late or damaged deliveries generate NPS damage, refund requests, and brand liability
