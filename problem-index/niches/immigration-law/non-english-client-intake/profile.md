# Non-English Client Intake

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/immigration-law|Immigration Law]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-0.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — client intake in immigration law is conducted in person or by phone with the attorney or a bilingual staff member acting as interpreter; intake forms are in English; document translation is coordinated through email and phone with certified translators; no platform handles multilingual intake-to-form-field mapping
**Target Buyer:** Immigration attorney serving Spanish, Chinese, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, Hindi, or other non-English-speaking communities
**Automation Potential:** Very High — multilingual intake, document translation tracking, and interpreter coordination are structured, repetitive workflows where language barriers create systematic bottlenecks

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Immigration law has the highest proportion of non-English-speaking clients of any legal practice area. An estimated 70-80% of immigration clients have limited English proficiency. Every interaction — intake, document gathering, form review, interview preparation — requires either a bilingual attorney/staff member or an interpreter. Client intake is the critical bottleneck: the client's personal information (full legal name, date of birth, address history, employment history, family relationships, immigration history, criminal history) must be collected accurately and mapped to English-language USCIS form fields. A client who speaks only Spanish provides information in Spanish, but the I-130, I-485, I-765, and every other USCIS form is in English. A bilingual paralegal must translate the client's responses into English and enter them into the correct form fields — a manual process that takes 1-3 hours per intake and introduces translation errors (name transliterations, date format confusion, address formatting). Document translation compounds the problem: birth certificates, marriage certificates, police records, and military records from the client's home country must be certified-translated into English. Tracking which documents have been translated, which translations have been received, and which are still outstanding is managed through spreadsheets and email.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Docketwise provides client intake forms and USCIS form population, but all intake is in English. The client must bring a bilingual family member or the firm must provide a bilingual staff member to interpret during intake. No immigration platform offers multilingual intake where the client answers questions in their language and the system maps responses to English form fields. For document translation, firms use a network of certified translators contacted by email — there is no platform that manages the translation workflow (send document to translator, track status, receive translation, match to case file). Google Translate and similar tools cannot be used for official document translation (USCIS requires certified human translation with a certificate of accuracy).
