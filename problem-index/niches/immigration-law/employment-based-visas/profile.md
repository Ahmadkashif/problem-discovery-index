## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Employment-based immigration serves corporate clients (not individuals) — an HR department at a tech company, hospital system, or consulting firm sponsors a foreign worker for an H-1B, L-1, O-1, or permanent residence through PERM labor certification. The workflow is fundamentally different from family immigration: the attorney manages a relationship with corporate HR (who may sponsor 10-500 employees per year), must comply with Department of Labor regulations on prevailing wages and recruitment, and faces PERM audits where the DOL requests documentation proving that no qualified U.S. worker was available for the position. PERM labor certification alone requires a structured recruitment campaign (job postings in specific media, resume collection, documented evaluation of each applicant, written explanation for why each U.S. applicant was rejected) — a process that takes 6-12 months and generates 50-200 pages of documentation per case. Experienced business immigration attorneys develop pattern recognition for which PERM applications will be audited — certain job titles, wage levels below the prevailing wage mean, and recruitment patterns trigger audits at higher rates — but this knowledge is tacit and learned through years of filing.

## Current Tools & Gaps
INSZoom dominates corporate immigration case management: case tracking, form population, corporate client portals, H-1B cap tracking. Docketwise serves smaller business immigration practices. The DOL's FLAG system handles electronic PERM filing. But no platform analyzes historical PERM audit patterns to predict audit risk, automates the assembly of PERM recruitment documentation (which must be compiled in exact chronological order with specific formatting), or drafts RFE responses using precedent analysis. Prevailing wage determinations require comparing DOL's Online Wage Library data with the specific job duties, which attorneys do manually for each case.
