## Why These Niches

Immigration law is not a single practice — it fragments along case type (family vs. employment vs. removal), client population (corporate HR departments vs. non-English-speaking families vs. unaccompanied minors), stakes level (visa convenience vs. deportation), and operational bottleneck (substantive legal work vs. administrative form assembly). These 8 niches cover the full span: the two largest revenue segments (family-based petitions at ~40% of the market and employment-based visas at ~28%), the two most digitally neglected (removal defense with no immigration court integration and DACA/TPS where policy changes create emergency mass-filing events), the two most underserved populations (unaccompanied minors requiring dual-court coordination and non-English-speaking clients locked out of English-only intake systems), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (case status tracking across 4+ government portals and form preparation where a single data entry error adds 2-4 months of delay). Excluded: investor visas (EB-5 is a niche financial product more than a legal practice), consular processing (State Department workflow, not USCIS), and immigration policy advocacy (not a legal services market).
