# Intelligent Form Assembly with Cross-Validation

**Niche:** [[niches/immigration-law/form-preparation-filing/profile|Form Preparation & Filing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/immigration-law|Immigration Law]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Enter client data once and generate a complete, cross-validated filing package — all required forms, cover letter, evidence index, fee calculation, and a pre-filing consistency check that catches the discrepancies USCIS would reject for — replacing 4-8 hours of manual assembly per case.

## The Problem
A family-based adjustment of status case requires filing 6 forms simultaneously: I-130 (petition), I-485 (adjustment application), I-765 (work authorization), I-131 (travel document), I-864 (affidavit of support), and I-693 (medical exam). Each form is 10-20 pages. The applicant's name appears on all 6 forms and must be identical — "Maria de los Angeles Garcia Lopez" must be rendered the same way on every form (not "Maria Garcia Lopez" on one and "Maria D. Garcia" on another). Date formats must match. Address history must be consistent. Immigration history must be identical. A-number (if previously assigned) must appear on every form. Prior applications referenced on the I-485 must match what's stated on the I-130. Currently, a paralegal populates each form (via Docketwise or manually), prints all forms, and then manually reviews every form side by side to check for consistency — a 1-2 hour review process that catches most but not all discrepancies. The forms are then assembled with a cover letter (listing each form and its page count), an evidence index (table of contents for supporting documents organized by exhibit letter/number), filing fees (calculated based on applicant age, category, and fee waiver eligibility), and assembled in USCIS's expected order. This assembly process takes 2-4 additional hours per case.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Docketwise solves the data-entry-once problem but treats form population as the finish line. Cross-validation requires understanding which fields on different forms must match and what constitutes a "match" (is "Maria de los Angeles Garcia Lopez" the same as "Maria Garcia-Lopez"?). The matching rules are not documented by USCIS — attorneys learn through rejection notices which inconsistencies USCIS catches. Form editions change irregularly (USCIS updates forms without a predictable schedule), meaning the system must continuously track which form version is current. Fee calculations are category-dependent and change periodically. Cover letter and evidence index generation requires understanding the case type and which documents are required — a logic layer that Docketwise doesn't provide. Building this end-to-end requires deep immigration law domain knowledge combined with document assembly engineering.

## What to Build
An end-to-end filing package assembly system with five components: (1) smart intake with case-type logic — based on the case type (family adjustment, employment-based, naturalization, etc.), automatically determine which forms are required and which are optional, (2) cross-form validation engine — after form population, automatically compare every shared field across all forms and flag discrepancies (name mismatches, date inconsistencies, address differences, A-number conflicts) with specific correction suggestions, (3) cover letter generator — produce a filing cover letter listing all enclosed forms, documents, and fees in USCIS's expected format, customized to the filing type and USCIS office, (4) evidence index generator — create a table of contents for the supporting document package with exhibit labels, document descriptions, and page references, (5) filing readiness dashboard — a pre-filing checklist confirming: all required forms present and completed, all required signatures obtained, correct form editions used, correct fees calculated (including fee waiver if applicable), required photos attached, and all required supporting documents present. An LLM generates natural-language descriptions for evidence index entries and cover letter text.

## Target Customer
Immigration firms processing 10+ filing packages per month, where 4-8 hours of paralegal time per package is spent on assembly, review, and consistency checking, and where USCIS rejections for technical deficiencies occur on 5-10% of filings.

## Impact If Built
Reduces filing package assembly from 4-8 hours to 1-2 hours (attorney review only). Eliminates technical deficiency rejections — saving 2-4 months per rejected case. For a firm filing 20 packages per month, this recovers 60-120 hours of monthly paralegal time ($8K-15K/month) and prevents 1-2 rejections per month that each cost the client months of delay and the firm hours of re-filing work.
