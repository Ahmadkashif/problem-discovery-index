# DACA/TPS & Humanitarian Relief

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/immigration-law|Immigration Law]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-0.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — DACA renewal and TPS re-registration are filed on paper forms or through USCIS's limited online filing system; community organizations serving this population use spreadsheets and shared drives; no platform handles mass filing events or policy change alerts
**Target Buyer:** Community organization attorney, nonprofit immigration services director, legal aid program coordinator
**Automation Potential:** Very High — DACA renewals are near-identical across applicants (same form, same evidence, updated address/employment), TPS re-registration is a mass event with identical filing requirements, and U-visa/T-visa/VAWA cases have structured evidence requirements that can be systematized

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
DACA/TPS and humanitarian relief (U-visa, T-visa, VAWA self-petitions) serve the most vulnerable immigrant populations through legal mechanisms that are politically volatile, time-sensitive, and operationally bursty. DACA renewals must be filed every two years for ~600,000 recipients — when a renewal window opens or a court injunction changes, organizations must process hundreds of renewals within weeks. TPS re-registration follows a similar pattern: the government designates or extends TPS for a country, and all beneficiaries from that country must re-register within a narrow window (often 60-90 days). These mass filing events overwhelm the capacity of legal aid organizations that normally handle cases one at a time. U-visas (for crime victims), T-visas (for trafficking victims), and VAWA self-petitions (for domestic violence victims) involve trauma survivors who require sensitive intake, extensive evidence documentation (police reports, medical records, personal declarations), and coordination with law enforcement for certifications. The client base has very limited English proficiency, may fear government interaction, and often lacks stable contact information.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard immigration case management (Docketwise, INSZoom) is designed for one-case-at-a-time processing, not mass filing events. When USCIS announces a DACA renewal period, an organization with 200 clients needs to: verify each client's current information, identify what has changed since the last renewal, populate forms with updated data, collect updated evidence (new employment records, address changes), and file 200 near-identical applications within a compressed window. No platform supports this batch workflow. For humanitarian cases (U-visa, T-visa, VAWA), no system structures the evidence collection process around the specific requirements of each form (I-918 for U-visa, I-914 for T-visa, I-360 for VAWA) or tracks the law enforcement certification process that U-visas and T-visas require.
