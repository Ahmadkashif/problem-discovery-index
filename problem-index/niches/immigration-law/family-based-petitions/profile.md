## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Family-based immigration is the highest-volume segment of immigration law — I-130 petitions for spouses, parents, children, and siblings account for roughly 40% of all USCIS filings. The workflow is high volume but individually lower complexity than employment-based or removal cases: collect biographical data, assemble evidence of the qualifying relationship (marriage certificates, birth certificates, photos, affidavits), file the I-130 petition, wait for priority date to become current (which can take 1-25 years depending on category and country of birth), then file the I-485 adjustment of status or route through consular processing. The client base is largely non-English-speaking, calls weekly asking "when will my case be approved?", and needs hand-holding through a process they don't understand. The attorney's challenge is managing 100-200 active cases at different stages of a multi-year pipeline while keeping clients informed and filing adjustment paperwork at exactly the right moment when priority dates become current. Missing a visa bulletin movement means a client waits an additional month or more.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Docketwise populates USCIS forms from intake data and provides basic case management. INSZoom serves larger firms. Neither platform monitors the monthly visa bulletin, correlates bulletin movements with specific client priority dates, or automatically alerts attorneys when a client's category becomes current. Attorneys manually check the visa bulletin each month, compare it against their case list, and determine which clients can now file adjustment applications. For client communication, most firms rely on phone calls and ad-hoc text messages — there is no self-service portal where a family member can check their petition status, see estimated wait times, or upload requested documents without calling the office.
