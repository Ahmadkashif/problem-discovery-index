## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Retiree advisory is operationally different from accumulation-phase wealth management. The advisor's job shifts from "grow the portfolio" to "sequence withdrawals across taxable, tax-deferred, and tax-free accounts to minimize lifetime tax liability while maintaining income stability." This requires coordinating RMDs (required minimum distributions), Roth conversion windows, Social Security timing, Medicare IRMAA surcharge thresholds, and estate planning liquidity — a multi-variable optimization problem that most advisors solve with intuition and spreadsheets. Client communication frequency doubles in distribution phase because retirees monitor spending against income more anxiously than accumulators monitor returns.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Income Lab and Timeline are purpose-built for distribution planning but are standalone tools that don't integrate with portfolio management systems (Orion, Black Diamond) or CRM workflows. Most advisors still run Roth conversion analyses in Excel because planning tools lack the tax-bracket-aware multi-year optimization needed. Social Security optimization tools (Maximize My Social Security, Social Security Solutions) exist but are disconnected from the broader financial plan.
