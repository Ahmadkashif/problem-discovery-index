# All-in-One Solo RIA Operating System

**Niche:** [[niches/wealth-management-rias/solo-advisor-rias/profile|Solo Advisor RIAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform today integrates CRM, financial planning, portfolio management, compliance tracking, and client communication into a single interface priced for a solo practitioner.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #ai-platform

## The Problem
Solo advisors juggle 5-8 disconnected tools to run their practice: a CRM, a planning tool, a custodian portal, a compliance tracker, a document management system, an email platform, and a reporting tool. Each requires separate login, separate data entry, and manual synchronization. A single client meeting requires pulling data from 3-4 systems into a prep document, then updating 2-3 systems afterward. The cognitive overhead of context-switching between tools consumes 8-12 hours per week — time that directly reduces the advisor's capacity to serve clients.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Enterprise RIA platforms (Orion, Black Diamond, Advyzon) are designed for multi-advisor firms with operations staff and $20,000+/year budgets. Building a simplified version that covers CRM, planning, portfolio tracking, and compliance at a $200-400/month price point requires ruthless feature scoping — covering 80% of the workflow with 20% of the complexity. Startups have attempted this (Snappy Kraken, Pulse360) but focused on individual functions rather than the integrated operating system.

## What to Build
A single-platform practice management system for solo advisors that combines: (1) a lightweight CRM with client household profiles and meeting notes; (2) a simplified financial planning module for retirement projections and cash flow analysis; (3) custodian account aggregation with position-level data from Schwab/Fidelity/Pershing APIs; (4) SEC/state compliance tracking with ADV amendment reminders and annual filing checklists; and (5) client-facing portal for document sharing and performance summaries. Priced at $200-400/month, not $1,500-4,000/month.

## Target Customer
Solo RIA advisors managing $20-100M in AUM who currently spend $3,000-8,000/year on fragmented tools and 8-12 hours per week on tool-switching overhead.

## Impact If Built
Solo advisors reclaim 8-12 hours per week from tool-switching, reduce software costs by 40-60%, and gain capacity to serve 20-30 additional households without hiring staff — equivalent to $100,000-$200,000 in additional annual revenue capacity.
