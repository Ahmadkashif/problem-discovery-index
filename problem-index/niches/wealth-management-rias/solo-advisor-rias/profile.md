# Solo Advisor RIAs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $400-600B AUM
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10-12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many solo advisors use free or entry-level tools (Wealthbox free tier, basic Excel spreadsheets for planning) and lack the budget or time to implement enterprise-grade platforms
**Target Buyer:** Solo advisor managing 80-200 households with no dedicated operations staff
**Automation Potential:** High — the solo advisor performs every function (planning, portfolio management, compliance, client communication, operations) and is the most constrained on time

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Solo RIA advisors are their own planner, portfolio manager, compliance officer, operations staff, and marketing department. They cannot afford the $20,000-$50,000/year tech stacks that multi-advisor firms use (Orion + Redtail + MoneyGuidePro + Tamarac), so they cobble together free tiers, consumer-grade tools, and manual processes. Their operational bottleneck is not knowledge — most are experienced advisors who left wirehouses or larger RIAs — but time. Every hour spent on compliance paperwork, custodian portal navigation, or report formatting is an hour not spent advising clients or growing the practice.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many solo advisors use Wealthbox or a spreadsheet for CRM, Schwab or Fidelity's custodian portals for trading and money movement, and basic Excel models for financial planning. Some use Riskalyze for risk assessment and RightCapital or eMoney's lower tiers for planning. The gap is an integrated all-in-one platform designed for the solo practitioner's workflow — current tools assume a division of labor between advisor and operations staff that does not exist for solos.
