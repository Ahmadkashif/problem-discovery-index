# Simplified Compliance Filing for Single-Advisor Firms

**Niche:** [[niches/wealth-management-rias/solo-advisor-rias/profile|Solo Advisor RIAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Compliance tools like RIA in a Box and SmartRIA exist but are built for multi-advisor firms with dedicated CCOs — solo advisors need a stripped-down version that guides them through filings step by step.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #quick-win #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Solo advisors are their own Chief Compliance Officer. They must file annual ADV amendments, maintain books and records per SEC Rule 204-2, conduct annual compliance reviews, and prepare for state or SEC examinations — all without compliance staff. Current compliance platforms present enterprise-grade dashboards with hundreds of tasks, many irrelevant to a single-advisor firm, creating overwhelm and confusion about what actually needs to be done and when.

## What Already Exists
RIA in a Box, SmartRIA, and NRS (National Regulatory Services) provide compliance management platforms with policy templates, ADV filing assistance, and exam preparation checklists. These tools are comprehensive and built for firms with 5-50+ employees and a designated CCO.

## The Customization Gap
Solo advisors need a simplified compliance workflow that: (1) strips out tasks irrelevant to single-advisor firms (employee supervision policies, branch office oversight, trading desk procedures); (2) provides calendar-driven reminders with plain-language explanations of what each filing requires; (3) auto-populates ADV amendments from the advisor's CRM and custodian data rather than requiring manual form-filling; and (4) generates the annual compliance review as a guided questionnaire rather than a blank-page exercise.

## Target Customer
Solo RIA advisors who currently spend $2,000-5,000/year on compliance consulting because they don't trust themselves to handle SEC/state requirements alone.

## Impact If Solved
Solo advisors complete compliance tasks in 50% less time, reduce reliance on external compliance consultants (saving $2,000-4,000/year), and enter SEC/state examinations with confidence that their documentation is complete and current.
