# Military & Federal Employee Advisory

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $200-350B AUM
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — advisors specializing in military/federal benefits use mainstream RIA tools but lack planning modules for TSP optimization, FERS/CSRS pension modeling, VA disability benefit integration, and Survivor Benefit Plan analysis
**Target Buyer:** Advisor specializing in military retirement, federal employee benefits, or veteran transition financial planning
**Automation Potential:** Medium — benefit structures are rule-based and calculable, but the rules are complex and change with legislation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Military and federal employee financial planning requires specialized domain knowledge that most advisors lack: TSP (Thrift Savings Plan) allocation strategies across L, C, S, I, and F funds; FERS pension calculations with COLA projections and early retirement supplements; military retirement system comparisons (legacy High-3 vs. BRS Blended Retirement System); VA disability benefit tax treatment and its interaction with retirement income planning; Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) vs. commercial life insurance trade-offs; and TRICARE health coverage transitions at age 65 to Medicare. The advisor must understand both the financial planning and the federal benefits regulatory framework — a dual competency that takes years to develop.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Mainstream planning tools (MoneyGuidePro, eMoney) model Social Security and 401(k)s well but have limited support for TSP-specific features (G Fund characteristics, Roth TSP vs. traditional TSP optimization, TSP loan rules), FERS pension calculations with interim COLA projections, or military retirement pay with concurrent receipt rules. Advisors use federal benefits calculators (GRB Platform, My Federal Retirement) alongside their planning tools but cannot integrate the outputs.
