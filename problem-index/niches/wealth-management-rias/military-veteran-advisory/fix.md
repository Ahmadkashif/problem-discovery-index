# Military-to-Civilian Financial Transition Gap

**Niche:** [[niches/wealth-management-rias/military-veteran-advisory/profile|Military & Federal Employee Advisory]]
**Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Service members transitioning to civilian careers face a 6-12 month financial planning void where military benefits are ending, civilian benefits haven't started, and no advisor can model the transition comprehensively.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A service member leaving active duty after 10-20 years must simultaneously navigate: TSP rollover decisions (leave in TSP, roll to IRA, or convert to Roth), VA disability claim timing (which determines tax-free income for life), TRICARE to civilian health insurance transitions (with COBRA-like Transitional Healthcare options), GI Bill benefit transfers or usage decisions, SBP enrollment windows (irrevocable 1-year deadline), and the complete restructuring of their financial plan from military pay tables to civilian compensation. These decisions interact — the VA disability rating affects whether TRICARE remains available, which affects healthcare cost projections, which affects retirement income needs — but no tool models them together, and most advisors lack the military-specific knowledge to guide the transition.

## Why It's Still Broken
The Department of Defense's Transition Assistance Program (TAP) provides a 5-day financial planning workshop that covers basics but cannot provide individualized analysis. Military-savvy financial advisors exist but are hard to find — the Certified Financial Planner Board does not have a military specialization designation. The transition itself compresses dozens of irreversible financial decisions into a 3-6 month window around the separation date, creating time pressure that prevents methodical planning.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A military transition financial planning toolkit that: (1) generates a personalized timeline of decision deadlines based on the service member's separation date, years of service, and benefit eligibility; (2) models each major decision (TSP rollover, SBP enrollment, VA disability claim timing) with scenario analysis showing long-term financial impact; (3) connects the transitioning member with vetted military-specialized advisors in their area; and (4) integrates with the DoD's myPay and DFAS systems to pull actual military pay and benefit data into the planning scenarios.

## Who Feels the Pain
Service members in their last 12 months of active duty who face irreversible financial decisions worth $100,000-$1M in lifetime impact but lack access to advisors who understand military benefit structures.

## Impact If Fixed
Transitioning service members avoid the estimated $50,000-$200,000 in lifetime financial losses from suboptimal TSP rollover decisions, missed SBP enrollment windows, and delayed VA disability claims — decisions that are irreversible and disproportionately affect the 200,000+ service members who separate annually.
