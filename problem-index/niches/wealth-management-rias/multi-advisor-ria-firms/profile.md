# Multi-Advisor Ensemble RIA Firms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5-1.8T AUM
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~30-35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — firms use Orion/Black Diamond for portfolio management, Redtail/Wealthbox for CRM, and Tamarac for rebalancing, but cross-advisor consistency and client handoff remain manual
**Target Buyer:** COO or managing partner at a 5-20 advisor firm
**Automation Potential:** Medium — complexity lies in standardizing advisory processes across multiple advisors with different investment philosophies and client communication styles

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multi-advisor RIAs face a management challenge that solo practices and large wirehouses do not: maintaining consistent client experience across 5-20 advisors who each have their own investment preferences, meeting cadences, and communication styles. When a client is reassigned due to advisor departure, the receiving advisor inherits a book of relationships with no systematic knowledge transfer — investment rationale, client behavioral patterns, and relationship context live in the departing advisor's head. The firm's enterprise value depends on AUM retention during advisor transitions, making knowledge management an existential business risk.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Practice management platforms (Orion Planning, Advyzon) provide firm-level dashboards and performance attribution, but they do not capture the qualitative advisory context — why a specific allocation was chosen for a client, what conversation themes resonate, or which clients need proactive outreach during volatility. CRM notes exist but are inconsistently entered and rarely structured for cross-advisor knowledge transfer.
