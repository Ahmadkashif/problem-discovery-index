# Advisor Departure AUM Retention Crisis

**Niche:** [[niches/wealth-management-rias/multi-advisor-ria-firms/profile|Multi-Advisor Ensemble RIA Firms]]
**Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When an advisor leaves, the firm has a 90-day window to save 70-85% of that advisor's book — but no structured process exists for the critical first-week client outreach.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Industry data shows that when an advisor departs an RIA, 20-40% of their AUM follows them within 6 months. The firm scrambles to reassign clients, but the receiving advisors have no context on the departing advisor's relationships and must cold-call clients who may have already been contacted by the departing advisor. There is no system to prioritize which clients to call first (largest AUM? longest relationship? highest flight risk?), no playbook for the reassignment conversation, and no way to identify which clients the departing advisor has already solicited.

## Why It's Still Broken
Advisor departures are emotionally charged events that firms handle reactively. Non-solicitation agreements are difficult to enforce, and the departing advisor has a relationship advantage. Firms lack a data-driven approach to triage: which clients are most at risk (based on relationship depth, engagement recency, asset concentration with the departing advisor) and what messaging will resonate with each. CRM data is too shallow to support this level of client-specific outreach planning.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A departure response protocol that: (1) automatically scores each client in the departing advisor's book by flight risk using CRM engagement data, AUM, tenure, and relationship concentration metrics; (2) generates a prioritized contact list with suggested talking points per client based on their communication history and investment profile; (3) assigns clients to receiving advisors based on profile similarity and capacity; and (4) tracks outreach completion and client retention outcomes to improve the process over time.

## Who Feels the Pain
Managing partners and COOs at multi-advisor RIAs who face potential AUM losses of $50-200M per advisor departure event, with each lost client representing $5,000-$20,000 in annual revenue.

## Impact If Fixed
Improving post-departure AUM retention from 60-80% to 85-95% through structured, data-driven outreach could save a typical multi-advisor RIA $500K-$2M in annual revenue per departure event.
