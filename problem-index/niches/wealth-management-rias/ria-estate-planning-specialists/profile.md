# RIA Estate Planning Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $300-500B AUM
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8-10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — advisors use generic financial planning tools for estate projections but model complex trust structures, generation-skipping transfers, and charitable remainder trusts in spreadsheets or on paper
**Target Buyer:** Lead advisor specializing in estate and intergenerational wealth transfer for $5M+ households
**Automation Potential:** Medium — estate planning involves creative legal-financial structuring that resists full automation, but scenario modeling and document coordination are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Estate-focused RIAs operate at the intersection of financial planning, tax law, and trust/estate law — a domain complexity that generic wealth management tools do not address. The advisor must model scenarios involving irrevocable life insurance trusts (ILITs), grantor retained annuity trusts (GRATs), qualified personal residence trusts (QPRTs), charitable lead trusts, dynasty trusts, and generation-skipping transfer tax strategies — each with different tax treatment, funding requirements, and legal constraints. The planning horizon spans decades and involves coordinating with estate attorneys, CPAs, and insurance specialists who each use different tools and speak different professional languages.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MoneyGuidePro and eMoney include basic estate planning modules (beneficiary modeling, estate tax projections), but they cannot model the tax implications of specific trust structures or compare GRAT vs. ILIT vs. direct gift strategies side by side. WealthCounsel and Interactive Legal produce estate planning documents but have no integration with financial planning tools. The gap is an analytical platform that models advanced estate planning strategies with financial-plan-quality projections.
