# Multilingual Client Portal for RIAs

**Niche:** [[niches/wealth-management-rias/minority-owned-rias/profile|Minority-Owned & Diversity-Focused RIAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Client portals like Orion Portal and eMoney's client site exist but only support English — advisors serving Spanish, Mandarin, Korean, or Vietnamese-speaking clients have no way to provide a native-language digital experience.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #quick-win

## The Problem
Advisors serving non-English-speaking clients conduct meetings in the client's language but then hand them a portal, performance report, and financial plan written entirely in English. The client cannot independently review their own plan, understand report commentary, or navigate the portal to upload documents. This creates a dependency on the advisor for every interaction and prevents clients from becoming financially literate in the context of their own wealth — undermining the advisory relationship's educational purpose.

## What Already Exists
Orion Portal, eMoney's client-facing site, and RightCapital's client experience provide performance summaries, financial plan access, and document vaults. These portals are polished and functional but English-only. Google Translate and browser-based translation produce awkward financial terminology ("your assets under management" becomes garbled in many languages).

## The Customization Gap
A localization layer for RIA client portals that goes beyond simple translation: (1) professional-grade financial terminology in Spanish, Mandarin, Korean, Vietnamese, and Hindi — the top 5 non-English languages among US immigrant wealth management clients; (2) culturally appropriate framing of concepts like risk tolerance, estate planning, and insurance (which carry different connotations across cultures); (3) bilingual report generation so the advisor can review in English while the client reads in their language; and (4) support for right-to-left scripts (Arabic, Urdu) for firms serving Middle Eastern and South Asian communities.

## Target Customer
Diversity-focused RIA advisors whose client base is 40%+ non-native English speakers and who currently lose engagement because clients cannot interact independently with digital planning tools.

## Impact If Solved
Clients engage with their financial plans 2-3x more frequently when materials are in their native language, reducing advisor time spent on basic explanations by 30-40% and improving plan adherence — particularly for action items like increasing 401(k) contributions or updating beneficiary designations.
