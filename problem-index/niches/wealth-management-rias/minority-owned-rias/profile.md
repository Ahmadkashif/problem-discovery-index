# Minority-Owned & Diversity-Focused RIAs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/wealth-management-rias|Wealth Management RIAs]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $150-250B AUM
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3-5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — firms use mainstream RIA tools but lack culturally adapted client-facing materials, financial planning templates for community-specific wealth patterns, and marketing tools that resonate with their target populations
**Target Buyer:** Founder of an RIA serving Black, Hispanic, Asian-American, or immigrant communities
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the advisory work itself is similar, but client acquisition, financial literacy education, and community-specific planning scenarios are unaddressed by current tools

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Minority-owned RIAs serve client populations with distinct financial patterns: higher reliance on extended family financial support networks, different homeownership and real estate wealth structures, remittance obligations, immigration-related financial planning needs (tax treaty complexities, FBAR/FATCA reporting), cultural attitudes toward debt and risk that differ from the assumptions baked into mainstream financial planning tools, and intergenerational wealth transfer patterns that don't fit the standard "married couple passes to 2.3 children" model. These advisors also face unique business challenges: smaller average AUM per client (often $100K-$500K vs. $1M+ industry average), higher client education burden, and difficulty accessing the referral networks (CPAs, estate attorneys) that drive growth for mainstream RIAs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
These firms use the same tools as mainstream RIAs — Wealthbox, RightCapital, Schwab's custodian platform — but find that client-facing materials, planning assumptions, and onboarding workflows assume a client profile that doesn't match their market. Financial planning tools default to Social Security optimization, 401(k) rollover analysis, and traditional estate planning — missing scenarios like optimizing for remittance costs, navigating dual-country tax obligations, or planning for cultural obligations (family financial support, community-based savings circles like tandas or susus).
