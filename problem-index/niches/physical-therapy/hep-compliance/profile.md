# Home Exercise Program (HEP) Delivery & Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B (embedded across all PT practices)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Touches all PT practices — cross-cutting clinical function
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — HEP platforms like MedBridge and PhysiTrack deliver exercise videos, but the compliance feedback loop back to the clinician remains broken
**Target Buyer:** Clinic owner-PT and treating therapists seeking better patient outcomes and documentation evidence
**Automation Potential:** Very High — exercise recommendation, compliance tracking, difficulty adaptation, and clinical documentation integration are all automatable with current technology

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
PTs prescribe home exercise programs to every patient — exercises to perform between visits that are critical to recovery. Adherence determines outcomes: a patient who does their exercises recovers faster, uses fewer visits, and has better long-term results. But compliance is invisible. Patients receive a printed handout or a link to a video library and the PT has no idea whether they exercised until the next visit. Non-adherence is the single largest factor in poor outcomes and extended treatment duration — which burns through insurance authorization faster, creating a financial penalty for the clinic on top of the clinical one. This niche focuses on the technology gap between exercise prescription and compliance verification.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MedBridge is the dominant HEP platform — it delivers video-guided exercise programs to patients via a mobile app with a library of 5,000+ exercises. PhysiTrack and PT Wired offer similar capabilities. These tools solve the content delivery problem (patient can see how to do the exercise) but don't solve the compliance problem (did they actually do it?). Patient self-report check-ins exist but are unreliable. No platform feeds compliance data back into the PT's clinical documentation — so even when compliance data is captured, it doesn't inform the clinical record, treatment plan adjustments, or medical necessity narratives for authorization extensions.
