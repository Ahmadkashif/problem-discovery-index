# Adaptive HEP with Real-Time Compliance Feedback Loop

**Niche:** [[niches/physical-therapy/hep-compliance/profile|Home Exercise Program (HEP) Delivery & Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** PTs see whether patients actually did their exercises before the next visit — and the program adapts in real time based on patient-reported pain, difficulty, and adherence patterns, closing the blind spot that drives poor outcomes.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #tacit-knowledge-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
The PT prescribes 5-8 exercises with specific sets, reps, and frequency. The patient goes home. Until the next visit (3-7 days later), the PT has zero visibility into whether the patient exercised, whether they did the exercises correctly, whether the exercises caused pain, or whether the difficulty level was appropriate. When the patient returns and reports "I did my exercises" or "I forgot a few days," the PT adjusts the treatment plan based on unreliable self-report. If the patient didn't exercise and isn't improving, the PT may incorrectly conclude the treatment approach isn't working and change the plan — when the real issue was non-compliance. Conversely, if exercises are causing pain the PT doesn't know about, the patient suffers unnecessarily between visits. Experienced PTs develop intuition for reading patient compliance from subtle cues — how the patient moves, whether their strength has changed, how they describe their week — but junior PTs lack this tacit knowledge and take patient self-reports at face value.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
MedBridge and PhysiTrack deliver exercise content effectively but stop at delivery — they're content platforms, not compliance systems. Building a true compliance feedback loop requires: (1) reliable compliance capture (patient self-report is unreliable, wearable integration is technically complex, and camera-based motion tracking raises privacy concerns), (2) clinical interpretation logic (knowing that 60% compliance on a 5-exercise program is acceptable for post-op week 1 but insufficient by week 4), (3) adaptive difficulty adjustment algorithms (reducing reps when pain increases, progressing exercises when difficulty ratings drop — decisions that currently require PT judgment), and (4) EMR integration to feed compliance data into the clinical record. Each component is buildable, but combining them into a system that PTs trust enough to act on (and patients actually use consistently) requires solving UX, clinical logic, and integration challenges simultaneously.

## What to Build
A patient-facing exercise platform with a clinician feedback loop: (1) Patient experience: video-guided exercises with completion check-in (tap to confirm each exercise done), pain and difficulty ratings after each exercise (1-5 scale), and optional notes. Gamification elements (streaks, progress badges) for engagement. Push notification reminders at the patient's preferred exercise time. (2) Adaptive logic: when a patient reports pain above threshold on an exercise, automatically suggest a regression from the PT's pre-defined exercise progression ladder. When difficulty drops below threshold for 3 consecutive sessions, suggest a progression. Flag these adaptations for PT review rather than auto-applying. (3) PT dashboard: before each visit, show a compliance summary — which exercises were completed, which were skipped, pain trends, difficulty trends, and any adaptive flags. Highlight patients who haven't exercised in 3+ days for proactive outreach. (4) Clinical integration: push compliance data into the EMR as structured documentation — "Patient completed 80% of prescribed HEP over 7 days, reported increased pain on exercise 3 (hip abduction with band), difficulty decreased on exercises 1 and 2" — supporting treatment plan adjustments and medical necessity documentation.

## Target Customer
PT clinic owners and treating therapists who want to close the HEP compliance blind spot — moving from "hope the patient exercised" to "know the patient exercised and how it went."

## Impact If Built
Improves patient outcomes by enabling real-time treatment plan adjustment based on actual compliance data rather than unreliable self-report. Reduces average visits per episode by 15-25% through better compliance-driven recovery. Provides clinical documentation evidence (compliance data) that strengthens medical necessity narratives for authorization extensions — if the patient is compliant and still not improving, that's a stronger case for continued treatment than "patient reports doing exercises."
