# Cash-Pay / Direct-Access PT

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — practices cobble together Calendly, Square, and Google Sheets because no purpose-built platform exists for insurance-free PT practices
**Target Buyer:** Cash-pay PT practice owner (often solo or 2-3 PTs)
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — subscription management, patient progress visualization, and direct-to-consumer marketing attribution are all automatable but require a purpose-built platform

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
A rapidly growing segment driven by therapist frustration with insurance authorization burden and patients willing to pay out-of-pocket for faster access and longer sessions. Direct access laws (now legal in most US states) allow patients to see PTs without physician referral, removing the referral dependency that defines traditional PT. The business model is fundamentally different: subscription packages or per-session pricing ($120-$200/session) replace insurance billing, direct-to-consumer marketing replaces physician referral cultivation, and longer sessions (45-60 minutes vs. 20-30 minutes in insurance-based practice) create a premium service experience. No prior authorization, no utilization review, no claim denials — but also no insurance infrastructure to fall back on.

## Current Tools & Gaps
WebPT, Clinicient/Prompt, and TheraOffice are built around insurance billing workflows — authorization tracking, claims submission, ERA processing — that cash-pay practices don't need. Using these tools forces cash-pay PTs to pay for and navigate around irrelevant features. Simple scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) handle appointments but lack clinical documentation, outcome tracking, or billing. Payment processors (Square, Stripe) handle transactions but don't manage subscription packages or generate superbills. Cash-pay PTs typically use 4-5 disconnected tools to run their practice, with manual data transfer between them.
