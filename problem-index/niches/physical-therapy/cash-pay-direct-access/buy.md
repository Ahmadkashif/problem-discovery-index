# Lightweight EMR Without Insurance Billing

**Niche:** [[niches/physical-therapy/cash-pay-direct-access/profile|Cash-Pay / Direct-Access PT]]
**Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Cash-pay PTs get clinical documentation and outcome tracking without the insurance billing bloat — a streamlined EMR that replaces claims management with cash-pay billing, packages, and memberships.
**Tags:** #feature-engineering #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Cash-pay PT practices need clinical documentation — they're still licensed healthcare providers who must maintain medical records. But every PT EMR on the market is architected around insurance billing: authorization tracking modules, claims submission engines, ERA processing, denial management workflows. A cash-pay practice pays for and navigates around all of this dead functionality. WebPT charges $3-5 per completed visit for claims processing that cash-pay practices never use. The insurance billing module isn't just unused — it actively clutters the interface, adds complexity to onboarding, and creates confusion when features reference payers, authorizations, and billing codes that don't apply to cash-pay operations.

## What Already Exists
WebPT, Clinicient/Prompt, and TheraOffice are comprehensive PT EMRs built for insurance-billed practices. Jane App (Canadian origin, expanding to US) is lighter weight but still built around insurance billing. SimplePractice targets behavioral health cash-pay practices but lacks PT-specific clinical features (outcome measures, exercise prescription, treatment plan templates for orthopedic/sports conditions). Practice Better and Healthie target functional medicine and nutrition — adjacent but not PT-specific.

## The Customization Gap
The adaptation needed is a PT EMR that replaces the insurance billing layer with a cash-pay business layer while maintaining clinical depth. Clinical side: full PT documentation (subjective, objective, assessment, plan), integrated outcome measures (Oswestry, DASH, LEFS, NPRS), exercise prescription with video, treatment plan templates by condition, and discharge summaries. Business side: replace claims/authorization modules with session package management, recurring membership billing, single-session checkout, superbill generation (with correct CPT and ICD-10 codes for patient self-submission), and payment history tracking. The key insight is that cash-pay PTs need the same clinical documentation quality — they just need a completely different billing and administrative infrastructure surrounding it.

## Target Customer
Cash-pay PT practice owners who are currently either overpaying for insurance-focused EMRs they use at 50% capacity or using non-PT documentation tools that lack clinical specificity.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces EMR costs by 30-50% by eliminating payment for unused insurance billing features. Simplifies the practice technology stack from 4-5 tools to 1-2. Maintains clinical documentation quality while removing administrative friction, allowing the PT to focus on the premium patient experience that justifies cash-pay pricing.
