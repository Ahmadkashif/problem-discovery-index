## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%

**Target Buyer:** Rehab department director within a hospital or health system
**Automation Potential:** High — 8-minute rule compliance, internal referral tracking, and PT-specific documentation within generic EMR workflows are all automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Physical therapy delivered within hospital or health system facilities operates under fundamentally different constraints than private practice PT. Billing follows hospital outpatient rates under CMS OPPS payment rules — higher reimbursement but subject to the 8-minute rule for timed CPT codes, which creates complex unit-counting logic across multiple service codes in a single session. Compliance burdens include JCAHO accreditation, hospital-specific documentation policies, and health system IT governance that mandates Epic or Cerner. Therapists are hospital employees, not practice owners — they have less autonomy but more institutional support. Internal referrals from system surgeons create a captive patient stream, but also create internal politics when patients leak to external PT providers.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Epic Rehab and Cerner Rehab modules handle documentation and scheduling but are widely regarded as inferior to dedicated PT EMRs (WebPT, Prompt) for therapy-specific workflows. PT-specific outcome measures, functional limitation tracking, and treatment plan templates are bolted onto a generic medical record rather than purpose-built. Rehab department directors cannot easily extract PT-specific analytics from the hospital's enterprise reporting tools — report requests go through IT queues with weeks-long turnaround. The 8-minute rule compliance burden falls entirely on the therapist during documentation, with no real-time guidance or error checking.
