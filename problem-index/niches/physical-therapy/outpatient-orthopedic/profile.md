# Outpatient Orthopedic PT Clinics

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-28B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~45%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most clinics use WebPT or Clinicient/Prompt for documentation and billing, but practice analytics and referral management remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Clinic owner-PT or clinic director managing single or multi-location private practices
**Automation Potential:** High — referral tracking, patient dropout detection, and outcome-to-referral-source correlation are all data problems with clear inputs and actionable outputs

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The dominant segment of physical therapy. Post-surgical rehab (total knee, ACL reconstruction, rotator cuff repair), sports injuries, chronic pain management, and work injury rehabilitation. Single or multi-location private practices billing standard commercial insurance and Medicare. Entirely referral-dependent — orthopedic surgeons are the patient pipeline, and the clinic's survival hinges on maintaining those referral relationships. Competition comes from hospital-based PT departments (which capture internal referrals) and corporate chains (ATI, Athletico, USPH) that offer volume discounts to surgeons. Independent clinics differentiate on clinical outcomes and patient experience, but lack data to prove either to referral sources.

## Current Tools & Gaps
WebPT is the market leader for documentation and billing, followed by Clinicient/Prompt and TheraOffice. These handle daily notes, scheduling, and claims submission adequately. The gap is operational intelligence: no tool correlates referral source to patient outcomes, tracks referral volume trends by surgeon, measures patient dropout rates, or provides clinician productivity benchmarking. Practice owners rely on gut feel and manual spreadsheet tracking for business-critical decisions about referral relationship management.
