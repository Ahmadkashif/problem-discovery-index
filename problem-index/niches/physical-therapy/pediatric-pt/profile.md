# Pediatric PT Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — pediatric PTs use adult-oriented EMRs that lack developmental outcome measures, IEP/IFSP documentation support, or age-normed milestone tracking
**Target Buyer:** Pediatric PT practice owner or early intervention agency director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — developmental milestone tracking, multi-state billing compliance, and IEP progress reporting are all automatable but require pediatric-specific clinical logic

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Treating children with developmental delays, cerebral palsy, Down syndrome, autism-related motor challenges, torticollis, toe-walking, and congenital conditions. Every aspect of practice differs from adult PT: outcome measures are developmental (PDMS-2, Bayley, GMFM — not Oswestry or DASH), payers include early intervention state programs (Part C of IDEA), school-based services (Part B of IDEA), Medicaid EPSDT, and commercial pediatric riders — each with unique authorization and documentation rules. Treatment sessions are play-based, not exercise-protocol-based. Parents are co-therapists who must be trained on home carryover activities. Progress is measured against age-normed developmental milestones, not against a patient's pre-injury baseline. Documentation must serve IEP/IFSP teams (special education context) in addition to clinical and billing purposes.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard PT EMRs (WebPT, Clinicient) are designed for adult orthopedic rehabilitation. They lack: pediatric outcome measures (PDMS-2, GMFM, Alberta Infant Motor Scale) with age-normed scoring, developmental milestone tracking against normative curves, IEP/IFSP progress report templates, play-based treatment documentation frameworks, and parent education tracking. Pediatric-specific tools exist for speech therapy and ABA (Central Reach) but not for pediatric PT. Early intervention billing systems vary by state, and no EMR handles multi-state Part C program differences.
