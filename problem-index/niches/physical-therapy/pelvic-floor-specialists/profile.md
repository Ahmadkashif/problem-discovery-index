# Pelvic Floor PT Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — specialists use general PT EMRs that lack pelvic-floor-specific assessment templates, biofeedback integration, or sensitive intake workflows
**Target Buyer:** Pelvic floor PT specialist (often solo or small group practice)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — patient intake sensitivity, biofeedback data integration, and provider-patient matching based on symptom profiles are all automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Treating pelvic floor disorders — urinary and fecal incontinence, pelvic organ prolapse, post-partum recovery, chronic pelvic pain, vaginismus, dyspareunia, and male post-prostatectomy incontinence. The patient population is extremely sensitive: many patients are embarrassed, have never discussed these symptoms with anyone, and may have experienced trauma. Clinical assessment includes internal examination and EMG biofeedback — modalities that require specialized training and documentation. Insurance coverage is inconsistent: many payers don't recognize pelvic floor PT as a distinct specialty or require specific diagnosis codes that patients may not have. Marketing is fundamentally different — patients don't know pelvic floor PT exists, and the condition carries stigma that suppresses demand even when patients are aware.

## Current Tools & Gaps
General PT EMRs (WebPT, Clinicient) handle documentation but lack pelvic-floor-specific assessment templates (pelvic floor muscle grading scales, bladder diary integration, sexual function questionnaires). Biofeedback devices (Pericoach, Elvie, Pathway by Prometheus) capture pelvic floor EMG data but don't integrate with PT clinical documentation — the data lives on a separate device/app with no connection to the treatment plan. Patient intake forms are generic PT forms that don't ask about pelvic symptoms, forcing awkward verbal screening conversations. No directory effectively connects patients with pelvic floor symptoms to qualified pelvic floor PTs.
