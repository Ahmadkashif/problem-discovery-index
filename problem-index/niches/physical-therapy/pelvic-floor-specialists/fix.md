# Pelvic Floor PT Discovery and Referral Matching

**Niche:** [[niches/physical-therapy/pelvic-floor-specialists/profile|Pelvic Floor PT Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/physical-therapy|Physical Therapy]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Patients with pelvic floor symptoms find qualified specialists through symptom-based matching — and OB/GYNs who should refer to pelvic floor PT learn that a qualified provider exists 10 minutes from their office.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Two broken discovery loops keep pelvic floor PT underutilized. First, patients can't find providers: general PT directories (Psychology Today-style) don't sub-specialize in pelvic floor, Google searches return confusing results mixing pelvic floor PT with urogynecology and general PT, and patients searching symptom-based terms ("leaking urine when exercising") don't find PT as a solution because pelvic floor PTs don't rank for symptom-based keywords. Second, referring physicians (OB/GYNs, urologists, colorectal surgeons) who should refer patients to pelvic floor PT often don't know a qualified provider exists nearby — or they give patients a name they found years ago without knowing whether that PT is still practicing, accepting new patients, or in the patient's insurance network. The result: millions of patients with treatable pelvic floor conditions never receive PT.

## Why It's Still Broken
General healthcare directories (Zocdoc, Healthgrades) categorize by "Physical Therapy" without subspecialty distinction. The APTA Section on Pelvic Health maintains a provider directory, but it's a basic search with no symptom matching, no insurance filtering, no availability information, and no physician-facing interface. Building a specialized directory is economically challenging — the pelvic floor PT market is small, providers are geographically dispersed, and the patients who need the directory often don't know they need pelvic floor PT specifically. Unlike orthopedic PT (where patients have a surgeon referral in hand), pelvic floor patients often need to self-identify as candidates for PT — which requires education that happens at the point of discovery, not just a directory listing.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A dual-interface platform: (1) Patient-facing: symptom-based search ("I leak urine when I run," "sex is painful since childbirth," "I can't empty my bladder completely") that maps symptoms to pelvic floor PT as a treatment option, then matches to qualified local providers filtered by insurance, availability, and specific condition expertise. Includes patient education content that normalizes seeking treatment and explains what to expect at a pelvic floor PT visit. (2) Provider-facing: referral facilitation tools for OB/GYNs and urologists — a searchable directory of pelvic floor PTs with verified credentials, subspecialty focus (postpartum vs. chronic pain vs. male pelvic floor), insurance panels, and current availability. One-click referral that sends the patient's information to the PT practice with an intake link. Provider profiles include outcome data and patient reviews (anonymized given the sensitivity).

## Who Feels the Pain
Pelvic floor PTs who spend significant marketing effort educating a market that doesn't know their specialty exists — many report that 50%+ of new patients say "I didn't know this existed." OB/GYNs who know they should refer for pelvic floor dysfunction but don't have a reliable, updated provider resource. Patients suffering in silence from treatable conditions because the path to care is invisible.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases new patient volume for pelvic floor PT practices by 20-40% by making the specialty discoverable at the point of patient need. Shortens the average time from symptom onset to treatment initiation (currently 6-8 years for many pelvic floor conditions). Establishes a referral infrastructure that creates the same surgeon-to-PT pipeline that orthopedic PT enjoys, but between OB/GYNs, urologists, and pelvic floor PTs.
