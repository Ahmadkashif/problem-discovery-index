# Residential Inspection Tools Adapted for Phase Inspections

**Niche:** [[niches/home-inspection/new-construction-inspections/profile|New Construction Inspections]]
**Industry:** [[industries/home-inspection|Home Inspection]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Spectora and HomeGauge are built for single-visit existing-home inspections — new construction requires multi-phase tracking where defects found in Phase 1 must carry forward to Phase 2 verification, and the final report aggregates findings across all phases into a comprehensive construction quality record.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
When an inspector performs a 3-phase new construction inspection, Spectora treats each phase as a separate inspection — separate appointment, separate report, separate invoice. There is no continuity between phases. If Phase 1 (pre-pour) identifies that rebar spacing doesn't meet spec, the inspector must manually remember or search their old report to verify the correction at Phase 2 (framing). The buyer receives three separate reports with no aggregated view of construction quality. And the builder's punch list — items identified at each phase that require correction — is tracked informally through text messages and verbal agreements, with no structured verification at the next phase.

## What Already Exists
Spectora handles scheduling, photo capture, checklist-based reporting, and client delivery. Its single-inspection workflow is polished and efficient. HomeGauge offers similar functionality. Both support custom checklists that could be adapted for construction phases. Neither provides multi-inspection continuity — linking Phase 1, Phase 2, and Phase 3 of the same project into a single record.

## The Customization Gap
A multi-phase project module that: (1) creates a project container linking 2-4 inspection phases for the same property address, with a single buyer/client record; (2) carries forward open items — defects identified in Phase 1 appear as verification items in Phase 2, with status tracking (corrected/not corrected/partially corrected); (3) provides construction-phase-specific checklists (pre-pour, framing, pre-drywall, final) rather than the existing-home system categories (roof, electrical, plumbing, structure); (4) generates a comprehensive construction quality report aggregating all phases — the buyer receives one document showing the complete inspection history, all findings, correction status, and overall construction quality assessment; and (5) tracks builder punch list items with photo evidence of the defect and photo evidence of the correction at the next phase.

## Target Customer
Inspectors currently offering new construction inspections using Spectora or HomeGauge but managing phase continuity manually. Spectora seeking to capture the growing new construction inspection segment.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates the manual tracking of open items between phases, catching 20-30% of defects that are currently "lost" between Phase 1 and Phase 2 because the inspector forgot to re-check. Produces a comprehensive construction quality record that justifies the premium pricing of phase inspections ($600-1,500 per project vs. $400-500 for a single visit). Reduces the per-phase report writing time by 30-40% through pre-populated continuity from prior phases.
