# Residential Inspection Software Adapted for Commercial ASTM Reports

**Niche:** [[niches/home-inspection/commercial-inspections/profile|Commercial Inspections]]
**Industry:** [[industries/home-inspection|Home Inspection]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Spectora and HomeGauge dominate residential inspection software but their commercial modules are afterthoughts — commercial inspectors need ASTM E2018 report structure, Replacement Reserve tables, multi-system section templates, and institutional-grade deliverables that residential platforms don't support.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #automation #large-language-models

## The Problem
Commercial inspectors who use Spectora for their residential work must switch to Microsoft Word for commercial reports because Spectora's report format doesn't match ASTM E2018 requirements. The ASTM standard prescribes specific sections (Purpose & Scope, System Descriptions, System Observations, Document Review, Opinions of Probable Cost), specific language conventions (opinions of probable cost, not estimates), and specific deliverables (Replacement Reserve table with annual projections). Spectora's checklist-and-photo format works well for residential but produces reports that look unprofessional for commercial clients — institutional buyers and lenders expect formatted narratives with engineering-grade language, not checkbox summaries.

## What Already Exists
Spectora handles photo management, cloud-based report delivery, and scheduling — all useful for commercial work. HomeGauge has a basic commercial module. Both produce web-based interactive reports. But commercial clients expect PDF deliverables in ASTM format, and neither platform structures data according to ASTM E2018 sections or generates Replacement Reserve tables.

## The Customization Gap
A commercial inspection module for Spectora (or HomeGauge) that: (1) restructures the data capture interface around ASTM E2018 sections — each building system gets a structured form with standard fields (manufacturer, model, age, capacity, condition, observed deficiencies, remaining useful life, recommended action); (2) generates ASTM-formatted narratives from structured inputs — the inspector enters structured data on-site and the system produces paragraph-form descriptions meeting ASTM language conventions; (3) includes a Replacement Reserve calculator integrated with the inspection data — the inspector's condition assessments automatically feed into the reserve table; and (4) outputs a professional PDF in ASTM E2018 format suitable for inclusion in commercial transaction due diligence packages. The NLP component translates structured field entries into professional narratives ("The HVAC system consists of three Trane Voyager 20-ton rooftop units, manufactured in 2015. The units were observed to be in fair condition with visible corrosion on the condenser coils and evidence of refrigerant leakage at the service valves. Estimated remaining useful life: 5-7 years.").

## Target Customer
Inspectors who perform both residential and commercial inspections and want one software platform for both. Commercial inspection firms currently using Word templates who want the efficiency of a structured software platform.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces commercial report writing time by 40-50% by generating ASTM-format narratives from structured inputs. Enables Spectora/HomeGauge to capture the commercial inspection market ($0.8-1B) that they currently lose to Word templates. Saves 8-12 hours per commercial report for inspectors currently switching between residential software and Word.
