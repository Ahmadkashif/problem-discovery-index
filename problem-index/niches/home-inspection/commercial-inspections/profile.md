# Commercial Inspections

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-inspection|Home Inspection]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.8-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — commercial inspection firms use generic project management tools and custom Word/Excel templates. Spectora and HomeGauge are residential-focused and don't handle multi-system commercial reports. Most commercial inspectors produce custom reports in Word.
**Target Buyer:** Commercial property inspector or inspection firm owner specializing in commercial property condition assessments (PCAs)
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — commercial inspections follow ASTM E2018 standard structure, making report generation automatable, and building system condition scoring is a structured classification task

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial property inspections assess office buildings, retail centers, industrial facilities, and multi-family apartments for buyers, lenders, or portfolio owners. The scope is broader and more complex than residential: a commercial PCA (Property Condition Assessment) covers structural systems, building envelope, mechanical (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, fire protection, elevators, conveyance), site improvements (parking, drainage, landscaping), and regulatory compliance (ADA, environmental). Reports follow ASTM E2018 format and include Replacement Reserve tables projecting capital expenditure needs over 12-year horizons. A single commercial inspection takes 4-8 hours on-site and 8-20 hours for report writing, priced at $3,000-15,000 depending on building size. The multi-system nature means commercial inspectors must evaluate systems outside their primary expertise — an inspector with electrical background must also assess HVAC, plumbing, and fire protection.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No commercial-specific inspection platform dominates the market. Most firms use custom Word templates with ASTM E2018 sections, Excel for Replacement Reserve calculations, and file-sharing for photo management. Spectora's commercial module is limited — it doesn't generate ASTM-format reports or Replacement Reserve tables. The gap is particularly acute in condition scoring: the inspector must assess remaining useful life for every building component (roofing, HVAC units, elevator systems, parking surfaces) and project replacement costs over 12 years — a calculation that requires both engineering judgment and current cost data.
