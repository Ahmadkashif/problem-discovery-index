# Bilingual IT Onboarding and User Training Materials

**Niche:** [[niches/it-managed-services/non-english-businesses/profile|MSPs Serving Non-English-Speaking Businesses]]
**Industry:** [[industries/it-managed-services|IT Managed Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** IT security awareness training and new-user onboarding materials exist in English but not in the languages spoken by the employees of immigrant-owned businesses — leaving these workers vulnerable to phishing and basic security mistakes.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #compliance

## The Problem
When an MSP onboards a new client — say, a Vietnamese-owned nail salon chain — the employees who use the POS system, email, and scheduling software speak limited English. The MSP's standard onboarding materials (password policy, phishing awareness, how to submit a support ticket) are in English. Security awareness training platforms (KnowBe4, Breach Secure Now) offer limited language options — Spanish and a few others — but not the full range of languages spoken in US immigrant business communities (Vietnamese, Korean, Mandarin, Haitian Creole, Tagalog, Arabic). The result: employees at non-English-speaking businesses receive training they cannot understand, fail to follow security procedures, and generate tickets for issues that onboarding should have prevented.

## What Already Exists
KnowBe4 offers security awareness training in ~35 languages. Breach Secure Now and other MSP-focused training platforms offer fewer language options. Generic onboarding tools (Trainual, Lessonly) provide template-based training creation but not IT-specific content.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is not just translation but cultural and contextual adaptation. A phishing awareness training for employees at a Korean-owned dry cleaning business needs to show phishing examples relevant to their industry (fake supplier invoices, fraudulent payment links) in Korean, not generic examples from a corporate environment translated to Korean. IT onboarding materials need to cover the specific tools used in the business (Clover POS, Square, specific industry apps) with screenshots in the relevant language settings. The training must be deliverable on personal smartphones (many workers do not have work computers) in short modules that fit into a workday.

## Target Customer
MSPs onboarding employees at non-English-speaking businesses, typically in retail, food service, personal care, and light manufacturing sectors in metro areas.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces security incidents at non-English-speaking client sites by 40-60% through comprehensible training, decreases onboarding-related support tickets by 30-50%, and demonstrates the MSP's commitment to serving the entire workforce — strengthening client relationships.
