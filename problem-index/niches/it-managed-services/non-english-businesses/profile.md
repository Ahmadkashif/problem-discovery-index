# MSPs Serving Non-English-Speaking Businesses

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-managed-services|IT Managed Services]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — language barriers compound the existing SMB tech adoption challenges; most IT support is informal and relationship-based
**Target Buyer:** MSP owner or account manager serving immigrant-owned businesses in metro areas where a significant non-English-speaking business population exists
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — the same automation opportunities but constrained by the need for multilingual interfaces and culturally adapted support workflows

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
In major US metro areas, significant populations of businesses operate primarily in Spanish, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Korean, Arabic, or other languages. These businesses need IT support — POS systems, accounting software, email, security — but mainstream MSPs market and deliver services exclusively in English. Business owners who speak limited English avoid engaging with IT providers because they cannot understand the proposals, contracts, or support interactions. The result is a large underserved market of businesses running outdated, insecure IT infrastructure because there is no provider that speaks their language. MSPs that can deliver bilingual service in these communities face essentially zero competition.

## Current Tools & Gaps
All major MSP tools (ConnectWise, Datto, NinjaRMM) operate in English only. Support tickets, client portals, reports, and proposals are generated in English. There are no MSP-specific tools with multilingual capabilities. The few MSPs serving this market rely on bilingual staff for all client interactions, which creates a bottleneck — the bilingual technician becomes the only person who can handle those clients.
