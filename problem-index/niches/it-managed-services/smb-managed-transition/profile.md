# SMB Break/Fix-to-Managed Transition MSPs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-managed-services|IT Managed Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most have adopted ConnectWise or Datto but underutilize automation features; many still operate with break/fix habits layered over managed contracts
**Target Buyer:** Owner of a 5-20 person MSP transitioning from hourly break/fix billing to flat-rate managed service agreements
**Automation Potential:** High — the transition to managed services requires systematizing reactive workflows into proactive monitoring and automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The majority of MSPs by count are small firms (5-20 people) in the process of transitioning from a break/fix model (bill per incident) to a managed services model (flat monthly fee per endpoint). This transition is the defining business challenge for thousands of MSPs: it requires shifting from reactive problem-solving to proactive monitoring, standardizing client environments for supportability, pricing contracts correctly (too low and you lose money, too high and you lose deals), and building the operational muscle to deliver consistent service quality across 50-200 clients. Many firms are "hybrid" — running managed contracts for some clients while still doing break/fix for others — creating operational chaos as the team context-switches between two fundamentally different service delivery models.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ConnectWise Manage/Automate or Datto/Kaseya for PSA/RMM, with Syncro or NinjaRMM for smaller firms. These tools provide the technical infrastructure for managed services but do not guide the business transition: pricing managed contracts, standardizing client environments, building SOPs, training technicians to work proactively instead of reactively, or measuring per-client profitability. The gap is the business intelligence and operational playbook layer that sits above the tools.
