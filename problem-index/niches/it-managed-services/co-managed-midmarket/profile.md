# Co-Managed IT for Mid-Market

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-managed-services|IT Managed Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — clients have internal IT staff with their own tools; the MSP must integrate with existing infrastructure rather than replacing it
**Target Buyer:** vCIO or service delivery manager at an MSP providing co-managed IT to companies with 100-500 seats that have internal IT departments
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the co-management relationship adds coordination complexity that pure automation cannot address, but reporting, escalation, and knowledge sharing between internal and external teams are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Co-managed IT is a fundamentally different service model than fully managed: the client has an internal IT team (typically 1-5 people) that handles day-to-day operations, and the MSP provides supplemental expertise — L2/L3 escalation support, project work, cybersecurity, compliance, and strategic advisory (vCIO). The coordination challenge is unique: who owns which tickets, how does the MSP technician get access to the client's systems, how do escalation handoffs work between internal and external teams, and who is responsible when something falls through the gap? The service agreement is more complex than a fully managed contract because it must define the boundary between internal and external responsibility.

## Current Tools & Gaps
The MSP uses ConnectWise/Datto, but the client's internal team may use a different ticketing system (Jira Service Management, Freshdesk, Zendesk) and different RMM tools. The MSP must either install their own RMM alongside the client's tools (creating duplication) or gain access to the client's platform (creating security and access control challenges). No tool manages the co-managed relationship boundary: defining which systems the MSP monitors vs. the internal team, routing tickets to the correct team based on issue type, and providing shared visibility into open issues across both teams.
