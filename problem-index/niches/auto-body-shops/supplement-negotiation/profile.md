# Supplement Negotiation Workflow

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Supplement submission happens through insurer portals, but the analysis of what to supplement, how to document it, and how to negotiate is entirely manual experience-based work.
**Target Buyer:** Body shop estimator/manager who writes and negotiates supplements
**Automation Potential:** High — Supplement identification follows predictable patterns (impact type to hidden damage correlation), and negotiation arguments are largely template-based with insurer-specific variations.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Supplements are additional repair costs discovered after the initial insurance estimate — hidden damage behind a bumper cover, bent reinforcement bars only visible after disassembly, paint blend requirements on adjacent panels. Industry-wide, supplements add 30-50% to the initial estimate on average, representing $5B+ in annual repair revenue. Writing supplements is a specialized skill: the estimator must identify hidden damage, document it to insurer standards, write it in CCC/Mitchell line-item format, and negotiate with the adjuster. A skilled supplement writer recovers $2,000-$5,000 per claim that an unskilled one misses.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CCC ONE and Mitchell handle supplement submission as line-item additions to the original estimate. But they don't help the estimator identify what to supplement (that's tacit knowledge based on impact type and vehicle structure), how to document it (which photos, which measurements, which OEM repair procedures to cite), or how to negotiate when the adjuster pushes back. The tool records the supplement; it doesn't help create or defend it.
