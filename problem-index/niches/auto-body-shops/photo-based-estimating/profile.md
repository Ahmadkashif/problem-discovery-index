# Photo-Based Damage Estimating

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Insurers are pushing photo-based estimating hard (it saves adjuster visits), but the shop-side tools for creating accurate estimates from photos are primitive.
**Target Buyer:** Body shop estimator handling virtual/remote estimates from customer-submitted or shop-taken photos
**Automation Potential:** High — Damage detection, severity assessment, and repair operation selection from photos are pattern-matching tasks that experienced estimators do by sight.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Photo-based estimating has exploded since 2020 — insurers now accept (and prefer) photo-based claims where the customer submits phone photos and receives an estimate without an adjuster visit. Body shops are increasingly asked to provide estimates from photos before the vehicle arrives, or to supplement insurer photo-estimates with shop-based photo documentation. This creates a workflow where the estimator must assess damage severity, identify repair vs. replace decisions, and estimate costs from 2D images — a task that experienced estimators do well but junior estimators do poorly because they can't gauge depth, panel deformation, or hidden damage from photos alone.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Tractable and Solera (Audatex) offer AI-powered photo damage assessment for insurers, but these tools serve the insurer side (minimizing payouts) not the shop side (ensuring full repair scope). CCC ONE accepts photos but doesn't analyze them — the estimator still manually interprets every photo. No shop-facing tool helps the estimator extract maximum information from customer-submitted photos or guides the customer on which photos to submit.
