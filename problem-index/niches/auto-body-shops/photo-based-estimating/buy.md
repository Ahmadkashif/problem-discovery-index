# Guided Photo Capture for Customer-Submitted Damage Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/photo-based-estimating/profile|Photo-Based Damage Estimating]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Broadly and Podium enable customer photo submission, but customers take useless photos — too close, wrong angle, poor lighting — and the estimator can't assess damage from them.
**Tags:** #computer-vision #automation #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a body shop asks a customer to "send us photos of the damage," they receive 3-5 photos that are usually: too close to the damage (no context of which panel is affected), taken in shade or at night (no surface detail visible), focused on the most obvious damage (ignoring adjacent panel involvement), and missing critical angles (no photo of the panel gap or the damage depth). The estimator can't write an accurate estimate from these photos and either asks the customer to retake (50% don't bother) or writes a lowball estimate and explains "the actual cost may be higher once we see the vehicle."

## What Already Exists
Broadly and Podium allow shops to receive customer photos via text. Claimable and other insurer-focused tools guide consumers through photo capture for insurance claims. But no body-shop-specific tool guides the customer through a damage-specific photo sequence tailored to collision estimating needs — the right angles, distances, lighting, and reference shots an estimator needs.

## The Customization Gap
A guided photo capture flow that the shop sends to the customer via SMS link. Based on the customer's initial description ("hit in the rear"), the tool guides them through a specific photo sequence: (1) full-vehicle rear view from 10 feet, (2) damage close-up with hand for scale reference, (3) left quarter panel for adjacent damage check, (4) trunk/tailgate gap alignment, (5) taillight and bumper fitment from below. Each step shows an example photo of what the estimator needs. The completed photo set arrives at the shop pre-organized and estimator-ready.

## Target Customer
Body shop estimators receiving 10-20 customer photo submissions per week, of which 60-70% are currently unusable for accurate estimating.

## Impact If Solved
Increases usable customer photo submissions from 30-40% to 80-90%, reducing the "please retake your photos" cycle that loses 20-30% of potential customers. Enables more accurate remote estimates on the first attempt, improving estimate-to-repair conversion rates by 15-25%.
