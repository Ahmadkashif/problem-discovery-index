# Remote Supplement Documentation for Adjuster-Scarce Areas

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/rural-body-shops/profile|Rural Body Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CompuScope and Audatex support photo-based supplement submissions, but their formats don't meet the enhanced documentation standards rural shops need when adjusters can't visit in person.
**Tags:** #cnn #object-detection #computer-vision #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a rural shop discovers hidden damage during disassembly (additional damage behind a bumper cover, for example), they need to file a supplement with the insurer for additional repair cost approval. In urban areas, the adjuster drives over and inspects within 24 hours. In rural areas, the nearest adjuster might be 2 hours away — so the insurer asks for photo documentation instead. But the photos the shop submits via text or email are often unclear, poorly lit, taken from wrong angles, and lack measurement references. Adjusters reject 30-40% of rural supplement submissions for "insufficient documentation," delaying repairs by 1-2 weeks while the shop re-photographs and re-submits.

## What Already Exists
CCC ONE and Mitchell support photo upload with supplement submissions. CompuScope provides structured photo documentation workflows. But none guide the technician on which specific photos to take, from which angles, with which measurement references — the exact information adjusters need to approve a supplement remotely without visiting.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to provide adjuster-perspective photo guidance: "For this supplement line (R&R front bumper reinforcement), take photos from these 3 specific angles, include a measuring tape showing the deformation depth, and capture the adjacent panel alignment gap." It should pre-populate the supplement with line items matching the visible damage, suggest supporting photos for each line, and flag missing documentation before submission. The goal is first-submission approval rates above 85%.

## Target Customer
Rural body shop estimators filing 5-10 supplements per month remotely, with current first-submission approval rates below 60%.

## Impact If Solved
Increases first-submission supplement approval rates from 55-60% to 85-90%, eliminating 1-2 week delays on 30-40% of supplements. Recovers $1,500-$3,000 per month in labor and rental car costs caused by supplement delays.
