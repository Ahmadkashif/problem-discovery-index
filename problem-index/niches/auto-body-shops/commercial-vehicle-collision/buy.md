# Commercial Vehicle Parts Sourcing Beyond Consumer Catalogs

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/commercial-vehicle-collision/profile|Commercial Vehicle Collision]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** PartsTech and LKQ source passenger vehicle collision parts efficiently, but commercial vehicle panels, bumpers, and structural components require separate supplier networks not covered by standard catalogs.
**Tags:** #data-integration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a body shop needs a fender for a 2020 Camry, they search PartsTech and have 5 options from local distributors in seconds. When they need a cargo door skin for a 2019 Freightliner Sprinter 3500 or a side panel for a Hino box truck, the standard parts catalogs come up empty. Commercial vehicle body parts come from specialized suppliers (Truck Spring, FleetPride, commercial-specific LKQ yards) that aren't integrated into the parts platforms body shops use daily. The estimator spends 1-3 hours on the phone sourcing parts for a single commercial vehicle repair — time that gets buried in the repair cost.

## What Already Exists
PartsTech aggregates collision parts from consumer-vehicle-focused distributors. LKQ Corporation has both consumer and commercial inventories but their commercial catalog isn't fully integrated into shop-facing parts platforms. FleetPride and TruckPro serve the commercial market but require separate accounts, separate ordering systems, and phone-based ordering.

## The Customization Gap
The parts sourcing platform needs to include commercial vehicle distributors (FleetPride, TruckPro, Rush Truck Center parts departments) alongside standard collision parts distributors. It should handle commercial vehicle VIN decode (which differs from passenger car — commercial VINs encode GVWR, body type, and upfit configuration), and cross-reference commercial body parts across OEM and aftermarket sources. The same search workflow the estimator uses for a Camry fender should work for a Sprinter cargo door.

## Target Customer
Body shop estimators handling 5-15 commercial vehicle repairs per month who spend 1-3 hours per vehicle sourcing parts through phone calls and separate supplier websites.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces commercial vehicle parts sourcing time from 1-3 hours to 15-30 minutes per vehicle, recovering 5-20 hours per month of estimator time. Faster parts sourcing shortens commercial vehicle repair cycle time by 1-2 days, directly reducing fleet downtime costs.
