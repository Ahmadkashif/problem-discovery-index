# Restoration Project Management with Evolving Scope Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/classic-car-restoration/profile|Classic & Specialty Car Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No project management tool handles restoration's unique pattern: long-duration projects with continuously evolving scope, milestone-based billing, and photo-documented progress.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A restoration shop managing 10 concurrent projects tracks each one on a whiteboard or in the owner's head. When a 1967 Camaro is disassembled and extensive rust is found in the floor pans, the scope expands by $8,000 and 6 weeks — but this change is communicated verbally to the customer, noted on a Post-it, and the revised estimate never gets formally documented. Six months later, the customer disputes the final bill because they "only approved $45,000" and the shop has no paper trail showing the 7 scope changes that brought it to $62,000. This scope-creep documentation failure is the #1 source of disputes in restoration.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Collision repair vendors (CCC ONE, Mitchell) serve 90% of body shops and have no reason to build restoration features. Generic PM tools don't understand automotive phases, parts tracking, or the visual documentation requirements. The restoration market is perceived as too small and fragmented for a purpose-built SaaS. But at $8B and growing (driven by baby boomer wealth and collector car appreciation), the market justifies a vertical solution.

## What to Build
A restoration-specific project management platform with: phase-based project templates (disassembly through final detail), scope change tracking with customer approval workflow (digital sign-off on each change order), milestone-based billing triggers, integrated photo documentation organized by project phase, and a customer portal showing real-time project status. The system produces an auditable trail of every scope change, approval, and payment — eliminating end-of-project billing disputes.

## Target Customer
Restoration shop owners managing 5+ concurrent projects with average values of $30K-$150K who currently track scope changes on paper and experience 2-3 billing disputes per year.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates 80-90% of end-of-project billing disputes (worth $5,000-$20,000 each in write-offs or legal costs). Reduces scope tracking overhead by 5-8 hours per week. Enables shops to take on 20-30% more projects by removing the management bottleneck.
