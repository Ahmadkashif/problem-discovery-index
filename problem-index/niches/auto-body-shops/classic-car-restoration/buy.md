# Parts Sourcing Platform for Discontinued and NOS Components

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/classic-car-restoration/profile|Classic & Specialty Car Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** PartsTech and WorldPac source current-production parts, but restoration shops need discontinued, NOS (new-old-stock), and reproduction parts from fragmented specialty suppliers.
**Tags:** #recommendation #data-integration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Restoring a 1969 Mustang requires parts that haven't been manufactured in 50 years: specific trim pieces, weatherstripping profiles, interior hardware, and body panels. These parts come from a fragmented ecosystem of reproduction manufacturers (Dynacorn, AMD), NOS dealers, swap meets, eBay sellers, and marque-specific suppliers (NPD for Ford, AMES for GM). A restoration technician spends 3-5 hours per week hunting parts across 15+ websites, calling specialty vendors, and verifying fitment for specific model years and sub-models. A wrong part delays the project by weeks.

## What Already Exists
PartsTech aggregates modern aftermarket parts from mainstream distributors. eBay Motors has a massive catalog of classic parts but with unreliable fitment data and no quality verification. Marque-specific catalogs (NPD, AMES) are comprehensive for their brands but require separate searches. No platform aggregates across the classic parts ecosystem with verified fitment data.

## The Customization Gap
A restoration parts sourcing platform needs to aggregate inventory from reproduction manufacturers, NOS dealers, and verified eBay/swap meet sellers into a single searchable catalog, organized by year/make/model/sub-model with verified fitment data. It should track which parts the shop has ordered, which are on backorder (common for reproduction parts with 8-12 week lead times), and which are needed but not yet sourced. Integration into the restoration project timeline prevents the "project stalled waiting for one trim piece" bottleneck.

## Target Customer
Restoration shop owners sourcing 50-200 parts per active project across 5-15 concurrent restorations, currently spending 15-25 hours per month on parts hunting.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces parts sourcing time by 50-70% (saving 8-15 hours per month), prevents 2-3 project delays per month caused by fitment errors on mis-ordered parts, and recovers $2,000-$5,000 per month in labor previously consumed by parts hunting instead of billable restoration work.
