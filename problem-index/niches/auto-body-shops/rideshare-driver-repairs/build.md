# Rideshare Vehicle Platform Compliance Repair Certification

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-body-shops/rideshare-driver-repairs/profile|Rideshare & Gig Driver Repairs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-body-shops|Auto Body Shops]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No body shop produces documentation that certifies a repaired vehicle meets Uber/Lyft/DoorDash platform vehicle requirements, even though platform deactivation is the driver's biggest fear.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
After a collision repair, a rideshare driver needs to prove to Uber or Lyft that their vehicle meets platform standards — no visible damage, functional safety systems, clean interior presentation. Platforms require photo documentation and sometimes a third-party inspection. Body shops hand the driver their keys and say "it's done" — the driver then scrambles to take their own photos, hope they pass platform review, and potentially wait days for reactivation. If the platform rejects the repair (visible overspray, uneven panel gaps, misaligned bumper), the driver loses additional income days returning to the shop for corrections.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Traditional body shops don't know (or care about) rideshare platform vehicle requirements. The requirements vary by platform, change periodically, and aren't published in a format shops can easily reference. The rideshare driver segment is perceived as low-margin walk-in work, not a customer base worth building specialized workflows for.

## What to Build
A certification module that maintains current vehicle requirements for major rideshare/delivery platforms, generates a platform-compliant photo package documenting the completed repair, produces a "repair completion certificate" formatted for platform submission, and helps the driver submit directly to their platform for faster reactivation. The shop charges $50-$100 for the certification service, and the driver gets reactivated 1-2 days faster.

## Target Customer
Body shops near airports and urban cores where rideshare/delivery drivers represent 15-25% of walk-in collision work.

## Impact If Built
Gets drivers reactivated 1-2 days faster ($150-$600 in recovered driver income), generates $50-$100 per repair in certification revenue for the shop, and creates a loyalty loop — drivers return to shops that understand their platform compliance needs.
