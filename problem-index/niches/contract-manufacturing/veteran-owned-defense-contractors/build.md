# SDVOSB Defense Compliance Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/contract-manufacturing/veteran-owned-defense-contractors/profile|Veteran-Owned Defense Contractors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/contract-manufacturing|Contract Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform integrates ITAR export control management, CMMC cybersecurity compliance tracking, government cost accounting requirements, and DCMA audit preparation into a single system designed for small defense manufacturers — forcing them to manage these compliance domains through separate consultants, spreadsheets, and written procedures.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
A veteran-owned machine shop that wins its first $2M DoD contract must simultaneously implement: ITAR access controls (restricting access to controlled technical data to US persons), CMMC Level 2 cybersecurity practices (110 NIST SP 800-171 controls), government contract cost accounting (segregating direct and indirect costs per contract per FAR requirements), and quality management under AS9100 or customer-specific requirements. Each domain has its own regulatory framework, documentation requirements, and audit expectations. A 50-person shop without dedicated compliance staff must hire 3-4 different consultants — ITAR, cybersecurity, government accounting, quality — each operating independently, creating redundant documentation efforts and gaps between compliance domains.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Each compliance domain (ITAR, CMMC, FAR cost accounting, AS9100 quality) has specialized software vendors — but they don't integrate. ITAR compliance tools (EASE, OCR Services) focus on export licensing. Cybersecurity tools (Hyper Vigilance, Beryllium InfoSec) focus on CMMC assessment. Government contract accounting is a function within specialized ERPs (Deltek Costpoint, Unanet). Combining these into a single platform requires cross-domain regulatory expertise that is rare, and the small-manufacturer market is too fragmented for any single vendor to justify the integration investment.

## What to Build
An integrated compliance management platform for small defense manufacturers: ITAR module (access control lists, technology control plans, classification guidance for controlled items, shipping compliance checklists), CMMC module (control implementation tracking against NIST 800-171, evidence collection, self-assessment scoring, audit preparation), cost accounting module (contract-level cost segregation, indirect rate calculation, incurred cost submission preparation), and quality module (AS9100 procedure templates, audit preparation checklists). The platform guides the business through each compliance domain with implementation wizards and pre-built templates specific to small manufacturing operations.

## Target Customer
Program managers and owners at SDVOSBs and other small defense contractors with $1-20M in annual defense contract revenue and fewer than 100 employees. Approximately 5,000-8,000 companies in the US.

## Impact If Built
Reduces defense compliance consulting spend by 40-60% — from $50,000-$150,000/year in consultant fees to $15,000-$30,000/year in platform subscription plus targeted consulting for complex issues. Accelerates compliance implementation from 6-12 months to 2-4 months, enabling faster ramp-up on new contracts. Reduces the risk of compliance findings that can result in contract termination and debarment.
