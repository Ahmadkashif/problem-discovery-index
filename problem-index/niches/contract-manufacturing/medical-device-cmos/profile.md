**Target Buyer:** VP of Quality or Plant Manager at contract manufacturers operating under FDA 21 CFR Part 820
**Automation Potential:** High — DHR compilation, CAPA documentation, and process validation record assembly are rule-heavy workflows with defined regulatory templates

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Medical device contract manufacturers operate under FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (Quality System Regulation) and ISO 13485, which impose documentation requirements that far exceed commercial manufacturing. Every production lot generates a Device History Record (DHR) — a complete record of materials, process parameters, in-process inspections, and final acceptance testing traceable to the finished device for its entire useful life (often 10-20 years). Every non-conformance triggers a CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) investigation with root cause analysis, effectiveness verification, and regulatory filing requirements. Every process change requires Installation, Operational, and Performance Qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ) documentation. These documentation requirements consume 20-30% of total production labor in a medical device CM.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Greenlight Guru and MasterControl provide quality management systems (QMS) designed for medical devices. Epicor and Plex serve as ERP/MES platforms. These systems manage document control and CAPA workflow routing but do not automate DHR compilation (assembly of production records into a device-traceable package), process validation documentation (IQ/OQ/PQ report generation from test data), or predictive quality analytics (identifying which process drift patterns precede quality events).
