# Simplified ERP for First-Time Manufacturing Business Owners

**Niche:** [[niches/contract-manufacturing/rural-reshoring-job-shops/profile|Rural Reshoring Job Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/contract-manufacturing|Contract Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Manufacturing ERP systems like Epicor and JobBOSS assume the user understands manufacturing terminology (routing, BOM structures, work center loading, overhead absorption) — concepts that a first-time shop owner who is an expert machinist but a novice business manager has never encountered.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Reshoring job shops are often started by experienced machinists or production managers who have deep manufacturing skills but limited experience with business management systems. They can set up a CNC machine and produce parts to aerospace tolerances, but they've never built a BOM structure in an ERP, configured a work center with capacity constraints, or set up overhead cost allocation. Manufacturing ERP systems assume familiarity with these concepts — their setup wizards ask questions like "Enter your burden rate percentage" that a first-time owner doesn't know how to answer. The result: the shop either doesn't adopt ERP (remaining on spreadsheets and paper) or buys an ERP that is only partially configured and underutilized.

## What Already Exists
JobBOSS, E2 Shop System, and Prodsmart provide job shop ERP at various price points. These platforms are functional for experienced ERP users but present a steep learning curve for first-time users. Implementation typically requires a vendor-led implementation project ($5,000-$25,000) plus ongoing consulting for configuration changes.

## The Customization Gap
A first-time-owner-friendly ERP needs: (1) guided onboarding that uses plain language — "How much does it cost per hour to run your shop, including rent, utilities, and equipment payments?" rather than "Enter your shop overhead absorption rate"; (2) progressive complexity — start with basic quoting and job tracking, then introduce BOM management, scheduling, and quality tracking as the business grows; (3) built-in financial education — explaining why tracking actual vs. quoted costs matters, how to calculate a competitive hourly rate, and when to raise prices; (4) template-based setup — pre-configured for common manufacturing operations (CNC machining, welding, assembly) so the owner doesn't build everything from scratch.

## Target Customer
First-time manufacturing business owners with 3-20 employees, typically experienced tradespeople starting their own shops. Approximately 3,000-5,000 new manufacturing businesses start annually in the US.

## Impact If Solved
Gets first-time shop owners to job costing and production visibility 3-6 months faster than current ERP implementations. Prevents the 40-50% of small manufacturing ERP implementations that fail due to complexity — leaving the shop on spreadsheets. For a new shop with $1-3M in revenue, achieving per-job profitability visibility in the first year (rather than discovering margin problems after 18 months) can prevent the business failures that claim 30% of new manufacturing businesses in their first 3 years.
