# Rural Reshoring Job Shops

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/contract-manufacturing|Contract Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-20B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4-5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — limited broadband access, minimal IT infrastructure, and difficulty attracting technology-skilled workforce to rural locations
**Target Buyer:** Plant owner or production manager at manufacturing job shops in rural areas benefiting from reshoring trends
**Automation Potential:** Medium — basic production management, quoting, and quality tracking are automatable with tools designed for low-connectivity, low-IT-resource environments

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Reshoring — the return of manufacturing from overseas to domestic facilities — is creating new contract manufacturing capacity in rural communities where labor costs are lower, industrial real estate is affordable, and state incentive programs subsidize facility construction. These new or expanding shops face a unique combination of challenges: geographic isolation limits access to skilled labor (process engineers, quality professionals, IT support), poor broadband infrastructure limits cloud-based tool adoption, and the owners often have deep manufacturing expertise but limited experience with the business systems needed to manage a growing operation. They are building 21st-century manufacturing capacity with 1990s-era business infrastructure.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many use QuickBooks for accounting, spreadsheets for quoting and scheduling, and paper-based quality records. ERP adoption is rare due to cost, implementation complexity, and the absence of local implementation consultants. Cloud-based manufacturing SaaS requires reliable broadband that many rural locations lack. The tools that urban manufacturers take for granted — cloud ERP, online collaboration platforms, video-based remote support — are unavailable or unreliable in these settings.
