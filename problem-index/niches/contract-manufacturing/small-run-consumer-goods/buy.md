# Job Costing Adapted for High-Changeover Manufacturing

**Niche:** [[niches/contract-manufacturing/small-run-consumer-goods/profile|Small-Run Consumer Goods CMs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/contract-manufacturing|Contract Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Accounting platforms like QuickBooks and Xero track revenue and expenses at the company level, but can't tell a small CM which of their 150 active products makes money and which loses money — the per-product job costing that determines whether the business survives on 5% margins.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #linear-regression #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Small-run CMs quote hundreds of products based on estimated labor, material, and overhead costs. Actual production costs vary — a product that takes 45 minutes to produce when quoted at 30 minutes is losing money on every run. But without job-level costing, the CM can't identify which products are profitable. Owners discover margin problems when quarterly financials show declining profits, but can't attribute the shortfall to specific products, customers, or process steps. By then, they may have been producing 20 products at a loss for months.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks, Xero, and FreshBooks handle general business accounting. Fishbowl and inFlow provide inventory management with basic job costing. Manufacturing ERP systems (Epicor, JobBOSS) offer detailed job costing but cost $500-$2,000/month and require weeks of implementation — pricing and complexity inappropriate for a 10-person shop.

## The Customization Gap
A small-CM job costing tool needs: (1) per-job actual cost capture that is fast enough for a high-changeover environment — operators log time per job with a single tap, not a multi-field form; (2) automatic material cost calculation from BOM and current material prices; (3) overhead allocation methodology appropriate for small manufacturers (simplified activity-based costing, not full factory overhead absorption); (4) product profitability dashboard showing actual margin per product over time, highlighting products trending toward unprofitability.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators at consumer goods CMs with 50+ active products and no current per-product profitability visibility.

## Impact If Solved
Surfaces the 15-25% of products losing money that the CM currently doesn't know about. Enables price adjustments or product discontinuation that improves overall margin by 2-5 percentage points. For a $3M revenue CM operating at 5% EBITDA, improving margin by 2 points adds $60,000 in annual profit — meaningful for a small business.
