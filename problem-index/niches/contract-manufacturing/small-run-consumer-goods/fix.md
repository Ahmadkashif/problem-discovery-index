# Paper Travelers and Manual Production Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/contract-manufacturing/small-run-consumer-goods/profile|Small-Run Consumer Goods CMs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/contract-manufacturing|Contract Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Small CMs track production jobs on printed paper travelers that accompany each batch through the shop — and when the paper gets lost, wet, or illegible, the production status of that job becomes a mystery until someone physically locates the batch on the floor.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A paper traveler is a printed job sheet listing the BOM, production steps, quality checkpoints, and packaging instructions for a specific production batch. At small CMs, the traveler moves with the batch from station to station. Operators check off completed steps and note actual quantities and any deviations. When the owner or scheduler wants to know the status of a job, they either walk the floor to find the traveler or call/text the operator. When travelers are lost (happens 2-5 times per week in a busy shop), the job's status, any quality notes, and the operator's annotations are lost with it. Multiple copies create version confusion — the office has an outdated copy while the floor has the current one.

## Why It's Still Broken
Small CMs have tried going paperless with general-purpose tools (Google Sheets, Trello, Monday.com) but found them too slow for the production floor — operators need to access and update job information in 10-15 seconds while wearing gloves, standing at a workstation. Manufacturing-specific digital traveler systems exist (Paperless Parts, 1factory) but target regulated industries (aerospace, medical) with pricing ($300-$1,000/month) and feature complexity inappropriate for a cosmetics or supplement CM with no regulatory compliance requirements.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A tablet-mounted digital traveler with a job list view showing all active jobs, their current step, and progress. Operators tap to advance the job to the next step — one tap, not a multi-field data entry form. Glove-compatible touch interface with large buttons. Quality checkpoints are confirmed with a single tap or a quick photo capture. Job status is visible to the owner and scheduler in real time from any device. Lost paper is eliminated. Historical production records are searchable.

## Who Feels the Pain
Production floor operators who waste time looking for misplaced travelers or re-creating lost production notes, and owners who can't determine job status without walking the floor.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 30-60 minutes of daily production time lost to traveler management and status inquiries across the shop. Provides real-time job visibility that enables the owner to make scheduling decisions from their desk rather than the floor. Creates a searchable production history — useful for customer quality inquiries, re-order quoting, and process improvement.
