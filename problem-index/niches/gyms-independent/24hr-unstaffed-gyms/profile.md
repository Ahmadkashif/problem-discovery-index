# 24-Hour Unstaffed Gyms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/gyms-independent|Independent Gyms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 10%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Access control and billing are digital by necessity, but remote facility monitoring, equipment status tracking, cleaning verification, and incident response are handled through periodic in-person checks and security cameras reviewed after the fact.
**Target Buyer:** Owner-Operator / Remote Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Remote facility operations (cleaning verification, equipment status, safety monitoring, emergency response) are highly automatable with IoT and computer vision but currently depend on periodic human visits.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The 24-hour unstaffed gym model (popularized by Anytime Fitness, Snap Fitness, and now adopted by many independents) operates with 0-2 staff on-site during peak hours and fully unmanned during off-peak hours (typically 9 PM-6 AM). This model slashes labor costs by 60-70% but creates a remote operations challenge: how do you ensure the facility is clean, equipment is functional, the environment is safe, and rules are followed when nobody is watching? Independent 24-hour gyms lack the corporate infrastructure (remote monitoring centers, standardized IoT deployments) that franchise chains invest in, leaving owners to check cameras on their phones and hope nothing goes wrong overnight.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Access control systems (Kisi, Brivo, Openpath) handle keycard/app-based entry. Basic security cameras provide visual monitoring. Gym management platforms handle billing. But no integrated system provides real-time facility status: Is the gym clean? Did the last cleaning shift actually complete? Is any equipment visibly damaged or out of position? Are members following rules (re-racking weights, not propping open doors)? Is anyone in medical distress? The owner reviews camera footage reactively (after a complaint or incident) rather than proactively.
