# Large-Floor Independent Gyms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/gyms-independent|Independent Gyms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 20%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most use gym management software (Gym Assistant, ClubReady, ABC Fitness) for basic member check-in and billing, but utilization analytics, space planning, and equipment ROI tracking are manual or nonexistent.
**Target Buyer:** Gym Owner / General Manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Billing, check-in, and basic scheduling are automated, but floor utilization analysis, equipment lifecycle management, and staff scheduling remain manual.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Large-floor independent gyms (5,000-30,000 sq ft, offering free weights, cardio, machines, and sometimes group fitness rooms) compete directly with franchise chains (Planet Fitness, LA Fitness, Anytime Fitness) on price and convenience. Their advantage is local ownership, community feel, and equipment flexibility — they can stock specialty equipment that franchises won't. They typically charge $30-70/month and have 500-3,000 members. Their operational challenge is facility management at scale: knowing which equipment zones are over/underutilized, which machines need maintenance before they break, how to schedule staff for actual traffic patterns rather than fixed shifts, and how to price memberships competitively without franchise chain data.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ABC Fitness Solutions, ClubReady, and Gym Assistant handle member management, billing, and check-in. Jonas Fitness and Daxko serve larger facilities. But none provide floor utilization analytics (which zones and equipment are used when), equipment ROI analysis (is that $15K cable machine earning its keep?), or competitive pricing intelligence. Owners make capital expenditure and pricing decisions based on gut feel and member complaints rather than data.
