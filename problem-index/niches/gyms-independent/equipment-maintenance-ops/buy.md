# Lightweight CMMS for Single-Location Gyms

**Niche:** [[niches/gyms-independent/equipment-maintenance-ops/profile|Equipment Maintenance Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/gyms-independent|Independent Gyms]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** UpKeep, Fiix, and Limble CMMS platforms serve manufacturing plants and multi-site operations, but a single-location gym owner managing 50 machines doesn't need (or want) enterprise maintenance management — they need a simple equipment inventory with service reminders.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Independent gym owners know they should track equipment maintenance but the available tools are overwhelming. Enterprise CMMS platforms require configuring work order workflows, defining asset hierarchies, setting up technician roles, and learning a complex interface — all designed for a facility manager overseeing 500+ assets across multiple sites. A gym owner with 50 machines and no maintenance staff needs a dramatically simpler tool: a list of their equipment with purchase dates, warranty expiration, manufacturer-recommended service intervals, and automatic reminders when service is due. They need a maintenance reminder app, not a maintenance management platform.

## What Already Exists
UpKeep, Fiix, Limble CMMS, and Maintenance Connection serve facility maintenance teams at $50-200/user/month with full work order management, parts inventory, technician dispatch, and reporting. These platforms work well for large fitness chains with dedicated maintenance staff. Free tools like Google Calendar reminders are too unstructured — they don't store equipment details, service history, or vendor contacts. The gap is a gym-specific equipment management tool between "enterprise CMMS" and "calendar reminder."

## The Customization Gap
The needed product is a radically simplified CMMS pre-configured for gym equipment: (1) an equipment inventory with pre-loaded maintenance schedules for common brands (Life Fitness, Precor, Hammer Strength, Rogue), (2) automatic service reminders based on purchase date and manufacturer intervals, (3) a simple service log (date, what was done, who did it, cost), (4) warranty tracking with expiration alerts, (5) a vendor directory for local equipment service companies, and (6) annual maintenance cost reporting for budgeting. No work orders, no technician management, no parts inventory — just equipment tracking and reminders.

## Target Customer
Owners of independent gyms with no dedicated maintenance staff who currently track equipment service in their memory or a notebook.

## Impact If Solved
Extends average equipment lifespan by 20-30% through consistent preventive maintenance, avoiding $10K-30K in premature equipment replacement costs per gym over a 5-year period — while reducing the stress of surprise breakdowns.
