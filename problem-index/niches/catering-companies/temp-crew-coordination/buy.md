# Gig Platform with Caterer-Trained Workers

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/temp-crew-coordination/profile|Temp Crew Coordination & Day-Of Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Qwick and Instawork supply on-demand hospitality workers but do not pre-train them to a specific caterer's standards — resulting in temp staff who cannot execute silver service, do not know the BEO, and require 30 minutes of on-site orientation.
**Tags:** #recommendation #ranking #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a caterer needs 3 additional servers for Saturday's wedding and their own roster is tapped out, they turn to gig platforms. Qwick or Instawork sends workers who have passed a generic hospitality screening but have never worked with this caterer: they do not know the plating standards, the service sequence, the dietary protocol, or the venue layout. The service captain spends 30-45 minutes of pre-event time orienting gig workers — time that should be spent on final prep and coordination.

## What Already Exists
Qwick and Instawork provide on-demand hospitality staffing with worker ratings, shift matching, and payment processing. Pared offers similar services for back-of-house roles. These platforms verify general hospitality experience but do not certify workers against a specific caterer's operational standards.

## The Customization Gap
The platform must support caterer-specific training modules (video walkthroughs of service standards, BEO reading, dietary protocol) that gig workers complete before their first shift with that caterer. Workers who have completed training and worked 3+ events with the caterer earn a "trained" badge and are prioritized for future assignments. The caterer builds a extended workforce of platform workers who function like their own trained team, without the overhead of full-time employment.

## Target Customer
Catering operations managers who use gig platforms 5-15 times per month and spend 30-45 minutes per event orienting unfamiliar workers to their standards.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminating per-event orientation saves 3-8 hours per week for the service captain. Trained gig workers deliver 30-40% fewer service errors than first-time workers, reducing quality incidents that damage the caterer's reputation.
