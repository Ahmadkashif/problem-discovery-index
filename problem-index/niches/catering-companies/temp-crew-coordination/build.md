# Catering-Specific Temp Crew Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/temp-crew-coordination/profile|Temp Crew Coordination & Day-Of Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages a caterer's private temp crew roster — scheduling per-event, tracking reliability and skill ratings, and automating the confirmation/reminder cycle that currently runs on WhatsApp.
**Tags:** #recommendation #ranking #tabular-ml #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A mid-size caterer maintains a roster of 80-150 temp workers with varying skills (silver service, bartending, buffet service, kitchen prep), availability patterns, reliability histories, and pay rates. When staffing a Saturday wedding (need: 4 servers, 2 bartenders, 1 service captain, 3 kitchen assistants), the operations manager mentally filters the roster, texts candidates, waits for responses, follows up on non-responders, and assembles the crew over 3-5 days of back-and-forth messaging. There is no system that matches event requirements to worker skills, ranks candidates by reliability, or automates the ask-confirm-remind cycle.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Gig platforms (Qwick, Instawork) solve the supply problem but own the worker relationship — the caterer pays a 30-50% markup and gets workers they have never trained. Scheduling tools (7shifts) assume a fixed workforce. The middle ground — a platform for managing a caterer's own private temp pool — has not been built because the market is perceived as too niche and the per-worker revenue too low for gig-platform economics.

## What to Build
A crew management platform where the caterer maintains their own roster: each worker has a profile (skills, certifications, availability windows, pay rate, reliability score based on past events, performance ratings). For each event, the system recommends the optimal crew based on role requirements and worker attributes, sends availability requests, tracks confirmations, automates reminders, and provides a day-of roster with contact info and role assignments.

## Target Customer
Operations managers at catering companies with 50+ temp workers in their pool who currently coordinate staffing through group texts and personal relationships.

## Impact If Built
Reducing staffing coordination time from 5-8 hours per event to 30 minutes recovers 300-500 hours annually. Reliability-based crew selection reduces no-show rates from 10-15% to 3-5%, preventing $500-$2,000 per event in scramble costs.
