# Temp Crew Coordination & Day-Of Staffing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded labor cost affecting all caterers above 3 employees
**Digital Adoption:** Low — temp crew management runs on text messages, WhatsApp groups, and day-of phone calls; no structured scheduling or performance tracking
**Target Buyer:** Operations manager or event captain hiring 10-30 temp service staff per event
**Automation Potential:** High — crew scheduling, confirmation, and day-of deployment follow repeatable patterns with clear rules (role requirements, certifications, reliability history)

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Catering labor is fundamentally different from restaurant labor: 50-70% of the service team are temporary staff hired per-event, not full-time employees. A mid-size caterer maintains a roster of 50-150 temp workers and fills 10-30 positions per event from this pool. The operations manager texts 20 people to fill 10 slots, gets 7 confirmations, chases 3 more, has 2 no-shows day-of, and scrambles to find replacements at 2 PM for a 5 PM event. This process repeats 3-5 times per week and is entirely manual.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Gig platforms (Qwick, Instawork, Pared) provide on-demand staffing but charge 30-50% markup and supply workers unfamiliar with the caterer's standards. Scheduling tools (7shifts, Homebase) are designed for fixed employee rosters, not per-event temp pools. WhatsApp and group texts are the de facto scheduling tool — the operations manager sends "Who's available Saturday 4-10 PM?" and manages responses manually.
