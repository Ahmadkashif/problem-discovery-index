# Venue-Integrated Service Planning

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/wedding-catering-operations/profile|Wedding Catering Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** AllSeated and Social Tables offer venue floor planning but do not connect the floor plan to the caterer's service plan — how many servers per section, which stations serve which tables, what the kitchen-to-plate route looks like.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
Every wedding venue has different kitchen access, plating space, service routes, and logistical constraints. A caterer working at 20 different venues must build a service plan for each — server sections, station placement, kitchen timing, plate-up workflow — from scratch each time, or from memory of the last time they worked that venue. A new venue requires a walkthrough, and the notes from that walkthrough live in a notebook or email that is hard to retrieve when the next event at that venue occurs 8 months later.

## What Already Exists
AllSeated and Social Tables provide 3D venue floor planning tools that show table layouts, seating arrangements, and guest flow. The Knot and WeddingWire connect caterers with couples. But these tools focus on the couple's experience (seating chart, event flow) not the caterer's operational needs (where to stage, how to plate, how many runners per section).

## The Customization Gap
The caterer needs a venue database that stores operational intelligence: kitchen dimensions and equipment, plating area location and capacity, distance from kitchen to farthest table (determines plating temperature and runner count), power outlets for chafing stations, loading dock access, and any restrictions (no open flame, no red wine on white carpet). This data, combined with the event's floor plan, should automatically generate a staffing recommendation and service timeline.

## Target Customer
Wedding caterers working across 15+ venues per year who currently rebuild service plans from scratch for each event and rely on memory for venue-specific operational details.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminating service-plan rework saves 1-2 hours per event (80-160 hours/year). Venue-specific staffing recommendations prevent the 15-20% of events where the caterer sends too few or too many service staff, saving $200-$500 per event in labor misallocation.
