# Wedding Catering Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-18B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — HoneyBook and Aisle Planner used for client communication but production planning is entirely manual
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator or executive chef of a wedding-focused catering company doing 50-150 weddings per year
**Automation Potential:** Medium — weddings are high-customization events but follow predictable seasonal patterns and service formats

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wedding catering is the most emotionally charged, highest-stakes segment: a single failure (running out of entrees, late cake delivery, wrong allergen handling) generates disproportionate reputational damage in an industry where 70% of new business comes from referrals and venue partnerships. Each wedding is a unique project with 6-12 months of pre-production planning, 3-5 client tastings, and a service plan that integrates with the event planner, florist, DJ, photographer, and venue coordinator. The long sales cycle, high customization, and referral dependency create a business model fundamentally different from corporate catering.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HoneyBook and Dubsado handle client communication and contract management. Aisle Planner coordinates with other wedding vendors. CaterTrax handles BEO generation. But no tool manages the wedding-specific production planning pipeline: converting a tasting menu selection into a scaled BEO, tracking guest count changes across 6 months of planning, managing the venue walkthrough checklist, or producing the day-of production timeline that coordinates kitchen, service, and vendor timing.
