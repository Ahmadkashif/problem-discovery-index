# Venue Power and Infrastructure Assessment Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/mobile-onsite-production/profile|Mobile & On-Site Production Caterers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Event venue platforms (Peerspace, The Knot) list venues by aesthetics and capacity but provide zero data on kitchen infrastructure, power capacity, or loading dock access — the information caterers actually need.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
When a caterer books an event at a new venue, they need to know: total electrical capacity (amps) and outlet locations, kitchen facilities (if any), refrigeration access, water supply, loading dock dimensions, distance from kitchen/staging to service area, and any restrictions (no open flame, no gas, no grease). This information is never in the venue listing. The caterer must schedule a site visit, walk the space with an electrician's eye, and take notes — notes that are handwritten, stored in the operations manager's notebook, and lost when the staff turns over.

## What Already Exists
Peerspace, The Knot, and Venue Report list event venues with photos, capacity, and amenities. AllSeated provides 3D venue models. But none include the operational infrastructure data caterers need — electrical capacity, kitchen specs, loading access, distance measurements. This data requires a physical survey and does not exist in any digital platform.

## The Customization Gap
The tool must crowdsource venue infrastructure data from caterers (who visit dozens of venues per year), structured into a searchable database: electrical capacity by zone, kitchen equipment inventory, refrigeration capacity, loading access dimensions, and caterer-submitted notes and photos. A collaborative venue database where caterers share operational intelligence — not aesthetics — about the venues they work at.

## Target Customer
Operations managers who visit 10-30 new venues per year and currently store venue infrastructure notes in personal notebooks that are inaccessible to the rest of the team.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminating redundant site visits saves $200-$500 per venue (travel time + personnel). Preventing power overloads and equipment mismatches at new venues avoids $1,000-$5,000 per incident in emergency generator rentals, blown circuits, and service disruptions.
