# Mobile & On-Site Production Caterers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-7B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — production happens at venues with no connectivity, kitchen infrastructure, or digital systems; everything runs on printed prep lists and walkie-talkies
**Target Buyer:** Operations manager coordinating off-premises food production at event venues, private estates, and outdoor sites
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the logistics are complex but follow patterns that could be systematized once digitally captured

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobile and on-site production caterers cook at the event venue rather than in a commissary kitchen — setting up temporary kitchens in tents, garages, backyards, and ballroom corridors. Every event is a logistics puzzle: what equipment fits in the truck, what the venue's power capacity is (running two convection ovens on a 20-amp circuit trips breakers), how to maintain food temperatures across a 100-yard walk from kitchen to service, and how to coordinate timing when the chef cannot see the dining room. This is the most operationally complex segment of catering, and it runs entirely on paper, experience, and improvisation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No catering software addresses on-site production logistics. CaterTrax generates BEOs but assumes commissary production. Equipment rental platforms (CORT Events, Party Rental Ltd) handle ordering but not load planning. Communication during events relies on walkie-talkies and runners — no tool provides real-time coordination between kitchen and service when they are in different buildings.
