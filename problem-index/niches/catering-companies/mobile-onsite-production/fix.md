# Kitchen-to-Service Communication at Remote Venues

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/mobile-onsite-production/profile|Mobile & On-Site Production Caterers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When the kitchen is in a tent 100 yards from the dining room, the chef cannot see when to fire courses — and the current solution is a runner jogging back and forth yelling "fire mains."
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
On-site catering separates the kitchen from the dining room by 50-200 yards. The chef needs to know when speeches end, when salad plates are cleared, when to fire mains, and when the cake is cut — but has no line of sight. Communication happens through runners (staff members jogging between kitchen and service), walkie-talkies (which are dropped, run out of battery, and garble in noisy environments), or the service captain's personal phone (texting the chef). Mistimed course fires — mains arriving while speeches are still going, or a 15-minute gap between courses — are the most common service failures in on-site catering.

## Why It's Still Broken
Restaurant KDS (kitchen display systems) assume the kitchen and dining room are in the same building with shared WiFi. On-site events have no WiFi, inconsistent cellular coverage, and equipment that must be set up and torn down in 2 hours. The communication tool must work in a field, a barn, a beach, or a parking structure with no infrastructure.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A lightweight, offline-capable course-fire coordination app: the service captain taps "clear salad" on a phone, the kitchen display (a ruggedized tablet) shows "fire mains in 8 minutes" based on the programmed course interval. No WiFi required — the app works on Bluetooth mesh or local hotspot between 2-3 devices. Pre-programmed timeline with live adjustments, battery-optimized for 6+ hour events.

## Who Feels the Pain
The chef who fires mains 10 minutes early because the runner forgot to come back, and the service captain who is blamed when courses arrive during speeches because communication broke down.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminating course-timing failures on 10-15% of on-site events prevents the reputational damage that costs wedding caterers $5K-$15K in lost referrals per incident.
