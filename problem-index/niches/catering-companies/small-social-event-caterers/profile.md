# Small Social Event Caterers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~14%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most operate on personal phones, Venmo for payments, text messages for coordination, and no formal business software
**Target Buyer:** Owner of a 1-5 person catering operation serving birthday parties, graduation celebrations, baby showers, and family gatherings
**Automation Potential:** Medium — simpler events but the entire business infrastructure is absent, not just specific tools

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small social caterers (1-5 person operations, often home-based or commissary-kitchen-based) serve the high-frequency, low-budget end of the market: 20-80 person events for $500-$3,000. Many are run by talented home cooks who transitioned to catering without any business infrastructure — no CRM, no invoicing system, no food cost tracking, no contracts. They find clients through Instagram, word of mouth, and community Facebook groups. The business runs on the owner's phone: inquiries come through DMs, quotes are sent as text messages, deposits are collected on Venmo, and recipes are scaled by multiplying handwritten notes. This informality works at 2-3 events/month but collapses at 8-10 as the owner loses track of quotes, double-books weekends, and cannot calculate profitability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HoneyBook and Dubsado serve creative freelancers (photographers, planners) with proposal and contract features, but their templates and workflows are not designed for food production — they do not handle BEO generation, recipe scaling, prep timelines, or food cost tracking. CaterTrax and Better Cater are too complex and expensive for a 1-3 person operation doing $50K-$200K in revenue.
