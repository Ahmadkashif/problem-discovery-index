**Target Buyer:** Kitchen manager or executive chef coordinating 3-6 simultaneous events from a single commissary kitchen
**Automation Potential:** High — production scheduling is a constrained optimization problem with clear inputs (events, menus, equipment, staff) and objectives (on-time delivery, quality, minimal overtime)

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mid-size caterers ($500K-$5M) routinely produce for 3-6 events simultaneously from a single kitchen. A Saturday might include a 300-person corporate lunch shipping at 10 AM, a 50-person rehearsal dinner plated at 5 PM, and a 120-person wedding buffet loading at 3 PM — each with different menus, service styles, and dietary matrices. The kitchen manager must sequence prep tasks across all events, allocate oven and stovetop time, assign cooks to production lines, and manage the loading dock schedule — all while ensuring every event's food is at the right temperature at the right time. This is a combinatorial scheduling problem that is solved entirely in the kitchen manager's head.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No catering software handles multi-event production scheduling. CaterTrax and Better Cater manage events individually but do not show a unified kitchen production view across concurrent events. Google Sheets and whiteboard grids are the universal tool — the kitchen manager writes event timelines on a whiteboard and mentally resolves conflicts. Scheduling software (Monday.com, Asana) lacks the domain-specific constraints (oven capacity, blast chiller availability, cook station assignments).
