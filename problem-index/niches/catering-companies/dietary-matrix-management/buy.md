# Recipe-Level Allergen Cross-Reference

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/dietary-matrix-management/profile|Dietary Matrix & Allergen Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Recipe management tools like ChefTec and RecipePal track ingredients but do not automatically flag the 14 major allergens at the recipe level or cross-reference against a specific event's dietary matrix.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A caterer with 150 recipes needs to know which recipes contain each of the 14 major allergens (milk, eggs, wheat, soy, peanuts, tree nuts, fish, shellfish, sesame, etc.). When an event has 8 guests with nut allergies, the chef must manually review every recipe on the menu to confirm none contain tree nuts or peanut-derived ingredients — including hidden allergens in sauces, marinades, and garnishes. This review happens mentally, under time pressure, and misses cross-contamination risks.

## What Already Exists
ChefTec, RecipePal, and Meez offer recipe management with ingredient lists and nutritional calculations. Food labeling tools (FoodReady, Nutritics) handle allergen declaration for packaged food products. But neither category connects recipe-level allergen data to a specific event's guest dietary restrictions — they are ingredient databases, not event-aware safety systems.

## The Customization Gap
The tool must maintain allergen flags at the ingredient level (not just the recipe level — sesame oil in a marinade makes the entire dish a sesame allergen), automatically propagate allergen flags through sub-recipes and compound ingredients, and cross-reference against an event's dietary matrix to produce a safety report: "Menu item X contains tree nuts — 3 guests at this event have tree nut allergies. Modified version available? If not, substitute recommendation: [Y]."

## Target Customer
Executive chefs and event coordinators at catering companies who manually review recipes for allergens before each event and have no systematic way to confirm safety.

## Impact If Solved
Automated allergen cross-referencing reduces review time from 30-60 minutes per event to 2 minutes, while eliminating the human error that causes 1-2 allergen incidents per year at a typical mid-size caterer — each costing $10K-$100K in liability.
