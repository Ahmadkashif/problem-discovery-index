# Service-Team Dietary Delivery Accuracy

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/dietary-matrix-management/profile|Dietary Matrix & Allergen Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** The kitchen prepares the correct modified plate, but the server delivers it to the wrong guest — because dietary assignments are communicated verbally in a 2-minute pre-service briefing.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
At a 200-person plated dinner with 20 dietary modifications, each modified plate must reach the correct guest. The service captain briefs the team: "Table 4, seat 3, gluten-free chicken. Table 7, seat 1, vegan entree. Table 12, seat 5, nut-free dessert." The servers memorize their tables' modifications or write them on a notepad. During service, a server delivers the nut-free dessert to seat 4 instead of seat 5 — an error that could be life-threatening. The error rate on dietary plate delivery is 3-8% per event, and most errors are caught only when the guest asks "is this gluten-free?" after receiving the wrong plate.

## Why It's Still Broken
The information exists — the coordinator has a seating chart with dietary assignments. But it is not accessible to the server during service in a usable format. Printed seating charts with highlighted dietary notes are the best-case scenario; verbal briefing with no documentation is the norm. The server carrying 8 plates does not have a free hand to check a printout, and the ballroom lighting is too dim to read small type.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A server-facing mobile app (or smartwatch app) that shows dietary assignments per table. The server glances at their phone before approaching each table: "Table 7: Seat 1 — vegan (green tag), Seat 4 — nut-free (red tag)." Modified plates are physically tagged in the kitchen (colored picks or labels) matching the app's color coding. The service captain confirms deliveries via the app, creating an audit trail.

## Who Feels the Pain
The server who is terrified of causing an allergic reaction but has no reliable system to verify plate assignments, and the guest with a severe allergy who must interrogate every plate before eating.

## Impact If Fixed
Reducing dietary delivery errors from 5% to under 1% prevents 1-3 allergen incidents per year at a busy caterer — avoiding $10K-$100K per incident in liability, and eliminating the single highest anxiety source for service staff at formal events.
