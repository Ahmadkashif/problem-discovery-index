# Dietary Matrix & Allergen Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded operations challenge affecting 80%+ of events
**Digital Adoption:** Low — dietary tracking is manual, typically on printed BEOs with highlighter marks, or in the event coordinator's head
**Target Buyer:** Event coordinator managing 5-15 dietary restrictions per event across 50-200 guests
**Automation Potential:** High — allergen tracking and dietary accommodation are rule-based processes with severe consequences for error (anaphylaxis, liability)

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Dietary restriction complexity has exploded: a single 150-person event may include 5 vegans, 3 celiac guests, 2 nut allergies, 4 lactose intolerant, 1 halal, 1 kosher, and 8 "gluten-free by preference." The event coordinator captures these restrictions from the guest RSVP, communicates them to the chef, who creates modified dishes, which the service team must deliver to the correct guest — without error. A single allergen mistake creates a medical emergency and a six-figure lawsuit. The entire process is manual: spreadsheets, highlighted BEOs, plating labels, and verbal briefings.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No catering software provides end-to-end dietary management: capturing restrictions at RSVP, mapping them through menu modifications, generating kitchen prep instructions for modified dishes, and producing place-card-level guidance for the service team. CaterTrax has a dietary notes field. Wedding platforms (The Knot, Zola) capture RSVP dietary info but do not transmit it to the caterer in structured format. The gap between "Aunt Susan is allergic to tree nuts" on the RSVP and "Aunt Susan gets the panna cotta made with coconut cream instead of hazelnuts" at the place setting is bridged entirely by human memory.
