# Corporate Event Catering

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — CaterTrax adopted at larger operations but most mid-size firms use spreadsheets and generic CRMs
**Target Buyer:** Catering sales director or owner at a company handling 500-2,000 corporate events per year
**Automation Potential:** High — corporate events are more standardized than social events, with recurring clients, repeatable menus, and predictable service patterns

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate catering is the highest-volume, most standardized segment: quarterly board meetings, annual holiday parties, weekly team lunches, and conference breakfasts recur with similar specifications. Clients are businesses with procurement processes — they expect formal proposals, itemized invoices, W-9s, and net-30 payment terms. The sales cycle is relationship-driven with rebooking rates of 40-60% for recurring events. This standardization makes corporate catering the most analytically tractable segment — there is enough pattern repetition to train predictive models on event profitability, prep quantities, and staffing needs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CaterTrax handles proposal generation and event management for larger operations. Better Cater and Caterease offer similar features. But none connect the proposal to actual post-event financials, none predict event profitability at the quote stage, and none manage the corporate client lifecycle (tracking which clients have annual events coming up, predicting rebooking probability, identifying upsell opportunities).
