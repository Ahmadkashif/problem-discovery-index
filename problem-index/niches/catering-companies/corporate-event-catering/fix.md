# Corporate Procurement Compliance and Invoicing Friction

**Niche:** [[niches/catering-companies/corporate-event-catering/profile|Corporate Event Catering]]
**Industry:** [[industries/catering-companies|Catering Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Corporate clients require PO numbers, itemized invoices, W-9s, insurance certificates, and net-30 terms — but catering billing systems are designed for consumer deposits and final payments, not corporate procurement workflows.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
When a corporate client books catering, their procurement department requires a W-9, certificate of insurance, vendor registration, PO-referenced invoices, and net-30/60 payment terms. The caterer's billing system (QuickBooks, Wave, or CaterTrax invoicing) generates consumer-style invoices that do not include PO numbers, do not match the client's GL codes, and do not flow into the client's AP system. The result: 30-45 days of back-and-forth on invoice formatting, delayed payments, and corporate clients who prefer competitors with cleaner administrative processes.

## Why It's Still Broken
Catering billing tools are built for the consumer model (deposit + final payment) and treat corporate billing as an afterthought. Corporate AP systems (Coupa, SAP Ariba, Oracle) require structured data that no catering platform generates natively. The caterer's office manager manually reformats invoices, tracks PO numbers in a spreadsheet, and follows up on net-60 receivables — a process that scales poorly as corporate clients grow to 40-50% of revenue.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An invoicing module that captures the client's PO number at proposal stage, generates invoices in the format their AP system accepts (including GL code mapping), attaches required compliance documents (COI, W-9) automatically, and tracks payment against net terms with automated reminders. Integration with common corporate AP portals (Coupa, Ariba) eliminates the manual reformatting step entirely.

## Who Feels the Pain
The office manager who spends 10-15 hours/week on corporate invoice formatting and payment follow-up, and the sales director who loses clients because the administrative experience does not match the food quality.

## Impact If Fixed
Reducing average payment collection from 52 days to 30 days improves cash flow by $50K-$200K in float for a mid-size caterer, while eliminating invoice friction prevents 5-10% of corporate client churn.
