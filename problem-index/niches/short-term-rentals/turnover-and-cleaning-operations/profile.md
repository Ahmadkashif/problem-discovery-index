# Turnover & Cleaning Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B (services)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15-18% (embedded operational cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Breezeway and Turno used by larger operators, but most cleaning teams coordinate through WhatsApp and phone calls with paper or photo-based inspection checklists
**Target Buyer:** Cleaning team coordinator, STR operations manager, or independent cleaning company serving STR clients
**Automation Potential:** High — scheduling, route optimization, quality inspection, supply restocking, and cleaner payment are all rule-based and highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
STR turnover cleaning is fundamentally different from residential or commercial cleaning: the window is tight (2-6 hours between checkout and check-in), the standard is hospitality-grade (not "clean enough" but hotel-level), inspection must be photo-documented for damage protection, and scheduling changes daily based on booking patterns. A cleaning team serving 20 STR properties handles 8-15 turnovers per day with scheduling that changes completely each week. The cleaning coordinator spends 1-2 hours daily on scheduling alone — assigning cleaners to units, optimizing routes, handling last-minute booking changes that add or remove turnovers, and managing cleaner no-shows.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Breezeway and Turno (formerly TurnoverBnB) provide cleaning task management and marketplace for finding cleaners. These platforms handle scheduling and photo checklists but lack: real-time route optimization when schedules change mid-day, predictive turnover time estimation (a 3-bedroom after a week-long stay takes 2x longer than after a 1-night stay), automatic supply restocking triggers, and cleaner payment processing integrated with per-turnover pricing.
