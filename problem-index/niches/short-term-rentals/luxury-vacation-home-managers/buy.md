# Owner-Facing Property Performance Dashboard

**Niche:** [[niches/short-term-rentals/luxury-vacation-home-managers/profile|Luxury Vacation Home Managers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** AppFolio and Buildium provide property management owner portals for long-term rentals, but luxury STR owners need to see nightly revenue, occupancy rates, competitor pricing, maintenance spend, and net-of-management-fee returns — metrics that long-term rental portals don't calculate and that management companies currently deliver via monthly PDF reports compiled manually in Excel.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A luxury homeowner paying 25-35% management fees on a $150,000/year rental property expects transparent reporting: how much did the property earn this month, what occupancy rate vs. market average, how does my pricing compare to comparable listings, what was spent on maintenance, and what's my net return after management fees? Management companies compile this information manually from PMS reports (revenue, bookings), accounting software (expenses), and competitive analysis (manual PriceLabs export) into a PDF or spreadsheet emailed monthly. For a company managing 40 properties, monthly owner reporting takes 20-30 hours.

## What Already Exists
Guesty and Hostaway provide owner access portals with booking calendars and revenue summaries. AppFolio and Buildium provide owner portals for long-term rentals. None provide the luxury-specific metrics that high-value homeowners demand: competitive rate positioning, revenue per available night vs. market, maintenance cost trending, and property condition photo documentation from cleaning inspections.

## The Customization Gap
A luxury owner portal needs: (1) real-time revenue and occupancy dashboards with year-over-year comparison; (2) competitive positioning (how does my property rank against similar listings in nightly rate, occupancy, and reviews?); (3) itemized expense reporting by category (cleaning, maintenance, supplies, concierge vendor costs); (4) property condition documentation (inspection photos from each turnover); (5) net revenue calculation after management fees, OTA commissions, cleaning costs, and maintenance. The portal replaces the monthly PDF report with a self-service dashboard.

## Target Customer
Luxury STR management companies with 20+ properties whose homeowner communication is a significant operational burden — approximately 2,000-3,000 companies nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Monthly owner reporting drops from 20-30 hours to zero (self-service dashboard replaces compiled reports). Owner satisfaction improves from transparency — the leading cause of management company churn (owners switching managers) is distrust of financial reporting. Reducing owner churn by 10-15% saves $50,000-$150,000 per year in lost management fee revenue for a 40-property company.
