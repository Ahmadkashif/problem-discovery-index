# Luxury Vacation Home Managers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Lodgify or Guesty for bookings, but concierge services, property care coordination, and high-touch guest experience management remain manual and relationship-driven
**Target Buyer:** Luxury vacation rental management company owner or director managing 10-80 high-end properties ($300-$2,000+/night)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — concierge request fulfillment, property condition monitoring, and owner reporting are automatable; high-touch guest experience elements resist full automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Luxury vacation home management differs from standard STR management in three ways: (1) owner expectations are higher — homeowners with $1-5M properties expect detailed property condition reporting, transparent financial accounting, and zero tolerance for guest damage; (2) guest expectations include concierge-level service — restaurant reservations, private chef bookings, boat charters, ski equipment delivery — not just a clean room and WiFi password; (3) property maintenance complexity is greater — pools, hot tubs, smart home systems, wine cellars, and outdoor entertainment areas require specialized vendor relationships. Revenue per property is 3-5x higher than standard STR, but operational cost is also 2-3x higher.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Guesty and Lodgify handle bookings and channel management. No platform combines luxury-specific workflows: concierge request tracking and fulfillment, detailed property condition reporting with photos for owners, pre-arrival property preparation checklists for complex homes, and owner-facing financial reporting that separates management fees, maintenance costs, and net revenue by property.
