# Same-Day Turnover Coordination Across Dense Portfolios

**Niche:** [[niches/short-term-rentals/urban-multi-property-managers/profile|Urban Multi-Property Managers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** On peak turnover days, urban STR managers coordinate 15-25 same-day cleanings across a city through WhatsApp messages and phone calls — a manual dispatch process where one cleaner running late cascades into 3-4 late check-ins and triggers the negative reviews that destroy portfolio performance.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A busy Friday in a 60-unit urban STR portfolio: 18 checkouts by 11 AM, 18 turnovers to complete by 3 PM, 18 check-ins starting at 3 PM. The operations manager assigns cleaners to units based on proximity, cleaner speed, and unit complexity (studio vs. 2-bedroom). At 11:30 AM, one cleaner calls in sick; another is running 45 minutes behind because the previous guest left the unit in poor condition. The manager must reassign 3 units to other cleaners, recalculate routes, and notify guests of delayed check-in times — all through WhatsApp messages while fielding guest inquiries. A single cascading delay affects 4-5 guests and generates 1-2 negative reviews.

## Why It's Still Broken
Breezeway and Properly manage cleaning task assignment but don't provide real-time dispatch optimization with route recalculation when disruptions occur. The urban density factor (cleaners traveling between units in traffic, elevator wait times in high-rises, building access logistics) creates constraints that suburban/vacation STR cleaning platforms don't model. Real-time dispatch optimization requires GPS tracking of cleaners and dynamic rerouting — capabilities that exist in food delivery and rideshare but haven't been adapted for STR cleaning.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A real-time cleaning dispatch system that: assigns cleaners to turnovers based on proximity and unit complexity; tracks cleaner progress via GPS; automatically reroutes assignments when a cleaner falls behind or cancels; sends updated ETAs to affected guests; and alerts the operations manager only when human intervention is needed (no available reassignment, guest-facing issue). The system learns from historical turnover times per unit and cleaner to improve scheduling accuracy.

## Who Feels the Pain
Operations managers and cleaning coordinators at urban STR companies who spend 2-4 hours daily on manual dispatch coordination, and cleaners who receive chaotic reassignments via text messages throughout the day.

## Impact If Fixed
Guest-arrives-to-unready-unit incidents drop from 3-5% to under 1%. Operations manager dispatch time drops from 2-4 hours daily to 30 minutes of exception management. Cleaner satisfaction improves from predictable, optimized routes — reducing cleaner turnover, which costs $500-$1,000 per replacement in recruiting and training.
