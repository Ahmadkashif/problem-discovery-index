# Preservation-Compliant Maintenance Vendor Network

**Niche:** [[niches/short-term-rentals/heritage-and-historic-property-hosts/profile|Heritage & Historic Property Hosts]]
**Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Thumbtack and Angi connect property owners with general contractors, but historic property maintenance requires vendors who understand period-appropriate materials, preservation standards, and the Secretary of the Interior's Standards — using the wrong contractor on a registered historic property can violate preservation easements and trigger tax credit clawback.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
A plaster ceiling crack in an 1870s Victorian needs a plasterer experienced with lime plaster, not a drywall contractor who will skim-coat with modern compound (violating preservation standards). A window replacement in a National Register property must use materials compatible with the Secretary of the Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation — vinyl windows will trigger review by the State Historic Preservation Office and potentially void historic tax credits. Finding vendors who understand these constraints requires word-of-mouth referrals from preservation organizations — there's no searchable directory of preservation-qualified contractors by trade and location.

## What Already Exists
Thumbtack, Angi, and HomeAdvisor connect property owners with contractors by trade and location. The National Trust for Historic Preservation provides general guidance. State Historic Preservation Offices (SHPOs) sometimes maintain informal referral lists. None provide a searchable, reviewed network of preservation-qualified vendors organized by trade, location, and period specialty.

## The Customization Gap
A preservation vendor network needs: (1) contractor profiles with preservation qualifications (SHPO-approved projects, period specialties, material expertise); (2) matching by property era and architectural style (a contractor experienced with Federal-era buildings may not know Craftsman-era techniques); (3) compliance documentation (work descriptions and photo documentation formatted for SHPO review); (4) integration with historic tax credit requirements (documenting that repairs meet the Secretary of the Interior's Standards).

## Target Customer
Historic property owners and STR operators managing registered historic buildings — approximately 40,000-60,000 property owners nationally (not all STR, but all need preservation-qualified maintenance).

## Impact If Solved
Property owners find preservation-qualified contractors in days instead of weeks of networking. Improper repairs that trigger SHPO violations and tax credit clawback (potentially $50,000-$200,000 in recaptured credits) are prevented. Maintenance costs may increase 10-20% using qualified vendors, but the preservation compliance and property value protection justify the premium.
