# Automated Occupancy Tax Calculation and Filing for STR

**Niche:** [[niches/short-term-rentals/regulatory-compliance-tracking/profile|Regulatory Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Avalara automates sales tax for e-commerce, and Airbnb collects occupancy tax in some jurisdictions, but STR operators in the 2,000+ jurisdictions where platforms don't collect tax must calculate, file, and remit occupancy taxes manually — each jurisdiction with different rates, filing periods, exemptions, and portal logins.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
An STR operator with 15 properties across 4 counties in Tennessee must file: state sales tax (7%), county hotel/motel tax (varying by county, 2.5-5%), city occupancy tax (where applicable, 1-3%), and a tourism development zone tax in one county (additional 2%). Each has a different filing period (monthly, quarterly, annually), different filing portal, and different exemption rules (stays over 30 days exempt from some taxes but not others). Monthly tax compliance across 4 jurisdictions takes 4-8 hours. Errors in calculation or late filing produce penalties of 5-25% of tax owed.

## What Already Exists
Avalara provides automated tax calculation for e-commerce. Lodging Tax provides STR-specific tax calculation for some jurisdictions. Airbnb collects and remits occupancy taxes in approximately 1,000 jurisdictions but does NOT collect in thousands of others — and Vrbo and direct booking channels never collect. The operator is responsible for tax on non-Airbnb bookings in all jurisdictions and for all bookings in non-collected jurisdictions.

## The Customization Gap
An STR occupancy tax tool needs to: (1) aggregate booking revenue by jurisdiction from all channels (Airbnb, Vrbo, Booking.com, direct bookings); (2) calculate applicable taxes by jurisdiction with correct rate, exemptions, and filing period; (3) net out taxes already collected by Airbnb from total obligation; (4) generate filing-ready returns for each jurisdiction; (5) support e-filing or provide populated form PDFs for manual submission. The jurisdiction-specific rate database and Airbnb-collected-tax netting are the STR-specific requirements that generic tax tools miss.

## Target Customer
STR operators filing occupancy taxes in jurisdictions where Airbnb doesn't collect — approximately 50,000-100,000 operators nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Monthly tax compliance drops from 4-8 hours to 30-60 minutes across multiple jurisdictions. Late filing penalties ($500-$5,000/year for a typical operator) eliminated. Tax under-payment risk (audit exposure) reduced through accurate multi-jurisdiction rate application.
