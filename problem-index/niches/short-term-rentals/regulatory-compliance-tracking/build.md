# Multi-Jurisdiction STR Regulatory Change Monitoring

**Niche:** [[niches/short-term-rentals/regulatory-compliance-tracking/profile|Regulatory Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/short-term-rentals|Short-Term Rentals]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool monitors municipal council agendas, ordinance updates, and zoning changes across the 5,000+ US jurisdictions with STR regulations — operators learn about new rules when they receive violation notices, not when the rules are proposed or adopted.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #automation #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
A multi-market STR operator with 40 properties across 8 cities discovers that City A passed a new ordinance 3 months ago requiring all STR hosts to obtain a fire inspection certificate and post the certificate number on their listing. The operator missed the ordinance change (it was buried in a city council meeting agenda) and now faces $2,000 in fines and a 30-day compliance deadline. Meanwhile, City B is proposing a cap on STR permits that would prevent the operator from renewing 3 of their 7 permits in that market — but they don't know about the proposal because they don't attend city council meetings in 8 different cities.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
STR regulations are enacted at the municipal level — 5,000+ jurisdictions with separate council agendas, ordinance databases, and zoning codes, each in different formats. Monitoring these requires scraping city council agenda PDFs, parsing ordinance text for STR-relevant changes, and classifying changes by impact type (new permit requirement, tax rate change, zoning restriction, enforcement enhancement). Granicus does this for municipalities (selling enforcement tools) but not for operators. The data assembly challenge — monitoring 5,000+ municipal websites for regulatory changes — is massive.

## What to Build
A regulatory change monitoring service that: scrapes city council agendas and ordinance databases for STR-related proposals and adopted changes; classifies changes by type and severity (new requirement, amendment, enforcement change); alerts operators when a jurisdiction where they operate has a pending or adopted change; provides a plain-language summary of the change and required actions; tracks compliance deadlines for adopted changes. Start with the top 200 STR markets (covering 80%+ of professional operator properties) and expand.

## Target Customer
Multi-market STR operators with properties in 3+ jurisdictions — approximately 5,000-10,000 operators nationally. Average contract value: $100-$300/month.

## Impact If Built
Operators learn about regulatory changes at the proposal stage (4-8 months before enforcement) instead of at the violation stage. Compliance fines ($500-$5,000 per violation) are avoided. Operators proactively adjust their portfolios when jurisdictions become hostile to STR — selling or converting properties before permits are denied rather than after.
