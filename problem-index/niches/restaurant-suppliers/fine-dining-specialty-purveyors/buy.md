# CRM Adapted for Consultative Chef Relationships

**Niche:** [[niches/restaurant-suppliers/fine-dining-specialty-purveyors/profile|Fine Dining Specialty Purveyors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/restaurant-suppliers|Restaurant Suppliers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CRM platforms track contacts and deals, but specialty purveyor sales reps need to track each chef's culinary direction, current menu themes, seasonal preferences, sustainability commitments, and personal taste — relationship intelligence that determines which products to present and how.
**Tags:** #nlp #recommendation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A specialty purveyor's sales rep visits 20-30 executive chefs weekly. Each conversation is consultative: the rep presents new products, discusses upcoming menu changes, suggests pairings, and listens for signals about what the chef is looking for next season. The rep's notebook contains entries like: "Chef Maria is shifting toward whole-animal butchery for spring menu — source heritage pork and lamb. Prefers small farms, USDA organic not required but humane certified. Interested in offal cuts for tasting menu. Doesn't want anything from California right now (former business partner issues)." This relationship intelligence drives the rep's product recommendations and is the foundation of the consultative value. It lives entirely in the rep's personal notebook and memory.

## What Already Exists
Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zoho CRM provide contact management, activity tracking, and notes. None structure the specific relationship data a specialty food rep needs: chef's culinary philosophy, current menu direction, ingredient preferences and avoidances, sourcing standards (organic, humane, local, sustainable), past product feedback, and personal relationship notes.

## The Customization Gap
A specialty purveyor CRM needs: (1) chef profile with structured fields for culinary philosophy, menu direction, sourcing standards, and ingredient preferences/avoidances, (2) product recommendation engine that matches available products to chef profiles ("Heritage breed pork belly available — relevant for: Chef Maria [whole-animal], Chef James [charcuterie program], Chef Lin [ramen special]"), (3) visit note templates that capture culinary intelligence ("menu changing to: ___, interested in: ___, avoiding: ___"), (4) product feedback tracking ("Chef Maria tried the Berkshire pork — excellent, wants 20 lbs weekly; found the goat too gamey for her clientele"), and (5) seasonal planning view showing each chef's typical menu rotation and upcoming product needs.

## Target Customer
Sales managers at specialty purveyors with 2+ outside sales reps serving fine dining accounts. Approximately 300-500 US purveyors at this scale. Price point: $100-$200/rep/month.

## Impact If Solved
Product-to-chef matching accuracy improves — reps present relevant products 80-90% of the time vs. 60-70% with memory alone. New rep onboarding accelerated from 6-12 months to 2-3 months using existing chef profiles. Chef relationship continuity maintained when reps leave — the next rep inherits structured intelligence rather than starting from scratch. Cross-sell revenue increases 10-15% through systematic preference matching.
