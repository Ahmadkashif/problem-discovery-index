# Sales Territory Optimization

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/restaurant-suppliers|Restaurant Suppliers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-7B (embedded — sales labor and revenue optimization across independent restaurant supply)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8% of operating costs dedicated to sales team management
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — sales managers assign territories and visit schedules manually; no independent restaurant supplier uses ML-driven territory optimization, visit prioritization, or account assignment balancing
**Target Buyer:** Sales Manager / VP Sales responsible for sales team productivity and territory assignment
**Automation Potential:** High — territory assignment, visit prioritization, and account churn detection are well-defined optimization problems solvable with existing ERP data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Sales territory optimization in restaurant supply is a high-leverage problem because the sales rep is the primary customer retention mechanism. An independent supplier's competitive advantage against Sysco and US Foods is the personal relationship between the rep and the chef — but this relationship is only effective if the rep visits the right accounts at the right frequency with the right information. Currently, territory assignment is done when a rep is hired ("you get the north side"), visit scheduling is based on the rep's personal judgment, and account prioritization is by revenue size (visit the biggest accounts most often). This approach misallocates 20-30% of sales effort: reps visit stable high-revenue accounts weekly (who do not need the attention) while visiting struggling mid-size accounts monthly (who need weekly touch to prevent churn).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Sales managers track territory performance in spreadsheets and hold weekly ride-alongs to evaluate rep effectiveness. No independent restaurant supplier uses territory optimization software (tools like Xactly, Varicent, and Territory.io serve enterprise SaaS sales, not food distribution). Route optimization tools (used for delivery) are not applied to sales routes. The sales manager's territory decisions — who gets which accounts, which accounts need more visits, which reps are under/over-visiting — are made from experience rather than data.
