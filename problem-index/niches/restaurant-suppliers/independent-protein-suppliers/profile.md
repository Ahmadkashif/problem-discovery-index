**Market Size:** $20-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%

**Target Buyer:** Owner / Sales Manager at an independent protein supplier ($5M-$100M revenue)
**Automation Potential:** High — custom-cut order accuracy, protein procurement timing, and customer yield education are automatable but require integration with cutting room operations

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent protein suppliers are the backbone of restaurant protein sourcing outside the Sysco/US Foods duopoly. These companies purchase boxed beef, pork, poultry, and seafood from packers and processors, then add value through custom cutting (portioning steaks to the chef's specification), dry aging, marination, and specialty preparation (frenched lamb racks, custom sausage blends). The sales relationship is intensely personal — a protein sales rep visits the same chef weekly, knows their menu by memory, and serves as an advisor on protein selection, preparation, and yield. Switching suppliers means the chef loses this advisory relationship and must rebuild trust with a new rep who understands their menu and standards. This relationship depth is the competitive moat against Sysco, but it also means the business's revenue is concentrated in the sales reps' personal networks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Protein-specific ERPs handle catch-weight inventory and invoicing. CRM adoption is near zero — reps track accounts in personal notebooks. Order intake happens via phone call and text message, with the office staff manually entering orders into the ERP. No tool helps the sales rep track customer-level order patterns, anticipate needs, or systematically identify cross-sell opportunities across the protein portfolio.
