# CRM Adapted for Protein Sales Rep Workflows

**Niche:** [[niches/restaurant-suppliers/independent-protein-suppliers/profile|Independent Protein Suppliers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/restaurant-suppliers|Restaurant Suppliers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CRM platforms (Salesforce, HubSpot) track pipeline stages and contact info, but protein sales reps need to see each chef's order history, custom-cut preferences, menu items, and protein price sensitivity — data that lives in the ERP and the rep's head, not the CRM.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #recommendation #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A protein sales rep managing 80-120 restaurant accounts visits each account weekly or biweekly. Before each visit, an effective rep reviews: what the account ordered last week (any changes in volume or product mix?), what they ordered this time last year (seasonal menu changes coming?), which products they buy from us vs. competitors (where can we grow wallet share?), and what the current market offers in terms of value (is there a great deal on short loins this week that this chef should know about?). Currently, this pre-visit intelligence assembly takes 10-15 minutes per account and requires checking the ERP for order history, reviewing personal notes for menu and preference information, and checking market reports for pricing opportunities.

## What Already Exists
Salesforce and HubSpot provide contact management, activity logging, and mobile access. Neither integrates with food distribution ERP systems to surface customer order data. Pepper (formerly Handshake) and BlueCart provide order-taking tools but not the sales intelligence the rep needs before the visit.

## The Customization Gap
A protein sales CRM needs: (1) ERP integration that surfaces per-account order history, product mix, and trend data within the CRM contact view, (2) pre-visit briefing cards auto-generated from order analysis ("Tony's Pizzeria: protein spend down 12% vs. 90-day average, stopped ordering lamb 4 weeks ago, still buying all seafood from us"), (3) market-driven opportunity alerts ("Choice beef strip loins dropped $1.50/lb this week — Tony's uses 30 lbs/week, mention the savings"), (4) custom-cut spec library per account accessible during visits for spec verification, and (5) competitor intelligence capture (rep logs which competing supplier's truck they saw at the restaurant).

## Target Customer
Sales managers at independent protein suppliers with 3+ outside sales reps. Approximately 1,500-2,500 US suppliers at this scale. Price point: $75-$150/rep/month.

## Impact If Solved
Rep visit effectiveness improves 20-30% through pre-visit intelligence. Account retention improves as reps detect and respond to churn signals 4-6 weeks earlier. Cross-sell revenue increases 8-12% as market-driven opportunities are systematically presented to relevant accounts. Combined annual impact of $150K-$500K per supplier in retained and expanded revenue.
