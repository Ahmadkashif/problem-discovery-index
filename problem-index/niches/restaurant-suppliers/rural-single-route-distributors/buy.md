# Invoicing App Adapted for Route-Based Delivery Operations

**Niche:** [[niches/restaurant-suppliers/rural-single-route-distributors/profile|Rural Single-Route Distributors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/restaurant-suppliers|Restaurant Suppliers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Invoicing apps (QuickBooks, Wave, FreshBooks) generate invoices but do not support the delivery-route workflow — generating a batch of invoices organized by stop sequence, recording proof of delivery and payment at each stop, and reconciling the route at end of day.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A single-route operator generates 15-25 invoices per delivery day. With QuickBooks, each invoice is created individually — enter customer, add line items, calculate total, print or email. For 20 stops, this is 60-90 minutes of invoice preparation before the route starts. During the route, the operator must track which invoices are paid at delivery and which are left for next-visit payment. At end of day, the operator reconciles cash and checks against the invoice list. QuickBooks can do each step individually, but the workflow is not route-organized — there is no "build today's route invoices, print in stop order, record payments at each stop, reconcile at end of route."

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks Online handles invoicing, payment recording, and basic accounting. Wave provides free invoicing with similar capability. Route accounting systems (Rutherford & Associates, Encompass) handle delivery-based invoicing but are designed for multi-route operations with warehouse staff and cost $200-$500/month — 5-10x what a single-route operator can justify.

## The Customization Gap
A route-based invoicing adaptation needs: (1) batch invoice generation organized by route and stop sequence (select today's customers, auto-populate from their standing order or last order, generate all invoices in stop order), (2) route-view invoice list showing each stop with invoice total, payment status, and outstanding balance, (3) at-stop payment recording (tap: paid cash, paid check #, partial payment, will pay next time), (4) end-of-route reconciliation comparing collected cash/checks against invoiced amounts, and (5) customer statement generation for accounts with outstanding balances. All from a mobile device with offline capability.

## Target Customer
Single-route operators currently using handwritten invoices or basic QuickBooks. Approximately 8,000-12,000 US operators. Price point: $20-$40/month.

## Impact If Solved
Invoice preparation time reduced from 60-90 minutes to 15-20 minutes through batch generation and standing order templates. Payment collection accuracy improves — the operator knows at each stop what is owed and can address outstanding balances. End-of-day reconciliation drops from 30-45 minutes to 10 minutes. Accounts receivable visibility reduces aging receivables from 20-30 days average to 10-15 days.
