# Produce ERP with Restaurant-Specific Quality Standards

**Niche:** [[niches/restaurant-suppliers/produce-specialty-suppliers/profile|Produce & Specialty Suppliers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/restaurant-suppliers|Restaurant Suppliers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Produce ERPs (Produce Pro, Edible Software) track lots and invoicing, but do not maintain per-restaurant quality standards — Chef A wants large, vine-ripe tomatoes while Chef B wants small, firm Romas for sauce, and the system treats both as "tomatoes."
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #quick-win

## The Problem
A restaurant produce supplier serves 100 chefs, each with specific quality preferences that go beyond USDA grade standards. Chef A wants heirloom tomatoes that are 80% ripe for tomorrow's salad. Chef B wants the same heirloom tomatoes at 60% ripe because they will use them in 3 days. Chef C wants only red heirlooms, not yellows. These preferences are known by the packer who builds each restaurant's order — but they exist only in the packer's memory. When the regular packer is out, the substitute packs to generic standards, and three chefs call to complain. At scale (100+ accounts with 20-50 items each), the packer carries 2,000-5,000 preference entries mentally.

## What Already Exists
Produce Pro and Edible Software track lots, pricing, and customer-level pricing tiers. Neither maintains per-customer product quality preferences at the item level. The packing slip says "10 cs Heirloom Tomatoes" — it does not say "Chef A prefers vine-ripe, large, assorted colors, no green shoulders."

## The Customization Gap
A restaurant produce ERP adaptation needs: (1) per-customer quality preference profiles at the item level (ripeness, size, color, variety preferences stored alongside the order), (2) packing slip annotations that display customer preferences to the packer (printed or tablet-displayed during packing), (3) substitution rules per customer (Chef A accepts grape tomatoes as a sub; Chef B does not — when heirlooms are unavailable, the system suggests different substitutes for each), and (4) preference capture workflows that let the sales rep record chef preferences during visits ("Tony mentioned he wants smaller basil bunches — update his spec").

## Target Customer
Operations managers at produce suppliers serving 50+ restaurant accounts with custom quality requirements. Approximately 1,000-2,000 US produce suppliers at this scale.

## Impact If Solved
Packing accuracy for customer-specific preferences improves from 85-90% to 97%+. Quality-related complaints reduced by 60-70%, retaining $50K-$200K in revenue from accounts that would otherwise churn due to quality inconsistency. Substitute packer capability improves — any packer can pack any customer's order using the system's guidance rather than relying on one person's memory.
