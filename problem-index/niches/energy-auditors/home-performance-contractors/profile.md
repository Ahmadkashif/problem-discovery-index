# Home Performance Contractors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/energy-auditors|Energy Auditors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M-1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~16-24%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most home performance contractors use paper field notes, personal photos, and desktop energy modeling software; mobile-first field tools are rarely adopted
**Target Buyer:** Home performance business owner / lead auditor
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Residential audits follow repeatable patterns, but the wide variety of home types and conditions requires judgment that resists full automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Home performance contractors (HPCs) are the frontline of residential energy efficiency — they audit homes, recommend retrofits, and often perform the installation work themselves (insulation, air sealing, HVAC upgrades, duct sealing). Unlike utility program auditors who follow prescribed protocols, HPCs operate in the private market where the audit must also sell the retrofit work. The auditor is simultaneously a building scientist, a salesperson, and a project estimator. Their technology needs span field data collection, energy modeling, sales proposal generation, project estimation, and permit documentation — a workflow that no single tool addresses. Most HPCs are 1-5 person companies where the owner is the lead auditor, the salesperson, and the installer.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TREAT and Ekotrope handle energy modeling. Generic CRM tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) manage scheduling and invoicing but have no audit-specific features. Proposal tools (PandaDoc, Proposify) generate documents but cannot pull from energy model outputs. Photo documentation uses personal phone cameras with no structured organization. The HPC uses 4-6 disconnected tools and a paper notebook, spending more time on data management than on building science.
