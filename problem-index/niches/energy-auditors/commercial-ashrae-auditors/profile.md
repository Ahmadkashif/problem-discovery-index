# Commercial ASHRAE Auditors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/energy-auditors|Energy Auditors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — eQUEST, EnergyPlus, and Trane TRACE model commercial buildings, but field data collection and report formatting remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Commercial building owner / ESCO principal / facilities manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — ASHRAE audits follow structured levels (I/II/III) with defined deliverables, but the complexity of commercial HVAC systems and building envelopes limits full automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
ASHRAE Level I, II, and III audits for commercial buildings (offices, retail, warehouses, institutional) represent the highest per-project revenue in energy auditing — Level II audits bill $10K-$50K and Level III audits $50K-$200K. These audits evaluate complex HVAC systems (chillers, cooling towers, boilers, VAV boxes, BAS controls), lighting systems with hundreds of fixture types, building envelope assemblies with varying insulation and glazing, and plug load profiles that differ by tenant and use case. The deliverable is a detailed engineering report with energy conservation measures (ECMs), cost estimates, savings projections, and financial analysis (simple payback, IRR, NPV). Each report requires 40-200 hours of engineering time, with a significant portion spent reformatting data between field notes, modeling software, and the final report template.

## Current Tools & Gaps
eQUEST and EnergyPlus handle energy modeling but require manual input of hundreds of building parameters. ASHRAE's Standard 211 defines audit procedures but provides no software toolchain. Lighting audit tools (Lighting Analysts' AGi32, Cree lighting calculators) are component-specific. No platform integrates field data collection, energy modeling, ECM analysis, and ASHRAE-compliant report generation into a single workflow.
