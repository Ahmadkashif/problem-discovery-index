# In-Home Audit Communication Support Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/energy-auditors/non-english-homeowner-audits/profile|Non-English Homeowner Audits]]
**Industry:** [[industries/energy-auditors|Energy Auditors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Real-time translation tools exist for general conversation, but none handle the technical vocabulary of in-home energy audits where the auditor must explain building science concepts to a non-English-speaking homeowner.
**Tags:** #llm #nlp #speech-processing #worker-facing

## The Problem
During a 2-3 hour home audit, the auditor needs to communicate constantly with the homeowner: explaining why they are opening the attic hatch, asking about the home's heating history, describing what the blower door test measures, requesting permission to access the crawlspace, and discussing preliminary findings. When the auditor speaks English and the homeowner speaks Spanish (or Mandarin, Vietnamese, etc.), this communication breaks down. Generic real-time translation apps (Google Translate voice mode) handle conversational requests ("Where is your furnace?") but fail on technical explanations ("Your duct system is losing 30% of conditioned air through disconnected joints in the attic").

## What Already Exists
Real-time translation apps (Google Translate, Apple Translate, Microsoft Translator) provide voice-to-voice translation for general conversation. Medical interpretation services (Martti, AMN Language Services) provide on-demand video interpretation for healthcare settings. Specialized translation glossaries exist for legal and medical domains. No real-time translation tool has been trained on building science, energy efficiency, or home improvement terminology.

## The Customization Gap
Energy audit in-home communication needs a real-time translation tool with: a building science domain vocabulary in the top 5 LEP languages, contextual translations that explain concepts rather than just translating words ("air sealing" becomes not just the literal translation but a brief explanation of what it means and why it matters), visual aids that the auditor can show on a tablet alongside spoken translation (diagrams of heat loss, before/after insulation images), and the ability to handle the back-and-forth of a home walkthrough conversation at natural speed.

## Target Customer
Energy auditors working in LEP communities who currently either decline audits for non-English households, bring a community interpreter (adding $100-$200 per audit), or attempt to communicate through gestures and broken phrases.

## Impact If Solved
Enables any English-speaking auditor to conduct effective home audits with LEP homeowners, expanding the addressable market by 15-20% without requiring bilingual staffing. Eliminates the $100-$200 per-audit interpreter cost for firms that currently provide interpretation.
