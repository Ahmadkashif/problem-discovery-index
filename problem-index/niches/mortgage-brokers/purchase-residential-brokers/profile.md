# Purchase-Focused Residential Brokers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/mortgage-brokers|Mortgage Brokers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $250-300B in annual origination volume
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~55% of broker originations
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Encompass and Byte handle loan origination workflows; CRM tools automate borrower communication; but lender selection intelligence, pipeline risk monitoring, and referral partner analytics are manual
**Target Buyer:** Brokerage owner or senior loan officer at a purchase-focused residential mortgage brokerage
**Automation Potential:** High — Purchase transactions follow predictable timelines with clear milestones, contract deadlines, and decision points that enable pipeline automation and risk prediction

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Purchase-focused brokers operate under time pressure that refinance brokers don't face — purchase contracts have hard close deadlines, and a missed closing date can kill the deal and expose the borrower to legal liability. This creates a pipeline management challenge: the broker must track 20-50 active files simultaneously, each at a different processing stage, each with a different close deadline, and each vulnerable to different risk factors (appraisal shortfall, title issues, employment verification delays). Senior loan officers manage this complexity through experience and personal tracking systems. Junior loan officers miss critical dates, fail to anticipate common delays, and lose deals that experienced brokers would have saved.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Encompass and Byte provide milestone tracking and task management but without intelligent risk prediction — they show where each file is, not which files are likely to have problems. CRM tools (Jungo, Surefire) automate borrower communication on a fixed schedule, not triggered by pipeline risk signals. Pricing engines (Optimal Blue, Mortech) provide rate comparisons but not lender selection intelligence. The gap is decision intelligence: which files need attention today, which lender to submit to, and when to lock the rate.
