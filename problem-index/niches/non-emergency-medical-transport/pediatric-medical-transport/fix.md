# Parent/Guardian Consent and Communication Gap

**Niche:** [[niches/non-emergency-medical-transport/pediatric-medical-transport/profile|Pediatric Medical Transport]]
**Industry:** [[industries/non-emergency-medical-transport|Non-Emergency Medical Transport]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Parents of pediatric patients have no visibility into who is driving their child, when the vehicle will arrive, or whether the trip is proceeding as planned — creating anxiety and compliance gaps.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
When a Medicaid-covered child needs transport to a specialist appointment, the parent typically receives a confirmation call 24 hours before the trip and nothing else. On the day of transport, the parent does not know: the driver's name or credentials, the vehicle description, the exact arrival time, or whether the trip has been delayed or reassigned. For parents of medically complex children (ventilator-dependent, seizure-prone), this opacity creates significant anxiety — they are entrusting their vulnerable child to an unknown driver with no real-time information. When the vehicle is late, the parent calls dispatch repeatedly, tying up scheduling staff. If the parent is not at the pickup location when the driver arrives (common when parents step inside a multi-family building), the driver waits or marks a no-show, and the child misses their appointment.

## Why It's Still Broken
NEMT providers have historically communicated only with the entity paying for the trip (the Medicaid broker or healthcare facility), not with the patient or family directly. Building parent-facing communication requires collecting and managing phone numbers, handling text message opt-in consent (TCPA compliance), and integrating real-time vehicle tracking into a parent-facing interface — technology investments that NEMT providers have not prioritized. The cost-reimbursement model of Medicaid NEMT does not incentivize customer experience improvements that do not directly reduce costs.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated parent notification system that sends: (1) a trip confirmation 24 hours before with driver name, photo, vehicle description, and credentials, (2) a real-time tracking link 15 minutes before pickup showing vehicle position and ETA, (3) an "arriving now" alert when the vehicle is 2 minutes away, and (4) a trip completion notification when the child arrives at the destination. Parents can respond to notifications with questions or changes. The system is SMS-based (no app download required) with TCPA-compliant opt-in. For medically complex patients, the notification includes driver training certifications relevant to the child's conditions.

## Who Feels the Pain
Parents of pediatric patients who experience anxiety and helplessness during their child's medical transport, dispatchers who field 5-15 daily "where is the driver?" calls from parents, and drivers who wait for parents unaware of their arrival.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces parent-initiated dispatch calls by 70-80%, decreases parent-not-present no-shows by 50%, and provides the transparency that parents of vulnerable children deserve — potentially becoming a competitive differentiator for NEMT providers pursuing pediatric-heavy MCO contracts.
