# Fall Risk Communication from Healthcare to NEMT

**Niche:** [[niches/non-emergency-medical-transport/elderly-homebound-populations/profile|Elderly Homebound Populations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/non-emergency-medical-transport|Non-Emergency Medical Transport]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Healthcare providers assess elderly fall risk at every visit, but this critical safety information never reaches the NEMT driver who physically assists the patient into a vehicle.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Every primary care visit for an elderly patient includes a fall risk assessment (Timed Up and Go test, Morse Fall Scale, or similar). When a high-risk patient is transported by NEMT, the driver — who physically assists the patient walking to the vehicle, navigating steps, and boarding — has no knowledge of this assessment. A patient classified as "high fall risk" by their physician is handled identically to a patient with no fall risk by the NEMT driver. Falls during NEMT-related activities (walking to/from vehicles, boarding/alighting) are the most common adverse events in medical transport, and elderly patients are the most frequently injured.

## What Already Exists
Electronic Health Records (Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth) store fall risk assessments as structured data. Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) enable data sharing between healthcare entities. Care coordination platforms (CarePort, Netsmart) share patient data between settings. NEMT dispatch systems manage trip assignments. None of these systems share fall risk data from healthcare providers to NEMT drivers.

## The Customization Gap
NEMT needs a lightweight, HIPAA-compliant data bridge that receives fall risk scores from EHR/HIE systems and surfaces them to drivers at the point of care — not as a detailed clinical report but as a practical safety alert: "HIGH FALL RISK — assist walking on both sides, use gait belt, extra time for stairs." The system must handle consent (patient must authorize sharing), format translation (clinical scores to driver-actionable guidance), and integration with NEMT driver tablets. The information must be current — a patient's fall risk may change between visits.

## Target Customer
NEMT providers serving elderly populations in partnership with health systems and Area Agencies on Aging, where fall prevention is a quality metric tied to Medicaid contract performance.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces transport-related falls among elderly patients by an estimated 30-40% through informed driver behavior, decreases the liability exposure from fall-related injuries (average claim: $25K-$75K), and positions the NEMT provider as a care-coordinated partner rather than a commodity transportation vendor.
