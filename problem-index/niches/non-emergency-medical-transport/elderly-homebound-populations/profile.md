# Elderly Homebound Populations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/non-emergency-medical-transport|Non-Emergency Medical Transport]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Elderly patients and their caregivers book rides through phone calls; digital scheduling tools are inaccessible to the primary user population
**Target Buyer:** Home health agency director / Area Agency on Aging coordinator / senior living facility administrator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Recurring appointment patterns and stable patient populations enable predictive scheduling, but the door-through-door assistance requirements add non-automatable human touchpoints

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Elderly homebound patients — the fastest-growing NEMT segment as the 65+ population increases — require fundamentally different service than ambulatory or standard wheelchair patients. These patients often need door-through-door assistance (not just curb-to-curb), including help navigating stairs, managing walkers and oxygen equipment, and communicating with facility reception staff. Many have cognitive impairments (mild dementia, confusion) that require drivers with patience and basic care skills beyond standard NEMT training. Falls during transport-related activities (getting in/out of vehicles, walking to vehicles) are the most common adverse event in NEMT, and elderly patients are disproportionately affected. The emotional dimension is significant: for homebound elderly patients, the NEMT driver may be the only non-family human contact they have in a day.

## Current Tools & Gaps
NEMT dispatch systems flag patients as "ambulatory" or "wheelchair" but have no category for "ambulatory with door-through-door assistance" — the service level that most elderly homebound patients actually need. No scheduling tool accounts for the extra 10-15 minutes that door-through-door service requires per pickup. Area Agencies on Aging coordinate Medicaid NEMT but have no integration with provider dispatch systems. Fall risk assessment is performed by healthcare providers but never communicated to NEMT drivers.
