# Behavioral Health Transport

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/non-emergency-medical-transport|Non-Emergency Medical Transport]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $600-900M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Behavioral health facilities coordinate transport through social workers making individual phone calls; no digital tools address the safety protocols specific to behavioral health patient transport
**Target Buyer:** Behavioral health facility administrator / crisis services coordinator / Medicaid MCO behavioral health director
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — Safety protocols and de-escalation requirements add manual, judgment-based steps that resist automation, but scheduling and documentation can be streamlined

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Behavioral health patients — those attending outpatient substance use treatment, psychiatric appointments, partial hospitalization programs, and residential facility transfers — present unique transport challenges that standard NEMT services are not designed to handle. Patients may be in acute emotional distress, under the influence of substances, experiencing psychotic symptoms, or at risk of self-harm during transport. Drivers need de-escalation training, awareness of trauma-informed communication, and clear protocols for managing unsafe situations (patient wants to exit a moving vehicle, verbal threats, self-harm behavior). Many behavioral health patients also face stigma from standard NEMT drivers who lack training and comfort with this population, resulting in patient complaints and refusals to serve.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard NEMT dispatch systems have no behavioral health flags, safety protocols, or driver certification tracking for de-escalation training. Behavioral health facilities use social workers to individually call NEMT providers and verbally communicate patient safety considerations — a process that takes 15-30 minutes per trip and relies on the social worker's judgment about what to share. No tool supports the safety communication workflow between behavioral health clinicians and NEMT drivers while respecting HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2 substance use treatment confidentiality rules.
