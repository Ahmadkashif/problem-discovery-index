# Large-Scale Row Crop Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/crop-farming|Crop Farming]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $120-140B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~52%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — GPS guidance, yield monitors, and variable-rate controllers are standard on large operations, but the data these systems generate rarely drives automated agronomic decisions
**Target Buyer:** Farm Owner or Operations Manager at row crop operations (1,000-10,000+ acres, corn/soybeans/wheat/cotton)
**Automation Potential:** High — input prescription generation, harvest timing optimization, and grain marketing decisions are all automatable with existing data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Large-scale row crop operations (1,000-10,000+ acres of corn, soybeans, wheat, or cotton) are the backbone of US agriculture and the primary adopters of precision agriculture hardware. These operations generate massive datasets — yield maps, planting maps, soil test data, weather data, satellite imagery — but the farmer's ability to act on this data is limited by agronomic expertise and time. The operator is managing 20-50 fields across multiple landlords, each field with its own soil characteristics, yield history, and input response curve. Agronomic decisions (what to plant, when to plant, what inputs to apply, when to harvest, when to sell) are made by the farmer using experienced judgment informed by data but not driven by it. The tacit knowledge challenge is extreme: an experienced farmer reads a field's crop canopy and knows within 5 bushels/acre what the yield will be, senses weather risk from cloud patterns and wind shifts, and times grain sales based on an intuitive reading of market conditions developed over decades.

## Current Tools & Gaps
John Deere Operations Center, Climate FieldView (Bayer), and Trimble Ag Software aggregate machine data and display yield maps. These platforms collect data but do not make recommendations. Crop consulting companies (agronomists) provide field-specific advice but cover only 3-5 farm visits per year per client. No platform closes the loop from data → recommendation → execution at the field level — the farmer remains the decision engine.
