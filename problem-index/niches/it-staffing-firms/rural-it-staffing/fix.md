# Broadband and Infrastructure Reality Checks

**Niche:** [[niches/it-staffing-firms/rural-it-staffing/profile|Rural & Small-Market IT Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Rural IT placements fail because neither the staffing firm nor the candidate verifies whether the employer's site has adequate internet, modern hardware, and basic IT infrastructure until the contractor arrives on-site.
**Tags:** #quick-win #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A rural IT staffing firm places a network administrator at a county government office 90 miles from the nearest metro area. The contractor arrives to discover the site runs on a 10 Mbps DSL connection shared by 40 employees, the "server room" is an unlocked closet with a consumer-grade router, and the "IT budget" is $2,000/year. The contractor cannot perform the work as scoped, the client blames the staffing firm for sending someone who "cannot handle" the environment, and the placement fails. This scenario repeats across rural hospitals, school districts, and municipal offices where IT infrastructure expectations are miscalibrated.

## Why It's Still Broken
Rural employers often do not know what they have — they describe their setup in aspirational rather than factual terms ("we have a server" means "we have a 10-year-old Dell tower under a desk"). Staffing firms take job requirements at face value without auditing the client's technical environment because on-site assessments are impractical when clients are 60-120 miles apart. There is no standardized pre-placement infrastructure checklist for IT staffing, and creating one is not part of any ATS workflow.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A client-completed infrastructure survey (web form, 15-20 questions) that the staffing firm sends before placement, covering: internet speed (with speed test verification link), hardware inventory, software versions, IT budget, physical security of server/network equipment, and existing vendor relationships. Results auto-generate a "placement environment brief" shared with the contractor before they accept the assignment, eliminating surprise discoveries on day one.

## Who Feels the Pain
IT contractors placed in rural assignments who discover unusable work environments, and rural staffing firm owners whose reputation depends on successful placements in a market where every client relationship is critical.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces infrastructure-related placement failures from 20-25% to under 5%, saving $10K-$25K per failed placement. For a rural staffing firm making 60-100 placements/year, that is $120K-$500K in preserved revenue.
