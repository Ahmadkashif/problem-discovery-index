# Veteran IT Career Placement

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Veteran-focused job boards exist (Hire Heroes USA, RecruitMilitary) but do not integrate with staffing firm workflows or translate military experience into civilian IT role requirements.
**Target Buyer:** Veteran transition program coordinators at staffing firms, or owners of veteran-focused IT placement agencies.
**Automation Potential:** High — Military-to-civilian skill translation follows structured mapping rules (MOS/AFSC codes to civilian IT certifications and role titles).

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Veterans transitioning out of military IT roles (signal corps, cyber operations, communications, intelligence systems) possess substantial technical skills but describe them in military terminology that civilian ATS systems cannot parse. An Army 25B (Information Technology Specialist) with experience in "SIPR/NIPR network administration" and "DISA STIG compliance" is functionally equivalent to a "Network Administrator with security hardening experience" but will not surface in keyword searches. The translation problem is bidirectional: veterans cannot identify which civilian roles match their military experience, and civilian recruiters cannot evaluate military qualifications.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Hire Heroes USA and American Corporate Partners provide career coaching but not staffing services. RecruitMilitary operates a job board and career fairs but lacks skill-translation technology. O*NET's Military Crosswalk provides MOS-to-occupation mapping but at too high a level (MOS 25B maps to "Computer Network Support Specialist" — not useful for distinguishing a junior helpdesk role from a senior network engineer position). No platform combines military skill translation with staffing firm placement workflows.
