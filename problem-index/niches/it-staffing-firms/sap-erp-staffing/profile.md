# SAP/ERP Implementation Staffing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Relies heavily on personal recruiter networks and LinkedIn searches; most firms lack structured databases of ERP module expertise and project-phase experience.
**Target Buyer:** ERP practice leads at staffing firms like Eliassen Group, Clarkston Consulting's staffing arm, or specialized SAP staffing boutiques.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Module-to-role matching is rule-based, but consultant reputation and project-phase fit require judgment.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
SAP/ERP staffing operates on a different talent model than general IT staffing. Consultants are matched not just on technology (SAP S/4HANA, Oracle Cloud, Workday) but on specific module expertise (SAP MM, FI/CO, PP, SD), project phase experience (blueprint, build, test, cutover, hypercare), and industry vertical knowledge (a retail SAP SD consultant cannot easily substitute for a manufacturing SAP SD consultant). The talent pool is small and highly networked — the same 5,000-10,000 consultants rotate through implementations globally. Recruiters rely on personal relationships and conference networking rather than digital sourcing.

## Current Tools & Gaps
LinkedIn Recruiter is the primary sourcing tool, but SAP module expertise is inconsistently listed in profiles. Specialized SAP staffing firms maintain proprietary databases in Access or FileMaker Pro, organized around module expertise and project history. Bullhorn and other mainstream ATS platforms cannot model multi-dimensional matching (module + phase + industry + geography + rate). No platform captures the "project genealogy" — which implementations a consultant has worked on and with which teams.
