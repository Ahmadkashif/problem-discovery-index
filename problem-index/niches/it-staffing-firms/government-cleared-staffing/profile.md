# Government & Cleared IT Staffing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 27%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Large primes use Deltek/Costpoint and clearance-specific ATS modules, but clearance verification and ITAR compliance tracking remain heavily manual.
**Target Buyer:** Federal staffing program managers at firms like Booz Allen, SAIC sub-tier staffing partners, and boutique cleared-staffing agencies.
**Automation Potential:** High — Clearance level matching, ITAR/EAR compliance checks, and DD-254 processing follow rigid rule-based workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Government IT staffing operates under an entirely different set of constraints than commercial staffing. Candidates must hold active security clearances (Confidential, Secret, Top Secret, TS/SCI, with possible polygraph), which take 6-18 months to obtain and cannot be sponsored by most staffing firms. This creates a constrained candidate pool where clearance status is the primary filter, not technical skills. Contract vehicles (GWACs, BPAs, IDIQs) dictate which firms can even bid on work, adding a procurement layer absent in commercial staffing. Labor category (LCAT) matching must align with specific contract line items, not just job descriptions.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ClearanceJobs.com dominates candidate sourcing for cleared roles but functions as a job board, not a staffing operations platform. Deltek Costpoint handles government contract financials but lacks staffing-specific workflows. Generic ATS platforms like Bullhorn cannot model clearance hierarchies, polygraph types, or LCAT structures. Firms bridge these gaps with SharePoint lists and manual cross-referencing between contract LCATs and candidate qualifications.
