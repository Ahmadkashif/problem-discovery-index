# Facility-Specific Onboarding Bottleneck

**Niche:** [[niches/it-staffing-firms/healthcare-it-staffing/profile|Healthcare IT Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/it-staffing-firms|IT Staffing Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Every health system has a unique onboarding checklist for IT contractors, and staffing firms rebuild it from scratch for every placement because no system captures facility-specific requirements persistently.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
When a healthcare IT contractor is placed at a new facility, the onboarding process involves 15-40 discrete steps: system access requests, badge provisioning, parking credentials, HIPAA attestation specific to that system, EHR environment access, VPN setup, and facility-specific security training. Each health system has different requirements, different forms, and different timelines. Staffing firm coordinators manage this via email chains with facility HR contacts, losing an average of 5-8 business days per placement to onboarding delays.

## Why It's Still Broken
Health systems have no incentive to standardize their onboarding processes — they see contractor onboarding as the staffing firm's problem. Staffing firms have attempted to build internal checklists, but these go stale quickly as facilities change requirements without notification. The coordinator who "knows" a facility's quirks becomes a single point of failure; when they leave, the institutional knowledge about which facilities require what disappears.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A facility-specific onboarding template system that captures and versions each health system's requirements, auto-populates known contractor information, tracks completion status across all active onboardings, and flags when a facility's requirements have likely changed (based on returned/rejected submissions). Integrates with the ATS to trigger onboarding workflows at placement confirmation.

## Who Feels the Pain
Onboarding coordinators at healthcare IT staffing firms who manage 10-30 simultaneous facility onboardings, and the contractors themselves who sit idle for days waiting for access credentials.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average onboarding time from 8 days to 3 days, recovering $2K-$5K in billable contractor time per placement. For a firm making 200 placements/year, that is $400K-$1M in recovered revenue.
