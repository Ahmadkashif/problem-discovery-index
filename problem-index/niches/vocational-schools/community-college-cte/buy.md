# Perkins Act Performance Reporting Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/community-college-cte/profile|Community College CTE Divisions]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Institutional research tools exist for academic reporting but don't model Perkins V performance indicators — CTE divisions manually assemble completion, placement, and credential data across disconnected systems for annual federal reporting.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
The Perkins V Act requires CTE programs to report on specific performance indicators — completion rate, placement rate, credential attainment rate, and nontraditional enrollment/completion — disaggregated by race, gender, disability status, and economic disadvantage. Each indicator requires pulling data from different sources: completion from the SIS, placement from career services surveys, credential attainment from certification body records, and demographic data from admissions. Institutional research (IR) offices at community colleges handle this annually, but the CTE-specific assembly is a manual, multi-week project.

## What Already Exists
Institutional research platforms (Tableau, SPSS, Watermark/EAC) handle general academic reporting and assessment. State longitudinal data systems (SLDS) aggregate some workforce outcomes. Accreditation management tools like Watermark Planning & Self-Study support document assembly. None of these model Perkins V performance indicators as pre-built report templates or automate the cross-system data joining required for CTE-specific reporting.

## The Customization Gap
A Perkins-specific reporting module needs to: (1) map automatically to the exact indicator definitions in each state's Perkins plan (which vary in how they define "placement" and "credential attainment"); (2) pull completion data from the SIS, employment verification from career services, and credential data from certification bodies or self-reported records; (3) compute indicators with correct denominator definitions and demographic disaggregation; and (4) generate state-formatted submission files. The customization required is state-by-state indicator definition mapping — a configuration challenge, not a technical one.

## Target Customer
CTE deans and institutional research staff at community colleges who spend 3-6 weeks annually on Perkins reporting and face pressure from state education agencies to improve data quality and timeliness.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces annual Perkins reporting effort from 3-6 weeks to 2-3 days per institution. Improves data accuracy by eliminating manual cross-system joins that introduce calculation errors. Enables mid-year performance monitoring against Perkins targets instead of annual rear-view reporting.
