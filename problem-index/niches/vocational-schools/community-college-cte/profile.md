# Community College CTE Divisions

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — CTE divisions operate within larger community college IT ecosystems (Banner, PeopleSoft) designed for academic transfer programs, not hands-on trades; competency tracking, shop floor assessments, and employer engagement run on paper and spreadsheets alongside enterprise systems they can't customize
**Target Buyer:** CTE dean or workforce development director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Constrained by institutional IT governance and state reporting requirements, but competency documentation and Perkins reporting are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Community college CTE divisions enroll more vocational students than all private trade schools combined but operate under severe technology constraints. They inherit enterprise SIS platforms (Ellucian Banner, Oracle PeopleSoft) that their IT departments control and that are designed for credit-hour academic programs, not competency-based trades training. CTE deans cannot add software without IT approval, customize data fields, or build integrations. Meanwhile, they face unique federal reporting requirements under the Perkins Act (performance indicators on completion, placement, and credential attainment by demographic subgroup) that require data assembly from multiple disconnected systems.

## Current Tools & Gaps
The college-wide SIS tracks enrollment and grades but has no competency module for trades programs. Faculty use paper skill sheets or personal spreadsheets for hands-on assessment. Institutional research offices compile Perkins reports annually from SIS data, supplemented by manual career services placement surveys. LMS platforms (Canvas, Blackboard) serve theory components but don't capture shop-floor performance. The gap is widest for dual-enrollment and apprenticeship programs where students split time between the college and employer worksites.
