# CDL & Truck Driving Schools

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — FMCSA ELDT (Entry Level Driver Training) mandate requires digital training record submission since 2022, pushing some adoption, but most schools still use paper logbooks for in-cab training hours and spreadsheet-based student tracking
**Target Buyer:** School owner or operations director at private CDL training academies
**Automation Potential:** High — CDL training follows a rigid FMCSA-mandated curriculum with specific hours-by-topic requirements, making compliance tracking and skills verification highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
CDL programs are the second-largest vocational segment by enrollment and operate under unique federal regulation (FMCSA ELDT requirements effective February 2022 mandate specific curriculum, trainer qualifications, and digital record-keeping). The training cycle is short (4-8 weeks) with extremely high throughput — a 50-truck school may graduate 500+ students per year. Placement is virtually guaranteed given the chronic driver shortage, but the placement tracking obligation persists for accreditation. The in-cab training component creates a distinctive assessment challenge: instructors evaluate driving skill while sitting in the passenger seat at highway speed, making real-time documentation impossible.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ELDT-compliant training providers (about 6,000 registered) must submit completion records to FMCSA's Training Provider Registry (TPR). Platforms like TruckingOffice and DriverReach handle fleet management but aren't built for training schools. CDL-specific SIS options are limited — Luma and DriveTest handle basic enrollment and ELDT compliance but lack in-cab assessment tracking, skills gap identification, and employer placement analytics.
