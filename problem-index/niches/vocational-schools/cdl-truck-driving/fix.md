# Post-Graduation Employment Tracking at Scale

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/cdl-truck-driving/profile|CDL & Truck Driving Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** CDL schools graduate 500+ students per year but track employment outcomes through manual phone calls — a process so labor-intensive that most schools report incomplete placement data, risking accreditation standing.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
Accreditors (ACCSC, COE) and state regulators require CDL schools to document employment outcomes for graduates — typically 70%+ placement rates within 6 months. With 500+ graduates per year, career services staff must make thousands of outreach calls to verify employment. Graduates who got jobs don't answer calls from their old school. Carrier HR departments don't respond to verification requests. The result: schools report 65-75% verification rates when actual employment may be 85-90%, because unverified graduates are counted as "not placed" — artificially depressing the metrics that drive accreditation and enrollment marketing.

## Why It's Still Broken
No structured feedback loop exists between CDL training schools and the carriers that hire their graduates. The trucking industry tracks drivers through FMCSA's licensing database, but schools don't have access to verify whether a graduate obtained a CDL and is actively driving for a carrier. Carriers have no incentive to report back to training schools. Career services coordinators resort to phone and email outreach that generates 30-40% response rates, with the remainder classified as "unable to verify."

## What a Fix Looks Like
A multi-channel automated verification system that combines: (1) direct carrier partnership integrations where large fleet employers (Schneider, Werner, CRST) automatically report hiring status for school graduates; (2) state CDL licensing database queries to verify active license status as a proxy for employment; (3) automated text/email/LinkedIn outreach sequences to graduates with a one-click employment verification form; (4) aggregation of results into accreditation-ready placement reports. The system transforms placement tracking from a manual calling campaign into an automated data pipeline with manual follow-up only for unresolved cases.

## Who Feels the Pain
Career services coordinators who spend 20-30 hours per week on phone-based employment verification during peak reporting periods, and school directors whose accreditation standing depends on placement metrics that undercount actual outcomes due to verification failures.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases verified placement rates by 10-20 percentage points (by reaching graduates who don't answer phone calls), strengthening accreditation standing and enrollment marketing. Reduces career services staff time on verification by 60-70%, freeing them for employer relationship development and student career coaching.
