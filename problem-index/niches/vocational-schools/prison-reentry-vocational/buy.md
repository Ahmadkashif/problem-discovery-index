# Offline-First Vocational Assessment for Correctional Settings

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/prison-reentry-vocational/profile|Prison Reentry Vocational Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Digital assessment tools exist for vocational education but require internet connectivity — correctional facilities need an offline-first assessment system that works within security restrictions and syncs when connectivity is available.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
Vocational instructors in correctional facilities assess student competencies on paper because their facilities prohibit internet access and restrict device usage. When digital tools are allowed, they must operate on locked-down tablets with no network access during instructional periods. Paper assessments are difficult to aggregate, cannot produce real-time progress reports for program administrators, and are frequently lost during student transfers between facilities.

## What Already Exists
Competency assessment tools for vocational education exist in various forms — NCCER's module-based testing, trade-specific evaluation platforms, and general LMS platforms with offline modes. Tablet-based assessment tools like GoReact capture performance for review. MDM (Mobile Device Management) platforms manage restricted-use tablets in educational settings.

## The Customization Gap
Correctional settings require a fundamentally different deployment model: devices must be MDM-locked to prevent unauthorized use; all data must be stored locally and synced only during approved windows; the interface must work without any network connectivity for weeks at a time; content must pass security review (no photos of facility layout, no personal information stored on-device). Additionally, student rosters change unpredictably as inmates transfer or are released, requiring flexible enrollment management that paper systems handle trivially but most digital systems don't. The tool must also accommodate instructors who may have limited tech literacy and are teaching under time pressure in a controlled environment.

## Target Customer
Corrections education departments and contracted vocational training providers (like UNICOR/Federal Prison Industries partners) operating in state and federal correctional facilities with active trades training programs.

## Impact If Solved
Replaces paper-based assessment with structured digital records — reducing annual reporting effort for DOC education departments by 40-60%. Enables real-time program effectiveness analysis across multiple facilities within a state DOC system, currently impossible with paper records. Provides individual student competency records that survive facility transfers.
