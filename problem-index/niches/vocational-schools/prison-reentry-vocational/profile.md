# Prison Reentry Vocational Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5-2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Correctional facilities restrict internet access and personal devices; training documentation is paper-based by policy; post-release tracking relies on parole officer reports and manual phone follow-up with reentry services
**Target Buyer:** Reentry program director or corrections education coordinator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Assessment and documentation workflows are automatable, but facility security restrictions limit digital tool deployment during incarceration; post-release placement tracking is the highest automation opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Vocational training within correctional facilities and reentry programs operates under constraints that make it fundamentally different from civilian vocational education. Internet access is prohibited or severely restricted, ruling out cloud-based SIS platforms. Students cycle through programs unpredictably as sentences end, transfers occur, or disciplinary actions interrupt enrollment. Post-release employment outcomes — the most critical metric for program funding — are the hardest to track because formerly incarcerated individuals change addresses, phone numbers, and sometimes names. Yet these programs receive significant federal and state funding (Second Chance Act, DOJ reentry grants) with strict reporting requirements on placement and recidivism outcomes.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most corrections education uses paper-based tracking within facilities. Some state DOC systems (like JPAY for communication or APDS for program tracking) exist but are archaic, facility-specific, and not designed for vocational competency assessment. Post-release, community-based reentry organizations use generic case management tools (Apricot, Penelope) that don't integrate with corrections records. No system bridges the pre-release training record with post-release employment tracking.
