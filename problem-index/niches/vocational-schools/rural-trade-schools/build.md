# All-in-One Micro-School Management for Rural Trades

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/rural-trade-schools/profile|Rural Trade Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A single platform that handles enrollment, billing, attendance, competency tracking, placement documentation, and accreditation reporting for a 50-200 student trade school run by 1-3 administrative staff — designed for low-bandwidth, low-tech-literacy environments.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A rural trade school director with 100 students juggles 6-8 disconnected tools and processes: QuickBooks for tuition, Excel for student records, paper for competency assessments, a personal contact list for employer relationships, Word templates for accreditation documentation, email for admissions, and a filing cabinet for financial aid paperwork. Each system requires separate data entry, nothing integrates, and the director — who is also teaching classes — spends 15-20 hours per week on administrative tasks that larger schools distribute across 5-10 specialized staff.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
SIS vendors target the volume segments: large private chains (UTI, Lincoln Tech) with custom enterprise solutions, or community colleges with Banner/PeopleSoft. The 50-200 student rural school market is fragmented (thousands of schools) with low per-customer revenue potential ($2,000-5,000/year for software). Existing affordable options (Populi, Campus Cafe) are designed for mid-size schools with dedicated administrative staff and assume reliable broadband. Nobody has built the "Shopify for trade schools" — a single-screen platform where one person manages everything.

## What to Build
A mobile-first, offline-capable school management platform combining: student enrollment and billing, daily attendance tracking (tap-in from a tablet at the shop door), competency checklist scoring by trade, employer contact management with placement tracking, and accreditation report generation. The entire system is operable by one person from a tablet. Offline mode handles the broadband reliability issue — data syncs when connectivity returns. Pricing at $50-100/month makes it accessible to owner-operated schools.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators of trade schools with fewer than 200 students in rural areas who currently manage operations across 5+ disconnected tools and have no dedicated administrative staff.

## Impact If Built
Saves school directors 10-15 hours per week on administrative tasks. Reduces accreditation documentation preparation from weeks to days. Enables real-time visibility into student progress and placement metrics that currently require end-of-term manual compilation.
