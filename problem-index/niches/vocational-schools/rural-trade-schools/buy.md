# Regional Employer Network Matching

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/rural-trade-schools/profile|Rural Trade Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Job matching platforms exist for urban labor markets but are useless when a rural trade school's entire employer network is 15 local companies — none of which post jobs online.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #recommendation #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Rural trade schools place graduates into a small, stable employer network — 10-30 local contractors, manufacturers, utilities, and agricultural operations. These employers don't use Indeed or ZipRecruiter; they call the school director when they need someone. The matching problem isn't search (there's no searchable pool) — it's relationship management: knowing that Smith Electric needs two journeyman electricians next quarter, that the sawmill is expanding a shift, and that the county road department has retirements coming. This intelligence lives in the school director's personal relationships and is never documented.

## What Already Exists
Job matching platforms (Indeed, Handshake, CareerBuilder) serve large, liquid labor markets where employers post jobs and candidates apply. CRM tools (HubSpot, Salesforce) manage contact relationships. Workforce development boards operate regional job banks. None of these work for a market with 15 employers who never post online, where placement happens through personal phone calls and the school director knowing that "Jim at the co-op mentioned he's looking for a mechanic."

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a lightweight employer relationship tool built for small, relationship-based labor markets. It needs to track: employer hiring patterns (seasonal, expansion-driven, replacement-driven), specific skill requirements by employer, historical placement outcomes (which graduates stayed vs. left within 90 days, and why), anticipated future needs captured during informal conversations, and employer satisfaction with past graduates. The system converts informal relationship knowledge into structured data that survives when the school director retires or moves on.

## Target Customer
School directors at rural trade schools who personally manage all employer relationships and have no structured record of hiring patterns, employer needs, or placement outcomes beyond their own memory.

## Impact If Solved
Preserves institutional employer relationship knowledge that is currently lost with staff turnover. Improves placement match quality by surfacing employer-specific fit criteria (which welding processes an employer actually uses, which shifts they need filled) rather than matching on program completion alone. Reduces 90-day placement failure rate by 15-25% through better initial matching.
