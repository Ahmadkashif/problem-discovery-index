# Evidence Collection Fragmentation

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/accreditation-compliance-ops/profile|Accreditation & Compliance Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Accreditation evidence lives in 8-12 disconnected locations — SIS, shared drives, filing cabinets, faculty email, financial software — and reassembling it every 3-5 years is a scavenger hunt that consumes months of staff time.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
An ACCSC self-study requires evidence across every operational dimension: student outcome data from the SIS, financial stability documentation from accounting software, faculty credential files from HR, student satisfaction surveys from SurveyMonkey or paper forms, advisory board meeting minutes from email attachments, catalog and enrollment agreement copies from the admissions office, and facility inspection records from physical files. Each accreditation cycle, a compliance officer hunts through these 8-12 locations, discovers that some evidence was never collected, some was lost when a computer was replaced, and some is in a format that can't be extracted. The evidence search alone consumes 4-8 weeks of a 3-6 month preparation timeline.

## Why It's Still Broken
Schools don't maintain a centralized evidence repository because accreditation is a once-every-3-5-years event — the immediate cost of organizing evidence continuously exceeds the perceived benefit until the cycle approaches. Staff turnover means the person who compiled evidence last cycle has left, and institutional knowledge of where evidence lives is lost. No off-the-shelf document management system understands the mapping between accreditor standards and evidence types, so even schools using SharePoint or Google Drive have undifferentiated file dumps rather than standard-organized evidence libraries.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A standard-mapped evidence repository where every document and data artifact is tagged to the accreditor standard it supports at the time of creation. Faculty credentials are uploaded once and linked to the faculty qualifications standard. Student outcome data is exported quarterly from the SIS and filed under the student achievement standard. Advisory board minutes are uploaded after each meeting and tagged to the program effectiveness standard. When self-study preparation begins, the evidence library is 80-90% populated — the remaining effort is gap-filling and narrative writing, not scavenging. Automated reminders prompt evidence collection on a rolling schedule aligned to the accreditation cycle.

## Who Feels the Pain
Compliance officers who spend 4-8 weeks locating and assembling evidence before they can begin the analytical work of self-study writing, and school directors who discover evidence gaps too late to collect retroactively (advisory board minutes from 2 years ago that were never documented).

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces evidence collection phase from 4-8 weeks to 3-5 days per accreditation cycle. Eliminates "evidence not found" gaps that result in accreditor findings and required follow-up documentation. Converts accreditation preparation from a periodic crisis project into a continuous, low-effort maintenance process.
