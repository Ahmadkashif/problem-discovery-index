# Accreditation & Compliance Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M-1.2B (services and embedded cost)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5% (but touches every institution)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Document assembly happens in Word and shared drives; self-study reports are manually compiled from multiple data sources; accreditation management software exists but is underadopted in vocational education
**Target Buyer:** Compliance officer, school director, or designated accreditation coordinator
**Automation Potential:** High — Accreditation self-study is a document assembly and evidence collection process following well-defined standards (ACCSC, COE, state boards) with repeatable data requirements

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Accreditation is the existential compliance requirement for vocational schools — without ACCSC, COE, or regional accreditation, schools lose Title IV financial aid eligibility, which provides 50-80% of most schools' revenue. The accreditation cycle (every 3-5 years with annual reporting) consumes 3-6 months of administrative effort per cycle. The process follows a predictable structure: self-study documentation addressing specific standards, evidence collection from multiple data sources, metric computation (completion rates, placement rates, licensure pass rates), narrative writing justifying compliance, and site visit preparation. Every element is rule-based and documentable — yet almost entirely manual at most vocational schools.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Accreditation management platforms exist for higher education broadly (Watermark/EAC, Anthology Compliance Assist, Weave) but are designed for regional accreditation of degree-granting institutions, not national accreditation of trade schools. ACCSC and COE have specific self-study templates and metric definitions that differ from regional accreditor requirements. Schools use Word documents, shared drives, and spreadsheets to compile self-studies — a process that is error-prone, time-consuming, and starts nearly from scratch each cycle because evidence from the previous cycle is poorly organized.
