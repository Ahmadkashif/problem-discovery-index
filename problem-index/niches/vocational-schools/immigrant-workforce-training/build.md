# Language-Integrated Vocational Assessment Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/immigrant-workforce-training/profile|Immigrant Workforce Training Centers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A vocational skills assessment tool that separates language ability from trade competency — so a skilled welder who doesn't speak English can demonstrate their expertise without being penalized by an English-language test.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #multimodal #worker-facing

## The Problem
An immigrant student arrives with 10 years of welding experience in their home country but limited English. The vocational school's competency assessment is written in English — the student fails not because they lack welding knowledge but because they can't read the questions. Current assessment tools conflate language proficiency with trade competency, placing experienced practitioners in beginner-level programs where they waste months relearning skills they already have. This misplacement wastes training resources, frustrates students, and delays their entry into the workforce.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Separating language from competency requires either multilingual assessment content (expensive to develop and maintain across dozens of languages and trade specialties) or visual/performance-based assessments that don't require reading (difficult to standardize and score consistently). The immigrant workforce training market is fragmented across thousands of small nonprofits with minimal software budgets, and no single trade certification body (AWS, NCCER, OSHA) offers multilingual assessments. The intersection of vocational competency testing and multilingual assessment is a niche within a niche.

## What to Build
A multimodal vocational assessment platform that uses visual and demonstration-based assessment methods — video prompts showing a task to be performed, image-based multiple choice for safety identification, and practical performance evaluation rubrics with multilingual instructor interfaces. LLM-powered real-time translation enables assessment delivery in 20+ languages. The platform separates scores by dimension: trade competency score (from visual/performance assessment) and workplace English readiness score (from targeted vocational English evaluation), placing students in the appropriate training level for each.

## Target Customer
Directors of immigrant workforce training centers, refugee resettlement agencies with job training programs, and community college ESL-to-CTE bridge program coordinators serving 50+ immigrant students annually.

## Impact If Built
Reduces misplacement of experienced immigrants into beginner programs by 50-70%, saving 3-6 months of training time per student. Accelerates workforce entry by matching students to their actual competency level from day one. Increases student retention by eliminating frustration of skilled workers stuck in entry-level courses.
