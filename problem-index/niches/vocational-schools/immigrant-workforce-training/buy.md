# Foreign Credential Evaluation Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/immigrant-workforce-training/profile|Immigrant Workforce Training Centers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Credential evaluation services exist for academic degrees but have no equivalent for vocational certifications — an electrician licensed in Mexico cannot easily demonstrate equivalency to U.S. trade standards.
**Tags:** #nlp #text-classification #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
An immigrant arrives with vocational certifications from their home country — an electrical license from the Philippines, a welding certification from Guatemala, an HVAC technician credential from Ukraine. No standardized process exists to evaluate what these credentials mean in U.S. trade certification terms. Centers spend hours researching foreign credential systems, contacting certification bodies in other countries, and trying to map foreign training content to U.S. standards (NEC for electrical, AWS for welding, EPA for refrigerant handling). Each case is a one-off research project.

## What Already Exists
Academic credential evaluation services (WES, ECE, NACES members) evaluate foreign university degrees for U.S. academic equivalency — a well-established industry. Some state licensing boards accept specific foreign credentials (e.g., Canadian journeyman certificates in border states). ONET and O*NET-SOC provide occupational classification systems. But no service systematically evaluates foreign vocational/trade certifications against U.S. trade standards.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a vocational credential equivalency database and evaluation workflow. It needs to: (1) map foreign trade certification systems to U.S. equivalents across major source countries (Mexico, Philippines, Guatemala, India, Ukraine, Haiti); (2) identify which U.S. trade certification requirements are met by the foreign credential and which gaps remain; (3) generate a personalized training plan that covers only the gap areas rather than the full U.S. certification curriculum; (4) produce documentation that state licensing boards and employers can reference. This requires NLP-based analysis of foreign curriculum documents and a structured mapping to U.S. competency frameworks.

## Target Customer
Workforce development coordinators at immigrant-serving organizations and community college CTE bridge programs who currently spend 3-5 hours per student case researching foreign credential equivalency manually.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces credential evaluation time from 3-5 hours to 30 minutes per student case. Shortens training paths for credentialed immigrants by 40-60% by identifying existing competencies rather than starting from zero. Helps 200,000+ skilled immigrants per year in the U.S. re-enter their trades faster.
