# Trades-Specific Competency Management System

**Niche:** [[niches/vocational-schools/welding-programs/profile|Welding & Skilled Trades Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/vocational-schools|Vocational Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic LMS platforms track course completion and grades but cannot model the multi-position, multi-process certification pathways that define welding and trades education progression.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem
A welding student's progression isn't linear — they must demonstrate competency across multiple weld positions (flat, horizontal, vertical, overhead) and multiple processes (stick, MIG, TIG), each with distinct pass criteria. Current SIS and LMS platforms treat each course as an independent unit, losing the cross-dimensional view that tells an instructor "this student excels in flat-position MIG but struggles with vertical stick." Programs track this on paper skill sheets that don't aggregate, don't transfer between instructors, and aren't queryable.

## What Already Exists
LMS platforms (Canvas, Moodle) track course-level grades and completion. Skills management platforms like Degreed and Credly handle digital credentials but are designed for corporate training, not hands-on trade certification paths with physical performance requirements. SIS platforms (Populi, SONIS) manage enrollment and transcripts but have no concept of multi-dimensional competency matrices.

## The Customization Gap
The missing layer is a competency management module built for trade certification structures — modeling the specific AWS, NCCER, or state licensing competency frameworks as multi-dimensional skill matrices, capturing per-dimension assessment scores (not just overall course grades), and visualizing student readiness for specific certification tests. This requires understanding trade-specific evaluation criteria (AWS D1.1 visual acceptance criteria, NCCER performance profiles) and mapping them to a digital skill tracking structure that instructors can update from the shop floor.

## Target Customer
Program directors and lead instructors at welding and trades programs with 30+ students who need competency tracking beyond what their SIS provides, particularly for accreditation reporting and employer skills verification.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 3-5 hours per week per instructor spent on paper-based skill sheet management. Gives career services coordinators granular skill data for employer matching instead of binary "completed program" status. Reduces accreditation documentation effort by 50-70% with automatically generated competency reports.
