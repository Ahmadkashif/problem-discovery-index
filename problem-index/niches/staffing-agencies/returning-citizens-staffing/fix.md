# Reentry Candidate Retention Support and Employer Communication

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/returning-citizens-staffing/profile|Returning Citizens (Ex-Offender) Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** 40-60% of placed reentry candidates leave or are terminated within 90 days — not because they cannot do the work, but because post-placement support drops off and small workplace issues escalate without intervention.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Returning citizens face post-placement challenges that mainstream temp workers do not: transportation instability (many lack driver's licenses or reliable vehicles), housing instability (transitional housing programs have curfews that conflict with shift work), parole/probation appointment conflicts with work schedules, substance abuse recovery needs, and unfamiliarity with workplace norms after years of incarceration. When a placed worker misses a shift or has a conflict with a supervisor, mainstream staffing agencies simply replace them. Reentry programs know that intervention at the first sign of trouble — a call from a case manager, a conversation with the site supervisor, a schedule adjustment — can save the placement, but the coordinator does not know there is a problem until the worker is already terminated.

## Why It's Still Broken
Reentry staffing programs have case managers and coordinators who provide retention support, but their caseloads (30-50 active candidates per coordinator) make proactive monitoring impossible. They rely on the worker or the employer to report problems, which usually happens only after the situation has escalated beyond repair. There is no system that tracks early warning signals — missed punches, declining hours, supervisor feedback — and triggers coordinator intervention before the placement fails. The staffing ATS tracks placement status (active/terminated) but not the behavioral signals that precede termination.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A retention monitoring system that ingests time and attendance data from the employer or staffing firm's time tracking system and flags early warning patterns: first missed punch, declining hours trend, schedule inconsistency with known probation/parole appointment times. When a flag triggers, the system sends an alert to the assigned case manager with context ("Marcus missed his Thursday shift — his parole officer appointment is Thursday at 2 PM — schedule conflict likely"). The coordinator intervenes proactively, the employer accommodates, and the placement is saved. The system also facilitates structured employer feedback at 30/60/90-day checkpoints, surfacing concerns before they become termination events.

## Who Feels the Pain
Reentry candidates who lose jobs they were performing well in due to preventable issues, and case managers who invest 10-20 hours placing each candidate only to see 40-60% fail within the first 90 days.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces 90-day turnover from 40-60% to 20-30%, saving $1,000-$2,500 per retained placement in re-recruitment costs and increasing program success metrics that drive grant funding and employer partnerships.
