# Employer Second-Chance Policy Database and Matching

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/returning-citizens-staffing/profile|Returning Citizens (Ex-Offender) Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Employer databases and job boards exist but none flag which companies have explicit second-chance hiring policies and what conviction categories they accept.
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #tabular-ml #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Many major employers have adopted second-chance hiring policies — Walmart, Target, JP Morgan Chase, Koch Industries — but the details of these policies are scattered across press releases, policy statements, and informal HR practices. A reentry staffing coordinator knows from experience that "Amazon will hire misdemeanor convictions after 1 year but not felony drug distribution" — but this knowledge is held in the coordinator's memory, not in a searchable system. New coordinators must rebuild this knowledge through trial and error. Meanwhile, many mid-size employers are willing to hire returning citizens but have never been asked or connected with reentry programs.

## What Already Exists
General job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn) list open positions without criminal history context. Specialized platforms like 70MillionJobs and Honest Jobs connect returning citizens directly to employers, functioning as job boards rather than staffing tools. Dave's Killer Bread Foundation maintains a second-chance employer directory, but it is an advocacy resource, not a matching tool. None of these integrate with a staffing agency's ATS or provide structured data on employer policies by conviction type.

## The Customization Gap
The missing tool is a structured employer policy database that captures: which conviction categories each employer accepts, the look-back period they apply, whether they use an individualized assessment process (EEOC guidance), specific positions that are excluded even under a second-chance policy, and the HR contact for second-chance hiring inquiries. This database must integrate with the staffing agency's ATS so that when a new job order comes in from an employer, the system automatically flags it as second-chance eligible and identifies which candidates in the pipeline qualify.

## Target Customer
Staffing coordinators at reentry programs and second-chance staffing agencies who need to quickly match candidates with conviction-friendly employers.

## Impact If Solved
Accelerates employer matching from days to hours, increases the pool of identified second-chance employers by 50-100% (many employers are willing but have never been cataloged), and improves placement rates by 20-30%.
