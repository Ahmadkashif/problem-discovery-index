# Returning Citizens (Ex-Offender) Staffing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~1.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most reentry staffing operates through nonprofit programs and government agencies using case management tools not designed for staffing workflows
**Target Buyer:** Reentry program staffing director, social enterprise operator, or staffing agency owner specializing in second-chance employment
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — background check navigation and employer matching have significant automation opportunity, but the human support component is critical to success

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Over 600,000 people are released from US prisons annually, and 70+ million Americans have some criminal record. These individuals face systematic barriers to employment that mainstream staffing agencies are not designed to navigate: background check policies that vary by employer, industry, and jurisdiction; "ban the box" laws in 37 states with different rules about when criminal history can be inquired; occupational licensing restrictions that bar certain convictions from specific industries; and employer liability concerns about negligent hiring. The staffing model for this population must include case management, employer education, and ongoing retention support — services that standard staffing agencies do not provide and are not compensated for.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Reentry programs use case management platforms (Apricot, Salesforce Nonprofit) for tracking participant progress, but these are not staffing tools. Mainstream ATS platforms have no mechanism for recording criminal history categories, matching them against employer background check policies, or identifying which job orders are accessible given a candidate's specific record. Background check companies (Checkr, Sterling) run checks but do not advise on which results would disqualify a candidate from which employers or jurisdictions.
