# Mass Communication Platform with Shift Confirmation Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/light-industrial-warehouse/profile|Light Industrial & Warehouse Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Mass text and communication tools exist but none are designed for the real-time shift confirmation workflow where a dispatcher needs to know within 30 minutes which of 200 contacted workers have confirmed, declined, or gone silent.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
When a client order comes in for 25 workers for tomorrow's 6 AM shift, the dispatcher sends a mass text or call blast to 40-60 eligible workers. Responses trickle in over 2-4 hours: some confirm immediately, some respond "maybe," some ask about pay rate, and half never respond. The dispatcher manually tracks who confirmed, re-contacts non-responders, and builds the dispatch list from a combination of text conversations, voicemail callbacks, and the ATS. By 9 PM, they may still be short 5 workers and must start a second round of outreach. The entire process is tracked in the dispatcher's head and phone screen.

## What Already Exists
Sense, Herefish (Bullhorn), and Staffing Referrals offer mass communication tools for staffing. Generic SMS platforms (Twilio, TextMagic) provide bulk messaging. These tools send messages effectively but do not provide a real-time confirmation board that shows, for each open shift, which workers have confirmed, declined, not responded, and which backup workers should be contacted next.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs shift-level confirmation tracking: a visual board where each shift shows confirmed/pending/declined workers in real time, with automatic escalation to backup candidates when confirmations fall below the required headcount. Worker responses ("yes," "what time?", "can't tomorrow but available Thursday") must be parsed and categorized automatically. The system should know each worker's typical response pattern and adjust outreach timing accordingly — some workers respond to texts at 7 PM, others at 5 AM.

## Target Customer
Dispatchers at light industrial staffing branches who spend 2-4 hours per evening confirming the next day's dispatch through manual text and phone outreach.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces dispatch confirmation time from 2-4 hours to 30-60 minutes per evening, improves fill rates by 10-15% through faster backup escalation, and gives dispatchers their evenings back.
