# Light Industrial & Warehouse Staffing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $55-65B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Bullhorn and TempWorks are standard, but candidate communication is heavily phone/text-based and shift scheduling is often manual
**Target Buyer:** Branch manager at a light industrial staffing agency operating 1-5 branch offices
**Automation Potential:** High — high-volume, repetitive placements with standardized skill requirements and frequent same-day dispatch needs

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Light industrial staffing is the highest-volume segment of the staffing industry: a single branch may dispatch 100-500 temp workers daily to warehouse, manufacturing, and distribution center assignments. The operational model is fundamentally different from professional staffing — speed and reliability matter more than skill matching. A client calls at 6 AM needing 15 warehouse workers by 7 AM, and the branch must fill those spots from its active pool within an hour. No-show rates run 15-25%, requiring over-dispatch to compensate. The business runs on thin margins (15-25% gross margin) with high volume, making operational efficiency the difference between profit and loss.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TempWorks and Avionté dominate the light industrial ATS segment; Bullhorn serves larger multi-vertical firms. These platforms manage candidate records and job orders but are not optimized for same-day dispatch workflows. Shift scheduling is done through phone trees, mass text blasts, and the dispatcher's personal knowledge of which workers are reliable. No tool predicts no-show risk at the individual worker level or optimizes the dispatch list for reliability.
