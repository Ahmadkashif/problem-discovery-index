# Foreign Credential Evaluation and Skill Translation Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/refugee-immigrant-placement/profile|Refugee & Immigrant Workforce Placement]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Credential evaluation services exist but do not translate foreign work experience and education into US occupational categories that staffing agencies and employers understand.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #recommendation #worker-facing

## The Problem
A refugee who was a licensed electrician in Syria, a civil engineer in Afghanistan, or a nurse in the Democratic Republic of Congo arrives in the US with credentials that no American employer recognizes. Formal credential evaluation services (WES, ECE) can assess the equivalency of degrees but take 4-8 weeks and cost $200-$400 per evaluation. They also do not translate vocational skills, trade experience, or informal competencies. The placement coordinator must manually research which US certifications correspond to the worker's foreign qualifications, determine which skills transfer directly vs. which require bridging courses, and match the worker to appropriate job categories — all without a systematic framework for skill translation.

## What Already Exists
World Education Services (WES) and Educational Credential Evaluators (ECE) provide degree-level equivalency assessments. O*NET provides a US occupational classification system. Some resettlement agencies maintain informal "skill mapping" guides created by experienced coordinators. No tool systematically maps foreign occupational experience to US job categories with a clear pathway to re-credentialing.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs a structured mapping between foreign vocational and professional qualifications (by country of origin) and US equivalents: what does a "Technician Grade III" from an Ethiopian vocational institute map to in US terms? What additional training or certification does a Congolese nurse need to work as a CNA in the US? The output should be: (1) the US occupational category closest to the worker's experience, (2) the gap between their current qualifications and US requirements, (3) the specific steps to bridge that gap (courses, exams, supervised hours), and (4) the interim roles the worker can fill while pursuing full credentialing. The mapping database must cover the top 20 origin countries for US refugees.

## Target Customer
Workforce development coordinators at resettlement agencies and immigrant services organizations placing 50-300 workers annually.

## Impact If Solved
Accelerates placement into appropriate roles by 30-60 days, increases initial placement wage by 15-25% by matching workers to skill-appropriate roles instead of entry-level defaults, and provides workers with a concrete pathway to restore their professional careers.
