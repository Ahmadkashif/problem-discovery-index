# Multilingual Onboarding and Workplace Orientation

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/refugee-immigrant-placement/profile|Refugee & Immigrant Workforce Placement]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Onboarding materials, safety training, and workplace expectations are delivered in English to workers who speak Dari, Swahili, or Burmese — leading to safety incidents, workplace misunderstandings, and early termination.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #seq2seq #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a refugee worker starts a new assignment, they receive the same English-language onboarding packet as every other temp: safety procedures, workplace policies, time and attendance expectations, and emergency contacts. Workers with limited English proficiency may sign acknowledgment forms they do not understand, miss critical safety instructions, and fail to grasp cultural expectations that are unstated in the written materials (calling in advance when sick rather than simply not showing up, understanding progressive discipline vs. immediate termination for certain behaviors). The result is a higher early termination rate for immigrant workers (30-40% vs. 15-20% for native speakers) driven by preventable misunderstandings, not lack of capability.

## Why It's Still Broken
The refugee and immigrant population speaks 50+ languages across a typical resettlement city. Translating onboarding materials into even the top 10 languages requires ongoing investment as policies change, and formal translation services cost $0.15-$0.30 per word. Staffing agencies serving this population are typically small and cannot afford professional translation of all materials into all needed languages. Bilingual staff members informally translate during onboarding, but coverage is inconsistent and does not scale.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A multilingual onboarding platform that delivers orientation materials (safety procedures, workplace expectations, scheduling rules, contact information) in the worker's preferred language via smartphone. The platform uses LLM-powered translation calibrated for workplace-specific vocabulary in the target languages (not generic translation — "PPE" must translate to the correct term, not a generic acronym expansion). Short video modules with subtitles or voiceover in the worker's language cover the critical first-day topics: safety, attendance, communication with supervisors, and what to do if they feel unsafe. Cultural orientation modules explain US workplace norms that are implicit for native workers.

## Who Feels the Pain
Immigrant and refugee workers who feel confused and anxious on their first day because they cannot understand written policies, and placement coordinators who invest time in placing workers only to see them terminated within 2 weeks due to preventable miscommunication.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces early termination rate for immigrant workers from 30-40% to 15-20% (matching the general population rate), saving $500-$1,500 per avoided turnover event. Improves safety compliance and reduces workplace injury risk for workers who previously could not understand safety procedures.
