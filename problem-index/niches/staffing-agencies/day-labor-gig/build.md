# Digital Dispatch and Daily Pay Platform for Day Labor

**Niche:** [[niches/staffing-agencies/day-labor-gig/profile|Day Labor & Gig-Adjacent Temp Staffing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/staffing-agencies|Staffing Agencies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No purpose-built platform exists for the day labor dispatch workflow: worker walks in, gets assigned a site, works the day, and gets paid — all tracked digitally instead of on paper.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Day labor dispatch offices process 20-200 workers per morning using a fundamentally analog workflow: workers sign a paper sheet, the dispatcher calls job sites to confirm headcount needs, assigns workers verbally or on a whiteboard, hands out paper job tickets, collects signed time tickets at end of day, and manually calculates daily pay. This process is slow (workers wait 1-2 hours at the dispatch hall), error-prone (paper tickets get lost, hours are miscounted), and opaque (workers do not know their earnings until pay is distributed). The analog nature also limits the operation's scale — a single dispatcher can manage about 50-80 workers per morning before the process breaks down.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The day labor population presents unique challenges for digital solutions: many workers have limited English proficiency, some lack smartphones, few have bank accounts for direct deposit, and turnover is extreme (a worker may appear once and never return). Building for this population requires an interface that works on feature phones (SMS-based) as well as smartphones, supports daily pay via prepaid debit cards or cash pickup points, and is simple enough to require zero training. The market is fragmented and low-margin, making it unattractive for venture capital.

## What to Build
A mobile and SMS-based dispatch platform where: workers check in via text, app, or kiosk at the dispatch hall; the dispatcher sees available workers with their skills, certifications, and reliability history; assignments are pushed to workers' phones with job site address and report time; time capture happens via GPS check-in/check-out at the job site; daily pay is calculated automatically and disbursed via prepaid card, push-to-debit, or cash-out at the dispatch office. The system builds a persistent worker profile over time, even for workers who only appear intermittently.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators of day labor dispatch offices and temp agencies specializing in daily-pay labor, processing 30-200 workers per day.

## Impact If Built
Reduces morning dispatch time from 1-2 hours to 20-30 minutes, eliminates paper ticket errors that cost $500-$2,000/month in payroll disputes, and increases worker capacity per dispatcher from 50-80 to 150-200 daily workers.
