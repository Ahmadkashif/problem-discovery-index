# Emerging Market Agricultural Exporters

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cold-chain-logistics|Cold Chain Logistics]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6-8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — export documentation is largely paper-based, temperature monitoring during international transit is inconsistent, and phytosanitary compliance is managed manually
**Target Buyer:** Export operations manager or trade compliance lead at agricultural exporters shipping perishable products to international markets
**Automation Potential:** Medium — phytosanitary documentation, export compliance, and multi-modal temperature tracking are rule-based processes amenable to automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Agricultural exporters shipping perishable products (fresh fruit, seafood, cut flowers, specialty produce) to international markets face cold chain challenges that domestic carriers never encounter: multi-modal transit (truck to port to ocean container to truck at destination), phytosanitary certificates required by importing countries with different requirements per product per destination, pre-cooling protocols mandated by some importers (e.g., USDA cold treatment for fruit fly prevention), and temperature documentation that must satisfy both US export requirements and destination country import inspections. These exporters are typically mid-size agricultural companies ($10-200M revenue) without dedicated cold chain technology teams, navigating regulations in multiple languages and jurisdictions.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Freight forwarders handle ocean booking and customs documentation. Sensitech and Emerson loggers are placed in ocean containers for transit monitoring. USDA APHIS issues phytosanitary certificates. No platform integrates export cold chain management — connecting pre-cooling compliance, in-transit temperature monitoring across ocean and land segments, phytosanitary documentation, and destination-country import requirements into a single workflow.
