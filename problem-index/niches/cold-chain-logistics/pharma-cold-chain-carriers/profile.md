**Automation Potential:** High — GDP compliance documentation, MKT calculations, and excursion reporting follow strict templates that are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pharmaceutical cold chain carriers operate under Good Distribution Practice (GDP) regulations that impose documentation and temperature control requirements far beyond food-grade cold chain. Every shipment requires validated packaging qualification, continuous temperature monitoring with MKT (Mean Kinetic Temperature) calculation, GDP-formatted compliance reports with electronic signatures, and excursion event investigation with root cause analysis and corrective action documentation. A single excursion event on a biologic shipment can result in $500,000+ product loss and FDA regulatory action. These carriers invest heavily in monitoring hardware but lack software that automates the compliance documentation workflow — the gap between raw telemetry data and an audit-ready GDP report.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Sensitech ColdTrak and Controlant provide real-time temperature monitoring and basic alerting. FedEx Custom Critical and UPS Temperature True offer managed pharma logistics services with proprietary platforms. These tools capture data but do not generate GDP-formatted compliance narratives, calculate MKT automatically across shipment legs, or produce the excursion investigation documentation that pharma customers and FDA auditors require. Compliance analysts manually assemble these documents for every shipment.
