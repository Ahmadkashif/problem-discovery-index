**Target Buyer:** Fleet operations manager or VP of logistics at regional perishable food distributors

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Perishable food distribution fleets — companies like US Foods, Sysco regional divisions, and independent produce distributors — move temperature-sensitive food products under FSMA Rule 204 requirements across multi-stop routes. Unlike pharma cold chain (single-origin, single-destination, highest regulatory intensity), food distribution involves multi-temperature trailers serving 8-15 stops per route with different temperature zones (frozen, refrigerated, ambient) and frequent door-open events at each delivery. The core challenge is maintaining temperature integrity across a multi-stop route where every door-open event raises cargo temperature, and the last delivery on the route receives product that has experienced the most cumulative temperature exposure.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TMW Systems and McLeod TMS handle routing and dispatch. Carrier Transicold and Thermo King telematics provide reefer unit monitoring. Emerson and Sensitech loggers capture shipment-level temperature records. No platform optimizes route sequencing to minimize cumulative temperature exposure across multi-stop deliveries, or predicts which route plans will result in temperature compliance issues at the final stop based on ambient conditions, stop duration history, and reefer unit performance characteristics.
