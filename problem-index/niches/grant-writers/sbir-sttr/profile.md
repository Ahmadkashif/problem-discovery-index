# SBIR/STTR Grant Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $980M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 9%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — SBIR.gov submission is digital, but technical proposal development and commercialization planning are manual.
**Target Buyer:** Grant writer specializing in SBIR/STTR or technology commercialization consultant
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Technical writing requires domain expertise, but compliance checking, budget formatting, and commercialization plan templates are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research) and STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer) grants fund technology startups and small businesses to develop innovations with commercial potential. These grants are unique: they require both deep technical writing (describing the innovation at PhD level) AND commercial viability arguments (market size, business model, go-to-market strategy). Most grant writers can do one or the other — few can do both. The technical founders who would benefit most from SBIR funding often cannot find grant writers who understand their technology well enough to write a competitive proposal. This is the most underserved segment in the grant writing market.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SBIR.gov handles submission. Some accelerators (NSF I-Corps, DOD SBIR Direct to Phase II) provide proposal development support. No tool helps with the specific SBIR challenge: translating technical innovation into the format agencies require, generating the required commercialization plans, or analyzing the competitive landscape of previously funded SBIR topics. Previous SBIR award data is publicly available but rarely analyzed to inform new proposals.
