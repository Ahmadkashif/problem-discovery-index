# Business Plan Tool Adapted for SBIR Commercialization Requirements

**Niche:** [[niches/grant-writers/sbir-sttr/profile|SBIR/STTR Grant Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Standard business plan tools generate investor-oriented plans, but SBIR commercialization sections require a specific format emphasizing technology readiness levels, dual-use potential, and government-specific market analysis.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Every SBIR Phase II proposal requires a commercialization plan — a document that demonstrates the technology's market viability and the company's ability to transition from research to revenue. This is not a standard business plan: it must address Technology Readiness Level (TRL) progression, dual-use potential (military and commercial applications for DOD topics), government customer identification, manufacturing scalability, and intellectual property strategy in formats that SBIR reviewers expect. General business plan tools (LivePlan, Bizplan) generate investor-ready documents that miss SBIR-specific requirements and terminology.

## What Already Exists
LivePlan and Bizplan generate structured business plans with financial projections. SBIR.gov provides a commercialization plan template but it's a blank Word document. Some accelerators (I-Corps, TechLink) offer commercialization training. No tool generates SBIR-specific commercialization plans with: TRL mapping, dual-use analysis frameworks, government customer identification databases, or SBIR-specific financial projection models (which differ from startup projections because Phase III revenue sources are government contracts, not venture-backed growth).

## The Customization Gap
The business plan tool needs SBIR-specific intelligence: (1) TRL assessment framework with evidence documentation for each level, (2) dual-use market analysis comparing military/government and commercial applications, (3) government customer identification using USAspending.gov and FPDS data, (4) manufacturing scalability plan templates appropriate for hardware, software, and bio-tech ventures, (5) IP strategy guidance specific to government-funded research (Bayh-Dole implications, DFARS clauses), and (6) financial projections modeling Phase III government contract revenue alongside commercial revenue.

## Target Customer
SBIR grant writers who spend 8-12 hours per Phase II proposal developing the commercialization plan, and technical founders who need to demonstrate business acumen to reviewers.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces commercialization plan development time from 8-12 hours to 3-4 hours, improves plan quality by ensuring all SBIR-specific sections are addressed, and increases Phase II success rates by strengthening the commercialization narrative — typically the weakest section of technical founder proposals.
