# Compliance Tracking System Adapted for Pass-Through Federal Grants

**Niche:** [[niches/grant-writers/municipal-government/profile|Municipal & Government Agency Grant Seekers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Compliance management tools track regulatory requirements generally but don't model the layered compliance chains of pass-through federal grants — where federal, state, and local requirements stack and sometimes conflict.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A city receiving CDBG funds through the state must comply with: federal 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance), HUD-specific regulations (24 CFR Part 570), state-added requirements (which vary by state), and local procurement rules. These requirements overlap and sometimes conflict — the federal sole-source threshold may differ from the state's, which differs from the city's purchasing policy. A grant coordinator must apply the most restrictive requirement at each decision point but has no system that maps the layered compliance hierarchy. Violations discovered during audit can trigger fund repayment, future grant disqualification, or federal program sanctions.

## What Already Exists
Compliance management platforms (ZenGRC, LogicGate, Hyperproof) track regulatory compliance for corporate environments. Government compliance tools (FedRAMP, FISMA trackers) focus on IT security compliance. Grant-specific tools (Fluxx, AmpliFund) track grant reporting deadlines. None model the multi-layer compliance chain specific to pass-through grants: identifying which requirements apply at each layer, resolving conflicts between layers, and documenting compliance decisions for audit defense.

## The Customization Gap
The compliance system needs pass-through grant intelligence: (1) layered compliance frameworks showing federal, state, and local requirements for each grant type, (2) conflict identification when requirements at different levels contradict, (3) resolution guidance ("apply the most restrictive threshold"), (4) per-grant compliance checklists that combine all applicable layers, (5) documentation templates for each compliance requirement (procurement records, NEPA reviews, Davis-Bacon wage determinations), and (6) audit trail linking compliance actions to specific regulatory citations.

## Target Customer
Municipal grant coordinators managing 5+ pass-through federal grants who have been cited in audits for compliance failures or who live in fear of upcoming Single Audits.

## Impact If Solved
Prevents compliance violations that average $50,000-500,000 in repayment per incident, reduces audit preparation time by 40-60%, and builds institutional compliance knowledge that survives staff turnover.
