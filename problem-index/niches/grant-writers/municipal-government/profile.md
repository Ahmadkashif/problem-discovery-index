**Target Buyer:** Municipal grant coordinator or government affairs officer
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Federal-to-local grant compliance chains are rule-based but involve complex cross-agency documentation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Municipalities and government agencies pursue grants differently than nonprofits. They deal with intergovernmental grants (federal to state, state to local) with pass-through compliance requirements that chain through multiple levels of government. A city receiving a CDBG (Community Development Block Grant) from HUD through the state must comply with federal regulations, state-specific additional requirements, and local procurement rules — simultaneously. Grant writers serving municipalities must understand public procurement law, prevailing wage requirements, environmental review (NEPA), and citizens' participation processes that don't apply to nonprofit grants.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Government agencies use procurement systems (OpenGov, Tyler Technologies) for purchasing and budgeting, but these don't handle grant applications. Some use GrantSolutions or SAM.gov for federal grant tracking. Most municipal grant coordination happens in shared drives, email chains, and spreadsheets maintained by a single grant coordinator. No platform manages the full municipal grant lifecycle: opportunity identification, application development, intergovernmental compliance tracking, and pass-through reporting.
