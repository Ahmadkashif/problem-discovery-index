**Parent Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Grants.gov submission is digital, but proposal development, budget building, and compliance checking remain largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Grant writing firm owner or senior federal grant writer
**Automation Potential:** High — Federal grant applications follow rigid templates, scoring criteria, and compliance requirements that are deterministic and well-documented.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Federal grants (NIH, NSF, DOE, USDA, DOD, HHS, EPA) represent the largest funding pool but have the most demanding application requirements. Each agency has its own submission portal, formatting requirements, budget templates, assurance forms, and review criteria. A single NIH R01 application requires: a 12-page research strategy, budget justification, biographical sketches in NIH format, facilities description, equipment list, and 15-20 supporting documents — all formatted to exact specifications. A formatting error can result in administrative rejection before peer review. Federal grant writers must master agency-specific requirements while managing 4-8 concurrent proposals with overlapping deadlines.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Grants.gov and agency-specific portals (eRA Commons for NIH, Research.gov for NSF) handle submission. Cayuse and SPAR handle some institutional workflow. Word and Excel dominate proposal writing and budget development. No platform provides: agency-specific compliance checking before submission, automated budget-to-narrative consistency validation, or proposal section reuse across applications with automatic formatting adaptation.
