## Why These Niches

Federal grants represent the largest funding pool but involve the most complex application requirements (grants.gov, SF-424 forms, CFDA compliance), while foundation grants require entirely different proposal formats and relationship management. Small nonprofit grant writers are the most digitally neglected — they serve organizations with $500K-5M budgets using Word documents and spreadsheet calendars. SBIR/STTR specialists serve a growing but underserved market where technical founders need grant writing help but can't find writers who understand deep tech. Rural and tribal communities face acute grant access inequality — they need the most funding but have the least grant-writing capacity. The automation niches target the two most time-intensive non-writing activities: finding eligible grants (currently a manual research process consuming 30-40% of a grant writer's time) and post-award compliance reporting (which most grant writers don't offer, leaving clients scrambling).
