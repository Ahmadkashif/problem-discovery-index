# CRM Adapted for Grant Writer Funder Pipeline Management

**Niche:** [[niches/grant-writers/foundation-corporate/profile|Foundation & Corporate Grant Writers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Standard CRMs track customer relationships but don't model the grant writer's unique pipeline — where the "customer" is a funder, the "deal" is a proposal, and the "sales cycle" includes LOIs, full proposals, site visits, and multi-year renewal cycles.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A grant writer managing relationships with 100+ foundations needs to track: which foundations are in the research phase (potential fit but no contact), LOI submitted (waiting for invitation to full proposal), full proposal submitted (under review), funded (active grant with reporting requirements), and declined (with notes on why, for future attempts). This is a pipeline — but not a sales pipeline. The stages, timelines, and required actions at each stage are specific to grant writing. CRMs model leads → opportunities → closed/won. Grant writing needs prospects → LOI → invitation → proposal → review → funded/declined → reporting → renewal.

## What Already Exists
Salesforce for Nonprofits handles donor pipeline management. GrantHub and Submittable track grant applications. HubSpot and Pipedrive model sales pipelines. None model the grant writer's specific pipeline: the LOI-before-proposal workflow, the invitation-to-apply stage (unique to foundation grants), multi-year grant renewal cycles, or the need to track the same funder-client pair across multiple grant opportunities simultaneously.

## The Customization Gap
The CRM needs grant-specific pipeline stages and data fields: (1) funder profile with giving history, deadlines, and program officer contacts, (2) LOI stage with word count requirements, submission method, and response timeline, (3) invitation tracking with full proposal deadline and requirements, (4) proposal version management linked to the funder record, (5) review stage with site visit scheduling, (6) funded stage linking to reporting requirements and renewal dates, (7) declined stage with debrief notes and re-approach timeline, and (8) multi-client views (tracking the same foundation across multiple client engagements).

## Target Customer
Grant writing firms managing 20+ active funder relationships across 5+ clients, currently tracking pipeline status in spreadsheets or generic CRMs.

## Impact If Solved
Prevents missed LOI deadlines (estimated 5-10% of opportunities lost annually), enables systematic follow-up on declined proposals, and provides pipeline visibility that supports workload planning and revenue forecasting.
