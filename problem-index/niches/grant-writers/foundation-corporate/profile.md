## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Foundation and corporate grant writing is relationship-driven in ways that federal grant writing is not. A federal grant is evaluated anonymously by peer review. A foundation grant is evaluated by program officers who know the applicant organization, have preferences for certain approaches, and respond to relationship cultivation. Success depends as much on understanding a foundation's giving priorities, program officer preferences, and proposal framing as on the quality of the project itself. Grant writers must track relationships with hundreds of foundations, monitor giving trends, manage LOI (Letter of Inquiry) submissions that precede full proposals, and customize proposal language to match each foundation's values and terminology.

## Current Tools & Gaps
GrantHub and Fluxx handle some grant tracking and reporting. Foundation Directory Online (Candid) provides funder research. CRM tools (Salesforce for Nonprofits, Bloomerang) track donor relationships but aren't designed for the grant writer's workflow. No tool combines: funder relationship CRM, LOI tracking and management, proposal version control across multiple funders, and foundation-specific language pattern analysis that helps writers match their proposals to each funder's voice.
