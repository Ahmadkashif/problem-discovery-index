# LOI Tracking & Follow-Up Workflow Gaps

**Niche:** [[niches/grant-writers/foundation-corporate/profile|Foundation & Corporate Grant Writers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Letters of Inquiry are submitted to foundations with no systematic tracking of response timelines, follow-up actions, or conversion to full proposal invitations — LOIs disappear into a black hole.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A grant writer submits an LOI (Letter of Inquiry) to a foundation, which may respond in 2 weeks or 3 months, or not at all. The LOI might go through a foundation portal, email, or even postal mail. The writer tracks LOI status in a spreadsheet or, more commonly, in their memory. When managing 15-30 active LOIs across multiple clients and foundations, follow-up falls through the cracks. A foundation that invites a full proposal may send the invitation via email — which gets buried. A foundation that doesn't respond within their stated timeline doesn't receive a polite follow-up because the writer forgot the timeline. Each lost LOI follow-up represents a potential $25,000-500,000 grant opportunity abandoned.

## Why It's Still Broken
LOIs have no standardized submission format — some go through Fluxx or Submittable portals, others through email, others through foundation-specific web forms. There's no single system that captures all LOI submissions and their expected response timelines. Grant management tools (GrantHub) track applications but don't model the pre-application LOI phase as a distinct workflow. The result is that the most critical conversion point in foundation grant writing — LOI to invitation — has the least systematic management.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An LOI tracking workflow that: (1) logs every LOI submission with funder, client, submission date, method, and expected response window, (2) sets follow-up reminders at the end of the expected response window, (3) prompts follow-up actions ("no response from Smith Foundation after 6 weeks — send polite inquiry?"), (4) tracks conversion metrics (LOI to invitation rate by funder, by topic area, by writer), (5) archives declined LOIs with reasons for future reference, and (6) alerts when an invited LOI's full proposal deadline is approaching. Simple enough to maintain with 2 minutes of data entry per LOI.

## Who Feels the Pain
Grant writers who discover they missed a full-proposal invitation buried in email 3 weeks ago, and clients who learn that a promising funder lead was never followed up on.

## Impact If Fixed
Recovers 10-15% of opportunities currently lost to follow-up failures, improves LOI-to-invitation conversion through systematic follow-up, and provides data on which LOI strategies produce the highest invitation rates.
