# Funder Relationship Intelligence & Proposal Matching Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/grant-writers/foundation-corporate/profile|Foundation & Corporate Grant Writers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform analyzes foundation giving patterns, program officer preferences, and historical award data to help grant writers match proposals to the funders most likely to fund them.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #nlp #recommendation #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
An experienced grant writer "knows" which foundations are likely to fund a youth mental health program — not just from published giving priorities, but from years of reading annual reports, attending conferences, tracking board member changes, and noting which proposals succeeded and which were declined. This tacit knowledge about funder alignment takes years to develop and cannot be transferred to junior writers. A senior writer retiring or leaving a firm takes hundreds of funder relationships and alignment insights with them, leaving the firm to rebuild from public data alone.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Foundation giving data is partially public (990 tax forms show past grants) but funder priorities, program officer preferences, and evaluation criteria are not systematically published. Building a matching platform requires: aggregating 990 data across 100,000+ foundations, analyzing giving trends over time, extracting priority signals from annual reports and guidelines, and incorporating win/loss data from grant writing firms. The grant writing market is fragmented (mostly solo practitioners and small firms) with low per-firm technology spend, making this a difficult business model.

## What to Build
A funder intelligence platform that: (1) aggregates foundation 990 data to show giving history by topic, geography, and grant size, (2) analyzes foundation annual reports and guidelines to extract current priority signals, (3) tracks program officer tenure and focus area changes, (4) accepts grant writer win/loss data to build a community-contributed alignment model, (5) matches new project descriptions against funder profiles to rank likely alignment, and (6) generates funder-specific proposal framing recommendations ("this foundation emphasizes community voice — lead with participatory design, not expert intervention").

## Target Customer
Grant writing firms managing 50+ funder relationships who currently rely on senior writers' institutional memory for funder matching.

## Impact If Built
Increases proposal win rates by 10-20% through better funder-project matching, enables junior writers to identify appropriate funders without senior writer guidance, and preserves funder relationship intelligence when staff transitions occur.
