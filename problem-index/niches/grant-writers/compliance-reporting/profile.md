# Compliance & Reporting Post-Award

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/grant-writers|Grant Writers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.6B (admin cost pool)
**Share of Parent Industry:** 14% (of total grant management costs)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Post-award reporting is largely manual, using spreadsheets and word processors to produce funder-required reports.
**Target Buyer:** Grant manager, development director, or grant writing firm offering post-award services
**Automation Potential:** High — Reporting requirements are structured, deadlines are predictable, and much of the data is already collected for programmatic purposes.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Most grant writers disengage after award, leaving the funded organization to handle compliance and reporting alone. This creates a massive service gap: the organization received $500,000 because of the grant writer's proposal, but must now submit quarterly financial reports, annual programmatic reports, and a final close-out report — all in funder-specific formats they've never seen. The administrative burden of grant compliance causes many small organizations to dread receiving grants almost as much as they dread not having funding. Post-award compliance is the most automatable phase of the grant lifecycle and the most underserved by technology.

## Current Tools & Gaps
AmpliFund and Fluxx handle some grant reporting for larger organizations. Most small nonprofits use Word templates and Excel for financial reports. Government grantees use GrantSolutions and PMS (Payment Management System) for drawdowns and some reporting. No affordable tool provides: multi-funder report generation from a single data set, deadline tracking across 10-30 active grants, financial-to-programmatic data linkage, and automated compliance checking against grant-specific terms and conditions.
