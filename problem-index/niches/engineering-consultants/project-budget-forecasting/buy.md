# Change Order Workflow Adapted for Engineering Firms

**Niche:** [[niches/engineering-consultants/project-budget-forecasting/profile|Project Budget Forecasting & Change Orders]]
**Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Construction change order management tools exist (Procore, PlanGrid) but don't support the engineering consultant's change order workflow — where the "change" is additional scope requested by the client, not a field condition change, and the output is a fee amendment letter, not a construction change order.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
When a client requests work outside the original scope — an additional analysis, a redesigned alternative, an extra set of construction documents for a phased project — the PM must recognize it as out-of-scope, calculate the additional fee, draft a change order letter, get it approved, and update the project budget. In practice, PMs are reluctant to file change orders because it creates friction with the client, and they lack a standard workflow for calculating the additional fee. The result: 40-60% of out-of-scope work is never formalized as a change order, and the firm absorbs the cost.

## What Already Exists
Procore, PlanGrid (now Autodesk Build), and Fieldwire provide construction change order management — tracking RFIs, field change orders, and cost impacts for general contractors and owners. These tools model construction changes (material substitutions, field conditions, design revisions) not professional service scope changes. Deltek and BST include contract modification modules, but they are cumbersome to use and disconnected from the PM's daily workflow.

## The Customization Gap
Engineering change orders are simpler than construction change orders but have unique requirements: (1) the scope change must be mapped to specific proposal phases and additional hours, (2) the fee calculation must use the contract's billing rate schedule, (3) the approval must be client-signed before work begins, and (4) the approved change must flow back into the project budget so burn tracking reflects the updated scope. The gap is a lightweight, PM-friendly change order tool that integrates with the firm's timesheet/billing system: the PM describes the additional scope, the tool calculates the fee from rate schedules, generates a client-ready letter, tracks approval status, and updates the project budget when approved.

## Target Customer
PMs at engineering firms who currently avoid filing change orders because the process is too cumbersome, resulting in 40-60% of out-of-scope work going uncompensated.

## Impact If Solved
Converting even 30% of currently unfiled change orders to approved additional services recovers 2-4% of firm revenue. For a $3M/year firm, this represents $60K-120K in recaptured fees annually.
