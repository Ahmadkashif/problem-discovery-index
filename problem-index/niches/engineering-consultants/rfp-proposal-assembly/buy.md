# AEC Proposal Tool Scaled Down for Mid-Size Firms

**Niche:** [[niches/engineering-consultants/rfp-proposal-assembly/profile|RFP/Proposal Assembly Workflows]]
**Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Enterprise AEC proposal and CRM tools exist (Cosential/Unanet CRM, Deltek Vision) but assume a dedicated marketing department — mid-size firms need the content management without the CRM complexity, at one-third the price.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
A 20-person engineering firm needs to manage project sheets, staff resumes, SF330 forms (required for federal proposals), and proposal templates — but they don't need a full CRM with pipeline tracking, contact management, and business development workflows. Enterprise AEC tools bundle proposal content management with CRM, pipeline, and marketing automation features that a 20-person firm will never use, at a price point ($300-500/month per user) that only makes sense for firms with dedicated marketing staff. The firm needs a content vault, not a CRM.

## What Already Exists
Cosential (now Unanet CRM) provides project tracking, resume management, and SF330 generation for AEC firms. OpenAsset manages project photography and imagery for proposals. Deltek Vision/Vantagepoint includes a proposal module alongside its ERP. All are designed for firms with 50+ employees and full-time marketing coordinators.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is extracting the content management core from the CRM wrapper and packaging it at a price and complexity level appropriate for firms with 10-30 employees and no dedicated marketing staff. Specifically: (1) a structured resume database where staff profiles auto-update as projects are completed, (2) a project sheet library with consistent formatting and searchable metadata (client, project type, fee size, completion date, services provided), (3) an SF330 generator that auto-populates from the resume and project databases, and (4) a proposal template builder that pulls from both databases. No CRM pipeline, no contact management, no email marketing — just content management for proposals. Price point: $100-200/month for the firm (not per user).

## Target Customer
Engineering firms with 10-30 employees that currently manage proposal content in Word documents and shared drives, intimidated by the complexity and cost of enterprise AEC CRM tools.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 50-60% of proposal assembly time by providing structured, searchable content instead of folder-diving. SF330 generation alone — currently a 4-8 hour manual process per federal proposal — drops to 30-60 minutes. For a firm submitting 10+ federal proposals per year, this saves 35-75 hours on SF330s alone.
