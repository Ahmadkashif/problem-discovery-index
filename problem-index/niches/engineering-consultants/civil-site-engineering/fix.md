# Municipal Plan Submittal Package Assembly

**Niche:** [[niches/engineering-consultants/civil-site-engineering/profile|Civil/Site Engineering Firms]]
**Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Assembling a municipal plan submittal — reformatting plan sheets, compiling calculation books, populating application forms, and organizing digital uploads — takes 6-12 hours per project and is done differently for every jurisdiction.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
After completing the engineering design, the civil engineer or a CAD technician must assemble a submittal package that meets the specific municipality's requirements: plan sheet sizes and title block formats, required sheet order, calculation report organization, permit application forms, fee calculations, and digital upload specifications (some municipalities want PDFs, some want DWG files, some want both, some require specific file naming conventions). A firm submitting to 10 different municipalities maintains 10 different submittal checklists, each partially outdated. Missing a required item triggers a "submittal incomplete" rejection that delays the project by 2-4 weeks and wastes 4-8 hours of staff time resubmitting.

## Why It's Still Broken
Municipal submittal requirements are published in scattered locations — some on the planning department website, some in the engineering standards manual, some only communicated verbally at pre-application meetings. Requirements change without notice when municipalities update their portals or adopt new review software (e.g., switching from paper submittals to Bluebeam Studio or ProjectDox). No central repository of submittal requirements exists, and municipalities have no incentive to standardize because their formats serve internal review workflows. Engineering firms treat submittal assembly as grunt work assigned to the most junior staff member, ensuring maximum error rate.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A submittal assembly tool that maintains structured profiles for each municipality: required documents, format specifications, sheet order, naming conventions, and digital upload requirements. When a project reaches the submittal milestone, the tool generates a populated checklist, auto-formats plan sheets to the correct title block and size, compiles calculation reports in the required order, and validates the package against the municipality's requirements before upload. For municipalities using digital review platforms (ProjectDox, ePlan), the tool auto-maps files to the correct upload categories.

## Who Feels the Pain
Junior engineers and CAD technicians who spend 6-12 hours per submittal assembling packages from memory and incomplete checklists, and project managers who absorb the 2-4 week delay when submittals are rejected as incomplete.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces submittal assembly from 6-12 hours to 1-2 hours per project. Eliminates 80% of "submittal incomplete" rejections, each of which costs $2,000-5,000 in delayed revenue and staff rework time. For a firm submitting 40 packages/year, this saves $30,000-60,000 annually.
