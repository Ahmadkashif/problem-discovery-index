# Civil/Site Engineering Firms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $70-80B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~23%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Civil 3D is standard for design, but stormwater modeling, grading optimization, and municipal submittal workflows are poorly automated
**Target Buyer:** Civil engineering firm owner or senior project manager at a 5-30 person site/land development firm
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — grading calculations, stormwater detention sizing, and utility layout follow deterministic engineering rules that are still solved manually or semi-manually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Civil/site engineering firms design the horizontal infrastructure for land development: grading, stormwater management, utilities (water, sewer, storm), roadway geometry, and erosion control. These firms are the interface between land developers and municipal permitting authorities, spending as much time navigating jurisdiction-specific design standards and submittal requirements as they do on engineering design. A typical 15-person civil firm handles 30-60 active projects simultaneously across 5-15 different municipalities, each with its own stormwater manual, road cross-section standards, utility specifications, and plan sheet formatting requirements. The work is high-volume, moderate-margin, and extremely repetitive across projects of similar type — yet each project requires manual adaptation to the specific municipality's standards.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Autodesk Civil 3D dominates design production. Hydrology/hydraulic modeling uses StormCAD, HydroCAD, or EPA SWMM, but results must be manually transferred to Civil 3D. Municipal submittal preparation is entirely manual — reformatting plan sets, generating calculation books, and populating permit application forms for each jurisdiction. No platform connects the design model, engineering calculations, and municipal submission requirements in one workflow.
