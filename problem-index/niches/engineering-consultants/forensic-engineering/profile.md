**Parent Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-7B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~2%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — field inspections use cameras and handwritten notes, report generation is Word-based, case management is spreadsheets or generic legal tools
**Target Buyer:** Forensic engineering firm principal or senior engineer serving insurance carriers, attorneys, and property owners
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the engineering analysis is bespoke, but field documentation, photo management, report templating, and case tracking are highly repetitive

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Forensic engineering firms investigate failures — building collapses, water intrusion, fire damage, foundation settlement, roof wind damage, vehicle accidents. Their workflow is fundamentally different from design engineering: they inspect existing conditions, form expert opinions on causation, and produce reports that become evidence in insurance claims and litigation. The typical forensic firm handles 200-500 cases per year, each requiring a site inspection (photos, measurements, material samples), research into applicable codes and standards at the time of construction, analysis of failure mode, and a written report with opinions on causation and repair cost. The deliverable is not a design — it is a persuasive technical narrative that must withstand cross-examination. No purpose-built software exists for this workflow.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Field documentation uses smartphone cameras with photos organized in folders by case number. Reports are generated in Word from firm-specific templates. Case tracking uses Excel or generic project management tools (Monday.com, Asana) that don't model the forensic workflow (inspection → analysis → report → deposition → trial). Photo management is the biggest pain point: a single inspection generates 100-500 photos that must be labeled, organized by location/deficiency, and embedded in the report. No tool connects the field inspection, photo library, code research, and report generation in a forensic-specific workflow.
