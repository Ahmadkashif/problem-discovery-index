# Photo Management Adapted for Forensic Case Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/engineering-consultants/forensic-engineering/profile|Forensic Engineering Consultants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Photo management tools exist for construction and inspection, but none handle the forensic workflow — photos must be organized by deficiency type, linked to code violations, timestamped for evidentiary chain, and formatted into report-ready photo plates with captions.
**Tags:** #cnn #multiclass-classification #computer-vision #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
A single forensic inspection produces 100-500 photos. The engineer returns to the office and spends 2-4 hours sorting photos into folders (by elevation, by room, by deficiency type), deleting duplicates, selecting the best representative images, writing captions, and arranging photo plates for the report (typically 4-6 photos per page with numbered captions). When the attorney requests additional photos or the case goes to deposition, the engineer must search through the original photo set — organized only by filename timestamp — to find specific images. This photo management workflow consumes 15-20% of total case hours.

## What Already Exists
CompanyCam ($19/user/month) handles construction photo documentation with geolocation and project tagging. OpenSpace and HoloBuilder provide 360-degree capture for construction progress documentation. Adobe Lightroom handles professional photo organization and editing. None of these tools model the forensic workflow: deficiency-based categorization, evidentiary metadata (chain of custody, timestamp verification), code violation linking, and report-ready photo plate generation.

## The Customization Gap
Forensic photo management needs three capabilities that generic tools lack: (1) deficiency-based auto-tagging — a photo of a cracked foundation should auto-categorize under "foundation > settlement > cracking" with the engineer confirming or correcting, (2) evidentiary metadata — original file hash, GPS coordinates, capture timestamp, and a chain-of-custody log for legal defensibility, and (3) report plate generation — select 6 photos, auto-generate a formatted page with numbered captions and figure references that embed directly into the Word report template. A CompanyCam-style platform with these three forensic-specific features would eliminate 60-70% of the photo management burden.

## Target Customer
Forensic engineers conducting 150+ inspections per year, currently spending 2-4 hours per case organizing photos in Windows Explorer and manually building photo plates in Word.

## Impact If Solved
Cuts photo management from 2-4 hours to 30-60 minutes per case. For a firm handling 300 cases/year, this saves 450-900 hours annually. Improves deposition preparation — the engineer can instantly retrieve any photo by deficiency type and location rather than scrolling through hundreds of timestamped files.
