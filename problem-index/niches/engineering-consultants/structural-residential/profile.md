# Structural Engineering for Residential

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — structural analysis software (RISA, Enercalc) is used for calculations, but design workflows are still heavily manual for residential-scale projects
**Target Buyer:** Structural PE serving residential builders, architects, and homeowners, typically at a 1-5 person firm
**Automation Potential:** High — residential structural design involves repetitive calculations (beam sizing, header schedules, deck connections) governed by prescriptive code provisions that are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential structural engineers design foundations, floor systems, roof framing, retaining walls, and lateral force-resisting systems for homes and small multi-family buildings. The work is high-volume, low-fee: a typical residential structural package (foundation plan, framing plan, details, calculations) pays $1,500-5,000, compared to $50,000+ for a commercial structural project. A busy residential PE processes 100-300 projects per year, each requiring essentially the same types of calculations adapted to slightly different spans, loads, and soil conditions. The prescriptive provisions of the IRC (International Residential Code) cover 70-80% of typical residential framing without engineering calculations, but builders need a PE stamp for anything beyond prescriptive limits — cantilevered balconies, large openings, unusual soil conditions, or renovation of existing structures. This creates a market of small, repetitive engineering tasks that are too numerous to ignore but too low-fee to justify spending 20 hours on each.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Structural analysis software (RISA, Enercalc, Tekla Tedds) handles individual calculations (beam design, column load analysis) but doesn't automate the full residential workflow: loading the floor plan, identifying structural elements, running all required calculations, and generating the stamped structural drawing set. The engineer manually interprets architectural plans, sets up each calculation individually, and drafts structural plans in AutoCAD or hand-sketches with markups. No end-to-end residential structural design tool exists that takes an architectural floor plan and produces the structural package.
