## Why These Niches

Engineering consulting fragments along discipline (MEP, civil, structural, environmental), firm size (sole practitioner vs. 200-person multi-discipline), client type (private developer vs. municipality vs. insurance/legal), and business function (design delivery vs. business development vs. financial management). These 8 niches cover the two dominant revenue segments (MEP and civil/site), the two most digitally neglected segments (sole practitioners who run everything on spreadsheets and forensic engineers whose litigation-driven workflow has no purpose-built software), the two most underserved client-facing niches (municipalities that need infrastructure engineering but get treated as low-margin afterthoughts, and residential structural PEs who are too small for commercial software), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (proposal assembly and project budget forecasting, which together consume 15-25% of non-billable hours at most firms). Excluded: geotechnical engineering (specialized enough for its own industry entry), traffic/transportation engineering (heavily public-sector with distinct procurement), and surveying (covered separately as land-surveyors).
