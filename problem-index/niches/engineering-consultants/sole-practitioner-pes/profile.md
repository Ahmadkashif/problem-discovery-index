# Sole Practitioner PEs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/engineering-consultants|Engineering Consultants]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most run AutoCAD (not Revit), track projects in Excel or QuickBooks, and manage proposals in Word documents
**Target Buyer:** Solo Professional Engineer or 1-3 person firm principal who handles all engineering, project management, and business functions
**Automation Potential:** High — the sole practitioner is the bottleneck for every function, so any automation of non-engineering tasks directly increases billable capacity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Approximately 15,000-20,000 PE-licensed engineers in the US operate as sole practitioners or micro-firms (1-3 people). They typically specialize in a single discipline — structural residential, small commercial HVAC, site grading, or plan review — and compete on responsiveness and local relationships rather than firm size. The sole practitioner is simultaneously the engineer, project manager, proposal writer, bookkeeper, and marketing department. They can't afford Deltek ($500+/month) or full Autodesk suites ($3,000+/year per seat), and enterprise engineering tools are designed for teams, not individuals. Their "project management system" is a combination of email, a spiral notebook, and memory. Business development is entirely referral-based with no CRM. Cash flow management is seat-of-the-pants — they know they are busy but can't tell you whether they are profitable until tax season.

## Current Tools & Gaps
AutoCAD LT ($460/year) or older perpetual-license AutoCAD versions are common; Revit adoption is <10% among sole practitioners. Accounting is QuickBooks Self-Employed or a spreadsheet. Time tracking ranges from manual timesheets to nothing. Proposal generation is copying last year's Word document. No engineering-specific practice management tool exists at the price point ($50-100/month) and simplicity level these practitioners need.
