# International Student Admissions Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$4B in tuition revenue from international students at US private K-12 schools (boarding and day schools with I-20 programs)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — admissions CRMs handle domestic applications but lack international-specific workflows; SEVIS compliance is managed through a separate government system; cross-time-zone communication tools are generic
**Target Buyer:** Admissions director or international student coordinator at schools enrolling 20+ international students per year
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — SEVIS compliance, I-20 document generation, and international transcript evaluation follow deterministic rules with high administrative overhead

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Schools that enroll international students (primarily boarding schools and some day schools with homestay programs) operate a parallel admissions and compliance process: I-20 form generation, SEVIS registration, visa interview preparation support, international transcript evaluation (converting grading scales from China, South Korea, India, etc.), English proficiency assessment (TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo), and ongoing SEVIS reporting (enrollment status, address changes, program completion). This compliance overhead is managed by a single international coordinator who is typically not the admissions director, creating workflow fragmentation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Admissions CRMs (Ravenna, SchoolAdmin) handle domestic applications but do not include international-specific fields (passport information, visa status, SEVIS ID, English proficiency scores). SEVIS is a government portal with a clunky interface that does not integrate with any school system. International transcript evaluation is done manually or outsourced to credential evaluation services (WES, ECE) at $150-$300 per evaluation. No tool combines international admissions, SEVIS compliance, and transcript evaluation into a unified workflow.
