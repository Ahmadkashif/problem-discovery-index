# Single-Campus Rural Private Schools

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$3B in tuition revenue across ~5,000 small private schools in rural and exurban areas
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many operate with basic tools (Google Workspace, paper records, QuickBooks for billing); SIS adoption is inconsistent; IT support is typically a tech-savvy teacher or parent volunteer
**Target Buyer:** School administrator (often a combined principal/administrator role) or board chair
**Automation Potential:** Medium — standard school workflows apply but are constrained by minimal budget, staff, and technical expertise

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Rural private schools operate with extreme resource constraints: a typical school has 50-200 students, 5-15 teachers, 1-2 administrative staff, and a total operating budget under $1M. The administrator wears every hat — principal, admissions director, business office, communications, and facilities manager. These schools cannot afford Blackbaud or Veracross ($10K-$30K/year), and their needs are simpler: they need enrollment tracking, tuition billing, basic grade recording, and parent communication. The challenge is finding affordable tools that work for a one-person admin office.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many rural private schools use Google Workspace (Sheets for grades, Forms for enrollment, Drive for documents), QuickBooks for billing, and Mailchimp for parent newsletters. Some have adopted affordable SIS tools (Gradelink, FACTS basic tier). The gap is integration — each tool operates independently, and the administrator manually transfers data between them. There is no affordable, all-in-one solution designed for a school with 100 students and a $500/year technology budget.
