# Comment Bank System with Student-Data Integration

**Niche:** [[niches/k12-private-schools/report-card-narrative-generation/profile|Report Card Narrative Generation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Comment bank tools exist for schools, but they are static phrase libraries disconnected from student data — they cannot suggest relevant comments based on a student's actual academic performance and behavior patterns.
**Tags:** #recommendation #nlp #data-integration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Schools maintain comment banks — organized collections of narrative phrases like "demonstrates strong critical thinking skills" or "would benefit from additional practice with fraction operations." Teachers browse these banks and assemble narratives by selecting and customizing phrases. But the banks are not connected to student data: a teacher looking at a student with a 92% math average and strong assignment completion must manually identify which comments are relevant. The comment bank cannot recommend "demonstrates mastery of multiplication and division concepts" based on the student's actual assessment results.

## What Already Exists
Several comment bank tools exist for schools (Report Card Writer, Comment Builder, TeacherEase comments module). These are organized by subject, skill area, and performance level. SIS platforms (Veracross, FACTS) store student grades and assessment data. The comment banks and the SIS are not connected.

## The Customization Gap
A data-integrated comment bank must: (1) ingest student performance data from the SIS (grades, assessment scores, assignment completion rates); (2) map performance patterns to relevant comment categories (a student with declining math grades gets different suggestions than one with rising grades); (3) allow school-specific customization of the comment library (each school has its own communication style and vocabulary); (4) track which comments have been used for the same student in prior reporting periods to avoid repetition across cycles.

## Target Customer
Teachers and division heads at schools already using static comment banks who want to reduce the time spent browsing and selecting relevant phrases.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-student narrative assembly time from 8-10 minutes to 4-5 minutes by surfacing relevant comments automatically. Eliminates cross-cycle repetition that parents notice and find frustrating. Improves comment specificity by grounding suggestions in actual student data.
