# Learning Difference & Special Needs Schools

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$3B in tuition revenue across ~2,000 private schools specializing in learning differences (dyslexia, ADHD, autism spectrum, executive function disorders)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — standard SIS tools do not accommodate individualized learning plans, goal tracking, or therapeutic service documentation; schools use parallel paper or spreadsheet systems for IEP-equivalent documentation
**Target Buyer:** Head of school or director of learning specialists at schools serving students with learning differences
**Automation Potential:** Medium — individualized goal tracking, accommodation documentation, and progress reporting follow structured frameworks (Orton-Gillingham levels, sensory integration benchmarks) but are highly manual

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Private schools serving students with learning differences (often called "LD schools") operate fundamentally differently from traditional private schools: every student has an individualized learning profile, academic goals are measured on individual trajectories rather than grade-level standards, and therapeutic services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling) are integrated into the school day. Documentation requirements are intense — schools must maintain IEP-equivalent plans, track progress against individualized goals, and provide detailed reports to parents and educational psychologists. Standard SIS tools measure students against grade-level standards, which is the opposite of what LD schools need.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard SIS tools (FACTS, Veracross) track grades and attendance but cannot manage individualized learning plans with non-standard goal structures. Some schools use IEP management tools designed for public schools (GoalBook, Branching Minds), but these assume a public school special education context (IDEA compliance, CSE meetings) that does not apply to private LD schools. Therapeutic service documentation is typically maintained in separate systems by individual therapists. No tool provides a unified view of a student's academic progress, therapeutic goals, and accommodation implementation.
