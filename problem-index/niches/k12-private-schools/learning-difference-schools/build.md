# Individualized Learning Profile and Goal Tracking Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/k12-private-schools/learning-difference-schools/profile|Learning Difference & Special Needs Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform tracks individualized academic and therapeutic goals, accommodations, and progress for students with learning differences in a private school context where IEP-like documentation exists without IDEA's regulatory framework.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #time-series-forecasting #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Every student at an LD school has an individualized learning plan with academic goals (e.g., "read at Orton-Gillingham Level 4 by June"), therapeutic goals (e.g., "independently use sensory regulation strategies in 3 of 5 observed situations"), and classroom accommodations (e.g., "extended time on tests, preferential seating, text-to-speech access"). Progress against these goals must be tracked by 3-5 different staff members (classroom teachers, reading specialists, OT, speech, counseling), compiled into coherent progress reports, and shared with parents and external evaluators. This tracking happens across 4-6 disconnected systems and paper files.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The LD school market is small (~2,000 schools) and the goal structures are highly variable — each school uses different assessment frameworks, different therapeutic modalities, and different progress reporting formats. Building a platform requires supporting the diversity of LD approaches (Orton-Gillingham, Wilson Reading, Lindamood-Bell, sensory integration, executive function coaching) without imposing a single framework. Public school IEP tools assume a regulatory context (IDEA, CSE, transition planning) that does not apply.

## What to Build
A student-centric platform that maintains each student's learning profile (diagnoses, assessments, accommodation plan) and allows multiple staff members to track progress against individualized goals across academic and therapeutic domains. The platform generates unified progress reports that synthesize data from all contributors, visualizes growth trajectories on individual (not normative) scales, and manages the accommodation plan so every teacher knows what accommodations to provide without checking a separate document.

## Target Customer
Heads of school and learning specialist directors at LD schools with 80+ students, where 5-10 staff members contribute to each student's progress documentation.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-student progress reporting time from 3-5 hours per cycle (across all contributing staff) to 1-2 hours. Ensures accommodation consistency across all classrooms and activities. Provides parents with a unified view of their child's progress that currently requires assembling reports from 3-5 different staff members.
