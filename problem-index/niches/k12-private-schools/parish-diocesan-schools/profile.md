# Parish & Diocesan Schools

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$20B in tuition and parish subsidies across ~6,000 Catholic parish and diocesan schools in the US
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — FACTS SIS and Smart Tuition are widely adopted, but technology decisions are often made at the diocese level, limiting individual school flexibility; many schools run on skeleton IT budgets
**Target Buyer:** Principal (school-level decisions) or diocese superintendent (system-wide decisions)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — enrollment management, parish subsidy tracking, and diocesan reporting follow standardized processes across schools within a diocese

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Parish schools operate under a governance structure unique in K-12: the school answers to both the parish pastor and the diocese, with funding split between tuition, parish subsidy, and diocesan grants. This creates complex financial dynamics — a parish struggling financially may cut its school subsidy mid-year, forcing emergency tuition adjustments. Technology purchasing is often constrained by diocesan contracts (the diocese negotiates system-wide SIS/billing deals), limiting individual schools' ability to adopt best-of-breed tools. Teacher compensation is 20-30% below independent school and public school peers, creating retention challenges that compound the administrative burden on a thin staff.

## Current Tools & Gaps
FACTS SIS (Blackbaud) dominates parish school administration, with Smart Tuition handling billing. Dioceses often mandate these tools system-wide. The gaps are in enrollment marketing (parish schools compete with public schools and charters but lack marketing budgets), parish subsidy forecasting (no tool models the financial impact of parish funding changes on school viability), and diocesan reporting (schools manually compile reports for the diocese office using spreadsheets).
