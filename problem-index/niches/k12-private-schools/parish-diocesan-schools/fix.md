# Enrollment Marketing Gap Against Public School Competition

**Niche:** [[niches/k12-private-schools/parish-diocesan-schools/profile|Parish & Diocesan Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Parish schools compete for enrollment against free public schools and publicly funded charter schools but have zero marketing budget and no data-driven way to identify and reach prospective families in the parish and surrounding community.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
Parish school enrollment has declined 30% nationally over the past two decades. Schools compete against public schools (free), charter schools (free with marketing budgets), and independent schools (larger marketing budgets). A parish school's marketing typically consists of a parish bulletin announcement, a school website last updated 18 months ago, and word-of-mouth from current families. The principal — who also teaches, manages the building, and handles discipline — has no time for marketing strategy and no budget for advertising.

## Why It's Still Broken
Parish schools have not historically needed to market — enrollment came through the parish community automatically. As Catholic Mass attendance has declined and parish boundaries have become less meaningful, the automatic enrollment pipeline has dried up. Schools need to reach families outside the parish, including non-Catholic families, but lack the tools, budget, and expertise. Dioceses have not centralized marketing support because each school serves a distinct geographic community.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A low-cost, data-driven enrollment marketing system that identifies prospective families in the school's geographic area using public data (census demographics, residential sales, birth records, preschool enrollment in the area), generates targeted digital marketing (Facebook, Instagram, Google) within a $500-$2,000 annual budget, tracks inquiries through a simple admissions CRM, and measures yield by marketing channel. The system is simple enough for a principal with no marketing experience to operate.

## Who Feels the Pain
Parish school principals who watch enrollment decline by 5-10 students per year (worth $40K-$80K in tuition) with no tools or budget to reverse the trend, and diocese superintendents who oversee system-wide enrollment erosion.

## Impact If Fixed
Stabilizing enrollment by attracting 5-10 additional students per school generates $40K-$80K in annual tuition revenue — often the difference between viability and closure for a parish school operating on thin margins.
