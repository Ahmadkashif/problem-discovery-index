# Diocesan Reporting Automation from FACTS Data

**Niche:** [[niches/k12-private-schools/parish-diocesan-schools/profile|Parish & Diocesan Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** FACTS SIS holds all the data dioceses need for system-wide reporting, but extracting and formatting it into diocesan templates is a manual process that consumes 5-10 hours per school per quarter.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Dioceses require quarterly and annual reports from each school: enrollment counts by grade and demographic category, financial aid distribution, tuition collection rates, teacher credentials, sacramental participation, and standardized test scores. This data exists in FACTS SIS and Smart Tuition, but the diocese reporting templates are custom Excel or PDF formats that do not match FACTS' export structure. Each school's office manager spends 5-10 hours per quarter manually extracting data from FACTS, reformatting it into the diocesan template, and submitting it — often with errors that trigger follow-up requests.

## What Already Exists
FACTS SIS provides standard reports and data exports. Blackbaud offers some diocesan-level reporting tools, but they require system-wide implementation and do not accommodate the custom reporting templates that each diocese has developed over decades. Generic BI tools (Power BI, Tableau) could automate reporting but require technical expertise that parish schools lack.

## The Customization Gap
Each diocese has its own reporting templates, definitions (what counts as "Catholic" varies by diocese), and submission schedules. A reporting automation tool must be configured per diocese — mapping FACTS data fields to the specific template cells, applying diocesan-specific calculations (e.g., "net tuition revenue" may include or exclude parish subsidy depending on the diocese), and formatting output to match the exact template the diocese expects.

## Target Customer
Diocese superintendents seeking system-wide reporting automation across 10-50 schools, and individual school office managers spending 5-10 hours per quarter on report compilation.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-school reporting time from 5-10 hours to under 1 hour per quarter. At a 40-school diocese, that is 160-360 hours saved per quarter. Eliminates reporting errors that consume additional follow-up time and delay diocesan planning.
