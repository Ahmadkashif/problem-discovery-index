# Independent Day Schools (Non-Sectarian)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$18B in tuition revenue across ~3,000 non-sectarian independent day schools (NAIS member and non-member)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Veracross, Blackbaud, and Finalsite are widely adopted; schools have dedicated IT staff and technology budgets; but data remains siloed across 4-6 systems with minimal integration
**Target Buyer:** Head of school, CFO, or director of enrollment management
**Automation Potential:** Medium — enrollment management, advancement/fundraising, and parent communication are data-rich workflows with integration gaps

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent day schools charge $20K-$50K tuition and compete for affluent families on the basis of academic outcomes, community culture, and extracurricular programs. These schools have the most sophisticated technology adoption in the K-12 private sector — dedicated IT staff, budgets for best-of-breed tools — but suffer from a "best-of-breed fragmentation" problem: Veracross for SIS, Finalsite for web/CMS, Ravenna for admissions, a separate system for advancement/donor management, Google Classroom for LMS, and various point solutions for athletics, transportation, and health records. Data exists everywhere but flows nowhere.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Veracross and Blackbaud offer the closest thing to an integrated platform, but most schools supplement with Finalsite (web), Ravenna or SchoolAdmin (admissions CRM), and a separate advancement platform. The critical gap is a unified family data layer — admissions, enrollment, billing, academic progress, parent engagement, and giving history sit in separate systems, preventing the holistic family view that would enable yield prediction, retention intervention, and donor cultivation.
