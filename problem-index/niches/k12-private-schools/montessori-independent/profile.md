# Montessori Independent Schools

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$5B in tuition revenue across ~4,500 Montessori schools in the US (private/independent)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — standard SIS tools are designed for traditional grade-level, subject-based assessment; Montessori's competency-based, multi-age, narrative assessment model does not fit; many Montessori schools use paper-based observation records
**Target Buyer:** Head of school or owner-operator at a single-campus Montessori school
**Automation Potential:** Medium — observation recording, progress documentation, and parent communication follow consistent Montessori frameworks but are labor-intensive because they are narrative rather than numeric

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Montessori education uses a fundamentally different assessment model than traditional K-12: no letter grades, no standardized report cards, and multi-age classrooms where a "3rd grader" may be working at different levels across different competency areas. Teachers document student progress through written observations, work samples, and competency checklists aligned to Montessori scope and sequence (not Common Core grade levels). Standard SIS tools assume grade-level-subject-grade data structures that do not accommodate this model, forcing Montessori schools to maintain parallel paper-based records.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Transparent Classroom is the only Montessori-specific SIS, but it serves primarily the observation and lesson planning function, not the full school management suite (billing, admissions, communications). Most Montessori schools bolt Transparent Classroom onto a general SIS (FACTS, Gradelink) for billing and enrollment, creating a two-system workflow. Parent communication about student progress requires teachers to write lengthy narrative reports because the competency data does not translate into a standard report card.
