# Delinquent Tuition Follow-Up in a Relationship Context

**Niche:** [[niches/k12-private-schools/tuition-billing-aid-automation/profile|Tuition Billing & Financial Aid Optimization]]
**Industry:** [[industries/k12-private-schools|K-12 Private Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Schools have no automated, relationship-sensitive escalation workflow for delinquent tuition — the business office director sends awkward personal emails to parents they see at school events every week.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a family falls behind on tuition, the business office director — who knows the family personally, sees them at school events, and may have their child in a carpool — must send collection emails, make phone calls, and eventually escalate to the head of school. This is emotionally difficult and operationally ad hoc: some families get gentle reminders while others get firm notices, depending on the director's personal relationship with the family. There is no standardized escalation workflow, no automated communication sequence, and no objective criteria for when to escalate to the head of school or when to offer a hardship payment plan.

## Why It's Still Broken
Commercial collection workflows (automated escalation, dunning sequences, payment reminders) feel inappropriate in a school community context. Smart Tuition sends automated payment reminders, but these are generic and not sensitive to the relationship dynamics. Schools fear that aggressive collection damages the community culture they carefully cultivate. The result is that collection is either too soft (families owe 3+ months before any follow-up) or too personal (the business director handles every conversation individually).

## What a Fix Looks Like
A tuition collection workflow designed for school communities: automated, relationship-sensitive communication sequences that escalate gradually from friendly reminder to concerned check-in to formal conversation request. The system triggers based on objective criteria (15 days late: automated reminder; 30 days: personal email from business office with hardship plan offer; 60 days: head of school notification; 90 days: enrollment impact conversation). The tone is school-appropriate — warm but clear — and the workflow removes the personal burden from the business office director by making the process systematic rather than individual.

## Who Feels the Pain
Business office directors who dread collection conversations with families they see daily, and heads of school who discover delinquent balances of $10K-$30K that were not escalated because the business office director did not want to damage the relationship.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average days delinquent from 45-60 days to 20-30 days through earlier, consistent communication. Recovers $50K-$200K per year in tuition that is currently written off or collected months late. Removes the emotional burden from the business office director by systematizing a process that currently depends on individual relationships and judgment.
