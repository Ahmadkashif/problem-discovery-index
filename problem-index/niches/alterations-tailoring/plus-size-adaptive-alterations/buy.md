# Extended-Range Measurement & Fit System

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/plus-size-adaptive-alterations/profile|Plus-Size & Adaptive Clothing Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Body measurement and fit assessment tools (tape measures, body scanners, fit apps) are calibrated for standard size ranges and don't account for the additional measurements, structural considerations, and fit assessment criteria needed for plus-size and adaptive garment work.
**Tags:** #cnn #regression #computer-vision #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
Standard alteration measurement protocols capture 15-20 points (chest, waist, hips, inseam, etc.) that are sufficient for standard size ranges. Plus-size bodies require additional measurements that aren't in any standard protocol: upper arm circumference (where sleeves bind), cross-back width at multiple heights (not just one), thigh circumference (for pants fit), and belly shape (apron, round, high, low — each requiring different dart and seam placement). Body scanning technology (3D scanners, phone-based apps) is calibrated on standard body shapes and produces inaccurate measurements for plus-size bodies, particularly around areas where tissue is non-rigid.

## What Already Exists
3D body scanning (Fit3D, Size Stream), phone-based measurement apps (MTailor, Sizer), and traditional measurement protocols all exist. These tools work well for bodies within standard size ranges but fail for extended sizes: scanners lose accuracy where soft tissue doesn't hold a consistent shape, apps can't handle the additional measurement points needed, and traditional protocols don't include the measurements that matter most for plus-size fit.

## The Customization Gap
The measurement system needs: an extended measurement protocol with 25-30 points specific to plus-size fit (including mobility measurements — how does the body change when seated, reaching, bending?), a fit assessment framework that accounts for fabric stress points unique to extended sizes (underarm binding, waistband roll, thigh friction), and reference sizing data for plus-size garment construction. For adaptive clothing, mobility and range-of-motion measurements must be captured alongside standard measurements.

## Target Customer
Tailors who serve plus-size clients and currently rely on experience (or trial and error) to take the additional measurements and make the structural adjustments that standard protocols don't cover.

## Impact If Solved
Enables tailors without plus-size experience to achieve first-fitting accuracy comparable to specialists, reduces the number of fittings per garment from 2-3 to 1-2 for plus-size clients, and eliminates the guesswork that currently makes plus-size alteration pricing unpredictable.
