# Adaptive Modification Technique Library

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/plus-size-adaptive-alterations/profile|Plus-Size & Adaptive Clothing Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No searchable reference exists for adaptive clothing modifications — how to convert standard garments for wheelchair users, add magnetic closures for limited dexterity, or adjust for prosthetic limbs — techniques scattered across OT forums and disability blogs.
**Tags:** #nlp #recommendation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A wheelchair user brings a blazer to a tailor and asks for it to be modified for seated wear — shorter in the front (which bunches when sitting), longer in the back (which rides up), with the button position adjusted for a seated torso angle. The tailor has never done this modification. They can either refuse the work, attempt it by intuition (risking a poor result), or spend hours researching adaptive modification techniques that are scattered across occupational therapy journals, disability advocacy websites, and specialized sewing forums. The knowledge exists — but it's not organized, searchable, or accessible to the average tailor at the moment they need it.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Adaptive clothing modification sits at the intersection of three communities that rarely overlap: tailoring (craft knowledge), occupational therapy (disability expertise), and disability advocacy (user needs). No single entity has the combined expertise and incentive to build a comprehensive reference. The disability community is relatively small, the modification types are diverse (each disability creates unique needs), and the potential revenue from a reference library alone is modest.

## What to Build
A searchable technique library organized by modification type (seated wear, one-handed dressing, sensory sensitivity, prosthetic accommodation, catheter access) and garment type (tops, bottoms, dresses, outerwear). Each entry includes: before/after photos, step-by-step instructions with diagrams, material requirements (magnetic snaps, Velcro, adaptive zippers), estimated time and pricing guidance, and notes from the disability community on what matters most to the wearer. Content is developed in partnership with adaptive clothing designers, occupational therapists, and disabled sewists who already do these modifications for themselves.

## Target Customer
Tailors who want to serve adaptive clothing customers but lack the specialized knowledge — estimated 50,000+ US alteration shops that currently refuse or struggle with adaptive modification requests.

## Impact If Built
Enables any competent tailor to perform adaptive modifications (expanding the available provider network by 10-50x for disabled customers), creates a new revenue stream for tailors ($50-200 per adaptive modification), and improves quality of life for disabled people who currently can't find skilled adaptive alteration services.
