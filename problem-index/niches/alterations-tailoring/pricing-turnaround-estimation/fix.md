# Pricing Inconsistency Across Staff

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/pricing-turnaround-estimation/profile|Pricing & Turnaround Estimation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Different staff members quote different prices for the same alteration — a customer who paid $15 for a hem last month is quoted $22 this month by a different intake person, creating trust-destroying inconsistency.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
In a 2-3 person alterations shop, each staff member prices based on their own judgment. The owner (20 years of experience) prices accurately. The part-time intake person (6 months of training) either underprices complex work (costing the shop money) or overprices simple work (losing customers). When a returning customer gets a different price from a different person for the same type of alteration, they feel cheated — even if both prices are technically reasonable. The inconsistency is the problem: customers can't predict what they'll pay, so they can't make informed decisions about whether the alteration is worth it.

## Why It's Still Broken
The owner's pricing knowledge is in their head. Training a new intake person to price accurately takes months, and even then they'll diverge from the owner's prices on complex or unusual garments. Written price lists are incomplete (they can't cover every garment-alteration combination) and quickly become outdated. There's no system that enforces consistent pricing while allowing the flexibility needed for unusual garments or complex situations.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A shared pricing system (tablet or register-integrated) where every alteration is priced from the same configured rate structure. The rate structure is built by the owner, incorporating their pricing knowledge: base rates by alteration type, modifiers by garment complexity (lined +$5, beaded +$10, rush +50%), and the ability to override for unusual situations (logged with a reason for future reference). Every staff member quotes from the same system, producing consistent prices. The owner reviews override logs weekly to decide whether new rate adjustments are needed.

## Who Feels the Pain
Shop owners who receive customer complaints about pricing inconsistency, intake staff who feel insecure about quoting prices without guidance, and returning customers who lose trust when prices vary unpredictably.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates pricing complaints (currently 2-4 per week in a busy shop), reduces underpricing of complex work by 10-15% (recovering $200-500/month in undercharged revenue), and enables new intake staff to quote accurately from day one.
