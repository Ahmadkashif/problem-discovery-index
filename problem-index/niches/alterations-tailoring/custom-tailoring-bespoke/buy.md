# Digital Measurement & Pattern Archive

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/custom-tailoring-bespoke/profile|Custom Tailoring & Bespoke]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Cloud storage and document management tools exist but none are structured for bespoke tailoring data — client measurements with body-shape annotations, pattern modifications, fabric-behavior notes, and fitting history spanning years or decades.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
A master tailor's most valuable business asset — client measurement cards and custom patterns — exists exclusively on paper. Some tailors have 2,000+ client cards accumulated over 30 years, each with 20+ measurements plus handwritten notes about body asymmetries, fabric preferences, and pattern modifications. If the shop floods, burns, or the tailor dies, this irreplaceable knowledge is lost. Clients who have been coming for 20 years would need to start from scratch with a new tailor — losing the accumulated understanding of their body that took dozens of fittings to develop.

## What Already Exists
Google Drive, Dropbox, and Evernote offer cloud storage and note-taking. Fashion CAD systems (CLO3D, Gerber AccuMark) handle digital patterns for mass production. Body scanning services (MTailor, Sizer) capture measurements digitally. But none of these tools are designed for the bespoke context: measurements with qualitative annotations ("right hip 1cm higher, compensate in trouser rise"), pattern modifications stored as deviations from a base block, and fabric-behavior notes linked to specific garments and clients.

## The Customization Gap
The archive needs to store structured measurements (not just numbers, but body-shape observations tied to each measurement), pattern modifications as named deviations (e.g., "forward shoulder adjustment +3mm" as a reusable template), fitting notes per session, and fabric records (mill, weight, behavior observations from previous garments in that fabric). The system should be searchable: "show all clients with similar measurements to this new client" to accelerate initial pattern drafting by starting from a close match rather than from scratch.

## Target Customer
Master tailors with 100+ client records who recognize the vulnerability of paper records but haven't found a digital system that fits their workflow — typically tailors who tried generic tools and abandoned them because the data model didn't match their needs.

## Impact If Solved
Protects decades of accumulated knowledge from physical loss, enables client measurement lookup and pattern retrieval in seconds rather than minutes of filing cabinet searching, and allows pattern knowledge to be shared with apprentices and successors.
