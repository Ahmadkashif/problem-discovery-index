# Institutional Billing & PO Management

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/uniform-workwear-alterations/profile|Uniform & Workwear Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks and invoicing tools handle billing but don't support PO-based institutional invoicing where individual employee alterations must be aggregated into corporate invoices with cost center allocation.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Corporate uniform alteration accounts pay differently than retail customers: they issue purchase orders, require itemized invoices by employee and alteration type, pay on net-30 terms, and may need charges allocated to different departments or cost centers. A tailor shop doing 200 alterations for a corporate client must aggregate individual alteration tickets into a single invoice, match it to the PO, and wait 30 days for payment — all while managing cash flow on individual retail transactions. This bookkeeping burden often deters shops from pursuing corporate business despite its higher volume.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks, FreshBooks, and Xero handle invoicing and accounts receivable. These tools can create corporate invoices and track PO numbers. But they don't connect individual alteration orders (each on a separate ticket) to a corporate invoice automatically. The tailor must manually aggregate — pulling 200 paper tickets, summing costs by employee, and creating a consolidated invoice. For shops without bookkeeping support, this aggregation can take 3-5 hours per billing cycle.

## The Customization Gap
The billing system needs to link individual employee alteration orders to a corporate account, auto-aggregate completed orders into periodic invoices (weekly, bi-weekly, or upon program completion), match to PO numbers with line-item detail (employee name, alteration type, cost), and support cost center allocation if required by the client. The shop should see outstanding corporate AR separately from retail revenue, with aging reports and payment tracking per account.

## Target Customer
Alterations shops serving 2+ institutional clients where manual invoice aggregation takes 3-5 hours per billing cycle and delays payment.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces invoice preparation from 3-5 hours to 15-30 minutes per billing cycle, accelerates payment collection (professional invoices get paid faster), and removes the bookkeeping barrier that prevents shops from pursuing corporate accounts worth $5,000-50,000 per year each.
