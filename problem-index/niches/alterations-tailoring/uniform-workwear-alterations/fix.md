# Employee Scheduling & Tracking Coordination

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/uniform-workwear-alterations/profile|Uniform & Workwear Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Corporate uniform programs stall at 75-85% completion because the final 15-25% of employees never schedule their fitting — and neither the HR coordinator nor the tailor has a systematic way to track and nudge them.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
In any corporate uniform rollout, there's a predictable completion curve: 60% of employees schedule within the first week, another 15-20% in weeks 2-3, and the final 15-25% either procrastinate, forget, or intentionally avoid the fitting. The HR coordinator sends a mass email reminder, then individual follow-ups, then escalates to managers — a cycle that can take 2-3 months to close. The tailor has finished all the eager employees' work and is waiting for stragglers, with no visibility into the program's completion status or ability to prompt employees directly.

## Why It's Still Broken
The coordination problem is split between two parties (HR and the tailor) with no shared tracking system. HR tracks employee completion in a spreadsheet. The tailor tracks completed work on paper tickets. Neither can see the other's data without manual reconciliation. Automated nudging doesn't exist because the tailor doesn't have employee contact information (it flows through HR), and HR doesn't know which employees the tailor has already served (it flows through the shop's paper tickets).

## What a Fix Looks Like
A shared tracking dashboard accessible to both HR and the tailor: every employee is listed with their status (not scheduled, scheduled, fitted, alteration complete, picked up). Automated reminders are sent to employees who haven't scheduled (escalating from gentle nudge to manager CC over weeks). The tailor marks employees as fitted/complete, and the status updates for HR in real time. The program has a defined completion deadline with escalation protocols. Both parties see the same data — no manual reconciliation needed.

## Who Feels the Pain
HR coordinators who spend 5-10 hours chasing the final 15-25% of employees in every uniform rollout, and tailor shops that lose momentum and revenue when corporate programs drag out over months instead of completing in weeks.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases uniform program completion from 75-85% to 95%+ within the target timeframe, reduces HR coordination time by 70%, and compresses program timelines from 2-3 months to 3-4 weeks for the same employee count.
