# Corporate Uniform Alteration Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/uniform-workwear-alterations/profile|Uniform & Workwear Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform gives corporate clients a branded portal to manage employee uniform alterations — scheduling fittings, tracking alteration status, and monitoring program completion rates across the workforce.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #ai-platform #data-integration #automation

## The Problem
When a company outfits 200 employees with new uniforms requiring alterations, the HR coordinator becomes a human scheduling relay: emailing employees with the tailor's phone number, fielding questions about the process, tracking who has been fitted, following up with employees who haven't scheduled, and verifying completion. The tailor shop receives 200 individual phone calls or walk-ins, with no context that these employees are part of the same corporate program. Neither the HR coordinator nor the tailor can see the overall program status: 127 fitted, 58 in progress, 15 haven't scheduled yet.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The intersection of corporate uniform management and local alteration services is a narrow market. Uniform suppliers (Cintas, UniFirst) handle their own fitting for rental programs but don't serve purchase programs. Corporate HR has no budget line for "alteration management software." The tailor shop is happy to take the business but isn't going to build a corporate portal. The opportunity falls between three worlds: corporate HR tech, uniform supply chain, and local service providers.

## What to Build
A corporate-facing portal where the HR coordinator uploads the employee roster, the system generates individual scheduling links for each employee, and employees self-schedule at the contracted tailor. The tailor receives a dashboard of all upcoming appointments with employee names and alteration types. As fittings and alterations are completed, the program status updates automatically. The HR coordinator sees a dashboard: 85% fitted, 10% scheduled, 5% not yet scheduled (with automated reminder nudges to the holdouts).

## Target Customer
Alterations shops serving 3+ corporate accounts annually with 50+ employees each, and corporate HR departments managing uniform programs that currently require 10-20 hours of manual coordination per rollout.

## Impact If Built
Reduces corporate uniform program coordination from 10-20 hours to 1-2 hours per rollout, increases employee completion rates from 75-85% to 95%+ through automated nudges, and positions the alteration shop as a professional corporate partner rather than a retail shop handling bulk walk-ins.
