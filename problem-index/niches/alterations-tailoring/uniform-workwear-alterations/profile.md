# Uniform & Workwear Alterations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $560M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Uniform programs send employees to local tailors with no digital coordination; bulk orders are managed via spreadsheets and phone calls.
**Target Buyer:** Alterations shops serving corporate clients, military installations, hospitals, hospitality chains, or law enforcement agencies
**Automation Potential:** High — Standardized alteration types, bulk order management, and repeat sizing across employees are highly systematizable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Uniform and workwear alterations serve institutional clients rather than individual consumers: a corporation outfitting 500 employees in branded suits, a military installation needing dress uniform alterations, a hospital providing scrub fitting, or a hotel chain requiring staff uniform adjustments. The workflow is fundamentally different from retail alterations: bulk orders with similar alteration types, institutional billing (PO numbers, net-30 terms), size standardization charts, and repeat business as new employees join or existing employees' bodies change. The shop becomes an extension of the client's HR or supply chain function.

## Current Tools & Gaps
There is no software designed for the institutional uniform alteration workflow. Shops manage corporate accounts with spreadsheets, track employee measurements in paper files, and invoice via QuickBooks with manual PO matching. The corporate client has no visibility into which employees have been fitted, which alterations are in progress, and which are complete. Communication happens via email and phone between the shop owner and an HR coordinator.
