# Fit Assessment & Pinning Workflow

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** Cross-segment (applies to all alterations involving garment fitting)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting operational function
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Fit assessment is entirely visual and tactile; pinning is physical; no digital tool assists with either.
**Target Buyer:** Any tailor conducting fitting consultations, particularly those training new staff
**Automation Potential:** High (with ML) — Fit assessment is the industry's deepest tacit knowledge problem: experienced tailors assess fit by eye in seconds, a skill that takes years to develop.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Fit assessment is the most skill-intensive moment in the alteration process: the tailor looks at how a garment sits on a body and simultaneously identifies what's wrong (too tight in the chest, shoulder dropping, collar standing away from the neck), what's causing it (the back is too short, the shoulder seam is too far forward, the dart needs repositioning), and what the fix requires (let out the back seam 1cm, move the shoulder seam forward 5mm, lower the dart 2cm). This assessment happens in 30-60 seconds for an experienced tailor and takes an apprentice 3-5 years to develop. It is the quintessential tacit knowledge problem in the alterations industry.

## Current Tools & Gaps
There are zero digital tools for fit assessment. The tailor uses their eyes, hands, and experience. They pinch the fabric to estimate take-in amount, assess drape by sight, check alignment against body landmarks (shoulder point, waist crease, balance line), and make mental calculations about how changing one measurement will cascade through the garment. No reference system, no digital overlay, no training tool helps with this process. Apprentices learn by watching and practicing under supervision for years.
