# Bridal Alterations Project Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/bridal-formal-alterations/profile|Bridal & Formal Wear Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool tracks a bridal gown alteration from first fitting through final adjustment, with construction notes, photo documentation, and deadline management working backward from the wedding date.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A bridal seamstress managing 15-25 active bridal projects simultaneously must track: which gowns need which alterations, what was decided at each fitting, what construction work remains between fittings, when each bride's next fitting is scheduled, and how that timeline aligns with the wedding date. This is managed with paper fitting tickets (pinned to the gown's hanging bag), a physical appointment book, and the seamstress's memory. When a bride calls asking "what's the status of my dress?", the seamstress must physically find the gown and read the paper ticket. When a fitting is missed or delayed, there's no automatic alert that the timeline is at risk.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The bridal alterations market is fragmented among independent seamstresses and small shops — no concentrated buyer exists to drive software development. Wedding tech companies (Zola, The Knot) focus on planning and vendor discovery, not the operational workflow of alterations. Building a tracker for this market requires understanding the multi-fitting construction process (which is taught through apprenticeship, not standardized), and the user base has limited tech sophistication and minimal software budgets.

## What to Build
A project tracker where each bridal client has a timeline: wedding date → final fitting → second fitting → first fitting, with automatic scheduling working backward. Each fitting session records: photos (before/during/after pinning), construction notes (what needs to be done before the next fitting), material notes (matching lace, button sourcing), and pricing updates. The seamstress sees a dashboard of all active projects with traffic-light timeline status: green (on track), yellow (next fitting needs to be scheduled), red (at risk of missing the wedding date).

## Target Customer
Independent bridal seamstresses and bridal shop alteration departments managing 10-25+ concurrent bridal projects, where timeline management is the primary source of stress and client complaints.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates timeline management anxiety, reduces "where's my dress?" calls by 70% through proactive status updates, and prevents the catastrophic scenario of a gown not being ready for the wedding — an event that destroys reputation and generates liability.
