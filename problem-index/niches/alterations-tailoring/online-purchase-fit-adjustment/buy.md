# E-Commerce-to-Tailor Referral Integration

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/online-purchase-fit-adjustment/profile|Online-Purchase Fit Adjustment Services]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** E-commerce return platforms (Happy Returns, Loop Returns) handle returns and exchanges but don't offer "alter instead of return" as an option, leaving money on the table for both the retailer and local tailors.
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a consumer initiates a return for an online purchase that "didn't fit," no platform offers the alternative: "this garment could be altered to fit you for $25 at a tailor 1.2 miles from your home — would you prefer that to a return?" The retailer processes a return that costs them $10-15 in shipping and handling, the consumer waits 5-10 days for a replacement (which may also not fit), and a local tailor misses a potential $25 alteration. All three parties lose — the retailer, the consumer, and the local tailor — because the "alter instead of return" option doesn't exist in the returns workflow.

## What Already Exists
Happy Returns, Loop Returns, and Returnly manage e-commerce returns with options like exchanges, store credit, and instant refunds. These platforms integrate into the retailer's checkout and post-purchase experience. Separately, alteration shop finders (Yelp, Google Maps, Thumbtack) list local tailors. But no one has connected these: the returns platform doesn't know about local tailors, and local tailors don't know about the returns flow.

## The Customization Gap
The integration needs to: (1) intercept the returns flow at the "reason: didn't fit" step, (2) present the consumer with a "get it altered instead" option showing estimated alteration cost and nearest qualified tailors, (3) offer the retailer an incentive structure (subsidize some or all of the alteration cost, which is cheaper than processing the return), and (4) route the consumer to the selected tailor with the garment details and alteration specification pre-communicated. The tailor receives a new client with a clear scope of work.

## Target Customer
Mid-to-premium e-commerce apparel brands ($40-200 price point) losing 30-40% of orders to returns, and alterations shops seeking new customer acquisition channels beyond foot traffic and Google search.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces retailer return rates by 5-10% (saving $3-8 per diverted return in processing costs), generates $15-40 per alteration in new revenue for local tailors, and provides consumers with a better-fitting garment faster than the return/exchange cycle.
