# Online-Purchase Fit Adjustment Services

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $720M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~11%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Some e-commerce brands offer alteration partnerships, but most online shoppers find local tailors through Yelp/Google with no integration.
**Target Buyer:** Consumers buying clothes online that don't fit perfectly and need local alteration, plus alteration shops seeking this growing customer segment
**Automation Potential:** High — Fit assessment from garment photos, alteration scoping, and pricing can be partially automated.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
E-commerce clothing purchases have a 30-40% return rate, with "didn't fit" as the #1 reason. Many of these garments are close enough to fitting that a $15-30 alteration would save the $8-12 shipping cost and the hassle of a return/exchange cycle. But there's no seamless connection between "this shirt is almost right" and "a tailor near me can fix it for $20." The consumer must independently find a local tailor, bring the garment in, wait for the alteration, and pay out of pocket — a friction-heavy process that makes returns seem easier. This represents a massive latent demand for alteration services from a customer segment that traditional shops don't actively target.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some e-commerce brands (Suit Supply, Indochino, MTailor) offer alteration partnerships, but these are brand-specific closed loops. For the general e-commerce consumer, there's no platform that connects an online purchase that doesn't fit to a local alteration shop that can fix it. Google Maps and Yelp list alteration shops but provide no information about the specific alteration needed, pricing expectations, or turnaround time.
