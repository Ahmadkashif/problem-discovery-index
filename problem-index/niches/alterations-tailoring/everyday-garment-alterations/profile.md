# Everyday Garment Alterations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~37%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most alterations shops use paper tickets, handwritten measurements, and cash registers with no digital order tracking.
**Target Buyer:** Alterations shop owner or solo tailor handling high-volume daily garment work
**Automation Potential:** High — Order intake, pricing, turnaround estimation, and client notification are all systematizable in a high-volume operation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Everyday alterations — hemming pants, taking in waists, shortening sleeves, tapering shirts, replacing zippers — is the bread-and-butter revenue for most alteration businesses. The work is high-volume (20-50+ garments per day in a busy shop), fast turnaround (same-day to 3 days), and price-sensitive ($10-75 per alteration). The challenge is throughput management: tracking dozens of garments through the shop, matching completed work to the right customer, pricing quickly and consistently, and communicating "ready for pickup" without spending all day on the phone.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most alterations shops use a multi-part paper ticket system: one copy pinned to the garment, one copy given to the customer. Pricing is mental math based on the tailor's experience. "Ready for pickup" notifications are phone calls or the customer checking back. No alterations-specific POS or order management system exists with meaningful market share — shops use generic cash registers or Square without order tracking. The paper ticket system works but creates chaos at scale: lost tickets, misattributed garments, and no data on throughput or pricing consistency.
