# Alterations-Specific Order Management System

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/everyday-garment-alterations/profile|Everyday Garment Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No POS or order management system is designed for the alterations workflow — garment intake with alteration specifications, multi-garment orders per customer, work-in-progress tracking, and ready-for-pickup notification.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
An alterations shop processing 30+ garments per day manages each one with a paper ticket: customer name, garment description, alterations needed (often abbreviated in personal shorthand), price, and promised date. The ticket is pinned to the garment, which is hung on a rack. Completed garments are moved to the "done" rack, and the customer is called or waits for them to check back. At any given time, the shop may have 100-200 garments in progress with no digital record of any of them. If a ticket falls off, the garment becomes an orphan. If the customer loses their receipt, finding their garment requires searching every hanger. There's no way to see throughput, average turnaround time, or which alteration types are most profitable.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The alterations market is fragmented among thousands of small shops with minimal technology budgets ($0-50/month). Shop owners are often immigrant entrepreneurs with limited English and strong digital literacy barriers. Building for this market requires: radically simple UX (ideally touchscreen tablet, no keyboard), multilingual support, and a price point that competes with "free" (paper tickets cost nothing). No software company has found the unit economics to serve this market.

## What to Build
A tablet-based order management system with dead-simple UX: photograph the garment, tap alteration types from a visual menu (icons for hemming, waist, zipper, etc.), auto-calculate price based on configured rates, print a barcoded ticket (that matches the digital record), and mark as complete when finished. Customer lookup by phone number (no account creation). Auto-send SMS when the garment is ready. Daily dashboard shows garments received, in progress, and ready for pickup. Multi-language support (English, Spanish, Mandarin, Korean, Vietnamese — reflecting the industry's demographics).

## Target Customer
Alterations shops processing 20+ garments per day that use paper tickets and have no digital order tracking, estimated at 60,000+ shops in the US.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates garment-to-customer mismatches (1-2 per week in a busy shop), automates "ready for pickup" notifications (saving 30-60 minutes of daily phone calls), and provides the shop's first data on throughput, revenue by alteration type, and turnaround performance.
