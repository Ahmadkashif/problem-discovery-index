# Lost Ticket and Garment Misattribution

**Niche:** [[niches/alterations-tailoring/everyday-garment-alterations/profile|Everyday Garment Alterations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/alterations-tailoring|Alterations & Tailoring]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Alterations shops lose 1-2 paper tickets per week — disconnecting garments from customers and creating embarrassing, time-consuming searches that erode trust.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
A paper ticket pinned to a garment in a busy alterations shop is a fragile system: tickets fall off hangers, get detached during work, or are accidentally swapped between garments. When a customer arrives to pick up their pants and the ticket is missing, the shop must search through 100+ garments to find the right pair — a 10-20 minute interruption that holds up the line and embarrasses the shop owner. Worse, if two garments of the same type have their tickets swapped, a customer may take home the wrong garment. These incidents happen 1-2 times per week in a busy shop, but each one is memorable and damages customer trust.

## Why It's Still Broken
Paper tickets are the universal standard because they're cheap, require no technology, and work acceptably 95% of the time. The 5% failure rate is treated as an acceptable cost of doing business. Shop owners don't see the cumulative impact: 1-2 incidents per week = 50-100 per year = dozens of frustrated customers and hours of wasted search time. The fix (digital order tracking with barcode labels) seems like overkill for a $12 hem — the technology cost feels disproportionate to the alteration value.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A printed barcode label (from a thermal label printer, $100-200) attached to each garment at intake, linked to a digital order record. When the customer arrives for pickup, their phone number or ticket number retrieves the order, which shows the garment barcode. The shop locates the garment by scanning — no searching. If a label detaches, the garment's photo (taken at intake) identifies it visually. The barcode system costs less than 5 cents per label, which is negligible against the $20-50 average alteration value.

## Who Feels the Pain
Shop owners who experience the embarrassment and time loss of missing tickets, and customers who lose confidence in the shop after a misattribution incident — often switching to a competitor.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates ticket loss and garment misattribution (saving 1-3 hours per week in search time), prevents the customer trust damage that costs $500-2,000/year in lost clients, and provides a professional pickup experience that differentiates the shop.
