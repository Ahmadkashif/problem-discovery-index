# Lightweight Remodeling PM for Small Crews

**Niche:** [[niches/general-contractors/residential-remodeling/profile|Residential Remodeling Contractors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/general-contractors|General Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Buildertrend and CoConstruct were built for custom home builders running million-dollar projects — a 5-person remodeling crew doing $40K kitchens needs something simpler that handles scope changes, homeowner communication, and payments without 200 features they'll never use.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #feature-engineering #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
A remodeling contractor running 3-6 simultaneous kitchen and bath renovations needs three things: (1) a way to communicate with homeowners about schedule, progress, and changes without drowning in text messages; (2) a way to track and bill for change orders as they happen on-site; (3) a way to collect progress payments tied to project milestones. They don't need submittal management, RFI workflows, or CPM scheduling — features that dominate construction PM tools designed for larger projects. But every construction PM tool on the market was built for either custom home builders (Buildertrend, CoConstruct) or commercial contractors (Procore), and the simplest tier still requires 10+ hours of setup and training that a 5-person crew won't invest.

## What Already Exists
Buildertrend ($99-399/month) is the closest fit but was designed for custom home builders managing $300K-$2M projects with selections, allowances, and draw schedules. Its feature set overwhelms a remodeling crew doing $30-80K projects. Jobber and Housecall Pro target home services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) with dispatch and service-call workflows that don't fit remodeling's multi-week project model. General tools like Monday.com or Trello can track tasks but lack construction-specific features: change order management, progress billing tied to project phases, and photo documentation linked to project timelines.

## The Customization Gap
A remodeling-specific PM tool would strip construction PM to its essential core: (1) project timeline with 5-8 phases (demo, rough-in, inspection, drywall, paint, tile/flooring, fixtures, punch list) not 200-line CPM schedules; (2) photo-first daily logs that auto-share with the homeowner; (3) integrated change order capture (photo + description + price = homeowner approval via text); (4) milestone-based payment collection with integrated payment processing; (5) a homeowner portal that shows "what happened today, what's next, what's changed" without requiring a login. The key insight is that remodeling PM should be as simple as a text conversation with the homeowner, but with structured data behind it.

## Target Customer
Remodeling contractors with 3-15 employees who currently manage projects through text messages, paper folders, and QuickBooks invoices — and who have tried and abandoned Buildertrend or similar tools because the setup and complexity didn't justify the cost.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces homeowner communication overhead by 4-6 hours per week (currently consumed by text messages, phone calls, and in-person updates). Improves change order capture rate from an estimated 50-60% to 90%+ by making documentation instant. Accelerates payment collection by tying invoices to documented milestone completion rather than manual billing cycles.
