# Certification Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/general-contractors/minority-women-owned/profile|Minority/Women-Owned Contractors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/general-contractors|General Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Document management and compliance tracking platforms exist for every regulated industry — but nobody has built one for the unique multi-agency certification lifecycle that MBE/WBE/DBE contractors must navigate to maintain their competitive advantage.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #feature-engineering #compliance #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A typical MBE/WBE/DBE contractor holds 3-6 certifications from different agencies: SBA 8(a) (federal), state DOT DBE, city MBE, county WBE, and possibly industry-specific certifications (NMSDC for corporate supply chain programs). Each certification has different renewal timelines (annual to triennial), different documentation requirements (financial statements, personal net worth affidavits, ownership structure verification, control documentation), and different submission processes (some online, some paper, some require in-person interviews). Missing a renewal deadline means losing certification status — and immediately losing eligibility for the set-aside contracts and subcontracting commitments that may represent 40-70% of the firm's revenue.

## What Already Exists
Generic compliance platforms (Diligent, NAVEX) handle corporate compliance tracking but know nothing about construction certifications. Document management tools (Google Drive, SharePoint) store files but don't understand certification renewal workflows. The SBA's certify.sba.gov handles 8(a) applications but not other certifications. Each certifying agency has its own portal for renewal submissions. No tool aggregates the multi-agency certification lifecycle into a single management view.

## The Customization Gap
A certification management platform for DBE/MBE/WBE contractors would: (1) maintain a certification registry showing all held certifications, their agencies, expiration dates, and renewal requirements; (2) generate renewal timelines with task lists — "90 days before SBA 8(a) renewal: update financial statements; 60 days: prepare personal net worth affidavit; 30 days: submit renewal package"; (3) store and version all certification documents (financial statements, ownership documents, tax returns) with mappings showing which documents each certification requires; (4) pre-populate renewal applications by pulling from stored documents and prior submissions; (5) alert the contractor when business changes (new partner, ownership percentage change, revenue growth past SBA size standards) might affect certification eligibility. This requires deep understanding of the certification regulatory landscape — knowledge that currently lives in certification consultants' heads.

## Target Customer
DBE/MBE/WBE certified contractors holding 3+ certifications who currently track renewal deadlines in calendars and compile renewal documentation manually from scattered files. Also serves certification consultants who manage renewals for multiple client firms.

## Impact If Solved
Prevents certification lapses that result in immediate revenue loss (losing a DBE certification can remove a firm from active subcontracting commitments worth $500K-$2M). Reduces renewal preparation time from 20-40 hours per certification to 5-10 hours by pre-populating from stored documents. Eliminates the $3,000-$8,000 per certification that firms pay consultants for renewal assistance.
