# Multi-Project Portfolio Management for Small Commercial

**Niche:** [[niches/general-contractors/commercial-tenant-improvement/profile|Commercial Tenant Improvement Contractors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/general-contractors|General Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Procore manages individual commercial projects well, but TI contractors running 15 small jobs need portfolio-level visibility — cross-project resource views, aggregate financial dashboards, and shared building coordination — that no single-project tool provides.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Commercial TI contractors manage 10-20 projects simultaneously, each worth $50K-$500K with 6-10 week timelines. They need portfolio-level answers: What's my total committed backlog? Which projects are profitable and which are bleeding margin? How many labor hours am I committed to next month across all projects? Where am I double-booked on subcontractors? Single-project PM tools force them to open each project individually and mentally aggregate the answers — or maintain a parallel spreadsheet that's always out of date.

## What Already Exists
Procore handles single commercial project management with RFIs, submittals, daily logs, and budget tracking — but its portfolio view is designed for large GCs with a few mega-projects, not for 15 small concurrent TI jobs. Per-project pricing ($500-1000/project/month) makes it prohibitively expensive for small TI portfolios. Buildertrend has multi-project views but targets residential, lacking commercial features like ADA compliance tracking, submittal management, and landlord coordination. Monday.com and Asana handle multi-project portfolios but know nothing about construction — no submittal workflows, no trade sequencing logic, no AIA billing format.

## The Customization Gap
The missing layer is TI-specific portfolio intelligence on top of a multi-project PM tool. It needs to understand: (1) projects share resources — the same electrician sub works across 5 jobs and can't be double-booked; (2) projects in the same building compete for shared infrastructure (elevators, loading docks, after-hours access); (3) TI projects have dual approval chains (tenant and landlord/property manager); (4) fast-track TI schedules have minimal float, so any delay on one project must immediately surface at the portfolio level; (5) financial rollups must show margin by project type (office vs. retail vs. restaurant TI) and by building/property manager relationship, not just by individual project.

## Target Customer
TI contractor owners and project managers running 8+ simultaneous commercial interior projects who use Procore or Buildertrend for individual projects but manage cross-project coordination through spreadsheets and Monday-morning meetings.

## Impact If Solved
Provides real-time portfolio margin visibility, surfacing underperforming projects before they consume the profit from healthy ones. Eliminates subcontractor double-booking across projects (estimated 5-10% of schedule delays are caused by sub conflicts across the TI contractor's own portfolio). Reduces portfolio-level financial reporting from 4-6 hours per week to automated dashboards.
