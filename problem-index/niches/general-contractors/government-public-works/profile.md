# Government & Public Works Contractors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/general-contractors|General Contractors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-35B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — compliance reporting is largely manual; most firms use Sage or Viewpoint for accounting but certified payroll and DBE tracking are spreadsheet-driven
**Target Buyer:** GC owner or compliance officer specializing in government/public sector construction (schools, municipal buildings, infrastructure)
**Automation Potential:** Very High — certified payroll, prevailing wage compliance, DBE tracking, and bid document preparation are all rule-heavy, data-intensive processes with clear regulatory specifications

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Government contracting adds a layer of regulatory compliance that private-sector GCs never encounter. Every worker on a federal project must be paid prevailing wages under Davis-Bacon (or state equivalents like California's DIR requirements), with certified payroll reports submitted weekly. Contracts often require minimum percentages of work to be performed by Disadvantaged Business Enterprises (DBE), Minority Business Enterprises (MBE), or Women Business Enterprises (WBE), with ongoing tracking and reporting. Bidding is a formal process with strict qualification requirements, bonding thresholds, and sealed-bid procedures. Payment comes through government bureaucracies with 45-90 day cycles. The compliance burden is so heavy that many capable GCs avoid government work entirely — and those who specialize in it spend 15-25% of their administrative capacity on compliance documentation rather than project management.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Sage 300 CRE and Viewpoint Vista handle construction accounting and can produce certified payroll reports, but the wage rate determination (matching each worker's classification to the correct prevailing wage rate for the specific project location and trade) is largely manual. LCPtracker and Elations handle certified payroll submission but don't integrate with the contractor's accounting system, creating double-entry. No tool automates DBE/MBE/WBE subcontract tracking against contract goals — compliance officers maintain spreadsheets comparing committed DBE spend to contract requirements and manually compile reports for the contracting agency.
