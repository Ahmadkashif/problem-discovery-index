# Residential Custom Home Builders

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/general-contractors|General Contractors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $70-80B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most use Buildertrend or CoConstruct for project management, but client communication and allowance tracking remain spreadsheet-driven
**Target Buyer:** Custom home builder owner managing 3-15 concurrent builds
**Automation Potential:** High — allowance tracking, selection management, and client-facing progress reporting are all structured data problems with clear decision logic

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Custom home building is fundamentally different from spec building or production housing because every project is a unique scope negotiated with a specific client. The GC manages hundreds of finish selections (tile, countertops, fixtures, hardware, flooring) each with allowance budgets, lead times, and client approval gates. When a client exceeds their cabinet allowance by $12K, it cascades through the budget — but the GC discovers this weeks later when the cabinetmaker invoices. Meanwhile, clients expect real-time visibility into construction progress and budget status that no residential PM tool provides at the detail level custom homes demand. The combination of high client involvement, long lead times on luxury materials, and constant design changes creates an operational complexity that production builders never face.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Buildertrend and CoConstruct handle residential project management — scheduling, daily logs, and basic client portals — but their allowance and selection tracking is rudimentary. Neither tool connects selection deadlines to the construction schedule (if tile isn't selected by week 8, framing can't close the shower niche). Budget tracking shows line-item totals but doesn't surface allowance overages until invoices arrive. Client portals show photo updates and schedule milestones but lack real-time budget-to-allowance comparison, selection status dashboards, or design change impact analysis.
