# USPAP Compliance Pre-Submission Checker

**Niche:** [[niches/real-estate-appraisers/report-writing-and-compliance/profile|Report Writing & USPAP Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Grammar checkers like Grammarly catch writing errors, and CU catches valuation flags after submission, but no tool checks the appraisal report for USPAP compliance issues before submission — missing certifications, incomplete scope of work statements, unsigned addenda, and missing required disclosures that trigger lender rejection.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #automation #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
USPAP compliance requires specific elements in every appraisal report: a scope of work description, defined appraisal purpose and intended use, identification of the client and intended users, a signed certification, limiting conditions, and extraordinary assumptions if applicable. Each AMC and lender adds their own compliance overlay — specific certification language, additional disclosures, and supplemental forms. An appraiser processing 20 reports per week under multiple AMC clients must ensure each report meets the specific AMC's compliance requirements in addition to USPAP. Missing a required form or certification language triggers a "conditions" response from the lender — adding 30-60 minutes of correction work per rejected report.

## What Already Exists
TOTAL validates that required form fields are populated but doesn't check the content quality or compliance of narrative sections. AMC portals check for specific required attachments at upload. No tool comprehensively checks USPAP compliance, AMC-specific requirements, and internal report consistency before the appraiser submits.

## The Customization Gap
A USPAP compliance checker needs to: (1) verify all USPAP-required elements are present and complete (scope of work, purpose, intended use, certification, limiting conditions); (2) check AMC-specific requirements per the active AMC's compliance checklist (stored as profiles); (3) flag internal inconsistencies (market trend checkbox vs. narrative description, subject characteristics vs. adjustment grid, date discrepancies); (4) verify all addenda are signed and dated; (5) check for common CU triggers (excessive adjustments, distant comps, market commentary inconsistencies).

## Target Customer
Every residential lending appraiser — approximately 50,000-60,000 nationally, of whom all submit to AMCs and lenders with compliance requirements.

## Impact If Solved
Lender-rejection rate for compliance issues drops from 8-12% to under 3%. Correction cycle time (currently 30-60 minutes per rejection) is recovered. Appraiser stress from compliance anxiety decreases — currently cited as a top-5 industry pain point.
