# Litigation & Expert Witness Appraisers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12-15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — use TOTAL for report structure but produce custom narrative reports, rely on manual research for historical valuations, and maintain case files in paper or basic folder systems
**Target Buyer:** Forensic appraiser or expert witness practitioner specializing in divorce, estate, tax appeal, eminent domain, or litigation assignments
**Automation Potential:** Medium — historical data research, case file management, and report generation are automatable; expert opinion and testimony preparation resist automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Litigation appraisers work at 3-5x the fee of lending appraisers ($350-$500/hour vs. $65-$100/hour effective for lending work) because their reports must withstand cross-examination and opposing expert challenge. The workflow is fundamentally different: retrospective valuations (what was the property worth on the date of death 3 years ago?), hypothetical conditions (what would the property be worth if the contamination hadn't occurred?), multiple value premises (market value vs. liquidation value vs. investment value), and exhaustive documentation (every data source, every adjustment, every assumption must be explicitly supported because opposing counsel will challenge each). Report length is 40-80 pages vs. 15-20 for lending. Case preparation includes depositions and trial testimony.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TOTAL provides the lending appraisal form — litigation appraisers use it as a starting framework but produce custom narrative reports in Word. Historical MLS data access is inconsistent (some MLSs purge data after 3-5 years). Case file management uses basic folder structures. No tool supports the litigation appraiser's specific workflow: retrospective market data assembly, case file organization with opposing expert tracking, and narrative report generation with citation management.
