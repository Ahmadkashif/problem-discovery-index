# Expert Witness Case File Management for Appraisers

**Niche:** [[niches/real-estate-appraisers/litigation-and-expert-witness-appraisers/profile|Litigation & Expert Witness Appraisers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Clio and PracticePanther manage case files for attorneys, but appraiser expert witnesses need to track: their own report versions, the opposing expert's report and critique points, deposition transcripts with searchable testimony, and a reference library of prior cases where they testified on the same issue — organized by valuation issue, not by client.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A litigation appraiser managing 15-20 active cases tracks: their draft and final reports per case, the opposing expert's report (with notes on disagreements), deposition transcripts (their own and opposing), court orders and scheduling, attorney correspondence, and billing. Additionally, they need to cross-reference prior cases where they addressed similar valuation issues (to ensure testimony consistency — opposing counsel searches for inconsistencies across cases). This information lives in email, file folders, Word documents, and the appraiser's memory. Case preparation for trial takes 4-8 hours, much of it reassembling the case file from scattered sources.

## What Already Exists
Clio and PracticePanther provide legal case management for attorneys. These tools track clients, matters, documents, billing, and deadlines. TOTAL manages appraisal reports for lending. No case management tool is designed for the appraiser expert witness workflow — which combines appraisal report management, opposing expert tracking, testimony history, and cross-case issue indexing.

## The Customization Gap
An appraiser case management tool needs: (1) report version control with side-by-side comparison between the appraiser's report and the opposing expert's report; (2) issue-based indexing across cases (all cases where the appraiser opined on highest and best use, or environmental impairment, or date-of-death valuation); (3) deposition transcript search (find what the appraiser said about adjustment methodology in a 2019 deposition); (4) billing integration for hourly litigation work (not per-assignment like lending). The cross-case issue indexing is the unique requirement — it protects the appraiser from opposing counsel who searches for testimony inconsistencies.

## Target Customer
Litigation appraisers handling 10+ expert witness cases per year — approximately 3,000-5,000 practitioners nationally. Average contract value: $150-$300/month.

## Impact If Solved
Case preparation time drops from 4-8 hours to 1-2 hours per case. Testimony consistency risk (the number-one vulnerability for expert witnesses) is managed through cross-case issue indexing. Case file assembly for deposition prep drops from 2-3 hours to 30 minutes.
