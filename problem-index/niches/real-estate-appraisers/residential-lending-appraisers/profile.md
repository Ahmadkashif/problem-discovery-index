# Residential Lending Appraisers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~65-70%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — TOTAL by a la mode is near-universal for report writing, MLS access is standard, but comp analysis, adjustment development, and market commentary are manual
**Target Buyer:** Independent residential appraiser or small appraisal firm owner (2-8 appraisers) processing AMC and lender-direct orders
**Automation Potential:** High — comp selection, adjustment calculation, market commentary writing, and CU pre-screening are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential lending appraisers produce the appraisal reports required for mortgage origination — the volume backbone of the industry. They work under AMC (Appraisal Management Company) fee compression ($350-$500 per assignment for 4-6 hours of work), FNMA/FHFA regulatory standards (USPAP + Collateral Underwriter scoring), and turnaround time pressure (24-48 hour expected turn times). The economic model depends on volume: an appraiser earning $350/assignment needs 4-5 assignments daily to earn $300,000-$400,000 annually, creating intense pressure to reduce per-report time without sacrificing quality. Every minute saved in comp selection, adjustment development, and report writing directly translates to income.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TOTAL by a la mode handles report writing and UCDP submission. MLS provides comp data. CompStak provides some commercial data but not residential. No tool automates the three workflow bottlenecks: comp selection (finding the best 3-5 comps from 20-50 candidates), adjustment development (calibrating market-supported adjustments from local data), and narrative writing (market conditions commentary, comp selection rationale).
