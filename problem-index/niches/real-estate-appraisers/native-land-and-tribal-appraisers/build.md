# Trust Land Valuation Methodology Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/real-estate-appraisers/native-land-and-tribal-appraisers/profile|Native Land & Tribal Property Appraisers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No valuation tool implements the specialized methodology required for tribal trust land — where properties cannot be sold on the open market, standard comparable sales analysis doesn't apply, and the appraiser must use replacement cost, ground lease capitalization, or restricted-market-adjusted comparables — leaving the 200-400 qualified appraisers to manually navigate a methodology that no training program adequately covers.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
When a tribal member applies for a HUD Section 184 loan to build or buy a home on trust land, the lender requires an appraisal. The appraiser faces a methodological challenge with no parallel in standard practice: the land component can't be valued from comparable sales (the land can't be sold), so the appraiser must either capitalize ground lease rents (if the tribe leases residential lots), extract land value from nearby fee-simple sales and adjust for the trust-status market restriction, or default to replacement cost (value = cost to build the improvements minus depreciation, with only a nominal land value). Each approach requires calculations and adjustments not found in standard appraisal training or tools.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The market is extremely small: 200-400 qualified appraisers handling approximately 3,000-5,000 tribal trust appraisals annually. Appraisal software companies serve the 60,000+ residential lending appraiser market and can't justify development investment for 200-400 users. BIA and HUD have published guidance but not funded tool development. Tribal housing authorities have requested better tools for years without result.

## What to Build
A trust land valuation module that integrates with TOTAL: provides the cost approach template with tribal-specific depreciation schedules (reflecting construction cost variations on remote reservation land), ground lease income capitalization calculator (using tribal residential lease terms), fee-to-trust adjustment methodology (deriving trust land value from nearby fee-simple sales with a market restriction discount), and BIA-required report addenda. Includes a reference library of trust land appraisal methodology guidance from BIA, USPAP, and Appraisal Institute.

## Target Customer
BIA-qualified tribal trust land appraisers — approximately 200-400 nationally, but each processing 10-30 tribal assignments per year at $500-$800 per assignment. Average contract value: $50-$100/month.

## Impact If Built
Trust land appraisal methodology implementation drops from 2-3 hours of manual calculation to 30-45 minutes per assignment. Appraisal quality and consistency improves across the small practitioner pool. Most importantly, HUD Section 184 loan processing delays (often 3-6 months waiting for appraisals) decrease as more appraisers can efficiently complete tribal assignments — accelerating homeownership for tribal members.
