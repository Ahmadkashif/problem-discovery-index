# Comp Selection & Adjustment Workflow

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-2B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15-20% (embedded workflow cost — this phase consumes 30-40% of every appraisal assignment)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — MLS search is standard, but comp ranking, adjustment development, and adjustment application are entirely manual
**Target Buyer:** Any residential appraiser (workflow segment applicable across all assignment types)
**Automation Potential:** High — comp ranking by similarity, adjustment calculation from market data, and adjustment grid population are rule-based and highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Comp selection and adjustment is the core analytical task of every residential appraisal — and the most time-consuming phase. The appraiser: searches the MLS for candidate comps (10-30 candidates), evaluates each candidate by similarity to the subject (location, size, features, condition, recency), selects 3-5 best comps, develops dollar adjustments for differences between each comp and the subject, populates the adjustment grid, and reconciles the adjusted values to a final value opinion. This process takes 45-90 minutes per assignment — 30-40% of total assignment time. The process is identical across lending, litigation, estate, and tax appeal assignments, making it the highest-leverage automation target in the profession.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MLS provides the candidate comp pool. TOTAL provides the adjustment grid form. Between those two steps — comp ranking, adjustment development, and grid population — the process is entirely manual. a la mode's CompFinder provides basic comp search but no ranking or adjustment. Some MLS systems offer "comp analysis" features that simply display raw data without ranking or adjustment calculation.
