# One-Click Adjustment Grid Population from MLS Data

**Niche:** [[niches/real-estate-appraisers/comp-selection-and-adjustment/profile|Comp Selection & Adjustment Workflow]]
**Industry:** [[industries/real-estate-appraisers|Real Estate Appraisers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** TOTAL provides a blank adjustment grid that the appraiser fills in manually — property characteristics from MLS, dollar adjustments from mental calculation — when the MLS already has the comp data and market-calibrated adjustments could auto-populate 80% of the grid in seconds.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
After selecting 3-5 comps, the appraiser must populate the TOTAL adjustment grid: enter each comp's address, sale price, sale date, GLA, bedroom count, bathroom count, garage type, lot size, condition, age, and other characteristics — then enter a dollar adjustment for each difference from the subject. This is primarily a data transcription task: 80% of the data is in the MLS, and the adjustments follow the appraiser's standard per-unit amounts. Manual grid population takes 15-25 minutes per report and is entirely mechanical — no professional judgment is required for transcribing MLS data into TOTAL fields.

## What Already Exists
TOTAL allows importing comp data from some MLS systems via a la mode's data integration. However, the import typically brings only property characteristics — not adjustments. The appraiser must still manually enter every adjustment amount. Some TOTAL users have created personal adjustment templates (saved per-unit amounts), but these apply static adjustments without market calibration.

## The Customization Gap
A one-click grid population tool needs to: (1) import comp property data from the MLS directly into the TOTAL grid (address, sale price, characteristics); (2) calculate the per-attribute difference between subject and comp (subject has 3 bedrooms, comp has 4 — difference is -1 bedroom); (3) apply market-calibrated per-unit adjustment amounts from the appraiser's stored calibration or a market model; (4) populate the adjustment grid with both data and adjustments, ready for appraiser review and modification.

## Target Customer
Every residential appraiser using TOTAL — approximately 50,000-60,000 nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Adjustment grid population drops from 15-25 minutes to 2-3 minutes per report. At 20 reports per week, this recovers 4-7 hours weekly. Transcription errors (wrong sale price, incorrect GLA) are eliminated because data comes directly from MLS.
