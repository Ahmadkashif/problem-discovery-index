# Medical Intake & Documentation Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/tattoo-studios/cosmetic-medical-tattoo/profile|Cosmetic & Medical Tattoo Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tattoo-studios|Tattoo Studios]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** HIPAA-compliant intake platforms exist for medical offices but don't handle cosmetic tattoo workflows — pigment allergy screening, photo documentation protocols, or touch-up scheduling.
**Tags:** #compliance #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation

## The Problem
Paramedical and cosmetic tattoo providers operate in a regulatory gray zone between beauty services and medical procedures. Many states require medical intake forms, allergy screening (especially for iron oxide pigments), and photo documentation. Providers piece together generic forms, paper waivers, and phone photos with no unified system — creating compliance risk and making it impossible to track client history across multiple sessions and touch-ups.

## What Already Exists
Jane App, IntakeQ, and DrChrono offer HIPAA-compliant intake and documentation for medical practices. Vagaro and Boulevard handle beauty/salon booking with consent forms. Neither category fits: medical platforms don't understand cosmetic tattoo procedures, and salon platforms don't meet medical documentation standards or handle the multi-session pigment-specific workflow.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs cosmetic-tattoo-specific intake templates (pigment allergy history, skin condition assessment, medication interactions with healing), standardized photo documentation workflows (before/during/after/healed at specific angles and lighting), and a touch-up tracking system that connects initial procedure records to subsequent visits. For paramedical providers seeking insurance reimbursement, the documentation must generate procedure notes that map to CPT codes.

## Target Customer
PMU technicians and paramedical artists handling 30+ client records, especially those doing insurance-reimbursable work (areola restoration, scar camouflage) who need audit-ready documentation.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces documentation time from 20-30 minutes per client to under 10 minutes, eliminates compliance gaps that could result in licensing issues, and enables insurance billing for paramedical procedures worth $500-2,000 per case.
