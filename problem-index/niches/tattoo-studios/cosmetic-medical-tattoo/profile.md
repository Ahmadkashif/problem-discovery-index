# Cosmetic & Medical Tattoo Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tattoo-studios|Tattoo Studios]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $620M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most providers track clients on paper, store before/after photos in phone galleries, and handle medical intake forms manually.
**Target Buyer:** Permanent makeup (PMU) technicians, paramedical tattoo artists (areola restoration, scar camouflage), microblading specialists
**Automation Potential:** High — Medical intake, consent documentation, color matching, and follow-up scheduling are all highly systematizable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cosmetic and medical tattooing operates under different regulatory frameworks than decorative tattooing — many states require separate licensing, medical intake forms, and documentation standards. The client base is fundamentally different: these are often post-surgical patients (breast reconstruction, burn survivors) or clients seeking semi-permanent cosmetic enhancement (microblading, lip blush, eyeliner). The work requires color theory expertise specific to skin undertones and healing behavior, along with medical sensitivity that decorative tattoo tools don't address.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PMU technicians use generic salon booking tools (Vagaro, Acuity) that don't handle medical intake forms, photo documentation requirements, or the color-matching workflow specific to skin-tone cosmetic work. No tool integrates the consultation-to-procedure-to-touchup pipeline with the medical documentation and photo tracking that insurance reimbursement (for paramedical work) requires.
