# Consent & Aftercare Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tattoo-studios|Tattoo Studios]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** Cross-segment (applies to all licensed tattoo studios)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting regulatory function
**Digital Adoption:** Low — The vast majority of studios use paper consent forms, physical filing, and verbal aftercare instructions.
**Target Buyer:** Studio owner or manager responsible for health department compliance and liability
**Automation Potential:** High — Consent forms, ID verification, age verification, allergen screening, and aftercare delivery are entirely rule-based.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every tattoo studio in every jurisdiction must collect signed consent forms before tattooing — but the requirements vary by state, county, and city. Some jurisdictions require photo ID on file, some require parental consent forms for minors, some mandate specific allergen disclosures for ink ingredients, and some require 24-hour waiting periods between consent signing and the procedure. Studios manage this with paper forms, physical filing cabinets, and manual ID verification. Aftercare instructions are delivered verbally or on printed cards that clients immediately lose. When a health department audit arrives, studios scramble to find forms in filing cabinets that may go back years.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some studios use Jotform or DocuSign for digital consent, but these are generic form tools with no awareness of jurisdiction-specific tattoo regulations. No tool auto-generates the correct consent form for a given jurisdiction, verifies that all regulatory requirements are met before the procedure begins, or delivers digital aftercare with follow-up. The filing system is disconnected from the booking system — there's no check that says "this client has a booking but hasn't completed consent."
