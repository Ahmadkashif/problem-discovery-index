# Digital Aftercare Delivery Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/tattoo-studios/consent-aftercare-compliance/profile|Consent & Aftercare Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tattoo-studios|Tattoo Studios]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Patient engagement platforms like Klara and Luma Health deliver post-procedure instructions digitally, but none are adapted for tattoo aftercare — which varies by tattoo location, size, ink type, and client skin type.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #recommendation #worker-facing

## The Problem
Tattoo aftercare instructions are delivered verbally at the end of a session when the client is tired, excited, and not listening carefully. Some studios hand out printed cards with generic instructions. But aftercare varies significantly: a full-color sleeve requires different care than a small black text tattoo; a foot tattoo has different healing constraints than a forearm piece; clients with eczema or sensitive skin need modified care; and some ink brands have specific aftercare requirements. Generic instructions lead to suboptimal healing, client callbacks asking "is this normal?", and occasional touch-up requests that result from poor aftercare rather than poor artistry.

## What Already Exists
Klara, Luma Health, and Relatient deliver post-procedure digital instructions in healthcare. Mailchimp and Twilio enable automated SMS/email sequences. These tools can send aftercare messages but don't know anything about tattoos — they can't customize instructions based on tattoo placement, size, color, ink brand, or client skin type. The content library doesn't exist for tattoo aftercare.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs a tattoo aftercare content library structured by placement (arm, torso, foot, hand, face), tattoo type (color, black/gray, fine line, traditional), and client factors (skin type, allergies, occupation involving sun/water exposure). After each session, the artist selects the relevant parameters and the system sends a timed aftercare sequence: immediate care (day 0), first week, peeling stage, and long-term maintenance. Include photo examples of normal healing vs. signs of infection.

## Target Customer
Studios handling 15+ tattoos per week that currently field 5-10 aftercare calls/texts per week from clients asking "is this normal?" — each call taking 5-10 minutes of artist time.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces aftercare-related client callbacks by 60-70%, preventing 3-5 hours per week of artist disruption, and decreases touch-up requests caused by poor aftercare by 30-40%.
