# Tip Pooling & Workforce Scheduling Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-20B (embedded in labor costs)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded cost center affecting 100% of tipped restaurants
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — scheduling tools widespread but tip distribution and compliance are manual or spreadsheet-based
**Target Buyer:** GM or floor manager responsible for tip distribution, payroll compliance, and scheduling at a 15-50 employee restaurant
**Automation Potential:** High — tip pooling rules, tip credit calculations, and overtime tracking are entirely rule-based processes with state-specific regulations

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Tip distribution is one of the highest-liability, most manual processes in independent restaurants. Federal and state tip credit laws, tip pooling legality (varies by state — some allow back-of-house inclusion, some do not), overtime calculations on tipped wages, and reporting for tax purposes create a compliance minefield. Most independents handle tip distribution with a calculator and a spreadsheet — or worse, the manager counting cash and distributing it by feel. A single miscalculation can trigger Department of Labor complaints, class-action lawsuits, or IRS audits. The recent tip-credit rule changes (2024 DOL ruling, state-by-state divergence) have made this more complex than ever.

## Current Tools & Gaps
7shifts and Homebase handle scheduling and basic tip distribution but do not adapt to state-specific rules, do not handle complex pooling arrangements (e.g., weighted pools by role with different tip credit rates), and do not produce the audit-ready records needed to survive a DOL inquiry. Payroll processors (Gusto, ADP) handle tip reporting for tax purposes but do not touch the distribution logic. No tool bridges the gap between "how tips should be distributed tonight" and "how tips are reported to the IRS."
