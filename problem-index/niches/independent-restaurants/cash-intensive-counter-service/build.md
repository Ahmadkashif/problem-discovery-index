# Unified Cash-and-Card Revenue Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-restaurants/cash-intensive-counter-service/profile|Cash-Intensive Counter-Service Restaurants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No POS or financial tool gives a cash-heavy restaurant a true, real-time picture of total revenue — the 30-70% of sales in cash are either untracked, manually tallied at end-of-night, or lost.
**Tags:** #anomaly-detection #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact #compliance

## The Problem
A taqueria doing $800K/year with 50% cash has accurate digital records for only $400K. The other $400K is tracked via end-of-night cash counts that are error-prone, delayed, and disconnected from item-level sales data. The owner cannot calculate food cost ratios, cannot prove revenue to a lender, and cannot detect employee theft from the cash drawer — because the baseline is unknown.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Cash tracking requires physical infrastructure (smart cash drawers, bill counters) combined with software that reconciles cash and card transactions into a unified ledger. POS companies focus on card processing margins and have no incentive to improve cash handling. Smart cash management tools (CashSimple, Glory) target enterprise retail, not single-location restaurants. The cultural dimension matters too: in some segments, cash opacity is a feature, not a bug — but the majority of operators would prefer accurate records if the tool were simple enough.

## What to Build
A smart cash drawer integrated with the POS that logs every cash transaction at the item level, reconciles with card transactions in real time, produces a unified daily revenue report, and flags anomalies (expected vs. actual cash-in-drawer at shift end). The output is a single revenue dashboard that treats cash and card equally — enabling food cost analysis, lender-ready financials, and theft detection for the first time.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators of counter-service restaurants with 30%+ cash transactions who currently have no item-level visibility into cash sales and cannot prove their true revenue to lenders or insurers.

## Impact If Built
Accurate total-revenue tracking enables food cost analysis (recovering $10K-$30K in margin), access to appropriately priced lending (reducing capital costs by 20-40%), and theft detection (reducing cash shrinkage by 50-80%, saving $5K-$15K annually).
