# Same-Day Menu Change Propagation

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-restaurants/farm-to-table-fine-dining/profile|Farm-to-Table & Seasonal Menu Restaurants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When the menu changes at 2 PM based on the morning delivery, the POS, website, Google listing, and printed menus must all update — and they almost never do in sync.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Farm-to-table restaurants change menu items same-day when a farm delivery brings unexpected availability or a key ingredient does not arrive. The chef updates the kitchen's menu board, but the POS item list, the restaurant's website, the Google Business listing, and the printed menus at the table are updated separately, inconsistently, or not at all. Guests order items that are 86'd, servers spend time explaining substitutions, and online listings show yesterday's menu.

## Why It's Still Broken
Each channel is a separate system with its own login and editing interface. Updating the Toast POS requires navigating a menu management screen. Updating the website requires accessing Squarespace or WordPress. Updating Google requires editing the business profile. No product provides a single "menu source of truth" that propagates changes across all channels simultaneously — the assumption built into every tool is that menus change infrequently.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A central menu editor where the chef enters today's menu once (or voice-dictates changes), and changes propagate to POS, website, Google listing, and a printable PDF in under 5 minutes. The system should also push a "today's menu" notification to the restaurant's Instagram story and email list.

## Who Feels the Pain
Servers who apologize 10-15 times per night for items that are unavailable, and the host who handles frustrated guests who came in specifically for a dish they saw online that is no longer available.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminating menu-channel mismatch reduces guest complaints by 20-30%, saves servers 15-20 minutes per shift in explanations, and prevents the reputation damage of online listings showing dishes that do not exist tonight.
