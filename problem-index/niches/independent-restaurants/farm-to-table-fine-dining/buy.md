# Multi-Vendor Procurement for Small Farm Networks

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-restaurants/farm-to-table-fine-dining/profile|Farm-to-Table & Seasonal Menu Restaurants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** BlueCart and Sourcery handle broadline distributor ordering but cannot manage the 10-20 small-farm vendor relationships with variable availability, informal invoicing, and delivery-day uncertainty.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #nlp

## The Problem
Farm-to-table chefs juggle procurement across 10-20 small farms, each with different communication preferences (text, email, phone call), delivery schedules (some deliver Tuesday/Thursday, some "when the harvest is ready"), and invoicing formats (Venmo, handwritten notes, PDFs, nothing at all). The chef spends 5-8 hours per week on procurement coordination that a broadline restaurant handles with a single Sysco order.

## What Already Exists
BlueCart, Sourcery, and MarketMan offer procurement and vendor management for restaurants, but they assume standardized catalogs, EDI-capable vendors, and consistent delivery schedules. Small farms do not have product catalogs, do not use EDI, and cannot commit to availability more than 48 hours in advance.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to handle unstructured vendor communication (parsing "I'll have about 30 lbs of arugula Thursday or Friday" from a text message into a structured availability record), manage variable delivery windows, track informal payments (Venmo, cash, check), and present a unified "what's available this week across all my farms" view that the chef can use to plan the weekly menu.

## Target Customer
Chef-owners sourcing from 8+ local farms who currently manage procurement through a combination of text messages, phone calls, and memory.

## Impact If Solved
Reducing procurement coordination time from 8 hours/week to 2 hours/week frees 300+ chef-hours annually — time that directly converts to menu development, staff training, and service quality.
