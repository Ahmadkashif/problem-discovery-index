# Farm-to-Table & Seasonal Menu Restaurants

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-50B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — chef-owners tend to be digitally literate but existing food cost tools are designed for stable menus, not weekly-rotating seasonal concepts
**Target Buyer:** Chef-owner sourcing from 10-20 local farms and changing 30-50% of the menu weekly
**Automation Potential:** Medium — menu volatility makes automation harder but the payoff per dish is high (average check $60-$120)

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Farm-to-table restaurants operate on a fundamentally different procurement and menu cycle than conventional independents. Instead of ordering from 2-3 broadline distributors with fixed pricing, they source from 10-20 small farms with variable availability, inconsistent delivery schedules, and pricing that changes with harvest conditions. The menu rotates weekly or even daily based on what arrives, making traditional recipe costing impossible — by the time a dish's food cost is calculated, the dish is off the menu. This creates a paradox: the segment with the highest average check ($60-$120) has the least visibility into per-dish profitability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
POS systems handle transactions but recipe costing tools (xtraCHEF, MarketMan) assume stable menus and broadline distributor invoices. Farm invoices arrive as handwritten notes, Venmo requests, or text messages — completely outside the OCR pipelines of existing tools. No product addresses the "I got 40 lbs of unexpected heirloom tomatoes this morning — what should I make and what should I charge?" workflow.
