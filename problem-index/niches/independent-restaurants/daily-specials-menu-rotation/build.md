# Automated Daily Specials Costing and POS Entry Pipeline

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-restaurants/daily-specials-menu-rotation/profile|Daily Specials & High Menu Rotation Restaurants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-restaurants|Independent Restaurants]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product automates the daily cycle of costing a special, setting a price, pushing it to the POS, briefing servers, and updating the menu board — forcing chefs to either skip costing or spend 30 minutes on manual data entry every morning.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A chef at a seafood restaurant receives the day's fish delivery at 8 AM: 20 lbs of swordfish at $14/lb, 15 lbs of halibut at $18/lb. They decide on two specials — swordfish piccata and halibut crudo. They need to calculate food cost, set a menu price, enter both items into the POS, tell the servers what the specials are and how to describe them, and update the menu board. This workflow happens every day and takes 20-40 minutes, mostly spent on POS data entry and server communication. The food costing step is usually skipped because the chef does not have time to calculate ingredient costs, portion sizes, and margins at 8 AM before prep starts.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
POS companies view specials as edge cases handled by their "add new item" feature. But that feature requires manual entry of name, price, category, and modifiers — designed for items that persist for months, not items that exist for one day. A specials-specific workflow needs to be faster (under 2 minutes), include cost calculation, and automatically archive the item after service for historical tracking.

## What to Build
A mobile app where the chef photographs the day's delivery invoice, selects or voice-dictates specials with ingredient lists, gets an instant cost calculation and suggested price (based on target margin), and pushes the specials to the POS, a server-facing prep sheet (with descriptions and allergy info), and the digital menu board — all in under 2 minutes. Historical tracking shows which specials sold well and were profitable, enabling repeat decisions.

## Target Customer
Chefs and kitchen managers at restaurants running 2-5 daily specials who currently enter them manually into the POS as "open items" and skip food costing entirely.

## Impact If Built
Saving 30 minutes/day of chef time recovers 180 hours/year. Adding food cost visibility to specials (which often run higher cost than the core menu) prevents the 3-5% margin erosion that unprofitable specials create — worth $15K-$40K annually.
