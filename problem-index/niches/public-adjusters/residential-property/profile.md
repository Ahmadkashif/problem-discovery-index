# Residential Property Claims

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 33%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most PAs use Xactimate for estimates and basic CRMs, but documentation, photo management, policy analysis, and client communication remain largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Solo/Small Firm PA
**Automation Potential:** High — Estimate creation, policy coverage analysis, and documentation workflows follow repeatable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential property claims (wind, hail, water, fire damage to single-family homes) are the bread-and-butter of public adjusting. A typical PA handles 30-80 residential claims per year, each requiring property inspection, damage documentation, Xactimate estimate creation, policy coverage analysis, and carrier negotiation. The work is a blend of field inspection (climbing roofs, crawling attics) and desk work (writing estimates, reading policies, negotiating via email). Solo PAs and 2-5 person firms dominate, with most leads coming from referrals and door-knocking after storms.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Xactimate (Verisk) is the universal estimating platform. CRMs like Hail Trace and Job Nimbus track leads and claims. ClaimWizard handles workflow management. The gap is in the pre-estimate and post-estimate phases — no tool helps PAs systematically analyze policy language for coverage arguments, organize field photos into estimate-ready documentation packages, or track carrier response patterns to optimize negotiation timing.
