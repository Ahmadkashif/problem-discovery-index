# Multilingual Claims Communication Platform for PAs

**Niche:** [[niches/public-adjusters/non-english-policyholders/profile|Non-English-Speaking Policyholders]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform enables PAs to communicate with LEP clients in their native language across the entire claims lifecycle — from intake through settlement — with insurance-specific terminology accuracy.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #ai-agent

## The Problem
A bilingual PA can speak with clients in person, but the rest of the claims process is English-only: representation agreements, proof-of-loss forms, carrier correspondence, estimate explanations, settlement breakdowns, and claims status updates. LEP clients sign documents they can't read, receive carrier communications they don't understand, and call their PA repeatedly to ask "what does this letter mean?" The PA becomes a full-time translator in addition to being an adjuster, spending 30-50% more time per LEP client than per English-speaking client.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Insurance translation requires domain expertise — terms like "actual cash value," "subrogation," "loss of use," and "replacement cost endorsement" must be translated with precise legal meaning, not literal translation. A generic translation API that renders "deductible" as a general word meaning "able to be deducted" rather than the insurance concept of "the amount the policyholder pays before insurance coverage begins" creates confusion and potential legal liability.

## What to Build
An LLM-powered communication platform that handles the full client communication lifecycle in the policyholder's language: generates translated representation agreements, translates incoming carrier correspondence with insurance-concept explanations (not just word-by-word translation), creates client-facing claim status updates in the client's language, and produces translated settlement breakdowns showing what each payment component means. Supports the 8 languages most common among US LEP homeowners.

## Target Customer
PAs serving LEP communities — particularly in Florida, Texas, California, New York, and New Jersey where LEP homeowner populations are concentrated and property claims volume is highest.

## Impact If Built
Reduces PA time per LEP client by 30-40% by automating translation tasks. Enables English-only PAs to serve LEP clients for the first time, expanding their addressable market by 15-25%. Improves LEP client satisfaction and referral rates by making the claims process comprehensible.
