# Non-English-Speaking Policyholders

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $400M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Non-English-speaking policyholders have virtually no access to PA services in their language, receive carrier communications they cannot read, and navigate the claims process through informal community translators.
**Target Buyer:** Bilingual PA
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Communication automation is straightforward with LLMs, but culturally appropriate claims advocacy requires human relationship management.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Over 25 million US homeowners have limited English proficiency (LEP). When they suffer property damage, they face a double barrier: navigating a complex insurance claims process and doing so in a language they don't fully understand. Carrier adjusters typically communicate only in English. Policy documents are in English. Xactimate estimates are in English. LEP policyholders settle for significantly less — studies suggest 20-40% lower settlements — because they can't effectively advocate for themselves and don't know public adjusters exist. PAs who speak Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, or Haitian Creole and actively market to LEP communities capture an underserved market with minimal competition.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Google Translate handles basic communication but produces inaccurate results for insurance-specific terminology. No PA-specific tools support multilingual client communication, document translation, or claims explanation in the policyholder's native language. Bilingual PAs operate identically to English-only PAs, just with verbal translation — all documentation, estimates, and carrier communications remain in English.
