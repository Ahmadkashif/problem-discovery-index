# Commercial Property Claims

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 24%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Commercial PAs use Xactimate, but business interruption calculations, commercial policy analysis, and large-loss documentation remain spreadsheet-heavy manual processes.
**Target Buyer:** Mid-Size PA Firm Owner
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Property damage estimates are automatable, but business interruption and extra expense calculations require judgment and forensic accounting skills.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial property claims are larger ($100K-$10M+), more complex, and require different skills than residential work. A commercial PA must handle not just physical damage estimates but also business interruption (BI) loss calculations, extra expense claims, ordinance-or-law coverage, and multi-location losses. Commercial policies are far more complex than homeowner policies — manuscript endorsements, layered excess coverage, coinsurance penalties, and agreed-amount provisions. The PA often works alongside forensic accountants, engineers, and attorneys, acting as a project manager for the entire claim.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Xactimate handles the structural estimate portion. Forensic accountants use Excel for BI calculations. Commercial policy analysis is done manually by reading 200-500 page policy documents. The gap is in BI loss modeling — no tool connects a business's financial records (P&L, revenue trends) to policy provisions (BI coverage limits, waiting periods, period of restoration) to produce a defensible BI claim calculation that the PA can negotiate from.
