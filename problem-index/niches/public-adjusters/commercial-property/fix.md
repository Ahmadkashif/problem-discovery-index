# Multi-Party Large-Loss Coordination Breakdown

**Niche:** [[niches/public-adjusters/commercial-property/profile|Commercial Property Claims]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Large commercial losses involve the PA, policyholder, carrier adjuster, building engineer, forensic accountant, attorney, and restoration contractor — all communicating through disconnected email chains with no shared project timeline.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
A $1M+ commercial property loss involves 6-10 parties who must coordinate inspections, document exchanges, expert reports, estimate reviews, and settlement negotiations over 6-18 months. The PA acts as the policyholder's project manager, tracking deadlines, scheduling joint inspections, sharing documentation, and ensuring all parties have current information. This coordination happens through email, phone calls, and shared Dropbox folders — with no unified timeline, task tracking, or document version control. A missed inspection deadline or an outdated estimate version sent to the carrier can delay settlement by months.

## Why It's Still Broken
Each party uses different tools — the PA uses ClaimWizard or a CRM, the carrier uses their claims system, the engineer uses project management software, the attorney uses a case management system. No neutral platform exists for multi-party claim collaboration because there's no single party with the authority or incentive to mandate adoption. The PA can't require the carrier to use their platform, and vice versa.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A claim collaboration portal that any party can access via web link (no software installation or account creation required). Provides a shared timeline of claim milestones, document repository with version control, joint inspection scheduling with availability polling, and communication log with threaded discussions by topic (coverage, estimate, BI, engineering). The PA controls the portal and invites other parties, who access their relevant sections. Critical: must work for parties who refuse to create accounts — share-by-link with optional authentication.

## Who Feels the Pain
PAs managing 5-10 active large-loss claims simultaneously, each with different parties at different stages. Policyholders who have no visibility into claim progress and rely on the PA for status updates.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average large-loss claim duration by 60-90 days through better coordination. Eliminates 5-10 hours per week per claim of coordination overhead for the PA. For a firm managing 8 active large losses, saves 40-80 hours per week in coordination time.
