# Carrier Negotiation & Supplement Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $700M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Supplement requests are submitted via email with PDF attachments; carrier responses are tracked in spreadsheets or not tracked at all. No structured negotiation workflow exists.
**Target Buyer:** PA Negotiator / Office Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Supplement requests follow predictable patterns (carrier denies item, PA provides justification), and response tracking is a pure workflow automation opportunity.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
After a PA submits an initial estimate, the carrier's adjuster reviews it and typically responds with a lower counter-estimate, agreeing on some items and disputing others. The PA then submits "supplements" — additional documentation justifying disputed items, adding missed items discovered during repairs, and correcting carrier pricing errors. The average residential claim requires 2-4 supplement rounds over 3-6 months. Each supplement requires the PA to compare the carrier's estimate line-by-line against their own, identify discrepancies, write justification narratives, and submit with supporting photos. This negotiation cycle is the core revenue activity — but it's managed through email threads with no systematic tracking.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Email is the primary communication channel. Some PAs use ClaimWizard or Xact supplement features to track submissions. No tool provides a structured negotiation workflow: side-by-side estimate comparison, item-level dispute tracking, automated justification letter generation, deadline monitoring, and outcome analytics showing which arguments succeed with which carriers.
