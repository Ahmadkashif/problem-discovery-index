# Catastrophe (CAT) Response

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 16%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — CAT response teams deploy to disaster zones with limited connectivity, using paper intake forms, offline photo capture, and manual claim triage with no digital workflow infrastructure for field operations.
**Target Buyer:** CAT Team Manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Intake and triage are automatable, but field conditions (no power, no internet, emotional policyholders) require flexible, offline-capable workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
After major hurricanes, tornadoes, wildfires, and floods, public adjusters deploy teams of 10-50 adjusters to affected areas, signing hundreds of clients in days and processing claims over months. CAT response is fundamentally different from daily claims work — it requires rapid client intake, mass triage of damage severity, coordination of field teams across geographic zones, and management of claim volumes 10-20x normal. PAs who do $2M in annual revenue might handle $15M in CAT years. The operational challenge is scaling from a 5-person firm to a 50-person operation overnight in a disaster zone.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Basic CRMs (Job Nimbus, Hail Trace) track leads but aren't designed for mass intake. Xactimate handles estimates but requires connectivity. Communication relies on cell phones and group texts. No platform supports the full CAT deployment lifecycle — mass client intake, field team dispatch, offline inspection capability, triage-based prioritization, and centralized estimate review across dozens of adjusters working simultaneously.
