# Condo Association & HOA Claims

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $500M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — HOA management software handles dues and maintenance, but insurance claims for common elements involve manual coordination between the association's master policy, individual unit owner policies, and the PA.
**Target Buyer:** PA Specializing in Associations
**Automation Potential:** Medium — The complexity lies in policy layering (master policy vs. unit owner policy), not in processing steps.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Condo association and HOA property claims are structurally different from single-family residential claims. Damage to common elements (roofs, hallways, pools, parking structures) is covered under the association's master policy, while damage to individual units may fall under the unit owner's HO-6 policy, the master policy's "all-in" or "bare walls" provisions, or both. A single storm event can trigger 50-200 individual unit claims plus a master policy claim, each requiring coordination. Most PAs avoid this niche because the legal complexity of determining which policy covers what is daunting, and the association board decision-making process is slow.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HOA management platforms (AppFolio, Buildium, CINC) handle property management but not insurance claims. PA CRMs track individual claims but can't manage the many-to-one relationship of 100 unit claims linked to one event. No tool maps the coverage boundaries between master policies and unit owner policies to determine claim allocation before estimates are written.
