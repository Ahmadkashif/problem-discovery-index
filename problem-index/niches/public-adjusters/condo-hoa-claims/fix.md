# Board Decision Paralysis on Claims Engagement

**Niche:** [[niches/public-adjusters/condo-hoa-claims/profile|Condo Association & HOA Claims]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** HOA boards take 4-8 weeks to decide whether to hire a PA — burning through carrier response deadlines — because they lack the financial analysis to compare "accept carrier offer" versus "hire PA and negotiate."
**Tags:** #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a condo association suffers property damage, the carrier makes an initial offer. The board must decide whether to accept it or hire a PA to negotiate a higher settlement. This decision requires comparing the carrier's offer to the likely true cost of repairs, factoring in the PA's contingency fee (10-15%), and considering the risk that negotiation could delay repairs. Board members are volunteers — homeowners, not insurance professionals — and they lack the tools to make this analysis. Board meetings happen monthly. By the time the board votes to hire a PA, 4-8 weeks have passed, carrier-imposed deadlines may have lapsed, and evidence of damage may have been compromised by emergency repairs.

## Why It's Still Broken
PAs pitch boards with anecdotal evidence ("we typically get 3x the carrier's initial offer") but can't provide property-specific analysis before engagement. The PA can't inspect the property or review the policy until the board signs a representation agreement. This creates a chicken-and-egg problem: the board needs data to make the decision, but the PA can't generate data without the engagement. Boards default to inaction, accepting carrier offers that average 40-60% of actual repair costs.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A pre-engagement analysis tool that PAs can use in board presentations: input the property type, size, damage type, carrier name, and initial offer; output a data-driven comparison showing (1) typical settlement uplift for this property/damage type based on historical PA performance data, (2) net benefit after PA fees, (3) estimated timeline for negotiated settlement versus immediate acceptance. Includes anonymized case studies from similar properties. Gives the board enough information to make an informed decision in one meeting instead of three.

## Who Feels the Pain
PA firms that lose 40-60% of HOA prospects because the board can't make a timely decision. HOA boards that accept inadequate settlements because they couldn't evaluate the PA option quickly enough.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces board decision time from 4-8 weeks to 1-2 weeks. Increases PA conversion rate on HOA prospects from 30-40% to 60-70%. Recovers an average of $200K-$500K in additional settlement per association that engages a PA instead of accepting the carrier's initial offer.
