# Contents & Inventory Claims

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $600M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Contents inventories are built manually in spreadsheets, with replacement values researched one item at a time through retail websites. Photo documentation of personal property is ad hoc.
**Target Buyer:** PA / Contents Specialist
**Automation Potential:** High — Contents inventory creation is a repetitive research task where each item requires the same process: identify, describe, find replacement, price.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Contents claims — personal property damaged or destroyed in a loss — are the most labor-intensive component of residential and commercial claims. A fire or flood loss may involve 500-5,000 individual items across a home, each requiring identification, description, age estimation, replacement cost research, and depreciation calculation. Contents specialists spend 40-80 hours on a single large contents inventory. The work is tedious, detail-oriented, and poorly compensated relative to the structural estimate — yet the contents claim often represents 30-50% of the total settlement value.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Contents inventory software (ContentsTrack, Encircle) provides templates and room-by-room organization. Replacement cost databases (XactContents within Xactimate) offer standardized pricing for some categories. The gap is massive: these tools still require manual item-by-item entry, manual replacement cost research for non-standard items, manual depreciation calculations, and manual photo-to-item matching. The core workflow is unchanged from 20 years ago — someone sits with a spreadsheet and types.
