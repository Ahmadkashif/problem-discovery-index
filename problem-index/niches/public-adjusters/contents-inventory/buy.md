# Replacement Cost Research Engine

**Niche:** [[niches/public-adjusters/contents-inventory/profile|Contents & Inventory Claims]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-adjusters|Public Adjusters]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Price comparison engines exist for retail shopping, but none are designed for insurance contents claims — matching damaged items to comparable replacement products with documented pricing evidence acceptable to carriers.
**Tags:** #llm #recommendation #nlp #automation

## The Problem
For each item in a contents inventory, the specialist must find a replacement product of "like kind and quality" and document its current retail price. For a 2,000-item inventory, this means 2,000 individual product searches — checking Amazon, Wayfair, Home Depot, and specialty retailers to find the closest match and capture a screenshot of the price as evidence. A 30-second search per item adds up to 17 hours of pure price research on a single claim. Items that are discontinued, specialty, or antique require even more time.

## What Already Exists
XactContents (within Xactimate) provides a standardized pricing database for common contents categories with pre-set replacement values. Google Shopping and retail aggregators enable product search. Neither provides the specific output PAs need: a matched replacement product with "like kind and quality" justification, current retail price from a verifiable source, and a formatted evidence page (screenshot with URL, date, price) that can be submitted to the carrier.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a search engine purpose-built for contents claims: enter an item description (e.g., "Samsung 65-inch 4K Smart TV, 2021 model"), and the engine returns the closest current replacement product with its retail price from a major retailer, a "like kind and quality" comparison justification, and an auto-generated evidence page. Must handle discontinued products by finding the successor model and specialty items by searching niche retailers.

## Target Customer
Contents specialists processing 5-15 contents claims per month, each requiring 500-3,000 item price lookups.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces replacement cost research time by 70-80% (from 30 seconds to 5-7 seconds per item). On a 2,000-item inventory, saves 12-14 hours of research time per claim. Improves consistency and defensibility of replacement values, reducing carrier disputes over individual item pricing.
