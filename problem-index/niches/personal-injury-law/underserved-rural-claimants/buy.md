# Remote Evidence Collection Toolkit

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-injury-law/underserved-rural-claimants/profile|Underserved Rural Claimants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Rural PI clients capture admissible injury and accident scene evidence from their phones — guided photo/video workflows replace the investigator site visit that's impractical when the accident scene is 100 miles from the attorney's office.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
PI cases require visual evidence: injury progression photos, vehicle damage documentation, accident scene conditions (road markings, signage, sight lines, hazards), and property damage. In urban markets, the firm sends an investigator or the client comes to the office. In rural markets, neither option is economical — a private investigator charges mileage plus hourly for a 200-mile round trip, and the client can't take a day off to drive in. So evidence goes uncollected. Photos that the client does take on their own are often inadmissible or ineffective: wrong angles, no scale reference, poor lighting, missing context (they photograph the dented bumper but not the intersection where the accident occurred). By the time the attorney realizes the photos are inadequate, the vehicle has been repaired and the accident scene has changed.

## What Already Exists
Photo and video capture apps exist (smartphone cameras). Evidence documentation tools for insurance adjusters (ClaimXperience, Snapsheet) handle guided photo capture for vehicle damage but are designed for claims adjusters, not PI clients. CaseSnap provides visual documentation for construction and real estate but not PI. Generic secure messaging apps (Signal, WhatsApp) are used informally for client evidence sharing but offer no guidance on what to photograph or how.

## The Customization Gap
The missing layer is a PI-specific evidence capture workflow that runs on the client's phone and guides them through collecting admissible evidence. For a vehicle accident: guided photo sequence (front, rear, sides, interior, dashboard, odometer, VIN plate, license plates, accident scene from four cardinal directions, signage, road conditions, skid marks). For injuries: time-stamped progression photos with body-part labels, scale reference instructions, and reminders at 1-day, 1-week, 2-week, and 1-month intervals. For premises liability: guided scene documentation before the property owner remedies the hazard. Each photo is automatically geotagged, time-stamped, and stored with chain-of-custody metadata. The app provides real-time feedback: "This photo is too dark — try again with the flash" or "Include a ruler or coin for scale reference." Output syncs to the case management system as organized evidence folders.

## Target Customer
Paralegals and case managers at PI firms serving rural clients who currently receive inadequate evidence photos from clients and cannot economically send investigators to rural accident scenes.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers evidence that would otherwise be lost in 30-40% of rural PI cases due to distance-related collection barriers. Eliminates $300-800 per case in investigator travel costs for rural site visits. Produces more complete and better-organized visual evidence than most client-collected photos, strengthening case value by 10-20% through better documentation.
