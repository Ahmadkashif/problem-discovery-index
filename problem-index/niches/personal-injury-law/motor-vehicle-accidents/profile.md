# Motor Vehicle Accidents

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $22-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~45%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — firms use Filevine or SmartAdvocate for case management, but settlement intelligence and opposing-counsel analytics are nonexistent
**Target Buyer:** MVA-focused PI firm owner handling 200-2,000+ active cases
**Automation Potential:** High — liability determination, police report parsing, insurance adjuster behavior tracking, and PIP/no-fault calculations are all structured enough to automate

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
MVA is the volume backbone of personal injury law — the bread and butter that pays the bills while bigger cases gestate. What makes MVA operationally distinct is the adversarial intelligence problem: the insurance company's claims adjuster uses Colossus or similar evaluation software to generate a settlement range, and the plaintiff's attorney has no visibility into how that number was computed. The defense side has algorithmic consistency; the plaintiff side has gut feel. Additionally, MVA cases involve multi-state complexity (accident in one state, treatment in another, client domiciled in a third), police report parsing for liability facts, PIP/no-fault threshold analysis in relevant states, and high-volume case management where a single firm may have 500+ active MVA files. The economics are contingency-driven: a 2% improvement in average settlement value across 500 cases is worth more than one large verdict.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Filevine and SmartAdvocate handle case management, task tracking, and document storage. Clio and MyCase serve smaller firms. None of these platforms offer opposing-side intelligence — they don't track which adjusters at which carriers settle at what ranges for what injury types in which jurisdictions. CaseGlide exists for defense-side litigation management but has no plaintiff counterpart. Police report parsing is manual. Multi-state statute of limitations tracking is calendar-based with no automated conflict detection when a case touches multiple jurisdictions.
