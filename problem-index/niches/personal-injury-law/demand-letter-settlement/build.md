# AI Demand Letter Generator with Settlement Intelligence

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-injury-law/demand-letter-settlement/profile|Demand Letter & Settlement Valuation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-injury-law|Personal Injury Law]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** PI attorneys get a first-draft demand letter that pulls medical chronology, injury narrative, special damages totals, and comparable verdict data automatically — cutting demand preparation from 8-20 hours to 2-4 hours of review and customization.

## The Problem
Drafting a demand letter is a multi-step process requiring synthesis across the entire case file. The writer must: (1) construct a liability narrative from police reports, witness statements, and expert opinions, (2) build an injury narrative from medical records showing causation, treatment progression, and ongoing impairment, (3) calculate special damages (medical bills, lost wages, future medical costs, future lost earnings), (4) research comparable verdicts and settlements in the jurisdiction for similar injuries, (5) determine a demand amount that reflects the case value while leaving room for negotiation, and (6) write persuasively in the firm's established style and tone. This process takes a senior paralegal or associate attorney 8-20 hours per demand letter. At a firm producing 20-30 demands per month, that's one full-time employee doing nothing but demand drafting. The quality varies wildly — the firm's best demand writer produces letters that settle cases 20-30% higher than the average writer because they know how to frame the narrative, what comparable to cite, and how to structure the damages presentation for maximum impact.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
LLMs can generate demand letter prose, but producing a legally accurate, case-specific demand requires integration with the full case file — medical records, billing data, wage verification, liability evidence, and comparable verdicts. No LLM has access to a firm's case management data, and no case management platform has integrated LLM-powered document generation with the depth required. The comparable verdict problem is particularly hard: verdict databases contain raw data (case type, injury, venue, amount) but the attorney must select the 3-5 most persuasive comparables from thousands of options, considering not just injury type but venue, defendant type, plaintiff demographics, and whether the case went to trial or settled. This selection requires judgment that current tools don't automate.

## What to Build
An end-to-end demand letter generation platform. Input: case data from Filevine/SmartAdvocate via API (medical chronology, billing data, wage records, liability documents). Processing: (1) LLM generates liability narrative from case facts, (2) LLM generates injury narrative from medical chronology, (3) system calculates special damages from billing and wage data with itemized totals, (4) ML model searches comparable verdict databases and selects the 3-5 most persuasive comparables based on case similarity features (jurisdiction, injury type, plaintiff age, treatment duration, surgery involvement), (5) gradient-boosted model predicts settlement range based on case features and comparable outcomes, (6) LLM assembles the full demand letter in the firm's customized style template. Output: a first-draft demand letter with embedded settlement range recommendation and supporting comparable citations. The attorney reviews, adjusts the narrative and demand amount, and sends. The firm's style is learned from 50-100 prior demand letters used as fine-tuning examples.

## Target Customer
Managing partners and senior paralegals at PI firms producing 15+ demand letters per month who want to standardize quality, reduce preparation time, and ensure every demand is supported by comparable verdict research.

## Impact If Built
Reduces demand letter preparation from 8-20 hours to 2-4 hours of review. Standardizes demand quality across the firm — the junior associate's demand is now informed by the same comparable research and case analysis that the senior partner would apply. The settlement range prediction captures tacit valuation knowledge, preventing both overvaluation (demands that get rejected and delay resolution) and undervaluation (demands that leave 20-30% on the table). Estimated revenue impact: 10-15% improvement in average settlement value through better-informed demands and consistent quality.
