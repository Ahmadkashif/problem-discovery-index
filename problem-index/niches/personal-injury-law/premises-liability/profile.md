## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Premises liability cases require a fundamentally different evidence-gathering approach than MVA cases. Instead of police reports and vehicle damage photos, the attorney must prove the property owner knew or should have known about a hazardous condition. This requires establishing "notice" — prior complaints, inspection failures, code violations, or similar incidents on the same property. The critical evidence challenge is time-sensitivity: security camera footage overwrites every 30-90 days, incident reports get "lost," and property managers destroy maintenance logs. An attorney who doesn't send a spoliation letter within days of intake may lose the case before it starts. Additionally, property ownership chains matter — the actual defendant may be a holding company, LLC, or management company three layers removed from the property name on the door.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Filevine and SmartAdvocate manage the case once opened but have no premises-specific intake checklists, evidence preservation workflows, or property research integrations. PropertyShark and county assessor databases provide ownership data but require manual searches — no tool automatically traces the full ownership chain for a given address. Code violation databases exist at the municipal level but are not aggregated or searchable across jurisdictions. No PI case management tool triggers automated spoliation letter generation upon case intake for premises cases.
