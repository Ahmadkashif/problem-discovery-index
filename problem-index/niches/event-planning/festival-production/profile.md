# Festival & Multi-Day Event Production

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Ticketing is digital, but production logistics (stage scheduling, vendor load-in coordination, safety compliance, crew management) rely on spreadsheets and radio.
**Target Buyer:** Festival producers and multi-day event organizers managing complex, multi-venue, multi-stage productions.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Stage scheduling, vendor load-in sequencing, and crew shift management are optimizable; creative and safety decisions require experienced judgment.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Festivals and multi-day events are orders of magnitude more complex than single-day events. A 3-day music festival manages: 5+ stages with overlapping performance schedules, 50-200 vendors (food, merchandise, sponsors), 200-500 crew members across production, security, medical, and operations departments, permit compliance across multiple government agencies (health, fire, police, noise), infrastructure buildout (temporary power, water, waste management), and weather contingency planning. The production timeline spans months, the buildout takes days, and the event itself creates real-time logistical challenges that single-day events don't face.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Ticketing platforms (Eventbrite, Universe, Tixr) handle ticket sales. Artist management tools (Master Tour, Gigwell) handle talent booking. Generic project management (Basecamp, Asana) handles planning. No platform integrates the festival-specific production workflow: multi-stage schedule management with changeover windows, vendor load-in/load-out sequencing, crew scheduling across departments, real-time operational communications, and post-festival settlement.
