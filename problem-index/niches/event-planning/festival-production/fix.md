# Vendor Load-In/Load-Out Gridlock

**Niche:** [[niches/event-planning/festival-production/profile|Festival & Multi-Day Event Production]]
**Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** 50 vendors with trucks all arrive during the same 4-hour load-in window, creating gridlock that delays setup by hours and damages vendor relationships.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
Festival buildout day: 50-200 vendors (food trucks, merchandise tents, sponsor activations, rental companies) all need to access the site, unload equipment and inventory, and set up in their assigned locations. Most festivals assign broad load-in windows ("food vendors: 8am-noon") without specific time slots, creating a bottleneck at the site entrance. Trucks wait in line for 1-3 hours, blocking roads and frustrating vendors who have perishable inventory or tight schedules. The same chaos repeats during load-out after the festival, often worse because everyone wants to leave simultaneously.

## Why It's Still Broken
Vendor load-in scheduling is an afterthought — the production team focuses on stage construction, artist logistics, and permits. Vendor load-in instructions are a paragraph in the vendor welcome packet: "arrive between 8am and noon, check in at Gate C." There's no time-slot reservation system, no route assignment based on vendor location within the site, and no real-time monitoring of load-in progress. The production team discovers the gridlock when vendors start calling the office complaining.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A vendor load-in/load-out scheduling system: each vendor receives a specific 30-minute arrival window based on their site location (vendors at the back of the site load first, nearest-to-gate vendors load last), vehicle size (18-wheelers before box trucks before vans), and setup time requirements. The system generates a route assignment per vendor (which gate, which internal road). Real-time check-in at the gate tracks progress and adjusts remaining windows if delays occur. Load-out uses the reverse sequence with departure time slots.

## Who Feels the Pain
Vendors who waste 2-3 hours in load-in lines with perishable goods and tight crew schedules, and production managers who spend load-in day managing traffic complaints instead of overseeing site buildout.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average vendor wait time from 90 minutes to 15 minutes, eliminates perishable goods spoilage during load-in delays, and improves vendor satisfaction — critical because 70% of festival revenue comes from vendor fees and concessions.
