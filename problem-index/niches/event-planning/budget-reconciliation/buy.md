# Event-Specific Financial Reporting for Corporate Clients

**Niche:** [[niches/event-planning/budget-reconciliation/profile|Budget & Invoice Reconciliation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Accounting software generates P&L reports for the planner's business — but corporate clients need event-specific financial reports formatted for their procurement and finance teams.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance

## The Problem
Corporate clients who hire event planners need detailed financial reports for internal accounting: cost breakdowns by category (venue, F&B, AV, entertainment, decor), comparison to approved budget, supporting documentation (vendor invoices), and sometimes cost allocation across departments or cost centers. The planner's accounting system (QuickBooks) tracks revenue and expenses for the planning business — not for the client's event. Building a client-facing financial report requires extracting event-specific costs from the planner's books, reformatting them to the client's required structure, and attaching supporting documentation.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks, Xero, and FreshBooks handle business accounting with reporting. Corporate expense management tools (Concur, Certify) handle employee expense reporting. Event budgeting tools within planning platforms (Honeybook, Aisle Planner) track estimated costs. None generate the corporate-compliant event financial report that a Fortune 500 client's procurement team requires: budget vs. actual, cost allocation by department, supporting invoices, and GL-code-mapped expense categories.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs to map event costs to the client's financial reporting structure: their GL codes, their cost center allocations, their budget categories. The event planner enters costs once; the system generates both the planner's internal P&L view and the client's required financial report format. Supporting invoices attach to the corresponding line items. The report exports in formats corporate finance teams accept (Excel with specific column structures, PDF with executive summary).

## Target Customer
Event planning agencies serving 5+ corporate clients per year who spend 4-8 hours per event building custom financial reports that satisfy different clients' procurement requirements.

## Impact If Solved
Saves 4-8 hours per corporate event in financial reporting, reduces payment delays caused by incomplete financial documentation (corporate AP won't pay without proper reporting), and positions the event planner as a financially sophisticated partner — differentiating from competitors who submit a single-line invoice.
