# Corporate Events & Conferences

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Registration platforms and venue booking tools are mature, but multi-stakeholder approval workflows, cross-event analytics, and post-event ROI reporting remain manual.
**Target Buyer:** Corporate event planners and agencies managing conferences, trade shows, retreats, product launches, and executive summits.
**Automation Potential:** High — RFP processes, venue comparison, attendee communication, and post-event reporting follow standardizable templates.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate events operate under procurement rules, budget approval chains, and measurable ROI expectations that consumer events don't face. The planner must navigate multi-stakeholder decision-making (marketing wants experiential, finance wants cheap, leadership wants impressive), detailed RFP processes for venue and vendor selection, and post-event ROI reporting that justifies the spend to CFOs. Events are budget line items — they must demonstrate business value (leads generated, relationships strengthened, brand visibility) or face cuts in the next fiscal year.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Registration platforms (Cvent, Eventbrite, Splash) handle attendee management. Venue sourcing tools (Cvent Supplier Network, HotelPlanner) handle RFPs. Project management tools (Asana, Monday.com) handle planning timelines. No platform integrates the corporate-specific workflow: multi-stakeholder approval chains for budget decisions, procurement-compliant vendor selection with comparative analysis, and post-event ROI dashboards linking event spend to business outcomes.
