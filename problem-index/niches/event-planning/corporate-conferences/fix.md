# Multi-Stakeholder Approval Bottleneck

**Niche:** [[niches/event-planning/corporate-conferences/profile|Corporate Events & Conferences]]
**Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Every major event decision requires approval from 3-5 stakeholders with different priorities — and chasing approvals via email delays timelines by weeks while venues and vendors hold options.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
Corporate events involve multiple decision-makers: the event planner proposes, the marketing VP approves the concept, the finance director approves the budget, the legal team reviews contracts, and sometimes the CEO signs off on high-profile events. Each approval requires a different information package (marketing cares about brand alignment, finance cares about cost breakdown, legal cares about liability clauses). Approvals happen via email chains that get lost, forwarded without context, or delayed by executives who are traveling. Meanwhile, the venue is holding a tentative date that expires in 72 hours.

## Why It's Still Broken
Corporate approval workflows are handled by email and ad hoc meetings because event planning tools don't model multi-stakeholder decision workflows. The planner knows they need finance approval before signing the venue contract, but there's no system that routes the decision with the right information to the right approver with deadline enforcement. Each approval chase consumes 2-4 hours of the planner's time — time spent crafting emails, following up, and re-summarizing for stakeholders who missed the original context.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A decision routing system for event planning: the planner initiates a decision request (venue selection, budget approval, contract review) with role-specific information packages. Marketing sees the brand and experience brief; finance sees the cost comparison matrix; legal sees the contract with flagged terms. Approvals are tracked with deadlines and escalation paths. The planner sees a single dashboard of pending decisions with status indicators. When a venue hold expires in 48 hours, the system escalates the budget approval to the VP's assistant with urgency flagging.

## Who Feels the Pain
Event planners spending 8-12 hours per event chasing approvals via email, losing venue options because approvals came 3 days late, and operating under constant stress from the mismatch between vendor hold deadlines and corporate approval timelines.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces approval cycle time from 2-3 weeks to 3-5 days, prevents lost venue options (each requiring the planner to restart the selection process), and reclaims 8-12 hours per event in follow-up time.
