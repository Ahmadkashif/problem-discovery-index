# Detail Confirmation Chaos

**Niche:** [[niches/event-planning/wedding-planning/profile|Wedding Planning]]
**Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Two weeks before the wedding, the planner discovers the caterer has the wrong meal count, the florist has the wrong color palette, and the DJ has the wrong first dance song — because detail confirmations were never systematically collected and verified.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
Weddings have hundreds of specific details that must be confirmed with each vendor: exact meal count per entree option, specific flower varieties and colors, song selections and do-not-play lists, linen colors and sizes, transportation pickup times and addresses, cake flavor per tier, hair/makeup trial scheduling. These details are communicated to vendors over months of planning, often changing multiple times as the couple makes decisions. The planner's nightmare: arriving at the final walkthrough and discovering the caterer has the meal count from 3 months ago, not the final count updated 2 weeks ago via text.

## Why It's Still Broken
Details are communicated ad hoc — a text here, an email there, a phone call where something was mentioned. There's no formal handoff that says "these are the FINAL confirmed details for this vendor." The couple changes their mind about the first dance song, the planner texts the DJ, but doesn't update the planning document. Two months later, the planner's document says "Can't Help Falling in Love" but the DJ has "At Last" — and no one catches it until sound check.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A vendor detail confirmation workflow: for each vendor, define the required detail fields (caterer: meal counts, dietary restrictions, service time, head count; DJ: song list, first dance, do-not-play, timeline). As details are decided, they populate into a structured form. Two weeks before the wedding, each vendor receives their detail sheet for confirmation — a digital form they must acknowledge. Discrepancies between the planner's record and the vendor's understanding are flagged for resolution. The planner sees a confirmation dashboard: green (vendor confirmed all details), yellow (sent, not confirmed), red (discrepancies identified).

## Who Feels the Pain
Wedding planners who discover detail mismatches during final walkthroughs (requiring frantic last-minute corrections), and couples who experience wedding-day surprises (wrong cake flavor, wrong song) that ruin their experience and the planner's reputation.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates wedding-day detail errors that occur in 15-25% of weddings (based on planner surveys), protects the planner's reputation and review ratings, and reduces the anxiety spiral in the final 2 weeks before each wedding.
