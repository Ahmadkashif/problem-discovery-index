# Wedding Planning

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/event-planning|Event Planning]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $70B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~55%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Couples use planning apps (The Knot, Zola), but planners themselves rely on spreadsheets, text messages, and email for vendor coordination and timeline management.
**Target Buyer:** Independent wedding planners and full-service wedding agencies managing 15-40 weddings per year.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Vendor coordination, timeline templates, and client communication follow patterns, but the emotional/creative aspects require deep personalization.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wedding planning is an emotionally charged, once-in-a-lifetime purchase with average spend of $35,000-$60,000. The planner manages the couple's vision, family dynamics, vendor relationships, and day-of logistics — all while maintaining emotional composure when the bride is in tears over centerpiece colors. The business operates on referrals and reviews — one bad wedding can destroy a planner's reputation. Weddings involve 10-25 vendors (venue, caterer, photographer, DJ, florist, officiant, rentals, cake, hair/makeup, transportation, lighting) who must all coordinate around a single 6-hour timeline. The planner is the central nervous system connecting all of them.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Couple-facing platforms (The Knot, Zola, WeddingWire) provide planning tools for couples but not for professional planners. Planner-specific tools (Aisle Planner, Honeybook, Dubsado) handle client management, contracts, and basic timelines. None solve the core operational challenge: coordinating 15-25 vendors who each use different communication channels, confirming details across all vendors as the wedding approaches, and managing the day-of timeline where a 15-minute delay cascades through every subsequent event.
