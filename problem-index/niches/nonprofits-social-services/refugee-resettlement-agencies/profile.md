# Refugee Resettlement Agencies

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — the nine national resettlement agencies use centralized systems but local affiliates rely on spreadsheets and paper; client-facing tools rarely accommodate limited English proficiency
**Target Buyer:** Resettlement Program Director at local affiliate agencies
**Automation Potential:** Medium — initial case setup and benefit enrollment are rule-based, but cultural mediation and housing search require human judgment

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Refugee resettlement operates under a unique service delivery model: local affiliates of nine national agencies (IRC, USCRI, HIAS, etc.) receive newly arrived refugees and must achieve specific self-sufficiency milestones within 90 days — housing, employment, school enrollment, benefits enrollment, and cultural orientation. The timeline is extreme, the population has limited English and digital literacy, documentation is in multiple languages, and case managers must navigate federal (ORR), state, and local benefit systems simultaneously. Generic social service case management tools were not designed for this intensity of service within a hard 90-day window.

## Current Tools & Gaps
National agencies use centralized databases (IRC has Integra, others use custom systems) for ORR reporting. Locally, affiliates use ETO, Apricot, or spreadsheets. No tool handles the multi-language document management problem (translating intake documents from Arabic, Dari, Swahili, etc.), and no platform tracks the 90-day self-sufficiency milestone timeline with the granularity case managers need (Day 3: social security appointment, Day 7: school enrollment, Day 14: job readiness assessment).
