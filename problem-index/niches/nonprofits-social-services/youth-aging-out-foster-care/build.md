# Engagement-First Mobile Case Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/nonprofits-social-services/youth-aging-out-foster-care/profile|Youth Aging Out of Foster Care]]
**Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool today provides aging-out foster youth with a mobile-first portal to manage their own transition to adulthood — seeing their goals, accessing resources, scheduling with their case manager, and tracking their benefits — instead of being passive recipients of institutional case management.
**Tags:** #ai-agent #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Youth aging out of foster care are 18-21-year-old digital natives who communicate via text and social media — but their case management system is a case manager's desktop database they never see. They miss appointments because reminders come via voicemail to a disconnected number. They don't know what benefits they're eligible for because the Chafee program is explained in a 20-page handbook nobody reads. They disengage from services because the system treats them as cases to be managed rather than young adults building their own lives.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Youth-facing technology in child welfare is politically sensitive — agencies worry about data privacy (foster care records are highly confidential), liability (what if a youth misunderstands their benefit eligibility based on app information?), and equity (not all youth have smartphones). Building for this population also requires understanding their specific barriers: frequent phone number changes, limited data plans, distrust of institutional systems, and the need for plain-language communication that doesn't feel patronizing.

## What to Build
A mobile app (with SMS fallback for low-data situations) where aging-out youth can see their transition goals and progress, access a personalized resource directory filtered by their location and eligibility, schedule and reschedule appointments with their case manager via text, upload required documents (pay stubs, lease agreements, enrollment verification) by photo, and receive proactive nudges about upcoming deadlines (Chafee education voucher renewal, Medicaid extension application). The case manager sees the same data in their desktop interface.

## Target Customer
Independent Living Program coordinators serving 50-200 youth annually who struggle with engagement and contact maintenance. There are 400+ ILP programs nationwide.

## Impact If Built
Increases youth engagement with services from 50% to 75%, reduces missed appointments by 40%, and improves Chafee benefit utilization from 35% to 60% by making benefits visible and accessible to the youth themselves.
