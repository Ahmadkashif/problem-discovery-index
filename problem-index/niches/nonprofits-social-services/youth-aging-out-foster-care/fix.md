# Post-Discharge Contact Loss

**Niche:** [[niches/nonprofits-social-services/youth-aging-out-foster-care/profile|Youth Aging Out of Foster Care]]
**Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** ILP programs lose contact with 40-50% of youth within 6 months of foster care discharge because youth change phone numbers, addresses, and email addresses — and programs have no secondary contact channel to re-engage them.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
When a youth ages out of foster care, the ILP coordinator's primary contact method is a cell phone number that may change within months (prepaid plans, lost phones, new carriers). The youth may not have a stable email address. Physical mail is unreliable because housing changes frequently. Within 6 months, 40-50% of youth become unreachable. These are often the youth who need services most — the ones whose initial housing didn't work out, who lost their job, or who are experiencing a crisis. Coordinators spend hours trying to locate lost youth through social media searches, calls to last-known employers, and asking other youth in the program.

## Why It's Still Broken
The root cause is single-channel contact dependency — if the coordinator has only a phone number and that number disconnects, there's no fallback. Youth consent to services but don't anticipate needing ongoing contact, so they don't provide backup contact methods. Programs don't collect social media handles (privacy concerns), and data sharing between programs is restricted by child welfare confidentiality rules, so one program can't check if a youth has popped up in another system.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A multi-channel contact management system that captures 3-5 contact methods at enrollment (primary phone, secondary phone, email, social media handle, trusted adult's contact info), monitors contact validity (detects disconnected phone numbers, bounced emails), and escalates through channels when primary contact fails. The system also enables opt-in text-based check-ins where the youth simply replies "I'm good" monthly to maintain contact, making engagement effortless rather than appointment-dependent.

## Who Feels the Pain
ILP coordinators who carry caseloads of 30-50 youth and spend 5-8 hours per month trying to locate youth they've lost contact with — knowing that each lost youth is at heightened risk for homelessness, incarceration, or trafficking.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces 6-month contact loss from 45% to 15%, enables coordinators to maintain support relationships during the highest-risk transition period, and recovers 6-8 hours per month of coordinator time currently spent on locate efforts.
