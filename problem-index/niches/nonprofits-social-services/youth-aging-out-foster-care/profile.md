# Youth Aging Out of Foster Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — state child welfare systems (SACWIS/CCWIS) track youth in care but independent living programs use paper-based service tracking; youth themselves are digitally fluent but underserved by institutional technology
**Target Buyer:** Independent Living Coordinator / Transitional Housing Program Manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Chafee benefit tracking and housing placement are automatable, but mentorship and life skills coaching require human relationships

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Each year, 20,000+ youth "age out" of foster care at 18-21 without family support — no co-signer for an apartment, no parent to call when the car breaks down, no safety net. Independent Living Programs (ILPs) and Transitional Living Programs (TLPs) serve this population with housing, education, employment, and life skills support, funded through Chafee Foster Care Independence Act dollars. The service model is unique: clients are legal adults who cannot be mandated into services, engagement is voluntary, and the outcome timeline is long (3-5 years to stable adulthood). Generic case management tools designed for mandated services fail because they assume compliance rather than engagement.

## Current Tools & Gaps
State CCWIS (Comprehensive Child Welfare Information System) tracks youth in care but stops at discharge. ILP programs use spreadsheets, Apricot, or ETO for post-discharge tracking. No tool handles the specific challenges of this population: tracking Chafee benefit utilization (education vouchers, housing subsidies), managing transitional housing placements with lease training, or maintaining engagement with a mobile population that changes phone numbers and addresses frequently.
