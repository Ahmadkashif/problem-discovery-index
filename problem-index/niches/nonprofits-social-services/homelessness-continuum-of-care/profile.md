# Homelessness Continuum of Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 22%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — HUD mandates HMIS (Homeless Management Information System) data entry, but agencies use it as a compliance tool rather than a case management platform
**Target Buyer:** CoC Lead Agency Director / HMIS Administrator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — HUD reporting is automatable, but client intake and coordinated entry require human judgment

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Continuums of Care (CoCs) are HUD-designated regional bodies that coordinate homelessness response across multiple agencies — shelters, rapid rehousing providers, permanent supportive housing operators, and outreach teams. The CoC model is unique because it requires data sharing and coordinated decision-making across independently operated organizations with different case management systems, funding sources, and service philosophies. A single CoC may coordinate 20-80 agencies serving 5,000-50,000 individuals annually, all reporting into a shared HMIS.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HMIS platforms (Clarity, ServicePoint/WellSky, Bitfocus Clarity) handle federally mandated data collection but are widely disliked by frontline workers for their clunky interfaces and rigid data models. Coordinated entry systems exist but coordination between agencies still relies on weekly meetings, shared spreadsheets, and phone calls. No platform effectively handles the real-time bed availability and service matching problem across a multi-agency CoC.
