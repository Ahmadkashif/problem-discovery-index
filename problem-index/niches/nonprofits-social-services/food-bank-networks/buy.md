# Distribution Route Optimization

**Niche:** [[niches/nonprofits-social-services/food-bank-networks/profile|Food Bank Networks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Route optimization tools like OptimoRoute and Route4Me handle delivery logistics but don't account for food bank-specific constraints — volunteer drivers, mixed temperature loads, time-sensitive perishables, and variable partner agency receiving windows.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Food banks distribute food via a fleet of 5-20 trucks (often a mix of owned, donated, and rented vehicles with different capacity and temperature capabilities) to 50-300 partner agencies scattered across a service area that can span an entire state. Route planning currently happens manually — the logistics coordinator builds routes in Google Maps, considering vehicle cold chain capability, agency receiving hours, load weight limits, and driver volunteer schedules. A single route change (agency closed, truck breakdown, last-minute donation pickup) requires re-planning the entire day.

## What Already Exists
OptimoRoute, Route4Me, and Routific provide vehicle routing with time windows, capacity constraints, and multi-stop optimization. DispatchTrack and Onfleet add real-time tracking. However, these tools are designed for commercial delivery with professional drivers and standardized vehicles — they don't handle volunteer driver skill variations, mixed temperature loads on the same truck, priority scoring based on food expiration urgency, or the constraint that some agencies can only receive on specific days with specific volunteers.

## The Customization Gap
Food bank routing requires a layer that manages volunteer driver availability (changing weekly), matches vehicle temperature capabilities to load requirements (frozen, refrigerated, dry), priorities based on perishable shelf life (deliver the yogurt before the canned goods), and handles the frequent re-routing caused by last-minute donation pickups. The tool must also work with the food bank's existing donor pickup workflow, where trucks pick up from grocery stores on the way to distribution sites.

## Target Customer
Logistics coordinators at food banks running 20+ delivery routes per week with mixed fleets of 5-15 vehicles.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces route planning time from 3 hours to 30 minutes per day, cuts fuel and vehicle costs by 15-25%, and enables 10-15% more deliveries per week with the same fleet by optimizing load consolidation and routing efficiency.
