# Food Bank Networks

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $9B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 33%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — large food banks use Link2Feed or Pantry Soft for client intake, but food sourcing, inventory management, and distribution logistics rely on spreadsheets and phone calls
**Target Buyer:** Food Bank Operations Manager / Logistics Coordinator
**Automation Potential:** High — food sourcing, inventory rotation, and distribution routing are optimization problems with clear objective functions

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Food bank networks are complex logistics operations disguised as charities. A regional food bank receives donated food from 200-500 sources (grocery chains, manufacturers, USDA commodities, food drives), warehouses it across 1-5 facilities, and distributes it to 50-300 partner agencies (food pantries, soup kitchens, shelters). The product is perishable, supply is unpredictable, demand fluctuates with economic conditions, and the workforce is 70% volunteer. The operational complexity rivals a mid-sized food distributor, but the technology budget is 1% of what a commercial distributor spends.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Link2Feed handles client intake at food pantries. Feeding America provides Meal Connect for food rescue. Some large food banks use Primarius or custom warehouse management systems. However, most food sourcing is managed via phone calls and email, route planning is done manually, and inventory tracking across multiple warehouses is maintained in Excel. No end-to-end platform connects food sourcing, warehouse management, and distribution routing for the food bank operating model.
