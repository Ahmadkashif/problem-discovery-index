# Multi-Funding-Stream Eligibility Engine

**Niche:** [[niches/nonprofits-social-services/federally-funded-workforce-programs/profile|Federally Funded Workforce Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic eligibility tools like Aunt Bertha/findhelp handle basic benefits screening but cannot determine co-enrollment eligibility across WIOA Adult, Dislocated Worker, Youth, TAA, and SNAP E&T funding streams.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A single job seeker may be eligible for 3-5 simultaneous funding streams — WIOA Adult, WIOA Dislocated Worker, Trade Adjustment Assistance, SNAP Employment & Training, and state-funded programs. Each stream has different eligibility criteria, documentation requirements, and enrollment procedures. Case managers currently assess eligibility for each program separately using paper worksheets, often missing co-enrollment opportunities that would bring additional resources to the participant and additional funding to the program.

## What Already Exists
Findhelp (formerly Aunt Bertha) screens for public benefits eligibility across 100+ programs. State workforce systems handle WIOA-specific eligibility. ETO and Apricot track enrollment status. However, none of these tools can assess simultaneous eligibility across multiple workforce-specific funding streams, identify optimal co-enrollment combinations, or generate the documentation packages required for each stream.

## The Customization Gap
The missing piece is a workforce-specific eligibility engine that knows the exact eligibility rules for WIOA, TAA, SNAP E&T, state workforce programs, and local initiatives in a given state/locality, can assess all simultaneously from a single intake interview, and outputs a recommended co-enrollment strategy with the specific documentation needed for each program. Rules must be updateable as federal and state guidance changes annually.

## Target Customer
Intake coordinators at American Job Centers who assess 50-200 new participants per month and currently spend 30-45 minutes per person on eligibility determination.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces eligibility assessment from 35 minutes to 8 minutes per participant, increases co-enrollment rates by 40% (unlocking additional per-participant funding), and eliminates the 10-15% of eligibility determinations that are later reversed due to documentation errors.
