## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Reentry services help formerly incarcerated individuals transition back into the community — securing housing, employment, identification documents, benefits, substance abuse treatment, and family reunification. The population faces unique barriers: criminal background checks block housing and employment, digital literacy gaps from years without technology access, active supervision requirements (parole/probation) that constrain availability, and deep distrust of institutional systems. Generic social service tools ignore these barriers entirely, and case managers must navigate a complex web of legal restrictions, community resources, and supervision requirements.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some reentry programs use Apricot or ETO for case management. Probation/parole departments use separate supervision systems (COMPAS, ORAS). Resource directories like 211 and findhelp list reentry-specific services but don't filter by conviction type (sex offense restrictions, violent crime restrictions). No tool connects the supervision side (parole requirements, court dates, drug testing schedules) with the services side (housing search, job placement, treatment enrollment).
