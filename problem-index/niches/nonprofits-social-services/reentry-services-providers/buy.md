# Reentry Case Plan Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/nonprofits-social-services/reentry-services-providers/profile|Reentry Services Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Case management platforms like Apricot and ETO provide general case plan templates but lack reentry-specific workflows — parole compliance tracking, court date management, conviction-specific service sequencing, and supervision appointment integration.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
Reentry case plans are uniquely complex: they must sequence services around parole/probation requirements (weekly check-ins, random drug tests, curfews), court-mandated programs (anger management, substance abuse treatment), and legal milestones (record expungement eligibility dates). A case manager building a plan in Apricot or ETO must manually add every parole appointment, cross-reference court orders, and sequence services to avoid conflicts — a process that takes 2-3 hours per client and must be updated every time a supervision requirement changes.

## What Already Exists
Apricot, ETO (Bonterra), and Penelope offer case plan templates with goal-setting and milestone tracking. Criminal justice case management systems (Northpointe, Tyler Technologies) handle supervision from the corrections side. However, these systems don't talk to each other — the case manager in a nonprofit reentry program cannot see their client's parole schedule, and the parole officer cannot see the reentry program's service plan.

## The Customization Gap
The missing capability is a reentry-specific case plan builder that imports supervision requirements (parole schedule, court dates, mandated programs) from corrections systems, sequences voluntary services (housing, employment, education) around mandatory obligations, and automatically adjusts the plan when supervision requirements change. The tool should flag scheduling conflicts (job interview conflicts with parole check-in) and suggest resolution options.

## Target Customer
Case managers at reentry nonprofits managing caseloads of 25-50 clients who spend 2-3 hours per case plan build and 30 minutes per week per client on schedule management.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces case plan creation from 3 hours to 45 minutes, eliminates scheduling conflicts that cause parole violations (estimated 5-10% of violations are due to service appointment conflicts), and enables case managers to increase caseloads by 20% without sacrificing plan quality.
