# Benefits Eligibility Screeners

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/nonprofits-social-services|Nonprofits Social Services]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 11%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — tools like findhelp and BenefitsCheckUp exist but are used as lookup tools, not integrated screening engines; intake workers still assess eligibility manually using program knowledge
**Target Buyer:** Intake Coordinator / Benefits Navigator at community-based organizations
**Automation Potential:** High — eligibility rules for SNAP, Medicaid, TANF, LIHEAP, WIC, and other programs are complex but fully codifiable based on household composition, income, and categorical criteria

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Benefits eligibility screening is the front door to social services — every nonprofit, healthcare provider, and government agency has intake workers who assess client eligibility for public benefits. The screening process is highly rule-based (income thresholds, categorical eligibility, asset tests) but enormously complex because rules vary by state, change annually, and interact with each other (receiving one benefit may make you categorically eligible for another). Experienced benefits navigators carry this knowledge in their heads; new workers take 6-12 months to become proficient. This is a prime automation target because the rules are documented but too complex for manual application.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Findhelp (Aunt Bertha) provides program directories with basic eligibility filters. BenefitsCheckUp (NCOA) screens for 2,000+ programs for seniors. Single Stop screens for benefits at community colleges. However, these tools screen one program at a time and produce binary eligible/not-eligible results without explaining why, suggesting next steps for borderline cases, or generating the application paperwork. No tool handles the sequential strategy of "apply for Medicaid first because that makes you categorically eligible for SNAP."
