# Self-Service Freight Quoting & Booking for Small Shippers

**Niche:** [[niches/freight-brokerage/small-shipper-spot-market/profile|Small Shipper Spot Market Participants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Small shippers (1-10 loads/week) have no self-service platform that lets them get instant freight quotes, compare carrier options by price and transit time, book loads, and track shipments without waiting for a broker to respond to an email or phone call.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #automation #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A small manufacturer in Ohio needs to ship 3 pallets to a customer in Georgia. They email their broker a quote request at 9am. The broker responds at 2pm with a single rate. The shipper has no idea if the rate is fair, has no alternative options to compare, and can't book until the broker confirms carrier availability — which might not happen until the next morning. By then, the pickup window may have shifted. Large shippers with TMS access and dedicated broker reps get instant quotes and same-hour booking. Small shippers get a friction-filled, opaque process that discourages shipping altogether.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The economics of serving small shippers individually don't support the broker's overhead: each $500-$1,500 load generates $75-$300 in gross margin, but the quoting and booking process costs $30-$50 in broker labor per transaction. A self-service platform that automates quoting and booking makes small shippers economically viable, but requires upfront investment in carrier rate API integration, automated carrier matching, and a shipper-facing UX that traditional brokerage tech teams don't build.

## What to Build
A shipper-facing web platform where small shippers: (1) enter load details (origin, destination, commodity, weight, dimensions); (2) receive instant quotes from multiple carriers ranked by price and transit time; (3) book a carrier with one click and receive a BOL; (4) track the shipment through delivery with POD. The platform operates as an automated brokerage backend — no human broker involved for standard loads, with escalation to a human for exceptions (hazmat, oversized, access issues).

## Target Customer
Shipping managers at small manufacturers, distributors, and e-commerce sellers shipping 1-10 FTL or LTL loads per week. An estimated 200,000+ small businesses in the US ship freight regularly without a dedicated logistics function.

## Impact If Built
Reducing quote-to-book cycle from 4-24 hours to 5 minutes for small shippers. Brokerages operating this platform can profitably serve the small-shipper segment at scale, accessing $8-10B in previously uneconomical freight spend.
