# Small Shipper Spot Market Participants

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — small shippers (1-10 loads/week) often request quotes by email or phone, don't have TMS access, and lack visibility into market rates
**Target Buyer:** Shipping manager or warehouse supervisor at small manufacturers, distributors, or retailers
**Automation Potential:** Medium — quoting and booking workflows are standardized enough for self-service, but small shippers value human support for exception handling

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small shippers (manufacturers, distributors, or retailers shipping 1-10 loads per week) represent a large but economically unattractive segment for traditional freight brokers. The per-load revenue doesn't justify the broker's time to quote, book, and manage each shipment individually. As a result, small shippers receive the worst service: slow quote responses (hours instead of minutes), limited carrier options (the broker uses whoever is available rather than optimizing), and no rate transparency (they can't verify whether the quoted rate is fair). Many small shippers don't even know their options beyond calling a single local broker or using UPS/FedEx Freight for LTL.

## Current Tools & Gaps
uShip provides a marketplace for small shippers to get carrier quotes. Freightquote (C.H. Robinson) offers online quoting for small shipments. These tools provide rate comparison but not ongoing shipper relationship management, proactive rate optimization, or the kind of consultative shipping advice that large shippers get from their dedicated broker. Small shippers are served transactionally, not relationally.
