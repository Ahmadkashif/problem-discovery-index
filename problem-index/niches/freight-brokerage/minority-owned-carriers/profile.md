# Minority-Owned & Emerging Carrier Networks

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — many minority-owned carriers are small fleets (1-10 trucks) with basic ELD compliance but no TMS, limited load board usage, and inconsistent digital documentation
**Target Buyer:** Carrier development manager at brokerages with diversity procurement programs, or owner-operators seeking brokerage partnerships
**Automation Potential:** Medium — carrier onboarding, compliance verification, and load matching for small carriers can be streamlined, but relationship development requires human engagement

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate shippers increasingly require their logistics providers to demonstrate supply chain diversity — routing a percentage of freight through minority-owned, women-owned, veteran-owned, or disadvantaged business enterprise (MBE/WBE/DBE) carriers. Brokerages that can verify and connect diverse carriers to shipper diversity procurement programs capture premium business. However, the diverse carrier pool is fragmented: most MBE/WBE carriers are small fleets (1-5 trucks) that lack the technology, compliance infrastructure, and brokerage relationships to access large shipper accounts. They operate on spot loads from load boards rather than contracted lanes.

## Current Tools & Gaps
NMSDC (National Minority Supplier Development Council) and SBA provide diversity certification directories. Load boards (DAT, Truckstop) don't filter by carrier diversity certification. No platform specifically connects certified diverse carriers with brokerages fulfilling shipper diversity requirements, or helps small diverse carriers build the compliance and operational infrastructure needed to handle contracted lanes.
