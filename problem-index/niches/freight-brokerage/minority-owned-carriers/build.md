# Diverse Carrier Network Marketplace

**Niche:** [[niches/freight-brokerage/minority-owned-carriers/profile|Minority-Owned & Emerging Carrier Networks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform connects verified MBE/WBE/DBE carriers directly to brokerages fulfilling shipper diversity procurement requirements — with integrated certification verification, performance tracking, and capacity visibility — creating a trusted network that turns diversity compliance from a box-checking exercise into a carrier development pipeline.
**Tags:** #data-integration #ai-platform #revenue-impact #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A Fortune 500 shipper requires their freight broker to route 15% of loads through certified diverse carriers. The broker's carrier development team spends weeks identifying diverse carriers, verifying certifications (NMSDC, SBA 8(a), WBE), onboarding them into the TMS, and testing them on loads. Most identified carriers are 1-3 truck operations that can handle spot loads but lack the capacity or operational maturity for contracted lanes. The broker needs a ready-to-deploy pool of verified, performance-tracked diverse carriers across major lanes — and no such pool exists.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Building a diverse carrier marketplace requires: (1) aggregating certification data from multiple organizations (NMSDC, WBENC, SBA, state DOTs) with inconsistent data formats; (2) verification and ongoing monitoring of certification status (certifications expire); (3) performance tracking to ensure the marketplace represents reliable carriers, not just certified ones; (4) integration with broker TMS systems for seamless load tendering. The diversity certification ecosystem is fragmented across federal, state, and private certification bodies.

## What to Build
A carrier marketplace specifically for certified diverse carriers that: (1) aggregates and verifies MBE/WBE/DBE certifications from all major certifying bodies; (2) provides lane-level carrier capacity (which diverse carriers operate on which lanes); (3) tracks on-time delivery rate, claims history, and communication responsiveness per carrier; (4) integrates with McLeod/Mercury Gate for direct load tendering; (5) generates the diversity spend reports shippers require for procurement compliance.

## Target Customer
Carrier development managers at mid-to-large brokerages with shipper diversity requirements. Approximately 500 brokerages handle accounts with formal diversity procurement mandates. Secondary buyer: diverse carrier owners seeking brokerage partnerships.

## Impact If Built
Brokerages meeting shipper diversity requirements capture premium accounts: Fortune 500 logistics contracts averaging $5-50M annually. Diverse carriers gain access to contracted lanes (stable, predictable revenue) instead of competing on spot load boards.
