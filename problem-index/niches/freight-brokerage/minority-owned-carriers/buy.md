# Compliance & Certification Management for Small Carriers

**Niche:** [[niches/freight-brokerage/minority-owned-carriers/profile|Minority-Owned & Emerging Carrier Networks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic carrier compliance tools (MyCarrierPortal, Carrier411) verify FMCSA authority and insurance, but don't track diversity certifications (MBE/WBE/DBE), alert carriers when certifications are expiring, or guide small carriers through the certification and renewal process.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A minority-owned carrier with 3 trucks wants to access contracted lanes through brokerages, but the path requires: maintaining FMCSA compliance (authority, insurance, ELD), obtaining diversity certification (NMSDC, WBENC, or SBA 8(a)), renewing certifications annually, and providing documentation that brokerages require for their shipper diversity reports. Small carriers manage this compliance burden with paper files and memory — leading to lapsed certifications that lock them out of diversity programs. A carrier that forgot to renew their NMSDC certification loses access to diversity-routed loads until renewal is processed (4-8 weeks).

## What Already Exists
MyCarrierPortal and Carrier411 monitor FMCSA compliance status. NMSDC and WBENC have their own certification tracking portals. No tool consolidates both FMCSA compliance and diversity certification status into a single carrier compliance dashboard, or provides proactive renewal alerts and guided application workflows.

## The Customization Gap
A small-carrier compliance tool for diverse carriers needs: (1) unified tracking of FMCSA authority, insurance, ELD, and diversity certifications in one dashboard; (2) proactive renewal alerts 90/60/30 days before any certification expires; (3) guided application workflows for initial diversity certification (NMSDC, WBENC, SBA) with document checklists; (4) a "compliance passport" that the carrier can share with brokerages — a single verified link showing all certifications are current.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators and small fleet managers (1-10 trucks) at minority-owned, women-owned, or veteran-owned carrier companies seeking to access contracted freight through brokerage diversity programs.

## Impact If Solved
Carriers with current certifications access contracted lanes averaging 10-20% higher per-mile rates than spot loads. Preventing certification lapses avoids 4-8 weeks of lost access to diversity-routed freight — potentially $10K-$30K in lost revenue per lapse.
