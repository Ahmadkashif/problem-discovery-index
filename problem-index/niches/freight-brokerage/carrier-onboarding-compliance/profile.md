# Carrier Onboarding & Compliance Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B (services)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3% (embedded operational cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most brokerages collect carrier packets via email and verify compliance manually through FMCSA and insurance certificate review
**Target Buyer:** Compliance manager or carrier setup coordinator at mid-to-large brokerages
**Automation Potential:** High — carrier onboarding is a document collection, verification, and data entry workflow with clear rule-based validation requirements

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Before a carrier can haul a single load for a brokerage, they must be onboarded: W-9 collection, FMCSA authority verification, insurance certificate of insurance (COI) review with minimum coverage thresholds, ELD compliance verification, and often a signed carrier agreement. A mid-market brokerage onboards 50-200 new carriers per month. Each onboarding takes 30-60 minutes of manual document review, data entry, and system setup. The compliance dimension makes this distinct from general onboarding: a carrier with lapsed insurance or suspended FMCSA authority must be immediately flagged and deactivated — failure to catch this creates liability for the brokerage in the event of an accident.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MyCarrierPortal provides carrier compliance monitoring. Highway provides carrier identity and compliance verification. These tools verify compliance status but don't automate the full onboarding workflow: document collection, data extraction from carrier packets, COI coverage verification against brokerage minimums, carrier agreement execution, and TMS carrier profile creation. The gap is between "compliance verification" and "onboarding automation."
