# Perishable & Produce Lane Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/freight-brokerage|Freight Brokerage]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — temperature monitoring is often paper-based (printed pulp temp recorders), lane-specific knowledge lives in broker notebooks, and USDA/PACA compliance tracking is manual
**Target Buyer:** Produce desk broker or owner at perishable freight brokerages
**Automation Potential:** Medium — temperature compliance documentation and PACA claim management are automatable, but the core perishable expertise is deeply tacit

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Perishable freight brokerage (produce, dairy, meat, flowers) operates under constraints that general dry van brokerages never encounter: continuous cold chain compliance from pickup to delivery, USDA inspection requirements at origin and destination, PACA (Perishable Agricultural Commodities Act) trust and prompt-payment rules, and extreme time sensitivity — a load of strawberries that's 6 hours late loses 20% of its shelf life and value. Produce brokers develop tacit knowledge about which carriers maintain proper reefer temperatures, which routes have seasonal congestion that threatens perishable timing, and how to read pulp temperature data to assess whether a carrier maintained the cold chain. This expertise cannot be transferred to a general dry van dispatcher.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Carrier reefer units have integrated temperature monitoring (Carrier ThermoKing, Emerson GO Real-Time), but the data flows to the carrier, not the broker. DAT provides reefer lane rates. PACA compliance tracking is done in spreadsheets. No integrated platform combines temperature monitoring visibility, PACA compliance tracking, and produce-specific lane intelligence for the perishable freight broker.
