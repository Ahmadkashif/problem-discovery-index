# Warranty Compliance Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/commercial-flat-roof/profile|Commercial Flat Roof]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Commercial roofers install $200K-2M roof systems with 20-year manufacturer warranties that require specific documentation at installation and annual compliance actions afterward — but 10-15% of warranty registrations are never completed and 25-40% of maintenance-required warranties lapse due to missed inspection deadlines.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #automation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Every major commercial roof manufacturer (GAF, Carlisle, Firestone, Johns Manville, Sika Sarnafil) offers tiered warranty programs with different documentation requirements. A GAF 20-year NDL warranty requires: certified installer status (renewed annually), use of GAF-approved materials with invoice documentation, installation photos at specified stages (deck prep, insulation, membrane installation, flashing details, completed roof), and online registration within 30 days of completion. A Carlisle warranty requires different documentation, different photo requirements, and a different registration process. A commercial roofer installing 30-50 projects per year across 3-4 manufacturers manages 100+ distinct warranty documentation requirements annually. The person responsible — usually an office manager or project coordinator — tracks deadlines in Outlook reminders and requirements in a spreadsheet. When that person is sick, on vacation, or leaves the company, warranties fall through the cracks. A voided $500K warranty on a 100,000 SF industrial roof because the registration was submitted on day 35 instead of day 30 creates enormous liability and damages the contractor's relationship with both the building owner and the manufacturer.

## Why It's Still Broken
Manufacturer warranty portals are siloed — GAF's portal doesn't talk to Carlisle's portal, and neither integrates with the contractor's project management system. Each manufacturer updates their warranty requirements periodically (new photo requirements, changed documentation formats, updated material approval lists), and contractors learn about changes through manufacturer rep visits or by having a registration rejected. There is no unified standard for commercial roof warranty documentation across manufacturers. The inspection compliance problem is worse: a warranty that requires annual inspections means the contractor must schedule, perform, document, and submit inspection reports for every warranted roof in their portfolio, every year, for 15-30 years. A commercial roofer with 200 active warranties needs to complete 200 inspections per year just to maintain warranty compliance — and there's no system that tracks which inspections are due, which are overdue, and which warranties have already lapsed.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A warranty lifecycle management system that ingests manufacturer warranty requirements (documentation checklists, registration deadlines, maintenance obligations) and connects them to the contractor's project records. At project completion, the system generates a manufacturer-specific registration package from existing project documentation, flags missing items, and tracks submission through to confirmation. Post-registration, it maintains a compliance calendar across the entire warranty portfolio — alerting 60, 30, and 7 days before each annual inspection deadline, generating inspection report templates pre-populated with roof details, and tracking submission to the manufacturer. The system should integrate with CompanyCam or similar photo platforms to auto-pull installation photos that match manufacturer requirements.

## Who Feels the Pain
Office managers and project coordinators who spend 5-10 hours per week on warranty documentation across multiple manufacturer portals. Company owners who carry liability for voided warranties they didn't know had lapsed. Building owners who discover their "20-year warranty" is actually a 5-year warranty because the contractor missed a registration deadline.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates warranty registration failures on 10-15% of projects, preventing $50K-200K liability exposure per occurrence. Recovers 5-10 hours per week of administrative time currently spent on manual warranty tracking. Protects the contractor's manufacturer certification status, which requires warranty registration completion rates above 90% for programs like GAF Master Elite.
