**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28-32%

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial flat roofing is a lifecycle business, not a one-time installation. A commercial roof system (TPO, EPDM, or PVC membrane over insulation and cover board) costs $8-25 per square foot installed and carries a 15-30 year manufacturer warranty from GAF, Carlisle, Firestone, or Johns Manville — but only if the contractor meets specific installation requirements, uses approved materials, and completes warranty registration within 30-60 days of project completion. The ongoing revenue stream comes from maintenance contracts: annual inspections, drain clearing, seam re-welding, and condition reports that building owners need for insurance, lease compliance, and capital planning. An experienced commercial roof inspector can walk a 50,000 SF roof and identify membrane punctures, seam failures, ponding areas, flashing deterioration, and remaining service life within an hour. They know that a TPO membrane with chalking and micro-cracking has 3-5 years left while one with only surface dirt has 10+ years. Junior inspectors miss early deterioration signs and either over-recommend replacement (losing the client's trust) or under-report problems (creating liability). The manufacturer warranty ecosystem adds another layer: contractors must be "certified" by each manufacturer (GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster equivalent for commercial), use approved accessories, and document installation steps — miss any requirement and the 20-year warranty becomes a 5-year warranty.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Procore is used by larger commercial roofers for project management but has no roof-specific features — it doesn't track membrane type, warranty terms, or maintenance schedules. AccuLynx is residential-focused and doesn't handle commercial bid processes, multi-phase projects, or warranty compliance documentation. EagleView provides measurement for commercial roofs but not condition assessment. RoofSnap handles measurement but is residential-oriented. No platform connects the installation record (materials used, manufacturer lot numbers, installation photos) to the warranty requirements (manufacturer certification, approved materials, inspection intervals) to the maintenance lifecycle (annual inspection findings, repair history, remaining life estimates).
