## The Problem
When a commercial building owner faces a roof replacement decision, they receive bids from 3-5 contractors, each recommending a different membrane system at different price points with different warranty structures. A TPO roof costs $6-9/SF installed with a 20-year warranty. An EPDM system costs $5-8/SF with a 15-year warranty. PVC costs $7-11/SF with a 20-year warranty but better chemical resistance. The building owner has no way to compare total lifecycle cost because the true cost includes: installation, annual maintenance ($0.10-0.25/SF/year), expected repairs (seam failures, punctures, flashing issues), energy impact (reflective TPO vs. black EPDM), and end-of-life replacement timing. Experienced commercial roofing consultants carry this lifecycle knowledge intuitively — they know that an EPDM roof in a Midwest climate with heavy freeze-thaw cycles will need seam repairs starting at year 8-10, while a TPO in the same climate develops membrane shrinkage issues at year 12-15. This tacit knowledge about system performance by climate, building use, and maintenance quality drives their recommendations, but most building owners never hire a consultant and instead rely on the installing contractor's inherently biased recommendation.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Lifecycle cost modeling requires longitudinal data that no single contractor possesses. A roofing company sees its own installations over 10-20 years but has no visibility into competitor installations on similar buildings. Manufacturer warranty claim data (which would reveal actual failure rates by system, climate, and building type) is proprietary and never shared. Building owner maintenance records are fragmented across property management companies, in-house facility teams, and multiple roofing contractors over a roof's life. The modeling challenge is compounded by climate variation (a TPO roof in Phoenix performs very differently than one in Minneapolis), building use variation (a restaurant with grease-laden exhaust destroys certain membranes faster), and maintenance quality variation (a well-maintained EPDM outperforms a neglected TPO). The data sources exist in fragments — manufacturer technical bulletins, warranty claim patterns visible to large contractors, NRCA technical publications, building energy modeling databases — but nobody has assembled them into a predictive model.

## What to Build
A lifecycle cost modeling platform that takes inputs (building location, roof area, current system age/type/condition, building use, maintenance history, local climate data from NOAA) and outputs a total-cost-of-ownership comparison across membrane systems over 15-30 year horizons. The core model uses gradient-boosted regression trained on available data sources: manufacturer technical performance data, published warranty claim rates, NRCA maintenance cost benchmarks, and contractor-contributed anonymized job data (installation cost, repair history, replacement timing). For each system option, the model forecasts: year-by-year maintenance costs, probability of major repair by year, expected energy cost differential (using DOE roof reflectivity data and local energy rates), and optimal replacement timing. Output is a decision-support report that commercial roofers present to building owners — transforming the sales process from "trust my recommendation" to "here's the data."

## Target Customer
Commercial roofing contractors with $3-15M annual revenue managing 50-200 maintenance contracts and bidding 20-50 replacement projects per year. The platform doubles as a sales tool (data-driven system recommendations close at higher rates) and a maintenance contract justification tool (showing building owners how annual maintenance extends roof life by 5-10 years).

## Impact If Built
Increases commercial roof replacement close rates by 15-25% through data-backed lifecycle cost presentations versus competitor gut-feel proposals. Doubles maintenance contract attachment rates from 30% to 60% by quantifying the ROI of annual maintenance in extended roof life, adding $500K-2M in recurring annual maintenance revenue for a mid-size commercial roofer.
