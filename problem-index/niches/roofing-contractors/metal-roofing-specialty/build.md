# Metal Roof Panel Layout Optimizer

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/metal-roofing-specialty/profile|Metal Roofing Specialty]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A system that takes roof geometry (from EagleView or manual input), panel profile specifications, and installation constraints and generates an optimized panel layout that minimizes material waste — replacing the experienced estimator's mental spatial reasoning with algorithmic optimization that consistently achieves 5-8% waste on complex geometries versus the 15-25% waste that junior estimators produce.

## The Problem
Metal roof panel layout is a two-dimensional cutting and packing problem that experienced estimators solve intuitively. Given a roof with 8-12 facets of varying shapes (rectangles, triangles, trapezoids formed by hips, valleys, and dormers), the estimator must determine: panel orientation per facet (parallel to ridge vs. parallel to eave — different choices produce different waste), panel starting position (centering panels vs. starting from one edge affects the width of cut panels at each end), panel lengths (custom-cut to facet dimension, but panels from different facets can sometimes be cut from the same stock length), and trim requirements (each linear foot of ridge, hip, valley, eave, and gable requires specific trim profiles). An experienced metal roof estimator carries a spatial optimization model in their head — they can look at a complex hip roof and know that starting panels 3 inches off-center on the north facet will produce a cut piece that fits perfectly on the south dormer, saving an entire panel. This tacit spatial reasoning develops over hundreds of estimates and thousands of panels. A junior estimator facing the same roof orders each facet independently, producing 20-30% waste on a material that costs $4-12 per square foot. On a 3,000 SF standing seam job at $8/SF, that's $4,800-7,200 in unnecessary material cost.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The optimization problem is computationally tractable (it's a variant of the 2D cutting stock problem, which has well-studied algorithmic solutions), but the roofing-specific constraints are numerous and poorly documented. Panel layout must account for: thermal expansion gaps between panels, minimum panel width at cuts (panels narrower than 4" are structurally problematic), clip placement requirements that affect panel starting positions, snow guard placement on specific panels, and manufacturer-specific interlock tolerances. These constraints vary by panel profile (standing seam vs. snap-lock vs. mechanical-lock), manufacturer, and climate zone. The input data problem is also significant: optimizing panel layout requires precise roof geometry in 3D (not just 2D area measurements), including facet angles, ridge/hip/valley intersections, and penetration locations. EagleView provides 2D measurements; converting to the 3D geometry needed for panel layout optimization requires additional processing. SmartBuild and AppliCad solve parts of this problem but are designed for CAD-trained users, not field estimators.

## What to Build
A panel layout optimization engine that takes three inputs — roof geometry (imported from EagleView measurements plus pitch data, or manual input), panel profile specifications (width, available stock lengths, minimum cut width, expansion gap requirements), and installation constraints (clip spacing, snow guard locations, aesthetic preferences). The engine solves the 2D cutting stock problem per facet with cross-facet optimization (reusing off-cuts from one facet on another), then generates: a visual panel layout map per facet showing panel positions, lengths, and cut lines; a material order list with exact quantities by panel length; a trim takeoff with linear footage by profile type; and a waste report showing total waste percentage versus industry average. The optimizer should allow the estimator to override individual panel positions (for aesthetic reasons or site constraints) and re-optimize around the constraints. Output should export directly to manufacturer order formats.

## Target Customer
Metal roofing contractors with $1-8M annual revenue performing 30-100 metal roof installations per year. The sweet spot is companies growing beyond what their one experienced estimator can handle — they need to let junior estimators produce metal roof quotes without the 20-30% material waste penalty.

## Impact If Built
Reduces material waste from 15-25% (junior estimator average) to 5-8% (optimized layout), saving $2,000-6,000 per job on a typical residential metal roof. For a contractor installing 50 metal roofs per year, that's $100K-300K in annual material savings. Secondary impact: reduces estimation time from 2-4 hours of manual layout to 15-30 minutes of input and review, enabling 3-4x more quotes per estimator per week.
