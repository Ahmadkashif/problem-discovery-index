# Metal Roofing Specialty

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8-9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Metal roofing requires specialized panel layout calculations, custom trim fabrication, and material ordering processes that no mainstream roofing software handles. Most metal roofers estimate panel layouts on graph paper or in Excel, and material waste calculations are done by experienced estimators from memory.
**Target Buyer:** Metal roofing contractor owner or estimator specializing in standing seam, corrugated, or stone-coated metal systems
**Automation Potential:** High — Panel layout optimization is a geometric problem well-suited to algorithmic solutions, material waste is calculable, and lead time tracking is a structured data problem

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Metal roofing is fundamentally different from shingle roofing in estimation, installation, and material management. A shingle roof is estimated in "squares" (100 SF units) with standard waste factors (10-15%). A metal roof is estimated in individual panels — each panel is custom-cut to length, panels must be laid out to minimize waste from off-cuts, and the order of panel installation on complex roof geometries affects total material needed. Standing seam metal roofing panels come in 12"-18" widths and are factory-cut to lengths up to 40+ feet. The estimator must calculate: how many panels per roof facet, what length each panel needs to be, how to orient panels to minimize waste at valleys and hips, and how much custom trim (ridge cap, drip edge, valley flashing, gable trim, sidewall flashing) needs to be fabricated. An experienced metal roof estimator looks at a roof and mentally maps the panel layout — they know that rotating the panel direction 90 degrees on a complex hip roof saves 8% in material waste. Junior estimators order 20-30% more material than necessary because they can't optimize layout mentally. Material costs for metal roofing are 2-3x higher per square foot than shingles ($4-12/SF for metal panels vs. $1-3/SF for shingles), making waste far more expensive. Lead times for specialty metals (copper, zinc, Cor-Ten steel) run 6-12 weeks, and ordering the wrong quantity means either project delays or expensive waste.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EagleView and RoofSnap provide roof measurements but assume shingle installations — they report squares and waste factors, not panel counts and cut lengths. SmartBuild and AppliCad offer metal roof estimation but are desktop-only, expensive ($3,000-10,000+), and require significant training. No mainstream roofing CRM (AccuLynx, JobNimbus) has metal-specific estimation features. Material ordering for metal roofing requires direct coordination with manufacturers (ATAS, Drexel Metals, McElroy Metal, Sheffield Metals) on panel profiles, colors, gauges, and trim specifications — a process that happens via phone calls and PDF order forms, not digital integration.
