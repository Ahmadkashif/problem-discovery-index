# Roof Warranty & Maintenance Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/warranty-maintenance-tracking/profile|Warranty & Maintenance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A unified platform that tracks every warranty the contractor has registered — across GAF, CertainTeed, Owens Corning, Carlisle, Firestone, and every other manufacturer — with compliance monitoring, maintenance scheduling, and proactive alerts that prevent warranty lapses worth $50K-500K per occurrence.

## The Problem
A roofing contractor who has been in business for 15 years and installs 150 roofs per year has 1,500-2,000 active warranties across 3-5 manufacturers, each with different terms, different maintenance requirements, and different compliance obligations. The warranty portfolio is an asset — it represents the contractor's commitment to quality and their manufacturer certification status — but no contractor can effectively manage 2,000 warranties across 5 manufacturer portals. The failure modes are predictable: warranty registrations expire because they weren't submitted within the 30-60 day window (10-15% of installations), maintenance-required warranties lapse because annual inspections weren't scheduled (25-40% of commercial warranties), and warranty claims are denied because the contractor can't produce the installation documentation the manufacturer requires (original photos, material receipts, certification records). Each of these failures creates liability for the contractor, damages the manufacturer relationship, and erodes the customer's trust. The commercial side is particularly valuable: a 20-year NDL commercial warranty is worth $200K-500K in coverage, and the maintenance contract that keeps it in force generates $2,000-5,000 per year in recurring revenue — but only if the contractor tracks and fulfills the maintenance obligation.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Manufacturer warranty programs are proprietary systems with no standardized API or data format. GAF's warranty portal looks nothing like Carlisle's 360 platform, which looks nothing like CertainTeed's registration system. Building a cross-manufacturer platform requires either manufacturer cooperation (unlikely — warranty programs are competitive differentiators) or reverse-engineering each portal's requirements and building integration layers one manufacturer at a time. The business model challenge: the contractor is the user, but the value proposition spans the contractor (certification compliance), the building owner (warranty protection), and the manufacturer (registration completion rates). Pricing is unclear — is this a per-warranty fee, a monthly subscription, or a percentage of maintenance contract revenue? The market is fragmented: a platform that covers GAF and CertainTeed but not Owens Corning and IKO only serves contractors who exclusively use those two manufacturers.

## What to Build
A warranty lifecycle management platform with four layers. First, registration management: when a roofing job is completed (detected via integration with AccuLynx/JobNimbus or manual entry), the system generates a manufacturer-specific registration package — pre-populating the registration form with project data, validating that all required documentation is present (installation photos, material invoices, certification records), and submitting through the manufacturer's portal or generating a ready-to-submit package. Second, compliance monitoring: the system maintains a warranty compliance calendar across the full portfolio — tracking which warranties require annual inspections, which have upcoming registration deadlines, and which have lapsed. Alerts escalate from 60-day advance notice to urgent notifications. Third, maintenance contract management: for warranties that require periodic maintenance (commercial), the system schedules inspections, generates pre-populated inspection report templates, tracks completion, and submits reports to the manufacturer. Fourth, warranty analytics: dashboard showing registration completion rates (required for manufacturer certification), portfolio value under warranty, and revenue from maintenance contracts — with forecasting for upcoming maintenance revenue and warranty expirations that create re-roofing sales opportunities.

## Target Customer
Roofing contractors with 5+ years in business managing 200+ active warranties across 2-4 manufacturers. The sweet spot is contractors with both residential and commercial portfolios — they have the highest warranty management complexity and the most to lose from compliance failures.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates warranty registration failures on 10-15% of installations, preventing $50K-200K liability exposure per occurrence. Generates $100K-500K in annual maintenance contract revenue from the 25-40% of commercial warranties where maintenance requirements are currently untracked. Protects manufacturer certification status (GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster) by maintaining registration completion rates above the 90% threshold required for program participation.
