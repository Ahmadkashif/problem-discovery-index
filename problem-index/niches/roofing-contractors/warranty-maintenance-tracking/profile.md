# Warranty & Maintenance Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B (embedded across roofing segments)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded function — warranty registration, compliance tracking, and maintenance contract management occur across residential and commercial roofing but represent a distinct operational challenge with dedicated staff time and revenue implications.
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Warranty registration happens through manufacturer-specific web portals, maintenance schedules live in spreadsheets or calendar reminders, and compliance tracking is a manual process that fails silently until a warranty claim is denied for non-compliance. No platform unifies warranty management across manufacturers.
**Target Buyer:** Roofing company operations manager, office administrator, or warranty coordinator responsible for manufacturer warranty registration and maintenance contract fulfillment
**Automation Potential:** Very High — Warranty requirements are structured and manufacturer-specific, registration is a form-filling process, maintenance scheduling is calendar-driven, and compliance monitoring follows deterministic rules that can be fully automated

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Roofing warranty management spans the gap between installation and the 15-50 year warranty period. Major shingle manufacturers (GAF, CertainTeed, Owens Corning, IKO, Atlas) and commercial membrane manufacturers (Carlisle, Firestone, Johns Manville, Sika Sarnafil) each operate independent warranty programs with different tiers, different requirements, and different registration processes. A GAF Golden Pledge warranty (the highest tier) requires the contractor to be GAF Master Elite certified, use GAF accessories on the entire roof system, and complete online registration with installation photos within 30 days. A CertainTeed 4-Star SureStart warranty requires CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster certification, CertainTeed products throughout, and registration within 60 days. The contractor installing 100+ roofs per year across 2-3 manufacturer programs manages 100+ warranty registrations annually, each with manufacturer-specific documentation requirements and deadlines. The maintenance side is equally fragmented: commercial warranties often require annual inspections by a certified contractor, and missing an inspection can void a $500K warranty. No system tracks these obligations across manufacturers and across the contractor's full portfolio of warranted roofs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Manufacturer portals (GAF Warranty Portal, Carlisle 360) handle registration for their own products but don't aggregate across manufacturers. AccuLynx tracks job completion but doesn't trigger or track warranty registration workflows. No roofing CRM connects the installation record to the manufacturer's warranty requirements to the post-installation compliance obligations. Maintenance contract management is typically handled through generic scheduling tools (Google Calendar, Outlook) or spreadsheets that don't connect to the warranty requirements driving the maintenance schedule.
