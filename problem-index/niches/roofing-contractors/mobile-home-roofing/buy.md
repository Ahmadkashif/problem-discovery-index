# Mobile Home Adaptation for Roofing CRMs

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/mobile-home-roofing/profile|Mobile Home Roofing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Every roofing CRM assumes site-built residential construction — mobile home roofers need estimation templates, code references, and insurance workflows built for HUD-code structures with different materials, different structural limits, and different carrier processes than conventional homes.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
AccuLynx and JobNimbus estimation templates list materials and pricing for site-built residential roofing: architectural shingles by the square, ice-and-water shield, synthetic underlayment, standard flashing. Mobile home roofing uses different materials (TPO membrane, rubber coatings, metal roof-over panels, specialty mobile home shingles rated for lower-pitch applications), different quantities (mobile home roofs are typically 900-1,800 SF — smaller than most site-built homes), and different labor calculations (mobile home work often takes longer per square foot due to structural challenges, limited attic access, and the need to work on structures that flex underfoot). The CRM's insurance claim workflow assumes a standard homeowner's policy with a standard claims process — mobile home insurance through specialty carriers requires different documentation, different depreciation calculations, and different supplement processes.

## What Already Exists
AccuLynx and JobNimbus provide CRM, estimation, and project management for roofing companies. Both have customizable estimation templates, but the base templates and material databases assume site-built residential construction. Insurance claim tracking assumes standard carrier processes. Neither has HUD code references, manufactured home structural specifications, or specialty carrier workflow templates.

## The Customization Gap
Three adaptations are needed. First, mobile home estimation templates: pre-built templates for common mobile home roofing systems — rubber membrane coating, TPO membrane, metal roof-over, and shingle re-roof — with material quantities calculated for manufactured home dimensions and structural constraints. Each template should include structural prerequisite checks ("Verify rafter/truss capacity for this system weight") and HUD code compliance notes. Second, specialty carrier integration: workflow templates for Foremost, American Modern, and other manufactured housing insurance carriers with their specific documentation requirements, claim submission processes, and depreciation schedules — these differ significantly from State Farm/Allstate standard homeowner processes. Third, mobile home park workflow: when the customer is a mobile home park owner with 10-100 units, the CRM should support batch estimation, bulk scheduling, and park-level reporting rather than treating each unit as an independent residential project.

## Target Customer
Roofing contractors serving areas with significant manufactured housing, including mobile home parks, rural communities, and coastal regions. Companies doing 20-100+ mobile home roof jobs per year who currently create mobile home estimates manually because their CRM doesn't support the building type.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces estimation time for mobile home roofing from 45-60 minutes of manual calculation to 15-20 minutes with pre-built templates. Eliminates insurance claim documentation errors that cause 20-30% of mobile home roofing claims to be initially denied due to incorrect documentation format for specialty carriers, delaying payment by 30-60 days.
