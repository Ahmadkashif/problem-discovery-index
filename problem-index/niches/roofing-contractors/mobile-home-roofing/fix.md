# Insurance Claim Processes for Specialty Carriers

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/mobile-home-roofing/profile|Mobile Home Roofing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Mobile home insurance runs through specialty carriers like Foremost and American Modern with different claim processes, different depreciation schedules, and different coverage structures than standard homeowner policies — roofers trained on State Farm and Allstate claims lose 20-40% of mobile home insurance revenue to documentation errors and process misunderstandings.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #automation #compliance #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Standard homeowner insurance claims follow a relatively uniform process across major carriers: the adjuster inspects, writes an Xactimate estimate, and the contractor matches scope to the estimate or files supplements. Mobile home insurance operates differently in every dimension. Specialty carriers like Foremost (the largest manufactured home insurer) use actual cash value (ACV) calculations with different depreciation schedules than site-built policies — a 20-year-old mobile home roof may have 80% depreciation applied, leaving the homeowner responsible for the majority of replacement cost. Coverage limits are often lower ($50K-100K total dwelling coverage vs. $200K-500K for site-built homes). Some specialty policies cover "repair only" rather than "replacement" for roofing, requiring the contractor to demonstrate that repair is not feasible before replacement is approved. The supplement process is different — Foremost has a contractor portal with specific documentation requirements that differ from the email-based supplement process most storm damage roofers use. A roofer accustomed to standard carrier claims who takes on a mobile home insurance job without understanding these differences submits documentation incorrectly, triggers delays, and often leaves $2,000-5,000 of legitimate claim value unrecovered because they didn't navigate the specialty carrier's process correctly.

## Why It's Still Broken
Knowledge of specialty carrier processes is concentrated among the small number of contractors who specialize in mobile home work — and they treat it as competitive advantage rather than sharing it openly. No training materials, certification programs, or industry guides cover the differences between specialty carrier and standard carrier claim processes for roofing. AccuLynx and JobNimbus insurance workflows don't distinguish between carrier types — they provide the same claim tracking template whether the carrier is State Farm or Foremost. The specialty carriers themselves have limited contractor-facing documentation about their processes, and their claims departments are often harder to reach than major carrier claims departments. The depreciation calculation methodology differs by carrier and policy type, but contractors learn this only through experience — after losing revenue on their first several mobile home insurance jobs.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A specialty carrier knowledge base and workflow engine integrated into roofing CRMs. When a contractor enters a mobile home roofing lead and identifies the insurance carrier, the system loads the carrier-specific workflow: documentation requirements, claim submission process, depreciation calculation methodology, supplement filing procedures, and known approval patterns. An LLM-powered document generator produces carrier-specific documentation from the contractor's inspection findings — formatting the same damage assessment differently for Foremost vs. American Modern vs. a standard carrier. The system includes depreciation calculators that show the contractor and homeowner the expected out-of-pocket cost before work begins, preventing the common scenario where a homeowner signs a contract expecting insurance to cover the full cost and then discovers they owe $4,000-8,000 in depreciation. Pre-built supplement templates address the most commonly denied line items per carrier, with supporting documentation that historically achieves approval.

## Who Feels the Pain
Roofing contractors who take mobile home insurance jobs expecting a standard claim process and discover weeks later that the specialty carrier operates differently, delaying payment and reducing revenue. Mobile home owners who sign roofing contracts without understanding their policy's depreciation, leading to unexpected out-of-pocket costs and disputes with contractors. Office staff processing claims who must learn a different workflow for each specialty carrier through trial and error.

## Impact If Fixed
Recovers 20-40% of mobile home insurance claim revenue currently lost to documentation errors and process misunderstandings — worth $2,000-5,000 per job on the 25-35% of mobile home roofing jobs that go through specialty carriers. Reduces claim processing time from 60-90 days (typical for first-time specialty carrier claims) to 30-45 days by eliminating documentation rejections and resubmissions.
