# Mobile Home Roof Assessment & Repair Protocol Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/roofing-contractors/mobile-home-roofing/profile|Mobile Home Roofing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A structured assessment and repair protocol platform for mobile/manufactured home roofing that captures the specialized knowledge of experienced mobile home roofers — structural capacity evaluation, HUD code compliance, system-appropriate repair options, and specialty carrier documentation requirements — into a guided workflow that any roofing contractor can follow.
**Tags:** #tacit-knowledge-ml #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #worker-facing #compliance

## The Problem
Mobile home roof assessment requires specialized knowledge that most roofers don't have. An experienced mobile home roofer walks onto a manufactured home roof and immediately assesses: era of manufacture (pre-1976 HUD code vs. post-1976), structural system (truss type, rafter size, condition), current roofing system (original metal, asphalt shingles over metal, rubber membrane, prior roof-over), structural capacity for a new system (can it support shingles, or does it need a lightweight roof-over system?), and code-appropriate repair options. They know that a 1985 single-wide with a bowstring truss system can support a metal roof-over but not a dimensional shingle overlay, that a 2003 double-wide with a standard truss system can handle a tear-off and re-shingle, and that any pre-1976 unit needs structural evaluation before any roofing work. This assessment takes an experienced mobile home roofer 15-20 minutes. A general roofer unfamiliar with manufactured homes either declines the job (common — 60-70% of roofers won't touch mobile homes) or makes errors: installing materials too heavy for the structure, using site-built code standards instead of HUD requirements, or failing to document the work in the format required by specialty insurance carriers.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The mobile home roofing market is too small and too fragmented to attract platform investment from mainstream roofing software companies. The knowledge base is deeply tacit — experienced mobile home roofers developed their assessment skills over decades of working on thousands of units, and the variables (manufacturer, year, model, previous repairs, current condition) create more combinations than a simple decision tree can handle. HUD roofing standards are less well-documented and less frequently updated than IRC standards, and many local building departments don't understand the distinction between HUD and IRC requirements for manufactured homes. Training materials are scarce — no trade school or certification program specifically covers mobile home roofing systems. The insurance documentation requirements from specialty carriers like Foremost and American Modern are not publicly documented in formats that could be systematized.

## What to Build
A mobile-first assessment platform that guides contractors through a structured mobile home roof evaluation. The workflow starts with unit identification: year, manufacturer, model (from the HUD data plate inside the unit), single/double/triple-wide configuration. Based on unit identification, the system loads the known structural specifications for that manufacturer/year combination and presents a condition assessment checklist specific to that structure type. The contractor documents structural condition through guided photo capture (ceiling sag measurement, truss/rafter condition through attic access, existing roof system identification) and the system recommends appropriate repair/replacement options ranked by structural compatibility, cost, and longevity. A gradient-boosted decision model trained on historical job outcomes predicts which repair approach will succeed based on unit type, condition indicators, and climate zone. Output: a complete scope-of-work document with HUD code references, material specifications appropriate for the structure, and documentation formatted for the homeowner's specific insurance carrier.

## Target Customer
Roofing contractors in markets with significant manufactured housing stock (Southern and Midwestern states, rural areas, coastal regions with mobile home parks). The primary buyer is either a mobile home roofing specialist ($300K-2M revenue) who wants to systematize their knowledge for crew deployment, or a general roofer ($1-5M revenue) who currently declines mobile home work and wants a guided system to enter the market profitably.

## Impact If Built
Enables general roofing contractors to profitably enter the mobile home roofing market (currently declined by 60-70% of roofers), expanding their addressable market by $50K-200K annually in areas with significant manufactured housing. Reduces structural assessment errors that lead to warranty claims and liability — currently affecting 10-15% of mobile home roofing jobs performed by non-specialist contractors.
