# Mobile Home Roofing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/roofing-contractors|Roofing Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4-5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Mobile home roofing is an afterthought for the roofing industry. No mainstream roofing platform has mobile home-specific features, estimation tools, or code references. Contractors specializing in this segment use paper estimates and general knowledge passed between experienced installers.
**Target Buyer:** Contractor specializing in manufactured/mobile home roof repair and replacement, or general roofer serving mobile home parks
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Mobile home roof systems are different enough from site-built homes to require specialized assessment protocols, but standardized enough (manufactured to HUD code, limited roof styles) to be systematizable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobile and manufactured homes have fundamentally different roof systems than site-built homes. Pre-1976 mobile homes (built before HUD code) have flat or very-low-slope roofs with metal or rubber membrane over minimal structure — often with 2x2 rafters instead of 2x4 or larger. Post-1976 manufactured homes have low-slope gable or hip roofs built to HUD standards, not IRC (International Residential Code) like site-built homes. The structural capacity of a mobile home roof typically cannot support the weight of a second layer of shingles or heavy roofing materials — a re-roof often requires either a "roof-over" system (building a new structural frame over the existing roof) or complete tear-off to the structural frame. Insurance for mobile homes operates differently: many are insured through specialty carriers (Foremost, American Modern) rather than standard homeowners carriers, and claim processes, coverage limits, and depreciation schedules differ significantly. Mobile home parks add another layer — park owners may have approval requirements for roofing work, and contractors must navigate both homeowner and park management relationships. The workforce that specializes in mobile home roofing is small and aging — this knowledge is transferred through apprenticeship, not formal training.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No roofing estimation platform handles mobile home roof systems. AccuLynx, JobNimbus, and EagleView assume site-built residential construction with standard roof pitches, structural capacity, and material options. Mobile home roofing has different material options (TPO, rubber membrane, metal roof-over systems, specialty coatings), different structural assessment requirements (can the existing structure support the proposed system?), and different code requirements (HUD vs. IRC). Insurance claim processes for mobile homes require different documentation and follow different approval workflows than standard homeowner claims. Contractors serving this segment operate without any industry-specific digital tools.
