## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential storm-insurance roofing is a claims-driven business: a hailstorm or windstorm hits a neighborhood, homeowners file insurance claims, and roofing contractors compete for the right to perform the replacement under insurance coverage. The revenue model depends on three capabilities — getting to the homeowner first (door-knocking within 48 hours of a storm), accurately documenting damage to maximize the insurance payout, and navigating the supplement process when the initial adjuster estimate falls short of actual replacement cost. Experienced storm damage inspectors can walk a roof for 10 minutes and identify hail hits on shingles, wind-lifted tabs, damaged flashing, and compromised ridge caps — then translate those findings into Xactimate line items that maximize the approved scope. Junior inspectors miss 30-50% of legitimate damage, leaving thousands of dollars on the table per claim. The supplement process — requesting additional payment from the carrier after the initial estimate — accounts for 20-40% of total job revenue on storm claims, and approval rates vary dramatically by carrier, adjuster, and how the supplement is documented. This is a business where the difference between a $8,000 and a $18,000 job on the same roof comes down to inspection skill and documentation quality.

## Current Tools & Gaps
AccuLynx is the dominant CRM for insurance-restoration roofing, handling lead tracking, contract management, and basic Xactimate integration. EagleView provides aerial roof measurement reports (dimensions, pitch, facet count) from satellite and drone imagery but does not assess damage condition. Xactimate is the universal estimating language between contractors and insurance carriers. CompanyCam handles photo documentation. None of these tools classify damage type or severity from imagery, none generate supplement documentation, and none predict which carriers/adjusters are likely to approve or deny specific supplement line items. The gap between measurement (solved) and damage assessment (unsolved) is where the most value sits.
