# DOT Compliance Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/fleet-managers|Fleet Managers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — ELD mandates forced basic digital compliance, but the broader DOT compliance landscape (driver qualifications, vehicle inspections, drug testing, hours of service) is tracked in spreadsheets and filing cabinets.
**Target Buyer:** Fleet safety/compliance officer or owner-operator managing DOT-regulated vehicles
**Automation Potential:** High — DOT compliance is entirely rule-based with clear deadlines, required documents, and pass/fail criteria — the definition of automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
DOT compliance for commercial fleets involves tracking 15+ regulatory requirements per vehicle and per driver: driver qualification files (CDL, medical certificates, MVR checks), vehicle inspections (annual DOT inspection, pre/post-trip inspections), hours of service (ELD logs), drug and alcohol testing (pre-employment, random, post-accident), IFTA fuel tax reporting, IRP registration, and hazmat endorsements where applicable. A single compliance failure during a DOT audit can result in fines of $1,000-$16,000 per violation and potential shutdown orders. The compliance officer manages this with a combination of ELD data, paper files, spreadsheet trackers, and calendar reminders.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ELD platforms (Samsara, Motive, KeepTruckin) handle hours of service and basic driver scoring. DOT compliance-specific tools (J.J. Keller, Tenstreet) handle driver qualification files. IFTA reporting tools handle fuel tax. But no single platform covers all DOT compliance domains in a unified view. The compliance officer uses 3-5 different tools and still maintains spreadsheet trackers to ensure nothing falls through the cracks. A unified DOT compliance dashboard with automated alerts for every regulatory requirement doesn't exist.
