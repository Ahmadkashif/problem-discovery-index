# Aging Fleet Lifecycle Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/fleet-managers|Fleet Managers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $9B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 13%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Companies with aging fleets tend to be capital-constrained organizations that also underinvest in fleet technology.
**Target Buyer:** Fleet manager or business owner whose average vehicle age exceeds 8 years or 150,000 miles
**Automation Potential:** High — Replace-vs-repair decisions, residual value tracking, and lifecycle cost modeling are data-driven analyses currently done with gut feel and spreadsheets.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Post-COVID vehicle shortages and price inflation pushed average fleet age to historic highs — many fleets are operating vehicles 2-3 years beyond their planned replacement cycle. These aging fleets face a distinct management challenge: maintenance costs accelerate non-linearly after 150K miles, parts availability decreases for older models, safety technology gaps widen (no ADAS, no backup cameras), and residual values decline steeply. The fleet manager must make monthly replace-vs-repair decisions on individual vehicles without reliable data on projected maintenance costs, expected remaining life, or current market value.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Fleet management tools track historical maintenance costs but don't project future costs based on vehicle age/mileage curves. Vehicle valuation tools (KBB, NADA) provide wholesale values but don't integrate into fleet management for lifecycle decision-making. The replace-vs-repair decision — the single most important financial decision in fleet management — is made with a spreadsheet and gut feel, not with a data-driven model.
