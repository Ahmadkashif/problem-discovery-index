# HVAC/Plumbing Service Fleets

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/fleet-managers|Fleet Managers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 17%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Service dispatch uses field service tools (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro) but fleet management is typically a spreadsheet managed by the office manager, not a dedicated fleet system.
**Target Buyer:** Trades company owner or office manager responsible for 10-50 service vehicles
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Vehicle-to-technician assignment, tool/inventory tracking per vehicle, and fuel card reconciliation are automatable but currently manual.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
HVAC, plumbing, and electrical service companies operate fleets of 10-50 work trucks/vans that serve as mobile workshops — each vehicle carries $5,000-$20,000 in tools, parts, and equipment specific to the technician and their specialization. Fleet management for service companies is fundamentally different from delivery fleets: routes are unpredictable (emergency calls), vehicle assignment is tied to technician skill (the HVAC install van has different equipment than the diagnostic van), and vehicle downtime means lost revenue because the technician can't do their job without their truck. Yet these companies use consumer-grade fleet tracking or nothing at all.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ServiceTitan and Housecall Pro manage dispatch and scheduling but treat vehicles as interchangeable — they don't track what equipment is in which van, vehicle maintenance status, or fuel costs per vehicle. Samsara and Verizon Connect provide GPS tracking but are overkill for a 15-van HVAC company and don't integrate with field service dispatch. The trades company owner manages fleet as a side task using spreadsheets, missing the specialized tools that would optimize their most expensive asset category after labor.
