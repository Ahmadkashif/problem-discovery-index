# Mixed Fuel & EV Transition Fleets

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/fleet-managers|Fleet Managers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Fleet managers adopting EVs tend to be tech-forward, but the tools for managing a mixed ICE/EV fleet don't exist as a unified system.
**Target Buyer:** Fleet manager overseeing a fleet transitioning from internal combustion to electric vehicles (10-40% EV mix)
**Automation Potential:** High — EV charging scheduling, range-aware route assignment, and mixed-fleet TCO modeling are all quantifiable and automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Fleets in mid-transition (10-40% EV) face unique challenges that neither traditional fleet tools nor EV-specific tools address. ICE vehicles need oil changes, transmission services, and fuel management. EVs need charging infrastructure scheduling, range-aware route assignment, and battery health monitoring — completely different maintenance and operational paradigms. The fleet manager must optimize total cost of ownership across two fundamentally different vehicle types, decide which routes are EV-eligible (range + charging availability), and plan future EV purchases based on route analysis. No single tool manages both ICE and EV operations in a unified view.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Traditional fleet management (Fleetio, Samsara) handles ICE vehicles well and has added basic EV features (battery SOC display, charging session tracking). EV-specific tools (Driivz, ChargePoint Fleet Management) handle charging infrastructure but don't manage ICE vehicles. Fleet electrification planning tools (Geotab, Sawatch Group) help decide which vehicles to replace with EVs but don't manage the mixed fleet during transition. The transition period — which lasts 5-10 years for most fleets — falls between all three tool categories.
