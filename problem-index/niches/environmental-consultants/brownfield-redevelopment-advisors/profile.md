## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Brownfield redevelopment consulting sits at the intersection of environmental science, real estate finance, and regulatory law. Consultants help developers, municipalities, and nonprofits evaluate contaminated properties for redevelopment potential. The work combines standard environmental assessment (Phase I/II, remediation cost estimation) with brownfield-specific services: navigating state voluntary cleanup programs (VCPs), securing liability protections (no further action letters, covenants not to sue), stacking financial incentives (EPA Brownfields grants, state tax credits, TIF financing, New Markets Tax Credits, Opportunity Zone designations), and managing community engagement requirements. Each state's VCP has different enrollment criteria, fee structures, cleanup standards, and liability protections — there is no national consistency.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Environmental assessment tools are standard (EDR reports, lab analysis, remediation modeling). But the brownfield-specific advisory work — VCP comparison, incentive stacking, liability analysis — has zero purpose-built software. Consultants compile VCP requirements from 50 different state program websites, manually research available incentives, and produce custom financial feasibility analyses in Excel. The EPA Brownfields Program website provides grant information but not state VCP comparisons or incentive calculators.
