## Why These Niches

Environmental consulting fragments along service type (due diligence vs. remediation vs. compliance), regulatory driver (EPA vs. state DEQ vs. local), client type (real estate transactions vs. industrial operators vs. municipalities), and workflow function (field work vs. data management vs. report writing). These 8 niches cover the two dominant revenue generators (Phase I ESAs that drive 30-40% of small firm revenue and remediation consulting that drives long-term retainers), the two most digitally neglected segments (UST specialists who still fax forms to state regulators and brownfield advisors who navigate arcane voluntary cleanup programs with zero software support), two populations consistently underserved by existing tools and market attention (tribal/rural communities that lack basic environmental monitoring infrastructure and environmental justice communities now receiving unprecedented federal funding with no consulting capacity), and two workflow bottlenecks with the highest automation ROI (permit compliance tracking across client portfolios and lab data QA/QC review). Excluded: air quality consulting (specialized regulatory domain), hazardous waste management (distinct operational workflow), and ecological/wetland consulting (biology-driven rather than chemistry-driven).
