# Enterprise Compliance Platform Scaled for Mid-Size Consulting Firms

**Niche:** [[niches/environmental-consultants/permit-compliance-tracking/profile|Permit Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/environmental-consultants|Environmental Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Enterprise compliance management platforms (Intelex, Enablon) handle multi-facility environmental compliance, but they're designed for Fortune 500 EHS departments — mid-size consulting firms need the same capability at 10% of the cost and complexity.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
A 20-person environmental consulting firm managing compliance for 50 client facilities needs: permit condition tracking, compliance calendar management, DMR preparation and submission tracking, inspection scheduling, and client compliance status reporting. Enterprise platforms (Intelex at $50K+/year, Enablon/Wolters Kluwer at similar pricing, Sphera at $30K+/year) provide this functionality but are designed for a single corporation's EHS department, not a consulting firm managing multiple independent clients. The pricing, implementation timeline (6-12 months), and ongoing administration requirements are completely mismatched to a 20-person consulting firm.

## What Already Exists
Intelex, Enablon, Sphera, and Dakota Software serve the corporate EHS compliance market. These platforms handle permit tracking, compliance calendars, incident management, audit tracking, and regulatory reporting. They are mature, feature-rich, and expensive ($30,000-200,000/year depending on modules and users).

## The Customization Gap
Consulting firms need a multi-tenant version of enterprise compliance: (1) client-level access controls (the compliance specialist sees all their clients; each client sees only their own data), (2) a consultant dashboard showing compliance status across all clients (which clients have overdue tasks, upcoming deadlines, or recent exceedances), (3) client-facing compliance status reports generated from the same data the consultant uses, and (4) pricing per client-facility ($50-100/month per facility) rather than enterprise licensing. The underlying functionality (permit tracking, compliance calendars, DMR preparation) is identical to enterprise tools — the difference is the multi-client data model and the cost structure.

## Target Customer
Mid-size environmental consulting firms (10-30 people) managing compliance for 20-100 client facilities, currently using spreadsheets because enterprise platforms are too expensive and complex.

## Impact If Solved
Gives mid-size consulting firms enterprise-grade compliance management at 10-20% of enterprise pricing. Eliminates the spreadsheet-based compliance calendars that currently miss 3-5% of deadlines per year — each missed deadline potentially costing the client $25,000-100,000 in enforcement penalties.
