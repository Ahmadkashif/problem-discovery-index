# Remediation Support Consultants

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/environmental-consultants|Environmental Consultants]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-18B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — EQuIS and Geotracker are used for data management on large sites, but most mid-size firms rely on Excel for data analysis and Word for reporting
**Target Buyer:** Remediation project manager or practice leader at a 10-50 person environmental firm managing long-term cleanup projects
**Automation Potential:** Medium — remediation monitoring involves repetitive data collection, regulatory reporting, and trend analysis that follow established protocols

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Remediation consulting firms manage the investigation and cleanup of contaminated sites under federal (CERCLA, RCRA) and state regulatory programs. Unlike Phase I work (transactional, short-duration), remediation projects span 5-30 years with quarterly monitoring, annual reporting, and periodic regulatory submittals. A typical mid-size firm manages 30-80 active remediation sites simultaneously, each requiring: quarterly groundwater sampling, lab data analysis and comparison against cleanup criteria, regulatory compliance reporting, and periodic remedial strategy evaluation. The long duration and regulatory complexity create massive data management challenges — a single site with 20 monitoring wells sampled quarterly for 10 years generates 24,000+ analytical data points that must be tracked, trended, and reported.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EQuIS (EarthSoft) is the industry standard for environmental data management but costs $15,000-40,000/year and requires dedicated database administration staff — putting it out of reach for firms under 30 people. Most mid-size firms manage remediation data in Excel workbooks that grow unwieldy over multi-year projects. Regulatory reporting formats vary by state and program, requiring manual reformatting for each submittal. No affordable platform connects data management, trend analysis, regulatory reporting, and remedial strategy evaluation.
