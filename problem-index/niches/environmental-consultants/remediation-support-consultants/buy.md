**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
EQuIS (EarthSoft) provides comprehensive environmental data management: lab data import, data validation, regulatory comparisons, statistical analysis, GIS integration, and regulatory reporting. But it is designed for Superfund-scale remediation programs managed by firms with 100+ employees and dedicated data management teams. A 15-person firm managing 40 mid-size remediation sites can't justify $15,000-40,000/year for EQuIS, can't dedicate a staff member to database administration, and doesn't need GIS integration or the 80% of EQuIS features designed for mega-sites. They need data import, screening level comparison, trend analysis, and reporting — the core 20% of functionality.

## What Already Exists
EQuIS (EarthSoft) dominates the enterprise market. Locus Technologies offers a cloud-based alternative but targets the same large-site market. ESdat provides a lighter-weight option popular in Australia/Canada but with limited US regulatory framework support. For smaller firms, the "alternative" is Excel — which is how 70% of firms under 30 people manage remediation data today.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is packaging EQuIS-class data management capabilities in a form factor appropriate for mid-size firms: (1) no database administration — the system handles data model management, backup, and updates automatically, (2) simplified lab data import using AI-assisted column mapping rather than rigid EDD format specifications, (3) pre-loaded US regulatory screening levels (state-by-state, updated quarterly) instead of requiring the firm to build their own lookup tables, and (4) a reporting module with templates for the 10 most common state regulatory report formats rather than a configurable report builder that requires weeks to set up. The tool should feel like a spreadsheet with superpowers, not an enterprise database.

## Target Customer
Firm principals and project managers at 10-30 person environmental firms who recognize that Excel is inadequate for multi-site remediation data management but cannot justify EQuIS pricing or complexity.

## Impact If Solved
Gives mid-size firms enterprise-grade data management at 10-20% of the cost. Eliminates the spreadsheet errors (formula breaks, wrong screening level lookups, missed exceedances) that currently affect 5-10% of remediation data analyses and can trigger regulatory enforcement actions.
