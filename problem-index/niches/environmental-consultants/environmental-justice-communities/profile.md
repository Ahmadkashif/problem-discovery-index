# Environmental Justice Community Assessments

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/environmental-consultants|Environmental Consultants]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-3B (rapidly growing — IRA/BIL allocating $100B+ in environmental justice funding through 2030)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — EJ assessments combine environmental data, demographic analysis, and community engagement using fragmented tools and manual research
**Target Buyer:** EJ advocacy organization director, state DEQ environmental justice program officer, or community development consultant
**Automation Potential:** Medium — EJ screening uses publicly available datasets (EJScreen, CEJST) but combining these with site-specific environmental data and community health indicators is entirely manual

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Environmental justice (EJ) consulting is an emerging niche driven by Executive Order 14008 (Justice40) and unprecedented federal funding ($100B+ through IRA and BIL) directed to disadvantaged communities. EJ work combines environmental assessment with social science: identifying disproportionate pollution burdens on communities of color and low-income communities, evaluating cumulative environmental and health impacts, conducting community engagement, and directing federal investments to the most impacted areas. The consulting need is exploding — every federal agency must now screen for EJ impacts, and state agencies are creating EJ programs — but the consulting capacity doesn't exist. Environmental consulting firms are technical specialists, not community engagement experts, and the EJ tools (EPA EJScreen, CEJST) provide screening data but not the assessment frameworks or community engagement methodologies that agencies and developers need.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EPA's EJScreen and the White House CEJST (Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool) provide census-tract-level screening data for 12+ environmental and socioeconomic indicators. CalEnviroScreen provides California-specific cumulative impact scores. These tools identify *where* EJ concerns exist but don't guide *what to do about it* — they don't generate the community benefit agreements, mitigation plans, or stakeholder engagement strategies that projects in EJ communities require. No consulting toolkit connects EJ screening data, project-specific environmental assessments, and community engagement frameworks.
