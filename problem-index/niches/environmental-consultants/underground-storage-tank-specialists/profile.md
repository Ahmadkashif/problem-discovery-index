## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Underground storage tank (UST) management is a regulatory niche within environmental consulting driven by EPA's RCRA Subtitle I regulations and state equivalents. The US has approximately 550,000 active USTs at 190,000 facilities (gas stations, fleet fueling, heating oil, agricultural operations). Each tank requires: periodic tightness testing, continuous leak detection monitoring, financial assurance documentation, and operator training certification. State UST programs enforce these requirements with inspections that result in violations for 30-40% of facilities. UST consultants serve facility owners (often small gas station operators or heating oil companies) who lack the expertise to manage compliance themselves. The work is high-volume, low-complexity, and driven entirely by regulatory deadlines — making it an ideal automation target.

## Current Tools & Gaps
State UST databases (e.g., California CUPA, Florida DEP Storage Tank Program) track facility registration and inspection results but provide no compliance management tools for the facility owner or their consultant. Leak detection manufacturers (Veeder-Root, Franklin Fueling) provide monitoring hardware with proprietary software that doesn't integrate with compliance management workflows. No platform connects tank inventory, testing schedules, leak detection records, compliance status, and regulatory notifications into a single dashboard for the UST consultant managing 50-200 client facilities.
