## Why These Niches

Warehousing fragments along product type (ambient vs. cold vs. hazmat), client model (e-commerce DTC vs. B2B pallet-in/pallet-out vs. manufacturer overflow), operational function (receiving vs. picking vs. packing vs. shipping), and workforce characteristics. These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (e-commerce fulfillment and food/beverage cold storage), two digitally neglected segments (small manufacturer overflow with handshake-based operations and seasonal pop-up warehousing with no WMS), two underserved populations (subscription box kitting with unique operational needs and immigrant-majority workforces where training and communication are in English only), and two highest-ROI automation targets (receiving dock operations and client billing reconciliation). Excluded: hazmat/chemicals warehousing (distinct regulatory regime), pharmaceutical/GMP warehousing (separate industry), and large enterprise DCs (well-served by Manhattan Associates/Blue Yonder).
