# Photo-Based Receiving Damage Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/warehouse-3pl/receiving-put-away-automation/profile|Receiving & Put-Away Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/warehouse-3pl|Warehouse & 3PL]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** WMS platforms log receiving exceptions (damaged cartons, short counts) as text entries, but carrier damage claims require photographic evidence that the damage existed at receipt — and receivers don't have a systematic photo documentation workflow integrated with the receiving transaction.
**Tags:** #cnns #automation #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
When a receiver unloads a pallet with 3 crushed cartons, they enter a WMS exception ("3 cartons damaged, product X"). When the 3PL files a carrier damage claim 2 weeks later, the carrier demands photographic evidence: where is the photo showing the damage at delivery? The receiver took a photo on their personal phone but didn't link it to the receiving transaction. Now someone must search through the receiver's camera roll, find the photo, confirm the date matches the receipt date, and attach it to the claim. If the receiver has left the company or can't find the photo, the claim is denied — and the 3PL absorbs the $500-$5,000 product loss.

## What Already Exists
WMS platforms provide exception logging at receiving. Smartphone cameras are universally available. No WMS provides a receiving workflow that integrates photo capture with the exception transaction — linking the photo to the specific receipt, the specific carrier, and the specific product in a claim-ready format.

## The Customization Gap
A receiving damage documentation tool needs: (1) integration with the WMS receiving workflow (when the receiver enters a damage exception, the app prompts for a photo); (2) automatic linking of the photo to the receipt number, carrier, date, and damaged product; (3) a damage classification prompt (crushed, wet, punctured, open) attached to the photo; (4) claim-ready documentation export (organized by carrier, date, receipt, with photos and exception details).

## Target Customer
Receiving managers at 3PLs who process 5+ damage claims per month and struggle to assemble photographic evidence for carrier claims.

## Impact If Solved
Improving carrier claim success rate from 40% to 80% by having consistently documented photo evidence recovers $25K-$100K annually in damage costs that were previously absorbed. Photo capture adds 15 seconds per damaged item to the receiving workflow.
