# Food & Beverage Cold Storage

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/warehouse-3pl|Warehouse & 3PL]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — cold storage facilities use WMS for inventory tracking but temperature monitoring systems often operate independently, FSMA compliance documentation is paper-based, and lot traceability relies on manual processes
**Target Buyer:** Cold storage facility manager or food safety director
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — temperature monitoring, FSMA compliance documentation, lot traceability, and FIFO enforcement are all automatable with existing sensor and software technology

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cold storage warehousing operates under constraints that ambient warehousing doesn't face: continuous temperature monitoring across multiple zones (frozen -10F, refrigerated 34-38F, cool 45-55F), FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) compliance with traceability requirements, lot tracking with FIFO (First In First Out) expiration management, USDA/FDA inspection readiness, and allergen segregation. The workforce operates in physically demanding conditions — frozen warehouse workers face extreme cold, limited shift duration, and higher injury rates. Cold storage is capital-intensive (refrigeration infrastructure) and energy-intensive (40-60% of facility operating cost is energy), creating unique optimization opportunities around temperature zone utilization and energy management.

## Current Tools & Gaps
3PL Central and Deposco provide WMS with cold storage features. Emerson and Carrier provide temperature monitoring systems. These operate as separate systems — the WMS manages inventory locations, the temperature system monitors zones, and neither connects to FSMA compliance documentation or lot traceability in an integrated workflow. FSMA record-keeping (required for 2 years) is often paper-based or spreadsheet-managed.
