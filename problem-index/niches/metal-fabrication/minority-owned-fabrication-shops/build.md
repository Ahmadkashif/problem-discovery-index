# MBE/DBE Compliance and Government Contract Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/metal-fabrication/minority-owned-fabrication-shops/profile|Minority-Owned Fabrication Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform integrates DBE/MBE certification compliance, government contract bid preparation, prevailing wage administration, and DBE participation reporting — the administrative stack that minority-owned fabricators must manage to access the public infrastructure opportunities their certifications unlock.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
An MBE/DBE-certified fab shop wins a subcontract on a public highway bridge project. The administrative requirements immediately multiply: prevailing wage payroll (different wage rates for different trade classifications, different rates for different project locations, certified payroll reports due weekly to the prime contractor), DBE participation reporting (documenting the shop's work performed with its own forces to verify it meets the commercially useful function requirement), prompt payment compliance (documenting that the prime contractor paid within the required timeframe), and contract closeout documentation. These requirements are layered on top of normal business operations, and the shop owner — who is also the estimator, production manager, and lead fabricator — manages them personally because they can't afford an administrative hire.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The MBE/DBE market is fragmented across industries (construction, manufacturing, services, professional), and the specific administrative requirements vary by state, municipality, and contracting agency. Building a platform requires encoding the compliance rules for dozens of certification programs and contract administration requirements across jurisdictions. The revenue per customer is low ($100-$300/month for a small shop), making the market unattractive to enterprise software companies. Payroll vendors handle prevailing wage but not DBE compliance; government contracting platforms handle bidding but not contract administration.

## What to Build
An integrated platform for MBE/DBE-certified fabrication shops: certification management (tracking renewal deadlines, document requirements, and annual affidavits across all held certifications), bid opportunity matching (surfacing projects with MBE/DBE goals in the shop's capability area), bid preparation tools (templates for subcontractor bid packages with prevailing wage, insurance, and bonding documentation), contract administration (weekly certified payroll preparation, DBE participation reporting, prompt payment tracking), and closeout documentation assembly. The platform guides the owner through each administrative requirement with step-by-step workflows, reducing compliance from a 10-15 hour weekly burden to 3-4 hours.

## Target Customer
Owners of MBE/DBE-certified fabrication shops with $1-20M in annual revenue, where government contract administrative burden limits growth. Approximately 3,000-5,000 MBE/DBE-certified fabrication shops in the US.

## Impact If Built
Reduces government contract administrative burden by 60-70%, freeing the owner for revenue-generating work. Enables shops to pursue more government contract opportunities (many qualified shops limit their public work because of the administrative overhead). For a shop doing $3M in government work annually, reducing administrative overhead by 10 hours/week at $75/hour opportunity cost recovers $39,000 annually.
