# Minority-Owned Fabrication Shops

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3-4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — similar to other small shops, but additionally lacking access to the industry networks and mentorship that facilitate technology adoption
**Target Buyer:** Owner or business development manager at MBE/DBE-certified fabrication shops
**Automation Potential:** Medium — DBE/MBE certification compliance, prevailing wage documentation, and government contract bidding processes are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Minority-owned fabrication shops (certified as MBE — Minority Business Enterprise, or DBE — Disadvantaged Business Enterprise) access procurement opportunities through diversity set-aside programs on public infrastructure projects. These certifications unlock access to projects with MBE/DBE participation goals (typically 10-30% of contract value on public works), but the certification process, compliance documentation, and government contract bidding requirements create administrative burdens disproportionate to shop size. A 15-person MBE-certified fabrication shop may spend 15-20% of the owner's time on certification maintenance, prevailing wage reporting, DBE compliance documentation, and bid preparation — administrative tasks that the owner handles personally because they can't afford dedicated administrative staff.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Generic small business tools (QuickBooks, spreadsheets) for business management. Certification bodies (NMSDC, local UCP agencies) provide certification but not operational tools. Bid notification services (BidNet, DemandStar) list opportunities but don't help with bid preparation, prevailing wage calculation, or DBE reporting. No platform integrates the unique combination of certification compliance, government contract administration, and fabrication shop management that MBE/DBE shops require.
