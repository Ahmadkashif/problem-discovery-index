# Prevailing Wage Payroll for Small Fabricators

**Niche:** [[niches/metal-fabrication/minority-owned-fabrication-shops/profile|Minority-Owned Fabrication Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Payroll platforms like Gusto and ADP handle standard payroll, but they can't manage prevailing wage compliance — calculating different hourly rates for the same worker based on which government project they worked on, which trade classification applies, and the specific wage determination for that project's location and agency.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Workers on government-funded projects must be paid prevailing wages as determined by the Davis-Bacon Act (federal) or state prevailing wage laws. A welder working on a state highway project may have a different prevailing wage rate than the same welder working on a county building project — and a different rate than their normal shop rate for private work. A fabrication shop with 12 workers on 3 government projects simultaneously must calculate payroll using different wage rates per worker per project, produce certified payroll reports (WH-347 forms for federal, state-specific forms otherwise) weekly for each project, and maintain documentation that workers were correctly classified (welder vs. laborer vs. ironworker) per the wage determination.

## What Already Exists
Gusto, ADP, and Paychex handle standard payroll. LCPtracker and eMars provide certified payroll reporting for construction contractors. The construction-focused tools assume a general contractor workflow — workers assigned to job sites — that doesn't match a fabrication shop where workers may split time between shop work (private rate) and government project fabrication (prevailing wage rate) in the same day.

## The Customization Gap
A fabrication-shop prevailing wage payroll system needs: (1) split-rate time tracking — the same worker at different rates for different projects within the same pay period, with shop work at the normal rate and government project work at the prevailing rate; (2) wage determination lookup — automatically applying the correct prevailing wage rate from the wage determination document attached to each project; (3) certified payroll generation — WH-347 or state-specific forms auto-populated from time records; (4) trade classification management — tracking which workers are classified under which trade for prevailing wage purposes, ensuring the classification matches the work performed.

## Target Customer
Owners of small fabrication shops performing government-funded work, where prevailing wage payroll is managed manually in spreadsheets or outsourced to an accountant at $500-$1,500/month.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces prevailing wage payroll preparation from 3-5 hours/week to 30-60 minutes of review. Prevents prevailing wage violations that carry penalties of $1,000-$10,000 per violation plus contract debarment. Replaces outsourced payroll accounting ($6,000-$18,000/year) with automated software ($100-$200/month). For a shop with 15 workers on 3 government projects, the labor and compliance cost savings exceed $15,000 annually.
