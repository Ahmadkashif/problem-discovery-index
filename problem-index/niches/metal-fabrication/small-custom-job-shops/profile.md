# Small Custom Job Shops (<20 Employees)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $50-65B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15-18%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most operate on paper job tickets, whiteboard scheduling, and QuickBooks invoicing with no ERP or digital production tracking
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator or shop foreman at fabrication shops with 3-20 employees
**Automation Potential:** Medium — basic job tracking, quoting, and material management are automatable but the variety of custom work limits process standardization

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small custom fab shops represent the long tail of the metal fabrication industry — thousands of 3-20 person shops producing one-off and short-run fabrications for local industrial, commercial, and agricultural customers. Each job is unique: a set of handrails for a building renovation, a custom hopper for a grain handling system, a repair weld on heavy equipment, a prototype bracket for an OEM. The owner is typically the estimator, lead fabricator, and business manager simultaneously. These shops survive on local relationships, quick turnaround, and the owner's ability to estimate and produce almost anything from steel. They are the most digitally neglected segment of manufacturing because enterprise tools are priced and designed for operations 10-50x their size.

## Current Tools & Gaps
QuickBooks for invoicing and bookkeeping. Paper or whiteboard for scheduling. Phone calls and text messages for customer communication. Material ordering by phone from local steel suppliers. No per-job profitability tracking — the owner knows whether the shop made money last month from the bank balance, not from per-job analysis. No digital quoting — estimates are produced on paper napkins, scratch pads, or informal emails.
