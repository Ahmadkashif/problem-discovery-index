# Ornamental & Architectural Metal

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-20B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4-5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most use design software (AutoCAD, SketchUp) for visual design but paper-based production management and manual estimating
**Target Buyer:** Design-fabricator owner or project manager at ornamental metalwork shops
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — each project is highly custom with artistic judgment, but estimating, templating, and project management are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Ornamental and architectural metal fabricators produce decorative metalwork — staircases, railings, gates, screens, facades, light fixtures, and sculptural elements — where aesthetic judgment is as important as structural integrity. These shops sit at the intersection of art and fabrication: the owner is often a trained metalworker with design sensibility, producing custom pieces for architects, interior designers, and high-end residential clients. The workflow is design-intensive (client approval on visual design before fabrication begins), material-diverse (steel, stainless, aluminum, bronze, copper, and combinations), and finish-critical (patina, powder coat, hand-applied finishes where surface quality is the product differentiator). Generic fabrication tools designed for structural steel or industrial work don't accommodate the design-driven, finish-sensitive workflow.

## Current Tools & Gaps
AutoCAD, Rhino, and SketchUp for design visualization. Instagram and portfolio websites for client acquisition. QuickBooks for invoicing. No tool manages the ornamental metalwork workflow: design revision tracking with client approval, material specification per design element (different metals and finishes within a single project), shop drawing generation from the approved design, and finish specification management through production.
