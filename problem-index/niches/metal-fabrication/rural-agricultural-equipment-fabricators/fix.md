# Emergency Repair Triage During Harvest Season

**Niche:** [[niches/metal-fabrication/rural-agricultural-equipment-fabricators/profile|Rural Agricultural Equipment Fabricators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When 15 farmers call on the same Monday in October needing emergency equipment repairs, the shop owner spends 2-3 hours on the phone assessing each situation, mentally prioritizing, and making promises they may not keep — because they have no system to capture, triage, and communicate repair status across a surge of concurrent emergency requests.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Harvest-season equipment failures arrive by phone, text message, and in-person visits — simultaneously. The shop owner answers 15-25 calls in a day, each describing a different equipment failure. For each call, the owner mentally assesses: how critical is this equipment to the customer's harvest? Can the farmer work around it temporarily? What parts and materials will the repair require? How long will it take? Where in the queue should it go? The owner writes notes on paper scraps, makes verbal commitments ("I'll get to yours by Wednesday"), and then forgets half the details because they're also fabricating and welding between phone calls. When a farmer calls back asking for a status update, the owner has to reconstruct where the job stands from memory.

## Why It's Still Broken
The problem is too seasonal and too relationship-driven for generic ticket management. These aren't anonymous service tickets — they're calls from people the owner has known for 20 years, each with a unique situation and emotional urgency. The owner resists standardized intake because it feels impersonal. Paper-based notes and mental triage have worked well enough when the surge was 10-15 jobs; as customer concentration increases (fewer shops serving larger farming operations), the surge is growing beyond what mental tracking can manage.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A mobile repair request intake tool: when a farmer calls, the owner creates a repair entry in 60 seconds — customer name (from contact list), equipment description, problem description (voice-to-text), urgency assessment (dropdown: critical/harvest-stopping, important/degraded-capability, routine/can-wait), and estimated parts needed. The tool auto-sorts the queue by urgency, shows the owner's schedule capacity, and generates realistic completion estimates. Farmers receive an automated text with their queue position and estimated start date. Status updates are sent automatically when the owner moves a job to "in progress" and "complete."

## Who Feels the Pain
Shop owners who are overwhelmed during peak season and drop balls that damage decades-long customer relationships, and farmers who call repeatedly for status updates because they have no visibility into when their equipment will be repaired.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 2-3 hours of daily phone-based status management during peak season — time the owner redirects to actual repair work. Prevents the missed commitments and forgotten jobs that damage customer relationships. Provides the owner with a realistic capacity view that prevents over-promising. For a shop where 2 lost customers per year (due to poor communication during peak season) each represent $20,000 in annual revenue, preventing customer defection preserves $40,000+ annually.
