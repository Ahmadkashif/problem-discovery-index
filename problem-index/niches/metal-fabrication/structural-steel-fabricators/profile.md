# Structural Steel Fabricators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/metal-fabrication|Metal Fabrication]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $80-100B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25-30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Tekla/SDS2 for detailing is standard, but shop floor production tracking, weld documentation, and erection coordination remain manual-intensive
**Target Buyer:** Plant manager or estimating manager at AISC-certified structural steel fabricators
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — estimating from model takeoffs, weld procedure tracking, and erection sequence planning are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Structural steel fabricators produce building frames, bridge components, and industrial structures under AWS D1.1 (Structural Welding Code — Steel) and AISC certification requirements. The workflow is unique: a steel detailer creates a 3D model in Tekla or SDS2, generating shop drawings with piece marks, cut lists, and weld symbols. The shop receives these drawings and produces hundreds of unique pieces per project — each with specific cuts, holes, welds, and fit-up requirements. Estimating requires reading the 3D model and predicting fabrication labor — a skill that takes estimators years to calibrate. Erection (field assembly) coordination requires sequencing fabricated pieces for delivery in the order the ironworkers need them at the job site, not the order the shop produces them.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Tekla Structures and SDS2 dominate detailing. FabSuite and FabTrol provide fabrication management (material tracking, CNC file generation, shipping). No platform connects detailing model data to fabrication estimating — the estimator reads the model and manually estimates hours, a step that should be automated from the geometric data already in the model. Erection sequence planning is done by the project manager using experience and job-site knowledge, not algorithmically from the structural model.
