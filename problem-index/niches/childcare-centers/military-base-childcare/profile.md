## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Military Child Development Centers (CDCs) operate under a dual regulatory framework: they must meet both state licensing requirements and DoD Instruction 6060.02 standards, which impose additional requirements around caregiver background checks (National Agency Check with Inquiries), facility security clearances, and curriculum alignment with DoD-approved developmental benchmarks. The workforce is uniquely transient — military spouses constitute the majority of CDC staff, with PCS (Permanent Change of Station) cycles creating 40-60% annual turnover that dwarfs even the civilian childcare sector's high churn. CDCs also serve families with deployment-related behavioral needs in children (separation anxiety, attachment disruption) that generic childcare platforms do not address. Pricing is income-based using the Total Family Income fee scale, adding billing complexity absent in civilian centers.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MilitaryChildCare.com handles waitlist management and fee calculation at the DoD level, but individual CDCs rely on a patchwork of installation-specific systems, paper logs, and generic tools like Procare or brightwheel that have no awareness of DoD inspection criteria, TFI-based fee schedules, or deployment-sensitive developmental screening protocols. No existing platform integrates the dual-compliance documentation (state + DoD) into a single audit-ready view.
