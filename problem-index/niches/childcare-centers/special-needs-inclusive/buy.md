# Therapy Coordination Module for Childcare Settings

**Niche:** [[niches/childcare-centers/special-needs-inclusive/profile|Special Needs Inclusive Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/childcare-centers|Childcare Centers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Practice management tools like SimplePractice handle therapy scheduling and billing, but they don't coordinate with the childcare center's daily schedule, classroom activities, or teacher communication — therapists show up and pull children from activities without context.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem
A child with a speech delay receives 2 sessions per week from a speech therapist who visits the childcare center. The therapist arrives, pulls the child from whatever activity is in progress, provides 30 minutes of therapy in a separate room, writes a session note, and leaves. The classroom teacher has no visibility into what the therapist worked on, cannot reinforce strategies during classroom activities, and is frustrated by the disruption to the child's routine. The therapist has no visibility into the child's classroom behavior, cannot time sessions to align with optimal engagement windows, and receives no feedback on whether the child is generalizing skills in natural settings.

## What Already Exists
SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, and WebPT handle therapy scheduling, documentation, and billing from the therapist's perspective. Childcare management platforms handle the center's daily schedule and teacher communication. These systems do not communicate with each other.

## The Customization Gap
A therapy coordination layer that bridges therapist and childcare center systems: (1) allows the therapist to view the center's daily schedule and choose session times that minimize classroom disruption (during transitions, not during circle time); (2) shares the therapist's session goals and recommended carryover activities with the classroom teacher after each session; (3) enables the teacher to log observations relevant to therapy goals (the child spontaneously used a new word, the child struggled with a fine motor task) visible to the therapist before the next session; and (4) coordinates across multiple therapists (speech, OT, PT) serving the same child to prevent over-pulling and schedule conflicts.

## Target Customer
Directors of inclusive childcare programs hosting 3-10 therapy visits per week who currently coordinate therapist schedules via phone/email and receive therapy feedback inconsistently.

## Impact If Solved
Therapy-classroom coordination improves skill generalization rates by an estimated 30-40% (research shows carryover activities in natural settings are critical for early intervention effectiveness), reduces classroom disruption, and gives teachers the specific strategies they need to support children with disabilities throughout the day — not just during therapy sessions.
