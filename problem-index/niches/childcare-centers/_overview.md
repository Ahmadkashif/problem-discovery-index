## Why These Niches

Childcare centers fragment along age group served (infant/toddler vs. preschool), operator size (family home vs. single center vs. multi-site chain), population served (general vs. special needs vs. military vs. tribal), and operational function (scheduling vs. billing). These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (infant/toddler programs with the highest per-child cost and multi-site chains with the largest aggregate enrollment), two digitally neglected operator types (family childcare homes running on paper and special needs inclusive programs using generic tools not designed for IEP/IFSP integration), two underserved populations (military families subject to DoD-specific standards and tribal communities with culturally specific childcare needs), and two operational workflows with the highest automation ROI (ratio-compliant scheduling and subsidy billing). Excluded: nanny agencies (different business model), corporate-sponsored backup care (employer benefit territory), and summer camps (seasonal, different regulatory framework).
