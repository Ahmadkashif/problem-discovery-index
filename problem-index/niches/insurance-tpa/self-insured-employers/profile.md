# Self-Insured Employer Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-tpa|Insurance TPA]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 14%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Many self-insured employers rely on their TPA's legacy portal for reporting and have no independent visibility into claims patterns, stop-loss utilization, or plan performance.
**Target Buyer:** Risk Manager / CFO
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Analytics and reporting are automatable, but plan design decisions require human judgment about employee demographics and risk tolerance.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Self-insured employers bear the financial risk of their employee health claims and hire TPAs to administer them. The niche is distinct because the buyer (employer) has different needs than the TPA's operational focus. Employers need financial analytics — trend analysis, stop-loss attachment point optimization, plan design modeling, and benchmark comparisons — but TPAs are operationally oriented toward claims processing, not strategic consulting. This creates a gap where employers with $5M-$50M in annual claims spend make plan design decisions with surprisingly little data.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TPAs provide standard reports (paid claims by month, large claim listings, utilization summaries) through portals or Excel exports. Consulting firms like Milliman and Mercer provide actuarial analysis for large employers. The gap is the mid-market self-insured employer (200-2,000 employees) that can't afford actuarial consulting but needs more than basic TPA reporting to make informed plan design and stop-loss purchasing decisions.
