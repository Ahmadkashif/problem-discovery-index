**Automation Potential:** High — Regulatory filings follow strict formats with deterministic rules, making them ideal automation targets once the data extraction and validation steps are systematized.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
TPAs operate under a matrix of federal and state regulatory requirements — ERISA filings, ACA reporting (1095-C/1094-C), state TPA licensing, HIPAA compliance, CMS reporting for Medicare secondary payer, and state-specific mandated benefit reporting. Each requirement has specific data formats, deadlines, and penalties for non-compliance. A mid-market TPA may face 50-100 distinct regulatory filing obligations annually across its client base. The compliance burden is disproportionate — regulatory reporting consumes 15-20% of a TPA's administrative cost but generates no revenue.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ACA reporting vendors (ACAwise, Integrity Data) handle 1095-C generation. ERISA filing services handle 5500 preparation. State-specific compliance is managed through consulting relationships or manual tracking. No unified compliance platform covers the full spectrum of TPA regulatory obligations, tracks deadlines across jurisdictions, and automates data extraction from the claims system into required filing formats.
