# Rural Small-Group Plans

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-tpa|Insurance TPA]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most small-group self-insured plans in rural areas are administered via spreadsheets or minimal TPA systems, with members accessing benefits through paper EOBs and phone calls.
**Target Buyer:** Small Business Owner / Broker
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Standard claims processing applies, but the challenge is making TPA services economically viable at small scale.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small employers (25-200 employees) in rural areas increasingly self-insure to escape fully-insured premium increases of 8-15% annually. However, most TPAs won't serve groups this small because per-employee-per-month (PEPM) fees don't cover the fixed costs of plan setup, compliance, and ongoing administration. These employers are left with either expensive fully-insured options or bare-bones level-funded products from carriers. The result is a structurally underserved population that could benefit from self-insurance but lacks access to competent TPA services at an affordable price point.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Level-funded products from carriers (UHC, Cigna) serve as a proxy for self-insurance at the small-group level but offer no plan customization and limited transparency. A few tech-enabled TPAs (Gravie, Nava Benefits) target the small-group market but focus on urban employers. Rural employers face the additional challenge of narrow provider networks and limited access to care management services.
