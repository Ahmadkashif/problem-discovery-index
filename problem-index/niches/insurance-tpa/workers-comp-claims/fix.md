# Return-to-Work Coordination Fragmentation

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-tpa/workers-comp-claims/profile|Workers' Comp Claims TPA]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-tpa|Insurance TPA]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Return-to-work coordination splits across examiner, nurse case manager, employer HR, and treating physician with no shared visibility into modified duty availability or treatment progress.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Return-to-work (RTW) is the single highest-impact lever in workers' comp cost containment — every week of delayed RTW costs $1,500-$3,000 in indemnity alone. Yet the RTW process spans four separate parties (claims examiner, nurse case manager, employer, physician) who communicate through phone calls, faxes, and disconnected portals. The examiner doesn't know the employer has light-duty available. The physician doesn't know the employer's job demands. The nurse case manager tracks everything in a separate system.

## Why It's Still Broken
Each party uses a different system: the TPA uses a claims platform, the employer uses HR/payroll software, the physician uses an EHR, and the nurse case manager uses a utilization review tool. No one has built the connective tissue because it requires integration with systems on all four sides — and the economic incentive is diffused (the employer saves money, but the TPA manages the process, and the physician has no financial incentive to coordinate).

## What a Fix Looks Like
A shared RTW coordination dashboard accessible to all four parties via web portal. Employer posts available modified-duty positions with physical requirements. Physician updates functional capacity ratings after each visit. System auto-matches available positions to current restrictions and alerts all parties when a match exists. Examiner sees a single timeline of RTW milestones instead of reconstructing it from phone notes.

## Who Feels the Pain
Claims examiners spend 30-40% of their time on RTW coordination phone calls and follow-ups. Injured workers experience unnecessary delays in returning to productive work, extending their time on indemnity benefits.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average claim duration by 15-25%, saving $2,000-$5,000 per claim. For a TPA managing 10,000 lost-time claims, this represents $20M-$50M in client savings annually.
