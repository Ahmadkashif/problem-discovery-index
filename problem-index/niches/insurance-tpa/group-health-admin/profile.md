## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Large TPAs use platforms like HealthEdge and QNXT, but benefit plan configuration and eligibility management remain heavily manual for complex plan designs.
**Target Buyer:** Benefits Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Eligibility verification, claims adjudication, and EOB generation follow deterministic rules that are highly automatable once plan documents are structured.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Group health administration is the largest TPA segment by revenue, driven by self-insured employers who need a third party to adjudicate medical, dental, and pharmacy claims against their plan documents. The niche is distinct because it requires deep plan-document interpretation — every employer's Summary Plan Description (SPD) contains unique provisions for deductibles, copays, coinsurance, exclusions, and network tiers. TPAs must configure these rules into adjudication engines, a process that takes 40-80 hours per new client and is error-prone.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HealthEdge, QNXT (Cognizant), and Facets (Trizetto) handle claims adjudication for large TPAs. Mid-market TPAs use older systems like Diamond or custom-built solutions. The gap is in plan-document parsing — converting a 200-page SPD into adjudication rules still requires a benefits analyst to manually read and configure every provision, with errors discovered only when members dispute claim payments months later.
