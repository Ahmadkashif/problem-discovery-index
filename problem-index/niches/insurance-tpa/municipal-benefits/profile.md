## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Municipal and public entity benefits administration has unique requirements that commercial TPAs rarely handle well. Public entities must comply with union collective bargaining agreements (CBAs) that define benefit tiers by job classification, seniority, and bargaining unit. Retiree benefits represent a massive unfunded liability (GASB 75 reporting) and require tracking eligibility rules that span decades. Procurement rules require competitive bidding for TPA services, creating 3-5 year contract cycles with complex RFP processes. These constraints create a niche where generic commercial TPA platforms fall short.

## Current Tools & Gaps
A handful of specialized firms (Meritain Health, HealthSmart) serve public entities, often on legacy platforms. Many smaller municipalities use fully-insured plans because they can't find TPAs willing to handle their complexity at a competitive price. The gap is in CBA-compliant plan configuration — translating union agreements into adjudication rules, tracking multiple benefit tiers per entity, and generating compliance reports for public records requirements.
