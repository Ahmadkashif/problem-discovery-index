# Wedding & Event Shuttles

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5-2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Bookings happen through wedding planners, venue coordinators, and direct phone/email inquiries; most operators have no self-service booking or instant quoting capability
**Target Buyer:** Wedding planner / event coordinator / venue manager / direct bride/groom
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Trip parameters are highly variable (multiple stops, varying group sizes, tight timing around ceremonies), but the quoting and confirmation workflow is highly repeatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wedding and event shuttles are the second-largest charter revenue segment and operate under unique constraints: exact timing tied to ceremony schedules (a 15-minute late arrival can derail a wedding), multiple shuttles running between hotels, ceremony sites, and reception venues in coordinated windows, premium pricing expectations with corresponding service-level demands, and a sales cycle driven by emotional decision-making (couples prioritize reliability and aesthetics over cost). Unlike school or corporate charters, the customer is typically booking for the first and only time, has no procurement expertise, and relies heavily on referrals and reviews.

## Current Tools & Gaps
GOGO Charters and CharterBus.com provide lead-generation marketplaces where couples submit requests and receive operator quotes, but neither platform manages the multi-shuttle coordination, timing synchronization, or day-of communication that wedding charters require. Operators use generic CRM tools or paper to track the complex back-and-forth of wedding planning timelines (venue changes, guest count updates, schedule revisions). No tool connects the event timeline to the dispatch schedule.
