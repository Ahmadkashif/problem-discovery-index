# Corporate Shuttle SLA Dashboard

**Niche:** [[niches/charter-bus-operators/corporate-shuttle-contracts/profile|Corporate Shuttle Contracts]]
**Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product gives charter operators a corporate-grade service level dashboard to prove SLA compliance, track ridership trends, and generate sustainability reports for enterprise clients.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact #ai-platform

## The Problem
Corporate clients demand monthly or quarterly service reports covering on-time performance, ridership utilization, vehicle condition, driver consistency, and carbon offset metrics — standard expectations in enterprise procurement. Charter operators with 5-20 buses have no system to generate these reports. They manually compile GPS logs, rider counts, and maintenance records into ad hoc spreadsheets that look unprofessional next to the polished dashboards offered by large transportation management companies. This reporting gap is the primary reason mid-size charter operators lose corporate contracts to larger competitors despite offering 15-30% lower pricing.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The corporate shuttle niche is a small slice of the overall charter market, and fleet management software vendors (BusHive, Samsara) focus on operational tools for the operator, not client-facing analytics. Building an SLA dashboard requires integrating GPS data (for on-time tracking), rider data (manual counts or automated passenger counters), maintenance records (for vehicle condition reporting), and emissions calculations (for sustainability metrics) — data that lives across 3-4 systems that do not talk to each other.

## What to Build
A white-labeled SLA reporting platform that connects to the operator's GPS/ELD system and generates automated weekly and monthly reports: on-time arrival percentage, ridership by route and day, vehicle age/condition scores, driver assignment consistency, and CO2-per-rider-mile calculations. The corporate client gets a branded login with real-time dashboards. The operator gets a tool that makes their 10-bus fleet look as professionally managed as a 200-bus operation.

## Target Customer
Charter operators pursuing or retaining corporate shuttle contracts worth $100K+/year who cannot match the reporting capabilities of large transportation companies.

## Impact If Built
Enables mid-size operators to compete for and retain corporate contracts by closing the reporting gap, potentially increasing corporate revenue by 30-50% per operator. A single retained corporate contract is worth $100K-$500K/year in recurring revenue.
