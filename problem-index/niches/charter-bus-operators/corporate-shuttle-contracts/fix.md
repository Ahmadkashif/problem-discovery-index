# Contract Renewal Data Gap

**Niche:** [[niches/charter-bus-operators/corporate-shuttle-contracts/profile|Corporate Shuttle Contracts]]
**Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When corporate shuttle contracts come up for renewal, operators cannot demonstrate their performance history because service data was never systematically captured.
**Tags:** #data-integration #revenue-impact #automation

## The Problem
Corporate shuttle contracts renew annually, and the renewal decision hinges on documented service performance — on-time rates, ridership trends, incident history, and cost-per-rider metrics. Charter operators who have delivered excellent service for 3 years lose contracts at renewal because they cannot prove it. Their "data" is a folder of invoices and a dispatcher's verbal assurance that service was reliable. The competing bid from a large transportation company arrives with a polished proposal citing industry benchmarks and promising real-time dashboards. The incumbent operator, who knows the routes and drivers intimately, loses on presentation rather than performance.

## Why It's Still Broken
Corporate shuttle service generates data continuously — every trip has a departure time, arrival time, rider count, and route — but charter operators lack the infrastructure to capture, store, and retrieve this data in aggregate. ELD/GPS systems record vehicle movements but do not tag trips by contract or client. Driver trip sheets are filed in paper folders and never digitized. By the time contract renewal arrives, reconstructing 12 months of service history requires weeks of manual work that the operator does not have time for during a competitive rebid process.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated service history system that tags every trip to its corporate contract, captures on-time performance from GPS data, logs rider counts, and accumulates this into a queryable database. At renewal time, the operator generates a one-click performance report showing 12 months of SLA compliance, ridership trends, and cost efficiency — presented in a format that matches corporate procurement expectations.

## Who Feels the Pain
Charter operator owners and sales managers who invest months in corporate relationship building only to lose contracts at renewal due to documentation gaps, and dispatchers who are asked to reconstruct service history from memory and paper records.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases corporate contract renewal rates from an estimated 50-60% to 80-90% by providing the performance evidence that procurement teams require. Each retained contract preserves $100K-$500K in annual revenue that would otherwise be lost to rebidding.
