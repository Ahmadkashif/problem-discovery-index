# Corporate Shuttle Contracts

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12-18%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Large corporations use travel management platforms (Concur, Navan) but charter shuttle contracts are managed through procurement email chains and annual RFPs with no operational integration
**Target Buyer:** Corporate facilities manager / HR operations lead / office manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Recurring routes, fixed schedules, and predictable rider counts make corporate shuttles highly automatable for both scheduling and billing

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate shuttle contracts — employee campus shuttles, office-to-transit-hub routes, corporate event transportation — represent steady, recurring revenue for charter operators but are managed with surprisingly little technology. Contracts run 6-12 months with daily or weekly scheduled service, creating predictable utilization but also demanding consistent on-time performance with financial penalties for failures. The corporate buyer expects enterprise-grade reporting (ridership analytics, carbon offset calculations, service level agreement tracking) that charter operators are unequipped to provide, often losing contracts to larger transportation companies despite offering better pricing.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Corporate travel platforms (Concur, Navan, TripActions) manage air and car travel but do not handle group shuttle logistics. Employee commuter apps (Luum, RideAmigos) focus on transit subsidies, not charter operations. BusHive handles dispatch but cannot generate the SLA compliance reports, ridership dashboards, or sustainability metrics that corporate procurement requires. The gap is a corporate-facing operations layer that sits between the charter operator's fleet and the corporate client's expectations.
