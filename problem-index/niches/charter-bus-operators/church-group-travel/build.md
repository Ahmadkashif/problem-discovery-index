# Church Trip Coordinator Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/charter-bus-operators/church-group-travel/profile|Church & Faith Group Travel]]
**Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform connects church trip coordinators (usually volunteers) to charter operators with a structured trip request, member sign-up, and payment collection workflow.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Church trip coordinators are volunteers who organize 2-10 charter trips per year with no professional logistics training. Each trip requires them to: estimate group size (sign-ups trickle in over weeks), collect per-person payments (often $30-$80 cash or checks), communicate itinerary details to members, book the charter (via phone, often comparing 2-3 operators), and manage last-minute changes (no-shows, additions, dietary needs for stops). The coordinator does all of this using paper sign-up sheets, personal email, and phone calls. When the coordinator rotates out (common in volunteer roles), all institutional knowledge about preferred operators, route tips, and cost estimates is lost.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Church groups are a fragmented buyer — 350,000+ churches in the US, each booking a handful of charter trips per year. The per-transaction value is low ($1,500-$5,000 per trip), and the decision-maker is a volunteer who changes frequently. Building a two-sided platform requires reaching a diffuse, non-professional buyer base through channels (denominational networks, church management software integrations) that tech companies rarely access. The payment collection component adds PCI compliance complexity.

## What to Build
A lightweight web portal where church coordinators create a trip (destination, dates, estimated cost per person), share a sign-up link with church members (who register and pay online), and receive instant charter quotes from operators in their region. The platform manages the member roster, tracks payments, sends reminders, and provides the operator with a confirmed headcount and trip details. Trip templates persist year-to-year so the next coordinator inherits the previous year's setup.

## Target Customer
Charter operators seeking to capture and retain faith-based travel business (typically 15-30% of their annual bookings), and church administrators seeking to professionalize their volunteer-run trip coordination.

## Impact If Built
Reduces coordinator time per trip from 15-20 hours to 3-5 hours, increases charter operator conversion rates on church inquiries by 25-35% through faster quoting, and eliminates the knowledge-loss problem when volunteer coordinators rotate.
