# Multi-Day Tour Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2-1.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15-20%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Tour itinerary planning uses Word documents and spreadsheets; hotel blocking, attraction ticketing, and meal reservations are managed through separate phone calls and emails with no unified platform
**Target Buyer:** Tour company owner / operations manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Itinerary complexity is high, but recurring tour routes create reusable templates and vendor relationship databases

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multi-day charter tours (3-14 day trips to national parks, historical sites, seasonal destinations) combine transportation logistics with hospitality coordination — hotel blocks, restaurant reservations, attraction tickets, rest stop scheduling, and daily activity timing. The charter operator must function as a quasi-travel-agency, managing vendor relationships across multiple states and coordinating driver schedules that span days with mandatory HOS rest periods. This segment is underserved because single-day charter tools (dispatch, quoting) do not extend to multi-day itinerary management, and travel agency software does not handle fleet operations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Tour operators use Word documents for itineraries, Excel for budgets, and email/phone for vendor coordination. Travel agency platforms (Tourwriter, Rezdy) handle package pricing and customer booking but do not manage the charter fleet operations (driver HOS scheduling across multi-day trips, bus positioning for hotel overnights, fuel planning for multi-state routes). The gap is a tool that unifies itinerary planning, vendor coordination, fleet scheduling, and customer-facing trip management in one platform.
