# Grant-Funded Trip Reporting and Compliance

**Niche:** [[niches/charter-bus-operators/rural-community-transit/profile|Rural Community Transit Charters]]
**Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Rural transit reporting tools exist for fixed-route public transit but not for charter operators receiving FTA Section 5311 or state community transportation grants.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
Rural charter operators frequently receive government funding — FTA Section 5311 grants, state DOT community transportation grants, Area Agency on Aging contracts — that require detailed reporting: ridership by trip type, cost-per-trip breakdowns, ADA compliance documentation, vehicle maintenance logs, and annual NTD (National Transit Database) submissions. These reporting requirements are designed for fixed-route transit agencies with dedicated compliance staff. Rural charter operators (typically 1-3 person shops) spend 5-10 hours per month assembling reports manually from paper trip logs and fuel receipts, often missing deadlines that jeopardize future funding.

## What Already Exists
RouteMatch and Ecolane provide rural transit management software with NTD reporting, but these platforms are designed for public transit agencies operating fixed routes — the licensing costs ($20K-$50K/year) and feature complexity are prohibitive for a 2-bus charter operator. FleetComplete and Samsara track vehicle data but generate fleet management reports, not grant compliance reports. QuickBooks can categorize expenses by funding source but cannot generate the ridership and service metrics that grant reports require.

## The Customization Gap
Rural charter operators need a lightweight, affordable (<$200/month) reporting tool that captures trip data (ridership, miles, purpose code, ADA status), maps it to the specific grant's reporting template (FTA Section 5311, state DOT, AAA contract), and auto-generates submission-ready reports. The tool must handle multiple funding sources per trip (a trip may be 60% FTA-funded and 40% AAA-funded, requiring prorated reporting), and must work offline in areas with spotty connectivity.

## Target Customer
Rural charter operators receiving $50K-$500K/year in government grants who currently risk losing funding due to incomplete or late reporting.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces monthly reporting time from 8-10 hours to 1-2 hours, eliminates late submissions that jeopardize $50K-$500K in annual grant funding, and frees the operator to focus on service delivery rather than paperwork.
