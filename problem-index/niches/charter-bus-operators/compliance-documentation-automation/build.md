# Charter Operator Compliance Dashboard

**Niche:** [[niches/charter-bus-operators/compliance-documentation-automation/profile|DOT/FMCSA Compliance Documentation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No single dashboard tracks every DOT/FMCSA compliance requirement — driver qualifications, vehicle inspections, drug testing, operating authority filings — for charter bus operators in one place.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #workflow-orchestration #ai-platform

## The Problem
A charter operator with 12 buses and 18 drivers must maintain: 18 driver qualification files (each containing 8-12 documents with different expiration dates), 12 vehicle inspection files (daily pre-trips, annual DOT inspections, scheduled maintenance records), a drug/alcohol testing program (random selection pool, post-accident testing, return-to-duty records), operating authority documentation (MCS-150 biennial update, insurance filings, UCR registration), and safety management system records. These documents live in filing cabinets, email attachments, vendor portals, and a dispatcher's desk drawer. When a DOT auditor arrives unannounced (compliance reviews can happen without notice for passenger carriers), the operator scrambles for hours to assemble documentation that should be instantly accessible. One missing document can trigger a conditional or unsatisfactory safety rating, which can shut down operations entirely.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The charter bus market has fewer than 4,000 operators in the US, compared to 500,000+ trucking carriers. DOT compliance software vendors (J.J. Keller, Fleetio) target the larger trucking market where scale justifies development costs. Charter-specific compliance requirements (passenger carrier operating authority, bus-specific inspection items, enhanced background check requirements for drivers of vehicles carrying passengers) differ enough from trucking compliance that a freight-oriented tool leaves critical gaps.

## What to Build
A compliance management platform designed specifically for charter bus operators that tracks every required document across drivers, vehicles, and operating authority. The dashboard shows red/yellow/green status for every compliance element, sends automated alerts 30/60/90 days before expirations, generates audit-ready files organized by FMCSA review categories, and integrates with drug testing vendors, CDL verification services, and inspection shops for automated record ingestion. The system maps to the exact structure of an FMCSA compliance review so operators can self-audit before the real thing.

## Target Customer
Charter bus operators with 5-30 buses who currently spend 5-15 hours per week on compliance documentation management and live in fear of unannounced DOT audits.

## Impact If Built
Reduces compliance management time by 70-80% (from 10+ hours/week to 2-3 hours/week), eliminates audit surprises by maintaining continuous audit readiness, and prevents out-of-service orders that cost $5,000-$50,000 in lost revenue per incident. For an industry where a single unsatisfactory safety rating can destroy a business, this is existential risk mitigation.
