# School & Athletics Charters

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/charter-bus-operators|Charter Bus Operators]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.5-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~30-35%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — School districts use basic procurement portals but charter operators respond via email/phone; no integrated booking-to-dispatch platform exists for education charters
**Target Buyer:** School district transportation coordinator / athletic director
**Automation Potential:** High — Recurring routes, seasonal schedules, and standardized group sizes create predictable patterns ideal for automated quoting and dispatch

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
School and athletics charters represent the single largest charter bus revenue category, driven by field trips, band competitions, debate tournaments, and athletic away games. These trips have unique constraints: strict student-to-chaperone ratios, DOT requirements for vehicles carrying minors (pre-trip inspection documentation, driver background checks beyond standard CDL), narrow pickup/return windows dictated by school bell schedules, and seasonal clustering (March-June, September-November). Many districts rebid charter contracts annually, creating a high-volume, competitive quoting environment where operators who respond fastest with compliant bids win disproportionately.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Districts use procurement systems (BuyBoard, EDGAR) to post RFPs, but operators respond manually. BusHive handles fleet dispatch but has no school-district-facing portal for trip requests, approval workflows, or student manifest integration. The gap is an end-to-end platform connecting district trip requests to operator quoting, booking, compliance verification, and post-trip reporting.
