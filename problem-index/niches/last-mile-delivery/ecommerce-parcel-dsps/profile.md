## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Delivery Service Partners (DSPs) are independent businesses that contract with Amazon, FedEx Ground, or UPS SurePost to deliver parcels in designated service areas. They own or lease vehicles, hire drivers, and manage daily delivery operations. The DSP business model is uniquely constrained: the contracting platform sets the delivery standards, per-stop compensation rates, and route assignments, while the DSP controls labor, vehicles, and execution. A DSP with 40 vans delivering 8,000 packages/day operates on razor-thin margins (5-10% net) — the difference between profit and loss is driver productivity, vehicle uptime, and failed delivery rate. DSPs have limited technology choice: the platform dictates the dispatch app (Amazon's Rabbit, FedEx's GroundCloud), but DSPs need their own tools for fleet management, driver performance, and financial operations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Amazon provides the Rabbit app for driver dispatch. FedEx provides GroundCloud. Route4Me and Circuit offer route optimization. Samsara provides fleet telematics. The gap is in a unified DSP operations platform that connects route performance, driver productivity, vehicle maintenance, and financial metrics (per-stop profitability) in one view tailored to the DSP business model.
