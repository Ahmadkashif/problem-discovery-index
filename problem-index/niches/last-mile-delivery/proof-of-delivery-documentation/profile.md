## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3% (embedded operational cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — POD photos are captured digitally but quality assessment, dispute resolution, and claims management remain largely manual processes
**Target Buyer:** Claims manager or delivery quality coordinator at DSPs and regional carriers
**Automation Potential:** High — POD photo quality assessment, claim evidence assembly, and dispute resolution workflows follow clear patterns that can be automated

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
"Delivered but not received" (DBNR) claims cost the delivery industry $2-3B annually. When a customer claims they didn't receive a package, the carrier's defense is the proof-of-delivery photo: a picture of the package at the delivery location. But POD photos are often insufficient evidence — the photo shows a package on a porch but the address isn't visible, the lighting is poor, or the photo angle doesn't prove it's the correct address. A claims analyst reviews each DBNR claim by pulling the POD photo, assessing its evidentiary quality, and deciding whether to accept the claim (refund the customer) or deny it (insufficient evidence to pay). This manual review takes 5-15 minutes per claim, and the quality of POD photos is the primary determinant of claim outcome.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Delivery platforms capture POD photos as part of the delivery workflow. No platform assesses photo quality at capture time (before the driver leaves the address), classifies photos by evidentiary value, or automates the claim resolution workflow based on photo evidence quality. Claims processing is manual: a coordinator reviews the photo, the delivery record, and the customer claim, then makes a judgment call.
