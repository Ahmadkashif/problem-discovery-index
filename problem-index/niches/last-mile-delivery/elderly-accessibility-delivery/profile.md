# Elderly & Accessibility-Focused Delivery

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/last-mile-delivery|Last-Mile Delivery]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — elderly recipients often can't use delivery tracking apps, buzzer/intercom systems for apartment access, or digital signature pads; accessibility needs (wheelchair users, vision impaired) aren't captured in any delivery system
**Target Buyer:** Senior care coordinator, pharmacy delivery manager, or Meals on Wheels-type program director
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — recipient preference capture and delivery instruction customization are automatable, but the human interaction component (helping elderly recipients bring packages inside) resists automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Elderly and mobility-impaired recipients have delivery needs that standard "leave at front door" parcel delivery doesn't meet: packages left on a porch may be inaccessible to a wheelchair user, a deaf recipient can't hear the doorbell, a vision-impaired recipient can't confirm the POD signature pad, and an elderly recipient living alone may need the driver to place the package inside the front door rather than on the step. These recipients generate disproportionate failed deliveries, redelivery requests, and customer service calls — not because the delivery was executed wrong by standard metrics, but because the standard delivery model doesn't accommodate their needs. Pharmacy deliveries (medications requiring signature or refrigeration) are the highest-volume delivery type to elderly recipients.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No delivery platform captures recipient accessibility profiles (mobility limitation, hearing impairment, vision impairment, requires inside placement) or adjusts delivery procedures accordingly. Amazon, FedEx, and UPS allow "delivery instructions" as free text, but drivers rarely read them. Meals on Wheels and pharmacy delivery services develop informal knowledge about each recipient's needs through repeated visits — knowledge that is never systematized.
