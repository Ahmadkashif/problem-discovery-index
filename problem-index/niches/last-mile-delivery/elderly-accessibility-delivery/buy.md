# Delivery Notification System for Non-Smartphone Users

**Niche:** [[niches/last-mile-delivery/elderly-accessibility-delivery/profile|Elderly & Accessibility-Focused Delivery]]
**Industry:** [[industries/last-mile-delivery|Last-Mile Delivery]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Amazon, FedEx, and UPS send delivery notifications via app push notifications and email — channels that 30-40% of elderly recipients (75+) don't use — leaving them unaware that a delivery is coming and unable to be present to receive it.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #quick-win #data-integration

## The Problem
Delivery notification systems assume smartphone ownership: "Your package will arrive between 2-4pm" arrives as an app notification or email. An estimated 35% of Americans 75+ don't own a smartphone, and 25% don't use email regularly. These recipients don't know a delivery is coming until the driver arrives — and if they're in the back of the house, they miss the doorbell and the driver leaves. Pre-delivery notification via phone call (automated or human) would allow the recipient to be near the door during the delivery window, dramatically reducing failed first attempts.

## What Already Exists
Amazon, FedEx, and UPS provide app-based and email delivery notifications. Pharmacy delivery services sometimes call ahead manually. No delivery platform offers automated voice call notification as an alternative to app/email for non-smartphone recipients.

## The Customization Gap
An elderly-friendly notification system needs: (1) automated voice call delivery notification ("Hello, this is [pharmacy name]. Your delivery will arrive between 2 and 4pm today. Please be near your front door."); (2) recipient preference for notification method (call vs. text vs. app) stored per address; (3) confirmation callback option ("Press 1 to confirm you'll be available, press 2 to reschedule"); (4) caregiver notification option (send the notification to a family member who can alert the recipient).

## Target Customer
Pharmacy delivery services and senior care delivery programs where 30%+ of recipients are 75+ without smartphones.

## Impact If Solved
Voice call notification improves first-attempt delivery success for elderly recipients from 75-80% to 90-95%. For a pharmacy with 200 weekly elderly deliveries, eliminating 20-30 failed first attempts saves $120-$300/week in redelivery costs — $6K-$15K annually.
