# Wound Care Supply Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — wound care supply operations rely on faxed physician orders, paper-based wound assessment records, and manual matching of wound characteristics to product formularies
**Target Buyer:** Clinical coordinator or wound care nurse at a DME supplier specializing in wound care
**Automation Potential:** Medium — wound-to-product matching and reorder scheduling are structured, but clinical assessment requires specialized knowledge

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wound care supply management sits at the intersection of clinical assessment and product logistics: a wound care nurse evaluates a patient's wound (type, size, depth, drainage level, infection status, surrounding skin condition), selects appropriate products from a formulary of hundreds of dressings, negative pressure wound therapy systems, and skin care products, and establishes a supply schedule that changes as the wound heals (or worsens). Insurance coverage for wound care supplies requires detailed wound documentation — measurements, photographs, and clinical justification for each product — updated at regular intervals. The supply mix changes with every wound assessment, making this unlike any other recurring supply category in DME.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Brightree handles billing. Wound care documentation exists in home health agency EHRs (Homecare Homebase, WellSky) but not in the DME supplier's system. Product formularies are maintained on paper or in spreadsheets. No tool provides: wound assessment data integrated with product selection (given these wound characteristics, which dressings are appropriate and covered?), dynamic supply scheduling that adjusts as wound status changes, or clinical documentation assembly for insurance compliance.
