# Recurring Supply Fulfillment

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Brightree handles recurring order management, some suppliers use third-party resupply outreach services, but the patient engagement and timing optimization layers are underdeveloped
**Target Buyer:** Operations manager or customer service lead at a DME supplier with a recurring supply book of business
**Automation Potential:** High — patient outreach timing, insurance eligibility verification, order assembly, and shipment scheduling are all rule-driven processes that follow predictable cadences

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Recurring medical supply fulfillment (CPAP supplies, diabetic testing supplies, ostomy supplies, wound care, incontinence products) is the most predictable and highest-margin revenue stream in DME — once a patient is set up, they need replacement supplies on a regular schedule governed by insurance eligibility windows. The operational challenge is maintaining patient engagement: patients must be reached, their current needs confirmed, insurance eligibility verified, and orders shipped — all on a per-patient cadence that varies by supply category and payer. With 2,000+ recurring patients, the outreach volume is enormous (hundreds of calls per week), and the conversion rate per call is low (15-25% of outreach results in a confirmed order), creating a customer service labor challenge that is the #1 operational bottleneck for recurring supply revenue.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Brightree provides recurring order management and basic scheduling. Third-party resupply services (S3, SnapWorx, Bonafide) offer outsourced patient outreach for a per-order fee. Some suppliers use IVR (Interactive Voice Response) systems for automated outreach. No tool provides: predictive outreach timing (contact each patient when they're most likely to order, not on a fixed schedule), multi-channel engagement (phone, text, email, patient portal) with channel preference learning, insurance eligibility pre-verification before outreach, and order customization based on patient usage patterns.
