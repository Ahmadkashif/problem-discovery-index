# CPAP & Sleep Therapy Suppliers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Brightree handles billing, ResMed AirView and Philips Care Orchestrator provide compliance data, but patient onboarding and resupply workflows are fragmented
**Target Buyer:** Operations manager or sleep therapy specialist at an independent CPAP supplier
**Automation Potential:** High — patient compliance monitoring, resupply timing, and insurance eligibility verification are data-rich, rule-driven processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
CPAP suppliers have a unique business model: the initial equipment sale generates moderate revenue, but the recurring supply business (masks, filters, tubing replaced every 3-6 months) generates 40-60% of total revenue over the patient's lifetime. The operational challenge is patient retention and compliance: Medicare requires CPAP compliance data (minimum 4 hours/night for 70% of nights) within the first 90 days, or the supplier must retrieve the equipment at their cost. After the compliance period, the challenge shifts to resupply retention — competing against online DME retailers (CPAP.com, Easy Breathe) who aggressively market resupply to patients. Managing the patient lifecycle (onboarding, compliance monitoring, resupply, troubleshooting) requires integrating data from the CPAP device (via manufacturer cloud platforms), insurance eligibility systems, and the supplier's own billing/CRM.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Brightree handles billing and order management. ResMed AirView and Philips Care Orchestrator provide device compliance data. Some suppliers use CRM tools for patient communication. No tool provides: integrated patient lifecycle management (from onboarding through compliance through resupply), automated compliance risk identification (which patients are trending toward non-compliance at day 30?), resupply timing optimization (predict when each patient will need replacement supplies based on actual usage patterns), or competitive retention tools (identifying patients being targeted by online competitors).
