# Mobility & Wheelchair Dealers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-14B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Brightree handles billing, but the clinical fitting, equipment configuration, and patient assessment processes are paper-based and expertise-dependent
**Target Buyer:** ATP (Assistive Technology Professional) / owner / clinical coordinator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — equipment configuration and fitting workflows are structured but require clinical judgment; billing and documentation are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobility equipment (wheelchairs, power chairs, scooters, seating systems) is the most clinically complex and highest-value segment in DME. A complex power wheelchair can cost $15,000-40,000 and requires a multi-visit assessment process: initial evaluation, measurements, equipment trial, configuration specification, insurance authorization, delivery, and post-delivery adjustments. The ATP (Assistive Technology Professional) who performs the assessment must match the patient's diagnosis, functional abilities, body measurements, home environment, and lifestyle needs to a specific equipment configuration from hundreds of options across dozens of manufacturers. This expertise takes 3-5 years to develop, and experienced ATPs are in chronic shortage.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Brightree handles billing and order management. Manufacturer configuration tools (Permobil MyPermobil, Sunrise Medical) handle product-specific configuration but don't integrate with each other or with the clinical assessment. No tool provides: clinical assessment templates that map patient needs to equipment categories, cross-manufacturer configuration comparison, photo-documented fitting records, or outcomes tracking (did the equipment work for this patient after 6 months?).
