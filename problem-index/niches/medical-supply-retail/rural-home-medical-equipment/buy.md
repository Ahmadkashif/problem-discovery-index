# Patient Home Readiness Assessment and Communication

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-supply-retail/rural-home-medical-equipment/profile|Rural Home Medical Equipment Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Customer communication tools exist, but none prepare rural DME patients for equipment delivery — ensuring someone is home, the room is cleared, and the access path is navigable — leading to wasted 2-3 hour round trips when deliveries fail.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A rural HME driver arrives at a patient's home after a 45-minute drive to deliver a hospital bed. The patient's daughter, who was supposed to be there to receive the equipment, is at work. Or the bedroom hasn't been cleared for the bed. Or the driveway is impassable due to recent rain. The driver returns to the warehouse and reschedules — wasting 2-3 hours and $75-100 in fuel and labor. Failed deliveries run 10-15% at rural HME providers because patients (often elderly, living alone) don't understand what they need to prepare, and the provider's pre-delivery communication is a single phone call the day before.

## What Already Exists
Patient communication tools (Luma Health, Klara, Relatient) handle appointment reminders and confirmations in healthcare settings. Text messaging platforms (Twilio, SimpleTexting) enable automated messaging. CRM systems provide basic communication scheduling.

## The Customization Gap
Healthcare communication tools send reminders ("Your appointment is tomorrow at 2pm") but don't provide delivery-specific preparation instructions that vary by equipment type. A hospital bed delivery requires: "Clear the bedroom of existing furniture (we need a 6x4 foot area), ensure the front door and hallway are wide enough for a 36-inch bed frame, have someone 18+ present for the full 45-minute setup, and make sure the driveway is accessible for a large van." A CPAP delivery requires none of this. The communication must be equipment-specific, include preparation photos or videos (show what "clear the room" means), and confirm readiness before the driver departs — with automatic rescheduling if the patient reports they're not ready.

## Target Customer
Rural HME providers who experience 10-15% failed delivery rates due to patient unpreparedness, each failure costing 2-3 hours of driver time in a territory where re-delivery adds a full day of delay.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces failed deliveries from 12% to 4% — on 40 deliveries/month, preventing 3 failed deliveries saves 6-9 hours of driver time and $225-300 in fuel. Eliminates patient frustration from rescheduled deliveries (each delay extends equipment wait time by 3-5 days in rural areas). Simple automated communication that pays for itself within the first month.
