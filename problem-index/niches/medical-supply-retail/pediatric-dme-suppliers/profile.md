## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — pediatric DME operations use the same adult-focused systems (Brightree) with no pediatric-specific workflows for growth-based equipment changes, school coordination, or parent communication
**Target Buyer:** Pediatric equipment specialist, clinical coordinator, or DME company owner specializing in pediatric populations
**Automation Potential:** Medium — growth-triggered equipment reviews, school year coordination, and insurance re-authorization on age milestones are predictable and schedulable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pediatric DME is fundamentally different from adult DME because children grow. A wheelchair fitted to a 5-year-old will need seat width, depth, and back height adjustments within 12-18 months. A child's AFOs (ankle-foot orthoses) may need replacement every 6-12 months. This growth-driven replacement cycle creates a continuous re-evaluation and re-authorization workload that adult DME doesn't have. Additionally, pediatric DME involves three-party coordination: the child's parents (decision-makers and day-to-day users), the school system (which may need equipment for classroom use under IDEA/504 requirements), and the clinical team (physicians, therapists, and the equipment supplier). No existing DME workflow tool handles this three-party coordination or growth-triggered equipment review scheduling.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Brightree handles billing but has no pediatric-specific features. Growth charts and equipment sizing are tracked in the child's medical record, not in the DME system. School equipment coordination happens via phone calls and emails between the supplier, school nurse, and special education coordinator. No tool provides: growth-milestone equipment review scheduling, school-year equipment coordination workflows, parent-facing equipment status and training resources, or pediatric-specific insurance authorization guidance (Medicaid EPSDT coverage rules differ significantly from adult Medicare DME rules).
