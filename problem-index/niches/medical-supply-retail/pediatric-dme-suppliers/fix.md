# School System Equipment Coordination

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-supply-retail/pediatric-dme-suppliers/profile|Pediatric DME Suppliers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Children who need DME at school require coordination between the DME supplier, the school nurse, the special education team, and the parents — a four-way communication process managed entirely through phone calls and emails with no shared system.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
A child who uses a wheelchair at home and at school needs: equipment delivery and setup at the school (different from home setup if the school has different access constraints), training for school staff (teachers, aides, bus drivers) on equipment operation and safety, maintenance coordination (who handles repairs — the school or the parent?), and documentation for the child's IEP (Individualized Education Program) specifying equipment accommodations. The DME supplier coordinates with the school nurse, the special education coordinator, the child's therapist, and the parents — via a chain of phone calls and emails that spans weeks. When something changes (child gets a new chair, school moves to a new building, aide changes), the coordination cycle restarts.

## Why It's Still Broken
Schools and DME suppliers operate in different systems with different incentive structures: the school system uses educational record-keeping (IEP tracking, IDEA compliance), while the DME supplier uses medical billing systems. There's no shared platform, no standard communication protocol, and no defined responsibility matrix for equipment at school. Each school district handles DME differently — some have explicit equipment policies, others improvise. The DME supplier's relationship is with the patient (and their insurance), not with the school, creating a coordination gap that no party is responsible for closing.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A school-DME coordination workflow tool that: maintains a shared equipment record accessible by the supplier, parent, and school nurse (with appropriate access controls), provides school staff training checklists and videos specific to the child's equipment, coordinates delivery and setup scheduling aligned with the school year calendar, tracks maintenance responsibilities and schedules, and generates equipment documentation for the IEP process. Communication happens through the platform rather than ad hoc phone calls, creating an audit trail and ensuring nothing falls through the cracks during transitions (new school year, new aide, equipment upgrade).

## Who Feels the Pain
Parents who spend hours coordinating between the supplier and the school, often taking time off work for school meetings about equipment. DME clinical staff who make 3-5 phone calls per school-based patient to coordinate setup and training. School nurses who receive equipment they weren't trained to manage.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces school equipment coordination time by 60-70% per student (from 3-5 hours of calls and emails to a 30-minute platform-based workflow). Prevents equipment misuse at school that leads to damage, warranty claims, and student safety incidents. Streamlines IEP documentation for equipment accommodations, reducing parent stress and school administrative burden.
