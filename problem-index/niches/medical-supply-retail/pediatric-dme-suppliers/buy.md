# Parent-Facing Equipment Portal and Training Hub

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-supply-retail/pediatric-dme-suppliers/profile|Pediatric DME Suppliers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-supply-retail|Medical Supply Retail]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Patient portals exist in healthcare, but none are designed for DME families — parents need equipment training videos, maintenance schedules, insurance coverage status, and a way to report issues without calling during business hours.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #worker-facing #ai-platform

## The Problem
Parents of children with complex DME (power wheelchairs, standing frames, communication devices) receive equipment training during delivery — a 30-60 minute session covering operation, charging, maintenance, and troubleshooting. Within a week, they've forgotten half of what they learned and have questions they don't want to call about ("Is this clicking sound normal?", "How do I adjust the headrest?", "When should I replace the battery?"). They call the supplier's customer service line during business hours, wait on hold, and ask questions that could be answered with a video or FAQ. Meanwhile, they have no visibility into their child's insurance coverage status, upcoming equipment reviews, or warranty information.

## What Already Exists
Healthcare patient portals (MyChart, FollowMyHealth) provide medical record access. Video hosting platforms (YouTube, Vimeo) can host training content. General CRM portals provide customer-facing status views. DME manufacturer websites provide product manuals.

## The Customization Gap
No existing portal is designed for the pediatric DME family's specific needs: equipment-specific training videos (not generic, but "how to adjust the tilt angle on your child's Permobil M3"), maintenance checklists with reminders ("clean joystick contacts monthly"), insurance coverage timeline ("your child's wheelchair was authorized for 5 years — next replacement eligibility: March 2028"), equipment status tracking ("your child's new AFOs are in prior authorization — expected approval in 7-10 days"), and an issue reporting form that captures enough detail to triage without a phone call. The portal must serve parents who are overwhelmed, sleep-deprived, and caring for a child with complex needs — extreme simplicity is essential.

## Target Customer
Pediatric DME suppliers who handle 10+ parent phone calls per day for questions that could be self-served through a well-designed portal.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces customer service call volume by 30-40% for routine questions (maintenance, training, status updates). Improves parent satisfaction and retention — families who feel informed and supported refer other families. Reduces equipment misuse and damage from inadequate training retention, cutting warranty claims by 10-15%.
