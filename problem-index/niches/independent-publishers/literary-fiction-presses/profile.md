## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 20%

**Target Buyer:** Publisher / Editorial Director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Manuscript evaluation, comp title analysis, and marketing copy generation are strong automation candidates.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Literary fiction presses (Graywolf, Tin House, Catapult, Algonquin, etc.) publish 10–40 titles per year with editorial teams of 3–15 people. Their primary competitive advantage is editorial taste — the ability to identify manuscripts that will resonate with readers and critics. But operational overhead consumes editorial bandwidth: tracking 2,000+ unsolicited submissions per year, managing 12–18 month production timelines per title, coordinating with freelance copyeditors/proofreaders/designers, and running marketing campaigns on $2,000–$10,000 budgets per title. The economics are brutal — average indie literary fiction title sells 3,000–5,000 copies — making operational efficiency existential, not optional.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Submittable handles manuscript submissions. InDesign handles layout. QuickBooks handles finances. But the connective tissue — tracking a manuscript from submission through acquisition, editing, production, marketing, and royalty accounting — is managed in spreadsheets, shared folders, and institutional memory. Publishing-specific platforms (Consonance, Bibliocloud) exist but are designed for larger publishers and priced accordingly. No tool helps the editorial team answer the most important question: "Given our list, audience, and budget, which of these 50 promising manuscripts should we acquire?"
