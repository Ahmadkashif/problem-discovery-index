# Grant Reporting and Revenue Tracking for Arts Funding

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/poetry-chapbook-publishers/profile|Poetry & Chapbook Publishers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Poetry presses depend on NEA, state arts council, and foundation grants for 30–60% of revenue — but track grant deliverables, expenses, and reporting deadlines in email and memory, risking disqualification from future funding.
**Tags:** #compliance #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
A poetry press receiving 3–5 grants per year from NEA, state arts councils, and private foundations must track: application deadlines, award notification dates, grant periods, allowable expenses, deliverable requirements (number of titles published, readings organized, copies distributed), interim reporting dates, final reporting dates, and financial reconciliation. Each funder has different requirements, reporting templates, and fiscal year definitions. The editor-publisher tracks all of this in their personal calendar and a folder of grant letters. Missing a reporting deadline — even by a day — can disqualify the press from future funding. Given that grants represent 30–60% of revenue for many poetry presses, this is an existential risk managed by memory.

## Why It's Still Broken
Grant management software exists (Fluxx, Submittable Grants, Blackbaud) but it's designed for grantmakers (foundations), not grantees (arts organizations). The few grantee-focused tools (GrantHub, GrantTracker) are built for large nonprofits managing 20–50 grants simultaneously, priced at $100–$400/month — unaffordable for a poetry press with $50K–$200K in total annual revenue. QuickBooks can track expenses but doesn't model grant periods, restricted vs. unrestricted funds, or reporting deliverables. The result: the editor-publisher does it all by hand, and 10–15% of small literary presses miss a reporting deadline each year.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A lightweight grant tracker where the publisher: (1) enters each grant (funder, amount, period, deliverables, reporting dates), (2) tags expenses to specific grants for financial reporting, (3) receives automated reminders 30/14/7 days before reporting deadlines, (4) generates funder-specific financial reports showing grant expenditures against budget, (5) tracks deliverable completion (titles published, events held, copies distributed) against grant commitments, and (6) maintains a calendar of upcoming application deadlines for recurring grants. Priced at $15–$30/month. Simple enough that a poet who runs a press as a side project can use it without training.

## Who Feels the Pain
Editor-publishers who lose sleep over grant deadlines and spend 5–10 hours per grant report assembling financial data from QuickBooks, bank statements, and email receipts.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates missed reporting deadlines that jeopardize $10K–$50K in future funding per incident. Reduces grant reporting time from 8 hours to 2 hours per report. Provides financial visibility that strengthens future grant applications by demonstrating organizational capacity — a key evaluation criterion for arts funders.
