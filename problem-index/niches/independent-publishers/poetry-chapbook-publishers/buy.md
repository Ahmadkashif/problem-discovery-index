# Poetry-Specific Layout and Typesetting Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/poetry-chapbook-publishers/profile|Poetry & Chapbook Publishers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** InDesign handles professional typesetting but requires $23/month and 50+ hours of learning — poetry presses need a tool that understands line breaks, stanza spacing, and page-turn placement without a design degree.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
Poetry layout is fundamentally different from prose layout. In prose, text flows continuously and page breaks are cosmetic. In poetry, every line break is intentional, stanza spacing carries meaning, and where a poem breaks across pages can alter the reading experience. A skilled poetry typesetter knows that a poem with a dramatic turn in line 14 should have that turn at the top of a new page, not buried mid-page. Long-line poems need different margins than short-line poems. Indentation patterns must be preserved exactly. Most small poetry presses either pay a freelance designer $500–$2,000 per book or spend 20–40 hours per title doing it themselves in InDesign — software that wasn't designed for the poetry layout paradigm.

## What Already Exists
Adobe InDesign is the industry standard for book layout, with paragraph styles, master pages, and precise typographic control. Affinity Publisher is a lower-cost alternative. Reedsy Book Editor and Atticus offer simplified book layout for self-publishers. But none of these tools understand poetry-specific constraints: line break preservation from the source manuscript, stanza break handling (single vs. double space, with or without asterisk/section break markers), long-line wrapping rules (indent continuation lines vs. outdent), widow/orphan rules specific to poetry (never split a couplet across pages), and the relationship between poem length and page placement (short poems centered vertically, long poems starting at top).

## The Customization Gap
A poetry layout tool needs to: (1) import manuscripts with line breaks and stanza breaks preserved exactly as the poet intended, (2) apply poetry-specific typographic rules automatically (widows/orphans at the stanza level, not the paragraph level), (3) offer page-turn preview that highlights where poems break across pages so the editor can adjust placement, (4) handle mixed formats common in contemporary poetry (prose poems, visual/concrete poems, poems with unusual spacing), (5) generate print-ready PDF with bleeds and crop marks for short-run printers, and (6) have a learning curve of hours, not weeks. The key insight: poetry layout is highly constrained (fewer variables than prose layout) and therefore more automatable — but nobody has automated the right constraints.

## Target Customer
Editor-publishers at poetry presses who currently spend 20–40 hours per title on layout in InDesign or outsource design at $500–$2,000 per book.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces poetry layout time from 30 hours to 5–8 hours per title, saving $1,000–$2,000 per book in freelance design costs or 100+ hours per year of publisher time. Eliminates the 2–3 layout errors per book (bad page breaks, lost formatting) that require correction reprints costing $500–$1,500 each.
