# Visual Territory Rights Management Dashboard

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/foreign-rights-departments/profile|Foreign Rights Departments]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product provides a visual map-based interface showing which territories are sold, available, or approaching reversion for every title in a publisher's catalog — the fundamental question in every rights meeting.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
At Frankfurt or Bologna Book Fair, a rights manager sits in 30+ meetings over 3 days with foreign publishers. Each meeting starts with "what do you have available in [language]?" The rights manager flips through a printed spreadsheet or searches their laptop, cross-referencing titles against territory availability. They can't show the foreign publisher a visual representation of their catalog filtered by that publisher's territory because the data lives in a flat spreadsheet with no visual layer. Promising titles get overlooked because the rights manager doesn't remember they're available. Post-fair follow-up requires manually assembling a customized rights guide for each foreign publisher they met — a 40-hour post-fair project.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Rights management platforms (Klopotek, Firebrand, Vista) include rights tracking but present it as database tables, not visual interfaces. The Big 5 publishers have built internal tools, but these are proprietary. The indie press market (200–400 presses with active rights programs) is too small for enterprise vendors. And the data model is complex: rights can be split by language, territory, format (hardcover, paperback, ebook, audio), time period, and sublicense type — creating a multidimensional matrix that's hard to visualize without purpose-built UX.

## What to Build
A web-based rights dashboard with: (1) a world map view for each title showing territory status by color (green = available, red = sold, yellow = expiring within 12 months, gray = not applicable), (2) a catalog view that foreign publishers can browse with territory-specific filtering ("show me all available titles in Turkish"), (3) a meeting preparation mode that generates a customized digital rights guide for a specific publisher based on their territory and genre interests, (4) deal status tracking from initial pitch through contract to publication, and (5) a reversion calendar showing which sold territories approach contractual reversion dates. Shareable links allow foreign publishers to browse available titles between book fairs.

## Target Customer
Rights managers at independent publishers with 100+ backlist titles and active foreign rights programs across 10+ territories.

## Impact If Built
Increases foreign rights deals by 15–25% through better catalog visibility during meetings and follow-up. Reduces pre-fair preparation from 40 hours to 5 hours. Identifies 10–20 reverted territories per year that can be relicensed — each deal worth $2,000–$15,000 in advance revenue.
