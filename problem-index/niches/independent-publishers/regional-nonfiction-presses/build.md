# Local Bookstore and Gift Shop Distribution Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/regional-nonfiction-presses/profile|Regional Nonfiction Presses]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product helps a regional publisher track which local bookstores, gift shops, and museum stores carry their titles on consignment — how many copies are at each location, what's sold, and when to restock.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
A regional nonfiction publisher distributes to 30–100 local retail outlets: independent bookstores, museum gift shops, visitor centers, coffee shops, and specialty retailers. Most of these relationships are consignment — the publisher leaves 5 copies, checks back in 6 weeks, collects payment for what sold, and restocks. Tracking is done in a notebook or spreadsheet: which stores have which titles, how many copies, when they were delivered, what's been paid. The publisher drives a circuit monthly, often discovering that a store sold out weeks ago (lost sales) or that books have been damaged/lost with no payment. Scaling beyond 50 outlets is operationally impossible because tracking consumes more time than the sales justify.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Traditional distribution (Ingram, Baker & Taylor) handles bookstore ordering and fulfillment — but regional publishers' local retail relationships fall outside this system. Gift shops and visitor centers don't use ISBN-based ordering systems. The market size per publisher is tiny (50–100 accounts), and the publishers themselves are 1-person operations with no software budget. SaaS vendors can't justify building for customers who spend $50/month. The consignment model (publisher retains ownership until sale) adds inventory tracking complexity that wholesale distribution tools don't handle.

## What to Build
A mobile-first CRM for publisher-to-retailer consignment: (1) outlet profiles with contact info, terms, and title selection, (2) delivery logging (X copies of Title Y left at Store Z on Date), (3) a visit scheduler that optimizes driving routes based on outlet locations and last-visit dates, (4) a checkout screen used during store visits to record sales, collect payment, and log restocking, (5) a dashboard showing inventory-at-retail, sell-through rates per outlet, and revenue by location, and (6) simple invoicing for outlets that pay net-30 rather than on-visit. Priced at $30–$50/month for a solo publisher.

## Target Customer
Owner-publishers at regional nonfiction presses distributing to 20–100 local retail outlets on consignment.

## Impact If Built
Enables publishers to scale from 50 to 150+ outlets without additional time investment. Identifies high-performing outlets for increased stock (10–15% revenue lift from reduced out-of-stock). Reduces uncollected consignment revenue (currently estimated at 5–10% of retail sales) through systematic tracking.
