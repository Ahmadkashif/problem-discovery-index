# Print-on-Demand Integration with Consignment Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/regional-nonfiction-presses/profile|Regional Nonfiction Presses]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** IngramSpark handles print-on-demand but doesn't support the consignment-to-local-retail model — publishers need small-batch POD orders shipped directly to individual gift shops and bookstores.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Regional publishers face a classic inventory dilemma: print 1,000 copies of a new title at $3/copy and store them in the garage, or use print-on-demand at $6/copy and order as needed. Most do offset print runs for initial release (lower cost, better quality) but need POD for restocking consignment outlets — 5 copies here, 3 copies there. Currently, the publisher orders POD copies from IngramSpark, has them shipped to their home, then repackages and drives them to individual outlets. The handling cost of this middle step often exceeds the printing cost.

## What Already Exists
IngramSpark, Amazon KDP, and BookVault offer print-on-demand with global distribution. These platforms are designed for single-copy fulfillment (customer orders, bookstore wholesale orders via Ingram) but not for publisher-directed small-batch shipments to specific non-bookstore retail outlets. IngramSpark can drop-ship to bookstores that order through Ingram, but gift shops, visitor centers, and coffee shops don't have Ingram accounts and can't place orders through the system.

## The Customization Gap
A regional publisher needs: (1) the ability to place a POD order for 5 copies and have them shipped directly to "Mountain Heritage Gift Shop, 123 Main St, Asheville NC" — not to the publisher's address, (2) a stored list of outlet addresses for one-click restock orders, (3) split invoicing (printing cost to the publisher, not to the outlet), (4) the option to include a packing slip/invoice for the consignment record, and (5) cost-effective shipping for small batches (5–20 copies) that doesn't exceed the book's retail margin. This requires the POD platform to treat the publisher as a fulfillment coordinator, not just a customer — a workflow that doesn't exist in current POD systems.

## Target Customer
Regional publishers who use POD for restocking and distribute to 20+ local outlets that don't order through traditional wholesale channels.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates the publisher's middle-handling step, saving 3–5 hours per week of packing and driving. Reduces restock turnaround from 2 weeks (order → ship to publisher → drive to outlet) to 3–5 days (order → ship to outlet). Enables publishers to serve 50–100% more outlets without proportional time increase.
