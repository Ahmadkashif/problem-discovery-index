## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent children's book publishers occupy a unique position: they compete with Big 5 imprints for shelf space at bookstores and library orders, but operate with 1/100th the budget. The production process is fundamentally different from adult publishing because children's books are illustration-driven — the art director relationship with illustrators is the core creative workflow, involving concept sketches, color studies, multiple revision rounds, and color-critical printing. Sales channels skew heavily toward institutional (school and library) rather than retail, requiring specialized marketing through review journals (Kirkus, School Library Journal, Horn Book) and direct outreach to school librarians. The audience being served — children from diverse backgrounds, children with disabilities, bilingual families — is chronically underrepresented in Big 5 catalogs, creating both a cultural mission and a market opportunity for independents.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Adobe Creative Suite handles production. Edelweiss+ handles digital catalog distribution to libraries. But the illustrator workflow — sourcing, contracting, revision management, color proofing — runs entirely on email and Dropbox with no structured pipeline. School and library marketing happens through conference attendance, mailing lists, and personal relationships with librarians. No tool helps a 5-person children's publisher manage 15 simultaneous illustrator relationships across 12–20 active titles while tracking the 6–8 revision rounds each illustration goes through before final art.
