# ONIX Metadata Enrichment for Discoverability

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/academic-monograph-publishers/profile|Academic Monograph Publishers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Firebrand Title Management handles ONIX metadata but doesn't auto-generate the scholarly-specific metadata — BISAC subject codes, Thema classifications, and keyword taxonomies — that determine whether a monograph is discoverable in library catalogs and aggregator platforms.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Academic monograph discoverability depends almost entirely on metadata quality. A monograph's ONIX record must include accurate BISAC subject codes, Thema subject classifications, Library of Congress subject headings, series information, contributor affiliations, and keywords that match how scholars search. Most university presses assign metadata manually — a production assistant reads the book description and picks 2–3 subject codes from a list of 4,000+. Misclassification means a book about Ottoman economic history gets filed under "Middle Eastern Studies" instead of "Economic History," and the economic historians who would buy it never find it.

## What Already Exists
Firebrand Title Management, Consonance, and Bibliocloud manage ONIX records and distribute metadata to supply-chain partners (Ingram, Baker & Taylor, Amazon). These tools provide the framework for entering metadata but don't help generate it. BISACulator and Thema classification tools exist but require manual input and don't consider the full text or abstract. No tool auto-suggests subject headings based on the manuscript content, maps across classification systems (BISAC → Thema → LCSH), or identifies keyword gaps that reduce discoverability.

## The Customization Gap
Academic metadata has unique requirements: (1) multi-level subject classification across 3+ taxonomies (BISAC, Thema, LCSH) that must be consistent with each other, (2) contributor metadata including institutional affiliation and ORCID identifiers, (3) series and collection relationships that affect how aggregators bundle titles for institutional sales, (4) open-access status and license type that determine which institutional portals can host the content, and (5) related-works linking to previous editions, translations, and companion volumes. A vertical solution needs to read the manuscript abstract and table of contents, suggest classifications across all relevant taxonomies, identify keyword gaps by comparing to high-performing titles in the same field, and flag inconsistencies before the ONIX record is distributed.

## Target Customer
Production managers and metadata specialists at university presses and scholarly publishers distributing through JSTOR, Project MUSE, or EBSCO.

## Impact If Solved
Improved metadata accuracy increases discoverability by 20–35%, translating to an estimated 15–25% increase in institutional digital sales — worth $10K–$50K per title over its lifetime for a scholarly monograph. Reduces metadata preparation time from 45 minutes to 10 minutes per title.
