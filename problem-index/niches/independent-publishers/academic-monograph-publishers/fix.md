# Open Access Compliance Tracking Across Funder Mandates

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-publishers/academic-monograph-publishers/profile|Academic Monograph Publishers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-publishers|Independent Publishers]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Authors funded by NIH, NSF, Wellcome Trust, and the EU have different open-access requirements — and the publisher tracks compliance for each in a separate spreadsheet, missing deadlines that cost both the author and the press.
**Tags:** #compliance #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation

## The Problem
Open-access mandates from funding agencies are proliferating: NIH requires immediate OA for funded research (as of 2025), NSF has similar requirements, the EU's Plan S applies to Horizon Europe grants, and individual universities have their own OA policies. Each mandate has different requirements for embargo periods, acceptable licenses (CC-BY vs. CC-BY-NC), deposit repositories (PubMed Central, institutional repositories, OAPEN), and compliance timelines. A university press publishing 30 monographs per year may have 15–20 titles with some form of OA requirement, each with a different mandate, deadline, and deposit destination. Compliance is tracked manually, and a missed deadline can jeopardize the author's future funding eligibility.

## Why It's Still Broken
The OA landscape changes constantly — new mandates, revised policies, extended deadlines. No single reference source consolidates funder requirements for monographs (most OA tracking tools focus on journal articles). University presses lack dedicated compliance staff — the acquisitions editor or production manager adds OA tracking to their existing workload. The financial model is also confused: some funders provide Book Processing Charges (BPCs) to offset lost sales revenue, others don't, and tracking which titles have BPC funding versus which are unfunded OA is another manual process. Publishers fear that centralizing this data will reveal how much revenue they're losing to OA mandates.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A compliance dashboard that: (1) maintains an up-to-date database of funder and institutional OA mandates with specific requirements for monographs, (2) links each title to its applicable mandates based on author funding declarations captured at acquisition, (3) auto-calculates deposit deadlines and acceptable license terms, (4) tracks deposit status across repositories (OAPEN, institutional repositories, publisher platform), (5) flags titles approaching compliance deadlines, and (6) reports BPC revenue against OA-related sales displacement. Integration with ONIX records so that OA status and license information flow automatically to metadata distribution.

## Who Feels the Pain
Production managers who manually track OA compliance across 20+ titles with different mandates, and authors whose tenure cases and future grants depend on the publisher meeting deposit deadlines.

## Impact If Fixed
Achieves 100% OA compliance (vs. estimated 70–80% currently), protecting author funding eligibility worth $50K–$500K per grant cycle. Reduces OA tracking overhead from 5–8 hours per week to 1 hour. Identifies $20K–$100K in unclaimed or misapplied BPC funding annually.
