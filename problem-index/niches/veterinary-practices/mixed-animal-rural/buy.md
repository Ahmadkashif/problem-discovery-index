# Dual-Species PMS Configuration for Cornerstone

**Niche:** [[niches/veterinary-practices/mixed-animal-rural/profile|Mixed Animal Rural]]
**Industry:** [[industries/veterinary-practices|Veterinary Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Mixed practices running Cornerstone have retrofitted a companion-animal PMS for livestock work using workarounds that break reporting, complicate inventory tracking, and make the system harder for staff to learn — they need a professionally configured dual-species setup with templates, fee schedules, and workflows for both sides of the practice.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #automation

## The Problem
Most mixed animal practices purchased Cornerstone because it's the industry standard, then spent years building workarounds to handle livestock. They've created custom species entries (adding "Bovine," "Ovine," "Caprine" to a system designed for "Canine" and "Feline"), built livestock-specific invoice templates by hand, added farm call scheduling workarounds, and manually created herd health treatment records. These workarounds are fragile — they break when Cornerstone updates, they confuse new staff who were trained on standard Cornerstone, and they make reporting unreliable because the PMS doesn't know which revenue came from companion work vs. livestock work. The practice owner who built the workarounds is the only person who understands them, creating a dangerous single point of failure.

## What Already Exists
Cornerstone natively supports multiple species and has a large animal module that IDEXX has marketed to mixed practices. However, the large animal module is minimal — it adds basic herd record-keeping but doesn't include livestock-specific invoice templates, farm call scheduling with route optimization, food animal drug withdrawal tracking, or the ability to generate herd health reports in the format that cattle producers expect. Setup and configuration still requires 40-60 hours of practice-specific customization that most rural vets don't have time for.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is pre-built configuration, not new software. A dual-species Cornerstone package needs: (1) pre-configured species hierarchies with breed lists for cattle, horses, swine, goats, sheep, and poultry, (2) livestock-specific SOAP templates for common procedures (herd health checks, reproductive exams, dehorning, castration, dystocia), (3) dual fee schedules — companion animal pricing by individual procedure and livestock pricing by per-head or per-herd rates, (4) a farm client record structure that links individual animal records to a farm owner account with multi-animal invoicing, (5) production reports that separate companion and livestock revenue for accounting and tax purposes. Sold as a one-time configuration package ($2,500-5,000) plus ongoing support ($49-79/month).

## Target Customer
Mixed animal practices running Cornerstone with years of accumulated workarounds built by the practice owner, creating fragility and staff training burden — and new mixed practices setting up Cornerstone for the first time who want livestock-ready configuration from Day 1.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 40-60 hours of DIY PMS configuration and ongoing workaround maintenance for mixed practices. Enables accurate companion vs. livestock revenue reporting for the first time, which matters for practice valuation, tax planning, and strategic decisions about which side of the practice to grow. Reduces new staff training time by 50% by replacing idiosyncratic workarounds with standardized workflows.
