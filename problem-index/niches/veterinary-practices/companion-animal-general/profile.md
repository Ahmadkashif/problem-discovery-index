# Companion Animal General Practice

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/veterinary-practices|Veterinary Practices]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $22-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~46%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most practices run IDEXX Cornerstone or Avimark but use only basic charting and billing features; analytics, recall optimization, and clinical decision support are untouched
**Target Buyer:** Practice owner-vet at a 1-3 veterinarian clinic who is simultaneously the lead clinician, practice manager, and business strategist
**Automation Potential:** High — the owner-vet has no time for administrative optimization, meaning any tool that auto-surfaces practice health metrics or automates client communication delivers immediate ROI

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent companion animal practices — dogs and cats — make up roughly 22,000 of the ~33,000 veterinary hospitals in the US. The defining characteristic is that the owner IS the clinician. They spend 35-40 hours per week in exam rooms and surgery, leaving 5-8 hours for hiring, inventory management, marketing, financial planning, and continuing education. These practices deliver wellness exams, vaccinations, dental cleanings, soft tissue surgery, and basic diagnostic workups. They compete directly with corporate chains (VCA, Banfield) that have centralized marketing budgets, volume-discounted supplies, and standardized protocols — but independent practices retain clients through personal relationships and continuity of care. Buying decisions are personal, budget-sensitive, and driven by whether the tool saves the vet or their one office manager visible time within the first week.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IDEXX Cornerstone and Avimark hold roughly 55% combined market share in small animal practices. Vetspire and eVetPractice are cloud-native alternatives gaining share with younger practice owners. These systems handle medical records, scheduling, invoicing, and basic inventory, but their reporting is rudimentary — generating a "revenue per exam room hour" report requires manual data exports and spreadsheet work. Practice analytics tools like VetSuccess and Vetsource exist but are designed for multi-location groups, not solo practitioners. Patient communication tools (PetDesk, Vet2Pet) handle appointment reminders but don't integrate with clinical workflows. The gap is a practice health intelligence layer — production analytics, client retention tracking, and procedure acceptance rate monitoring — that sits on top of the existing PMS without requiring a system change.
