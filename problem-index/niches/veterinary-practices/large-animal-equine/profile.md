# Large Animal & Equine

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/veterinary-practices|Veterinary Practices]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many equine and large animal vets document on paper or dictate notes hours after farm calls; PMS adoption is limited because systems assume a fixed clinic workflow
**Target Buyer:** Equine or large animal veterinary practice owner, typically a solo practitioner or 2-3 vet group
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the mobile nature of the work creates unique documentation and invoicing challenges that technology could solve, but solutions must work offline in rural areas with poor connectivity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Large animal and equine veterinarians spend 60-80% of their working hours on the road, driving between farms, barns, and equestrian facilities. They perform lameness evaluations, reproductive work (breeding soundness exams, embryo transfer, foaling assistance), herd health programs for cattle, field surgery, and portable diagnostic imaging (digital radiography, ultrasound). The vet's "office" is the back of a truck stocked with drugs, instruments, and portable equipment. Documentation happens hours later — the vet returns home at 8 PM and spends 60-90 minutes typing up records from handwritten notes or memory. Invoicing is equally delayed and complicated: farm calls involve travel fees calculated by mileage, after-hours premiums, multi-animal discounts, and procedure bundles that vary by species and farm. No PMS handles this workflow natively because they are all designed around scheduled appointments at a fixed clinic.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IDEXX Cornerstone and Avimark are designed for fixed-clinic companion animal practices — they assume the patient comes to the clinic, not the other way around. Some large animal vets use these systems anyway, retrofitting them with workarounds (entering "Farm Call" as a location, manually calculating mileage fees). Equine-specific software like Platinum Performance and Equine Guelph exists but focuses on nutrition and research, not practice management. Sherpaa (now discontinued) and SmartFlow attempted mobile-first veterinary documentation but targeted emergency and specialty clinics. The gap is a mobile-first practice management platform designed from the ground up for ambulatory veterinary medicine — offline-capable documentation, GPS-based mileage tracking, multi-animal farm visit invoicing, and drug inventory management for the truck pharmacy.
