## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Chronic medication management is a growing revenue segment in veterinary practice as pets live longer and receive ongoing treatment for heart disease (pimobendan, enalapril), hypothyroidism (levothyroxine), seizure disorders (phenobarbital, levetiracetam, potassium bromide), arthritis (carprofen, gabapentin), and allergies (apoquel, cytopoint). A typical companion animal practice has 200-400 patients on chronic medications requiring refills every 30-90 days. Each refill involves: verifying that the patient has been examined within the VCPR validity period (varies by state, typically 12 months), confirming the prescription is still appropriate (bloodwork monitoring for drugs with hepatic/renal side effects), processing the refill in the PMS, and dispensing or calling in the prescription. Increasingly, clients request that prescriptions be sent to online pharmacies (Chewy, 1-800-PetMeds, Walmart Pet Rx) that undercut practice pricing by 15-40%, eroding a revenue stream that represents $60K-180K/year for a typical practice.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PMS platforms (Cornerstone, Avimark, Vetspire) store prescription records but have no proactive refill reminder system. The vet tech or receptionist must manually track which patients are due for refills, verify VCPR status by looking up last exam dates, check whether monitoring bloodwork is current, and contact the client — all while handling walk-ins, phone calls, and surgical prep. There is no tool that compares the practice's medication pricing to Chewy or 1-800-PetMeds, so practices have no visibility into where they are price-competitive and where they are losing clients to online pharmacies. DEA-controlled substance tracking (ketamine, phenobarbital, tramadol) adds a compliance layer that is managed through a separate paper log in most practices.
