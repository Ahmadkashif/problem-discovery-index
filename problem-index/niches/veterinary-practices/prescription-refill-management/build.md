# Automated Refill Management with VCPR Verification

**Niche:** [[niches/veterinary-practices/prescription-refill-management/profile|Prescription Refill Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/veterinary-practices|Veterinary Practices]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** An automated prescription refill platform that tracks every chronic medication patient, proactively alerts when refills are due, verifies VCPR validity and monitoring bloodwork status before authorizing refills, and manages DEA controlled substance logging — replacing the vet tech's manual tracking spreadsheet.
**Tags:** #decision-trees #feature-engineering #compliance #automation #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
A typical companion animal practice has 200-400 patients on chronic medications. Each patient needs refills every 30-90 days, and each refill requires a verification chain: Is the VCPR current (has the patient been examined within the state-mandated timeframe)? Is monitoring bloodwork up to date (liver values for dogs on phenobarbital, renal values for cats on methimazole, thyroid levels for dogs on levothyroxine)? Has the prescribing vet approved the refill? Is this a controlled substance requiring DEA log entry? Currently, this verification is done by a vet tech or receptionist who maintains a spreadsheet or mental list of chronic patients, manually checks the PMS for last exam and bloodwork dates when a refill request comes in, calls the client to schedule monitoring if needed, and processes the refill. When the practice gets busy, refill requests pile up, clients call multiple times asking about status, and controlled substance logs fall behind — creating both a client service failure and a compliance risk.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Veterinary prescription management has three complicating factors that don't exist in human pharmacy: (1) VCPR requirements vary by state (some require annual exam, some require exam within 12 months for each condition, some have different rules for food animals vs. companion animals), making a one-size-fits-all verification engine complex, (2) monitoring requirements are drug-specific and not standardized — some practices check liver values every 6 months for phenobarbital patients, others check annually, and the appropriate interval depends on patient-specific factors (age, concurrent conditions, duration on the drug), (3) DEA controlled substance regulations for veterinary practices differ from human pharmacies and vary by state, with some states requiring separate logs per drug, per prescriber, or per dispensing location. Building a refill management system that handles all three verification layers across 50 states requires deep regulatory knowledge and configurable rule engines.

## What to Build
A cloud-based refill management platform that integrates with the practice's PMS and automates the refill workflow. Core features: (1) chronic medication registry — auto-populated from PMS prescription records, showing every active chronic medication patient with drug, dose, frequency, last refill date, next refill due date, prescribing vet, and refill count remaining, (2) VCPR verification engine — state-specific rules that flag when a VCPR is expiring or expired, blocking refill authorization and triggering a client notification to schedule an exam, (3) monitoring bloodwork tracking — drug-specific rules (configurable per practice) that flag when monitoring labs are overdue, blocking refill until labs are performed and reviewed, (4) automated client refill reminders — SMS/email notification 5-7 days before a refill is due, with one-tap refill request, (5) DEA controlled substance logging — automatic entry into a digital controlled substance log when controlled drugs are dispensed, with inventory reconciliation and audit report generation. Decision-tree logic routes each refill request through the appropriate verification chain based on drug class, state, and patient status.

## Target Customer
Practice managers and vet techs at companion animal practices with 100+ chronic medication patients, currently tracking refills manually and spending 5-10 hours per week on refill processing, verification, and client communication, willing to pay $149-249/month for a platform that automates the workflow and ensures compliance.

## Impact If Built
Reduces vet tech time spent on refill management from 5-10 hours per week to 1-2 hours (verification exceptions only). Eliminates controlled substance logging errors that can trigger DEA enforcement action. Increases refill compliance rates — proactive client reminders ensure medications aren't interrupted, improving patient outcomes for chronic conditions. Retains pharmacy revenue by making in-practice refills frictionless before the client thinks to order from Chewy.
