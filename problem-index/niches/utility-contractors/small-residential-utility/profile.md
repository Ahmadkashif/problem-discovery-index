# Small Residential Utility

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/utility-contractors|Utility Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — small utility contractors doing residential service connections, meter settings, and small trenching jobs manage operations via phone calls, text messages, and paper work orders. Enterprise utility PM software is prohibitively complex and expensive.
**Target Buyer:** Small utility contractor (3-15 employees) doing residential gas, water, and electric service connections
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — service connection workflows follow predictable patterns and multi-party coordination (utility, contractor, inspector, homeowner) is a workflow automation opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small residential utility contractors perform the "last mile" of utility infrastructure: connecting homes to gas mains, water mains, and electric distribution. A typical job: trench from the main in the street to the home, install the service line, set the meter, and coordinate the utility inspection. These projects cost $3,000-15,000 and take 1-3 days. The work requires coordinating 4 parties: the utility (provides specifications and inspection), the contractor (performs the work), the local building department (issues the permit), and the homeowner (grants access and pays). The coordination is entirely manual — phone calls and text messages to schedule each step — and breaks down frequently when any party misses a step.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Enterprise utility PM tools (Procore, Viewpoint) are designed for $1M+ projects, not $5K service connections. Small contractor tools (Jobber, ServiceTitan) are designed for service businesses, not utility construction. Neither handles the multi-party coordination unique to utility service connections: one-call/811 locate requests, utility pre-construction meetings, permit applications, utility inspections, and meter set scheduling.
