# Generic Safety Platforms with Utility-Specific Templates

**Niche:** [[niches/utility-contractors/safety-compliance-training/profile|Safety, Compliance & Training]]
**Industry:** [[industries/utility-contractors|Utility Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** SafetyCulture and Intelex handle generic construction safety inspections but don't include utility-specific templates — OSHA 1926 Subpart P trenching assessments, NFPA 70E electrical work permits, confined space entry for utility vaults, and pipeline safety qualified person documentation require 100-200 hours of custom template development.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
A utility contractor using SafetyCulture must build custom templates for every utility-specific safety requirement: daily trenching competent person assessment (soil classification, depth, trench protection system, atmospheric conditions), electrical safe work condition verification (NFPA 70E arc flash assessment, PPE selection, energized work permit), confined space entry permit (atmospheric monitoring, rescue plan, entrant/attendant/supervisor designations for utility vaults and manholes), and pipeline safety operator qualification verification (49 CFR 192 OQ records). Each template requires understanding both the regulatory requirement and the software's form-building capabilities. Most utility contractors either build inadequate templates (missing required fields) or outsource template development at $5K-15K per engagement.

## What Already Exists
SafetyCulture handles inspection checklists, photo capture, action item tracking, and analytics. Intelex handles compliance management, training tracking, and incident investigation. Both support custom template creation. Neither ships with utility construction-specific templates.

## The Customization Gap
A "Utility Construction Safety Package" for SafetyCulture or Intelex that includes: (1) OSHA 1926 Subpart P trenching competent person assessment — daily trench safety form with soil classification guide, trench protection selection logic, and depth-based compliance requirements; (2) NFPA 70E electrical safety — energized work permit template with arc flash hazard analysis, PPE selection matrix, and approach boundary calculations; (3) confined space entry permit — atmospheric monitoring log, rescue plan checklist, and entrant/attendant/supervisor assignment for utility vault and manhole entry; (4) DOT pipeline safety OQ verification — covered task list with qualification status for each crew member; and (5) daily pre-task safety analysis templates specific to common utility construction activities (trenching, overhead line work, pipe installation, service connections).

## Target Customer
Utility contractors already using SafetyCulture or Intelex who have built partial custom templates and want comprehensive utility-specific coverage. SafetyCulture/Intelex as platforms seeking to capture the utility construction vertical.

## Impact If Solved
Saves utility contractors 100-200 hours of custom template development time. Ensures regulatory compliance by including all required fields and logic per OSHA, NFPA, and DOT standards. Reduces OSHA citation risk by standardizing safety documentation across crews. Could be offered as a premium industry template package at $50-100/month per contractor or as a one-time purchase at $2K-5K.
