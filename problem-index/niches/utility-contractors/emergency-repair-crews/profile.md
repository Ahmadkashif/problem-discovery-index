# Emergency Repair Crews

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/utility-contractors|Utility Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — emergency response is coordinated via radio, phone, and paper maps. No purpose-built dispatch system exists for utility emergency response. Mutual aid coordination between utilities during major events uses email and conference calls.
**Target Buyer:** Emergency response utility contractor, utility emergency operations manager, or mutual aid coordinator
**Automation Potential:** High — crew dispatch is an optimization problem, damage assessment is a visual classification task, and mutual aid coordination is a workflow management opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Utility emergency response — gas leaks, water main breaks, power outages, downed wires — requires immediate mobilization of specialized crews to locations where the incident type, severity, and required resources are often unknown until arrival. The coordination challenge: a gas utility receives a report of "gas smell at 123 Oak Street." The dispatcher must determine whether to send a leak investigator (small crew, detection equipment) or a full repair crew (backhoe, pipe, fittings). If it's a main leak, they need to isolate the main, notify affected customers, coordinate with fire department, excavate, repair, and restore service — a 4-12 hour process involving 3-5 crews. During major weather events, these emergencies multiply: a winter freeze may produce 50-100 water main breaks in 24 hours. The dispatching of scarce emergency crews across dozens of simultaneous incidents is a triage and optimization problem currently managed by experienced dispatchers using institutional knowledge, radio communication, and paper maps.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Utility SCADA detects some emergencies (gas pressure drops, water pressure loss) but most are reported by customers or first responders. Utility dispatch systems handle routine work orders but lack emergency triage, priority-based dispatch, and mutual aid coordination. No tool optimizes emergency crew routing across multiple simultaneous incidents.
