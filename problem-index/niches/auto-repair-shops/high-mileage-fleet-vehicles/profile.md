# High-Mileage Fleet Vehicles

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $28B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 22%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Fleet customers often use telematics, but the repair shop's intake and billing for fleet accounts is typically manual or semi-automated.
**Target Buyer:** Shop owner with fleet accounts (delivery companies, taxi/rideshare fleets, service companies)
**Automation Potential:** High — Recurring maintenance schedules, PO-based billing, and multi-vehicle service history create predictable, automatable workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Fleet vehicles arrive with 150K–400K miles and need repair shops that understand commercial-duty wear patterns distinct from consumer cars. Fleet accounts require purchase-order billing, net-30 terms, and consolidated invoicing across multiple vehicles — none of which standard consumer shop management systems handle well. The mechanic diagnosing a fleet van with 300K miles needs different failure-mode intuition than one working on a 60K-mile family sedan; components fail in different sequences and with different warning signs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Shop management systems like Mitchell 1 and ShopWare handle consumer workflows but lack fleet account management, PO billing, and multi-vehicle history views. Fleet-specific platforms like Fleetio manage the fleet side but don't integrate into the independent shop's repair workflow. The gap is a unified system that lets an independent shop serve both walk-in consumers and fleet accounts without dual data entry.
