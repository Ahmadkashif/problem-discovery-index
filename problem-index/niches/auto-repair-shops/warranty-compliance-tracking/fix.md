# Shops Eating Warranty-Eligible Parts Costs Due to Tracking Failures

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-repair-shops/warranty-compliance-tracking/profile|Warranty & Recall Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Shops replace failed parts at full cost that are still under manufacturer warranty because no one tracks warranty periods — the customer pays twice for the same part.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
A customer's alternator fails 10 months after the shop installed a new one. The service advisor pulls up the vehicle history, sees the previous alternator replacement, but doesn't know the warranty terms for that specific part brand. Rather than spend 20 minutes calling the parts supplier to check warranty status, the advisor quotes the customer for a new alternator at full price. The customer pays $450 for a part that should have been free under warranty. When the customer later discovers the part was warrantied, the shop loses credibility and often the customer permanently.

## Why It's Still Broken
Parts warranty information isn't standardized or digitally accessible. Each parts manufacturer has different warranty terms, and those terms aren't transmitted to the shop management system when the part is purchased. The warranty card goes into a physical box (if it's kept at all). Service advisors under time pressure take the path of least resistance — quoting full price rather than spending time researching warranty status on a per-part, per-supplier basis.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated warranty alert that fires when a vehicle with previously installed parts returns for service. The system checks the original part's install date against known warranty periods and displays a clear flag: "Alternator installed 10/15/2025 — Duralast Gold 3-year warranty active until 10/15/2028. Contact AutoZone Commercial for warranty replacement." The service advisor knows immediately that the part is warrantied without any research, and the customer gets a free replacement they're entitled to.

## Who Feels the Pain
Customers who unknowingly pay full price for parts still under warranty, and shop owners who lose long-term customers when the warranty coverage is discovered after the fact.

## Impact If Fixed
Saves customers $200-$600 per warranty-eligible incident and prevents the shop from losing the customer's lifetime value ($3,000-$8,000) over a warranty dispute. For a typical shop, this prevents 2-4 customer-loss events per month, protecting $6,000-$32,000 in annual lifetime value.
