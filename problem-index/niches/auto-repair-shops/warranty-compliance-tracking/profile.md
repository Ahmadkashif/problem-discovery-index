# Warranty & Recall Compliance Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Warranty tracking is mostly manual (stickers, mileage notes, memory), and recall awareness depends on the customer checking their mail or a technician manually searching NHTSA.
**Target Buyer:** Shop owner/service advisor managing warranty parts and recall awareness for returning customers
**Automation Potential:** High — Warranty expiration dates, recall lookups, and parts warranty claims are entirely rule-based and can be fully automated with VIN + date + mileage data.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent shops install aftermarket parts with 12-36 month warranties and perform repairs covered by extended vehicle warranties — but tracking these warranties is entirely manual. A customer returns with a failed water pump 11 months after replacement, and the service advisor has to dig through past invoices to find the original repair date, check whether the parts warranty covers it, and file a claim with the parts supplier. Meanwhile, open safety recalls go unmentioned because no one checked the NHTSA database for the customer's VIN. Both represent money left on the table and customer trust opportunities missed.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Shop management systems track repair history but don't flag when installed parts are still under warranty. NHTSA's recall lookup is free but requires manual VIN entry — no shop management system auto-checks recalls on vehicle intake. Parts supplier warranty claim processes vary by supplier and are entirely manual (phone, email, or supplier-specific portals). No system unifies parts warranty tracking, recall awareness, and claim filing.
