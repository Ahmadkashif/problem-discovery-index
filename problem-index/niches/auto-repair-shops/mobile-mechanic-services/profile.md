# Mobile Mechanic Services

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most mobile mechanics operate via phone calls, text messages, and handwritten invoices. Booking, dispatch, and payment are entirely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Mobile mechanic owner running 1-5 technician vans
**Automation Potential:** High — Route optimization, inventory tracking, and job quoting are all highly automatable but currently done by phone and memory.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobile mechanics bring the shop to the vehicle — driveways, parking lots, office parks. This inverts every assumption that shop management software makes: there's no fixed bay, no parts counter, no lift, no front desk. The mobile mechanic needs to carry the right parts in the van (limited space), quote accurately before arriving (no "let me look at it first" option for many customers), and manage a rolling schedule across geography. The business model is fundamentally different from a fixed shop, but mobile mechanics are forced to use tools designed for brick-and-mortar operations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some mobile mechanics use generic field service tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) designed for home services, but these lack automotive-specific features like VIN decode, labor time lookup, and parts catalog integration. Others use shop management systems (Tekmetric) on a phone, but the UI assumes a fixed location with multiple bays. No platform combines automotive repair workflow with mobile field service logistics.
