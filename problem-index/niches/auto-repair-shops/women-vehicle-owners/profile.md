**Market Size:** $52B

**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Women customers disproportionately value transparency tools (DVIs, text updates), but shop marketing and UX rarely targets their preferences.
**Target Buyer:** Shop owner seeking to grow female customer base or women-owned shop operator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Transparency-focused communication workflows (photo documentation, clear explanations, fair-price benchmarking) are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Women control or influence 65% of household vehicle maintenance decisions and represent 42% of auto repair spending, yet industry surveys consistently show women trust auto repair shops less than men do. The core issue isn't mechanical — it's communication and transparency. Women customers report being talked down to, pressured into unnecessary repairs, and given jargon-heavy explanations instead of clear descriptions. Shops that successfully serve this audience (like the AAA-certified "She Mechanic" model) see dramatically higher retention and referral rates. The opportunity isn't a pink-washed version of existing services — it's a transparency-first repair experience.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Digital Vehicle Inspections (DVIs) have improved transparency by showing photos and videos of vehicle condition. But DVI tools like AutoVitals and Tekmetric present findings in technical language and severity ratings designed for technicians, not customers. No platform optimizes the customer-facing experience specifically for clarity, trust-building, and non-jargon communication. Marketing tools like Broadly focus on reviews but don't address the in-shop trust gap.
