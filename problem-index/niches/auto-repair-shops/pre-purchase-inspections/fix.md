# PPIs Producing Unstructured Output Buyers Can't Use for Negotiation

**Niche:** [[niches/auto-repair-shops/pre-purchase-inspections/profile|Pre-Purchase Inspections]]
**Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Buyers pay $100-$250 for a PPI and receive a handwritten checklist they can't use to negotiate with the seller, making the service feel like wasted money.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
The typical PPI output is a handwritten or poorly formatted checklist with technician shorthand: "LF tire 4/32, RF tire 5/32, brakes 40%, oil leak at valve cover, AC blows warm." The buyer receives this list and has no idea what it means financially. Is a valve cover oil leak a $200 fix or a $2,000 fix? Should the worn tires reduce the asking price by $400 or $1,200? The PPI provides mechanical information but zero financial translation — the buyer can't walk into a negotiation and say "your car needs $3,400 in repairs, so I'll offer $14,600 instead of $18,000."

## Why It's Still Broken
Technicians are trained to diagnose and document mechanical condition, not to create buyer-ready financial documents. The shop management system treats a PPI like any other inspection — it captures findings but doesn't calculate aggregate costs or produce a negotiation-ready summary. The technician's job ends at documenting condition; no one's job is to translate that into buyer value.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An output layer that takes the technician's PPI findings, auto-populates repair cost estimates for each identified issue (using the shop's own pricing), totals the cost-to-repair, and generates a clean one-page summary the buyer can hand to the seller: vehicle condition grade, itemized repair list with costs, total estimated investment, and suggested fair-offer price. The technician fills out the same checklist they always do — the system does the financial translation automatically.

## Who Feels the Pain
Buyers who feel they didn't get $150 worth of value from a PPI because they can't use the output to negotiate, and shop owners who lose repeat PPI referrals because customers say "it wasn't worth it."

## Impact If Fixed
Increases PPI customer satisfaction from ~60% to ~90%, driving 2-3x more referrals per PPI customer. Shops that deliver negotiation-ready reports see 40-60% higher PPI volume from word-of-mouth alone, adding $500-$1,500/month in PPI revenue.
