# Pre-Purchase Inspections

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-repair-shops|Auto Repair Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 1.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most PPIs produce handwritten checklists or generic pass/fail forms with no structured data, no scoring, and no integration into the buyer's decision workflow.
**Target Buyer:** Shop owner offering PPI services as a revenue stream
**Automation Potential:** High — PPIs follow rigid checklists, produce standardized findings, and result in pass/fail decisions with clear cost-to-repair calculations — ideal for automation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pre-purchase inspections (PPIs) are a structured, checklist-driven service where a buyer pays $100-$250 to have a vehicle inspected before purchase. Unlike general repair (diagnose and fix), a PPI is purely diagnostic with a standardized output: condition assessment, estimated repair costs, and a buy/don't-buy recommendation. This makes it one of the most automatable workflows in auto repair — yet most shops still use paper checklists, handwrite findings, and give verbal recommendations. The output has high value to the buyer but is rarely delivered in a format they can use for negotiation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some shops use generic inspection templates in their DVI tools (AutoVitals, Tekmetric) for PPIs, but these are designed for maintenance inspections, not buyer-focused purchase assessments. There's no tool that generates a structured PPI report with condition scores, estimated repair costs, market value comparison, and a clear financial recommendation (buy at asking price / negotiate down by $X / walk away).
