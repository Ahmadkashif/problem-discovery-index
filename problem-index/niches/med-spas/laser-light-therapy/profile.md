# Laser & Light Therapy Clinics

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5.1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 24%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Treatment devices have sophisticated software but practice-level device utilization and outcome tracking are manual.
**Target Buyer:** Clinic director or lead laser technician
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Protocol selection is partially automatable; device parameter optimization requires clinical judgment.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Laser and light therapy (IPL, fractional CO2, Nd:YAG, diode hair removal, LED therapy) represents the most capital-intensive segment of med spa operations. A single laser device costs $50,000-200,000, and most practices operate 3-8 devices. The business challenge is maximizing device utilization — an idle laser earning zero revenue still depreciates and requires maintenance contracts. Treatment protocols vary by device, skin type (Fitzpatrick scale), treatment area, and clinical objective, creating a complex parameter space that technicians navigate through experience and training. Outcome variability is high and poorly tracked.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Device manufacturers provide their own treatment parameter guides and sometimes proprietary software. Practice management systems schedule laser appointments but don't track device utilization rates, treatment parameter history per patient, or outcomes by device/technician/skin type combination. No tool helps a clinic director answer: "Which of my 5 laser devices is underutilized, and which technician achieves the best outcomes on the Fitzpatrick IV-V population?"
