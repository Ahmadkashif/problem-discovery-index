# Remote Medical Director Compliance & Oversight Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/rural-suburban/profile|Rural & Suburban Med Spas]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the remote medical director relationship — documentation of oversight activities, protocol approvals, chart reviews, and compliance with state-specific supervision requirements.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #data-integration

## The Problem
Most states require med spas to operate under a medical director's supervision. In rural areas, the medical director is often remote — a physician in the nearest city who oversees 2-5 med spas. State regulations specify supervision requirements: chart reviews, protocol approvals, periodic on-site visits, and availability for consultation. These requirements vary dramatically by state and are poorly documented in practice. The medical director signs a boilerplate agreement, reviews a few charts per quarter, and both parties hope it's sufficient. When state boards audit, neither the practice nor the medical director can produce documentation of adequate oversight.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The remote medical director relationship is a compliance requirement that both parties treat as an administrative burden, not a value-add. Medical directors are paid $2,000-5,000/month for minimal involvement. Neither party has demanded better tooling because the compliance risk feels low — until an adverse event triggers a board investigation. The market is also fragmented: each state has different requirements, making a 50-state compliance platform expensive to build and maintain.

## What to Build
A compliance platform that: (1) codifies each state's medical director supervision requirements into a checklist and calendar, (2) facilitates chart review workflows (medical director reviews flagged charts asynchronously with structured feedback), (3) documents all oversight activities with timestamps for audit defense, (4) tracks protocol approval and revision history, (5) schedules and logs required on-site visits, and (6) provides a secure communication channel for consultation requests during procedures.

## Target Customer
Rural med spa owners paying $2,000-5,000/month for a remote medical director with no documentation of actual oversight, and medical directors overseeing 2+ remote practices who need efficient review workflows.

## Impact If Built
Transforms the medical director relationship from a checkbox to a documented compliance program, dramatically reducing regulatory risk. Enables medical directors to efficiently oversee more practices, potentially reducing per-practice fees by 20-30%.
