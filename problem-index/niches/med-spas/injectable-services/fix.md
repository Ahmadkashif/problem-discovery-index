# Multi-Injector Product Vial Sharing & Waste Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/injectable-services/profile|Injectable Services (Botox/Fillers)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When multiple injectors share Botox vials to minimize waste, there is no system to track who used what, leading to inaccurate cost allocation, product disputes, and 10-15% inventory shrinkage.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
In practices with 2-4 injectors, Botox vials are often shared to reduce waste — one injector uses 60 units, another uses the remaining 40 from the same vial. Without unit-level attribution tracking, practices cannot accurately calculate per-injector profitability, identify who is over/under-dosing relative to treatment norms, or reconcile purchased versus consumed product. Many practices discover 10-15% "missing" product during quarterly inventory counts with no way to determine if the gap is waste, miscounting, or theft.

## Why It's Still Broken
Vial sharing is a time-sensitive, physically distributed workflow — injectors draw product in their treatment rooms, not at a central dispensing station. Logging units drawn per injector adds friction to a procedure where the patient is already in the chair. Paper logs are inconsistent. No digital tool makes this logging effortless enough to be reliably adopted in a high-volume injectable practice.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A mobile-first logging tool (phone or tablet) where the injector scans a vial barcode, enters units drawn (or taps a preset for common amounts), and the system auto-attributes the usage to the current patient's chart and the injector's consumption record. Vial lifecycle tracking from opening to disposal, with waste flagged automatically when remaining units expire. Dashboard showing per-injector product efficiency, waste rates, and cost-per-treatment trends.

## Who Feels the Pain
Practice owners/managers doing manual inventory reconciliation monthly, and injectors who are blamed for product discrepancies they cannot explain because there is no usage audit trail.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 10-15% inventory shrinkage ($1,000-7,500/month per practice), enables accurate per-injector profitability analysis, and provides documentation that protects against product diversion claims.
