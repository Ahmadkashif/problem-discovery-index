**Automation Potential:** High — Inventory tracking, dosage documentation, and treatment planning follow predictable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Injectables are the highest-revenue, highest-margin service in most med spas, but operational complexity is underestimated. Each patient's face requires a custom injection plan — units of Botox per muscle, syringe volumes of filler per facial zone — that changes with every visit based on product migration, muscle adaptation, and aging. Injectors maintain mental maps of each patient's treatment history and product preferences. Product inventory involves tracking units across multiple vials with different lot numbers and expiration dates, often across multiple injectors sharing product. The difference between a profitable and unprofitable injectable practice often comes down to product waste management and accurate dosage documentation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Platforms like AestheticsPro, PatientNow, and Nextech offer med spa practice management with before/after photo storage. Allergan's Alle and Galderma's Aspire manage patient loyalty programs. None provide injection-mapping tools that track exactly where product was placed visit-over-visit, real-time vial-level inventory tracking across injectors, or waste analytics that identify product utilization inefficiencies.
