# Regenerative Aesthetics Protocol Registry & Outcome Database

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/regenerative-aesthetics/profile|Regenerative Aesthetics (PRP/Exosomes)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No shared database exists for regenerative aesthetics protocols and outcomes — each practitioner experiments in isolation, repeating mistakes and rediscovering best practices independently.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #regression #tabular-ml #data-integration #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
A physician offering PRP facials must decide: single-spin or double-spin centrifuge protocol? What PRP concentration? Microneedling depth? Number of sessions? These decisions are made based on manufacturer recommendations (which are marketing-influenced), conference presentations (which are anecdotal), and personal trial-and-error. There is no shared outcome database where practitioners can see "double-spin PRP at 4x concentration with 1.5mm microneedling produced better skin texture scores than single-spin at 2x concentration" across hundreds of patients. Each practice learns in isolation.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Regenerative aesthetics is fragmented across small practices with no shared data infrastructure. Protocol variations are enormous (centrifuge models, spin speeds, PRP activation methods, delivery techniques), making standardized data collection difficult. The regulatory ambiguity around exosomes discourages formal outcome tracking. And the competitive dynamics of aesthetic medicine make practitioners reluctant to share their "secret sauce" protocols.

## What to Build
An anonymized protocol registry where practitioners document their regenerative treatment protocols (product, preparation method, delivery technique, patient skin type, treatment area) and outcomes (standardized photography, patient satisfaction, complication rates). The system aggregates data across practices to identify which protocol variations produce the best outcomes for specific indications. Participating practitioners gain access to benchmarking data and evidence-based protocol recommendations.

## Target Customer
Physician-owners performing 20+ regenerative procedures/month who want evidence-based protocol guidance and competitive differentiation through outcome tracking.

## Impact If Built
Accelerates protocol optimization from years of individual trial-and-error to months of collective learning, reduces adverse events from untested protocol variations, and provides marketing-ready outcome data for participating practices.
