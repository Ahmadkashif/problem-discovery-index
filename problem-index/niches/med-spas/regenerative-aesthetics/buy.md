# Consent Management System for Novel Aesthetic Treatments

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/regenerative-aesthetics/profile|Regenerative Aesthetics (PRP/Exosomes)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** E-signature consent platforms handle standard procedure consents but lack the dynamic, treatment-specific risk disclosures needed for novel regenerative treatments with limited safety data.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
Consent for regenerative aesthetic procedures requires disclosures that standard consent templates don't cover: the treatment may use products not FDA-approved for this indication, long-term safety data may be limited, outcomes are variable, and the specific protocol being used may differ from what the patient has seen marketed. Generic consent platforms (DocuSign, IntakeQ) provide static forms, but regenerative treatments evolve so quickly that consent language needs frequent updates. A PRP consent from 2023 may be legally inadequate in 2026 as new risks are identified and state-level regulations change.

## What Already Exists
DocuSign, PandaDoc, and IntakeQ handle electronic consent with template management. HIPAA-compliant consent platforms like Klara and Phreesia offer medical-specific features. Dermatology-focused platforms include procedure-specific consent templates for well-established treatments. None offer regenerative aesthetics-specific consent templates that stay current with evolving regulatory guidance and emerging safety data.

## The Customization Gap
The consent system needs: treatment-specific templates for each regenerative procedure (PRP, exosomes, growth factor microneedling), dynamic risk disclosure sections that update as new safety data emerges, state-specific regulatory disclosures (some states require additional language for non-FDA-approved applications), product-specific lot number documentation within the consent, and automatic flagging when a consent template hasn't been reviewed in 6+ months. Integration with the practice's protocol documentation so the consent accurately reflects the specific technique being used.

## Target Customer
Physician-owners offering regenerative treatments who currently use generic consent forms drafted by their malpractice attorney 2+ years ago.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces malpractice liability exposure by ensuring consent language covers novel treatment-specific risks, saves 2-3 hours/month of attorney review time for consent updates, and improves patient trust through transparent, thorough informed consent.
