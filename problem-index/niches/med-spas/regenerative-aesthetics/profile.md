**Target Buyer:** Physician-owner in regenerative aesthetics
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — Protocol development is evolving too quickly for standardized automation, but patient tracking and consent management are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Regenerative aesthetics — PRP (platelet-rich plasma) facials, microneedling with growth factors, exosome therapy, stem cell-derived products — represents the fastest-evolving segment of med spa medicine. Protocols change every 6-12 months as new products and techniques emerge. Regulatory oversight is inconsistent: PRP is well-established, but exosome products exist in a gray area between FDA-regulated biologics and cosmetic treatments. Practitioners must track rapidly changing protocols, maintain defensive documentation, manage patient consent for novel treatments, and navigate advertising restrictions that vary by state.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No EHR or practice management system has regenerative aesthetics-specific features. Practitioners document PRP treatments in generic SOAP notes with no structured fields for centrifuge settings, PRP concentration, activation agents, or delivery method. Exosome product lot tracking is done manually (when done at all). Consent forms are drafted by individual practices from templates found online, with uncertain legal adequacy.
