# Protocol Management System Adapted for Med Spa Regulatory Requirements

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/protocol-compliance/profile|Treatment Protocol & Compliance Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Standard operating procedure (SOP) management platforms handle document versioning and acknowledgment tracking but don't embed med spa-specific regulatory logic into protocol documents.
**Tags:** #llm #text-classification #nlp #compliance #automation

## The Problem
Med spas maintain treatment protocols — step-by-step procedures for each service offered, approved by the medical director. These protocols live in binders or shared drives, rarely updated, and inconsistently followed. When a protocol is updated (new device settings, revised screening criteria), there's no system to ensure all staff read the update, acknowledge it, and are tested on the change before their next patient. Generic SOP platforms (Trainual, Process Street) handle document management but don't understand med spa regulatory context — they can't flag that a protocol change triggers re-training requirements under state law.

## What Already Exists
Trainual, Process Street, and SweetProcess offer SOP management with version control, acknowledgment tracking, and quiz-based comprehension verification. Healthcare-specific compliance platforms (Healthicity, Compliancy Group) focus on HIPAA and OSHA but not med spa scope-of-practice rules. None combine treatment protocol management with state-specific regulatory awareness.

## The Customization Gap
The SOP platform needs med spa intelligence: (1) protocol templates pre-structured for common med spa treatments with required regulatory sections (contraindications, scope-of-practice notes, supervision requirements), (2) state-specific regulatory annotations ("in CA, this protocol requires direct physician supervision"), (3) automatic re-acknowledgment workflows when protocols are updated, (4) competency quizzes linked to specific protocol sections, and (5) integration with the scheduling system to block staff from performing procedures until they've acknowledged the current protocol version.

## Target Customer
Med spa practices with 5+ treatment protocols and 3+ staff members where protocol compliance is managed informally through verbal reminders and binder reviews.

## Impact If Solved
Ensures every staff member is current on every protocol they perform, reduces adverse events from outdated procedure knowledge, and creates an auditable compliance trail that protects the practice during regulatory inquiries.
