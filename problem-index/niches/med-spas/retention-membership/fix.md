# Rebooking Workflow Gaps Between Treatment Cycles

**Niche:** [[niches/med-spas/retention-membership/profile|Client Retention & Membership Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Med spa clients leave without rebooking their next treatment 40-60% of the time — and the front desk has no system to identify who needs follow-up until weeks have passed.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
The highest-leverage moment in med spa client retention is checkout: when the client is satisfied with their treatment and most receptive to booking their next visit. But front desks are often busy (processing payment, greeting the next client, answering phones), and rebooking isn't systematically prompted. The client walks out planning to "book later" — and 40-60% never do without follow-up. By the time anyone notices the gap, 2-4 weeks have passed, the client has lost urgency, and re-engagement requires a marketing touch rather than a simple scheduling interaction.

## Why It's Still Broken
Rebooking at checkout requires the front desk to know: what treatment the client just had, what the recommended interval is (Botox: 12 weeks, filler: 9-12 months, laser: 4-6 weeks for series), and when the next available appointment falls. This information is in the chart but not surfaced to the front desk in the booking interface. PMS systems show the schedule but don't prompt "this client should rebook in 12 weeks — suggest October 15." The front desk asks "would you like to rebook?" but has no specific recommendation to offer, making the question easy for the client to deflect.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A rebooking automation system that: (1) automatically calculates the next recommended visit date based on the treatment just performed, (2) surfaces this recommendation at checkout ("recommend rebooking Botox for week of June 15"), (3) if the client declines to book, creates an automated follow-up sequence starting 2 weeks before the recommended date, (4) tracks rebooking rates by treatment type and provider, and (5) alerts management when rebooking rates drop below target thresholds. Total front desk interaction addition: 30 seconds at checkout.

## Who Feels the Pain
Front desk staff who get blamed for low rebooking rates without having the tools to systematically rebook, and practice owners who see preventable revenue leakage from clients who intended to return but never scheduled.

## Impact If Fixed
Increasing at-checkout rebooking from 40% to 70% could add $100,000-250,000 in annual revenue for a mid-size med spa by capturing visits that would otherwise require expensive re-engagement marketing.
