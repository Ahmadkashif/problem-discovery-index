# Client Retention & Membership Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B (retention revenue at risk)
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7% (of total revenue at risk from lapsed clients)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Many med spas have loyalty programs and memberships but manage them manually with inconsistent follow-up.
**Target Buyer:** Practice manager or marketing lead
**Automation Potential:** High — Retention workflows (rebooking reminders, membership renewals, lapsed client re-engagement) are trigger-based and highly automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Med spa revenue is disproportionately driven by repeat clients — a Botox patient who returns every 3-4 months is worth $3,000-5,000/year, while the acquisition cost for a new client is $150-400. Yet most med spas have no systematic retention engine. Membership programs (prepaid monthly plans for discounted treatments) are the fastest-growing revenue model but are managed with spreadsheets and manual billing. When a client misses their rebooking window, the practice relies on the front desk to notice and call — which happens inconsistently. The difference between a 60% and 80% annual retention rate can be $200,000-500,000 in revenue for a mid-size med spa.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Vagaro, Boulevard, and Booker offer basic rebooking reminders and loyalty points. Membersy and Kleer provide membership billing platforms. Email marketing tools (Mailchimp, Klaviyo) handle newsletters. None integrate these into a unified retention engine: predicting which clients are at risk of lapsing, triggering personalized re-engagement based on treatment history and spending patterns, managing membership utilization and upsells, and measuring retention economics by client segment.
