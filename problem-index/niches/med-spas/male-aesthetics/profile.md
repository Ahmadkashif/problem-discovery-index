# Male Aesthetics Clients

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/med-spas|Med Spas]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Male clients are tech-comfortable but med spa marketing, intake, and treatment protocols are female-centric by default.
**Target Buyer:** Med spa marketing director or owner seeking to capture male demographic growth
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Marketing segmentation and treatment protocol adaptation are automatable; clinical customization requires provider expertise.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Male aesthetics is the fastest-growing segment in the med spa industry (15-20% annual growth vs. 8-10% overall), but the industry's infrastructure was built for female clients. Marketing imagery, website copy, intake forms ("are you pregnant or breastfeeding?" on every form), treatment protocols (dosing calibrated for female facial musculature), and even the physical environment (décor, music, product displays) signal "this is not for you" to male prospects. Men who do come in have different treatment goals (jawline definition, hair loss, muscle definition rather than anti-aging wrinkle reduction), different scheduling preferences (during lunch, not Saturday mornings), and different communication styles (want efficiency, not pampering).

## Current Tools & Gaps
No med spa practice management or marketing platform offers male-specific features. CRM tools like Vagaro and Booker don't segment by gender for treatment protocol recommendations. Marketing platforms serve the same creative templates regardless of target demographic. Treatment planning tools don't adjust dosing recommendations for male anatomy (men typically require 1.5-2x the Botox units for the same result due to larger muscle mass).
