# Paper Timesheet Collection from Distributed Seasonal Sites

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/seasonal-workforce-programs/profile|Seasonal Workforce Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Seasonal employees at 10–20 municipal sites fill out paper timesheets that are hand-delivered to the finance office weekly — arriving late, illegible, or with math errors that delay payroll for 80+ workers.
**Tags:** #ocr #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Municipal seasonal employees — lifeguards, camp counselors, mowing crews, recreation aides — work at sites spread across the jurisdiction. Each site has a paper timesheet binder. Employees write their start/end times and the site supervisor signs off. On Friday, someone drives the timesheets to the finance office. The payroll clerk manually enters hours into the payroll system, reconciling discrepancies (illegible handwriting, math errors, missing signatures, overtime calculations). For 150 seasonal employees across 15 sites, this process consumes 12–20 hours of finance staff time per pay period. Payroll errors run 5–8%, resulting in over/underpayments that require correction checks.

## Why It's Still Broken
Time clock systems (Kronos/UKG, ADP) require hardware installation at each site — impractical when "sites" include outdoor pools, park shelters, and mowing routes with no power outlets or network connectivity. Mobile time-tracking apps exist but require smartphones, which seasonal staff (many of whom are teenagers) may not have or may not want to use for work purposes. Government payroll systems (Tyler Munis, Central Square) have time-entry modules but they're desktop-only, requiring staff to log in from a computer — which outdoor seasonal sites don't have. Budget-constrained departments can't justify $10K–$30K for a time-clock deployment that's only used 12 weeks per year.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A lightweight web-based time capture system where: (1) each site has a laminated QR code that employees scan with any phone to reach a simple clock-in/clock-out page (no app download required), (2) GPS confirms the employee is at the worksite, (3) supervisors approve timesheets on their phone with a swipe, (4) approved hours auto-export to the payroll system in the correct format, and (5) the system flags overtime before it happens so supervisors can adjust assignments. Cost: under $3/employee/month, billed only during the seasonal window. Works without installing anything — just print and laminate the QR code.

## Who Feels the Pain
Payroll clerks who spend every Monday correcting paper timesheet errors, and site supervisors who drive 30 minutes to deliver timesheets to the finance office.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 10–15 hours per pay period of manual time entry and error correction. Reduces payroll errors from 5–8% to under 1%. Saves $2,000–$5,000 per season in overpayment corrections. Frees supervisors from the weekly timesheet delivery trip, recovering 2–3 hours per week of field time.
