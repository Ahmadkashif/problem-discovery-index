## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Municipal governments are among the largest seasonal employers in the country. Every summer, parks departments hire lifeguards, camp counselors, mowing crews, and recreation program staff. Every winter, public works hires plow drivers and salt truck operators. The seasonal cycle involves mass hiring (50–500 positions in a 2-week window), background checks on minors and employees working with children, certifications (lifeguard, CDL, pesticide applicator), schedule management across dozens of locations, and payroll for employees who work irregular hours at different pay rates for different roles. Most municipalities handle this with paper applications, Excel scheduling, and hand-written timesheets.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Enterprise HR platforms (Workday, ADP) are designed for year-round corporate workforces — their onboarding, scheduling, and time-tracking modules assume a stable employee base, not a seasonal surge of 200 workers who will all leave in 12 weeks. Government-specific HR tools (NEOGOV, CivicHR) handle job posting and applicant tracking but don't extend to scheduling and time management. Seasonal scheduling tools (WhenToWork, Deputy) lack the government-specific requirements: background check clearance tracking, certification verification, union/civil service rules, and multi-location assignment management.
