# Institutional Knowledge Capture for Rural Infrastructure

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/small-town-public-works/profile|Small-Town Public Works]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product captures and structures the decades of institutional knowledge that small-town public works superintendents carry in their heads about infrastructure quirks, seasonal patterns, and equipment histories.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #tacit-knowledge-ml #data-integration

## The Problem
When a 30-year public works superintendent in a town of 5,000 retires, the replacement inherits a filing cabinet, a whiteboard, and no context. Which storm drains back up during heavy rain? Which road sections frost-heave every March? Where are the unmarked water shutoffs? Which piece of equipment needs the dealer-only part versus the aftermarket? This knowledge exists nowhere except the departing employee's memory. New superintendents spend 1–2 years relearning through trial and error — and some lessons only come once a decade (like the culvert that floods during a 50-year storm event).

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Knowledge management tools (Notion, Confluence) are designed for office workers who type, not for field workers in pickup trucks. Voice-to-text quality was insufficient until recently. The market segment — 15,000+ towns under 10,000 people — has never been a technology buyer, so no vendor has invested in understanding the workflow. The knowledge itself is unstructured, situational, and tied to specific geographic features that don't exist in any database.

## What to Build
A mobile-first knowledge base where the superintendent can record geo-tagged voice notes about specific infrastructure assets ("This manhole floods when the river hits 12 feet — sandbagging starts at 10 feet"). LLM transcription structures the note into searchable records linked to map locations. Over time, the system builds a location-aware institutional memory that survives staff turnover. Triggered alerts based on conditions (weather forecasts, seasonal dates) surface relevant notes automatically. Simple enough that a non-technical superintendent can use it from a truck cab.

## Target Customer
Town managers and public works superintendents in municipalities under 10,000 population, especially those with long-tenured staff approaching retirement.

## Impact If Built
Reduces new superintendent ramp-up time from 18 months to 3 months. Prevents an estimated $30K–$100K in annual avoidable damage from infrastructure surprises that the predecessor would have anticipated. Preserves knowledge across an average staff tenure of 25 years.
