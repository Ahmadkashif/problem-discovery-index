# Simplified Work Order System for Micro-Departments

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/small-town-public-works/profile|Small-Town Public Works]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Cartegraph and Cityworks are built for 50-person departments — a 4-person crew needs something that works from a truck cab with no training.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem
Small-town public works crews receive work requests via phone calls to the town office, voicemails, emails to the superintendent's personal phone, and council members flagging issues at meetings. There's no central list, no prioritization, and no tracking. The superintendent keeps it all in their head or on a legal pad. When a citizen calls to ask about the pothole they reported three weeks ago, nobody can tell them its status. When the superintendent is on vacation, urgent requests fall through the cracks.

## What Already Exists
Cartegraph, Cityworks (Trimble), and Lucity (Hexagon) offer comprehensive public works management — work orders, asset tracking, GIS integration, fleet management, reporting. These platforms cost $30K–$150K to implement and require IT staff to configure and maintain. They're designed for departments with 20–200 employees and dedicated administrative support. General-purpose tools like Trello or Monday.com lack the municipal-specific features: citizen request intake, council reporting, seasonal scheduling templates (snow routes, mowing schedules), and GASB-compliant asset depreciation tracking.

## The Customization Gap
A micro-department work order system needs to: (1) accept citizen requests from a simple web form or dedicated phone line with auto-transcription, (2) display all open work on a single-screen mobile dashboard sorted by priority, (3) allow one-tap status updates from the field, (4) auto-generate a weekly summary report for the town manager and council, (5) include pre-built seasonal task templates (spring cleanup, snow operations, fall leaf collection), and (6) cost under $200/month with zero configuration. The key design constraint: the primary user is wearing work gloves and standing next to heavy equipment.

## Target Customer
Public works superintendents and town managers in municipalities with 1,000–10,000 residents and 2–8 public works employees.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 3–5 hours per week of the superintendent's time spent tracking and reporting on work requests. Reduces citizen follow-up calls by 50% through automated status notifications. Prevents 10–20 dropped requests per month that currently disappear into voicemail.
