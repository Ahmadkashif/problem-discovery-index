# Playground and Facility Inspection Tracking Gaps

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/parks-recreation-departments/profile|Parks & Recreation Departments]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Monthly playground inspections required by CPSC guidelines are tracked on paper clipboards, creating liability exposure when a child is injured and the city can't prove the equipment was inspected.
**Tags:** #compliance #worker-facing #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
The Consumer Product Safety Commission publishes playground safety guidelines (ASTM F1487) that recommend monthly inspections of all public playground equipment. Parks departments are supposed to check fall zones, hardware tightness, surface depth, pinch points, and structural integrity at every playground — often 20–80 sites per jurisdiction. Inspections are recorded on paper forms that vary by department. When a child is injured and a lawsuit follows, the city attorney's first request is "show me the inspection record for that playground on that date." In 40% of cases, the form is missing, incomplete, or illegible.

## Why It's Still Broken
Inspection is performed by maintenance crews who are already overscheduled — mowing, trash collection, graffiti removal. The paper form is an afterthought; crews sometimes complete forms from memory at the end of the week. Departments have tried general-purpose inspection apps (iAuditor, GoCanvas) but these lack playground-specific checklists aligned to ASTM standards, don't auto-schedule based on site count and crew availability, and don't generate the specific report format that insurance carriers and city attorneys require. The budget for a dedicated inspection system is $5K–$15K, which falls below most enterprise vendors' minimum deal size.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A mobile-first inspection app with pre-built ASTM F1487-compliant checklists that auto-assigns inspections to crews on a monthly rotation, requires photo documentation of each equipment piece, flags overdue sites on a supervisor dashboard, and generates insurance-grade compliance reports. GPS confirms the crew was physically at the site. Offline mode for parks without cell coverage. Price point under $200/month to fit parks budgets.

## Who Feels the Pain
Parks maintenance supervisors who are personally liable for inspection compliance, and city risk managers who settle playground injury claims averaging $50K–$300K.

## Impact If Fixed
Achieving 100% documented inspection compliance reduces lawsuit settlement exposure by 40–60%, saving a typical city $100K–$500K annually in claims. Crews save 3–5 hours per week on paperwork.
