# Parks & Recreation Departments

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $65B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 19%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Online registration platforms exist for programs, but facility maintenance, trail management, and capital planning are largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Parks & Recreation Director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Program registration and fee collection are automatable; facility scheduling and maintenance tracking have clear digital gaps.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Parks and recreation departments manage a sprawling portfolio of physical assets — playgrounds, athletic fields, trails, pools, community centers — while simultaneously running hundreds of programs (youth sports leagues, senior fitness, summer camps, after-school care). The operational complexity rivals a mid-size hospitality business, but budgets are set annually by council vote and staff are generalists who rotate between mowing fields and coaching basketball. Revenue generation through program fees and facility rentals is increasingly important as tax-based funding tightens, yet most departments lack the tools to optimize pricing or track utilization.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CivicRec, ActiveNet (now ACTIVE Network), and RecDesk handle program registration and payment. But none of these platforms manage the physical asset side: playground equipment inspections, field condition tracking, irrigation scheduling, or tree inventories. Maintenance requests come in via phone, email, or in-person complaints with no ticketing workflow. The result is two parallel operational worlds — programs and facilities — managed with different tools (or no tools) by the same understaffed team.
