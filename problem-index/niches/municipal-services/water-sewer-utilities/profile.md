# Water & Sewer Utilities

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $120B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Larger utilities have SCADA and GIS; smaller ones rely on paper maps and manual meter reads.
**Target Buyer:** Utility Director / Public Works Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Meter reading, leak detection, billing, and compliance reporting are all rule-driven and high-volume.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Water and sewer utilities are the largest single revenue-generating operation in most municipalities, yet they operate on aging infrastructure with decades-old asset records. Unlike electric utilities, water systems lack real-time consumption visibility in most jurisdictions. The workforce skews older, with institutional knowledge about pipe locations, valve configurations, and system quirks concentrated in a handful of senior operators who are approaching retirement. Regulatory compliance (EPA Safe Drinking Water Act, Clean Water Act) creates enormous documentation overhead that is still largely manual.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Enterprise platforms like Tyler Technologies Munis and CentralSquare handle billing but not field operations. SCADA systems monitor pressure and flow at treatment plants but don't extend to the distribution network. GIS tools like Esri exist but most small-to-mid utilities haven't digitized their asset maps, creating a gap between what leadership thinks they have and what's actually in the ground.
