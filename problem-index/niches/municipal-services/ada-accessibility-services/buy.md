# Sidewalk and Curb Ramp Assessment with Accessibility Scoring

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/ada-accessibility-services/profile|ADA Accessibility Services]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Pavement management tools like StreetSaver and Cartegraph assess road conditions but don't score sidewalks and curb ramps for ADA compliance — leaving the highest-volume accessibility barrier untracked.
**Tags:** #cnn #object-detection #computer-vision #compliance #automation

## The Problem
Sidewalks and curb ramps are the single largest category of ADA complaints against municipalities. A cracked, heaved, or narrow sidewalk forces wheelchair users into the street. A missing or non-compliant curb ramp makes an entire block impassable. Most cities have thousands of sidewalk segments and hundreds of curb ramps, and they have no inventory of which ones meet ADA standards (slope, cross-slope, width, surface condition, detectable warning presence). When a complaint is filed, staff drive to the location, measure with a level, and write a report — reactive and one-at-a-time.

## What Already Exists
Cartegraph and StreetSaver assess pavement condition for roads using visual inspection and rating scales. GIS platforms (Esri) can map sidewalk networks. Specialty firms like Pathway Services use surveying equipment to assess sidewalks but charge $5–$15 per ramp for manual measurement. None of these tools combine automated condition assessment with ADA-specific compliance scoring (slope measurements, width calculations, surface condition grading) in a single workflow.

## The Customization Gap
A vertical solution needs to: (1) use vehicle-mounted cameras or smartphone-based imaging to capture sidewalk and ramp conditions at scale, (2) auto-measure cross-slope and running slope from imagery or LiDAR, (3) score each ramp against ADAAG requirements (max 8.33% slope, detectable warning presence, landing dimensions), (4) integrate with the municipality's GIS for spatial visualization, and (5) prioritize remediation by pedestrian traffic volume, proximity to transit stops, schools, and medical facilities. This requires understanding of both pavement engineering and ADA regulatory requirements that neither road management nor general GIS vendors currently combine.

## Target Customer
Public works directors and ADA coordinators at cities with 20,000+ residents managing 100+ miles of sidewalk network.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-ramp assessment cost from $10–$15 (manual surveying) to $1–$3 (automated imaging). Enables citywide ADA sidewalk inventory in weeks instead of years. Prioritized remediation prevents $50K–$500K per ADA lawsuit settlement — the average municipal ADA sidewalk settlement is $150K.
