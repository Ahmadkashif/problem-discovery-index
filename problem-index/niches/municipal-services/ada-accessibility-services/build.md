# Municipal ADA Transition Plan Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/municipal-services/ada-accessibility-services/profile|ADA Accessibility Services]]
**Industry:** [[industries/municipal-services|Municipal Services]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No self-service platform helps municipalities create, track, and update ADA transition plans — the legally required roadmap for making all public facilities accessible.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #data-integration #ai-platform

## The Problem
Every municipality with 50+ employees is required to maintain an ADA transition plan that identifies physical barriers in all public facilities, prioritizes removal, and sets a remediation schedule. Creating a transition plan requires surveying every building entrance, restroom, parking lot, sidewalk segment, and program space for compliance with ADA Accessibility Guidelines (ADAAG). Most municipalities either have no plan (risking DOJ enforcement action) or have a plan created 10+ years ago that has never been updated. When a complaint is filed or a lawsuit threatens, the city scrambles to produce documentation that doesn't exist.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
ADA consulting firms prefer to sell $50K–$200K assessment engagements rather than self-service tools that cannibalize their consulting revenue. The assessment methodology requires domain expertise in ADAAG standards that software engineers don't have. The market is also fragmented: 19,000 municipalities, each with different facility inventories and political willingness to fund accessibility improvements. And the "buyer" — typically an ADA coordinator who is also the HR director — has no software budget line item.

## What to Build
A SaaS platform with: (1) a mobile survey app with pre-built ADAAG checklists for building types (offices, parks, pools, parking lots), photo documentation, and GPS tagging, (2) an auto-generated transition plan document that meets DOJ expectations, (3) a project tracker connecting identified barriers to capital improvement budgets with prioritization logic (high-use facilities first, safety hazards first), (4) a grievance intake form that links complaints to specific facilities in the plan, and (5) an annual progress report generator for council and public posting. Templates for the most common facility types reduce survey time by 60% compared to blank-sheet assessments.

## Target Customer
ADA coordinators, city managers, and risk managers at municipalities with 5,000–100,000 residents who need to create or update their transition plan.

## Impact If Built
Reduces transition plan creation cost from $75K–$200K (consultant-led) to $5K–$15K (self-service with platform). Cuts DOJ enforcement exposure for the estimated 40% of municipalities operating without a current plan. Accelerates barrier removal timelines by 30–50% through structured project tracking.
