**Target Buyer:** ADA Coordinator / City Manager / Risk Manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Self-evaluation surveys, transition plan tracking, and grievance management are structured processes that can be automated.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act requires every state and local government to ensure that its programs, services, and facilities are accessible to people with disabilities. In practice, most municipalities under 50,000 population have no dedicated ADA coordinator — the responsibility is assigned to an HR generalist, city clerk, or risk manager as a secondary duty. Self-evaluation surveys (required every 3–5 years) are massive undertakings involving physical assessment of every public facility, sidewalk, curb ramp, and program. Transition plans documenting remediation timelines gather dust in filing cabinets. The population served — 61 million Americans with disabilities — has among the highest dissatisfaction rates with local government services.

## Current Tools & Gaps
There is no dominant ADA compliance software for municipalities. Some use general accessibility audit tools (AXE for websites, TPGi for documents) but these cover only digital accessibility. Physical accessibility — sidewalk conditions, building entrances, park amenities, public meeting accommodations — is tracked in spreadsheets or not at all. The few specialty tools that exist (CityAXIS, ADAag.com) are consulting-service wrappers, not self-service platforms. Grievance tracking uses generic complaint forms with no connection to the facility inventory or transition plan.
