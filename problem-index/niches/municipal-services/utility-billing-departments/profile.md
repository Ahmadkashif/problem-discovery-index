## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Municipal utility billing departments handle water, sewer, stormwater, and sometimes trash collection billing for every household and business in the jurisdiction. Unlike private utilities, municipal billing must navigate politically sensitive rate structures (council-approved), hardship programs, lien and shutoff policies with due process requirements, and public records requests. Billing clerks spend enormous time on exceptions: estimated reads, leak adjustments, vacancy credits, final bills, and billing disputes. Each exception requires manual intervention because the billing software treats every account as a standard monthly cycle.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Tyler Technologies Munis, Harris Northstar, and CentralSquare handle core billing — rate tables, meter read import, bill generation, payment posting. But the exception workflows are manual: a clerk receives a dispute call, pulls up the account, reviews read history, calculates an adjustment, gets supervisor approval, posts the credit, generates a revised bill, and mails it. Each step is a separate screen or paper form. Delinquency management is particularly manual — state-specific shutoff notice requirements (timing, delivery method, appeal rights) mean that the clerk must know the law and manually track compliance dates.
