# Infestation Diagnosis Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Pest diagnosis is entirely tacit knowledge today. No digital tools assist with infestation identification from environmental evidence.
**Target Buyer:** Pest control operators seeking to codify expert diagnostic knowledge and scale it across junior technicians.
**Automation Potential:** High — Expert pest identification from environmental signs follows learnable visual and contextual patterns that are prime candidates for ML.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
This niche represents the highest tacit-knowledge ML opportunity in pest control. Experienced technicians diagnose infestations without seeing the pest — they read environmental evidence: frass patterns indicate species (termite frass is hexagonal pellets, carpenter ant frass contains insect body parts), gnaw marks indicate rodent species (rats gnaw in streaks, mice leave finer marks), grease rub marks along baseboards indicate rodent travel paths, moisture patterns suggest conducive conditions for specific pests. A 20-year tech walks into a basement and knows "German cockroach colony behind the water heater" before opening a single cabinet. This pattern recognition takes 3-5 years to develop and is the primary bottleneck to scaling a pest control business.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No diagnostic tools exist for pest identification from environmental evidence. Pest ID apps (iNaturalist, PictureInsect) identify live insects from photos but don't diagnose infestations from evidence (frass, damage, tracks). Training is apprenticeship-based — junior techs ride along with seniors for months, absorbing pattern recognition through exposure.
