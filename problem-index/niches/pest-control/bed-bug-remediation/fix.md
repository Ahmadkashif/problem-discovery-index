# Tenant Preparation Failure

**Niche:** [[niches/pest-control/bed-bug-remediation/profile|Bed Bug Remediation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Technicians arrive for a $2,000 heat treatment and the tenant hasn't prepared — heat-sensitive items are still in the room, furniture isn't moved, and the treatment must be rescheduled.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Bed bug treatments require extensive tenant preparation: remove candles, medications, aerosol cans, plants, and pets; launder all bedding and clothing in hot water; move furniture away from walls; empty dresser drawers. Property managers and tenants receive preparation checklists, but 20-30% of appointments arrive to find the unit unprepared. Each failed start costs the company $300-$500 (truck roll, equipment setup/teardown, crew time) and delays treatment by days while the infestation worsens.

## Why It's Still Broken
Preparation checklists are sent via email or paper — once. There's no verification before the crew arrives. Tenants in distress over bed bugs are overwhelmed and procrastinate preparation. Property managers distribute checklists but don't verify compliance. The pest control company has no way to confirm preparation status before dispatching a crew with $50,000 in heat treatment equipment.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A multi-touchpoint preparation workflow: automated checklist delivery 7 days, 3 days, and 1 day before treatment via text/email. Photo-verified preparation check 24 hours before — the tenant submits photos of key areas (beds stripped, furniture moved, items removed) through a simple mobile interface. If photos show inadequate preparation, the system alerts the tenant, the property manager, and the pest company. Treatment is only confirmed when photo verification passes. For same-day issues, the crew receives a "preparation incomplete" warning before dispatching.

## Who Feels the Pain
Bed bug treatment technicians who drive 45 minutes to a job site with a trailer of heat equipment only to turn around, and company owners eating $300-$500 per failed start 2-3 times per month.

## Impact If Fixed
Reducing failed starts from 25% to 5% on 20 monthly treatments saves $4,000-$8,000/month in wasted labor and equipment deployment, plus accelerates treatment timelines for suffering tenants.
