# Bed Bug Remediation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most bed bug specialists use phone scheduling, paper inspection reports, and manual heat treatment monitoring. No vertical software exists for this specialty.
**Target Buyer:** Bed bug remediation specialists serving property managers, hotels, and residential customers.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Heat treatment monitoring is sensor-automatable; canine inspection scheduling and property manager coordination are workflow-automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Bed bug remediation is the highest-stakes, highest-price-point specialty in pest control. A single-room heat treatment costs $1,000-$3,000; a whole-unit chemical treatment costs $500-$1,500. The work is forensic — technicians must identify infestation extent (which rooms, which furniture, adjacent units), determine treatment method (heat vs. chemical vs. integrated), and coordinate with property managers on tenant preparation (removing heat-sensitive items, laundering bedding). Multi-unit buildings create cascade problems: treating one apartment while an adjacent infested unit goes untreated guarantees reinfestation. The emotional intensity is extreme — bed bug customers are distressed, sleep-deprived, and demand immediate response.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Generic pest control platforms (PestPac, FieldRoutes) handle scheduling but have no bed-bug-specific workflows: multi-room treatment planning, heat treatment temperature monitoring, canine inspection scheduling, tenant preparation checklists, or multi-unit building management. Most bed bug specialists track jobs on paper or in generic spreadsheets.
