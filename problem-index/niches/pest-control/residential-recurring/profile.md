# General Residential Pest Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Online booking and recurring billing are common, but treatment history tracking, seasonal protocol automation, and customer education are underdeveloped.
**Target Buyer:** Residential pest control company owners running quarterly/monthly subscription programs.
**Automation Potential:** High — Recurring service schedules, seasonal treatment protocols, and customer communications are highly rule-based.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
General residential pest control is a subscription business — customers pay $40-$80/month or $100-$200/quarter for regular perimeter treatments, interior sprays, and on-call service for breakthrough pest activity. Revenue is predictable but churn is significant (25-40% annually), driven by perceived lack of value ("I haven't seen any bugs, why am I paying?"). The work is routine: technicians follow seasonal protocols (ant/spider focus in spring, rodent focus in fall, mosquito in summer) with standardized product applications. The competitive landscape is brutal — customers compare on price and switch for $10/month savings.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PestPac, PestRoutes (now FieldRoutes), and Briostack handle scheduling, routing, and billing. These platforms are strong on operational logistics but weak on customer retention analytics, treatment effectiveness tracking, and value communication. No platform helps operators answer: "Which customers are about to churn, and what intervention prevents it?"
