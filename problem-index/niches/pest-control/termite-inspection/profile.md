# Termite Inspection & Treatment

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — WDO (Wood Destroying Organism) inspection reports are partially digitized, but damage assessment, treatment planning, and warranty tracking remain largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Termite-focused pest control operators and companies serving real estate transaction inspections.
**Automation Potential:** High — Inspection reports follow standardized state forms, damage patterns are visually classifiable, and warranty renewals are rule-based.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Termite work is unlike any other pest control service. It's deeply tied to real estate transactions — most states require a WDO inspection before property sale. The work splits into two revenue streams: one-time inspections (high volume, fast turnaround, tight real estate closing deadlines) and treatment/warranty programs (long-tail recurring revenue with annual renewals). Inspectors must identify subtle evidence of termite activity — mud tubes, frass patterns, wood damage, moisture staining — often in crawl spaces and attics with limited visibility. Treatment involves precise chemical application or bait station placement based on construction type, soil conditions, and infestation patterns. The stakes are high: a missed termite colony can cause $30,000+ in structural damage.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PEST (Pesticide Environmental Stewardship Tracker), PestPac, and ServSuite handle general pest control operations. WDO inspection report apps like HomeGauge and Spectora serve home inspectors but lack pest-specific treatment planning. No platform connects the full termite lifecycle: inspection findings to treatment design to warranty tracking to annual renewal management.
