# Warranty Renewal Revenue Leakage

**Niche:** [[niches/pest-control/termite-inspection/profile|Termite Inspection & Treatment]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Termite companies lose 20-30% of annual warranty renewals because tracking and outreach is manual — customers simply forget to renew and coverage lapses.
**Tags:** #automation #revenue-impact #data-integration

## The Problem
Termite treatment warranties are a high-margin recurring revenue stream — customers pay $150-$400 annually for continued coverage after the initial treatment ($1,000-$3,000). But renewal tracking is a mess. Warranty start dates, renewal dates, and property details are stored in spreadsheets, filing cabinets, or fragmented CRM entries. Renewal notices go out late (or not at all), follow-up is sporadic, and customers who don't renew simply fall off the radar. A company with 1,000 active warranties losing 25% annually is leaving $50,000-$100,000 on the table.

## Why It's Still Broken
Warranties span years (some are "lifetime" with annual renewals), and pest control CRMs weren't built for long-tail subscription management. The data entry challenge is real — technicians fill out paper warranty certificates in the field, and office staff must manually enter them into whatever tracking system exists. Properties change ownership (triggering warranty transfer negotiations), and there's no automated way to track real estate transactions against the warranty database.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated warranty lifecycle management system: warranty creation auto-populates from treatment records, renewal notices go out 60/30/7 days before expiration via email and text, non-renewals trigger a phone call task for the sales team, and property ownership changes (detected via public records or MLS data) trigger transfer outreach to the new owner. Dashboard shows total warranty book value, renewal rate trend, and at-risk warranties.

## Who Feels the Pain
Pest control company owners watching their warranty renewal rate drop from 80% to 55% over 5 years due to lack of systematic follow-up, leaving $40,000-$150,000 in annual revenue on the table.

## Impact If Fixed
Improving warranty renewal rate from 60% to 80% on a 1,000-warranty book at $250 average renewal adds $50,000 in annual high-margin recurring revenue.
