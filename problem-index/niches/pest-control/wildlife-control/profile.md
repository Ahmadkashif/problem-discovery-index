# Wildlife & Animal Control

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most wildlife operators run on phone calls, paper permits, and manual trap-check scheduling. No vertical software exists.
**Target Buyer:** Wildlife removal operators handling nuisance animal trapping, exclusion, and damage repair.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Permit tracking, trap-check scheduling, and customer documentation are automatable; animal handling and exclusion work require hands-on expertise.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wildlife control (raccoons, squirrels, bats, snakes, skunks, birds) is regulated differently than pest control — it falls under state wildlife agency jurisdiction, not EPA pesticide regulation. Operators need specific trapping licenses, must follow species-specific regulations (release requirements, euthanasia rules, endangered species protections), and often perform structural repairs (exclusion work) that require contractor skills. The work is unpredictable: a raccoon in an attic might take one trap-check or ten. Revenue comes from emergency calls (high price, low volume) and exclusion contracts (preventive sealing of entry points).

## Current Tools & Gaps
There is essentially no vertical software for wildlife control. Operators use paper permits, whiteboard scheduling for trap-check routes, and handwritten invoices. Generic field service tools don't handle species-specific regulations, mandatory trap-check intervals (many states require daily checks), or the photo documentation needed for insurance claims and permit compliance.
