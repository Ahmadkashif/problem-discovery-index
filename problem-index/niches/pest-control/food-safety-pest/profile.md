**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Pest control companies serving food facilities use standard platforms, but the audit-ready documentation, sanitation correlation, and regulatory compliance needs of food-service clients are not addressed.
**Target Buyer:** Pest control companies specializing in restaurants, food processing plants, and grocery/food retail requiring compliance with FDA, health department, and third-party audit standards.
**Automation Potential:** High — Audit documentation, service frequency compliance, and trending reports for food safety audits are highly automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Food facility pest management operates under a different compliance framework than residential or commercial pest control. FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA), third-party audits (AIB, SQF, BRC), and local health department inspections all require documented pest management programs with trending analysis, corrective actions, and root cause documentation. A cockroach sighting in a restaurant can trigger a health department closure. Pest companies serving this niche must provide not just treatment but data — trending reports, activity maps, corrective action logs, and audit-ready documentation packages.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PestPac and FieldRoutes handle service scheduling and basic documentation. RollCall and iQ from Rentokil offer some food safety reporting but are proprietary to those companies. Independent pest companies serving food facilities generate audit reports manually — pulling data from their CRM into spreadsheets and creating trending charts in Excel. No independent platform provides the audit-ready reporting package that food facility managers need.
