# Corrective Action Loop Failure

**Niche:** [[niches/pest-control/food-safety-pest/profile|Food Safety & Restaurant Pest Services]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pest-control|Pest Control]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Pest technicians recommend sanitation improvements to food facility clients, but there's no system to track whether recommendations are implemented — and auditors check.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
During service visits, pest technicians identify conditions that contribute to pest activity: food debris under equipment, damaged door sweeps, standing water near drains, gaps in exterior walls. They document these as "corrective action recommendations" on service reports. The problem: there's no follow-up system. The recommendation sits in a service report PDF that the facility manager may never read. Three months later, the auditor asks "what corrective actions were recommended and what was the result?" — and neither the pest company nor the facility can answer. The auditor scores this as a non-conformance.

## Why It's Still Broken
Corrective actions cross organizational boundaries — the pest company recommends, the facility must act, and the pest company must verify. Service reports are delivered as PDFs or paper copies with no structured tracking. The pest technician who made the recommendation may not return to that facility (different route assignment), and the new technician doesn't know what was previously recommended. There's no shared accountability system between pest company and client.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A shared corrective action tracker accessible to both the pest company and the facility manager. When a technician documents a finding (with photo), it creates a tracked corrective action with severity, recommended fix, and deadline. The facility manager receives a notification and can update status (acknowledged, scheduled, completed with photo verification). The pest technician verifies completion on the next visit. Unresolved actions escalate automatically. Audit reports show the full lifecycle: finding → recommendation → implementation → verification.

## Who Feels the Pain
Pest company account managers who lose food facility contracts when audits reveal unresolved corrective actions, and facility managers who get non-conformance findings because they never saw the recommendation buried in a service report.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces audit non-conformance findings by 40-60%, protects the pest company's food facility contracts (worth $5,000-$50,000/year each), and strengthens the client relationship by demonstrating proactive pest management rather than reactive treatment.
