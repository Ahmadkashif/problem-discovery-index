# Timezone-Aware Training Scheduler with Equity Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/corporate-training/remote-distributed-teams/profile|Remote & Distributed Team Training]]
**Industry:** [[industries/corporate-training|Corporate Training]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Calendar scheduling tools like Calendly and When2meet handle meeting coordination, but they don't track the cumulative timezone burden on remote employees — the same APAC team members always get the 9 PM training sessions.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
When L&D schedules live virtual training across timezones, they typically pick times that work for headquarters (US Eastern or US Pacific), forcing EMEA employees to attend in the evening and APAC employees to attend late at night or early morning. Over a year, the cumulative inconvenience falls disproportionately on the same employee populations — creating an equity issue that mirrors the broader power dynamics of distributed organizations. No scheduling tool tracks this cumulative burden or rotates inconvenient session times across regions.

## What Already Exists
Calendly, Doodle, When2meet, and Microsoft Bookings handle timezone-aware scheduling for individual meetings. World Time Buddy and similar tools help visualize timezone overlaps. LMS platforms like Cornerstone allow scheduling virtual ILT sessions. None track the historical distribution of timezone burden across employee populations.

## The Customization Gap
A training session scheduler that: (1) optimizes live virtual training times to minimize total timezone burden across participants (not just default to headquarters time); (2) tracks cumulative inconvenience scores per employee and per region over rolling 6-month windows; (3) rotates inconvenient session times across regions for recurring training (Q1: APAC gets the bad slot; Q2: EMEA; Q3: Americas); (4) provides L&D with an equity dashboard showing timezone burden distribution; and (5) offers hybrid scheduling that provides live attendance options at optimal times per region plus async catch-up for missed sessions.

## Target Customer
L&D Managers at companies with 200+ employees across AMER, EMEA, and APAC who run 10+ live virtual training sessions per quarter and have received feedback about timezone inequity.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces timezone-driven training absenteeism by 30-40% (employees currently skip sessions scheduled at inconvenient hours), improves perceived equity across regions (measurable via engagement survey scores), and gives L&D quantitative visibility into a previously invisible source of distributed team frustration.
