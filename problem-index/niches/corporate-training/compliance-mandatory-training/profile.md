# Compliance & Mandatory Training

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/corporate-training|Corporate Training]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — SAI360, NAVEX Global, and Traliant deliver compliance e-learning, but tracking completion across business entities, verifying comprehension beyond quiz scores, and mapping training to regulatory requirements remains manual
**Target Buyer:** Chief Compliance Officer or VP of HR at regulated companies (financial services, healthcare, manufacturing)
**Automation Potential:** High — compliance training follows regulatory calendars with defined populations, mandated content, and auditable completion records

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Compliance training is the one training domain where incompletion has direct legal and financial consequences. OSHA, HIPAA, SOX, AML, and industry-specific regulations mandate specific training for specific employee populations on specific schedules. A missed training completion is not just a development gap — it is an audit finding that can trigger fines ($100,000+ per violation in some regulatory domains). This creates a unique operational pressure: the L&D team must guarantee 100% completion across thousands of employees across multiple entities, often under different regulatory frameworks. The content itself is secondary to the tracking, documentation, and audit-readiness infrastructure.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SAI360, NAVEX Global, Traliant, and Skillsoft provide compliance training content libraries. LMS platforms track completions. The gap is multi-entity regulatory mapping: which regulations apply to which employees in which jurisdictions, what training satisfies each requirement, when it must be renewed, and whether the current completion state puts the organization at audit risk. Companies with operations in multiple states or countries face exponential complexity that no compliance training platform handles natively.
