# Assessment & Certification Grading Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/corporate-training|Corporate Training]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — multiple-choice assessments are auto-graded by LMS platforms, but constructed-response assessments (scenario-based questions, written analyses, video submissions) require manual SME grading at $50-150/hour
**Target Buyer:** L&D Operations Manager or Instructional Design Lead managing certification programs with 1,000+ annual test-takers
**Automation Potential:** High — grading rubrics are structured and codifiable, and the highest grading volume comes from standardized assessments with known correct-answer patterns

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate certification programs (internal promotions, professional development certifications, regulatory qualifications) increasingly use constructed-response assessments to evaluate applied competency rather than just knowledge recall. A customer service certification requires the learner to write a response to a difficult customer scenario. A leadership development program requires a written case analysis. A technical certification requires a video demonstration of a procedure. These assessments cannot be auto-graded by traditional LMS quiz engines and require subject matter expert (SME) evaluation using a rubric — a manual process that creates a grading bottleneck, delays certification decisions, and costs $50-150 per assessment per SME-hour.

## Current Tools & Gaps
LMS platforms auto-grade multiple-choice, matching, and fill-in-the-blank assessments. Turnitin provides plagiarism detection and some automated writing feedback for academic contexts. Gradescope (Turnitin) handles rubric-based grading for academic assignments. None of these tools are configured for corporate certification use cases — corporate rubrics, corporate scenario contexts, and corporate competency frameworks.
