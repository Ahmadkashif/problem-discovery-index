# Employee Onboarding Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/corporate-training|Corporate Training]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12-15%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — BambooHR, Workday, and Rippling handle HR onboarding paperwork; Lessonly and WorkRamp handle training content; but the full onboarding journey (IT provisioning, manager meetings, buddy assignments, 30/60/90-day milestones, system access, role-specific training paths) remains a manual, cross-departmental coordination effort
**Target Buyer:** HR Director or Onboarding Program Manager at companies hiring 100+ employees per year
**Automation Potential:** High — onboarding follows a predictable sequence of tasks owned by multiple departments (HR, IT, L&D, hiring manager) with defined triggers and deadlines

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Employee onboarding is the most cross-departmental process in any company: HR handles paperwork and benefits enrollment, IT provisions equipment and system access, L&D assigns training curricula, the hiring manager sets up initial projects and introductions, and a buddy or mentor provides social integration. Each department uses its own system and timeline. The result is that new hires frequently show up on Day 1 without a laptop, without system access, without a training plan, and with a manager who "didn't know you were starting today." The first-week experience drives 1-year retention: research shows that a structured onboarding program improves new hire retention by 82%.

## Current Tools & Gaps
BambooHR and Rippling automate HR paperwork (I-9, W-4, benefits enrollment). WorkRamp, Lessonly, and Trainual handle onboarding training content. Notion and Confluence host onboarding wikis. None of these tools orchestrate the full cross-departmental workflow — the HR Director maintains a checklist in a spreadsheet or project management tool (Asana, Monday.com) to track whether each department has completed their onboarding tasks for each new hire.
