# Small Business Compliance Health Check Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/small-business-compliance/profile|Small Business General Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** An automated assessment that takes a small business's profile — employee count, industry, state, city, and operational activities — and produces a complete compliance checklist of every federal, state, and local regulation that applies, with specific requirements, deadlines, and gap identification against the business's current practices.
**Tags:** #decision-trees #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #data-integration #compliance #automation #revenue-impact #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
A small business owner who opens a 30-employee light manufacturing facility in Austin, Texas, is subject to: federal OSHA general industry standards, Texas workers' compensation reporting (voluntary but practically required), ADA Title III for their customer-facing areas, FMLA (no — under 50 employees), Texas Payday Law, Texas unemployment insurance, I-9 for all employees, Austin's paid sick leave ordinance (if enacted), EPA small quantity generator requirements (if they produce hazardous waste), TCEQ stormwater permits (if they have industrial runoff), FDA registration (if they handle food-contact materials), and a dozen more depending on specific activities. No one — not the business owner, not their accountant, not even a generalist lawyer — knows all of these off the top of their head. An experienced compliance consultant has tacit knowledge built from hundreds of engagements: they see "30 employees, light manufacturing, Austin" and immediately start a mental checklist that a less experienced consultant would miss items on. This pattern recognition — knowing that a Texas manufacturer with 25+ employees probably needs a Hazard Communication program, that Austin has specific ADA enforcement, that the facility's proximity to a waterway triggers stormwater requirements — comes from years of experience and isn't codified anywhere.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The combinatorial complexity is daunting. There are approximately 180,000 federal regulatory requirements (per the Competitive Enterprise Institute), 50 states with different employment, environmental, and safety regulations, and thousands of local ordinances. No single database encodes all of these in a machine-queryable format with applicability criteria. Building the applicability engine requires: (1) a structured database of regulations with applicability triggers (employee count thresholds, industry codes, activity types, geographic scope), (2) a business profiling intake that captures enough detail to evaluate triggers without requiring the owner to understand regulatory terminology, and (3) continuous maintenance as regulations change. The federal and state regulatory databases are published in different formats with different update frequencies, and local ordinances are often not digitized at all. Experienced consultants compensate for this gap with memory and professional networks, but their knowledge doesn't scale.

## What to Build
A SaaS compliance health check platform used by consultants as their initial engagement tool. The consultant enters the client's business profile through a guided intake: NAICS code, employee count, state and city, specific activities (food handling, chemical storage, heavy equipment, customer-facing operations, federal contracts, etc.). A decision-tree model enriched with gradient-boosted classification evaluates the profile against a structured regulatory database and produces a prioritized checklist of all applicable regulations, organized by domain (employment, safety, environmental, industry-specific, local). Each regulation links to its specific requirements, key deadlines, and common compliance gaps for businesses of this type and size. The system captures the experienced consultant's tacit knowledge by learning from completed assessments — when a consultant adds a regulation that the system missed, or removes one the system incorrectly included, the model improves its applicability logic. Over time, the system develops the pattern recognition that currently takes 5-10 years of consulting experience to build.

## Target Customer
Generalist compliance consultants and fractional compliance officers serving small businesses with 10-100 employees, currently spending 8-15 hours on initial compliance applicability assessments for each new client, willing to pay $200-400/month for a platform that produces a first-draft assessment in minutes.

## Impact If Built
Reduces initial compliance applicability assessment from 8-15 hours to 1-2 hours of review and refinement, allowing a solo consultant to onboard 3-4x more clients per month. More importantly, the structured database catches regulations that even experienced consultants miss — reducing the risk of a client receiving a citation for a requirement the consultant didn't identify. For the small business owner, the health check provides clarity that currently doesn't exist: a single document listing everything they need to comply with, rather than the anxiety of not knowing what they don't know.
