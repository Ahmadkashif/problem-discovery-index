# Compliance Applicability from HR Platform Extensions

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/small-business-compliance/profile|Small Business General Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** HR platforms like Gusto and Rippling already know every small business's employee count, state, and payroll structure — but they only handle employment tax and benefits compliance, missing OSHA, ADA, environmental, and industry-specific requirements that affect the same businesses.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Modern HR platforms have remarkable data density about small businesses. Gusto knows the company has 37 employees in Texas and California, pays overtime to 22 of them, offers health insurance, and processes bi-weekly payroll. Rippling knows the company uses AWS, runs Slack, and has employee devices with specific OS versions. This data is sufficient to determine applicability for dozens of regulations beyond payroll tax — FMLA (50+ employees), ACA employer mandate (50+ full-time equivalent employees), COBRA (20+ employees), state-specific harassment training requirements, OSHA injury reporting thresholds — but the platforms only use it for their core employment compliance features. The small business owner using Gusto for payroll has no idea that Gusto could theoretically tell them they need a California Injury and Illness Prevention Program because they have employees in California, or that they need an OSHA 300 log because they have 11+ employees in a non-exempt industry.

## What Already Exists
Gusto provides payroll tax compliance, new hire reporting, workers' comp policy integration, and basic compliance alerts (minimum wage changes, poster requirements). Rippling adds IT compliance (device management, security policies) and benefits compliance. BambooHR handles HR documentation and some compliance tracking. Mineral (formerly ThinkHR/Mammoth) offers a compliance library and live advisor access, often bundled with HR platforms. None of these extend beyond employment and benefits compliance to cover OSHA workplace safety, ADA physical accessibility, environmental requirements, or industry-specific regulations.

## The Customization Gap
HR platforms already have the data needed to assess compliance applicability across a much broader regulatory scope than they currently address. The customization gap is extending the compliance engine beyond employment law to include: (1) OSHA requirements mapped to industry (NAICS code) and employee count, including specific standards like Hazard Communication, Lockout/Tagout, and Respiratory Protection that apply based on workplace activities, (2) ADA Title III accessibility requirements for customer-facing businesses, (3) basic environmental applicability (does this business generate hazardous waste, handle food, or operate near waterways?), and (4) industry-specific requirements (FDA registration for food/cosmetics, DOT requirements for commercial vehicles, state licensing requirements for regulated professions). The platform already knows the company's size, location, and industry — it just needs a broader regulatory applicability engine and a compliance consultant partnership model where consultants are the service layer for requirements the platform identifies but can't fulfill.

## Target Customer
Compliance consultants who want to generate leads from small businesses already using HR platforms, and HR platform vendors looking to expand their compliance value proposition beyond employment law.

## Impact If Solved
Transforms HR platforms from payroll compliance tools into comprehensive small business compliance hubs, extending their value proposition and reducing churn. For the small business owner, the platform that already handles their payroll now also tells them about their OSHA, ADA, and environmental obligations — eliminating the discovery gap that currently results in citations and complaints. For compliance consultants, platform integration creates a lead generation channel: the platform identifies requirements, the consultant fulfills them.
