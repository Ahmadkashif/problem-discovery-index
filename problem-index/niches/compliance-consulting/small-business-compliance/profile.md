# Small Business General Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most small businesses have no compliance tracking system at all; requirements are discovered reactively when an inspector shows up or an employee files a complaint
**Target Buyer:** Consultant or fractional compliance officer serving businesses with 10-100 employees across retail, services, light manufacturing, and professional services
**Automation Potential:** High — determining which regulations apply to a specific small business is a structured decision tree based on employee count, industry, location, and activities; the applicability assessment alone is worth automating

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small businesses with 10-100 employees face a compliance burden that is invisible until it isn't. OSHA applies once you have one employee. ADA Title III applies to public accommodations regardless of size. The FMLA kicks in at 50 employees. State-specific employment laws vary wildly — California requires sexual harassment training at 5 employees, New York at 1 employee, most states have no requirement. Worker's compensation requirements vary by state, industry, and employee count. Poster requirements change annually. I-9 compliance is universal but audited unpredictably. The business owner has no idea which of these apply, no system for tracking them, and no budget for a compliance department. They hire a compliance consultant or fractional compliance officer when something goes wrong — an OSHA citation, a wage-and-hour complaint, or a failed workers' comp audit. The consultant's first task at every new engagement is the same: figure out which regulations apply to this specific business based on its size, industry, location, and activities. This applicability assessment takes 8-15 hours and is repeated from scratch for each client because no tool produces it automatically.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HR platforms (Gusto, Rippling, BambooHR) handle payroll tax compliance, benefits administration, and some employment law posting requirements, but they don't cover OSHA, ADA, environmental, or industry-specific regulations. ComplyRight and J.J. Keller offer compliance poster kits and training content but don't assess which regulations apply to a specific business. SixFifty generates employment law documents but doesn't track ongoing compliance obligations. The gap is an applicability engine — a tool that takes a business profile (industry NAICS code, employee count, state/city, activities like food handling or chemical storage) and produces a complete list of applicable federal, state, and local regulations with their specific requirements, deadlines, and ongoing obligations. No such tool exists for the generalist compliance consultant.
