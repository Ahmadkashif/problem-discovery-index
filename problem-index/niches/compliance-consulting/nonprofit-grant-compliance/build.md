# Grant Deliverable Tracker with Compliance Evidence Mapping

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/nonprofit-grant-compliance/profile|Nonprofit & Grant Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A platform that maps every grant's deliverables, financial requirements, and reporting obligations to specific evidence sources in the nonprofit's systems — providing a live compliance dashboard that shows exactly what's been delivered, what's been spent, and what evidence has been collected versus what's still missing.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #data-integration #compliance #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A nonprofit with 5 active federal and state grants is juggling 5 sets of deliverables, 5 budget tracking requirements, 5 reporting schedules, and 5 sets of compliance documentation requirements. Grant A requires quarterly service delivery counts from the case management system plus staff time-and-effort certifications from HR. Grant B requires participant demographics from the intake database plus expenditure reports in a specific federal format. Grant C requires training completion records from the LMS plus match documentation showing in-kind contributions. The compliance consultant must track all of these simultaneously, alert the nonprofit when deliverables are falling behind, ensure evidence is being collected as services are delivered (not retroactively assembled at reporting time), and reconcile financial spending against budget categories. Currently, this tracking lives in a master spreadsheet with one tab per grant, maintained by the consultant and the nonprofit's grants manager through weekly email exchanges and monthly check-in meetings. Deliverable shortfalls are discovered at reporting time rather than in real time, evidence gaps surface during audit preparation rather than during service delivery, and budget overspending in one category is caught when the money is already gone.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The challenge is heterogeneity. Every funder has different deliverable definitions, different reporting formats, different budget category structures, and different evidence requirements. A platform that tracks grant compliance must be configurable per grant, per funder, per program — and nonprofits change their grant portfolio annually as awards begin and end. Building a rigid system that handles HHS grants but not DOL grants or state grants creates incomplete coverage. Building a flexible system that handles any grant requires a configuration layer that the nonprofit's grants manager (who is typically not technical) can set up and maintain. The second challenge is connecting evidence to deliverables: the evidence lives in 5-10 different systems (case management, HR, finance, LMS, program databases), and building integrations to all of them is a long-tail problem. The platform must work even when the only integration method is manual document upload.

## What to Build
A grant compliance management platform designed for the compliance consultant managing 10-20 nonprofit clients. For each client grant, the consultant configures: deliverables (with targets, reporting periods, and evidence sources), budget categories (with approved amounts and spending rules), and reporting schedule (with submission deadlines and format requirements). The platform provides a live dashboard showing deliverable progress (services delivered vs. targets), budget status (spent vs. approved by category), evidence collection status (documents collected vs. required), and upcoming reporting deadlines. For nonprofits with API-accessible systems (Salesforce, Sage Intacct), the platform pulls data automatically. For others, it provides structured upload templates. A gradient-boosted forecasting model predicts which grants are at risk of deliverable shortfall or budget overrun based on spending velocity and service delivery pace, alerting the consultant 60-90 days before the problem becomes a reporting issue. The system generates draft reporting narratives from the evidence and data it has collected, reducing report writing from 8-15 hours per grant per quarter to 2-4 hours of review and refinement.

## Target Customer
Grant compliance consultants serving nonprofits with 3+ active federal or state grants, currently tracking deliverables and evidence in spreadsheets, willing to pay $300-600/month for a platform that provides real-time compliance visibility and reduces reporting labor by 60-70%.

## Impact If Built
Transforms grant compliance from a reactive, reporting-driven activity to a proactive, continuous monitoring function. Deliverable shortfalls are identified 60-90 days before reporting deadlines, giving the nonprofit time to adjust service delivery. Evidence is collected as services are delivered, eliminating the 40-80 hour evidence scramble before each reporting period. Budget overruns are flagged when spending velocity exceeds plan, not after the money is spent. For the compliance consultant, the platform supports 2-3x more client grants without proportional staff increases, and the live dashboard creates a compelling value proposition for ongoing retainer engagements.
