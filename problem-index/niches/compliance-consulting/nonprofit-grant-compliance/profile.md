# Nonprofit & Grant Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — grant compliance tracking is almost entirely manual, with deliverables tracked in spreadsheets, evidence stored in email threads and shared drives, and financial reporting assembled from QuickBooks exports that don't map to grant cost categories
**Target Buyer:** Consultant serving nonprofits with federal and state grant compliance, particularly organizations spending $750K+ in federal funds that trigger single audit requirements
**Automation Potential:** High — grant deliverable tracking and evidence mapping are structured workflow problems, and single audit preparation follows a standardized framework (2 CFR 200 Subpart F) that lends itself to template-driven automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Nonprofit grant compliance is a specialized and underserved corner of compliance consulting. When a nonprofit receives federal or state grant funding, the money comes with strings: performance deliverables (serve X number of clients, conduct Y training sessions), financial requirements (spend funds only on approved cost categories, maintain time-and-effort documentation for personnel costs), and reporting obligations (quarterly progress reports, annual financial reports, closeout documentation). The compliance burden scales with funding complexity — a nonprofit with 3 federal grants from 2 agencies and 5 state grants has 8 different sets of deliverables, reporting schedules, and financial tracking requirements. When total federal expenditures exceed $750,000 in a fiscal year, the nonprofit triggers a single audit under 2 CFR 200 Subpart F — a full-scope financial and compliance audit that requires documented evidence for every major program. Single audit preparation is the most painful event in a nonprofit's fiscal year: the compliance consultant and finance director must assemble evidence that the organization met all grant requirements, maintained proper financial controls, and spent funds in accordance with grant terms. This evidence assembly takes 60-120 hours and the evidence lives in 5-10 different systems (case management software, HR records, financial systems, program databases, email).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Grant management platforms (Fluxx, Submittable, Instrumentl) focus on grant discovery and application, not post-award compliance. Financial systems (QuickBooks, Sage Intacct, Blackbaud Financial Edge) handle accounting but don't map expenses to grant cost categories automatically. Case management systems (Salesforce NPSP, Apricot, ETO) track program outcomes but don't connect to financial reporting. The gap is a post-award grant compliance platform that connects deliverable tracking to evidence documentation to financial reporting — a unified system that knows "Grant X requires serving 500 clients, we've served 347, here's the evidence from our case management system, and here's the $180,000 we've spent against the $250,000 budget."
