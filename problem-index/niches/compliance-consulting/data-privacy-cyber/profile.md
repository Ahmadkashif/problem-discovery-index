# Data Privacy & Cybersecurity Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — consultants use vulnerability scanners and penetration testing tools for cybersecurity assessments but privacy compliance mapping is done in spreadsheets and Word documents; the regulatory landscape changes faster than any tool can keep up
**Target Buyer:** Cybersecurity or privacy consultant serving mid-market companies (100-5,000 employees) navigating CCPA/CPRA, state privacy laws, GDPR, NIST CSF, and CMMC requirements
**Automation Potential:** High — privacy law compliance mapping is a structured comparison of client data practices against regulatory requirements, and regulatory change tracking across 15+ state privacy laws is a classification and alerting problem ideally suited to automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Data privacy and cybersecurity compliance consulting is the fastest-growing segment of the compliance market, driven by the explosion of state privacy laws (15+ comprehensive state privacy laws enacted since 2020, with new amendments and new states added annually), CMMC requirements for defense contractors, and the expanding scope of GDPR enforcement. The work splits into two streams: cybersecurity compliance (assessing technical controls against frameworks like NIST CSF, CIS Controls, or CMMC) and privacy compliance (mapping data collection, processing, and sharing practices against applicable privacy laws). The defining challenge is multi-jurisdictional complexity: a mid-market company with customers in 30 states must comply with every state privacy law that applies to it, each with different definitions of personal information, different consumer rights, different opt-out mechanisms, and different enforcement provisions. The consultant must determine which laws apply (based on the client's revenue, data volume, and geographic footprint), map the client's current data practices against each applicable law, identify gaps, and design a privacy program that satisfies all of them simultaneously. This is a combinatorial problem that grows with each new state law, and consultants currently solve it by maintaining a manually updated comparison spreadsheet that is outdated the week it's published.

## Current Tools & Gaps
OneTrust and TrustArc dominate enterprise privacy management with comprehensive privacy impact assessments, data mapping, consent management, and regulatory intelligence. They price at $50K-200K/year and target companies with dedicated privacy teams. BigID focuses on data discovery and classification. WireWheel offers mid-market privacy compliance. CMMC compliance tools (Exostar, Coalfire) serve defense contractors. For the privacy consultant serving mid-market clients, none of these are right: OneTrust is too expensive for the client and too complex for the consultant to deploy in a 3-month engagement. The consultant typically delivers a Word document — a privacy program gap assessment — built from a manually maintained spreadsheet of regulatory requirements. The gap is a platform that maintains a live, structured database of all applicable privacy and cybersecurity regulations, allows the consultant to map a client's data practices against all applicable requirements simultaneously, and generates a prioritized remediation plan with specific policy and technical control recommendations.
