# Environmental Compliance

**Market Size:** $1.5-2B

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Environmental compliance consulting serves manufacturers, construction companies, and industrial facilities that must comply with EPA regulations, state Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) requirements, and local environmental ordinances. The regulatory landscape is uniquely layered: a single manufacturing facility might hold an air quality permit (Clean Air Act), a stormwater discharge permit (Clean Water Act), a hazardous waste generator ID (RCRA), and multiple state-specific permits — each with different renewal dates, reporting requirements, and monitoring obligations. The consultant's job is to track all applicable permits, ensure monitoring is conducted on schedule, compile reporting data from multiple sources, and submit filings to the correct agencies before deadlines. A missed filing can result in $25,000-50,000/day penalties from EPA, making deadline management literally the most expensive failure mode. SMB manufacturers (50-500 employees) typically cannot afford a full-time environmental manager, so the compliance consultant serves as their outsourced environmental department. The work is heavily document-driven: Tier II chemical inventory reports, Form R toxic release inventory filings, emissions calculations using AP-42 factors, and waste profile sheets all require assembling data from multiple operational systems and formatting it to agency-specific templates.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Enterprise EHS platforms — Enablon (Wolters Kluwer), Cority, Intelex, Benchmark Digital Partners — provide comprehensive environmental management for Fortune 500 companies at $50K-200K/year. These are inaccessible to SMB manufacturers and their consultants. On the SMB end, ERA Environmental provides emissions calculation software but doesn't handle permit tracking or multi-regulatory compliance. SpheraCloud offers environmental reporting but again targets enterprise users. Most environmental compliance consultants use a combination of Outlook calendar reminders for permit deadlines, Excel spreadsheets for emissions calculations, and SharePoint or Dropbox for document storage. The gap is an integrated platform that tracks permits across all applicable jurisdictions, automates emissions calculations from monitoring data, and generates agency-formatted reports — designed for the consultant managing 15-30 industrial clients, not the Fortune 500 EHS department.
