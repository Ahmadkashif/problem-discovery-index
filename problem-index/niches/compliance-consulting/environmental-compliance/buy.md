# SMB Environmental Management from Enterprise EHS Platforms

**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Enablon, Cority, and Intelex solve environmental compliance management for Fortune 500 companies but price at $50K-200K/year with 6-month implementations — SMB manufacturers with 50-500 employees need the same permit tracking and emissions reporting at $500-1,500/month with consultant-managed setup.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Enterprise EHS platforms have solved environmental compliance management comprehensively — they track permits, calculate emissions, generate regulatory reports, manage inspections, and provide audit trails. But they are designed for companies with dedicated EHS departments of 5-20 people, IT teams to manage integrations, and budgets that treat $100K+ in software licensing as a rounding error. The 50,000+ US manufacturing facilities with 50-500 employees don't have EHS departments — they have an operations manager who wears the environmental compliance hat in addition to 5 other roles, supplemented by a compliance consultant who visits quarterly. These facilities need the same permit tracking and emissions reporting capabilities but at 1/10th the price, with 1/10th the implementation complexity, and with the consultant (not the client's IT department) managing configuration.

## What Already Exists
Enablon (Wolters Kluwer) covers air emissions, water discharge, waste management, and chemical inventory with deep regulatory content. Cority provides incident management, compliance tracking, and reporting. Intelex offers a modular EHS suite. All three target enterprise clients with $50K-200K/year licensing and 3-6 month implementations requiring dedicated project managers. ERA Environmental provides SMB-accessible emissions calculation software but lacks permit tracking, deadline management, and multi-regulatory compliance. ProcessMAP offers a mid-market option but still prices above $25K/year.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is deployment model and pricing, not functionality. Enterprise platforms already contain the regulatory knowledge (permit types by jurisdiction, reporting formats by agency, emissions calculation methodologies by pollutant). What's needed is a consultant-facing deployment wrapper: (1) multi-tenant architecture where one consultant manages 20 client facilities from a single interface, (2) pre-configured templates for the 20 most common permit types (Title V, synthetic minor, stormwater, RCRA generator, Tier II) that reduce setup from weeks to hours, (3) simplified data entry interfaces designed for the operations manager who has 30 minutes per month for environmental compliance, not the full-time EHS specialist, and (4) pricing at $500-1,500/month per facility with no implementation fee — the consulting firm absorbs setup cost as part of their advisory engagement and recovers it through the ongoing monitoring relationship. The consultant becomes the system administrator, creating a managed-service offering that generates recurring revenue.

## Target Customer
Environmental compliance consulting firms looking to move from project-based advisory to managed-service delivery, and SMB manufacturers with 50-500 employees who need systematic permit and emissions tracking but cannot justify enterprise EHS platforms.

## Impact If Solved
Gives 50,000+ SMB manufacturers access to systematic environmental compliance management that was previously available only to enterprise organizations. For the consulting firm, managed environmental compliance becomes a recurring revenue stream ($500-1,500/month per client vs. quarterly project invoices). For the manufacturer, systematic tracking reduces the risk of missed deadlines and the $25K-50K/day penalties they carry, while cutting the time their operations manager spends on compliance documentation by 60-70%.
