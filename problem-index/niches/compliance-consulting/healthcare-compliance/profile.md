# Healthcare Compliance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.5-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most firms use spreadsheet-based gap analysis templates and manual evidence collection; a handful have adopted Compliancy Group or HIPAA One but these are checklist tools, not intelligent workflow systems
**Target Buyer:** Healthcare compliance consulting firm principal serving hospitals, physician practices, home health agencies, and behavioral health clinics
**Automation Potential:** High — HIPAA risk assessments follow a structured control framework that maps directly to automatable gap analysis, and EHR systems contain audit evidence that is currently extracted manually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Healthcare compliance consulting is the largest single segment of SMB compliance work, driven by HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules, CMS Conditions of Participation, FDA requirements for device-adjacent facilities, and OSHA healthcare-specific standards. The work is structurally repetitive: every engagement follows the same framework (identify applicable regulations, map current controls, identify gaps, remediate, document evidence), but the details vary by facility type — a 10-provider behavioral health clinic has different HIPAA risks than a 200-bed rural hospital. Consultants spend 60-70% of their project hours on gap analysis and evidence collection rather than strategic advisory. The buyer is a firm principal who runs 8-15 concurrent client engagements with 2-5 junior consultants, each using a slightly different version of a shared spreadsheet template. Client facilities run Epic, Cerner, athenahealth, or one of 50+ smaller EHR systems, none of which export compliance-ready evidence packages. The result is that consultants manually screenshot access logs, pull training completion records, and chase down policy documents from IT directors who are already overloaded.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Compliancy Group and HIPAA One provide HIPAA compliance checklists and remediation tracking but function as structured to-do lists rather than intelligent systems — they don't analyze the gap pattern across clients or predict which controls are most likely to fail. Vanta handles SOC2/ISO compliance with automated evidence collection from cloud providers but has no healthcare-specific controls or EHR integrations. Enterprise GRC platforms (RSA Archer, ServiceNow GRC) are priced for health systems with dedicated compliance departments, not for the consulting firms that serve physician practices and clinics. The gap is a platform that understands healthcare-specific control frameworks (HIPAA, CMS CoP, state health department requirements), connects to EHR systems for automated evidence pulls, and learns from patterns across engagements to accelerate gap analysis for new clients.
