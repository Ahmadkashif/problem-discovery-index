# HIPAA Risk Assessment Automation with Control Gap Mapping

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/healthcare-compliance/profile|Healthcare Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]

**One-liner:** A platform that ingests a healthcare facility's technical environment, policies, and workforce profile, then automatically maps current state against HIPAA Security Rule controls, identifies gaps, and generates a prioritized remediation plan — replacing the 80-120 hour manual risk assessment with a 10-hour guided process.

## The Problem
HIPAA risk assessments are the bread and butter of healthcare compliance consulting — every covered entity and business associate must conduct one annually, creating a recurring engagement cycle. But each assessment follows the same fundamental structure: map the organization's administrative, physical, and technical safeguards against the 54 implementation specifications of the HIPAA Security Rule, identify where controls are absent or insufficient, score risk by likelihood and impact, and produce a remediation plan. A senior consultant can complete this in 80-120 hours for a mid-size practice; a junior consultant takes 150+. The bottleneck is not the framework — it's the investigative work of understanding what the client actually has in place, which requires reading policy documents, interviewing IT staff, reviewing system configurations, and cross-referencing against the control matrix. Experienced consultants develop tacit knowledge about which controls are most likely to be missing based on facility type, EHR vendor, and organizational size — a 20-provider group running athenahealth has a predictable gap pattern that a consultant who has assessed 50 similar organizations can identify in the first 30 minutes.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The challenge is encoding the experienced consultant's pattern recognition into a system. A senior healthcare compliance consultant looks at a client profile — 3 locations, 45 employees, athenahealth EHR, no dedicated IT staff, last assessment 2 years ago — and immediately knows which 15 of the 54 HIPAA controls are almost certainly deficient. This prediction is based on hundreds of prior assessments, not a decision tree anyone has written down. Building this requires a training dataset of completed risk assessments mapped to facility profiles, which no single firm has in structured format — the assessments live in Word documents and spreadsheets with inconsistent formatting. The second challenge is evidence interpretation: determining whether a client's access control policy actually satisfies the HIPAA requirement requires reading the policy and understanding both its intent and its gaps, a task that requires domain-specific language understanding.

## What to Build
A SaaS platform for healthcare compliance consultants that combines structured intake (facility type, size, EHR vendor, IT infrastructure, prior assessment history) with a gradient-boosted gap prediction model that identifies the most likely control deficiencies before the assessment begins, focusing the consultant's time on verification rather than discovery. The platform uses large language models to parse uploaded policy documents and map their content against HIPAA control requirements, flagging policies that are present but insufficient (e.g., an access control policy that doesn't address emergency access procedures). As the platform processes more assessments, the gap prediction model improves — learning that facilities with vendor X and no dedicated CISO have a 92% probability of failing control Y. Output is a pre-populated risk assessment with confidence scores, a prioritized remediation plan, and an evidence request list tailored to the specific gaps identified.

## Target Customer
Healthcare compliance consulting firm principals managing 8-15 concurrent HIPAA risk assessment engagements, currently spending $8K-15K in labor per assessment, willing to pay $500-800/month for a platform that cuts assessment time by 50-60%.

## Impact If Built
Reduces HIPAA risk assessment labor from 80-120 hours to 30-50 hours per engagement, allowing a 5-person consulting firm to handle 2-3x more concurrent clients without hiring. At $500-800/month per firm with an addressable market of 3,000-5,000 healthcare compliance consulting firms in the US, the platform supports a $20-50M ARR business. More importantly, the gap prediction model ensures that junior consultants perform assessments at near-senior quality, reducing the firm's dependency on experienced talent.
