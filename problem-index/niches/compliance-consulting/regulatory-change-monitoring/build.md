# AI Regulatory Change Classifier with Client Impact Mapping

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/regulatory-change-monitoring/profile|Regulatory Change Monitoring]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A platform that continuously scans regulatory sources across all compliance domains, uses a language model to classify each change by type and significance, and maps changes to specific client engagements based on their regulatory profile — replacing the 5-10 hours per week consultants spend reading agency publications with a prioritized, client-tagged change feed.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #attention-mechanisms #transfer-learning #data-integration #compliance #automation #revenue-impact #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
A compliance consulting firm covering healthcare, environmental, and privacy compliance must monitor: the Federal Register (250+ pages daily), CMS/HHS guidance documents, FDA guidance and enforcement actions, OSHA standards and enforcement memos, EPA rulemakings and state environmental agency bulletins, 50 state legislative databases for privacy and employment law changes, and dozens of industry-specific regulatory sources. The firm's senior consultants divide this monitoring among themselves — the healthcare lead reads CMS publications, the environmental lead reads EPA notices, the privacy lead monitors state legislatures. Each spends 5-10 hours per week scanning, filtering, and evaluating publications. The critical challenge is not finding the changes — it's determining which changes affect which clients. A CMS update to hospital discharge planning requirements affects 3 of the firm's 15 healthcare clients (the ones operating hospitals) but not the other 12 (physician practices and clinics). An EPA change to hazardous waste generator categories affects 5 of their 20 environmental clients (the ones generating hazardous waste) but not the other 15. This client-impact classification is where the consultant's tacit knowledge is essential: they know each client's regulatory profile intimately and can immediately assess whether a specific change matters. But this assessment is done mentally, across dozens of clients, for dozens of changes per week — and when the consultant is busy with a project, monitoring falls behind.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Enterprise RegTech platforms (Ascent, CUBE, Corlytics) have built regulatory change management systems for large financial institutions, but they focus exclusively on financial regulation and price at $50K-500K/year. Building a multi-domain version for SMB compliance consultants requires: (1) regulatory source coverage across healthcare, environmental, privacy, safety, and financial domains — a much broader scope than any existing platform, (2) a classification model that understands regulatory language well enough to determine the substantive impact of a change (not just keyword matching, which generates too many false positives), and (3) a client profile model that captures each client's regulatory footprint in enough detail to enable automated impact assessment. The training data challenge is significant: the consultant's client-impact assessments are performed mentally and not recorded anywhere, so there's no labeled dataset of "this regulatory change affected Client X but not Client Y."

## What to Build
A multi-source regulatory monitoring platform that: (1) continuously ingests publications from the Federal Register, CMS, FDA, EPA, OSHA, state legislative databases, and state agency websites, (2) uses a transformer-based language model to parse each publication and extract: what changed, what entity is affected, what the new requirement is, and when it takes effect, (3) classifies each change by regulatory domain (healthcare, environmental, privacy, employment, financial), significance (major rule change, guidance update, enforcement action, procedural update), and action requirement (compliance program update needed, client notification recommended, information only), and (4) maps classified changes to specific client engagements based on stored client profiles (industry, size, location, applicable regulations, current compliance program scope). The consultant receives a daily digest of changes ranked by client impact, with pre-drafted client notification language for changes that require client action. The system learns from consultant feedback — when a consultant marks a change as irrelevant to a client the system flagged, or identifies a client impact the system missed, the classification model improves.

## Target Customer
Compliance consulting firm owners managing multi-domain practices with 15-40 active client engagements, currently relying on senior consultants to manually monitor regulatory sources at 5-10 hours per week each, willing to pay $500-1,000/month for a platform that reduces monitoring time by 70-80% while improving change detection coverage.

## Impact If Built
Recovers 15-30 hours per week of senior consultant time across a typical multi-domain compliance firm — time that is currently the lowest-value use of the firm's most expensive resource. Eliminates missed regulatory changes, which are the most reputation-damaging failure mode in compliance consulting (a client's compliance program becomes deficient because a regulation changed and the consultant didn't catch it). The client-impact mapping creates a proactive advisory relationship: instead of waiting for clients to ask "does this new regulation affect me?", the consultant reaches out with analysis before the client is aware of the change.
