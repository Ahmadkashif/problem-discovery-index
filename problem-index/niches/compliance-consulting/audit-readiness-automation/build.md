# Automated Evidence Collection Agent for Multi-Framework Compliance

**Niche:** [[niches/compliance-consulting/audit-readiness-automation/profile|Audit Readiness & Evidence Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/compliance-consulting|Compliance Consulting]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** An AI agent that connects to client systems — cloud providers, HR platforms, EHR systems, financial software, ticketing systems — and automatically pulls, classifies, and organizes compliance evidence against any framework, replacing the 60-70% of consulting project labor currently spent on manual evidence collection.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #transformers #data-integration #compliance #automation #ai-agent #revenue-impact #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
A compliance consultant preparing a client for a HIPAA audit must collect evidence across 54 implementation specifications: access control policies from the IT department, workforce training completion records from the HR system, audit logs from the EHR, encryption verification from the IT infrastructure, backup and recovery test results, incident response documentation, and business associate agreements from the legal department. Each evidence type lives in a different system, is owned by a different person, and requires a different extraction method. The consultant sends 15-25 evidence request emails to various client stakeholders, waits for responses (average turnaround: 5-10 business days per request), receives evidence in inconsistent formats (screenshots, PDFs, CSV exports, Word documents), reviews each piece for completeness, and organizes everything into an audit-ready structure. This process takes 60-100 hours per engagement and repeats annually. An experienced consultant develops tacit knowledge about where evidence lives in different client environments — they know that athenahealth stores audit logs under Admin > Reports > Security Audit, that Gusto generates training completion reports under Compliance > Training, and that AWS access reviews are pulled from IAM Credential Reports. This system-specific navigation knowledge lives in the consultant's head and takes years to accumulate across dozens of different client technology stacks.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Vanta proved the model works for SOC2/ISO by building integrations with ~200 cloud and SaaS tools. Extending this to HIPAA, EPA, OSHA, and other frameworks requires: (1) integrations with industry-specific systems — EHR platforms (Epic, Cerner, athenahealth), environmental monitoring systems (CEMS vendors), financial core banking systems (Jack Henry, Fiserv), and HR platforms beyond what Vanta currently covers, (2) framework mapping — understanding which evidence from which system satisfies which control requirement for each compliance framework, and (3) evidence quality assessment — determining not just whether evidence exists but whether it's complete and current enough to satisfy an auditor. The integration challenge is the primary barrier: there are hundreds of systems across all compliance domains, many with limited or no APIs, and some (particularly legacy EHR and environmental monitoring systems) that require screen scraping or manual export with specific navigation instructions.

## What to Build
A multi-framework evidence collection platform with three tiers of automation: (1) API-connected collection — for systems with available APIs (cloud providers, modern HR platforms, modern EHR systems), the agent pulls evidence automatically on a scheduled basis and maps it to applicable control requirements, (2) guided collection — for systems without APIs, the agent provides system-specific instructions (with screenshots) for manual evidence extraction, tracks which evidence has been collected, and follows up on outstanding items, and (3) intelligent classification — when evidence is uploaded (manually or via API), a large language model classifies which control requirements it satisfies, identifies gaps (a policy document that addresses access control but not emergency access), and flags evidence that is expired or insufficient. The system maintains a knowledge base of evidence locations per client system configuration that grows as consultants use it — encoding the tacit knowledge about where evidence lives in specific technology stacks. The consultant manages evidence collection across 15-30 clients from a single dashboard, with each client's evidence status showing completion percentage per framework requirement.

## Target Customer
Compliance consulting firms where evidence collection consumes 60-70% of project labor across HIPAA, SOC2, environmental, financial, and general compliance engagements, currently spending $5K-15K in labor per client per audit cycle on evidence gathering, willing to pay $300-800/month per client for a platform that automates 50-70% of evidence collection.

## Impact If Built
Reduces evidence collection labor from 60-100 hours to 20-40 hours per engagement by automating API-connected collection and streamlining manual collection with guided instructions. For a 10-person compliance consulting firm spending 60% of project hours on evidence collection, this recovers 3,000-5,000 hours annually — equivalent to 1.5-2.5 FTEs — that can be redirected to advisory work at higher margins. The encoded knowledge base of evidence locations means junior consultants can collect evidence at near-senior efficiency, reducing the firm's dependency on experienced talent. Continuous collection (rather than annual snapshot) also means evidence is always current, eliminating the staleness problem.
