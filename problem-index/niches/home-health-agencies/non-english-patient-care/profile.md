# Non-English-Speaking Patient Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — No home health platform supports multilingual OASIS administration, non-English patient education, or interpreter coordination for home visits
**Target Buyer:** Agency director serving immigrant and limited-English-proficiency (LEP) communities
**Automation Potential:** High — Translation, multilingual content delivery, and interpreter scheduling are all automatable with current LLM and speech technology

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Over 25 million US residents have limited English proficiency, and many require home health services. When a Spanish-speaking patient receives a home health admission, the nurse must administer an OASIS assessment through an interpreter (adding 30+ minutes), deliver wound care and medication education in a language the patient understands, reconcile medications that may have been prescribed by providers documenting in different languages, and train family caregivers who may also have limited English. Every touchpoint is degraded by the language barrier: assessment accuracy drops, patient education retention plummets, and medication errors increase.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No home health platform offers multilingual OASIS administration support, patient education materials in non-English languages, or interpreter coordination integrated into the visit scheduling workflow. Interpreter services (LanguageLine, Stratus) exist for phone/video interpretation but aren't built into the home visit workflow — clinicians must separately arrange interpretation, which adds complexity and cost. Patient education materials (handouts, videos) are almost exclusively English-only.
