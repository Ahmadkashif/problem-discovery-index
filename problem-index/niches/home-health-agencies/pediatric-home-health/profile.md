# Pediatric Home Health

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Generic home health platforms don't handle shift-based nursing; pediatric-specific software barely exists
**Target Buyer:** Clinical director at pediatric home health agency
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Shift scheduling is automatable, but the clinical complexity and family coordination require human judgment augmented by better tools

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pediatric home health serves medically complex children — ventilator-dependent, tracheostomy, feeding tube, seizure disorder patients — with shift-based nursing (8-16 hour shifts, not 45-minute visits). The family is a co-caregiver, the school system is a care setting (nurses accompany children to school), and documentation requirements blend pediatric developmental milestones with acute medical monitoring. Staffing is the existential challenge: pediatric nurses are scarce, emotionally taxed by the intensity of caring for critically ill children, and leave the field at rates exceeding 40% annually.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Generic home health EMRs (WellSky, Axxess) are designed for visit-based adult care and handle shift-based pediatric scheduling poorly. MatrixCare has some shift-scheduling capability but lacks pediatric clinical documentation. No platform integrates shift scheduling with parent/guardian coordination, school calendar management, backup nurse matching for call-outs, or pediatric-specific clinical documentation (hourly vent checks, seizure logs, growth tracking).
