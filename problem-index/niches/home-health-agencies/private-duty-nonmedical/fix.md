# Private-Pay Billing and Family Transparency Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/home-health-agencies/private-duty-nonmedical/profile|Private-Duty Non-Medical Home Care]]
**Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Private-pay families dispute hours, invoices arrive weeks late, and there's no real-time visibility into care delivered — creating billing friction that damages the agency-family relationship and delays revenue collection.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Private-pay home care billing is chaotic. Families pay hourly rates ($25-$45/hour) but have no real-time visibility into when the caregiver arrived, when they left, or what care was provided. Invoices are generated weekly or biweekly from timesheet data, often arriving 2-3 weeks after service. Families frequently dispute hours, claiming the caregiver arrived late or left early. The agency's office staff spends hours reconciling EVV clock-in/out data with timesheets, generating invoices, chasing payments, and resolving disputes — all while trying to maintain a relationship with the family who is also the referral source.

## Why It's Still Broken
EVV (Electronic Visit Verification) was mandated for Medicaid-funded personal care, which drove adoption of clock-in/out tracking. But private-pay clients aren't subject to EVV mandates, so many agencies don't use EVV for private-pay cases — or if they do, the data doesn't flow to billing. The scheduling platforms (ClearCare, AxisCare) have billing modules, but they're designed for agency-side invoicing, not family-facing transparency. No platform connects real-time visit verification to an automated invoice-to-payment flow with a family-facing portal showing care delivered.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An integrated timesheet-to-invoice-to-payment system with a family portal: (1) caregiver clocks in/out via mobile with GPS verification, (2) families receive real-time notifications when the caregiver arrives and departs, (3) care tasks completed are logged and visible to the family in a daily summary, (4) invoices auto-generate from verified time data on a configurable schedule, (5) families pay via ACH/credit card through the portal, and (6) disputes are flagged instantly against the verified EVV data rather than argued weeks later.

## Who Feels the Pain
Office managers and billing staff who spend 8-12 hours per week on private-pay invoicing, dispute resolution, and collections. Families who feel they're paying for care they can't verify. Agency owners who carry 30-45 days of accounts receivable on private-pay cases.

## Impact If Fixed
Reducing private-pay AR from 35 days to 10 days and eliminating 80% of billing disputes would improve cash flow by $50K-$100K for a mid-size agency. Reclaiming 8-12 hours of weekly admin time and reducing family friction would improve retention of private-pay clients, who represent the agency's highest-margin revenue.
