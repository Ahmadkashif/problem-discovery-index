# Private-Duty Non-Medical Home Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-35B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Many small agencies and franchisees operate on spreadsheets; ClearCare and AxisCare adoption is growing but far from universal
**Target Buyer:** Agency owner / franchisee
**Automation Potential:** High — Caregiver matching, shift scheduling, and private-pay billing are repetitive workflows with clear optimization targets

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Private-duty non-medical home care employs home health aides who provide personal care (bathing, dressing, meal prep, companionship, transportation) — not skilled nursing. The workforce is fundamentally different (aides, not nurses), the regulation is different (state licensing, not CMS Conditions of Participation), the billing is different (hourly, not per-episode), and the payer mix is different (private pay, Medicaid waiver, long-term care insurance, VA). The core operational challenge is matching the right caregiver to the right client and retaining aides who earn $12-$16/hour in a market with 60%+ annual turnover.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ClearCare (now WellSky Personal Care) and AxisCare handle basic scheduling and client management. HHAeXchange dominates Medicaid-funded personal care. Many small agencies and franchisees still use spreadsheets, whiteboards, and group texts for scheduling. No platform offers intelligent caregiver-patient matching beyond geography and availability, workforce analytics that predict which aides are at turnover risk, or automated private-pay billing with family-facing transparency.
