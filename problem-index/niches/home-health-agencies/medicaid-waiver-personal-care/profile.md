# Medicaid Waiver Personal Care Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $18-22B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — State-mandated platforms, paper timesheets, and fragmented compliance requirements keep most agencies on manual workflows
**Target Buyer:** Program director at Medicaid-contracted agency
**Automation Potential:** Very High — Compliance rules are complex but deterministic, making them ideal for rule-engine automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Medicaid waiver personal care operates under state-specific rules that vary dramatically across all 50 states. New York's CDPAP lets patients hire family members as caregivers. California's IHSS has its own assessment and authorization system. Texas's CDS model gives patients employer-of-record responsibilities. Each state defines different authorized services, worker qualification requirements, EVV specifications, billing code sets, and prior authorization processes. Self-directed care models — where the patient, not the agency, is the employer — create a completely different operational and compliance framework than traditional agency-directed care.

## Current Tools & Gaps
HHAeXchange dominates Medicaid home care management, with some states mandating specific platforms (e.g., Sandata for EVV in certain states). Many states have built their own EVV and authorization tracking systems. Small agencies — especially those operating in self-directed care models — still run on paper timesheets submitted to fiscal intermediaries. No tool handles multi-state waiver complexity, and agencies expanding across state lines must learn entirely new compliance regimes for each state.
