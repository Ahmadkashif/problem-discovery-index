# Referral Intake & Insurance Verification

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/home-health-agencies|Home Health Agencies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (embedded across all home health agencies)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Touches all agencies
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Referral management is the weakest module in every home health platform; hospital-to-agency referrals still happen via fax and phone
**Target Buyer:** Intake coordinator / Referral manager
**Automation Potential:** Very High — Referral processing is a sequence of verifiable steps (OCR, eligibility check, service area match, clinician availability) that can be fully automated

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
When a hospital discharges a patient to home health, the agency receives a referral — typically by fax — and must execute a 2-4 hour manual process under extreme time pressure. The intake team verifies insurance eligibility, confirms service area coverage, matches ordered services to available clinician skills, obtains face-to-face encounter documentation from the referring physician, and schedules the start-of-care visit within 48 hours. Hospitals expect same-day acceptance or rejection. Agencies that respond in 4 hours win the referral; agencies that take 24 hours lose it to a competitor. Speed of intake directly determines census growth and revenue.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Referral management is the weakest feature in every home health platform. WellSky has basic referral tracking. Some agencies use Salesforce or generic CRMs for referral pipeline management. Hospital-to-agency referral transmission is overwhelmingly fax-based, with some systems using Curaspan/nH for electronic referral matching. No platform automates the end-to-end intake workflow: OCR the fax, extract structured data, verify eligibility, check service area, match to clinicians, and present an accept/reject recommendation.
