# Legacy Through-Hole Assembly Houses

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/electronics-contract-mfg|Electronics Contract Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most operate with paper travelers, manual inspection, and no MES; many are family-owned shops with 10-50 employees
**Target Buyer:** Owner or Shop Foreman at through-hole assembly shops (5-50 employees)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — wave solder process control, manual assembly guidance, and quality inspection are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Through-hole technology (THT) assembly — inserting axial and radial components, connectors, transformers, and relays through PCB holes and wave soldering — persists in industrial controls, power electronics, military/aerospace, and legacy product sustainment. These shops serve customers who cannot or will not redesign legacy products for SMT. The workforce is aging (average assembler age 45-55), hand insertion is physically demanding, and the skill of wave solder machine operation (adjusting conveyor speed, preheat temperature, solder pot temperature, and flux density for each board type) is a craft skill that takes years to develop. Many THT shops also perform hand soldering, cable assembly, and conformal coating — mixed manual processes that MES platforms designed for automated SMT lines do not address.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most THT shops have no MES — production is tracked with paper travelers that follow each board through insertion, wave solder, touch-up, inspection, and test. Quality inspection is 100% visual by experienced inspectors checking IPC-A-610 workmanship standards. Quotation and order management are spreadsheet-based. No platform addresses the specific workflow of mixed manual and wave solder assembly, provides visual workmanship guidance for THT-specific defect types, or captures wave solder process knowledge.
