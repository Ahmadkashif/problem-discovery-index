**Automation Potential:** High — PPAP documentation, FMEA updates, and SPC monitoring follow rigid standards with clear automation paths

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Automotive electronics EMS providers operate under IATF 16949 (automotive quality management standard), which adds layer upon layer of quality documentation requirements beyond standard ISO 9001: APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning), PPAP (Production Part Approval Process) with 18 required elements, DFMEA/PFMEA (Design and Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis), MSA (Measurement System Analysis), and SPC (Statistical Process Control) monitoring with capability indices (Cpk > 1.67 for critical characteristics). The zero-defect expectation from OEM customers (defect rates measured in DPPM — defects per million parts) creates a quality cost structure that is 2-3x higher than commercial electronics. Warranty chargebacks from OEM customers for field failures can reach millions of dollars per incident.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IATF 16949-specific quality tools exist (Plexus AIAG templates, Intelex, ETQ) for FMEA management, SPC charting (InfinityQS, WinSPC), and PPAP documentation. MES platforms manage production tracking. No platform automates PPAP package assembly from cross-system data, provides AI-assisted FMEA risk scoring based on historical failure data, or predicts which process characteristics will drift out of Cpk specification before they do.
