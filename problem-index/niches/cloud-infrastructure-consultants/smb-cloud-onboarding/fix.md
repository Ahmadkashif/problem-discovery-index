# Post-Migration User Abandonment

**Niche:** [[niches/cloud-infrastructure-consultants/smb-cloud-onboarding/profile|SMB Cloud Onboarding]]
**Industry:** [[industries/cloud-infrastructure-consultants|Cloud Infrastructure Consultants]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** 40% of SMB employees revert to old habits (local file storage, email attachments) within 60 days of cloud migration because no one follows up on adoption after the consultant leaves.
**Tags:** #quick-win #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
After the technical migration is complete, the consultant declares success and moves on. But SMB employees — especially those over 50 or in non-technical roles — continue saving files to their desktop, emailing attachments instead of sharing links, and calling the office manager when they can't find something in SharePoint. The cloud investment goes underutilized, and the SMB owner blames the consultant when nothing feels different.

## Why It's Still Broken
Consultants are paid for technical delivery, not adoption coaching. The economics don't support a 60-day follow-up engagement for a $15K migration. SMB owners don't have internal IT staff to drive adoption, and Microsoft's adoption tools (Adoption Score, Viva Insights) are designed for enterprises with dedicated change management teams, not a 15-person accounting firm.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated adoption monitoring and nudging system that tracks cloud service usage per employee post-migration, detects when users revert to old behaviors (local saves, attachment-based sharing), and sends contextual micro-training — a 30-second video or tooltip showing the cloud-native way to do what they just tried to do locally. The consultant gets a monthly adoption dashboard without any manual effort.

## Who Feels the Pain
SMB office managers who become the de facto IT support desk after migration, fielding 5-10 "where did my file go?" questions per day.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases cloud feature adoption from 40% to 75% within 90 days, reduces SMB help-desk burden by 60%, and improves consultant Net Promoter Score by turning technical success into user success.
