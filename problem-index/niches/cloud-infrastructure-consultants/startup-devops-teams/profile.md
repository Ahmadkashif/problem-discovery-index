# Startup DevOps Teams

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cloud-infrastructure-consultants|Cloud Infrastructure Consultants]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 4%
**Digital Adoption:** High — startups are cloud-native but lack infrastructure expertise, resulting in over-engineered or under-engineered deployments
**Target Buyer:** Startup CTO / Lead SRE at Series A-C companies (20-200 employees)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — many startup infrastructure patterns are repeatable, but each startup's tech stack introduces unique complexity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Startups need cloud infrastructure expertise but cannot afford dedicated platform engineering teams or enterprise consulting rates. They hire 1-2 DevOps engineers who learn on the job, resulting in Kubernetes clusters that are over-provisioned by 3x, CI/CD pipelines held together with shell scripts, and no disaster recovery plan. The consulting model for startups requires fractional engagement (10-20 hours/month), opinionated architecture decisions (not endless options), and willingness to do hands-on implementation — not just produce PowerPoint decks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Startups use Terraform, Kubernetes, and GitHub Actions, often with help from Gruntwork, Spacelift, or Pulumi. However, these tools assume someone knows what good infrastructure looks like — they automate deployment, not design. The gap is a "fractional platform engineering" model that combines architecture review, IaC implementation, and ongoing advisory at a price point startups can afford ($3K-$8K/month).
