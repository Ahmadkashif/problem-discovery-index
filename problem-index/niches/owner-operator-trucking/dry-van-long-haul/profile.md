# Dry Van Long-Haul Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/owner-operator-trucking|Owner-Operator Trucking]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $80-100B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35-40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Use load boards (DAT, Truckstop) and ELDs (Motive, Samsara) but lack profitability analytics, route optimization, and strategic planning tools
**Target Buyer:** Solo OTR operator running a 53-ft dry van across 48 states, making 100+ load selection decisions per month
**Automation Potential:** High — Load scoring, fuel optimization, and deadhead minimization are all data-rich problems with clear economic payoff

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Dry van is the commodity segment of trucking — the most loads, the most operators, the thinnest margins. Because any dry van can haul any dry freight, competition is pure price. The operators who thrive do so through superior load selection (avoiding bad lanes, minimizing deadhead, timing market shifts) and cost management (fuel stops, maintenance timing, toll avoidance). These skills are tacit — developed over years of pattern recognition — and represent the highest-value ML target in the industry. The sheer volume of operators (~150,000 dry van OOs) makes even incremental per-load improvement worth billions in aggregate.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DAT and Truckstop.com show available loads and market rates but don't calculate true per-load profitability. Motive and Samsara track driving data but don't connect it to revenue. TruckSmarter and CloudTrucks are emerging operator platforms but remain early-stage with limited analytics. No tool answers the fundamental question: "Of the 50 loads available right now, which 3 will make me the most money after all costs?"
