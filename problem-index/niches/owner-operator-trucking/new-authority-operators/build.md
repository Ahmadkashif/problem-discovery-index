# New Authority Onboarding and Compliance Sequencer

**Niche:** [[niches/owner-operator-trucking/new-authority-operators/profile|New Authority Operators (First 2 Years)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/owner-operator-trucking|Owner-Operator Trucking]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool guides a new MC authority holder through the 17 sequential compliance and business setup steps — operators learn by costly mistakes and predatory vendor encounters.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #automation #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
A driver gets their MC authority and faces an unstructured avalanche: get insurance (but which kind? how much? from whom?), file BOC-3 (what is that?), register for UCR ($76 but scam sites charge $300), enroll in IFTA (which state?), set up drug testing consortium, choose an ELD provider, open a business bank account, get a fuel card, sign up for load boards, find a dispatcher or broker relationships. These steps have dependencies (can't start hauling until insurance is active, can't get insurance without a DOT physical, can't file BOC-3 without a registered agent) but no tool maps the sequence. Operators discover requirements after the deadline, paying rush fees and penalties.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The new-authority market is transient by definition — each operator is "new" for only 12-18 months. Customer lifetime in the "onboarding" phase is short, so SaaS economics are challenging. The knowledge is also cross-domain: regulatory (FMCSA), financial (insurance, accounting), operational (ELD, load boards), and legal (BOC-3, LLC formation) — no single vendor has expertise across all areas. Existing players (permit services, insurance agencies) profit from the confusion and have no incentive to create clarity.

## What to Build
A step-by-step guided onboarding platform that takes a new authority holder from "MC number issued" to "ready to haul" in 30 days. Each step includes: what it is, why it's required, typical cost range, recommended vendors (vetted, with transparent pricing), document requirements, and deadline. The system tracks completion, sends reminders, and flags when a step is blocking the next one. Revenue model: referral fees from vetted vendors (insurance, ELD, accounting) replace subscription fees, making the tool free to the operator.

## Target Customer
New MC authority holders in their first 90 days of operation, overwhelmed by compliance requirements and vulnerable to predatory vendors.

## Impact If Built
Reduces first-year compliance failures (which trigger FMCSA warnings and insurance cancellations) by 40-60%. Saves new operators $3,000-$8,000 in their first year by steering them away from overpriced services and unnecessary permits. Potentially reduces the 30-40% first-year failure rate by addressing the business-setup gap that kills more operators than bad driving.
