# Veteran Small Business Financing for Truck Purchase

**Niche:** [[niches/owner-operator-trucking/veteran-transitioning-operators/profile|Veteran-Transitioning Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/owner-operator-trucking|Owner-Operator Trucking]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** SBA veteran small business loans exist but no lender has packaged them for truck purchase — veterans default to predatory lease-purchase deals because they don't know better options exist.
**Tags:** #data-integration #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
A veteran buying their first truck faces two common paths: a commercial truck lender (requiring 2+ years of business history they don't have, or charging 12-18% APR for new authorities) or a lease-purchase deal from a carrier (structured to benefit the carrier, with maintenance markups and forced dispatch clauses). Many veterans qualify for SBA veteran loans (Patriot Express, SBA 7a) with far better terms (5-8% APR, longer repayment), but these programs aren't marketed to trucking operators, the application process is complex, and most SBA lenders don't understand trucking equipment valuations.

## What Already Exists
SBA lenders (SmartBiz, Lendio, Fundera) process veteran small business loans but don't specialize in commercial truck purchases. Commercial truck lenders (Daimler Financial, PACCAR Financial, CIT) understand truck valuations but don't offer veteran-specific programs. Lease-purchase operators (Schneider, Werner) target veterans aggressively through military job fairs but offer terms that benefit the carrier, not the operator.

## The Customization Gap
The bridge needs a lender or lending platform that: (1) understands SBA veteran loan programs and their eligibility criteria, (2) can value commercial trucks (new and used Class 8) for collateral purposes, (3) offers pre-qualified loan packages specifically for veteran truck purchases, and (4) provides financial modeling showing the veteran how SBA financing compares to lease-purchase (total cost over 5 years, ownership timeline, equity building). This combination of veteran lending expertise + trucking equipment knowledge doesn't exist in any single provider.

## Target Customer
Veterans with 2+ years of company driving experience preparing to purchase their first truck, currently considering lease-purchase deals because they're unaware of better financing options.

## Impact If Solved
Saves veterans $15,000-$40,000 over a 5-year truck ownership period through better financing terms vs. lease-purchase. Builds equity from day one (veteran owns the truck, not the carrier), creating long-term wealth that lease-purchase explicitly prevents.
