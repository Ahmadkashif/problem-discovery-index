# Veteran-to-Owner-Operator Business Transition Program

**Niche:** [[niches/owner-operator-trucking/veteran-transitioning-operators/profile|Veteran-Transitioning Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/owner-operator-trucking|Owner-Operator Trucking]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No program teaches a veteran how to run a trucking business — CDL school teaches driving, but nobody teaches the P&L, tax obligations, insurance, and broker relationships that determine whether the business survives.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A veteran completes CDL school, drives for a carrier for 1-2 years to meet insurance requirements, and decides to go independent. At this point they know how to drive, inspect, and maintain a truck. They do not know: how to calculate per-load profitability, how quarterly estimated taxes work for self-employment, what IFTA requires, how to evaluate lease-purchase vs. outright purchase, what insurance coverages are required vs. optional, how to negotiate with brokers, or how to build a book of direct shipper relationships. Every other profession has transition programs (medical residency, legal apprenticeship, franchise training); trucking has nothing between "here's your CDL" and "good luck running a business."

## Why Nobody Has Built This
CDL schools make money from training, not from ongoing business support. Carriers benefit from veterans staying as company drivers (lower cost than independent contractors). The trucking industry's fragmented vendor ecosystem (each selling one piece) has no incentive to create an integrated onboarding experience. Veteran-focused nonprofits connect vets to jobs but don't have trucking business expertise, and trucking business experts don't have veteran program expertise.

## What to Build
A structured 12-week business transition program (online, self-paced, compatible with driving schedule) that covers: trucking business financial model (revenue, costs, profit per mile), authority setup sequence, insurance evaluation, tax planning for self-employment, broker relationship building, load selection strategy, and maintenance budgeting. Delivered through a mobile app with 15-minute daily lessons, mentorship matching with veteran OO mentors (5+ year veterans), and milestone tracking. Leverages VA education benefits (GI Bill) and veteran small business grants for funding.

## Target Customer
Military veterans within 1-3 years of transitioning to owner-operator status, with CDL and 1+ years of company driving experience.

## Impact If Built
Reduces veteran owner-operator failure rate from 30-40% to 15-20% by closing the business knowledge gap. Each prevented failure saves $30,000-$80,000 in truck depreciation losses, lease penalties, and debt. Connects veteran operators to a peer network that reduces the isolation that contributes to both business failure and mental health challenges.
