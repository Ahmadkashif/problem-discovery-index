# Recurring Report Generation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/data-analytics-consultants|Data Analytics Consultants]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B (internal spend)
**Share of Parent Industry:** N/A (operational cost, not revenue)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Most consulting firms generate recurring client reports manually: pull data, update charts, write narrative, format PDF, email to client. Dashboards reduce some reporting burden but do not eliminate the need for narrative insight reports.
**Target Buyer:** Junior analysts and delivery managers at analytics consulting firms who spend 20-40% of their time producing recurring reports for clients.
**Automation Potential:** Very High — Recurring reports follow identical structures with updated data, narrative patterns that can be templatized, and distribution schedules that can be automated.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Analytics consulting firms generate three types of deliverables: project deliverables (one-time analyses), dashboards (self-service, ongoing), and recurring reports (analyst-written, periodic). Recurring reports — monthly performance reviews, quarterly business reviews, weekly KPI summaries — are the most labor-intensive per-unit deliverable. Each report requires: pulling current-period data, updating a template with new numbers, writing narrative commentary ("revenue increased 8% driven by new customer acquisition in the Northeast region"), formatting the document, and sending it to the right client contacts. A firm producing 20 monthly reports spends 80-100 hours/month on report generation — work that junior analysts find tedious and clients receive late because it is deprioritized against project work.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Power BI and Tableau support scheduled PDF exports from dashboards, but these are chart screenshots, not narrative reports with commentary. Automated reporting tools (Klipfolio, Databox) generate dashboard summaries but lack narrative generation. Google Docs and PowerPoint are the actual report creation tools, with no automation beyond manual copy-paste from data sources. No tool combines data pull, chart generation, narrative writing, formatting, and distribution into a single automated workflow for consulting firms producing reports across multiple clients.
