# Nonprofit Impact Analytics

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/data-analytics-consultants|Data Analytics Consultants]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Nonprofits track outcomes in spreadsheets, grant narratives, and disconnected program databases; most lack data infrastructure beyond a CRM (Salesforce NPSP) and an accounting system (Blackbaud, QuickBooks).
**Target Buyer:** Analytics consultants targeting foundations, social service agencies, and mid-size nonprofits ($5M-$100M annual budget) that face increasing funder pressure to demonstrate measurable impact.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Impact measurement frameworks are emerging (logic models, theories of change) but data collection methods vary dramatically across programs and funders.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Nonprofit impact analytics is fundamentally different from commercial analytics. The "customer" is a beneficiary, not a buyer. The "revenue" metric is donor retention, not sales. The "product" is a social outcome (reduced recidivism, improved literacy, fewer hospitalizations) that is inherently harder to measure than a commercial KPI. Funders (foundations, government agencies) increasingly demand quantitative impact evidence, but nonprofits lack the data infrastructure to provide it. Their data is scattered across case management systems (Social Solutions Apricot, Penelope), donor CRMs (Salesforce NPSP, Bloomerang), program spreadsheets, and participant surveys. An analytics consultant must integrate these sources, define measurable outcomes, and build attribution models that demonstrate program impact — all while respecting beneficiary privacy and funder-specific reporting requirements.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Salesforce NPSP provides donor analytics. Social Solutions and Penelope provide case management reporting. Fluxx and Submittable handle grant management. But none of these provide cross-program impact analytics — "across all our programs, how many individuals achieved self-sufficiency, and what was our cost per outcome?" Analytics consultants build this from scratch using spreadsheet data, manually linking beneficiaries across program databases, and creating custom outcome metrics. No tool provides a nonprofit impact analytics framework that consultants can deploy consistently.
