# Cross-Enterprise Dashboard for Tribal Economic Development

**Niche:** [[niches/data-analytics-consultants/tribal-enterprise-analytics/profile|Tribal Enterprise Analytics]]
**Industry:** [[industries/data-analytics-consultants|Data Analytics Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Power BI and Tableau provide enterprise dashboards for single-business analysis, but tribal councils need a unified view across multiple diverse enterprises (gaming, hospitality, retail, healthcare, government) with community impact metrics alongside financial KPIs.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
A tribal council oversees 6 enterprises: a casino generating $80M/year, a hotel ($15M), a gas station chain ($10M), a healthcare system (IHS-funded), a construction company ($8M), and an agricultural operation ($5M). Each enterprise reports to the council separately, using different accounting systems, different KPIs, and different reporting formats. The council asks: "What is our total employment? What is our net contribution to the general fund? Which enterprises are growing and which are declining? How do our enterprises compare to similar tribal operations?" No single dashboard answers these questions because the data sources are siloed and the metrics are not standardized across enterprises. The council makes $100M+ in resource allocation decisions based on individually presented enterprise reports, not consolidated analytics.

## What Already Exists
Power BI and Tableau can connect to multiple data sources and create multi-view dashboards. But they are designed for single-enterprise analytics — connecting to one company's ERP, CRM, and operational systems. Tribal cross-enterprise analytics requires connecting to fundamentally different systems (casino management, hotel PMS, gas station POS, healthcare EHR, construction project management) and presenting them in a unified framework. No BI template provides a cross-enterprise portfolio view with the specific metrics tribal councils need.

## The Customization Gap
The adaptation requires: (1) connectors to common tribal enterprise systems (Konami/IGT for gaming, Opera for hotels, Veeder-Root for fuel, IHS RPMS for healthcare), (2) a standardized enterprise performance framework that normalizes metrics across different business types (revenue, margin, employment, community benefit), (3) tribal-specific metrics not found in commercial dashboards (per-capita distribution impact, tribal member employment percentage, enterprise contribution to general fund, infrastructure investment), (4) council-appropriate presentation format (summary-level with drill-down, designed for 9-15 person council review sessions, not individual executive consumption), and (5) benchmarking against similar tribal enterprises using anonymized data from participating tribes.

## Target Customer
Analytics consultants serving tribal governments with 3+ enterprise subsidiaries, particularly gaming tribes that need consolidated performance reporting for council decision-making.

## Impact If Solved
Provides tribal councils with unified enterprise performance visibility for the first time. Improves resource allocation decisions across a $100M+ enterprise portfolio. Reduces council reporting preparation from 40-60 hours/month (per enterprise) to 10-15 hours/month (consolidated). Enables benchmarking that helps tribes learn from each other's enterprise performance.
