# Manufacturer Sizing Tools Unified Across Brands

**Niche:** [[niches/solar-installers/battery-storage-integration/profile|Battery Storage Integration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Tesla, Enphase, and Generac each offer battery sizing tools designed to sell their specific product — installers need a manufacturer-agnostic comparison tool that sizes and prices batteries across brands on the customer's specific rate structure and load profile.
**Tags:** #data-integration #quick-win #revenue-impact #gradient-boosting

## The Problem
A solar installer offering battery storage must currently run 2-3 separate manufacturer sizing tools to compare options for a customer. Tesla's Powerwall Design tool sizes Powerwalls and shows backup duration. Enphase's System Builder sizes IQ Batteries for self-consumption optimization. Generac's PWRcell configurator sizes their modular battery system. Each tool uses different input formats, different economic assumptions, and different output presentations — making apples-to-apples comparison difficult. The installer often defaults to whichever brand they're most familiar with rather than recommending the optimal product for the customer's situation.

## What Already Exists
Each manufacturer's tool is well-built for its product line. Tesla's tool shows elegant backup duration animations. Enphase's tool integrates with their microinverter ecosystem. Generac's tool handles their modular cabinet configurations. All are free to certified installers. None compare against competitors.

## The Customization Gap
A manufacturer-agnostic battery comparison platform that: (1) maintains a product database of all major residential battery systems (Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ Battery, Generac PWRcell, SolarEdge Home Battery, Franklin WH, Sonnen) with specifications (capacity, power output, round-trip efficiency, warranty terms, degradation curve, compatible inverters); (2) takes the customer's load profile, utility rate, solar system specs, and backup priority as inputs; (3) sizes the optimal configuration for each brand and outputs a side-by-side comparison: price, annual savings, payback period, backup duration, warranty coverage, and degradation-adjusted 10-year value; and (4) factors in brand-specific constraints — Tesla requires a Powerwall-compatible gateway, Enphase requires IQ microinverters, Generac works with any inverter through AC coupling. The output helps the installer recommend the right battery for the customer rather than the battery they're most comfortable selling.

## Target Customer
Multi-brand solar and storage installers who are certified to install 2+ battery brands and want to make data-driven recommendations. Solar companies transitioning from single-brand to multi-brand storage offerings.

## Impact If Solved
Enables informed battery brand recommendations, increasing customer trust. Saves 30-60 minutes per proposal currently spent running multiple manufacturer tools. Increases storage attach rate by 15-25% through better-matched product recommendations. Could be offered as a SaaS tool at $50-100/month for installers doing 10+ battery projects per month.
