# SolarAPP+ Coverage Expansion

**Niche:** [[niches/solar-installers/permitting-interconnection/profile|Permitting & Interconnection]]
**Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** SolarAPP+ automates building permit review for solar installations but only covers 300+ of 20,000+ US jurisdictions — expanding coverage to the top 2,000 jurisdictions (covering 80%+ of solar installations) would eliminate the permitting bottleneck for the majority of the solar industry.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
SolarAPP+ is the most promising permitting solution in the solar industry. Developed by NREL with DOE funding, it automates the code compliance review that building departments perform manually — the installer submits system specifications, SolarAPP+ checks them against the applicable code requirements, and if compliant, the permit is issued instantly. In participating jurisdictions, permitting time drops from weeks to minutes. The problem is adoption: only 300+ of 20,000+ US jurisdictions have adopted SolarAPP+. The remaining jurisdictions process permits manually, with review times ranging from 3 days to 8 weeks. Adoption is slow because: municipalities must sign an agreement and integrate SolarAPP+ into their permit workflow (IT and policy changes), building officials are skeptical about automated code review replacing human judgment, and small jurisdictions lack the staff to evaluate and implement the platform.

## What Already Exists
SolarAPP+ handles residential rooftop solar code compliance review for NEC, IRC, and IFC requirements. It verifies electrical design (conductor sizing, overcurrent protection, disconnect requirements), structural attachment (based on wind/snow loads), fire setbacks (per IFC), and equipment listings. The platform is free for jurisdictions and installers. It processes permits in minutes rather than weeks.

## The Customization Gap
Three adoption accelerators: (1) jurisdiction onboarding service — a turnkey implementation package for municipalities that handles the IT integration, staff training, and policy template creation, reducing the adoption effort from months to weeks; (2) state-level adoption support — work with state building code offices to mandate or incentivize SolarAPP+ adoption statewide (as California and Colorado have done), creating top-down adoption rather than municipality-by-municipality outreach; (3) a hybrid model for jurisdictions not ready for full adoption — allow building officials to use SolarAPP+ as a review aid (generating a code compliance report) rather than a permit issuance tool, letting them maintain final authority while benefiting from automated checking. Additionally, extending SolarAPP+ to cover battery storage installations and commercial rooftop systems (currently residential-only) would expand its applicability.

## Target Customer
NREL/DOE as the SolarAPP+ development team, seeking adoption strategies. State energy offices seeking to reduce solar soft costs. Municipal building departments overwhelmed with solar permit applications.

## Impact If Solved
Expanding SolarAPP+ to 2,000 jurisdictions would automate permitting for 80%+ of US solar installations. Reduces average permit processing time from 2-4 weeks to minutes for adopting jurisdictions. Saves the solar industry $500M-1B annually in permitting soft costs (coordinator labor, application fees, delay-related carrying costs). Accelerates solar deployment by removing one of the three primary soft cost barriers (permitting, interconnection, customer acquisition).
