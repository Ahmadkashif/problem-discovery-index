# Permitting & Interconnection

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B (embedded — permitting and interconnection costs are built into every solar installation)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting function — every grid-tied installation requires a building permit and utility interconnection agreement
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — SolarAPP+ handles automated permitting in participating jurisdictions (300+ out of 20,000+ US jurisdictions). Most jurisdictions still require paper permit applications submitted in person. Interconnection applications are utility-specific web portals or paper forms.
**Target Buyer:** Solar operations manager, permitting coordinator, or installer seeking to reduce soft costs
**Automation Potential:** Very High — permit applications follow structured formats by jurisdiction, interconnection applications follow utility-specific templates, and both are high-volume repetitive processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Permitting and interconnection are the largest "soft cost" components in residential solar installation — accounting for $0.50-1.50/watt of the total $3-4/watt installed cost, or $3,000-10,000 per residential system. The permitting process: prepare a plan set (site plan, electrical single-line diagram, structural attachment detail, equipment specifications), submit the application (in-person, online portal, or mail — varies by jurisdiction), pay permit fees ($200-1,500), schedule and pass inspection, and receive final approval. The interconnection process: submit an application to the utility (separate from the building permit), provide system specifications, receive utility approval, install the meter configuration, pass utility inspection, and receive Permission to Operate (PTO). Total timeline: 4-16 weeks from permit submission to PTO, varying dramatically by jurisdiction and utility. Some jurisdictions process permits in 3 days; others take 6-8 weeks. Some utilities process interconnection applications online in real-time; others have 12-week queues.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SolarAPP+ (developed by NREL) automates building permit review for participating jurisdictions — the installer submits system specifications, SolarAPP+ verifies code compliance, and the permit is issued automatically. But SolarAPP+ covers only 300+ of 20,000+ jurisdictions and only handles the permit review, not the plan set preparation. For the majority of jurisdictions and for all interconnection applications, the process is manual: the permitting coordinator prepares plan sets in AutoCAD or Aurora's permit package, submits applications through each jurisdiction's unique process, tracks status through phone calls and portal checks, and manages the inspection scheduling and PTO process.
