# Low-Income & Affordable Solar

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — low-income solar programs (SASH, DAC-SASH, IRA low-income bonus credits) require income verification and program application processes that are entirely manual. No solar design or sales tool integrates income-qualified incentive eligibility screening.
**Target Buyer:** Solar installer serving low-income programs, nonprofit solar organization (GRID Alternatives model), or community solar developer with low-income carve-outs
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — incentive eligibility screening is a rules-based automation opportunity, and program application coordination is a workflow management problem

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Low-income solar programs exist at federal (IRA low-income bonus credits — 10-20% additional ITC), state (California DAC-SASH, Illinois Solar for All, New York NY-Sun Affordable), and local (utility low-income programs, municipal incentive programs) levels. Navigating the eligibility requirements, application processes, and stacking rules across these programs is a specialized skill. A low-income household in California may qualify for: DAC-SASH (based on census tract disadvantaged community status), FERA/CARE utility discount rates (based on household income), the IRA low-income bonus credit (based on household income or project location), and state property tax exemption for solar. Each program has different eligibility criteria, different application forms, different processing timelines, and different benefit calculations. An installer specializing in this segment spends 3-5 hours per customer on program research and application processing — more time than the system design itself.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DSIRE (Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency) lists programs but doesn't screen eligibility or generate applications. Aurora Solar's incentive module covers major state and federal incentives but not income-qualified programs with eligibility verification requirements. No tool helps the installer determine which combination of programs maximizes the customer's financial benefit, complete the applications, and track approval status across multiple programs.
