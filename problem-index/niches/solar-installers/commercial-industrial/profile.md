# Commercial & Industrial Solar

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Aurora Solar and Helioscope handle C&I system design. Financial modeling requires custom spreadsheets because C&I rate structures (demand charges, ratchets, TOU periods) are far more complex than residential. Sales cycles are 6-18 months with multiple stakeholders.
**Target Buyer:** C&I solar developer, project manager, or energy consultant
**Automation Potential:** Medium — demand charge analysis requires utility-specific rate modeling that is currently manual; ROI calculation involves tax equity, MACRS depreciation, and ITC structures that change annually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial and industrial solar serves businesses with complex electricity consumption patterns and rate structures. A 500 kW rooftop system on a manufacturing facility interacts with demand charges (the customer pays for peak 15-minute power draw regardless of total consumption), time-of-use rates (energy costs vary by hour), and ratchet clauses (peak demand in one month sets a minimum billing demand for 6-12 subsequent months). Solar reduces energy charges but may not reduce demand charges — if the facility's peak demand occurs at 6 PM when solar production is minimal, demand charges remain unchanged. The financial analysis must model this interaction at 15-minute intervals across 8,760 hours per year, accounting for the specific utility's rate structure, demand charge calculation methodology, and ratchet provisions. This analysis is currently done in custom Excel models that take 4-8 hours to build per project.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Aurora Solar and Helioscope handle C&I system design and production modeling. Financial analysis tools exist but are residential-focused — they don't model demand charges, demand ratchets, or the complex C&I rate structures that vary by utility. Energy Toolbase is the closest to a C&I financial platform but requires extensive manual rate entry and doesn't integrate with design tools. No tool produces a turnkey C&I solar proposal from a utility bill and roof layout.
