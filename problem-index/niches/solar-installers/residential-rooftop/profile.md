# Residential Rooftop Solar

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-17B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~65%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Aurora Solar dominates system design, Solargraf and OpenSolar compete. CRM tools (HubSpot, Salesforce with solar-specific customization) manage the sales pipeline. But post-sale operations (permitting, interconnection, installation scheduling, commissioning) are managed through spreadsheets and email.
**Target Buyer:** Residential solar installer owner or sales manager
**Automation Potential:** High — system design is already semi-automated, savings projections are calculable, and the permitting/interconnection workflow follows jurisdiction-specific but structured processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential rooftop solar is a complex consultative sale with a long cycle (30-90 days from lead to installation) and high customer acquisition cost ($3,000-5,000 per customer). The sales process involves: lead generation, site assessment (roof orientation, shading, structural suitability), system design (panel layout, inverter sizing, production modeling), financial analysis (savings projection, financing options, incentive calculation), proposal presentation, contract signing, permitting, interconnection application, installation, inspection, and utility permission to operate. Each step has failure modes — an inaccurate production estimate leads to a disappointed customer; a missed incentive leads to a bad financial deal; a permit rejection delays the project by weeks. The experienced solar sales rep integrates roof assessment, shading analysis, financial modeling, and customer psychology into a consultative process that takes 3-5 years to master. Junior reps struggle with production accuracy, financing complexity, and the objection handling required for a $25K-40K purchase decision.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Aurora Solar ($200-400/month per user) handles system design, shading analysis, production modeling, and proposal generation. It's the industry standard but the design process still takes 20-30 minutes per proposal, limiting sales reps to 4-6 proposals per day. Savings projections use static utility rate assumptions that don't account for rate escalation patterns, time-of-use rate transitions, or net metering policy changes. Post-sale project management (permitting, interconnection, installation scheduling) is largely manual — most installers use Trello boards or spreadsheets to track project status.
