**Parent Industry:** [[industries/solar-installers|Solar Installers]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — off-grid system design is done with manual calculations or manufacturer sizing tools designed for grid-tied systems. Remote monitoring requires connectivity that off-grid sites often lack.
**Target Buyer:** Off-grid system designer, rural solar installer, or agricultural solar installer
**Automation Potential:** Medium — load profiling for off-grid sizing is a data collection problem, and remote monitoring with limited connectivity is a systems engineering challenge

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Off-grid solar fundamentally differs from grid-tied: there is no utility backup, so the system must be sized to meet 100% of the customer's energy needs through all seasons and weather conditions. Undersizing means the customer runs out of power; oversizing wastes money on unnecessary panels and batteries. The sizing calculation requires detailed load profiling (every appliance, its wattage, and its daily usage pattern), worst-case solar production (December insolation in the customer's location), and battery bank sizing for autonomy days (how many consecutive cloudy days the system must cover). This calculation is more complex than grid-tied design because there's no grid to fill production gaps. Off-grid customers also include agricultural applications — well pump solar, livestock water systems, remote monitoring stations, and electric fence energizers — that have unique load profiles and installation requirements.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Aurora Solar assumes grid-tied operation and doesn't model off-grid battery bank sizing, generator integration, or load prioritization. Off-grid installers use manual calculation spreadsheets, manufacturer sizing tools (Outback, Schneider XW), or simple online calculators that don't account for seasonal variation or autonomous day requirements. No comprehensive off-grid design and monitoring platform exists. Remote monitoring is particularly challenging: off-grid sites by definition lack reliable internet connectivity, requiring cellular or satellite data uplinks with limited bandwidth.
