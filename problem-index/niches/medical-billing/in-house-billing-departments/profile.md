# In-House Billing Departments

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-2B (embedded in practice operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — billing staff use the PM system's billing module but rarely leverage reporting, analytics, or workflow automation features
**Target Buyer:** Practice manager or office administrator handling billing internally alongside other front-desk duties
**Automation Potential:** High — non-expert staff performing expert-level work creates maximum opportunity for guided automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
An estimated 30-40% of small-to-mid-sized medical practices handle billing in-house rather than outsourcing. In these practices, billing is performed by 1-2 staff members who also manage the front desk, schedule patients, verify insurance, and handle patient inquiries. They are not certified coders or billing specialists — they learned on the job, typically trained by the previous person who held the role. The practice manager oversees billing as one of 15 responsibilities. When claims are denied, the response is often to write off the balance rather than appeal, because nobody has the expertise or time to identify the root cause and draft an appeal letter. AR over 90 days routinely reaches 15-25% of total receivables, compared to 5-8% at professional billing companies. The practice owner (physician) knows billing performance is poor but doesn't have visibility into how poor or what specifically is failing.

## Current Tools & Gaps
The practice's EHR/PM system (athenaHealth, eClinicalWorks, Practice Fusion, DrChrono) includes billing modules, but these modules assume a knowledgeable user. They present raw claim statuses, denial codes, and aging reports without interpretation. A denial code of CO-16 (claim lacks information) means nothing to a front-desk staffer without billing training. AR aging reports show dollar amounts by bucket but don't prioritize which accounts to work first or explain why claims aged. The gap is a guided workflow layer that translates billing complexity into step-by-step actions for non-expert staff — essentially an expert system that tells the user exactly what to do next, why, and how.
