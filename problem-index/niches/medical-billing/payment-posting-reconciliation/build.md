# Intelligent Auto-Posting with Contractual Rate Verification

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-billing/payment-posting-reconciliation/profile|Payment Posting & Reconciliation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** An auto-posting engine that reads ERA/835 remittances, applies payments with full adjustment logic (including complex multi-code scenarios), cross-references every payment against the provider's contracted rate per payer, and flags underpayments for automatic appeal — replacing the most labor-intensive and error-prone step in the billing cycle.

## The Problem
Payment posting consumes 15-25% of a billing operation's total labor hours. A billing company processing 8,000 claims/month receives 200-400 ERA/835 files per month from different payers, each containing dozens to hundreds of line items. Each line item includes a payment amount, one or more adjustment reason codes (CARC codes like CO-45 contractual obligation, PR-1 deductible, CO-97 bundling), and remark codes (RARC codes providing additional context). The posting specialist must: (1) match each payment line to the correct claim in the PM system, (2) interpret the adjustment codes to determine what portion is contractual write-off vs. patient responsibility vs. payer error, (3) verify the paid amount matches the provider's contracted rate with that payer, and (4) post the payment with correct adjustment allocations. When an ERA contains a payment that spans multiple claims (bundled payment), a takeback/reversal for a previously paid claim, or a zero-pay with adjustment codes indicating why, the complexity multiplies. Manual posting error rates run 3-8%, and each error creates downstream problems: incorrect patient balances, understated AR, and missed underpayment recovery.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
PM system auto-posting handles simple cases (one payment, one claim, standard adjustments) but fails on the 30-40% of ERA lines that involve complex scenarios. The fundamental problem is that auto-posting requires two knowledge sources that PM systems don't have: (1) The provider's contracted rate per CPT code per payer — this information lives in payer contracts (PDF documents, spreadsheet fee schedules) that are never loaded into PM systems because there's no standard format or import mechanism. (2) The business rules for interpreting adjustment code combinations — CO-45 (contractual) on its own means write off to contract, but CO-45 combined with OA-23 (payment adjusted due to impact of prior payer adjudication) in a secondary claim context means something different. These interpretation rules are learned through experience and vary by payer. Building intelligent auto-posting requires both structured fee schedule data and a trained model for adjustment code interpretation.

## What to Build
A payment posting platform that integrates with the billing company's PM system and clearinghouse. Core workflow: (1) ERA/835 ingestion and parsing — reads electronic remittances from all payers and normalizes the data. (2) Intelligent auto-posting — a decision-tree-based rules engine enhanced with a gradient-boosted exception handler that matches payments to claims, interprets adjustment code combinations in context, and posts with correct allocations. Handles complex scenarios: bundled payments, takebacks, zero-pays, secondary/tertiary payer adjudications, and patient payment splits. (3) Contractual rate verification — the platform ingests payer fee schedules (via PDF parsing or manual upload) and compares every posted payment against the contracted rate, flagging underpayments with the variance amount and payer contact information for recovery. (4) Exception queue — items that the auto-poster cannot resolve with high confidence are routed to a human reviewer with the system's best interpretation and confidence score, reducing manual review time from 5-8 minutes per exception to 1-2 minutes. (5) Underpayment recovery tracking — tracks flagged underpayments through the dispute process to resolution.

## Target Customer
Medical billing companies and in-house billing departments processing 3,000+ claims/month, currently employing 1-3 dedicated payment posting staff, experiencing 3-8% posting error rates and not systematically verifying contractual rate compliance, willing to pay $500-1,500/month for a platform that reduces posting labor by 70% and recovers underpayments.

## Impact If Built
Reduces payment posting labor from 40-80 hours/month to 10-20 hours/month by auto-posting 85-90% of ERA lines without human intervention (up from 60-70% with current PM auto-posting). Contractual rate verification catches 2-5% underpayment rate that most billing operations never detect — for a company posting $2M/month in payments, this recovers $40,000-100,000/month. The combination of labor savings ($2,000-4,000/month) and underpayment recovery ($40,000-100,000/month) makes this one of the highest-ROI tools in the medical billing stack.
