# DME Billing & CMN Lifecycle Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-billing/dme-surgical-billing/profile|DME & Surgical Billing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A billing platform purpose-built for small-to-mid-sized DME suppliers that manages the entire CMN lifecycle — from initial physician order through certificate tracking, rental period calculation, competitive bidding compliance, and reauthorization — replacing spreadsheet-based tracking with automated workflows.
**Tags:** #decision-trees #feature-engineering #time-series-forecasting #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
DME billing is structurally different from physician billing, yet most small DME suppliers (annual revenue $500K-5M) are forced to use either generic medical billing PM systems that don't support HCPCS codes, rental billing, or CMN tracking — or enterprise DME platforms (Brightree, NikoHealth) priced at $500-1,000+/month that are designed for national suppliers with dedicated billing teams. The core challenge is CMN (Certificate of Medical Necessity) lifecycle management: every DME item requires a CMN or DWO (Detailed Written Order) signed by the ordering physician, with clinical criteria specific to the product category. A CPAP machine CMN requires documented sleep study results and AHI scores. A wheelchair CMN requires mobility assessment documentation. These certificates expire and must be renewed — a missed CMN renewal automatically denies all subsequent claims for that item and patient. A DME company supplying 200 patients has 200+ CMN expiration dates to track across 15-20 product categories, each with different renewal intervals. Most track this in Excel spreadsheets or wall calendars.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The DME billing market is smaller and more specialized than physician billing, making it less attractive to PM software vendors. Brightree (acquired by ResMed for $800M) dominates the enterprise segment but prices out small suppliers. The mid-market gap persists because building a DME platform requires deep domain knowledge: HCPCS Level II code structure, CMN form variants by product category, rental vs. purchase billing logic, capped rental period calculations (36-month oxygen rule, 13-month CPAP rule), competitive bidding zones and pricing, and ABN (Advance Beneficiary Notice) requirements. This is a different knowledge domain from CPT-based physician billing, and few development teams have both the billing software expertise and DME regulatory knowledge.

## What to Build
A cloud-based DME billing platform for suppliers with 50-500 active patients. Core modules: (1) HCPCS-native claim creation — built for HCPCS codes with DME-specific fields (product category, rental/purchase flag, CMN reference, ordering physician NPI). (2) CMN lifecycle tracker — ingests CMN documents (scan or digital), extracts expiration dates and clinical criteria per product category, sends renewal alerts 30/60/90 days before expiration, and generates pre-populated renewal request forms for the ordering physician. (3) Rental period calculator — automatically tracks capped rental periods per patient per item, calculates ownership transfer dates, and adjusts billing codes from rental to maintenance at the correct interval. (4) Competitive bidding compliance engine — cross-references the supplier's service area against CMS competitive bidding zones and product categories, applying the correct bid pricing and flagging items that require bid contracts. (5) Revenue forecasting — a time-series model projecting monthly revenue based on active rentals, upcoming ownership transfers, and CMN renewal rates.

## Target Customer
Small-to-mid-sized DME suppliers (50-500 active patients, $500K-5M annual revenue) currently using generic PM systems or spreadsheets for DME billing, unable to justify Brightree's enterprise pricing, and experiencing claim denials due to CMN lapses and rental period miscalculations.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates CMN-related denials (estimated 8-15% of DME claims for small suppliers) by automating expiration tracking and renewal workflows. Prevents rental period billing errors that create overpayment recoupment risk (Medicare audits DME rental periods aggressively). For a DME supplier billing $1.5M annually, preventing 10% CMN-related denials and 3% rental period errors recovers $195,000/year. The estimated 5,000-8,000 small DME suppliers in the US at $200-400/month creates a $12-38M ARR opportunity.
