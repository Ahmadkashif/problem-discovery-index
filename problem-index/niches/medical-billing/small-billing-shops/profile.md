# Small Billing Shops

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many shops run entry-level PM software or even paper superbills with a clearinghouse portal, no analytics, no automation
**Target Buyer:** Solo biller or 1-3 person billing shop owner serving rural or small practices
**Automation Potential:** Very High — these shops have the most manual workflows in the industry, meaning even basic automation delivers outsized ROI

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small billing shops — typically 1-3 people serving 3-10 small provider practices — represent the long tail of the medical billing industry. The owner IS the biller, often working from a home office, handling everything from charge entry to denial follow-up to patient statements. There is no team, no IT support, no analytics capability. Many use entry-level PM systems like Kareo (now Tebra) at the cheapest tier, or even submit claims directly through a clearinghouse portal (Availity, Office Ally) without a PM system at all. Revenue tracking happens in Excel or QuickBooks. The owner typically learned billing at a larger company or practice, went independent, and now runs the business on expertise and personal relationships with their provider clients. These shops cannot afford enterprise RCM tools ($500-2,000/month), don't have time for complex software onboarding, and make purchasing decisions based on immediate cash flow impact.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Office Ally (free clearinghouse), Availity (free eligibility/claims), Kareo/Tebra ($150-300/month basic tier), and CollaborateMD ($200-400/month) are common. QuickBooks or Excel for revenue tracking. Phone and fax for payer communication. No denial analytics, no automated eligibility verification, no predictive cash flow modeling. The gap is an all-in-one platform purpose-built for the 1-3 person shop — combining claim submission, denial tracking, patient billing, revenue forecasting, and basic reporting in a single interface priced under $200/month with zero-configuration setup.
