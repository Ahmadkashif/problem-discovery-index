# Simplified Kareo/Tebra Configuration for Solo Billers

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-billing/small-billing-shops/profile|Small Billing Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Kareo/Tebra is the most common PM system for small billing shops, but its interface is designed for billing teams — a simplified configuration that strips away multi-user workflows and surfaces the 10 features a solo biller actually uses would cut onboarding from weeks to hours.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #automation #worker-facing #data-integration

## The Problem
When a solo biller signs up for Kareo/Tebra ($150-300/month), they are presented with an interface designed for a 5-10 person billing team: role-based access controls, multi-step claim workflows with handoffs, team dashboards, administrator settings, and configuration wizards that assume departmental structure. The solo biller doesn't have departments, roles, or handoffs — they are every role simultaneously. They need to enter charges, submit claims, check statuses, post payments, and generate client reports. These 5 tasks require navigating through 15-20 screens designed for a different user. Most solo billers use 20-30% of Kareo's features and are unaware the rest exist. The cognitive overhead of the unused 70% creates confusion and slows down daily work.

## What Already Exists
Kareo/Tebra is a full-featured PM system with claim creation, clearinghouse submission, ERA posting, denial worklists, patient billing, and reporting. It integrates with major clearinghouses and supports electronic eligibility verification. The product works — it's just not designed for the solo user's workflow.

## The Customization Gap
A "solo biller mode" configuration that: (1) Hides multi-user features — removes role-based access, team dashboards, claim assignment workflows, and administrator settings that don't apply to a single-user operation. (2) Creates a single-screen daily workflow — a unified view showing today's charges to enter, claims to submit, denials to review, payments to post, and client reports due, in the order a solo biller naturally processes their day. (3) Pre-configures the most common settings — default clearinghouse, standard billing formats, common payer IDs for the biller's state — eliminating the setup wizard that assumes IT support. (4) Adds client-level reporting shortcuts — one-click generation of the monthly collection report per provider client that solo billers currently build manually in Excel. This is a UX layer and configuration template, not a new product — it makes the existing product usable for its largest underserved user segment.

## Target Customer
Solo billers and 1-2 person shops currently using Kareo/Tebra but only accessing 20-30% of its capabilities, frustrated by interface complexity, and spending time navigating features they don't need.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces the solo biller's daily PM navigation time by 30-40 minutes by eliminating unnecessary screen transitions and surfacing relevant features in workflow order. Increases adoption of existing Kareo features (denial worklists, ERA auto-posting, eligibility verification) that solo billers currently skip because they don't know how to find or configure them. For Kareo/Tebra, this reduces churn in the small-shop segment — estimated at 25-30% annually — by making the product feel purpose-built for the solo user.
