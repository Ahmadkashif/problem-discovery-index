# Payer-Specific Appeal Deadline Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-billing/denial-management-appeals/profile|Denial Management & Appeals]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-billing|Medical Billing]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Every payer has different appeal windows (Medicare 120 days, Cigna 60 days, some Medicaid plans 30 days) and a missed deadline means permanent revenue loss — but no billing tool tracks these deadlines dynamically across payers, leaving denial analysts to manage timely filing in spreadsheets or memory.
**Tags:** #decision-trees #feature-engineering #automation #compliance #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a claim is denied, the billing operation has a limited window to file an appeal or corrected claim. This window varies dramatically by payer: Medicare allows 120 days for a redetermination (first-level appeal), commercial payers range from 30 to 180 days depending on the payer and the appeal level, and some state Medicaid programs allow as few as 20 business days. Within a single billing company, denial analysts manage appeals across 15-30 payers, each with different deadlines. A denial received today from UnitedHealthcare has a 90-day appeal window; a denial received the same day from a state Medicaid plan may have a 30-day window. Missing the deadline by even one day means the claim is permanently unrecoverable — the payer will not accept a late appeal regardless of the merit. No PM system or clearinghouse tracks payer-specific appeal deadlines dynamically. Denial worklists show denial date and dollar amount but not "days remaining to appeal."

## Why It's Still Broken
PM systems track claim dates but don't store payer-specific appeal deadline rules. Clearinghouses deliver denial notifications but don't calculate appeal due dates. Billing companies manage deadlines through: (1) the denial analyst's memory ("I know Aetna gives us 90 days"), (2) Excel spreadsheets with manually calculated due dates, or (3) generic calendar reminders that don't account for payer-specific windows. None of these methods handle the volume — a billing company receiving 500 denials/month across 20 payers would need to maintain 500 individual deadline entries with 20 different calculation rules. When a payer changes its appeal deadline (which happens without prominent notice), the spreadsheet and memory-based methods don't update. The result: an estimated 3-5% of appealed claims are filed past deadline, and an unknown additional percentage of denials are never appealed because the analyst assumed the deadline had passed when it hadn't.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A payer appeal deadline engine that integrates with the PM system or clearinghouse denial feed and adds dynamic deadline tracking: (1) Deadline calculator — maintains a database of appeal window rules per payer per appeal level (first appeal, second appeal, external review), and when a denial is received, automatically calculates the appeal due date and adds it to the denial record. (2) Deadline-aware worklist — re-sorts the denial worklist by urgency, floating claims approaching their appeal deadline to the top regardless of dollar amount. (3) Deadline alerts — sends escalating alerts at 30, 14, and 7 days before each appeal deadline, with the alert escalating to a supervisor if the claim hasn't been worked within 7 days of deadline. (4) Payer rule updates — monitors payer communications for appeal deadline changes and updates the calculation rules, retroactively recalculating deadlines for all active denials from that payer. (5) Missed deadline reporting — tracks claims where the appeal deadline was missed, showing the revenue permanently lost and identifying whether the miss was due to workload (too many denials, not enough analysts) or process failure (deadline not tracked).

## Who Feels the Pain
The denial analyst who mentally tracks dozens of payer-specific appeal windows while managing a worklist of 100-200 active denials, and the billing company owner who loses revenue to missed deadlines without visibility into how much is lost or why.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates missed appeal deadlines, recovering the 3-5% of appealed revenue currently lost to late filings. For a billing company filing 300 appeals/month at $200 average claim value, preventing 12-15 missed deadlines recovers $2,400-3,000/month. More significantly, the deadline-aware worklist ensures that time-sensitive high-value denials are worked first, increasing overall appeal recovery rates by prioritizing claims that would otherwise expire. The denial analyst's cognitive load drops substantially when deadline tracking is automated — they focus on the appeal argument rather than the calendar.
