# Trust Administration & Funding

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/estate-planning|Estate Planning Law Firms]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — trust accounting software exists but institutional coordination is entirely manual
**Target Buyer:** Trust administration paralegal, estate administrator, successor trustee
**Automation Potential:** Very High — asset retitling and institutional coordination follow documented procedures that vary by institution but are fully rule-based

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
After death, the successor trustee must inventory all assets, retitle them into the trust (if not already funded), notify beneficiaries, file tax returns (Form 1041, estate tax returns), and make distributions. Each financial institution (Fidelity, Schwab, Chase, Vanguard) has different procedures — different forms, different document requirements (original vs. copy of death certificate), different processing timelines. During the grantor's lifetime, 30-40% of assets never get properly titled into the trust, requiring the pour-over will to go through probate — defeating the purpose of the trust.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Trust accounting software (Fiduciary Accounting System, Sitgo) handles the accounting and distribution calculations but not the institutional coordination workflow. Practice management (Clio, Tabs3) tracks the matter but doesn't know Fidelity's specific retitling requirements vs. Schwab's. There is no database of institution-specific trust administration procedures.
