# Institution-Specific Trust Administration Procedure Database

**Niche:** [[niches/estate-planning/trust-administration-funding/profile|Trust Administration & Funding]]
**Industry:** [[industries/estate-planning|Estate Planning Law Firms]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Estate administrators instantly know the exact steps, forms, and documents required to retitle assets at any financial institution — eliminating the hours spent calling each institution's trust department to ask "what do you need?"
**Tags:** #large-language-models #bert #transfer-learning #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation

## The Problem
When a grantor dies and the successor trustee must retitle assets, each financial institution has its own procedures. Fidelity requires their specific trust certification form, a certified copy of the death certificate, and the full trust document or a certificate of trust. Schwab requires a different form, accepts copies, and has a different timeline. Chase requires an in-branch visit. Vanguard handles everything by mail. Estate administration paralegals spend 2-4 hours PER institution just figuring out what's needed, calling trust departments, and waiting on hold. Across a typical estate with 6-10 institutions, this is 15-30 hours of pure procedural research.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The information is institution-specific, changes periodically, and isn't published in a structured format. Building the database requires collecting and maintaining procedures from hundreds of financial institutions — a data collection challenge, not a technical one. Estate planning firms see this as paralegal work, not a product opportunity. No single firm has enough volume to justify the build, but the industry collectively does.

## What to Build
A searchable database of trust administration procedures by financial institution — for each institution: required forms (with links/PDFs), required documents (original vs. copy, notarized vs. not), processing timeline, contact information for the trust department, and any institution-specific quirks. Crowdsourced updates from the estate planning community keep it current. Integrated with a task tracker that generates the institution-specific checklist for each estate.

## Target Customer
Trust administration paralegals and estate administrators at estate planning law firms handling 20+ estates per year. Secondary: professional fiduciaries and corporate trustees.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-institution research time from 2-4 hours to 15 minutes. Across a typical estate, saves 15-25 hours of paralegal time. Reduces delays caused by submitting incomplete documents to institutions.
