# Lifetime Trust Funding Gap Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/estate-planning/trust-administration-funding/profile|Trust Administration & Funding]]
**Industry:** [[industries/estate-planning|Estate Planning Law Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Estate planning attorneys stop discovering at death that their client's trust was never funded — the #1 cause of unnecessary probate despite having a "complete" estate plan.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #cross-validation #data-integration #revenue-impact #compliance

## The Problem
The most common estate planning failure is not a bad document — it's an unfunded trust. The attorney drafts a beautiful revocable living trust, but 30-40% of clients never retitle their assets into it. The house stays in the client's individual name. The brokerage account stays individually owned. The bank account is never re-registered. When the client dies, these assets must go through probate via the pour-over will — exactly the process the trust was designed to avoid. The attorney typically doesn't discover this until death, when it's too late.

## Why It's Still Broken
Attorneys provide funding instructions at signing (a letter telling the client which accounts to retitle and how), but clients don't follow through. There's no systematic follow-up. The attorney has no visibility into whether the client actually completed the funding. Checking requires calling each financial institution — impractical for a firm with hundreds of clients. The incentive is misaligned: the firm gets paid for the plan drafting, not for the funding follow-up. But unfunded trusts generate probate work (which is revenue) and damage the firm's reputation (which is not).

## What a Fix Looks Like
A client-facing funding checklist portal where clients confirm each retitling as completed, with automated reminders at 30/60/90 days post-signing. The attorney's dashboard shows funding completion rates across all clients, with red flags for clients who haven't started funding 60+ days after signing. Annual "trust health check" reminders prompt clients to confirm their funding status is current (especially after acquiring new assets — a new home, a new investment account). Integration with financial institution APIs (where available) could auto-verify funding status.

## Who Feels the Pain
The client's family — who discovers at the worst possible time (death of a loved one) that probate is required despite paying for an estate plan that was supposed to avoid it. The estate planning attorney — who faces a reputational hit and an awkward conversation with the family. The trust administration paralegal — who must now administer both a trust AND a probate.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces unfunded trust rates from 30-40% to under 10%. Generates ongoing revenue for the firm through funded annual review engagements. Prevents $5,000-$30,000 in unnecessary probate costs per estate.
