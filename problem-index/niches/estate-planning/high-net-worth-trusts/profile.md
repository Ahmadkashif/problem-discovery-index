## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
HNW estate planning operates under entirely different constraints than basic wills. Attorneys must model irrevocable trusts (GRATs, QPRTs, ILITs, dynasty trusts, charitable remainder trusts) against current and projected federal estate tax law, state estate/inheritance tax rules, and generation-skipping transfer tax implications. A single client may have assets across multiple states and countries, requiring coordination with foreign counsel and compliance with international tax treaties. The 2026 estate tax exemption sunset (from ~$13M to ~$7M per person) creates an unprecedented urgency: every HNW client's plan must be reviewed and potentially restructured before the law changes, and attorneys are doing this analysis manually in Excel.

## Current Tools & Gaps
WealthDocx (by WealthCounsel) is the dominant drafting platform — it generates trust documents from templates with customizable provisions. But WealthDocx is a document assembly tool, not a planning tool. It doesn't model tax outcomes, compare trust structures quantitatively, or flag when a law change invalidates a client's existing plan. Tax modeling happens in Excel spreadsheets that each attorney builds and maintains independently. Estate planning software like Estates & Trusts (by Bloomberg Tax) provides tax reference but not client-specific scenario modeling. No tool connects the planning analysis to the document drafting workflow.
