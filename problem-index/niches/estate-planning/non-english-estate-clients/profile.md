# Non-English Estate Clients

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/estate-planning|Estate Planning]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.3-0.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — no estate planning tool handles foreign asset documentation, multi-jurisdiction planning, or non-English client intake; attorneys serving immigrant communities build their own workflows from scratch
**Target Buyer:** Estate planning attorney serving immigrant communities, often bilingual attorneys in metro areas with large immigrant populations
**Automation Potential:** Medium — foreign trust reporting compliance (Form 3520/3520-A) and cross-border asset documentation are systematizable; cultural inheritance expectation mapping requires more nuance

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Estate planning for immigrant families involves layers of complexity that standard tools don't address. Clients may hold assets in their home country (real estate, bank accounts, business interests) that must be accounted for in the US estate plan but are subject to foreign inheritance laws and tax treaties. US persons with interests in foreign trusts must file Form 3520 (annual return for transactions with foreign trusts) and Form 3520-A (annual information return of the foreign trust) — penalties for non-filing start at $10,000 per form per year. Cultural expectations about inheritance (primogeniture traditions, expectations of equal division among children, community property norms from civil law countries) may conflict with US legal defaults. Language barriers make client intake, document explanation, and signing ceremonies significantly more time-intensive.

## Current Tools & Gaps
WealthDocx and other document assembly tools generate US estate planning documents in English using US legal frameworks. They have no templates for clients with foreign assets, no integration with foreign trust reporting requirements, and no multilingual intake or document explanation capabilities. International tax compliance software (GoSystem, Lacerte) handles Form 3520/3520-A filing but doesn't connect to the estate planning workflow. No tool helps attorneys bridge the gap between a client's cultural inheritance expectations and US legal options.
