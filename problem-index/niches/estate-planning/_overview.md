## Why These Niches

Estate planning is not one market — it fragments along client wealth level (HNW multi-trust structures vs. middle-class simple wills), life circumstance (blended families, disabled dependents, immigrant assets), regulatory domain (federal estate tax vs. state Medicaid rules vs. SSI benefits), and workflow stage (planning vs. document drafting vs. post-death administration). These 8 niches cover the full span: the two largest revenue segments (HNW trusts and basic wills/POA), the two most digitally neglected (elder law/Medicaid and special needs trusts where state-specific rules make generic tools useless), the two most underserved populations (blended families with competing interests and non-English-speaking clients with cross-border assets), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (document assembly across 6-8 document packages and post-death trust administration coordination). Excluded: estate litigation (distinct practice area), corporate succession planning (overlaps with business law), and charitable giving vehicles (primarily tax advisory, not estate planning workflow).
