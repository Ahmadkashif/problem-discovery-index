**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%

**Target Buyer:** Elder law attorney specializing in Medicaid planning and asset protection for aging clients

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Elder law Medicaid planning sits at the intersection of estate planning and healthcare benefits law. The core problem: nursing home care costs $8,000-$15,000/month, Medicaid covers it but only for applicants with assets below state-specific thresholds (typically $2,000 for the applicant). Families must "spend down" or restructure assets to qualify, but Medicaid imposes a 5-year look-back period that penalizes transfers made to become eligible. The attorney must model: which assets are countable vs. exempt (home equity, car, burial funds), which spend-down strategies are permissible (Medicaid-compliant annuities, caregiver agreements, home modifications), and what the penalty period would be for any transfers already made. Every state has different income limits, asset limits, home equity caps, and look-back penalty divisors, making this intensely state-specific.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No estate planning software integrates Medicaid eligibility rules by state. Attorneys reference state Medicaid manuals (hundreds of pages, updated irregularly) and calculate eligibility manually. ElderCounsel (a WealthCounsel sister product) provides document drafting for elder law but not eligibility calculation or spend-down modeling. Medicaid planning software is essentially nonexistent — a few firms have built internal spreadsheets, but there is no commercial product that takes a client's asset picture and models pathways to Medicaid eligibility across different strategies.
