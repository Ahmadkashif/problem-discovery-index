# Community Mental Health Centers (CMHCs)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-18B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many run on legacy systems mandated by state contracts, some still use paper for portions of clinical workflows
**Target Buyer:** Executive director or IT coordinator (purchasing often constrained by state/county contracts and grants)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — state reporting, grant compliance documentation, and sliding scale eligibility are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
CMHCs serve the safety-net population — uninsured, Medicaid, court-ordered, crisis, and co-occurring disorder clients — funded through state/county contracts and federal grants (SAMHSA block grants), not commercial insurance. This creates unique requirements: state-mandated reporting formats that change annually, sliding-scale fee determination based on income verification, mandatory crisis and walk-in services, and compliance with CCBHC (Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic) standards. Their EHR is often dictated by the state contract, and staff are overwhelmed and under-resourced with no capacity for lengthy tool implementations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many CMHCs use Netsmart or state-mandated legacy systems; some use Qualifacts (CareLogic). These handle basic clinical documentation but fail at generating state-specific compliance reports without manual data extraction, managing sliding-scale eligibility documentation, tracking grant deliverables alongside clinical outcomes, and coordinating with courts for mandated treatment documentation.
