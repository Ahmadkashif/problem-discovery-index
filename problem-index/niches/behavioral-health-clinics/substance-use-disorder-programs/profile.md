# Substance Use Disorder Treatment Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — clinical workflows around MAT, group therapy, and ASAM assessments are poorly digitized; many programs still use paper ASAM forms and manual PDMP checks
**Target Buyer:** Program director or clinical director at IOP/PHP/residential SUD treatment facilities
**Automation Potential:** Medium — ASAM level-of-care assessments, group therapy documentation, and medication reconciliation are automatable but constrained by 42 CFR Part 2 privacy rules

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
SUD treatment is structurally different from talk therapy. Patients attend multiple group sessions daily (3-5 per day in IOP/PHP), receive medication-assisted treatment (MAT — Suboxone, methadone, naltrexone) requiring PDMP integration, undergo ASAM Criteria assessments for level-of-care placement across six clinical dimensions, and face 42 CFR Part 2 privacy restrictions that limit information sharing even with the patient's other providers. Billing uses H-codes (H0015 for group, H0004 for individual) often to Medicaid or state block grant programs with unique authorization rules. Relapse and readmission are part of the treatment model, not failures — outcome tracking must account for non-linear recovery trajectories.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Netsmart and Qualifacts have SUD-specific modules but are enterprise-priced. KIPU Health targets residential treatment but is weak on outpatient IOP workflows. ASAM assessments are still administered via paper/PDF with manual scoring. Group therapy documentation requires per-patient progress notes for sessions with 8-12 participants — current EHR group note features generate boilerplate identical notes that payers reject. MAT prescribing workflows don't integrate with state PDMP systems, and 42 CFR Part 2 consent management is tracked on paper or in spreadsheets.
