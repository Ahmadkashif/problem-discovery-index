# Intake Triage & Patient-Clinician Matching

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B (embedded in practice operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~2-3% (but every multi-clinician practice needs it)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — intake coordinators match patients to therapists via phone calls, spreadsheets, and institutional knowledge that lives in one person's head
**Target Buyer:** Intake coordinator, operations director, or practice owner at a multi-clinician group practice
**Automation Potential:** Very High — structured matching problem with clear inputs (patient needs), constraints (clinician availability, insurance, specialties), and measurable outcomes (retention past session 3)

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
When a new patient contacts a multi-clinician behavioral health practice, an intake coordinator must solve a complex matching problem: presenting concern must match clinician specialty; patient insurance must match clinician payer credentials; schedule preferences must align with availability; and softer factors (therapist gender preference, language, therapeutic modality preference like CBT vs. EMDR vs. psychodynamic) should be considered. In a 15-therapist practice, this matching logic lives entirely in the coordinator's head — when that coordinator leaves, the institutional knowledge walks out the door. Bad matches lead to patient dropout after session 1-2, wasting the intake investment and losing the patient. The average time from "I want to start therapy" to "I give up trying to find a therapist" is 72 hours, so matching speed is as important as matching quality.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EHRs like TherapyNotes and SimplePractice show individual clinician calendars and insurance credentials, but none synthesize this into a matching recommendation. SimplePractice has a basic intake form but no matching logic. Psychology Today directory listings drive inbound inquiries but route to the practice generically, not to the right clinician. Generic scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) handle booking but know nothing about clinical matching. The matching is 100% human judgment today.
