# Multi-Clinician Group Practices

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $35-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — outgrown solo tools like SimplePractice but can't afford Netsmart/Qualifacts enterprise platforms ($50K+ implementation)
**Target Buyer:** Practice owner or clinic director managing 5-30 clinicians
**Automation Potential:** High — scheduling, credentialing, and caseload balancing are all rule-heavy processes with clear decision logic

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
These practices sit in the "messy middle" — too big for solo-practitioner EHRs (which don't handle multi-provider scheduling, split billing, or cross-clinician patient visibility), too small for enterprise platforms (which require dedicated IT staff and six-figure contracts). They need practice-level intelligence: multi-provider scheduling that respects clinician specialties, insurance credentials, and caseload limits; cross-clinician clinical coordination for shared patients; and practice-wide analytics (revenue per clinician, no-show rates by provider, caseload distribution) that no current mid-market tool provides.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SimplePractice and TherapyNotes work for individual clinician workflows but strain at 10+ providers — they lack practice-level dashboards, caseload balancing, and multi-clinician credentialing tracking. Valant targets this segment but has limited analytics and rigid workflows. Practice Fusion and DrChrono are medical EHRs forced into behavioral health use, missing therapy-specific features like treatment plan tracking and session note templates.
