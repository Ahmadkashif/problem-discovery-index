# School-Based Mental Health Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — therapists embedded in schools use school IT systems, not healthcare EHRs; documentation is split between school records and clinical records
**Target Buyer:** School district mental health coordinator, or community mental health agency director contracting with school districts
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Medicaid school-based billing, IEP integration, and caseload tracking are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
School-based mental health providers operate at the intersection of healthcare and education — licensed clinicians (LCSWs, LPCs) providing therapy in school settings while navigating school calendars that break continuity of care, dual FERPA/HIPAA compliance requirements, IEP/504 plan documentation when mental health services are part of a student's educational plan, and state-specific Medicaid school-based billing rules entirely different from clinic-based Medicaid. Their referral pipeline is teachers and school counselors, not physicians, and their documentation must serve both clinical and educational purposes simultaneously.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most school-based therapists use the school district's student information system (SIS) for basic record-keeping and a separate personal system (sometimes Word documents or paper) for clinical notes. Purpose-built tools barely exist — SchoolMint and Branching Minds handle student interventions but aren't clinical EHRs. TherapyNotes works for clinical documentation but doesn't integrate with school SIS, IEP systems, or Medicaid school-based billing workflows.
