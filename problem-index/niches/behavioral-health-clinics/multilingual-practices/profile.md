# Multilingual / Non-English-Serving Practices

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — practices serving non-English populations typically use general EHRs with English-only interfaces; validated assessment tools may not be available in the patient's language or dialect
**Target Buyer:** Bilingual practice owner, community health center behavioral health director, or agency director serving immigrant/refugee populations
**Automation Potential:** Medium — translation of clinical materials, culturally adapted assessments, and interpreter coordination are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
These practices serve populations where the dominant language is Spanish, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Arabic, or other non-English languages, and the clinical workflow differs fundamentally from English-language practices. Assessment instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, CSSRS) have validated translations for some languages but not all, and the validated translations may not match the local dialect (Mexican Spanish vs. Puerto Rican Spanish vs. Central American Spanish). Cultural idioms of distress — "nervios," "susto," "ataque de nervios" — are real clinical presentations that English-language clinical tools don't capture. Interpreter coordination adds 15-20 minutes per session when therapist and patient don't share a language.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EHR interfaces are English-only — SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, and Valant offer no Spanish-language clinician interface. Patient portals may offer limited language options but clinical scoring and documentation remain English-only. Assessment tools exist in some languages through standalone platforms but aren't integrated into EHR workflows. Interpreter services (LanguageLine, Boostlingo) operate as disconnected call-ins, not integrated into scheduling or session documentation.
