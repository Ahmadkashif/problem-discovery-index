# Credentialing & Payer Enrollment Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/behavioral-health-clinics|Behavioral Health Clinics]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (outsourced credentialing services market)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3-4% (but touches every provider)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — CAQH ProView exists as a central data repository, but the actual credentialing workflow across payers is still manual portal navigation, faxes, and phone calls to payer credentialing departments
**Target Buyer:** Billing manager, practice manager, or credentialing specialist at a multi-clinician practice; also credentialing service companies
**Automation Potential:** Very High — highly repetitive, document-heavy, rule-based process with clear success/failure outcomes and predictable timelines

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every behavioral health clinician must be individually credentialed with every insurance payer the practice accepts. For a 15-therapist practice accepting 10 payers, that's 150 credentialing applications plus renewals every 2-3 years. Behavioral health adds specific complexity that medical credentialing platforms miss: multiple license types (LCSW, LMFT, LPC, PsyD, PhD, PMHNP) each with different scope-of-practice rules by state; pre-licensed clinicians (associates working toward full licensure) require supervision documentation submitted with credentialing packets; telehealth credentialing is often a separate application; and many payers have behavioral health carve-out networks (Optum Behavioral Health, Magellan, Carelon) with their own credentialing processes separate from the medical payer. Until credentialed, a clinician cannot bill — meaning revenue ramp-up for new hires takes 3-6 months.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CAQH ProView stores provider data centrally, but each payer still requires its own application process. Medallion and Modio Health handle credentialing workflows for medical providers but don't model behavioral health license types, supervision requirements, or carve-out networks. The workflow remains: update CAQH profile, submit application to each payer's portal or via fax, track status via phone calls to payer credentialing departments, respond to requests for additional documents, confirm effective date, and repeat every 2-3 years.
