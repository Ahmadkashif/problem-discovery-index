# Heritage Language Curriculum Platform for Bilingual Learners

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/heritage-language-schools/profile|Heritage Language Schools]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No curriculum platform exists for heritage language learners — children who understand the language from home but need to develop literacy, formal register, and cultural knowledge, a profile that neither ESL nor foreign language materials address.
**Tags:** #recommendation #nlp #text-classification #ai-platform

## The Problem
A 10-year-old in a Chinese heritage language school can carry on a conversation with her grandmother in Cantonese but cannot read a newspaper, write a formal email, or use academic vocabulary. Her textbook, imported from China, assumes a Mandarin-speaking child who already reads and writes at grade level — the lessons are incomprehensible. The volunteer teacher improvises, but without training in heritage language pedagogy, defaults to treating the class like a beginner foreign language course (teaching basic greetings the children already know) or like a native-language class (assigning grade-level reading the children cannot access). No curriculum platform addresses the heritage learner profile: strong oral/aural skills with weak literacy and formal register.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The market is fragmented across dozens of languages (Mandarin, Arabic, Hindi, Korean, Japanese, Persian, Russian, Vietnamese, Greek, Armenian...), each with its own script system, orthographic complexity, and cultural curriculum requirements. Building a heritage language curriculum platform for even one language requires: language-specific content creation, bilingual interface design, leveling that accounts for the heritage learner's split proficiency (strong listening, weak reading), and cultural content that resonates with diaspora identity. The total addressable market per language is small.

## What to Build
A modular curriculum platform that: (1) provides a heritage-learner-specific placement assessment (measuring receptive vs. productive skill gaps); (2) offers leveled reading and writing materials for heritage learners in top 5 heritage languages (Mandarin, Spanish, Arabic, Hindi, Korean); (3) leverages the learner's existing oral proficiency to accelerate literacy development (audio-supported reading, dictation exercises, oral-to-written bridging activities); (4) includes cultural content relevant to diaspora identity (not just homeland culture); and (5) provides lesson plans structured for the 2-hour weekly session format common in heritage schools.

## Target Customer
Directors of heritage language schools with 50-200 students who currently assemble curricula from imported textbooks, photocopies, and YouTube videos because no purpose-built materials exist for their learner profile.

## Impact If Built
Heritage learners reach literacy benchmarks 2-3x faster when materials match their actual proficiency profile (strong oral, weak written) instead of forcing them through beginner oral content they already know. Schools gain a structured curriculum that reduces volunteer teacher preparation time from 3-5 hours per week to 30-60 minutes.
