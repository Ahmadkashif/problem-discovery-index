# Multi-Level Classroom Management Tool for Solo Instructors

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/rural-community-esl/profile|Rural & Small-Town Community ESL]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Google Classroom and Canvas manage single-level courses, but a rural ESL instructor teaching beginners, intermediates, and advanced students simultaneously in one room needs a tool that orchestrates parallel activities for 3-4 levels within a single class session.
**Tags:** #recommendation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
A solo ESL instructor at a rural community college teaches a single evening class attended by 20 students ranging from pre-literate beginners to low-intermediate speakers. She cannot run a single lesson because the content that challenges intermediates is incomprehensible to beginners, and content appropriate for beginners bores intermediates into dropping out. She station-rotates: beginners work on alphabet worksheets, low-beginners do listening exercises on a shared tablet, intermediates practice conversation in pairs, and she circulates among groups. The logistics of planning and managing 3-4 simultaneous activity streams for a 2-hour class take 3-4 hours of preparation — unsustainable for a part-time instructor.

## What Already Exists
Google Classroom and Canvas provide course management for single-level classes. Differentiated instruction tools (Nearpod, Pear Deck) allow teachers to push different content to different student devices during a lesson — but assume each student has a device.

## The Customization Gap
A multi-level lesson orchestration tool that: (1) provides pre-built station-rotation lesson plans for 2-4 proficiency levels that can be taught simultaneously; (2) specifies what each level group does during each 15-20 minute rotation (clear activity instructions that can be printed and placed at each station); (3) includes both tech-based activities (for the 1-2 available tablets/computers) and paper-based activities (for groups without devices); (4) handles the pacing challenge — when the instructor is with one group, the other groups have self-directed activities with clear enough instructions for independent work; and (5) reduces lesson planning from 3-4 hours to 30-60 minutes per session.

## Target Customer
Solo ESL instructors at rural community colleges and community organizations who teach multi-level classes of 10-25 students and spend more time planning than teaching.

## Impact If Solved
Instructor preparation time drops by 60-70%, making the part-time rural ESL teaching position sustainable. Student learning outcomes improve 20-30% because each level receives appropriately targeted instruction rather than content pitched at the middle level (too hard for beginners, too easy for intermediates).
