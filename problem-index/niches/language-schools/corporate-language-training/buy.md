# Business-Context Proficiency Assessment Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/corporate-language-training/profile|Corporate Language Training Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** TOEIC and Linguaskill assess general English proficiency, but they don't measure whether an engineer can participate in a technical design review or whether a sales manager can negotiate contract terms — the business-context competencies that corporate clients actually care about.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #speech-processing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a pharmaceutical company invests $200K in English training for its German R&D team, the HR director wants to know: "Can Dr. Mueller now present clinical trial results to the FDA advisory committee in English?" The language training provider can report that Dr. Mueller improved from B1 to B2 on a general proficiency scale, but this does not answer the HR director's question. General proficiency assessments (TOEIC, Linguaskill, CEFR-based tests) measure language ability in abstract terms, not in the specific business contexts that motivated the training investment.

## What Already Exists
TOEIC (ETS) assesses workplace English proficiency. Linguaskill (Cambridge) provides rapid computer-adaptive testing for placement and progress measurement. Versant (Pearson) offers automated speaking proficiency assessment. All measure general proficiency on standardized scales.

## The Customization Gap
A business-context proficiency assessment framework that: (1) defines can-do competencies specific to business functions (participating in technical meetings, writing project status emails, negotiating vendor contracts, presenting to executive audiences); (2) assesses each competency through role-play scenarios and simulated tasks relevant to the learner's actual job; (3) maps assessment results to the general CEFR scale (for standardized reporting) while providing business-context-specific feedback (for HR ROI demonstration); and (4) allows the language training provider to customize the assessment scenarios to each corporate client's industry and job functions.

## Target Customer
Corporate language training providers who need to demonstrate business-relevant proficiency gains to HR buyers, not just CEFR level improvements — particularly when competing for contract renewals against providers who can only show general proficiency metrics.

## Impact If Solved
Training providers who can demonstrate business-context competency gains command 20-30% price premiums over providers reporting only general proficiency scores. Corporate clients retain training contracts 2x longer when they receive evidence that training produces job-relevant language capabilities.
