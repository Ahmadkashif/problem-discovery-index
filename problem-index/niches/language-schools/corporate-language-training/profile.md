# Corporate Language Training Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15-20%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — providers use Zoom for live instruction and generic LMS for content delivery, but lack tools for measuring business-context language performance, tracking ROI for the corporate client, and managing multi-site program logistics
**Target Buyer:** Operations Manager at a B2B language training company serving corporate clients (Berlitz, VOXY, Rosetta Stone Enterprise, or regional providers)
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — corporate language programs follow structured curricula, have measurable proficiency targets, and require standardized reporting to HR buyers

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate language training providers sell to HR or L&D departments, not to individual learners. The buyer (HR) measures different outcomes than the learner: HR wants to see proficiency gains mapped to business function (can this engineer participate in English-language design reviews?), compliance evidence (we trained all Germany-based employees on business English as committed), and ROI metrics (did English training improve cross-border team collaboration scores?). The provider must deliver instruction, measure progress, and report to the corporate client — three distinct workflows that most language schools handle manually.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Berlitz, VOXY, and Rosetta Stone Enterprise provide corporate language training with dashboards for HR buyers showing engagement and completion metrics. These enterprise solutions work well for large accounts but are too expensive and complex for the thousands of regional and independent language training companies serving mid-market corporate clients. Independent providers use Zoom, a generic LMS, and Excel to manage their corporate programs — lacking a purpose-built platform for B2B language training operations.
