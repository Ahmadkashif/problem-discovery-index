# B2B Language Training Operations Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/corporate-language-training/profile|Corporate Language Training Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform today manages the full workflow of a B2B language training company — client proposals, instructor assignment, multi-site scheduling, proficiency tracking, and corporate client reporting — in a single system designed for language training operations.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #ai-platform

## The Problem
An independent corporate language training company managing 15 corporate accounts with 300 total learners across 5 cities must: generate proposals and pricing for new accounts, assign instructors to groups based on language/level/schedule/location, schedule sessions across multiple client sites and timezones, track learner proficiency progress, generate monthly reports for each corporate client's HR department, and manage invoicing across different contract structures. The operations manager coordinates all of this via a combination of email, spreadsheets, a generic calendar tool, and a basic LMS — spending 30-40% of their time on administrative coordination rather than program quality.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The corporate language training market is fragmented across thousands of small regional providers (5-50 instructors each) and a few large global enterprises (Berlitz, EF). Large enterprises build proprietary platforms. Small providers cannot afford enterprise software and are too small individually to attract vendor investment. The workflows — multi-client scheduling, instructor assignment optimization, per-client reporting — are specific to B2B language training operations and do not exist in generic business management tools.

## What to Build
A B2B language training operations platform that: (1) manages corporate client accounts with contract terms, pricing, and reporting schedules; (2) optimizes instructor-to-group assignment based on language pair, proficiency level, schedule availability, and geographic proximity; (3) handles multi-site, multi-timezone scheduling with automated calendar invitations; (4) tracks learner proficiency progression using standardized assessments; (5) generates branded monthly reports for each corporate client showing attendance, proficiency gains, and program utilization; and (6) automates invoicing based on contract terms (per-hour, per-learner, or flat-fee models).

## Target Customer
Operations Managers at independent corporate language training companies with 5-50 instructors serving 10-50 corporate accounts, currently managing operations via spreadsheets and spending 15-20 hours per week on administrative coordination.

## Impact If Built
Operations coordination time drops by 50-60% (reclaiming 8-12 hours per week), instructor utilization improves by 15-20% through optimized assignment, and corporate client satisfaction increases through consistent, professional reporting — directly reducing account churn.
