# Grant-Compliant ESL Program Management System

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/refugee-esl-programs/profile|Refugee & Immigrant ESL Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform today manages the full workflow of a grant-funded refugee ESL program — intake assessment, level placement, attendance tracking, measurable gains reporting, and funder-specific outcome documentation — in a single system designed for small nonprofit staff.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A refugee resettlement agency runs ESL classes funded by WIOA Title II, ORR (Office of Refugee Resettlement), and a state adult education grant. Each funder requires different outcome metrics (CASAS score gains for WIOA, employment outcomes for ORR, attendance hours for state funding), different reporting formats, and different eligibility verification. The program coordinator maintains 3-4 separate tracking spreadsheets, manually cross-references attendance with assessment data, and spends 15-20 hours per quarter assembling funder reports. A single data entry error can trigger a compliance finding that jeopardizes the grant.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The market is almost entirely nonprofit organizations with annual budgets under $500K and technology budgets under $5K. Commercial software vendors cannot build a sustainable business at this price point. The funder reporting requirements are specific and change with each grant cycle. The few platforms that attempt this space (LACES, TOPSpro) are decades-old legacy systems with clunky interfaces designed for compliance rather than usability.

## What to Build
A lightweight, cloud-based ESL program management system that: (1) handles student intake with standardized assessment entry (CASAS, BEST Plus scores); (2) manages class enrollment and attendance with mobile check-in (for programs meeting in community spaces without computers); (3) tracks pre/post assessment gains per student per reporting period; (4) generates funder-specific outcome reports (WIOA NRS tables, ORR quarterly reports, state-specific formats) from the same underlying data; and (5) manages volunteer tutor assignments and scheduling. Priced at $50-150/month to fit nonprofit budgets.

## Target Customer
Program Coordinators at refugee resettlement agencies and adult education centers managing 100-500 ESL students with 2-4 concurrent grant funding sources, currently spending 15-20 hours per quarter on manual funder reporting.

## Impact If Built
Saves 60-80 hours per year in report preparation, reduces data entry errors that trigger compliance findings, and enables program coordinators to spend time on student support rather than spreadsheet management — directly improving the program's capacity to serve refugees during their critical resettlement window.
