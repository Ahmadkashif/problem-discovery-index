# Low-Literacy ESL Learning App

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/refugee-esl-programs/profile|Refugee & Immigrant ESL Programs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Duolingo and USA Learns teach English digitally, but they assume the learner can read instructions in a Roman script — pre-literate and semi-literate refugees cannot navigate text-based interfaces.
**Tags:** #speech-recognition #computer-vision #multimodal #worker-facing

## The Problem
A significant subset of refugee ESL learners (estimated 15-30% in US resettlement programs) are pre-literate: they have had no formal education and cannot read or write in any language. Standard ESL apps present text-based instructions ("Tap the correct answer," "Type the word you hear") that are inaccessible to this population. Even apps designed for beginners assume Roman alphabet literacy. Pre-literate learners need a fundamentally different interface: image-based, audio-driven, with speech input rather than text input.

## What Already Exists
Duolingo provides gamified language learning with a low barrier to entry. USA Learns offers structured ESL courses aligned with adult education standards. Burlington English provides classroom-integrated digital ESL. Cell-Ed offers SMS-based and voice-based English lessons for low-literacy populations (the closest existing solution).

## The Customization Gap
A voice-and-image-first ESL learning interface for pre-literate learners that: (1) uses illustrated scenarios and audio instructions (no reading required at any point in the beginner levels); (2) accepts voice input for all exercises (the learner speaks rather than types); (3) builds Roman alphabet literacy gradually alongside oral English development; (4) uses the learner's L1 for initial navigation and instructions (with audio, not text, in languages like Dari, Swahili, Burmese, and Rohingya that many literacy apps don't support); and (5) focuses on survival English topics (emergency services, medical appointments, children's school enrollment, public transportation) relevant to newly arrived refugees.

## Target Customer
Refugee resettlement agencies and adult education programs serving pre-literate and semi-literate learners who cannot use existing ESL apps, currently receiving instruction only during in-person class hours (4-6 hours per week).

## Impact If Solved
Pre-literate learners gain access to self-study practice between in-person classes, effectively doubling their exposure to English. Programs serving this population report that learners who practice between classes achieve CASAS score gains 40-60% faster than those limited to classroom instruction.
