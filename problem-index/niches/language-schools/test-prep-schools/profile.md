# Test Preparation Schools (TOEFL/IELTS)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20-25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Magoosh, Kaplan, and ETS Official Prep provide digital practice tests, but most brick-and-mortar test prep schools still rely on printed practice materials and generic classroom instruction
**Target Buyer:** Center Manager at a test prep school focused on TOEFL, IELTS, Cambridge (FCE/CAE), or PTE Academic
**Automation Potential:** High — test prep follows structured item formats with known scoring rubrics, making practice scoring and diagnostic feedback highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Test prep schools sell a measurable outcome: a target score on a specific exam. Unlike general language schools where "improvement" is loosely defined, test prep students have a concrete goal (TOEFL 100, IELTS 7.0) and a deadline (university application date). This creates intense performance pressure and clear accountability — the school's reputation depends on published score improvement statistics. The instructional approach is also distinct: rather than broad language development, test prep focuses on test-taking strategies, time management, format-specific skills (integrated writing, lecture note-taking), and targeted practice on weak item types.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ETS and British Council provide official practice tests. Magoosh, Kaplan, and Manhattan Prep offer online prep courses with adaptive practice. Brick-and-mortar schools use these alongside classroom instruction but lack diagnostic tools that identify each student's specific weak item types and prescribe targeted practice. Writing and speaking assessment remains the major bottleneck — the TOEFL iBT and IELTS both include subjective scored components (integrated writing, opinion essay, speaking tasks) that require human evaluation.
