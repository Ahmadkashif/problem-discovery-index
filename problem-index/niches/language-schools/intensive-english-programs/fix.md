# Intermediate-Level Student Plateau and Dropout

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/intensive-english-programs/profile|Intensive English Programs (IEPs)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Students who progress quickly through beginner levels hit a wall at B1-B2, where visible improvement slows dramatically — they lose motivation, question the investment, and drop out before reaching their university admission proficiency target.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #tabular-ml #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Language acquisition follows a well-documented pattern: rapid visible progress at beginner levels (A1-A2), followed by a plateau at intermediate levels (B1-B2) where improvement in fluency, accuracy, and range becomes harder to perceive even as the student is genuinely advancing. IEPs lose 25-40% of students during the intermediate plateau — students who have already invested $5,000-$15,000 in tuition and are 2-3 sessions away from their proficiency goal. Each dropout represents $3,000-$5,000 in lost session revenue and a failed educational outcome.

## Why It's Still Broken
Instructors know the plateau is happening but lack tools to show students their actual progress at intermediate levels. A B1 student's grades stay flat at 75-80% session after session, masking the fact that the B1.2 assessments are substantially harder than the B1.1 assessments. Students compare themselves to their beginner-level progress rate and conclude they've stopped learning. The program has no mechanism to visualize micro-progress (gains in lexical range, reduction in error frequency, increase in discourse complexity) in a way that motivates continued enrollment.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A student-facing progress visualization system that: (1) tracks granular proficiency indicators beyond grades (vocabulary range growth, error type reduction over time, speech fluency metrics from oral assessments, writing complexity indices); (2) presents progress as a continuous growth curve rather than session-by-session grades; (3) projects remaining sessions to reach the student's stated proficiency goal based on current trajectory; and (4) celebrates micro-milestones ("you used 15 new academic vocabulary words this week" or "your mean sentence length increased from 8 to 11 words") that are invisible in traditional grading but meaningful for motivation.

## Who Feels the Pain
Intermediate-level IEP students who are spending $2,000-$5,000 per session and feeling like they are not improving — a perception problem that causes real financial and academic decisions (dropping out, switching schools, returning home) with lasting consequences for the student and the school's revenue.

## Impact If Fixed
Reducing intermediate-level dropout by 20-30% retains 10-30 students per session at a mid-size IEP, translating to $30,000-$150,000 in recovered session revenue and significantly improving the program's completion rate metrics — which drive reputation and future enrollment.
