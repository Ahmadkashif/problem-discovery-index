# LMS with CEFR/ACTFL Proficiency Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/intensive-english-programs/profile|Intensive English Programs (IEPs)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Canvas and Moodle track course grades, but they have no concept of language proficiency levels — an instructor cannot see "this student is B1.2 in speaking but A2 in writing" within the LMS.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
IEPs assess students across 4 language skills (speaking, listening, reading, writing) at CEFR or ACTFL proficiency levels, but the LMS only records aggregate course grades (85% in Level 4 Reading). A student who scores 90% in Level 4 Reading and 65% in Level 4 Writing appears to be a solid Level 4 student — but the skill-level disaggregation reveals they need remediation in writing before advancing. Instructors track this informally; the LMS does not support skill-by-skill proficiency progression natively.

## What Already Exists
Canvas, Moodle, and Google Classroom provide course management, assignment submission, and gradebook functionality. Canvas supports outcomes-based grading via its "Outcomes" feature, which could theoretically map to CEFR descriptors. Some language-specific platforms (Schoology, Edsby) have been adapted for language schools.

## The Customization Gap
A proficiency tracking layer for LMS platforms that: (1) maps each assessment to specific CEFR/ACTFL descriptors and skill domains (speaking, listening, reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary); (2) maintains a per-student proficiency profile showing their current level in each skill domain, updated as assessments are graded; (3) generates can-do statements per CEFR level that communicate proficiency in plain language to students and partner universities; and (4) flags skill-domain imbalances (e.g., "strong in receptive skills, weak in productive skills") that inform differentiated instruction and level-advancement decisions.

## Target Customer
Instructors and Program Directors at IEPs using Canvas or Moodle who need skill-level proficiency data for placement decisions, student advising, and university partner reporting.

## Impact If Solved
Level advancement decisions become data-driven rather than impression-based, reducing misplacement rates from 15-20% to under 5%. University partners receive structured proficiency reports that replace subjective "ready for academic English" recommendations. Instructors gain a clear view of each student's skill profile to guide differentiated instruction.
