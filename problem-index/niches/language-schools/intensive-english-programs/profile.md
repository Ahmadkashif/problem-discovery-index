# Intensive English Programs (IEPs)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-7B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35-45%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most IEPs use a generic LMS (Canvas, Moodle) adapted from higher-ed, paper-based placement tests, and manual SEVIS reporting; purpose-built language school technology adoption is minimal
**Target Buyer:** Program Director at a university-affiliated or standalone IEP enrolling F-1 visa students
**Automation Potential:** High — IEPs have structured academic calendars, standardized proficiency levels, and regulatory reporting requirements that follow defined patterns

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Intensive English Programs are the backbone of US language education for international students. They operate on 8-16 week session cycles, enrolling students on F-1 visas who must maintain full-time enrollment (18+ hours per week) to keep their visa status. This creates a unique dual mandate: the program must deliver effective language instruction AND comply with federal immigration regulations (SEVP/SEVIS reporting). The student population is transient (average enrollment 2-4 sessions), culturally diverse (50+ nationalities in a single program), and academically goal-oriented (most are preparing for university admission). The business model depends on international student recruitment, which is sensitive to immigration policy, exchange rates, and geopolitical events.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IEPs use university LMS platforms (Canvas, Blackboard) designed for credit-bearing courses, not language proficiency progression. Student information systems (Terra Dotta, Sunapsis) handle immigration compliance but don't connect to academic placement or progression data. Placement testing is manual or uses expensive per-test licensed instruments (CASAS, BEST Plus). No integrated platform manages the IEP-specific workflow: placement, level assignment, progression tracking, SEVIS reporting, and re-enrollment.
