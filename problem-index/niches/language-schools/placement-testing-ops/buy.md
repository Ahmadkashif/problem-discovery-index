# Computer-Adaptive Placement Test for School-Volume Use

## The Problem
Schools conducting placement testing face a trade-off between assessment quality and cost/speed. Licensed standardized tests (CASAS, BEST Plus) cost $3-10 per administration and require trained proctors but provide validated scores. Computer-adaptive assessments like Duolingo English Test and Versant provide fast, proctorless testing but are priced for individual high-stakes use ($49-65 per test), making them unaffordable for school-volume placement (200-500 placements per year = $10,000-$32,000). Schools need a computer-adaptive placement test priced for institutional volume use.

## What Already Exists
Duolingo English Test provides a 45-minute computer-adaptive assessment accepted by thousands of universities. Versant (Pearson) provides automated speaking assessment. CASAS eTests offer computer-delivered versions of their standardized assessments. Oxford Placement Test provides a 30-minute adaptive test at approximately $5 per administration.

## The Customization Gap
An institutional-volume placement test that: (1) provides computer-adaptive proficiency assessment across reading, listening, writing, and speaking in under 15 minutes (schools need fast turnaround during peak enrollment); (2) is priced at $3-5 per test with annual institutional licenses; (3) provides CEFR-mapped scores plus school-level analytics (incoming proficiency distribution per session, level capacity planning data); (4) includes skill-disaggregated results (not just an overall score) to inform multi-skill class placement; and (5) offers a proctor-free administration model that students can complete on any device, reducing the need for scheduled testing sessions.

## Target Customer
Program Directors at language schools conducting 200+ placements per year who need a faster, cheaper alternative to human-administered standardized tests without sacrificing placement accuracy.

## Impact If Solved
Placement cost drops from $10-15/student (human tester time) or $49-65/student (Duolingo English Test) to $3-5/student, while placement accuracy remains comparable. Schools eliminate scheduling bottlenecks by offering on-demand online placement testing, reducing enrollment-to-first-class time from 5-7 days to same-day.
