# Placement Testing Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $300-500M (embedded operational cost)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded cost (~3-5% of school operating expenses)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most schools use paper-based standardized tests (CASAS, BEST Plus) or in-house oral interviews conducted by experienced testers, with results recorded manually and placement decisions made by committee or individual judgment
**Target Buyer:** Program Director managing student intake and level placement at any language school type (IEP, community ESL, test prep)
**Automation Potential:** High — placement testing follows structured protocols with defined proficiency scales, standardized tasks, and rubric-based evaluation — exactly the pattern that ML-based assessment can replicate

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Placement testing is the gateway function of every language school: every new student must be assessed and placed before instruction can begin. It is also the primary operational bottleneck during enrollment peaks — a school that can only test 10 students per day cannot enroll more than 10 students per day, regardless of classroom capacity. The testing process requires the school's most experienced staff (placement testers who have developed the tacit diagnostic skill described in the high-impact problem), creating a dependency on scarce human expertise for a high-volume, time-sensitive operation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standardized instruments (CASAS, BEST Plus, BEST Literacy) provide validated placement scores but require trained proctors, per-test license fees ($3-10 per test), and 30-60 minutes per student. In-house oral placement interviews provide richer diagnostic information but depend entirely on the tester's subjective judgment. Computer-adaptive tests (Duolingo English Test) exist for high-stakes contexts but are priced for individual test-takers, not for school-volume placement testing. No assessment tool provides the combined proficiency score + diagnostic profile (L1 interference patterns, fossilization markers) that experienced placement testers generate intuitively.
