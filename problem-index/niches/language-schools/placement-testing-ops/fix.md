# Placement Bottleneck During Peak Enrollment

**Niche:** [[niches/language-schools/placement-testing-ops/profile|Placement Testing Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/language-schools|Language Schools]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** During peak enrollment weeks, schools can only place as many students as their 1-2 experienced testers can assess — 8-12 per day — creating a queue that delays enrollment, loses walk-in students, and caps revenue at the tester's throughput.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A language school's September enrollment window is 2 weeks. During these 2 weeks, 150 students need placement. The school has 2 experienced testers who can each assess 6-8 students per day (20-30 minutes per placement interview plus 10 minutes for scoring and documentation). Maximum throughput: 120-160 placements in 2 weeks — barely covering demand. Any tester illness, scheduling conflict, or surge beyond expected enrollment creates a backlog that delays student start dates, loses walk-in prospective students who will not wait, and forces the program to start sessions with incomplete rosters.

## Why It's Still Broken
The tester's tacit skill is the bottleneck: less experienced staff can administer standardized paper tests, but only the experienced tester can conduct the oral placement interview that provides the diagnostic information needed for optimal class assignment. Schools have tried training additional staff as testers, but developing the placement diagnostic skill takes 1-2 years of supervised practice. Standardized tests (CASAS, BEST Plus) can be administered by any trained proctor, but they provide less diagnostic information and still require 30-60 minutes per student.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A tiered placement protocol that: (1) uses a 10-minute computer-adaptive screening test (reading + listening) to roughly sort incoming students into 3 bands (beginner, intermediate, advanced); (2) schedules only intermediate-band students (where misplacement is most costly) for the full oral placement interview with an experienced tester; (3) uses a shortened 5-minute oral interview protocol for beginner and advanced students (whose placement is more obvious); and (4) enables less experienced staff to administer the screening test, reserving the experienced tester's time for the cases that require diagnostic expertise. This effectively triples placement throughput without adding experienced testers.

## Who Feels the Pain
Program Directors who watch prospective students walk out the door during peak enrollment because they cannot be tested and placed fast enough — each lost student represents $3,000-$8,000 in tuition revenue for the coming session.

## Impact If Fixed
Placement throughput increases 2-3x during peak enrollment, enrollment capture rate improves by an estimated 15-25% (converting walk-ins who would have been lost to the queue), and experienced tester time is preserved for the cases where their diagnostic expertise is genuinely needed.
