# Seasonal Tax Preparation Practices

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/accounting-firms-smb|SMB Accounting Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $45-55B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — UltraTax, Lacerte, and Drake are universal, but workflow orchestration between document collection, preparation, review, and e-filing is still manual at most firms
**Target Buyer:** Managing partner at a 3-15 person tax-focused CPA firm
**Automation Potential:** High — tax prep follows a rigid annual cycle with highly structured inputs and outputs

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Tax-focused practices operate on a fundamentally different business model than year-round bookkeeping firms: 60-70% of annual revenue is compressed into January-April, with a secondary extension season in September-October. This creates a staffing crisis that drives the entire operational challenge — firms hire seasonal preparers, outsource to offshore teams, or simply turn away clients they cannot serve. The workflow is document-in, return-out, review-approve, e-file — a linear pipeline that should be highly automatable but remains manual because each client's tax situation requires different documents, different forms, and different review points. Firms in this niche are evaluated by returns-per-preparer-per-day and average turnaround time, not by advisory revenue.

## Current Tools & Gaps
UltraTax (Thomson Reuters), Lacerte (Intuit), and Drake dominate tax prep. Client portals like SafeSend, Liscio, and Canopy handle secure document exchange. The gap is the orchestration layer: no tool connects "which documents are still missing for this return" to "which preparer should work on it next" to "what does the reviewer need to check" in a single intelligent workflow. Firms cobble this together with spreadsheets, Karbon task lists, and institutional memory.
