# All-in-One Mobile Practice Management for Solo CPAs

**Niche:** [[niches/accounting-firms-smb/sole-practitioner-cpas/profile|Sole Practitioner CPAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/accounting-firms-smb|SMB Accounting Firms]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No practice management tool is designed from the ground up for a single-person CPA practice that runs primarily from a phone and a laptop, not a multi-user office environment.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Solo CPAs juggle client communication, document collection, preparation, billing, and marketing without any staff. Existing practice management tools (Karbon, Canopy, Jetpack Workflow) are built for teams with role-based workflows, delegation, and multi-user dashboards. A solo practitioner does not need a team assignment feature — they need a single view of "what do I need to do today, and which clients need my attention right now." The tools available are either too complex (enterprise-grade PM tools) or too simple (a spreadsheet). The solo CPA ends up managing their practice through a patchwork of calendar apps, sticky notes, and memory.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Software vendors target the 5-20 person firm market because the ARPU is higher and the buyer (managing partner) has budget authority. Solo practitioners are price-sensitive ($50-$100/month max), resistant to switching, and difficult to reach through traditional B2B marketing. The unit economics of selling to solo CPAs are unattractive unless the product is dead-simple, self-serve, and spreads virally through CPA communities and CPE events.

## What to Build
A mobile-first practice management app that gives the solo CPA a single daily dashboard: upcoming deadlines (with IRS/state regulatory dates pre-loaded), client status pipeline (documents pending, in-progress, ready for review, filed), quick-capture for client communications (log a phone call, snap a document photo, send a secure message), and one-click invoicing tied to completed work. An AI assistant answers quick tax research questions and drafts client emails, replacing the "ask a colleague" option the solo practitioner doesn't have.

## Target Customer
CPAs operating solo practices with 75-200 clients, typically working from home offices, who currently manage their practice through personal tools (Google Calendar, Gmail, Dropbox).

## Impact If Built
Saves 5-8 hours per week in administrative overhead, enabling the solo CPA to either take on 15-25% more clients or reclaim personal time that currently bleeds into evenings and weekends.
