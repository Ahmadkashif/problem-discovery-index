# Client Deadline Tracking Without Practice Management Software

**Niche:** [[niches/accounting-firms-smb/sole-practitioner-cpas/profile|Sole Practitioner CPAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/accounting-firms-smb|SMB Accounting Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Solo CPAs track 200+ client deadlines across federal returns, state returns, quarterly estimates, and extensions using Google Calendar and memory — and things fall through the cracks.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
A solo CPA with 150 clients has 300-500 distinct filing deadlines per year: individual federal returns, state returns (potentially multiple states per client), quarterly estimated payments, business entity returns with different due dates, extensions, payroll deposits, and sales tax filings for business clients. These deadlines are tracked in personal calendar systems, mental checklists, and spreadsheets. When a state changes a filing deadline (as happened with multiple states during COVID), the CPA must manually update every affected client. Missed deadlines result in penalties, client anger, and potential malpractice exposure — and the solo practitioner has no one else to blame or to catch the miss.

## Why It's Still Broken
Practice management tools with built-in deadline engines (Canopy, TaxDome) solve this problem technically, but solo CPAs resist adopting full PM platforms for deadline tracking alone. The tool they need is lightweight and specific: it should know every federal and state filing deadline, automatically generate a calendar for each client based on their entity types, filing states, and engagement scope, and send the CPA reminders with enough lead time to act. This narrow tool does not exist independently of full-suite PM platforms.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A standalone deadline management tool that imports a client list with entity types and filing states, auto-generates all applicable deadlines including quarterly estimates and state-specific variations, accounts for extensions already filed, and sends multi-channel reminders (push notification, email, text) at configurable intervals (30 days, 14 days, 7 days before due date). Updates automatically when IRS or state authorities change deadlines. Priced at $30-$50/month as a standalone tool, not bundled into a $200/month PM platform.

## Who Feels the Pain
Solo CPAs who have experienced the stress of nearly missing (or actually missing) a filing deadline because it was tracked only in their memory or a manual calendar.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates the 5-10 hours per month solo CPAs spend maintaining deadline spreadsheets and removes the malpractice risk of missed filings, which can cost $500-$5,000 in penalties per incident.
