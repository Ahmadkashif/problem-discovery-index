# Payroll-Centric Accounting Firms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/accounting-firms-smb|SMB Accounting Firms]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — payroll engines (Gusto, ADP Run, Paychex) are standard, but the surrounding compliance work (multi-state registration, workers' comp audits, year-end reconciliation) remains manual
**Target Buyer:** Firm owner managing 50-200 payroll clients alongside tax preparation services
**Automation Potential:** High — payroll processing is rule-based with deterministic calculations, and the compliance burden scales linearly with client count

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Many SMB accounting firms offer payroll as an adjacent service to tax preparation and bookkeeping. These firms handle biweekly or semi-monthly payroll runs for 50-200 small business clients, each with different pay schedules, benefit structures, state filing requirements, and workers' comp class codes. Unlike standalone payroll companies (ADP, Paychex), accounting firms bundle payroll with tax planning — they see the whole picture and can optimize. But the operational burden is enormous: each client requires quarterly 941 filings, annual W-2 generation, state unemployment reporting, workers' comp audit support, and new-hire reporting across potentially dozens of states. A single missed filing or incorrect withholding calculation creates immediate liability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Gusto, ADP Run, and Paychex Flex handle the core payroll calculation and direct deposit. QuickBooks Payroll integrates with QBO. These tools process payroll runs reliably but do not manage the compliance orchestration around payroll: tracking which state registrations are active, which quarterly returns are due, which workers' comp policies need audit support, and which year-end reconciliations are outstanding. Firms track this compliance layer in spreadsheets and calendars.
