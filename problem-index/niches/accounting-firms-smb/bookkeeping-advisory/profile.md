# Recurring Bookkeeping & Advisory Firms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/accounting-firms-smb|SMB Accounting Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-35B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — QBO/Xero are standard, Karbon and Jetpack Workflow are gaining traction for practice management, but advisory delivery and client benchmarking remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Firm owner shifting from compliance-only to advisory services
**Automation Potential:** High — monthly bookkeeping follows repeatable patterns per client, and advisory insights can be templated by industry vertical

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
These firms have deliberately moved away from the tax-season-only model toward year-round monthly engagements: recurring bookkeeping, controller services, CFO advisory, and cash flow forecasting for SMB clients. Their economics are fundamentally different — revenue is spread across 12 months, client relationships are deeper, and the value proposition is insight rather than compliance. The operational challenge is scaling advisory without scaling headcount proportionally: each bookkeeper manages 15-30 clients with monthly close cycles, and the "advisory" component (benchmarking, cash flow analysis, KPI dashboards) is assembled manually from QBO/Xero exports because no tool automates industry-specific advisory content generation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
QBO and Xero handle the ledger. Practice management tools (Karbon, Financial Cents, Jetpack Workflow) track recurring tasks and deadlines. The gap is between "books are closed" and "advisory insight is delivered": firms export trial balances to Excel, manually compare to prior periods and industry benchmarks, build ad-hoc charts, and paste them into a presentation. There is no tool that automatically generates industry-specific advisory narratives from a closed set of books.
