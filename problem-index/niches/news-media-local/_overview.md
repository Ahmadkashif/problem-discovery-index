## Why These Niches

Community papers and local TV stations represent the dominant revenue pillars of local news, but face radically different tech stacks and cost structures — community papers run on manual pagination and print-first workflows, while TV stations have invested in broadcast automation but lag in digital monetization. Digital-only local news and print ad sales teams highlight the two ends of the digitization gap: digital-native outlets lack the back-office infrastructure that legacy papers built over decades, while print ad sales teams still operate on handshake deals and spreadsheets despite the industry's existential revenue crisis. Minority-language outlets and rural news deserts represent populations actively losing access to local journalism, each with distinct content, distribution, and economic constraints that mainstream news tools ignore. Obituary/classified operations and event calendar curation are the most automatable revenue and engagement workflows in local news — high-volume, rule-heavy, and currently consuming editor time that should go to reporting.
