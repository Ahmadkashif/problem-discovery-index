# Local TV News Stations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $22B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 55%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Broadcast automation (ENPS, iNews) is mature, but digital revenue systems, social media workflows, and audience analytics are fragmented and underinvested.
**Target Buyer:** Station General Manager / News Director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Rundown creation, closed captioning, social clip packaging, and ad traffic scheduling are automatable; editorial judgment on story prioritization and live breaking-news decisions are not.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Local TV stations (roughly 1,750 commercial stations in the US) generate the largest share of local news revenue through broadcast ad sales, but their digital operations are typically an afterthought bolted onto a broadcast-first workflow. Newsrooms run on ENPS or iNews for rundowns, feed stories through Avid or Grass Valley for editing, and air through automated playout systems — but the bridge between broadcast content and digital distribution (website clips, social media posts, YouTube, OTT apps) remains manual and inconsistent. Stations are owned by groups (Sinclair, Nexstar, Gray) but operated locally, creating tension between corporate platform mandates and local newsroom needs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Broadcast production is well-served by ENPS, iNews, Dalet, and playout automation. Digital publishing typically runs through a corporate-mandated CMS (often WordPress or Jeebly). The gap is the connection layer: automatically repackaging broadcast segments for web and social, tracking which stories drive digital engagement, and enabling reporters to publish across platforms without re-entering metadata. Social media management tools like Sprout Social or Hootsuite aren't configured for newsroom cadences.
