# Newsroom Social Media Scheduling for Breaking News

**Niche:** [[niches/news-media-local/local-tv-stations/profile|Local TV News Stations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Sprout Social and Hootsuite handle scheduled social posts but break down during breaking news when a station needs to blast updates across 8 platforms in real-time while maintaining editorial accuracy.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #nlp #text-generation #quick-win

## The Problem
Local TV stations manage 6-10 social media accounts (Facebook page, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, plus reporter-specific accounts). During routine news days, generic social schedulers work adequately. But during breaking news — which defines a TV station's brand — the workflow collapses: producers need to push updates simultaneously across all platforms with platform-specific formatting, link to the live stream, update previous posts with corrections, and coordinate across reporters' personal accounts. Generic tools have no concept of "breaking news mode" or editorial approval chains for fast-moving stories.

## What Already Exists
Sprout Social, Hootsuite, and Buffer provide multi-platform scheduling with approval workflows, analytics, and content calendars. Some stations use Socialflow for optimized posting times. None of these tools understand newsroom workflows: they don't integrate with ENPS rundowns, can't auto-generate social copy from wire scripts, don't have a "breaking news" mode that overrides the scheduled queue, and don't support the reporter-producer coordination needed during live events.

## The Customization Gap
The missing layer is newsroom-aware social management: (1) a "breaking" toggle that suspends scheduled posts and enables rapid multi-platform blast with auto-formatted versions per platform, (2) integration with the rundown system so social posts can be auto-generated from story scripts, (3) an editorial approval chain that matches newsroom hierarchy (producer approves, not a marketing manager), and (4) auto-threading for developing stories that updates previous posts with corrections and new information.

## Target Customer
Digital producers and social media managers at local TV stations who currently toggle between 3-4 browser tabs during breaking news.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces breaking-news social publishing latency from 5-10 minutes to under 60 seconds per platform, increases social engagement by 30-50% during breaking events (the highest-traffic moments), and reduces the 15% error rate in rushed manual cross-posting.
