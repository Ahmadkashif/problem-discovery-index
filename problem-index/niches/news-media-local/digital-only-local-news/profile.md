# Digital-Only Local News Sites

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — These outlets are web-native but paradoxically under-digitized in back-office operations: most run on WordPress with no CRM, no subscriber analytics, and manual newsletter workflows.
**Target Buyer:** Founder / Editor-in-Chief
**Automation Potential:** High — Newsletter automation, subscriber segmentation, paywall optimization, and donor/member management can all be automated with existing technology that hasn't been packaged for this buyer.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Digital-only local news outlets (The Boardman, Block Club Chicago, The Colorado Sun, Berkeleyside, etc.) represent the fastest-growing segment of local journalism, with 500+ outlets launched since 2018. They're typically founded by former newspaper reporters, run as nonprofits or membership-supported businesses, and employ 2-10 people. Their editorial product is strong but their business infrastructure is improvised: subscriber management in Mailchimp, donations through Stripe or PayPal, analytics in Google Analytics with no segmentation, and paywall logic cobbled together through WordPress plugins. They're digital-first in content but analog in operations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
WordPress with various plugins (MemberPress, Leaky Paywall, WooCommerce) handles basic publishing and metering. Mailchimp or Substack manages newsletters. Payment is through Stripe, PayPal, or donation platforms like Donorbox. The gap is cohesion: no single platform connects subscriber data, reading behavior, donation history, and engagement metrics into a view that helps a 4-person team decide what to write next, who to ask for money, and which readers are about to churn.
