# Print Ad Sales Teams

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 16%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most print ad sales reps track prospects in personal spreadsheets or notebooks, generate rate cards from memory, and submit insertion orders by email or handwritten forms.
**Target Buyer:** Ad Sales Manager / Advertising Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Pipeline tracking, rate card generation, contract creation, campaign reporting, and renewal reminders are all rule-based workflows currently done manually.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Print ad sales teams at local newspapers and community publications are the revenue engine of print journalism, yet operate with some of the least digitized workflows in media. A typical 3-5 person ad sales team manages 100-400 local advertiser accounts — auto dealers, restaurants, real estate agents, medical practices — using personal relationships, memory, and handwritten notes. Rate negotiation is ad hoc, with no standardized pricing enforcement. Insertion orders are emailed as Word docs or faxed. Campaign performance reporting consists of the rep telling the advertiser "people are seeing your ad" with no data. This informality was sustainable when print was the only option, but as advertisers demand measurable results, the lack of sales infrastructure is accelerating revenue decline.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some larger papers use Salesforce or HubSpot for basic contact management, but the CRM is almost never customized for media sales workflows (rate cards, insertion orders, page-position inventory, make-good tracking). Most reps track their pipeline in personal Excel files. Ad management within the paper runs through WideOrbit or proprietary systems that don't share data with the sales team's tools. The gap is a media-sales-specific CRM that understands ad inventory, rate cards, insertion orders, and campaign reporting as native objects.
