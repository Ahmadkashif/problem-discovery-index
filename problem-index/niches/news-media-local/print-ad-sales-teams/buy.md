# Media-Specific CRM for Local Ad Sales

**Niche:** [[niches/news-media-local/print-ad-sales-teams/profile|Print Ad Sales Teams]]
**Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Salesforce and HubSpot track contacts and deals, but neither understands insertion orders, rate cards, page-position inventory, or make-good tracking — forcing ad sales teams to maintain parallel spreadsheets.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Ad sales reps at local papers need to track not just "deals" but media-specific objects: rate cards with frequency discounts, insertion orders with page position preferences, make-good commitments when ads run incorrectly, and seasonal campaign packages (e.g., a 12-week holiday package with escalating sizes). Generic CRMs treat all of this as "notes on a deal" rather than structured data. The result is that pipeline data lives in the CRM but operational data lives in spreadsheets, and the two are never reconciled.

## What Already Exists
Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive provide robust contact/deal management. Adpoint (by Lineup Systems) and Naviga Ad (formerly Newscycle) offer media-specific ad sales platforms, but they're priced for mid-to-large publishers ($30K-100K/year) and require months of implementation. For a 3-person ad team at a weekly paper, both the cost and complexity are prohibitive. The affordable CRMs don't speak "media sales," and the media-specific platforms don't serve small papers.

## The Customization Gap
A CRM customization layer (ideally on HubSpot or Pipedrive as the cheapest base platforms) that adds: (1) rate card objects with frequency/volume discount logic, (2) insertion order generation from deal records with digital signature, (3) page-position inventory awareness (front page, section front, ROP), (4) make-good tracking linked to specific insertion orders, and (5) renewal pipeline automation that triggers outreach 30/60/90 days before contract expiration. This is a vertical CRM template, not a new platform.

## Target Customer
Ad sales managers at community papers and small-market dailies with 2-5 person sales teams currently using generic CRMs or personal spreadsheets.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces administrative overhead by 5-8 hours/week per rep and increases renewal rates by 10-15% through automated follow-up — worth $30K-80K annually in recovered revenue and productivity per paper.
