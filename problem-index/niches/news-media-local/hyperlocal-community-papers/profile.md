# Hyperlocal Community Papers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 18%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most community papers still paginate in QuarkXPress or InDesign, email PDFs to the printer, and maintain a WordPress site updated sporadically.
**Target Buyer:** Publisher / Owner-Editor
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Pagination, photo cropping, legal notice formatting, and subscription fulfillment are all automatable, but editorial judgment on story selection resists automation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Hyperlocal community papers (weekly and bi-weekly publications serving towns under 50,000) are the backbone of local news, numbering roughly 6,500 outlets in the US alone. They operate on skeleton crews of 2-8 people where the editor also sells ads, shoots photos, and manages circulation. Their economics are fundamentally different from metro dailies — they depend on legal notice revenue, local advertiser relationships, and print subscriptions rather than digital traffic. The workflow is print-first with digital as an afterthought, meaning their tech needs center on production efficiency rather than audience growth.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most use a patchwork of InDesign for layout, Excel for ad tracking, and a basic WordPress or TownNews CMS for web. Industry-specific platforms like TownNews or Jeebly exist but are priced for larger papers. The critical gap is an integrated system that handles ad booking, pagination, subscriber management, and digital publishing from a single interface at a price point that works for a $300K/year operation.
