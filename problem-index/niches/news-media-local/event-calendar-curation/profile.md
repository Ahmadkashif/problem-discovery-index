# Event Calendar Curation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $500M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 1%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Most local news sites have a community calendar, but it's manually populated from email submissions, Facebook event scraping, and phone calls — a high-effort, low-engagement feature in its current form.
**Target Buyer:** Digital Editor / Community Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Event discovery, intake, deduplication, categorization, and distribution are highly automatable with existing NLP and web scraping capabilities.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Community event calendars are one of the most visited features on local news websites (often in the top 5 pages by traffic) but are severely underloved operationally. Maintaining an accurate, comprehensive local event calendar requires monitoring dozens of sources: venue websites, Facebook events, Eventbrite, library calendars, parks & rec schedules, church bulletins, school district announcements, and emailed submissions from community organizations. A single editor manually curating this spends 3-5 hours per week and still misses 40-60% of local events. The calendar is a natural audience retention and engagement driver, but its manual curation cost makes it a net negative for most newsrooms.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some outlets use Jeebly or TownNews calendar modules. Others embed Google Calendar or use WordPress event plugins (The Events Calendar). Patch.com automated community calendars for a while but de-prioritized the feature. Localist and Eventboard serve universities and municipalities, not news outlets. The gap is automated event discovery and intake: a system that continuously monitors all local event sources and populates a structured calendar with human approval for edge cases.
