# End-to-End Obituary Lifecycle Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/news-media-local/obituary-classified-ops/profile|Obituary & Classified Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the complete obituary lifecycle — intake from funeral homes, style formatting, family approval, print placement, web publishing, billing, and permanent archive — in one integrated system for community papers.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #nlp #text-generation #quick-win

## The Problem
A community paper publishes 5-20 obituaries per week. Each one requires: (1) intake from the funeral home (email, fax, or phone), (2) reformatting to the paper's style guide (consistent date formats, abbreviation rules, survivor listing order), (3) proofing against funeral home records for name accuracy, (4) family review and approval, (5) billing calculation based on word count and add-ons, (6) placement in the print layout, (7) web publishing, and (8) archive indexing. This 7-step process runs through 3-4 people using email, Word documents, and manual billing entries. A paper publishing 15 obituaries per week spends 10-15 hours on obituary operations.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Legacy.com dominates online obituary publishing but doesn't serve the print workflow — they're a destination website, not a production tool. Naviga and SCS handle classified billing but not the editorial formatting and approval steps. The community paper obituary market ($400-600M) is large in aggregate but fragmented across 6,000+ papers, each with slightly different style guides and billing structures. Building a platform that handles format customization per paper while maintaining a standard workflow has been seen as too niche for VC-backed software companies.

## What to Build
A SaaS platform where: (1) funeral homes submit obituaries through a branded intake form with structured fields (name, dates, survivors, service details), (2) the system auto-formats the submission to the paper's style guide, (3) a proof is auto-generated and sent to the family for digital approval, (4) billing is calculated automatically with an invoice sent to the funeral home, (5) the approved obituary is output as print-ready text for pagination and simultaneously published to the paper's website, and (6) all obituaries are archived with full-text search. The classified desk reviews exceptions rather than processing every obituary from scratch.

## Target Customer
Classified/obituary desk managers at community papers publishing 5+ obituaries per week who currently spend 30-45 minutes per obituary in manual processing.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-obituary processing time from 30-45 minutes to 5-10 minutes, saving 8-12 hours per week per paper and reducing billing errors by 80% — worth $15K-25K annually in labor savings plus reduced revenue leakage from billing mistakes.
