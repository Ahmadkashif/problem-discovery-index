# Classified Self-Service Portal for Community Papers

**Niche:** [[niches/news-media-local/obituary-classified-ops/profile|Obituary & Classified Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/news-media-local|Local News Media]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace killed classified revenue, but the remaining classified categories (legal notices, public announcements, real estate, employment) could be retained through self-service portals that existing e-commerce platforms don't offer in a newspaper-specific format.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Community papers still sell classified ads in categories that Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace don't serve well: legal notices (required by law to appear in print), public announcements (church events, community meetings), real estate listings (required by local custom), and employment ads from local government agencies. But the purchase process is archaic: a customer calls the office, describes the ad, the classified clerk types it up, reads it back, calculates the price, takes a credit card number by phone, and manually enters it into the billing system. This friction drives away potential advertisers who would happily self-serve if they could.

## What Already Exists
Shopify, Squarespace, and generic e-commerce platforms handle self-service purchasing. CMS platforms like TownNews include classified modules. Legacy classified systems (Naviga AdManager) handle print classified workflows. But none offer a lightweight self-service portal where a customer can: select a classified category, type their ad, see a live preview in the paper's format, select publication dates, pay online, and receive a proof — all without calling the newspaper office. The existing solutions are either too expensive (Naviga) or too generic (Shopify doesn't understand per-word pricing or publication date selection).

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a classified-specific self-service layer: (1) per-word and per-line pricing with category-specific rates, (2) publication date selection with deadline enforcement (ads must be submitted by Tuesday for a Thursday paper), (3) live preview showing the ad as it will appear in print, (4) automatic billing with the newspaper's payment processor, and (5) direct output to the pagination workflow so approved ads flow into the print layout without re-entry. This is a vertical e-commerce template, not a new platform.

## Target Customer
Office managers at community papers who spend 15-20 hours/week on phone-based classified intake that could be 90% self-service.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces classified intake labor by 70-80% (saving $15K-20K annually per paper) and increases classified ad volume by 15-25% by lowering the friction barrier for advertisers who won't pick up the phone.
