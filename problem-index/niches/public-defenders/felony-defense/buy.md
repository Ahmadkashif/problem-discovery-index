# Legal Research Adapted for Indigent Defense Caseloads

**Niche:** [[niches/public-defenders/felony-defense/profile|Felony Defense]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Westlaw and Lexis exist but are priced for BigLaw — public defenders need case law research that fits $0 per-seat budgets and 15-minute research windows.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #quick-win

## The Problem
Felony defenders need to research case law for motions to suppress, sentencing arguments, and trial strategy. They're constitutionally required to provide competent representation, which demands legal research. But many offices can't afford Westlaw or Lexis subscriptions, and even those that can find the tools designed for attorneys who spend hours on a single research question — not defenders who have 15 minutes between hearings.

## What Already Exists
Westlaw (Thomson Reuters) and Lexis+ (LexisNexis) dominate legal research. Free alternatives like Google Scholar and Casetext exist but lack the depth, citator tools, and jurisdiction-specific filtering that felony defense requires. CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters' AI) and Lexis+ AI offer LLM-powered research but are priced at $200-400/user/month.

## The Customization Gap
Public defenders need research pre-filtered to criminal defense — suppression motions, sentencing departures, ineffective assistance claims — with results ranked by their jurisdiction's appellate courts. They need outputs formatted as draft motion language, not research memos. The tool must work in 15-minute bursts between court appearances, surface the 3 most relevant cases, and generate a citation-checked paragraph ready to paste into a motion.

## Target Customer
Chief Public Defenders managing office-wide subscriptions, or state indigent defense commissions that negotiate statewide contracts.

## Impact If Solved
Gives every public defender access to research capability currently reserved for well-funded offices. Reduces motion drafting time by 60-70% and improves the legal quality of filings across the office.
