# Felony Defense

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most offices have basic case management (JusticeTrax, LegalServer) but use it as a filing cabinet, not a workflow engine.
**Target Buyer:** Chief Public Defender / County Administrator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Discovery review and motion drafting are automatable, but constitutional safeguards limit fully autonomous workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Felony defense consumes the largest share of public defender resources — these are the cases with the highest stakes (years of incarceration), the most complex discovery (thousands of pages per case), and the longest case lifecycles (6-18 months). Defenders handling felonies juggle 80-150 active cases simultaneously against prosecutors who have paralegals, investigators, and dedicated tech budgets. The asymmetry between prosecution and defense resources is most acute in this segment, making technology leverage disproportionately valuable.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most offices use LegalServer, Defender Data, or JusticeTrax for case tracking, but these tools don't help with the actual work — they don't analyze discovery, draft motions, or surface case law. Document review is done manually, and most defenders lack access to Westlaw or Lexis. The gap is a workflow tool that reduces the per-case time burden, not just tracks docket dates.
