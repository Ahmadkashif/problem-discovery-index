# Juvenile Defense

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $850M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~11%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Juvenile court records are sealed by statute in most jurisdictions, creating separate paper-based systems disconnected from adult case management.
**Target Buyer:** Juvenile Division Chief / State Juvenile Defender Office
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Rehabilitation-focused dispositions require nuanced assessment, but intake screening and school-coordination workflows are highly repetitive.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Juvenile defense operates under a fundamentally different legal framework than adult criminal defense. The goal is rehabilitation, not punishment. Dispositions include probation, diversion, residential placement, and community service — not prison. Defenders must coordinate with schools, child welfare agencies, mental health providers, and family courts. Juvenile records are confidential, creating separate information silos. The client is a minor, often with a parent or guardian who has their own agenda. These differences make adult criminal defense tools largely irrelevant.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most juvenile defenders use the same case management systems as adult defenders (LegalServer, JusticeTrax) with no juvenile-specific features. Sealed-record requirements mean many offices maintain parallel paper files. No tool integrates the cross-system data juvenile defenders need: school records, child welfare history, mental health assessments, prior juvenile adjudications. Communication with clients (minors) raises unique challenges — texting a 14-year-old about their case has ethical implications that adult defense tools don't address.
