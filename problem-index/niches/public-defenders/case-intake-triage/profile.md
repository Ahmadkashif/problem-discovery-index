# Case Intake & Triage

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $900M (labor cost)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12% of attorney and staff time
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Most offices have electronic intake forms but no automated triage, assignment optimization, or conflict checking beyond basic name matching.
**Target Buyer:** Intake Coordinator / Supervising Attorney
**Automation Potential:** High — Eligibility determination, conflict checking, case complexity assessment, and attorney assignment are all rule-based processes currently done manually.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Case intake and triage is the front door of every public defender office. A new case arrives (court appointment, walk-in, jail referral), and the office must: verify financial eligibility for indigent defense, check for conflicts of interest (does the office already represent a co-defendant or victim?), assess case complexity, and assign the case to an attorney with appropriate experience and available capacity. In busy offices, 50-200 new cases arrive daily. This process is almost entirely manual — intake staff phone-screen for eligibility, attorneys self-report their caseloads, and case assignment is done by a supervising attorney based on who's next on the rotation list, not who's best suited for the case.

## Current Tools & Gaps
LegalServer has intake and assignment modules, but they're configured as forms, not decision engines. Conflict checking is limited to name matching — it doesn't catch relationship conflicts (representing a co-defendant's family member) or entity conflicts (representing an employee of a company the office is suing). Case complexity assessment is entirely manual — the intake coordinator reads the charging document and guesses. No tool matches case complexity to attorney skill and capacity in an optimized way.
