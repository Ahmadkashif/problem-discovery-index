# Misdemeanor Volume Practice

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~26%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Many misdemeanor cases are processed so quickly that no digital record beyond the court docket is created by the defender.
**Target Buyer:** Managing Attorney / Court Administrator
**Automation Potential:** High — Misdemeanor defense is the most repetitive segment, with standard charge types, predictable plea offers, and templatable motions.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Misdemeanor cases represent the highest volume of public defender work — a single attorney may handle 400-700 active misdemeanor cases simultaneously, far exceeding ABA-recommended limits of 400/year. Cases move fast: arraignment to disposition in 30-90 days. The work is pattern-based — DUI, petty theft, simple assault, drug possession — with standard plea offers from prosecutors. The sheer volume means defenders spend less than 30 minutes total on most cases, making this the segment where technology leverage has the highest throughput impact.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most offices track misdemeanor cases in the same system as felonies (LegalServer, Defender Data) but the workflow is fundamentally different — it's about batch processing, not deep case preparation. No tool exists that helps defenders manage 500 cases at scale: prioritizing which ones need attention, auto-generating standard motions, or tracking plea offer patterns across the docket.
