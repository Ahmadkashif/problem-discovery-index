# Immigration-Criminal Crossover Research Tool

**Niche:** [[niches/public-defenders/immigration-facing-defendants/profile|Immigration-Facing Defendants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Immigration law databases (Westlaw Immigration, ImmigrationProf) exist for immigration attorneys, but public defenders need the opposite lens — starting from a criminal charge and mapping to immigration consequences.
**Tags:** #llm #text-classification #nlp #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
When a public defender identifies that a client is a noncitizen, they need to research how the specific criminal charge interacts with immigration law. Existing immigration law databases are organized for immigration practitioners — they start from the immigration provision (e.g., "aggravated felony") and map to criminal statutes. Public defenders need the reverse: start from the criminal charge (e.g., "California Penal Code 459.5 — shoplifting") and see all immigration consequences. This mismatch means defenders either spend hours doing cross-referencing research or they call an immigration attorney — if they know one.

## What Already Exists
Westlaw's Immigration module, LexisNexis Immigration, ILRC's Quick Reference Charts, and the National Immigration Project's practice advisories all contain the relevant legal analysis. The ILRC's online categorical approach tool provides some charge-to-consequence mapping but is limited to a subset of charges and jurisdictions.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to be organized by criminal charge, not immigration provision. Input: state statute number or charge description. Output: every immigration consequence, with case law citations, organized by the client's immigration status category. It should flag "immigration-safe" alternative pleas — charges in the same jurisdiction that carry similar criminal sentences but avoid triggering deportation. The interface must be usable by a criminal defense attorney with no immigration training — plain language, not INA section numbers.

## Target Customer
Public defender offices with dedicated Padilla compliance positions, and state indigent defense commissions providing tools statewide.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces immigration consequence research from 1-3 hours per case to 5-10 minutes. Enables every public defender — not just the immigration specialist — to provide constitutionally adequate Padilla advice. Estimated impact: 50-70% increase in cases receiving proper immigration screening.
