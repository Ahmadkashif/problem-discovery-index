# Competency Evaluation Bottleneck Causing Indefinite Pretrial Detention

**Niche:** [[niches/public-defenders/mental-health-court/profile|Mental Health Court Defense]]
**Industry:** [[industries/public-defenders|Public Defenders]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Defendants found incompetent to stand trial wait 6-12 months in jail for a bed at a state psychiatric facility — longer than the sentence they'd receive if convicted — because no one tracks the waitlist or advocates for alternatives.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a court orders a competency evaluation, the defendant typically waits 30-90 days in jail for a forensic psychologist to become available. If found incompetent, the defendant is ordered to a state psychiatric facility for competency restoration — but waitlists for these facilities average 6-12 months in most states. During this wait, the defendant sits in county jail, often without treatment, frequently decompensating. Defendants charged with misdemeanors (maximum sentence: 6-12 months) regularly spend longer waiting for competency restoration than they would have served if convicted. This is a constitutional crisis hiding in plain sight.

## Why It's Still Broken
State psychiatric facility capacity hasn't grown to match demand. Counties have no incentive to fund alternatives — the state bears the cost of restoration, so counties prefer to wait. Public defenders file habeas petitions when waits become egregious, but courts are reluctant to release defendants deemed incompetent. No one tracks the aggregate waitlist data at the case level — the public defender may not know where their client stands in the queue, when a bed might become available, or whether community-based restoration (an alternative to inpatient commitment) is available and appropriate.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A competency restoration tracking system accessible to defense attorneys: current waitlist position, estimated time to placement, available alternatives (outpatient restoration programs, jail-based competency restoration, community-based programs). The system triggers automatic habeas filing when a defendant's wait exceeds the maximum sentence for the charged offense. It identifies clients eligible for community-based restoration — a less restrictive alternative that's clinically appropriate for 40-60% of IST defendants but rarely used because defenders don't know it's available. The system connects defenders with restoration program coordinators and generates placement applications.

## Who Feels the Pain
Defendants with serious mental illness who sit in jail without treatment for months, and public defenders who know their clients are deteriorating but have no mechanism to expedite placement.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average competency restoration wait time by an estimated 30-50% through systematic identification of alternatives and automatic habeas filing. Prevents unconstitutional indefinite pretrial detention for misdemeanor defendants. Saves county jail costs of $100-150 per day per defendant waiting for state hospital placement.
