## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mental health courts and competency-based defense represent a growing share of public defender work as the criminal justice system increasingly intersects with behavioral health. Defenders in this niche handle clients with serious mental illness (schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, PTSD), intellectual disabilities, and substance use disorders. The legal work is fundamentally different: instead of trial preparation, defenders negotiate treatment plans, monitor compliance, coordinate with behavioral health providers, manage competency evaluations, and advocate for diversion rather than incarceration. The client population requires specialized communication skills and case management approaches that standard criminal defense tools don't support.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Mental health courts often use court-managed tracking systems (e.g., Northpointe/Equivant for risk assessment) but these are prosecution/court-oriented. Defense attorneys have no dedicated tools for: tracking client treatment compliance from the defense perspective, managing competency evaluation timelines (which have strict statutory deadlines), coordinating with multiple treatment providers, or maintaining the clinical documentation needed for diversion arguments. Defenders use spreadsheets and paper files to track treatment progress across dozens of clients.
