# Amazon FBA Aggregators & Multi-Brand Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/ecommerce-sellers|E-Commerce Sellers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $80-100B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — heavy users of Helium 10, Jungle Scout, and Amazon Seller Central but lack unified portfolio-level profit analytics across 10-50+ brands
**Target Buyer:** Portfolio operator, brand manager, or VP of Operations at an aggregator (Thrasio model and successors)
**Automation Potential:** High — portfolio-level COGS tracking, cross-brand ad budget allocation, and brand health monitoring are all rule-heavy and data-rich

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Aggregators operate 10-50+ Amazon brands simultaneously, each with its own P&L, supplier relationships, ad campaigns, and inventory pipeline. The operational challenge is fundamentally different from a single-brand seller: a portfolio operator must allocate capital across brands competing for the same ad dollars, detect underperforming brands before they drain the portfolio, and manage supplier relationships across dozens of manufacturers. Standard seller tools are designed for a single storefront, not a holding company running 30 storefronts with shared overhead costs and cross-brand capital allocation decisions.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Helium 10 and Jungle Scout provide single-brand analytics. Sellerboard and Inventory Lab offer Amazon-specific profitability views but are single-account tools with no portfolio aggregation layer. Aggregators build custom spreadsheet models and BI dashboards (Looker, Tableau) on top of raw API data, spending $200K-500K/year on internal data teams to produce reports that a purpose-built tool should generate automatically.
