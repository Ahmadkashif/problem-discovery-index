# Reseller & Arbitrage Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/ecommerce-sellers|E-Commerce Sellers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — rely on manual sourcing (store visits, clearance scanning, wholesale lot browsing) with minimal tooling beyond Keepa price charts and basic spreadsheets
**Target Buyer:** Solo reseller or small team sourcing lead
**Automation Potential:** Medium — product sourcing and price monitoring are data-intensive but require market intuition that resists full automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Resellers and arbitrage operators buy products at a discount (retail clearance, wholesale lots, liquidation pallets, thrift stores) and resell on Amazon, eBay, or Poshmark at a markup. Their core skill is sourcing — finding underpriced inventory faster than competitors. Unlike brand owners who control their supply, resellers compete on finding and evaluating deals in real time: scanning a clearance shelf and instantly calculating whether the net margin after Amazon fees, FBA shipping, and potential returns justifies the buy. This workflow is fundamentally different from brand-building or DTC selling and has almost zero purpose-built tooling.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Keepa provides Amazon price history and sales rank data. The Amazon Seller App allows in-store barcode scanning with basic profitability estimates. Tactical Arbitrage and BuyBotPro provide online arbitrage scanning. But none of these tools handle wholesale lot evaluation (Is this 500-unit pallet of mixed electronics worth $3,000?), liquidation marketplace analysis, or portfolio-level performance tracking across hundreds of sourced products. Resellers track everything in spreadsheets and mental notes.
