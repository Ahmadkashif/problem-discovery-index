# Reseller-Specific Profitability Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/ecommerce-sellers/reseller-arbitrage-ops/profile|Reseller & Arbitrage Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/ecommerce-sellers|E-Commerce Sellers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Seller profitability tools assume a brand owner with consistent SKUs and COGS — resellers have unique per-unit acquisition costs, mixed sourcing channels, and no recurring SKUs.
**Tags:** #linear-regression #regression #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A reseller buys 50 different products from 5 different sources (clearance shelf, liquidation pallet, wholesale lot, thrift store, garage sale) in a single week, each at a different acquisition cost. When those items sell over the next 1-6 months, the reseller needs to match each sale to its original acquisition cost to calculate true profit. Standard e-commerce profitability tools (Sellerboard, Inventory Lab) assume stable COGS per SKU — but a reseller might buy the same product three times at three different prices from three different sources.

## What Already Exists
Inventory Lab provides Amazon-specific profitability tracking with per-unit COGS entry and is popular among FBA resellers. Sellerboard covers Amazon profit analytics. AccelerList handles listing and cost entry for resellers. General accounting tools (QuickBooks, Wave) track income and expenses but not per-unit profitability.

## The Customization Gap
Inventory Lab comes closest but is Amazon-only and requires manual COGS entry for every unit at listing time. Resellers who sell across Amazon, eBay, and Poshmark need cross-platform profitability. The missing feature is sourcing-trip-level analytics: "My Tuesday Walmart clearance run cost $150 in gas + time and netted $420 in profit across 15 items — was it worth it?" This requires tracking not just per-item COGS but per-trip overhead (mileage, time, platform fees by channel) and providing sourcing channel ROI analytics that help resellers optimize where they spend their sourcing time.

## Target Customer
Full-time and part-time resellers doing $50K-300K/year across 2+ platforms who want to understand which sourcing channels and product categories produce the best returns on their time and capital.

## Impact If Solved
Enables resellers to identify and double down on their most profitable sourcing channels while dropping unprofitable ones. Typical finding: 30-40% of sourcing trips produce 70%+ of profit, but without per-trip analytics, resellers can't identify which trips to prioritize. Optimization typically improves hourly earnings by 20-40%.
