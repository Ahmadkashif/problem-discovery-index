# Materials Cost Tracking and True Pricing Calculator

**Niche:** [[niches/ecommerce-sellers/handmade-artisan-sellers/profile|Handmade & Artisan Sellers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/ecommerce-sellers|E-Commerce Sellers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** COGS tracking tools exist for purchased goods, but none handle the fractional materials costing and labor tracking that handmade sellers need to price products profitably.
**Tags:** #linear-regression #regression #tabular-ml #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
A candle maker uses $2.30 of wax, $0.85 of fragrance oil, $0.15 of wick, $0.40 of container, $0.20 of label, and 12 minutes of labor per candle — but Etsy only shows revenue minus fees. Without tracking fractional material costs and labor, most makers underprice by 20-40%, discovering they're losing money only when they run out of materials faster than expected. Materials costs change with every supplier order, and converting bulk purchases (a 50lb box of wax) into per-unit costs requires math that most makers do once and never update.

## What Already Exists
Craftybase provides materials tracking and COGS calculation for handmade sellers. QuickBooks and Wave handle general business accounting. Etsy's own analytics show revenue and fees but not COGS. Spreadsheet templates circulate in Etsy seller communities.

## The Customization Gap
Craftybase is the closest tool but lacks several critical features: automatic materials cost updates when new supplier orders arrive at different prices, labor time tracking integrated with production (not just a manual entry), marketplace fee modeling that reflects actual Etsy/Faire commission structures (Etsy's fee model alone has 6 components), and a "true price" calculator that takes the target margin and works backward to the minimum viable price. The tool also needs to handle one-of-a-kind items where materials vary per piece, not just batch-produced items.

## Target Customer
Etsy and Faire sellers doing $30K-200K/year who are currently guessing at pricing or using outdated spreadsheets with stale materials costs.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates systematic underpricing that affects an estimated 60-70% of handmade sellers. A maker who raises prices 15% based on accurate COGS data typically sees no volume decline (handmade buyers are less price-sensitive than commodity buyers), adding $4.5K-30K in annual profit on a $30K-200K business.
