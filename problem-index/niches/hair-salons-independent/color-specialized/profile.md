# Color-Specialized Salons

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $18B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 32%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Booking and POS are digital, but color formula management, inventory tracking, and continuing education remain largely analog.
**Target Buyer:** Salon owner or color director
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — Color formula management, product usage tracking, and reorder automation follow structured patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Color services (highlights, balayage, full color, corrective color) generate 40-60% of revenue for most independent salons and command the highest per-service prices ($150-400+). The operational challenge is formula management: each client has a unique color formula (mixing ratios, developer volumes, processing times) that must be reproduced consistently visit after visit — often months apart. Experienced colorists maintain this knowledge in personal notebooks, on sticky notes, or in memory. When a colorist leaves, all their client formulas leave with them. Color product inventory (50-200 individual shade tubes) requires constant monitoring, and color waste from unused mixed product is a significant cost center.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Booking platforms like Vagaro, Square Appointments, and Fresha handle scheduling. Some salons use SalonScale for color cost tracking by weight. No platform provides end-to-end color workflow management: formula storage and retrieval, product usage tracking per service, waste monitoring, reorder forecasting by shade, and colorist performance analytics.
