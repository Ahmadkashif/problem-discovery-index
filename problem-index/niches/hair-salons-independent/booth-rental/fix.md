# Common-Area Cost Allocation Between Booth Renters

**Niche:** [[niches/hair-salons-independent/booth-rental/profile|Booth-Rental Salon Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Shared costs (utilities, cleaning, reception, WiFi, product samples) are split arbitrarily among booth renters — creating disputes, resentment, and financial confusion for the salon owner.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A booth-rental salon has shared costs: electricity ($800/month), cleaning service ($600/month), reception/front desk ($2,500/month), WiFi ($100/month), coffee and refreshments ($200/month), and common-area product samples ($300/month). These $4,500/month in shared costs must be allocated among 8 renters, but the fair allocation is unclear. Does the renter who works 6 days/week pay more than the one who works 3 days? Does the colorist who uses more electricity than the cut-only stylist pay a larger utility share? Salon owners either bury these costs in rent (making pricing opaque) or split them equally (creating resentment from part-time renters subsidizing full-time ones).

## Why It's Still Broken
Cost allocation requires tracking renter-specific usage data (hours in the salon, electricity-intensive services, reception utilization) that doesn't exist. Even if it did, calculating fair shares monthly is an accounting task that hairstylist-owners aren't trained for. The informal relationship between booth-rental salon owners and their renters makes detailed cost accounting feel adversarial — "we're all friends here" conflicts with "you owe me $47.50 more this month because you used more electricity."

## What a Fix Looks Like
A transparent cost allocation system that: (1) tracks shared costs in defined categories, (2) applies allocation rules agreed upon in the lease (equal split, proportional to days worked, proportional to station size, or hybrid), (3) generates monthly cost allocation statements for each renter showing their share by category, (4) integrates allocated costs into the rent billing system, and (5) provides a dashboard showing total common-area costs over time so the owner can make decisions about cutting or adding shared services. Transparency reduces disputes because renters see exactly what they're paying for.

## Who Feels the Pain
Salon owners who absorb common-area cost overruns because they can't fairly allocate them, and booth renters who feel overcharged because they have no visibility into what shared costs actually are.

## Impact If Fixed
Recovers $500-1,500/month in unallocated common-area costs for the salon owner, reduces renter disputes, and provides financial clarity that makes booth-rental pricing competitive and fair.
