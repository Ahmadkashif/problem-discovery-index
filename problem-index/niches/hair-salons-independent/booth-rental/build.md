# Booth-Rental Salon Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/hair-salons-independent/booth-rental/profile|Booth-Rental Salon Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No salon management platform is built for the booth-rental model — where the salon owner is a landlord, not an employer, and needs lease management, rent collection, and occupancy tracking rather than appointment booking and commission calculations.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A booth-rental salon owner managing 8 stations deals with: monthly rent collection from 8 independent contractors (often via multiple payment methods), lease agreement tracking (start dates, terms, rate increases), supply-room access management (some renters pay for supplies, others bring their own), common-area maintenance coordination, and occupancy optimization (filling vacant booths quickly). These are property management tasks, not salon management tasks — but the owner is a hairstylist, not a property manager, and has no tools designed for this hybrid role.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Salon software companies build for the commission model because it's where the larger chains operate and where per-seat revenue is higher. Booth-rental operators are smaller, more fragmented, and price-sensitive. Property management software companies (Buildium, AppFolio) don't understand salon-specific needs. The booth-rental model falls between two industries — salon and property management — and neither has built for it.

## What to Build
A management platform for booth-rental salon owners that handles: (1) lease agreement creation and tracking with auto-renewal and rate increase scheduling, (2) automated rent collection via ACH with late-fee calculation and tracking, (3) common-area cost allocation and billing, (4) supply-room access management and product cost tracking, (5) occupancy dashboard showing vacant stations, lease expirations, and revenue forecasting, (6) renter communication tools for announcements, maintenance requests, and policy updates, and (7) 1099 generation at year-end.

## Target Customer
Salon owners operating 4+ booth-rental stations who spend 5-10 hours/month on rent collection, lease tracking, and renter management using Venmo, spreadsheets, and text messages.

## Impact If Built
Saves 5-10 hours/month of administrative time, eliminates late rent collection delays (currently 15-25% of renters pay late), and provides occupancy and financial visibility that enables better business decisions.
