# Tip Pooling & Distribution Compliance

**Niche:** [[niches/hair-salons-independent/commission-payroll/profile|Stylist Commission & Booth-Rent Payroll]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Salon tip distribution — splitting between stylists, assistants, and shampoo techs — is calculated manually, inconsistently, and often in violation of federal and state tip credit laws that salon owners don't fully understand.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A client leaves a $30 tip on a $150 color service. The tip goes to the stylist, right? Not necessarily. If the stylist had an assistant who mixed color and applied foils, the assistant is owed a share. If a shampoo tech did the wash, they get a share too. The salon may have a tip pooling policy that distributes tips based on time contributed to the service. But tip pooling rules vary by state, and federal law prohibits employers from keeping any portion of tips. Salon owners often implement tip-sharing policies that inadvertently violate wage-and-hour laws — for example, including booth renters (who are not employees) in a tip pool, or distributing tips to managers who supervise rather than serve clients.

## Why It's Still Broken
Tip distribution in salons is calculated manually at the end of each shift, typically by the salon owner or front desk. The calculation requires knowing: who contributed to each service, what the contribution percentages are, which tips are pooled and which are individual, and how credit card tip processing fees are allocated. This is a nightly 15-30 minute task that no one is trained to do correctly. Wage-and-hour attorneys report that tip violations are among the most common salon employment disputes, but salon owners don't know they're non-compliant until a lawsuit is filed.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated tip distribution system that: (1) assigns tips to the service provider by default but supports rule-based splitting when assistants or shampoo techs contribute, (2) enforces state-specific tip pooling legality rules (flagging non-compliant distribution configurations), (3) calculates distributions automatically from POS tip data, (4) handles credit card processing fee allocation per the salon's policy, (5) generates per-shift tip statements for each staff member, and (6) integrates with payroll for accurate tip income reporting. Setup includes a compliance wizard that asks "which state are you in?" and configures legal tip pooling rules accordingly.

## Who Feels the Pain
Salon owners who spend 30+ minutes nightly on tip distribution and don't know whether their tip pooling policy is legal, and assistants/shampoo techs who suspect they're being underpaid but have no documentation to verify.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 30+ minutes of daily manual tip calculation, ensures compliance with federal and state tip laws (avoiding $10,000-100,000+ in potential wage-and-hour penalties), and builds staff trust through transparent, documented tip distribution.
