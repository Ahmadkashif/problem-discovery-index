# Booking & Communication System with Senior-Friendly Modes

**Niche:** [[niches/hair-salons-independent/aging-clientele/profile|Salons Serving Aging Clientele]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Salon booking platforms default to app-based scheduling and text reminders — excluding older clients who prefer phone calls, struggle with small screens, and don't check text messages.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
A salon pushes online booking to reduce phone call volume. Their 35-year-old clients love it. Their 75-year-old clients — who represent 30% of revenue — can't or won't use it. The salon now runs two parallel systems: online booking for younger clients and phone-based booking for older clients, with the front desk manually managing both calendars. Appointment reminders sent via text go unseen by older clients who prefer phone calls. Cancellation and rescheduling links in SMS messages are unusable for clients with vision limitations or unfamiliarity with mobile web interfaces.

## What Already Exists
Vagaro, Square Appointments, Fresha, and Booksy handle online booking with text/email confirmations. Some (Vagaro, Boulevard) support phone booking with manual calendar entry. Apptoto and GReminders offer multi-channel appointment reminders including phone calls. None offer an integrated system where the booking platform recognizes client communication preferences and automatically routes through the right channel — phone call for Mrs. Johnson, text for her granddaughter.

## The Customization Gap
The booking system needs per-client communication preferences: (1) phone-call appointment reminders (automated or staff-assisted) for clients who don't text, (2) large-text confirmation emails for clients with vision limitations, (3) phone-based booking that's equally featured to online booking (not a manual workaround), (4) family member booking delegation (Mrs. Johnson's daughter books and receives confirmations on her behalf), and (5) automated recurring appointment scheduling for clients on fixed schedules ("every Thursday at 10am, until further notice").

## Target Customer
Salons with 30%+ senior clientele who currently run dual booking systems and spend 5-8 hours/week on phone-based scheduling for older clients.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates the dual-system scheduling burden, reduces no-shows from older clients by 30-40% through phone-call reminders, and retains senior clients who might otherwise stop coming because the booking process became too frustrating.
