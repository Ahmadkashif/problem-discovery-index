# Salons Serving Aging Clientele

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Salons serve older clients but haven't adapted their booking, communication, or service workflows for this demographic's preferences and needs.
**Target Buyer:** Salon owner in an aging-demographic market or serving 40%+ clients over age 65
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Appointment reminders, accessibility accommodations, and transportation coordination are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
As the US population ages (10,000 Americans turn 65 daily), many independent salons derive 30-50% of revenue from clients over 65 — a demographic with distinct needs that salon technology ignores. Older clients prefer phone booking over apps, need accessibility accommodations (wheelchair-friendly stations, adjustable chairs, lower sinks), may have cognitive or mobility limitations that affect service delivery, and increasingly need home or facility-based services as they age. The salon that serves a client from age 40 to 85 must adapt continuously — but salon management tools assume a digitally native, able-bodied, walk-in clientele.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard salon booking platforms (Vagaro, Square Appointments) default to online/app booking. Some salons maintain separate phone-based booking for older clients, creating dual scheduling systems. No salon platform addresses: accessibility accommodation tracking per client, aging-related service modifications (thinner hair, scalp sensitivity, mobility limitations), transition from in-salon to home-based service, or communication preferences (phone calls, large-text reminders).
