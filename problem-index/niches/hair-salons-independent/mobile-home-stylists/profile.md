# Mobile & Home-Based Stylists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most operate through personal phone, social media, and cash/digital payment apps with no business management tools.
**Target Buyer:** Solo stylist providing in-home or mobile services
**Automation Potential:** High — Route planning, client communication, and payment processing are all highly automatable for mobile service providers.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobile stylists bring salon services to the client's home, assisted living facilities, hospitals, or event venues. This is a growing segment driven by aging populations, convenience preferences, and post-pandemic comfort with at-home services. The operational challenges are unique: route optimization across multiple daily stops, equipment portability constraints (what services can you offer from a rolling cart?), variable pricing by location (travel fees, parking costs), client safety and vetting, and the complete absence of walk-in traffic that brick-and-mortar salons rely on. No salon management platform addresses any of these mobile-specific needs.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Mobile stylists typically use their phone contacts for client management, text messages for scheduling, Google Maps for navigation, and Venmo/Zelle for payment. Some use generic booking apps (Acuity, Calendly) but these don't account for travel time between appointments, service-area boundaries, or location-based pricing. The gap is total — no purpose-built mobile stylist management platform exists.
