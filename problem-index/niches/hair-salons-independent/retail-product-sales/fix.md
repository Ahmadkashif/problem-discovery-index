# Stylist Retail Performance Tracking & Incentive Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/hair-salons-independent/retail-product-sales/profile|Retail Product Sales & Inventory]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Salon owners want stylists to sell retail products but have no system to track per-stylist retail performance, set goals, or automate commission incentives — so retail selling effort is inconsistent and unrewarded.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #worker-facing

## The Problem
In most salons, retail product sales are attributed to whichever stylist rings up the transaction — but some stylists recommend products and the client buys at the front desk without attribution. Some stylists consistently recommend products and others never do, but the owner can't see who's performing because attribution is broken. Retail commission programs (typically 10-15% of retail sales) exist on paper but are calculated manually from POS reports at month-end. The delay between the behavior (recommending a product) and the reward (commission on the next paycheck) is too long to drive consistent behavior change.

## Why It's Still Broken
POS systems attribute product sales to the register operator, not the recommending stylist. When a client buys a product recommended by Stylist A but the front desk rings it up, the sale is unattributed or credited to the wrong person. Fixing this requires a recommendation-to-sale attribution system that connects the in-chair recommendation to the checkout transaction — a workflow step that adds friction. Owners avoid enforcing retail tracking because they don't want to alienate stylists, and stylists don't prioritize retail because the commission tracking is unreliable.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A retail attribution and incentive system that: (1) attributes product sales to the recommending stylist (via a "recommended by" field at checkout or automatic attribution to the client's current stylist), (2) tracks per-stylist retail revenue, conversion rate, and product mix in real time, (3) sets retail goals per stylist with visible progress dashboards, (4) auto-calculates retail commissions per pay period without manual spreadsheet work, and (5) provides coaching insights ("Stylist A recommends the finishing spray to 80% of blowout clients — Stylist B recommends it to 15%").

## Who Feels the Pain
Salon owners who know retail is underperforming but can't identify why or hold specific stylists accountable, and top-performing retail stylists who don't get credit for products sold through the front desk.

## Impact If Fixed
Proper attribution and real-time commission tracking increases per-stylist retail sales by 25-40%, adding $1,000-3,000/month in retail revenue for a 5-stylist salon. Coaching insights help bring bottom performers up to average, multiplying the effect.
