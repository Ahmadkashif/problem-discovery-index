# Retail Product Sales & Inventory

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hair-salons-independent|Independent Hair Salons]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4.8B (salon retail revenue)
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — POS systems track sales but inventory management, reordering, and product recommendation are manual.
**Target Buyer:** Salon owner/manager or product buyer
**Automation Potential:** High — Product reordering, inventory tracking, and personalized client recommendations follow repeatable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Professional haircare product retail (shampoo, conditioner, styling products, treatments) represents a high-margin revenue stream that most independent salons dramatically underperform. Industry benchmarks suggest retail should be 15-20% of salon revenue, but most independents achieve only 5-10%. The gap is operational: stylists don't recommend products because they forget what the client uses, inventory management is manual (shelf-walking), reordering is reactive (waiting until products run out), and there's no system to connect in-chair product usage with retail recommendations. The products the stylist uses during the service are the exact products the client should buy — but this recommendation pipeline is entirely dependent on the stylist remembering to mention it.

## Current Tools & Gaps
POS systems (Square, Vagaro, Fresha) track product sales. Salon distributor platforms (SalonCentric, CosmoProf) handle wholesale ordering. No platform connects the dots: tracking which products were used in which services, prompting stylists to recommend products based on the service just performed, automating reorder triggers based on depletion rate, or providing per-stylist retail performance analytics.
