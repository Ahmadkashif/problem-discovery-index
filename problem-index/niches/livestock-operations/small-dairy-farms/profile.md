# Small Dairy Farms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — operations under 200 head typically use paper breeding wheels, hand-written milk records, and basic spreadsheets; precision dairy platforms (DeLaval, Lely, Afimilk) price out of reach at $100-200/cow investment
**Target Buyer:** Dairy Farmer managing 50-200 head milking herd
**Automation Potential:** Medium — reproduction management, feed efficiency tracking, and bulk tank quality troubleshooting are automatable with affordable sensor solutions

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small dairy farms (under 200 milking cows) represent roughly 70% of US dairy operations by count but only 20% of milk production. These operations are typically family-run with 1-3 full-time workers, using older parlor systems (flat barns, herringbone parlors) rather than robotic milking. The farmer is the herdsman, nutritionist, and business manager simultaneously. Reproduction management — detecting heat (estrus), timing artificial insemination, confirming pregnancy, managing dry-off — is the highest-value decision process and the most dependent on tacit observation skills. The farmer who notices a cow's subtle behavioral change (restlessness, mounting, mucus discharge) at the right moment determines whether that cow calves again in 12 months or misses a cycle and costs $250-400 in extended days open.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Large-dairy precision tools (Afimilk's AfiAct, DeLaval's DelPro, SCR/Allflex HeatTime) provide automated heat detection via activity monitors at $15-25/cow, but the per-head economics only work when spread across 500+ cows with a $10K+ herd management computer. Small dairies that try these systems face integration challenges with older parlor equipment and support gaps from dealers focused on large-herd installations. DHIA (Dairy Herd Improvement Association) testing provides monthly milk composition data but no real-time management tools.
