# Herd Management Software Scaled for 50-200 Cow Operations

**Niche:** [[niches/livestock-operations/small-dairy-farms/profile|Small Dairy Farms]]
**Industry:** [[industries/livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Dairy herd management software (PCDART, DairyComp 305, BoviSync) is designed for herds of 500+ cows with dedicated herd managers — the interface complexity and price point exclude the 100-cow farmer who is also the milker, feeder, and bookkeeper.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A 100-cow dairy farmer wears every hat: milking twice daily, feeding, breeding, treating sick cows, maintaining equipment, managing finances. This farmer needs herd management decisions simplified to the essentials — which cows to breed today, which are due to dry off this week, which have SCC (somatic cell count) trending up — without navigating software designed for a full-time herd manager analyzing 2,000-cow datasets. Current software presents 50+ data fields per cow when the small-herd farmer needs 5-10 actionable items per day.

## What Already Exists
DairyComp 305 (Valley Agricultural Software) is the industry standard for herd management but assumes a dedicated herd manager and costs $2,000-5,000 with annual support fees. PCDART integrates with DHIA test data but is desktop-only with a legacy interface. BoviSync offers a cloud-based option but still presents the full complexity of large-herd management.

## The Customization Gap
A small-dairy adaptation needs: (1) a daily action-list interface ("today: breed #47, dry off #23, recheck #81 for mastitis") rather than query-based data exploration, (2) integration with DHIA monthly test data as the primary data source (since the farmer cannot afford daily milk meters), (3) simple financial tracking connecting milk checks, feed purchases, and veterinary bills to per-cow profitability, and (4) pricing at $30-50/month rather than $2,000+ upfront. The product competes with a whiteboard and a breeding wheel — it must be simpler than both.

## Target Customer
Dairy farmers with 50-200 milking cows who currently manage reproduction and health records on paper or simple spreadsheets. Approximately 18,000 US dairy operations at this scale.

## Impact If Solved
Reproduction management improvements from systematic tracking (rather than memory) reduce average calving interval by 15-25 days, worth $45-75/cow/year. Early mastitis detection from SCC trend monitoring reduces clinical mastitis incidence by 10-15%, saving $200-400/case. Total herd-level impact of $8,000-$15,000/year on a 100-cow dairy.
