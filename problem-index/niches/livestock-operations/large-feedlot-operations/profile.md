# Large Feedlot Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-50B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — large feedlots (5,000+ head) use pen management software (Micro Technologies, Performance Livestock Analytics) and some IoT monitoring, but mid-size feedlots (1,000-5,000 head) remain largely manual
**Target Buyer:** Feedlot Manager / Cattle Buyer at operations finishing 5,000-100,000 head annually
**Automation Potential:** High — pen riding health checks, cattle placement decisions, closeout projections, and marketing timing are all high-value automation targets

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Large feedlots are the finishing stage of beef production, purchasing feeder cattle from cow-calf operators and feeding them for 120-180 days to market weight. These operations are capital-intensive businesses with $10M-$100M+ in cattle inventory at any given time and razor-thin margins of $20-80/head on good closes. The feedlot manager must simultaneously optimize cattle placement (buying the right cattle at the right price), health management (detecting and treating BRD before it causes permanent lung damage), nutrition (formulating rations that maximize gain-to-feed ratio), and marketing (shipping cattle at optimal weight and quality grade for grid pricing). Each of these decisions involves pattern recognition that experienced feedlot managers develop over 10-20 years — reading cattle off the truck to assess health risk, walking pens to spot early illness, adjusting rations based on bunk calls, and timing shipments to hit quality grade premiums.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Performance Livestock Analytics (PLA) and Micro Technologies (Cattleman) provide feedlot management software covering processing records, treatment protocols, and closeout accounting. Walk-over weighing (Tru-Test) and IoT ear tags (Allflex SenseHub) exist but penetration is under 20% even in large feedlots. No platform integrates real-time health monitoring data with closeout economics to answer the question every feedlot manager asks daily: "Which pens should I ship this week to maximize profit?"
