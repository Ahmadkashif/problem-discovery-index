# Feedlot Management Software with Real-Time Health Analytics

**Niche:** [[niches/livestock-operations/large-feedlot-operations/profile|Large Feedlot Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/livestock-operations|Livestock Operations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Feedlot management software (Micro Technologies, PLA) tracks processing records and closeout accounting but does not integrate real-time health monitoring data to predict pen-level morbidity risk or treatment response.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A 20,000-head feedlot processes 80-150 animals through the hospital facility daily during peak BRD season. Treatment decisions follow rigid protocols (first pull gets Drug A, second pull gets Drug B, third pull goes to chronic pen), but the actual response rate varies dramatically by incoming cattle source, days on feed, weather stress, and the specific pathogen load in that pen. The feedlot health manager knows from experience which lots from which sources will "blow up" with BRD at day 14-21, but this knowledge is never formalized or used to adjust arrival processing protocols proactively.

## What Already Exists
Micro Technologies' Cattleman software and Performance Livestock Analytics handle individual animal processing records, treatment histories, and pen-level performance tracking. These systems are comprehensive databases but not analytical platforms — they record what happened but do not predict what will happen. The health manager must mentally integrate pen-level morbidity trends, weather patterns, and incoming lot characteristics to anticipate problems.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is an analytical layer on top of existing feedlot management software that: (1) predicts pen-level morbidity probability at arrival based on source, transport distance, commingling, arrival weight variation, and weather forecast, (2) recommends arrival processing protocol adjustments (metaphylactic treatment decisions) based on predicted risk, (3) tracks treatment response rates by protocol and pathogen pattern to identify when antibiotic resistance may be emerging, and (4) generates automated health summary reports for the consulting veterinarian that highlight anomalies rather than requiring manual chart review.

## Target Customer
Feedlot health managers and consulting veterinarians at operations with 5,000+ head capacity. Approximately 2,500 US feedlots at this scale, plus the 500-800 veterinary practices that serve them.

## Impact If Solved
Risk-adjusted arrival processing reduces BRD morbidity by 10-15% in high-risk lots, saving $15-25/head in treatment costs and death loss. Automated veterinary reporting saves the health manager 5-8 hours/week of manual data compilation. Earlier detection of protocol failures prevents 2-3% of chronic cases that currently cost $200-500/head in accumulated treatment before the animal is salvaged.
