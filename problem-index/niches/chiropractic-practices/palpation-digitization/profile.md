## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
This is the highest-potential tacit knowledge ML opportunity in chiropractic. Experienced chiropractors develop extraordinary tactile diagnostic abilities over 10-20 years of practice. Through palpation (hands-on examination), they detect subluxations (vertebral misalignments), myofascial trigger points, tissue texture changes, joint hypomobility/hypermobility, and subtle asymmetries that don't appear on X-rays or MRIs. This skill is the foundation of chiropractic care — the DC's hands are the primary diagnostic instrument. Yet the findings are entirely subjective, non-transferable (you can't teach someone to "feel" a subluxation in a textbook), and undocumentable (SOAP notes say "T5 subluxation" but not the tactile data that led to that conclusion).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some objective measurement tools exist: surface EMG (sEMG) measures paraspinal muscle activity, thermography detects temperature asymmetries, and algometers measure pressure-pain thresholds. These provide proxy measurements but don't capture what the chiropractor's hands actually feel — tissue compliance, joint end-feel quality, fascial restriction patterns, and the subtle "give" that indicates a successful adjustment. No tool translates tactile palpation into quantitative data.
