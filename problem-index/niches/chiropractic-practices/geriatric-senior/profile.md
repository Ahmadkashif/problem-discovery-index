# Geriatric & Senior Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most geriatric chiropractic patients are seen in practices without age-specific protocols. Documentation, fall risk assessment, and Medicare compliance are managed ad hoc.
**Target Buyer:** DC Near Senior Communities
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Medicare documentation and fall-risk screening are highly automatable; clinical technique modification requires practitioner judgment.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Americans over 65 are the fastest-growing chiropractic demographic, driven by Medicare coverage of chiropractic spinal manipulation. However, treating geriatric patients requires technique modification (osteoporosis risk, joint degeneration, medication interactions), fall-risk assessment integration, Medicare-specific documentation (medical necessity for each visit), and care coordination with primary care physicians and specialists. Most DCs are trained in general chiropractic and apply adult protocols to elderly patients without modification — risking injury, documentation audits, and missed opportunities for fall prevention and functional independence maintenance.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Chiropractic EHRs handle Medicare billing but don't provide geriatric-specific assessment templates, fall-risk screening tools, or age-appropriate outcome measures. Medicare compliance tools focus on billing codes (98940-98942) but not on the clinical documentation that supports medical necessity. No tool helps DCs implement geriatric-specific clinical protocols or coordinate care with the patient's primary physician.
