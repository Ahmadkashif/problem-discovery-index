# Medicare Medical Necessity Documentation Assistant

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/geriatric-senior/profile|Geriatric & Senior Care]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Medical necessity documentation tools exist for other specialties, but none address the specific Medicare requirements for chiropractic — documenting subluxation presence, demonstrating functional improvement, and justifying continued treatment beyond maintenance care.
**Tags:** #llm #text-classification #nlp #compliance #automation

## The Problem
Medicare covers chiropractic manipulation only for documented subluxation with demonstrated functional improvement. Each visit note must document the subluxation (location, exam findings supporting it), the functional impairment, and the treatment provided. Every 12 visits, the DC must demonstrate continued improvement to justify additional treatment. If the patient reaches "maximum therapeutic benefit," Medicare stops paying — the care becomes "maintenance" and the patient must pay out of pocket. The documentation burden is intense, and Medicare audits recover millions annually from chiropractors whose notes don't adequately support medical necessity.

## What Already Exists
Chiropractic EHRs include Medicare compliance templates. Revenue cycle management tools (Kareo, AdvancedMD) handle Medicare billing. Medicare compliance consulting services review documentation for audit risk. None provides real-time, visit-level medical necessity feedback — alerting the DC during documentation that "this note doesn't adequately support the subluxation finding" or "visit 11 of 12 — re-examination needed before next visit to demonstrate continued improvement."

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a real-time documentation assistant that understands Medicare's chiropractic-specific medical necessity requirements: (1) verifies each note contains required subluxation documentation elements, (2) tracks the visit count toward the re-examination threshold, (3) analyzes outcome trends to predict when the patient is approaching maximum therapeutic benefit, and (4) alerts the DC when documentation gaps would trigger an audit finding. Must understand chiropractic clinical language and Medicare's specific requirements for chiropractic (LCD L33604).

## Target Customer
DCs with 40%+ Medicare patient populations, typically seeing 50-100 Medicare patients per week, at high risk for Medicare audits.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces Medicare audit findings by 60-80%, preventing $50K-$200K in annual recoupment for high-volume Medicare practices. Eliminates 10-15 minutes per visit of documentation rework by getting it right the first time. Extends average Medicare treatment episodes by 15-20% through properly documented medical necessity.
