# Fall Risk and Functional Independence Assessment for Chiropractic

**One-liner:** No chiropractic tool integrates validated fall-risk screening (Timed Up and Go, Berg Balance Scale) with chiropractic spinal assessment to create a combined musculoskeletal-balance treatment plan that addresses both spinal dysfunction and fall prevention.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Falls are the leading cause of injury death in Americans over 65, and 30% of adults over 65 fall each year. Chiropractors who treat elderly patients are in a unique position to assess fall risk — they evaluate balance, gait, proprioception, and musculoskeletal function in every visit. But DCs don't routinely perform standardized fall-risk screenings because the tools weren't designed for chiropractic practice, and there's no clinical pathway connecting chiropractic spinal findings to fall-risk interventions. A DC who detects proprioceptive deficits in the cervical spine has no protocol for integrating this finding into a fall prevention care plan.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Fall-risk assessment belongs to the physical therapy and geriatric medicine domains. Chiropractic education includes basic geriatric training but doesn't integrate fall prevention into the clinical workflow. Building a chiropractic fall-risk tool requires mapping validated screening instruments (TUG, BBS, 4-Stage Balance Test) to chiropractic findings (cervical proprioception, lumbopelvic stability, lower extremity joint function) and creating clinical pathways that connect them.

## What to Build
A geriatric assessment module that adds validated fall-risk screening to the chiropractic examination workflow. The DC performs standard chiropractic assessment plus a 5-minute fall-risk screen (TUG, Single Leg Stance, 4-Stage Balance). The system combines spinal findings with balance scores to generate a combined musculoskeletal-balance risk profile and a treatment plan that addresses both spinal dysfunction and fall risk factors. Tracks outcomes over time — fall-risk score improvement correlated with chiropractic treatment — providing the evidence base for chiropractic as a fall prevention intervention.

## Target Customer
DCs treating 30+ Medicare-age patients per week, located near senior living communities, retirement areas, or Medicare Advantage-participating networks.

## Impact If Built
Positions chiropractic as a fall prevention service (a Medicare-billable value proposition), expanding the clinical scope and revenue for geriatric-focused practices. Reduces fall risk in treated populations by 20-30% through combined spinal and balance interventions. Provides outcomes data that supports Medicare coverage expansion for chiropractic.
