# Patient Engagement App with Plan Progress Visualization

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/treatment-compliance/profile|Treatment Plan Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Patient engagement apps exist for general healthcare, but none visualize chiropractic treatment plan progress in a way that motivates continued compliance — showing the patient where they are in their care journey and why each phase matters.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Chiropractic patients drop out of treatment plans primarily because they can't see their progress. After the initial relief phase, improvements are subtle — the patient feels "fine" and questions whether continued visits are worth the time and money. The DC explains progress during office visits, but the patient forgets the explanation by the time they're deciding whether to keep their next appointment. There's no persistent, patient-facing visualization of treatment progress that the patient can reference between visits when deciding whether to continue.

## What Already Exists
Patient portal apps (MyChart, Klara) provide appointment scheduling and messaging. Health apps (Apple Health, Fitbit) track fitness metrics. Therapy compliance apps (PT Pal, MedBridge) track exercise adherence for physical therapy. None is designed for chiropractic treatment plans — showing the three-phase care model (relief, corrective, maintenance), tracking visits completed versus planned, displaying outcome improvements over time, and providing phase-specific education about why this week's visits matter.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a chiropractic-specific patient app that shows: (1) a visual care journey timeline with the patient's current position (e.g., "You're in Week 6 of the Corrective Phase — 16 of 24 visits completed"); (2) outcome trend charts showing pain scores, ROM improvements, and functional assessments over time; (3) phase-specific educational content explaining what's happening physiologically during this phase and why visit frequency matters; and (4) gamification elements (visit streaks, milestone achievements) that leverage behavioral psychology to maintain engagement.

## Target Customer
Chiropractic practices with patient demographics under age 45 (comfortable with apps), offering treatment plans of 12+ visits, with completion rate challenges.

## Impact If Solved
Improves treatment plan compliance by 15-25% through persistent patient engagement between visits. Reduces "I forgot my appointment" no-shows by 30-40% through plan-aware reminders. Increases patient satisfaction scores by making progress visible and care journey transparent.
