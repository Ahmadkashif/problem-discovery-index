# Report of Findings Presentation Ineffectiveness

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/treatment-compliance/profile|Treatment Plan Compliance Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** The "Report of Findings" — where the DC presents the treatment plan to the patient — has a 50-60% acceptance rate because DCs present clinical findings in terminology patients don't understand, and patients can't evaluate whether the proposed plan is worth the investment.
**Tags:** #data-integration #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
After the initial examination, the DC presents a "Report of Findings" (ROF) to the patient — explaining the diagnosis, the treatment plan (visit frequency and duration), and the cost. This is the most important sales and clinical communication moment in chiropractic practice. Yet most DCs present the ROF using X-ray images the patient can't interpret, clinical terminology ("you have a C5-C6 subluxation with disc degeneration"), and treatment plans expressed in chiropractic concepts ("we need to correct the subluxation pattern through specific adjustments over 12 weeks"). Patients nod along but don't understand what's wrong, don't understand why the proposed number of visits is necessary, and leave uncertain about whether to commit. Acceptance rates average 50-60%.

## Why It's Still Broken
DCs are trained in clinical communication, not patient education. The ROF format hasn't evolved — it's still a clinician explaining findings to a layperson using clinical language and imaging. Practice management consultants teach "closing" techniques (financial urgency, scarcity) rather than genuine patient understanding. No tool helps the DC translate clinical findings into patient-understandable language with visual aids that show "here's what's wrong, here's what we're going to do about it, here's what you'll feel at each stage, and here's what happens if you don't get this treated."

## What a Fix Looks Like
A ROF presentation tool that auto-generates a patient-friendly visual presentation from the clinical findings: animated spine models showing the patient's specific issues in plain language, comparison images ("your spine" versus "normal spine"), a visual treatment timeline showing each phase with expected outcomes, cost breakdown with payment plan options, and before/after examples from similar patients (anonymized). The DC presents this visual report instead of explaining X-rays in clinical terminology. The patient receives a digital copy they can review at home before deciding.

## Who Feels the Pain
DCs who lose 40-50% of potential treatment plan patients at the ROF due to communication failures. Patients who decline treatment they would have accepted if they understood the clinical rationale.

## Impact If Fixed
Increases ROF acceptance rate from 55% to 75-80%. For a practice presenting 20 ROFs per month at $3,000 average plan value, improving acceptance by 20 percentage points adds 4 new plans per month — $12,000 per month or $144K per year in additional revenue.
