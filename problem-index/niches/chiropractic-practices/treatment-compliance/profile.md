# Treatment Plan Compliance Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Treatment plans are presented verbally or on paper during the report of findings. Compliance tracking is manual — the front desk checks if the patient has been coming as recommended.
**Target Buyer:** DC / Office Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Treatment plan adherence follows clear rules (recommended visit frequency, milestone re-examinations, home exercise compliance) that can be automatically tracked and communicated.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Chiropractic treatment plans typically prescribe a specific visit frequency over a defined period: "3x/week for 4 weeks, then 2x/week for 4 weeks, then 1x/week for 8 weeks." Patient compliance with these plans averages only 40-60% — patients attend the first few weeks enthusiastically, then drop off as symptoms improve (regardless of whether the underlying dysfunction is resolved). Non-compliant patients have worse outcomes, relapse more frequently, and are less satisfied — but the DC has no systematic way to track compliance, identify dropping patients, or intervene at the moment compliance breaks down.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Chiropractic EHRs display the treatment plan but don't track actual compliance against the plan in real-time. Appointment scheduling shows missed appointments but not compliance rate against the prescribed frequency. Automated reminder systems (Solutionreach, Demandforce) send appointment reminders but can't send plan-specific compliance communications ("You've attended 2 of the 3 recommended visits this week — your next adjustment is important for your recovery progress").
