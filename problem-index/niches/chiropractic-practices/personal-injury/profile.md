# Personal Injury / Auto Accident

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — PI practices use chiropractic EHRs (ChiroTouch, ECLIPSE) and case management tools, but the attorney communication, lien tracking, and medical-legal documentation workflows are largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** PI Chiropractor / Clinic Owner
**Automation Potential:** High — PI documentation follows strict medical-legal requirements with predictable templates, and lien/payment tracking is a pure workflow automation target.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Personal injury chiropractic operates on a fundamentally different business model than insurance or cash-pay practices. The DC treats auto accident patients on a lien basis — deferring payment until the attorney settles the case, which takes 6-24 months. Revenue per case is high ($5,000-$15,000) but cash flow is severely delayed. The documentation requirements are medical-legal: every treatment note must justify medical necessity, support causation (injury caused by the accident, not pre-existing), and withstand cross-examination if the case goes to trial. PI practices live and die on their documentation quality — sloppy notes result in attorney settlement reductions or IME challenges.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ChiroTouch, ECLIPSE, and Jane App handle clinical EHR. PI-specific tools like PI Docs and Attorney Gateway manage case information. The gap is in the documentation-to-settlement pipeline: no tool generates medical-legal narratives from treatment notes, tracks maximum medical improvement (MMI) timing, auto-generates final reports for attorneys, or provides real-time visibility into the lien portfolio (outstanding value, expected settlement timeline, attorney communication status).
