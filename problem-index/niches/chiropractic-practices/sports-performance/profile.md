## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Sports chiropractic focuses on performance optimization, injury prevention, and athletic rehabilitation — not pain relief. The patient is a competitive athlete (amateur to professional) who wants to run faster, lift heavier, or move more efficiently. The DC assesses biomechanical function, identifies movement restrictions that limit performance, and treats to restore optimal biomechanics. This requires different assessment methods (functional movement screens, sport-specific testing), different outcome measures (vertical jump, sprint time, range of motion under load), and different communication (athletes want data and metrics, not subjective assessments).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Functional movement screening tools (FMS, SFMA) provide standardized assessment protocols. Force plates and motion capture systems exist in elite sports medicine settings. Sports-specific EHR modules are absent — DCs use standard chiropractic documentation to record findings from sport-specific assessments. No tool connects the chiropractic spinal assessment to sport-specific performance metrics, enabling the DC to show "your T4-T7 restriction was reducing your thoracic rotation by 15 degrees, limiting your golf swing power by an estimated 8%."
