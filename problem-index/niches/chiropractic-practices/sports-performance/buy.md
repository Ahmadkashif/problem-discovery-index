# Movement Analysis with Chiropractic Treatment Mapping

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/sports-performance/profile|Athlete & Sports Performance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** AI-powered movement analysis apps exist for coaches and trainers, but none map movement deficiencies to specific spinal segments and joint restrictions that a chiropractor can treat — connecting the movement screen to the chiropractic treatment plan.
**Tags:** #cnn #pose-estimation #computer-vision #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
Computer vision movement analysis tools (from simple phone apps to advanced systems) can detect movement compensations, asymmetries, and range-of-motion limitations during functional movements (squat, lunge, overhead press). But the output is movement-level ("limited hip flexion on the left side") — it doesn't map to chiropractic treatment targets ("left sacroiliac joint restriction causing compensatory hip flexion limitation"). The DC must mentally bridge the gap between what the movement screen shows and what their hands should assess and treat. Experienced sports DCs do this automatically; newer practitioners miss the connection.

## What Already Exists
AI movement analysis tools (Physitrack, Kinetisense, Coach's Eye) use phone cameras to analyze movement quality and identify compensations. Functional Movement Screen (FMS) protocols provide standardized scoring. Neither maps movement findings to spinal/joint treatment targets — they output movement assessments, not chiropractic treatment plans.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a clinical reasoning layer that maps movement analysis findings to chiropractic examination priorities and treatment targets. When the movement screen identifies "limited thoracic rotation during a golf swing," the system suggests examining T3-T8 segmental mobility, checking rib articulation, and assessing thoracic paraspinal muscle tone — directing the DC's hands to the anatomical structures most likely causing the movement limitation. For experienced DCs, this confirms their clinical reasoning; for newer practitioners, it teaches the movement-to-treatment connection.

## Target Customer
Sports chiropractors who use movement screening protocols and want to connect video-based movement analysis to their clinical examination and treatment planning workflow.

## Impact If Solved
Bridges the gap between movement analysis and chiropractic treatment planning. Reduces the learning curve for sports chiropractic — a new CCSP can make treatment decisions with the clinical reasoning support that previously required 5-10 years of sports-specific experience. Improves treatment outcomes by ensuring the adjustment targets the root cause of the movement limitation.
