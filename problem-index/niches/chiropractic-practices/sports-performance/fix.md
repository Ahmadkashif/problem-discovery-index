# Evidence Gap for Performance-Based Chiropractic Claims

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/sports-performance/profile|Athlete & Sports Performance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Sports chiropractors claim they "improve athletic performance" but have no standardized way to measure and document the performance impact of their treatments — undermining credibility with sports medicine professionals and limiting referrals from coaches and teams.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
The sports chiropractic value proposition is "I help athletes perform better and avoid injury." But DCs can't systematically prove this. Treatment notes document spinal findings and adjustments but not performance outcomes. The athlete's coach tracks performance metrics (speed, power, game stats) but doesn't know which improvements to attribute to chiropractic versus training, nutrition, or recovery. Without controlled outcome data, sports chiropractic remains an anecdotal practice — individual athletes say "I feel better after my adjustment" but there's no aggregate data showing "athletes under chiropractic care demonstrate X% improvement in Y metric."

## Why It's Still Broken
Measuring the performance impact of chiropractic requires connecting two disconnected datasets: clinical treatment records (what was done) and performance outcomes (what changed). The DC has the clinical data, the coach/trainer has the performance data, and nobody has built the bridge. Additionally, performance changes are multifactorial — isolating the chiropractic contribution from training and other interventions requires controlled study designs that individual practices can't implement.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A practice-level outcomes database that standardizes the collection of pre/post treatment performance metrics alongside clinical data. Each athlete has a treatment-performance timeline: chiropractic interventions mapped against performance assessments (ROM, functional tests, sport-specific metrics). Aggregate across many athletes to show population-level trends — "athletes receiving cervical-thoracic adjustment showed a 9% improvement in overhead mobility within 2 visits (n=150)." This aggregate evidence supports marketing claims, referral conversations with coaches, and research publications.

## Who Feels the Pain
Sports DCs who can't compete with physical therapists and athletic trainers who have established evidence-based outcome tracking protocols. Athletes who want objective data on whether their chiropractic investment is worthwhile.

## Impact If Fixed
Creates the evidence base that sports chiropractic needs for professional credibility. Enables data-driven marketing — "our athletes average 15% ROM improvement and 8% power output improvement" is more compelling than testimonials. Opens referral pathways from coaches, athletic trainers, and sports medicine physicians who require evidence before referring.
