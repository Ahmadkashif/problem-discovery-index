## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pediatric chiropractic treats infants and children for conditions including colic, ear infections, torticollis, scoliosis screening, and neurodevelopmental support. The clinical approach differs significantly from adult chiropractic — gentler techniques (fingertip pressure rather than manual thrust), age-appropriate assessment methods (observation-based rather than orthopedic testing), and different outcome measures (developmental milestones, feeding patterns, sleep quality rather than pain scales). The market is controversial — pediatric chiropractic faces skepticism from pediatricians, which creates both a marketing challenge and a documentation imperative.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Adult chiropractic EHRs (ChiroTouch, ECLIPSE) are used for pediatric patients with workarounds — recording a 3-month-old's "chief complaint" in a system designed for adult back pain. No EHR provides pediatric-specific intake forms, age-appropriate assessment tools, developmental milestone tracking, or parent communication workflows. Pediatric DCs document on paper or in free-text notes because the EHR templates don't fit.
