# Pediatric Chiropractic Clinical Documentation System

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/pediatric-family/profile|Pediatric & Family Chiropractic]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No EHR module provides age-appropriate chiropractic assessment templates, developmental milestone tracking, and pediatric-specific outcome measures for chiropractors treating infants and children.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
A pediatric DC examining a 6-week-old for torticollis needs assessment templates for primitive reflex testing, cranial assessment, cervical range of motion (observed, not measured with a goniometer), and feeding/sleeping pattern questionnaires. None of this exists in adult chiropractic EHRs. The DC documents on paper or types free-text notes that don't capture structured data for outcome tracking. When the child returns at 3 months, the DC has no structured way to compare current findings to the initial assessment — they flip through paper notes trying to remember "was the Moro reflex normal last time?"

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The pediatric chiropractic market is small (estimated 10,000-15,000 DCs who regularly treat children) and concentrated in cash-pay practices (most insurance doesn't cover pediatric chiropractic wellness care). EHR vendors focus on the larger insurance-billing market. The clinical assessment templates require domain expertise — understanding which developmental milestones, reflexes, and functional assessments are relevant at each age and how they relate to chiropractic findings.

## What to Build
A pediatric chiropractic documentation module (plugin for existing EHRs or standalone) with age-appropriate assessment templates that adapt based on the patient's age: neonate (0-3 months) focuses on primitive reflexes, cranial assessment, and feeding patterns; infant (3-12 months) adds motor milestone tracking and postural development; toddler (1-3 years) adds gait assessment and speech development. Each template captures structured data that enables longitudinal tracking — showing parents a developmental progress chart over time.

## Target Customer
ICPA-certified (International Chiropractic Pediatric Association) DCs treating 30+ pediatric patients per week, currently documenting on paper or adult EHR workarounds.

## Impact If Built
Reduces pediatric documentation time by 40-50% through structured templates versus free-text notes. Enables longitudinal outcome tracking that demonstrates clinical value to parents and referring providers. Provides structured data for pediatric chiropractic research.
