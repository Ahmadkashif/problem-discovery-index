# Cash-Pay Wellness & Maintenance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 20%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Cash-pay practices use basic scheduling software and some use membership platforms, but patient engagement, outcomes tracking, and retention optimization are managed through personal relationships and intuition.
**Target Buyer:** Wellness-Focused DC
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Scheduling and membership management are automatable, but patient retention depends on clinical relationships that technology supports rather than replaces.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cash-pay wellness chiropractic operates outside the insurance model entirely. Patients pay out-of-pocket ($30-$75 per visit or $99-$199 monthly memberships) for maintenance adjustments, wellness care, and lifestyle optimization. The business model is subscription-like — revenue depends on patient retention, visit frequency, and lifetime value rather than per-case revenue. These practices market through social media, community events, and word-of-mouth rather than attorney referrals or insurance directories. The clinical approach emphasizes wellness, prevention, and vitalism rather than injury treatment.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Jane App, ChiroSpring, and Square Appointments handle scheduling. Membership platforms like Practice Better and Hint Health manage subscription billing. Generic CRMs handle patient communication. The gap is in patient engagement and retention analytics — no tool tracks patient visit frequency patterns, predicts dropout risk, triggers re-engagement campaigns at the optimal moment, or helps the DC understand which clinical and communication factors drive long-term retention.
