# Outcomes Documentation for Cash-Pay Justification

**Niche:** [[niches/chiropractic-practices/cash-pay-wellness/profile|Cash-Pay Wellness & Maintenance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/chiropractic-practices|Chiropractic Practices]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Cash-pay patients constantly question whether ongoing chiropractic care is "working" because the DC has no systematic way to track and visualize subjective and objective outcomes over time — leading to dropout when patients can't see their progress.
**Tags:** #data-integration #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Cash-pay patients pay $150-$200/month for chiropractic wellness care. After the initial relief phase (first 4-6 weeks), the dramatic improvements plateau, and patients ask: "Am I still getting better? Is this worth it?" The DC knows the patient's spinal function has improved (better range of motion, fewer subluxations on palpation, improved posture), but communicates this verbally — "your spine feels much better than when you started." Without visual outcome tracking, patients perceive maintenance care as stagnant and drop out. This is the #1 cause of cash-pay patient churn after the initial care phase.

## Why It's Still Broken
Chiropractic EHRs capture clinical data (exam findings, SOAP notes) in text format that isn't designed for patient-facing outcome visualization. Outcome measures like VAS pain scales, Oswestry Disability Index, and range-of-motion measurements are recorded inconsistently and not trended over time. No tool produces a patient-facing "health dashboard" showing their progress across multiple outcome dimensions (pain, function, posture, flexibility) in a format they can understand and that justifies continued investment.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A patient-facing outcomes dashboard (accessible via app or patient portal) that tracks key outcome measures over time: pain scores, functional assessments, range of motion measurements, posture analysis (from periodic photos), and subjective wellness ratings. Shows progress visually — charts, milestone markers, comparison to baseline — so the patient can see improvement even when they don't "feel" it visit to visit. Prompts the DC to capture standardized outcome measures at regular intervals and auto-generates progress summaries for patient review consultations.

## Who Feels the Pain
DCs who lose 5-8% of their cash-pay patients per month because patients can't see the value of ongoing care. Patients who quit maintenance care and later experience relapses because they lacked evidence of ongoing benefit.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces cash-pay patient churn during the maintenance phase by 30-40%. For a practice with 250 maintenance patients at $150/month, retaining 10 additional patients per month adds $18K per month or $216K per year in recurring revenue.
