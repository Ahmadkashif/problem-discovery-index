## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — prepress is digital, press operations use electronic ink key control on newer presses, but job tracking, waste management, and scheduling remain manual at most shops
**Target Buyer:** Owner or VP Production at mid-size commercial offset shops (10-100 employees)
**Automation Potential:** High — color calibration, waste tracking, and job scheduling are all automatable with historical production data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial offset printing (sheetfed and web) remains the dominant technology for runs above 1,000 impressions — business cards, brochures, catalogs, direct mail, and packaging. The defining operational challenge is makeready: the time and material consumed setting up each job (plate mounting, ink key adjustment, registration, color matching) before production printing can begin. Makeready consumes 15-45 minutes and 50-200 waste sheets per job. As average run lengths decline (customers ordering smaller quantities more frequently), makeready cost per impression increases, squeezing margins. The pressman's skill in achieving color match quickly — reading a press sheet and knowing exactly which ink keys to adjust — is the highest-value craft skill in the shop and the one most threatened by workforce aging.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EFI Pace and Avanti Slingshot dominate MIS for mid-to-large shops. Heidelberg Prinect and Komori KHS provide press-level automation on newer presses. Inline spectrophotometers (X-Rite IntelliTrax, Techkon) measure color during production. No platform provides ML-assisted color calibration (predicting ink key settings from spectrophotometer readings), predictive waste estimation per job type, or makeready time optimization based on historical performance by press/substrate/ink combination.
