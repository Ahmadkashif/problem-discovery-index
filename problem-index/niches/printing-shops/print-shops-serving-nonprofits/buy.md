# Multi-Reviewer Proofing Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/printing-shops/print-shops-serving-nonprofits/profile|Print Shops Serving Nonprofits & Churches]]
**Industry:** [[industries/printing-shops|Printing Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Online proofing tools (InDesign Review, Ziflow, ProofHQ) manage single-reviewer proof approval, but nonprofit and church print orders require committee approval (pastor + communications director + board member) with consolidated feedback — and no proofing tool handles the multi-reviewer, multi-round approval common in nonprofit buying.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
When a church orders a 50th anniversary booklet or a nonprofit orders a fundraising mailer, the proof must be reviewed and approved by 3-5 people: the communications coordinator (design accuracy), the executive director or pastor (messaging), a board member (budget approval), and sometimes a donor or honoree (factual accuracy). The print shop emails a PDF proof, the communications coordinator prints copies and distributes to the committee, collects feedback (often contradictory) over 3-7 days, consolidates the comments, and sends revision requests to the shop. Two to three rounds of this cycle add 1-3 weeks to the production timeline. The print shop has no visibility into the approval process — they send the proof and wait.

## What Already Exists
Online proofing platforms (Ziflow, ProofHQ, PageProof) provide markup and approval workflows for creative agencies and marketing departments. These tools assume a single decision-maker or a fast sequential approval chain. They cost $50-200/month per user — unsuitable for nonprofit committees who proof 2-3 times per year.

## The Customization Gap
The system must support: free viewer accounts for committee reviewers (they should not need to install anything or create an account beyond a link), parallel review (all reviewers see and comment simultaneously, not sequentially), automated comment consolidation (highlight contradictions: "Reviewer A says 'change to blue,' Reviewer B says 'keep it green'"), deadline tracking with automated reminders (the committee has 5 business days to approve; after 3 days, send a reminder), and a simple approve/reject per reviewer with the shop seeing status in real time. Cost must be zero for the nonprofit reviewers and $25-50/month for the print shop.

## Target Customer
Print shops serving 5+ nonprofit/church clients with committee-based approval processes. Approximately 3,000-5,000 US shops.

## Impact If Solved
Proof approval cycle drops from 3-7 days to 1-3 days. Revision rounds decrease from 2-3 to 1-2 (contradictions caught in first round rather than discovered in subsequent rounds). Shop production scheduling improves because proof approval timing becomes visible and predictable rather than a black box.
