# Print Shops Serving Nonprofits & Churches

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/printing-shops|Printing Shops]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — print production is standard, but the nonprofit ordering workflow (committee approvals, donor-funded budgets, recurring seasonal campaigns) is not addressed by any print shop tool
**Target Buyer:** Office Manager or Communications Director at nonprofits and churches; also the print shop owner who serves this segment
**Automation Potential:** Medium — recurring order management, template-based reordering, and budget-constrained estimating are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Nonprofits and churches represent 10-20% of revenue at many small print shops — a reliable, recurring customer base that orders bulletins, newsletters, event programs, fundraising materials, and donor communications on predictable schedules. But the buying behavior is distinct from commercial clients: purchase decisions involve committee approval (3-5 people review the proof), budgets are grant-funded or donation-constrained (price sensitivity is extreme), orders recur on predictable schedules (weekly bulletins, quarterly newsletters, annual fundraising campaigns), and the "client" is often a volunteer or part-time communications coordinator with no design or print buying experience.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Print shops serve nonprofits using the same workflow as commercial clients — but the nonprofit's approval process (multiple reviewers, slow turnaround) and recurring order patterns do not fit the one-off job workflow. No platform provides: template-based recurring ordering (same bulletin layout, different content each week), multi-reviewer proofing workflow (committee approval with tracked comments), budget tracking per account (how much of the annual print budget has been spent?), or nonprofit-friendly pricing (pre-agreed rate cards for standard items).
