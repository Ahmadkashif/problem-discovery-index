# Print Estimating Departments

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/printing-shops|Printing Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-8B (embedded in commercial print operations — estimating drives all revenue)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8% (estimating labor cost + margin impact from pricing errors)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — MIS provides estimating templates, but the judgment behind pricing (markup, competitive positioning, job complexity assessment) is entirely human
**Target Buyer:** Lead Estimator or Owner at commercial print shops (5-50 employees)
**Automation Potential:** High — standard product pricing, historical cost calibration, and quote generation are automatable; complex jobs still need human judgment

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Print estimating is the revenue-critical function at every commercial print shop — the estimator's ability to price jobs competitively while protecting margin determines the shop's financial health. A typical estimator handles 10-30 RFQs per day, and the estimating process (reading the job specification, selecting the optimal press/substrate/finishing combination, calculating material and labor costs, adding markup) takes 15-45 minutes per job. The estimator's judgment — developed over 10-20 years — determines which jobs to bid aggressively on (high probability of winning profitable work) and which to price conservatively (complex jobs with high risk of cost overrun). When the estimator retires, the shop's pricing intelligence retires with them.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MIS platforms (EFI Pace, Avanti Slingshot, PrintSmith) provide estimating modules with production standards (press speeds, labor rates, material costs) and markup tables. These tools automate the arithmetic but not the judgment — they calculate cost from the estimator's inputs but do not suggest optimal press assignment, predict actual production time versus standard, or calibrate estimates from historical actual-versus-estimated variance.
