# All-in-One Digital Shop Platform at Micro-Business Pricing

**Niche:** [[niches/printing-shops/small-run-digital-printers/profile|Small-Run Digital Print Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/printing-shops|Printing Shops]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform provides estimating, order management, job tracking, and invoicing for digital print shops at a $50-150/month price point — current MIS options start at $200-500/month and are designed for shops 5-10x larger.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A small digital print shop owner handles 50-200 orders per week across walk-in, email, and phone channels. Each order requires estimating (often using a calculator and the owner's knowledge of per-click and paper costs), recording the order (in a notebook, spreadsheet, or sticky note), tracking the job through prepress/print/finish/delivery, and invoicing (typically in QuickBooks, disconnected from the order). The owner spends 30-40% of their time on administrative tasks that a platform could handle, but MIS options (EFI Pace at $500+/month, Avanti at similar pricing, PrintSmith at $200+/month) are priced and featured for shops 5-10x larger. The small shop owner needs simplicity and low cost, not the feature depth of enterprise MIS.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The small digital print shop market is fragmented (15,000+ shops) with very low per-customer revenue potential ($50-150/month). MIS vendors target mid-to-large shops where contract values are $200-500/month and above. Building for the micro-business segment requires a fundamentally different product approach — mobile-first, minimal configuration, instant-value onboarding — that established MIS vendors are not structured to deliver.

## What to Build
A mobile-first shop management platform at $50-150/month: instant estimating from product templates (business cards, flyers, booklets with configurable pricing), order intake via web form (customer submits file + specifications), job board showing all active orders by status (received / in prepress / printing / finishing / ready for pickup), integrated invoicing (order → invoice → payment tracking), and basic CRM (repeat customer history, reorder prompts). Designed for a 1-person operation to set up in 30 minutes and use on a tablet at the counter.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators of small digital print shops with 1-10 employees and $10-30K monthly revenue, currently managing orders on paper or spreadsheets. Approximately 10,000-15,000 US shops. Target price: $75-125/month.

## Impact If Built
Administrative time drops by 30-40%, freeing 10-15 hours per week for production or sales. Order tracking errors (lost orders, missed deadlines) decrease by 70-80%. Invoicing becomes same-day instead of end-of-week batch, improving cash flow by 3-5 days on average.
