# Missing Document Chase Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/tax-prep-firms/document-intake-automation/profile|Document Intake Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tax-prep-firms|Tax Prep Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Preparers spend 15-20% of their season chasing clients for missing documents — sending individual emails, making phone calls, and tracking which clients have submitted which forms — with no automated system managing the process.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A client uploads 8 of their 12 expected documents to the portal. The preparer must identify which 4 are missing (by comparing the uploaded documents against the prior-year return and the client's expected income sources), send the client a specific request ("We still need your 1099-INT from Chase Bank, your property tax bill, your HSA contribution statement from Fidelity, and your childcare provider's EIN"), and follow up 7-10 days later if the documents have not arrived. Across 500 clients, document chasing generates 1,500-3,000 individual communication touchpoints during the season — each requiring the preparer to remember which client is missing which document.

## Why It's Still Broken
Client portals track whether documents have been uploaded but do not know which specific documents are expected. The "expected documents" list must be generated by comparing the client's prior-year return to the current year's expected income sources — a comparison that no portal or tax software performs automatically. Document chasing is manually managed through email, phone, and personal tracking systems (notebooks, task lists, CRM notes).

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated document chase system that: (1) generates an expected document checklist per client based on the prior-year return and known income sources; (2) tracks portal uploads against the checklist, marking received documents and identifying missing ones; (3) automatically sends the client a specific, personalized request listing the exact missing documents (with descriptions: "Your 1099-INT from Chase Bank — the one showing interest earned on your savings account"); (4) follows up automatically at 7-day intervals; (5) alerts the preparer when a client's file is complete and ready for preparation.

## Who Feels the Pain
Preparers and admin staff who spend 15-20% of their peak season on document chase communications — the least rewarding and most repetitive task in tax preparation — and clients who receive generic "please submit your remaining documents" messages that do not tell them specifically what is missing.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces per-client document chase time from 15-20 minutes (across the season) to under 3 minutes. Accelerates client file completion by 5-10 days on average through proactive, specific communication. Frees 250-500 preparer-hours per season at a 2,000-return firm.
