# Schedule C / Sole Proprietor Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tax-prep-firms|Tax Prep Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$4B in tax preparation fees for Schedule C / sole proprietor returns
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — tax software handles Schedule C calculations, but the upstream workflow (categorizing business expenses from bank/credit card feeds, separating business and personal transactions, determining home office eligibility) is highly manual
**Target Buyer:** Firm owner or managing partner at firms where 40%+ of clients are self-employed
**Automation Potential:** High — expense categorization, mileage tracking verification, and home office calculations follow rule-based logic that can be automated with transaction data integration

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The gig economy has exploded the number of Schedule C filers: Uber drivers, DoorDash couriers, Etsy sellers, freelance designers, and independent contractors of every type. These clients arrive with bank and credit card statements commingling personal and business transactions, incomplete mileage logs, vague expense descriptions, and no understanding of which expenses are deductible. The preparer spends 60-90 minutes per return on expense categorization and documentation before any tax calculation begins — time that is not recoverable through fee increases because gig workers are price-sensitive.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Tax software (Drake, Lacerte, ProConnect) handles Schedule C calculations once data is entered. QuickBooks Self-Employed and FreshBooks offer transaction categorization, but most sole proprietors do not use them consistently (or at all). No tool bridges the gap between the client's raw financial data (bank feed, credit card statements) and the preparer's need for categorized, deductible-classified transactions ready for Schedule C entry.
