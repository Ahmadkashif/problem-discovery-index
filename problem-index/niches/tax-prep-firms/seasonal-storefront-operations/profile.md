# Seasonal Storefront Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tax-prep-firms|Tax Prep Firms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$2B in preparation fees from seasonal storefront tax offices (non-franchise independent operators)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~14%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — many operate with basic tax software and paper-based client management; client portals are rare; marketing is limited to signage and word-of-mouth; seasonal staff onboarding is informal
**Target Buyer:** Seasonal storefront operator who opens January-April and may operate from a strip mall, shared office, or temporary retail space
**Automation Potential:** Medium — the compressed season creates intense operational bottlenecks in appointment scheduling, document intake, and seasonal staff management that are highly amenable to process improvement

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Seasonal storefront operations open in late January and close by mid-May, compressing all revenue generation, client management, and staff training into 14-16 weeks. These operators typically hire 3-10 seasonal preparers (often non-credentialed AFSP holders) who must be trained on the firm's workflow, tax software, and client communication standards in days rather than weeks. Walk-in traffic is the primary client acquisition channel, and the operator manages appointment scheduling, document intake, and quality review simultaneously. Off-season revenue is zero, creating intense pressure to maximize throughput during peak weeks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many storefront operators use basic tax software (Drake, TaxSlayer Pro) with minimal practice management tooling. Appointment scheduling is often a paper book or Google Calendar. Client document intake is paper-based (clients hand over documents at the counter). Quality review is the operator spot-checking returns before e-filing. There is no integrated workflow system designed for the high-throughput, compressed-timeline reality of a seasonal storefront.
