# Tax Treaty Benefit Lookup by Country and Income Type

**Niche:** [[niches/tax-prep-firms/military-expatriate-filers/profile|Military & Expatriate Filer Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tax-prep-firms|Tax Prep Firms]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Tax treaty texts and IRS treaty tables exist in research databases (RIA, CCH, IRS.gov), but no tool takes a specific taxpayer's country and income type and returns the applicable treaty benefits with Form 8833 disclosure guidance.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
The US has income tax treaties with 68 countries, each with different provisions for different income types (employment income, dividends, interest, royalties, capital gains, pensions). When an expat earns income in a treaty country, the preparer must identify whether a treaty benefit applies, determine the reduced tax rate or exemption, assess whether Form 8833 (Treaty-Based Return Position Disclosure) is required, and evaluate the interaction between treaty benefits and the FEIE. This requires reading the relevant treaty articles — often 40-60 pages of legal text — for each income type and each country involved.

## What Already Exists
RIA Checkpoint, CCH Intelliconnect, and the IRS Treaty Tables provide treaty text and summaries. These are research databases designed for reading and searching, not for structured lookup based on a specific taxpayer's situation. The IRS publishes reduced withholding rate tables by country, but these do not cover all income types or explain when Form 8833 is required.

## The Customization Gap
A taxpayer-specific treaty lookup must: (1) identify the relevant treaty by country; (2) locate the article applicable to the specific income type; (3) determine the benefit (reduced rate, exemption, credit method); (4) assess Form 8833 disclosure requirements; (5) flag interactions with FEIE (some treaty benefits cannot be claimed alongside the FEIE); (6) handle dual-treaty situations (a taxpayer who earned income in two treaty countries). The output must be structured — rate, article reference, 8833 requirement, and interaction notes — not a page reference to a 50-page treaty.

## Target Customer
Preparers handling expat returns involving treaty-country income who spend 15-30 minutes per return on treaty research that follows the same lookup pattern for every client.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-return treaty research time from 15-30 minutes to under 3 minutes. Prevents missed treaty benefits (estimated 10-15% of eligible expat returns do not claim available treaty provisions) and missed Form 8833 disclosures (which can result in penalties and loss of treaty benefits).
