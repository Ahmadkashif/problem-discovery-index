# Quality Inspection Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~1.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Quality inspection in cleaning is overwhelmingly manual walkthroughs with paper checklists or basic photo documentation.
**Target Buyer:** Quality assurance managers at large cleaning firms (50+ employees) and facility managers who oversee cleaning contractors.
**Automation Potential:** High — Cleanliness assessment follows visual patterns that are highly amenable to computer vision automation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Quality inspection is the accountability layer of cleaning — it determines whether the work was done to standard. Currently, this means a supervisor walks through the facility after the crew leaves, checks surfaces, floors, and restrooms against a checklist, and documents deficiencies. This process is expensive (supervisor time), subjective (one inspector's "clean" is another's "needs rework"), inconsistent (inspections happen sporadically, not every shift), and adversarial (crew disputes inspector findings). The shift toward computer-vision-based cleanliness verification represents a fundamental change in how quality is measured and enforced.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CleanTelligent and OrangeQC offer digital inspection checklists with photo capture. These digitize the form but don't change the process — a human still walks through and makes subjective judgments. No platform uses computer vision to assess cleanliness from images, detect missed areas, or quantify cleaning quality objectively.
