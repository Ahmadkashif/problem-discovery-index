# Post-Construction Cleanup

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most operators use paper checklists, phone-based scheduling, and handshake agreements with GCs. No dominant vertical software exists.
**Target Buyer:** Post-construction cleaning company owners working with general contractors and property developers.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Job scoping is complex and variable, but checklist workflows and GC communication patterns are standardizable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Post-construction cleaning is project-based, not recurring. Each job is unique: a gut-renovated brownstone requires different work than a new-build apartment complex. The work happens in tight windows between construction completion and certificate of occupancy (or tenant move-in), often under pressure from the GC who's already behind schedule. Crews deal with construction dust infiltrating HVAC systems, paint overspray on windows, adhesive residue on floors, and drywall dust in every crevice. The skill gap between "residential clean" and "post-construction clean" is enormous — sending a regular maid crew to a construction site results in re-work.

## Current Tools & Gaps
There is no dominant software platform for post-construction cleaning. Operators use generic tools (Google Calendar, WhatsApp group chats, paper checklists) to coordinate crews. Estimating and quoting is done by site walkthrough with pen and paper. The gap is total — no vertical solution addresses the project-based, GC-coordinated, multi-phase nature of this work.
