# Franchise Operations Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Franchisors provide some centralized tech (booking sites, brand portals), but franchisees still manage day-to-day operations with disconnected local tools.
**Target Buyer:** Multi-unit cleaning franchise owners (area developers) operating 3-10 franchise territories.
**Automation Potential:** High — Franchises run identical processes across locations, making standardization and automation highly leverageable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cleaning franchises (Molly Maid, MaidPro, Jan-Pro, ServiceMaster) operate under brand standards but are locally owned. Multi-unit owners manage multiple territories with separate P&Ls, crews, and customer bases — but must report to the franchisor on KPIs, royalties, and brand compliance. The operational challenge is unique: maintaining brand consistency across locations while optimizing each territory's staffing, routing, and profitability independently. Franchisees pay 5-8% royalty on gross revenue plus marketing fund contributions, making margin optimization critical.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Franchisors provide basic tech: centralized booking websites, brand asset portals, and sometimes proprietary CRMs. But multi-unit operators need cross-territory analytics that the franchisor's tools don't offer — comparative performance dashboards, labor allocation across territories, and consolidated financial reporting. They cobble together franchisor tools with Jobber, QuickBooks, and spreadsheets.
