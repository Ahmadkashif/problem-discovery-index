# Franchise-Aware Accounting & Royalty Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/cleaning-companies/franchise-operations/profile|Franchise Operations Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks handles general accounting but doesn't model franchise-specific costs — royalties, marketing fund contributions, and territory-specific fee structures.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
Franchise owners owe royalties (5-8% of gross revenue), marketing fund contributions (1-3%), and sometimes technology fees to the franchisor. These are calculated on gross revenue, not net — so they must be tracked separately from standard P&L. Additionally, each territory may have different fee structures based on contract vintage. Franchisees manually calculate royalties monthly, often discovering discrepancies with the franchisor's calculations that take hours to reconcile.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks, Xero, and FreshBooks handle standard small business accounting. Franchise-specific platforms like FranConnect focus on the franchisor side (managing many franchisees), not the franchisee side. No tool automatically calculates royalty obligations from revenue data, tracks them against franchisor invoices, or models net-of-royalty profitability per territory.

## The Customization Gap
The accounting system needs franchise fee awareness: automatic royalty calculation from revenue, marketing fund accrual, technology fee tracking, and reconciliation against franchisor statements. It should model "true profitability" after all franchise costs, compare royalty burden across territories, and alert on discrepancies between calculated and billed royalties.

## Target Customer
Multi-unit franchise owners spending 4-6 hours monthly on royalty calculations and reconciliation across territories.

## Impact If Solved
Saves 4-6 hours monthly in manual royalty accounting and catches billing discrepancies that average $500-$2,000 per territory per year in overpayments.
