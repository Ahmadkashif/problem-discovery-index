**Target Buyer:** Cleaning company owners specializing in medical/dental office contracts requiring infection control compliance.
**Automation Potential:** High — Regulatory compliance documentation, disinfection protocols, and audit preparation follow strict rule-based workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Medical facility cleaning is governed by infection control regulations (OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens, CDC guidelines, state health department requirements) that don't apply to commercial or residential cleaning. Technicians must follow specific disinfection protocols (contact time, EPA-registered disinfectants, terminal cleaning procedures), handle biohazardous waste properly, and maintain documentation proving compliance. The clients — healthcare practices — face regulatory liability if their cleaning provider cuts corners. This creates both a barrier to entry (certification and training requirements) and a retention advantage (practices don't switch cleaners casually).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Large hospital systems use enterprise EVS (Environmental Services) platforms like Clorox Healthcare's tools. But the thousands of outpatient clinics, dental offices, and urgent care centers served by small contract cleaning companies have no purpose-built software. These operators track compliance on paper logs, manage disinfectant contact times by memory, and prepare for health department inspections with binder-based documentation.
