# Cleaner-Client Matching Roulette

**Niche:** [[niches/cleaning-companies/residential-recurring/profile|Residential Recurring Services]]
**Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Sending the wrong cleaner to a home — wrong skill set, personality mismatch, or unaware of pet allergies — drives cancellations and complaints.
**Tags:** #recommendation #tabular-ml #worker-facing

## The Problem
Residential cleaning is an intimate service — strangers enter someone's home for hours. Clients develop strong preferences: they want consistency (same cleaner every visit), specific skills (eco-products only, deep-clean capability), and compatibility (some clients want chatty cleaners, others want invisible ones). When the regular cleaner is unavailable, dispatchers assign substitutes based on who's free, not who fits. The result: complaints, skipped cleanings, and cancellations.

## Why It's Still Broken
Matching preferences live in the dispatcher's head or in scattered notes. There's no structured profile system that captures client preferences (cleaning style, product preferences, pet situation, communication style) alongside cleaner capabilities and personality traits. When the dispatcher quits — and in this industry, everyone quits — institutional knowledge walks out the door.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A structured matching system that maintains client preference profiles (auto-populated from post-cleaning surveys and complaint history) and cleaner capability profiles (certifications, equipment, pet comfort, language, work style). When a substitute is needed, the system recommends the best-fit available cleaner and flags any potential issues (e.g., "this client has cats, substitute is allergic"). Over time, post-cleaning ratings refine the matching algorithm.

## Who Feels the Pain
Dispatchers scrambling to fill 5-10 substitute assignments per week, and clients who get a revolving door of unfamiliar cleaners.

## Impact If Fixed
Companies that maintain cleaner-client consistency see 25-35% lower churn. Even partial matching improvement (from random to 70% preference-aligned substitutes) can reduce cancellation-driven churn by 8-12%.
