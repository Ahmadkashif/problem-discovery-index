# Residential Recurring Services

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~17%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Online booking and payment are common, but route optimization, customer retention analytics, and dynamic pricing remain underdeveloped.
**Target Buyer:** Maid service owners and residential cleaning franchise operators managing 10-50 cleaners.
**Automation Potential:** High — Recurring schedules, route planning, and customer communications follow predictable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential recurring cleaning (weekly/biweekly home cleaning) operates on fundamentally different economics than commercial janitorial. Revenue comes from hundreds of individual households rather than a handful of large contracts. Customer acquisition cost is high ($80-$150 per customer), churn is brutal (30-50% annually), and the business lives or dies on route density — a cleaner driving 30 minutes between homes destroys margins. The workforce is largely part-time, turnover exceeds 100% annually in many markets, and matching cleaners to homes based on pet allergies, cleaning preferences, and personality fit is a constant puzzle.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Platforms like Launch27 (now ZenMaid), Housecall Pro, and Bookingkoala handle online booking and basic scheduling. None solve the core strategic problems: predicting which customers will churn, optimizing routes to maximize daily job density, or dynamically pricing based on home size, clutter level, and travel time.
