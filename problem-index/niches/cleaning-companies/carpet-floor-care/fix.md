# Damage Liability Without Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/cleaning-companies/carpet-floor-care/profile|Carpet & Floor Care Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Without structured before/after documentation, floor care companies pay damage claims for pre-existing conditions they didn't cause.
**Tags:** #computer-vision #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
Homeowners and property managers frequently blame floor care technicians for pre-existing damage — carpet delamination, stone cracks, discoloration from sun exposure. Without timestamped, structured documentation of floor condition before work begins, the cleaning company has no defense. Insurance claims and small-claims lawsuits cost $1,000-$10,000 per incident, and operators pay 2-3 of these per year even when they're not at fault.

## Why It's Still Broken
Technicians take occasional smartphone photos but don't follow a structured protocol — they miss areas, don't document close-ups of existing damage, and store photos in their personal camera rolls where they're impossible to retrieve for a claim filed 60 days later. There's no system that enforces a documentation checklist, timestamps photos to the job, and stores them in a searchable, client-linked archive.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A mobile documentation workflow triggered at job start: the app prompts the technician to photograph each room and close-up any existing damage (stains, tears, cracks, discoloration). Photos are GPS-tagged, timestamped, and linked to the job record. After service, the same rooms are re-photographed. The before/after pairs are stored in a cloud archive accessible by job ID or client name. When a damage claim arrives, the operator pulls the pre-service documentation in seconds.

## Who Feels the Pain
Floor care company owners paying $3,000-$15,000 annually in damage claims for conditions they didn't cause, and technicians who get blamed for pre-existing damage.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates 60-80% of fraudulent or mistaken damage claims, saving $2,000-$12,000 per year and reducing insurance premiums over time.
