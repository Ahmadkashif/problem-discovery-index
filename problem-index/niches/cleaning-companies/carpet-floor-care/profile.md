# Carpet & Floor Care Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Online booking exists for consumer-facing services, but job costing, chemical selection tracking, and floor condition documentation are almost entirely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Carpet and hard-floor cleaning business owners with 3-15 technicians.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Chemical selection and dwell-time protocols are rule-based and automatable; floor condition assessment has computer vision potential.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Carpet and floor care is a specialty trade within cleaning — technicians need training on fiber types, chemical compatibility, extraction equipment, and stain treatment protocols. A wrong chemical on a wool carpet or an overly aggressive scrub on marble can cause thousands of dollars in damage. The work is split between residential one-off jobs (move-out carpet cleans, hardwood refinishing) and commercial maintenance contracts (quarterly carpet extraction, monthly floor buffing). Pricing is complex — it depends on fiber type, soil level, square footage, furniture moving, and stain treatment add-ons.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Scheduling tools like Jobber and Housecall Pro handle booking. No platform helps technicians identify floor/fiber types, select appropriate chemicals, or document pre-existing conditions to protect against damage claims. Floor condition documentation is done with smartphone photos and no structured system.
