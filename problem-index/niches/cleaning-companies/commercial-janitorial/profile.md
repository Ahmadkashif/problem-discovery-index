# Commercial Janitorial Contracts

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cleaning-companies|Cleaning Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $65B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~55%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most firms use basic scheduling software but contract bidding, scope changes, and labor allocation are still spreadsheet-driven.
**Target Buyer:** Janitorial company owners with 20+ employees managing multiple commercial building contracts.
**Automation Potential:** High — Repetitive bid templates, labor hour calculations, and inspection checklists are highly standardizable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial janitorial is the backbone of the cleaning industry, dominated by multi-year contracts with office buildings, schools, and government facilities. Unlike residential cleaning, the sales cycle is long (3-6 months), contracts are won on detailed scope-of-work proposals, and profitability hinges on accurate square-footage-to-labor-hour calculations. Operators manage rotating night shifts across multiple buildings, each with different access protocols, security requirements, and client expectations. The niche has distinct pain points around contract profitability tracking — most owners don't know which buildings are profitable until year-end.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Janitorial-specific platforms like CleanTelligent and Swept handle inspection and time tracking, but none integrate bidding, labor scheduling, and profitability analysis into a single workflow. Most operators cobble together generic CRMs (Jobber, ServiceTitan) with spreadsheets for bid calculations, losing visibility into per-contract margins.
