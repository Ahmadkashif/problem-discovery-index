# Math Specialty Franchises

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tutoring-centers|Tutoring Centers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Franchise systems (Kumon, Mathnasium, Russian School of Mathematics) provide proprietary curriculum platforms, but student progress analytics beyond worksheet completion rates are limited; independent math tutoring centers use generic tools with no math-specific assessment integration
**Target Buyer:** Franchise owner or center director at a math specialty tutoring center
**Automation Potential:** High — Math tutoring follows sequential skill progressions with clear prerequisite dependencies, making diagnostic assessment, curriculum sequencing, and progress measurement highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Math specialty centers (Kumon with 1,500+ US locations, Mathnasium with 1,000+ locations, plus hundreds of independents) dominate tutoring center enrollment. Math is the most common reason parents seek tutoring, and math skills follow strict prerequisite chains (addition before multiplication, fractions before algebra, algebra before calculus) that make skill gap diagnosis and curriculum sequencing more structured than in reading or writing. The franchise model creates a distinctive technology constraint: franchisees must use corporate curriculum systems but often need supplementary tools for student analytics, parent communication, and operational management that the franchise platform doesn't provide.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Kumon uses a proprietary worksheet-based system with daily completion tracking. Mathnasium uses its Mathnasium@home digital platform alongside in-center instruction. Russian School of Mathematics uses a custom curriculum with periodic assessments. All franchise systems track curriculum position (which worksheet/lesson the student is on) but provide limited analytics on skill-level mastery, learning velocity, or diagnostic root cause for persistent struggles. Independent math centers use generic tutoring platforms (TutorCruncher, Teachworks) with no math-specific features.
