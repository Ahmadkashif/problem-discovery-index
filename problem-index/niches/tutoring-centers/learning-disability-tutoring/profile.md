# Learning Disability & Special Needs Tutoring

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tutoring-centers|Tutoring Centers]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5-2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~14%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — IEP goal tracking is paper-based or managed in school-specific systems that centers can't access; evidence-based intervention protocols (Orton-Gillingham, Wilson Reading) are delivered with paper materials; progress monitoring uses manual data collection on paper probe sheets
**Target Buyer:** Center director or lead specialist at a center specializing in learning disabilities (dyslexia, dyscalculia, ADHD-related learning challenges)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Progress monitoring and IEP goal reporting follow structured protocols, but intervention delivery requires human expertise and adaptation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Learning disability tutoring centers serve students with diagnosed conditions (dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, ADHD) who need specialized instructional approaches. These centers operate differently from general tutoring: they use evidence-based intervention programs (Orton-Gillingham for dyslexia, Lindamood-Bell, Wilson Reading System) that require specific training and certification; they must document progress toward IEP goals for school district reporting; and they serve a population where assessment sensitivity is critical — measuring skill gains in increments too small for standard grade-level tests to detect. Parents of learning-disabled students are the most demanding audience in tutoring — they're often spending $5,000-$15,000 annually on private intervention and need documented evidence for insurance reimbursement and school advocacy.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Intervention programs (Orton-Gillingham, Wilson) provide paper-based lesson plans and progress monitoring sheets. IEP management systems (IEP Pro, Goalbook) are used by schools, not external tutoring centers. Standardized assessment tools (DIBELS, AIMSweb) provide progress monitoring probes but results are recorded on paper. No platform integrates intervention delivery, progress monitoring, and IEP goal reporting for private tutoring centers serving learning-disabled students.
