# IEP Goal Alignment and Reporting for Private Tutoring

**Niche:** [[niches/tutoring-centers/learning-disability-tutoring/profile|Learning Disability & Special Needs Tutoring]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tutoring-centers|Tutoring Centers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** School IEP management systems track goals and accommodations internally but are invisible to private tutoring providers — the people parents hire specifically to help meet those goals.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
A student's IEP specifies measurable goals: "By June, student will read grade-level passages at 80 WPM with 95% accuracy" or "student will solve two-step word problems with 70% accuracy." Parents hire a private tutor specifically to help meet these goals, but the tutor often doesn't have the full IEP document (parents forget to bring it, or the school shares a summary), doesn't track progress against the specific IEP metrics (tracking their own curriculum position instead), and can't produce reports in the format the IEP team expects at quarterly review meetings. The tutoring center's work exists in a parallel universe from the school's IEP — both targeting the same student but neither coordinating data.

## What Already Exists
School-facing IEP management platforms (IEP Pro, Goalbook, Frontline) manage IEP documentation, goal tracking, and compliance for school districts. Progress monitoring tools (DIBELS, AIMSweb, FastBridge) provide standardized assessment probes. Tutoring management platforms (TutorCruncher, Teachworks) track sessions and billing. None of these bridge the gap between the school's IEP system and the private tutoring center's instruction.

## The Customization Gap
Private tutoring centers need a tool that: (1) accepts IEP goals (manually entered from the IEP document parents provide) and maps them to specific measurable metrics; (2) tracks tutoring session activities against those IEP goals — linking each session's work to the specific goal it addresses; (3) produces IEP-team-ready progress reports showing the student's trajectory toward each goal, with data from tutoring sessions as evidence; (4) aligns with common IEP goal structures (by annual review period, with quarterly benchmark checkpoints). The tool must understand IEP goal formatting and measurement conventions without requiring school system integration.

## Target Customer
Center directors and lead specialists at LD tutoring centers where 60-80% of students have IEPs and parents expect tutoring to directly support IEP goal attainment — typically spending $8,000-$15,000 annually on private intervention.

## Impact If Solved
Positions the tutoring center as an IEP team partner rather than an independent service — strengthening the center's value proposition to parents who attend 2-4 IEP meetings per year. Provides documented evidence of private tutoring effectiveness that parents can present to schools. Reduces tutor preparation time for IEP meetings from 2-3 hours to 30 minutes by auto-generating goal-aligned progress reports.
