# Test Prep Centers (SAT/ACT/GRE)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/tutoring-centers|Tutoring Centers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Digital practice platforms (Khan Academy, UWorld, Magoosh) are widely used alongside in-person instruction; but student-specific performance analytics and adaptive curriculum selection are manual; score prediction and progress tracking rely on practice test scores rather than granular skill-level modeling
**Target Buyer:** Center owner or franchise director (Kaplan, Princeton Review, independent test prep centers)
**Automation Potential:** High — Test prep follows structured content domains with standardized assessments, making skill gap identification and curriculum sequencing highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Test prep is the most data-rich segment of tutoring — every practice test produces granular performance data across specific question types, content domains, and difficulty levels. Yet most centers use this data superficially: "you scored 620 on math, you need to improve." An experienced test prep tutor reads deeper patterns: this student loses points on geometry questions but only when they appear late in the section (fatigue/time management issue), or this student consistently misses inference questions in reading but not detail questions (reasoning gap, not comprehension gap). The specificity of test prep creates the clearest ML opportunity in tutoring — structured input data (question-level responses), defined output target (score), and abundant historical data from thousands of students.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Practice platforms (Khan Academy Official SAT Prep, UWorld, Magoosh) provide practice questions and basic analytics. Score reporting tools (College Board, ACT Inc.) provide test-level breakdowns. Tutoring management platforms (TutorCruncher, Teachworks) handle scheduling and billing. No tool connects practice performance data to specific skill gaps with adaptive curriculum recommendations — tutors manually analyze practice test results and decide what to focus on next. Score prediction is based on practice test scores (noisy, infrequent measurement) rather than skill-level modeling (precise, continuously updated).
