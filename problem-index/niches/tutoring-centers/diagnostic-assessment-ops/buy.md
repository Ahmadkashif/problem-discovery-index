# Digital Diagnostic Probe Library by Subject

**Niche:** [[niches/tutoring-centers/diagnostic-assessment-ops/profile|Diagnostic Assessment Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/tutoring-centers|Tutoring Centers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Assessment item banks exist for standardized testing but not for diagnostic tutoring — tutors need brief, targeted probes that test specific prerequisite skills, not comprehensive assessments that take 45 minutes.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
When a tutor suspects a student has a fraction gap causing algebra difficulties, they need a quick diagnostic: 5-8 targeted problems testing fraction operations at increasing complexity, designed to pinpoint exactly where the student's understanding breaks down (equivalent fractions? Adding unlike denominators? Multiplying? Dividing?). No such targeted probe exists in a ready-to-use digital format. Tutors either design problems on the fly (inconsistent quality), photocopy from textbooks (not diagnostic in structure), or use standardized assessments (too long, too broad). The lack of targeted diagnostic probes forces tutors to spend 15-20 minutes of a 60-minute session on informal assessment instead of instruction.

## What Already Exists
Item banks (Illuminate, Edulastic) provide thousands of assessment items for teacher-created tests. Adaptive assessment platforms (iReady, MAP) provide comprehensive placement assessments. Curriculum-based measurement tools (DIBELS, AIMSweb) provide standardized progress monitoring probes. None of these provide brief, targeted diagnostic probes structured to isolate specific prerequisite gaps — the format tutors need during a session.

## The Customization Gap
Diagnostic probes for tutoring need to: (1) be brief (5-8 items, completable in 3-5 minutes); (2) test a single skill at progressive difficulty to pinpoint the exact breakdown point; (3) be organized by prerequisite skill chain (not by grade level or topic — by the specific gap they diagnose); (4) produce an immediate, interpretable result ("student breaks down at adding fractions with unlike denominators — equivalent fraction concepts are solid"); (5) be available as a searchable library where the tutor selects the suspected gap area and gets the appropriate probe. The organization principle is diagnostic (what gap am I testing for?) not curricular (what grade/topic?).

## Target Customer
Academic directors at tutoring centers who want consistent, structured diagnostic tools available to all tutors rather than relying on each tutor's ability to improvise diagnostic questions during sessions.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces in-session diagnostic time from 15-20 minutes to 5-7 minutes through pre-structured, targeted probes. Standardizes diagnostic quality across all tutors regardless of experience. Produces structured diagnostic data that aggregates into student skill profiles — impossible with improvised assessment questions.
