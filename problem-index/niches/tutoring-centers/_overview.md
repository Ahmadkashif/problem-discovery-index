## Why These Niches

Tutoring centers fragment along student population (college-bound test preppers vs. struggling learners vs. special needs vs. homeschool), business model (franchise vs. independent, affluent suburbs vs. Title I provider), and operational function (instruction vs. assessment vs. enrollment). These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (test prep and math specialty franchises), the two most digitally neglected service types (special needs tutoring with IEP-aligned documentation requirements, and homeschool enrichment centers operating outside standard ed-tech ecosystems), the two most underserved populations (low-income students in high-dosage tutoring programs funded by ESSER/Title I, and ESL families needing language-integrated academic support), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (diagnostic assessment — the core tacit knowledge bottleneck — and scheduling/enrollment operations). Excluded: online-only tutoring platforms (different delivery model), corporate/adult tutoring (different buyer), and private tutor marketplaces (marketplace business, not centers).
