# Commercial Property Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Guard tour systems and basic reporting exist, but incident analytics, client-facing dashboards, and post-staffing optimization are underdeveloped.
**Target Buyer:** Contract security companies serving office buildings, retail centers, and industrial properties with dedicated guard posts.
**Automation Potential:** High — Guard tour verification, daily activity reporting, and post-order management follow standardizable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial property security is the bread-and-butter of contract guard firms — long-term contracts (1-3 years) providing guards at office buildings, shopping centers, warehouses, and corporate campuses. Revenue is predictable but margins are thin (5-12%) because labor is the dominant cost (85-90% of contract value). The competitive dynamic is brutal: clients issue RFPs every contract cycle, and the incumbent often loses to a firm bidding 3-5% lower. Differentiation comes from reliability (guards actually show up), reporting quality (clients want to see what happened on their property), and incident response capability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Guard tour systems (TrackTik, Silvertrac, GuardsPro) verify that guards complete patrol checkpoints. Daily activity reports (DARs) are generated manually by guards. Client-facing portals exist in premium platforms but are underused. No platform helps security companies demonstrate measurable value — connecting guard activity to incident reduction, response times, or property-level risk trends.
