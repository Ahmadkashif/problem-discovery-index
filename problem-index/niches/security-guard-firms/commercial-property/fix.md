# Post Order Drift

**Niche:** [[niches/security-guard-firms/commercial-property/profile|Commercial Property Security]]
**Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Post orders define exactly what guards should do at each site — but they're written once, filed in a binder, and gradually diverge from actual practice as conditions change.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Post orders are the operating manual for each security site: patrol routes, checkpoint frequencies, access control procedures, emergency protocols, vendor escort rules, and client-specific instructions. They're created during contract setup, printed, and placed in a binder at the guard desk. Over months, conditions change: the client adds a new entrance, parking garage hours change, a new tenant moves in with different access requirements. Post order updates happen through verbal instructions, emails, or sticky notes on the binder. Within 6 months, the written post orders bear little resemblance to actual procedures. New guards assigned to the site follow outdated written orders, creating security gaps and client complaints.

## Why It's Still Broken
Post orders are documents, not workflows. They live in Word files or PDF binders with no version control, no distribution tracking, and no acknowledgment system. When operations managers update post orders, there's no way to verify that every guard assigned to that site has read and understood the changes. Guard turnover is 100-300% annually — by the time post orders are updated, half the guards who need them are new hires who've never seen the original version.

## What a Fix Looks Like
Digital post orders accessible on the guard's mobile device, version-controlled with change tracking. When post orders are updated, all guards assigned to that site receive a notification with the specific changes highlighted and must acknowledge reading them before their next shift. Post order content integrates with the guard tour system — patrol checkpoints, frequencies, and procedures displayed contextually during the patrol rather than memorized from a binder. Audit trail shows which version each guard was operating under during any given shift.

## Who Feels the Pain
Operations managers responsible for post order compliance across 20+ sites who discover during client audits that guards are following procedures from 6 months ago, and new guards who arrive at unfamiliar sites with outdated or missing post orders.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces client complaints from post order non-compliance by 50-70%, cuts new guard site orientation time from 4 hours to 1 hour, and provides audit-ready documentation proving procedural compliance at every site.
