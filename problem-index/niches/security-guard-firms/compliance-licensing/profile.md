## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Guard licensing, training certifications, and company registrations are tracked in spreadsheets or filing cabinets. State regulatory portals are fragmented and not API-enabled.
**Target Buyer:** Security company owners and HR managers responsible for maintaining guard licensing compliance across multiple states.
**Automation Potential:** High — License tracking, renewal scheduling, training requirement mapping, and compliance documentation are entirely rule-based and automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every security guard in the United States must hold a state-issued guard card or license, with requirements varying dramatically by state: California requires 40 hours of training and a BSIS-issued guard card; Texas requires a Level II or III license from DPS; Florida requires a Class D license from the Division of Licensing. Armed guards need additional permits, firearms qualifications, and re-qualification at mandated intervals. Companies operating across states must track different requirements for each jurisdiction. A guard working an event in a neighboring state may need a reciprocal license. Deploying an unlicensed guard is a criminal offense in most states.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Security company HR departments track guard licenses in Excel spreadsheets, Google Sheets, or basic HR systems (BambooHR, Gusto). These tools track expiration dates but don't know state-specific requirements, don't alert when a guard is scheduled at a site requiring credentials they don't hold, and don't integrate with state licensing portals. Training management is similarly manual — certificates are scanned and filed with no systematic tracking of what training is required vs. completed.
