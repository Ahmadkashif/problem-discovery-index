# Training Management Adapted for Security Requirements

**Niche:** [[niches/security-guard-firms/compliance-licensing/profile|Compliance & Licensing Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic LMS platforms deliver training content but don't map security-specific training requirements to state licensing mandates, site-specific needs, and guard qualifications.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
Security guards require multiple training categories: state-mandated guard card training (hours and topics vary by state), annual refresher courses (CPR/AED, bloodborne pathogens, use of force), site-specific orientation (post orders, emergency procedures, client protocols), and qualification-specific training (firearms re-qualification every 6-12 months for armed guards). Tracking which guard needs which training, when, is a manual nightmare. Guards get deployed to new sites without completing site orientation. Armed guards work past their firearms qualification expiration. Annual refreshers lapse because nobody tracks the anniversary dates.

## What Already Exists
Learning management systems (TalentLMS, Trainual, Lessonly) deliver online training and track completion. Security-specific training providers (Security Officer Network, Guard Training HQ) offer state-approved courses. Neither system maps the full training requirement matrix: state licensing requirements → guard type (unarmed/armed/supervisor) → site-specific requirements → individual guard completion status → scheduled deployment compatibility.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs a training requirements engine that auto-generates each guard's training plan based on their state(s) of operation, guard type, and assigned sites. It should track completion with expiration awareness (CPR cert expires in 2 years, firearms qual in 1 year, state refresher annually), block scheduling when required training is incomplete, and provide guards with a mobile view of their upcoming training requirements and deadlines.

## Target Customer
Security companies with 30+ guards spending 5-10 hours per week managing training compliance and occasionally discovering gaps during client or regulatory audits.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces training compliance management from 8 hours/week to 1 hour/week, eliminates deployment of under-trained guards (which violates contracts and creates liability), and provides audit-ready training records for state regulatory inspections and client reviews.
