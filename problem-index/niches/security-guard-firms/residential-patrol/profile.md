# Residential Community Patrol

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most residential patrol operations use paper log books, basic GPS trackers, and phone calls for reporting. HOA clients receive hand-delivered paper reports.
**Target Buyer:** Guard firms serving HOAs, gated communities, apartment complexes, and residential property management companies.
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — Patrol verification, resident communication, and incident reporting follow repeatable patterns but require community-specific customization.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential patrol security differs from commercial security in its customer relationship: the "client" is a community of residents, each with different expectations, and the HOA board that makes purchasing decisions is a committee of non-professionals. Guards interact with residents (checking guest access, enforcing community rules, responding to noise complaints) rather than managing empty office buildings. The work is vehicle patrol (not standing post), covering large geographic areas with relatively few guards. Resident satisfaction drives contract renewals, but residents' expectations range from "visible deterrence" to "personal concierge."

## Current Tools & Gaps
Basic GPS tracking (Linxup, Vyncs) shows vehicle location. Security guard tour systems (TrackTik) are designed for walking patrols, not vehicle routes covering 200+ home communities. No platform addresses the resident-facing needs: community incident notifications, guest pre-authorization, parking violation documentation, or HOA board reporting dashboards.
