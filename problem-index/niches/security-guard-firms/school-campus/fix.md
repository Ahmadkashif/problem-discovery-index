# Custody Alert Communication Failure

**Niche:** [[niches/security-guard-firms/school-campus/profile|School & Campus Security]]
**Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Custody restrictions — which parents or guardians are NOT authorized to pick up a child — are stored in the school office and often don't reach the security guard at the car line or front door.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
Custody disputes are one of the highest-risk situations in school security. A non-custodial parent arrives to pick up a child, and the guard must know whether that person is authorized. Custody restrictions are documented in the school's Student Information System (SIS) and maintained by the front office. The security guard — who may be stationed at the car pickup line, front entrance, or playground — often has no access to this information. Communication happens via paper lists updated weekly (or less), radio calls to the front office (which may not be staffed during dismissal rush), or the guard's memory of a photo printout reviewed during morning briefing.

## Why It's Still Broken
Student information systems are FERPA-protected — schools are reluctant to give security guards (especially contract employees) access to student records. The custody restriction is a sensitive legal document that changes when court orders are modified. Sharing custody alerts requires a system that provides the guard with just enough information (photo of restricted individual, student name) without exposing the full student record. No current system provides this narrow, permission-controlled alert capability.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A FERPA-compliant custody alert system that pushes restricted-individual alerts (photo, name, restriction type — no other student/family information) to the security guard's mobile device. When custody restrictions are updated in the SIS, the alert automatically updates in real time. During dismissal, the guard can quickly search by student name or scan a pickup code to verify authorization. The system logs every access check for legal documentation.

## Who Feels the Pain
Security guards making custody authorization decisions with outdated or missing information, school administrators liable for custody release errors, and children at risk when a restricted individual gains unauthorized access.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates the primary custody-related security gap at schools, provides real-time custody verification that protects children, and shields the school and security firm from catastrophic liability of releasing a child to a restricted individual.
