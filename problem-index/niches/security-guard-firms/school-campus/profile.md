# School & Campus Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Schools have visitor management systems and cameras, but security guard operations specific to educational environments (student interaction protocols, parent communication, lockdown drills) are minimally digitized.
**Target Buyer:** Guard firms serving K-12 schools, school districts, and university campuses with specialized student safety requirements.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Visitor screening, lockdown drill documentation, and parent notification workflows are automatable; the relationship-based aspects of school security require human judgment.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
School security guards operate in uniquely sensitive environments — they interact daily with minors, must balance security posture with welcoming atmosphere, and face intense public scrutiny. Guards need background checks beyond standard security licensing (FBI fingerprinting, sex offender registry), must understand FERPA student privacy requirements, and follow school-specific behavior management protocols. The threat model includes active shooter scenarios, student behavioral crises, custody disputes, and unauthorized campus access. Community expectations are emotional and politically charged — every decision is scrutinized by parents, school boards, and media.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Visitor management systems (Raptor, LobbyGuard) screen visitors against sex offender databases. School cameras and access control are managed by the school's IT department. Emergency notification systems (SchoolMessenger, ParentSquare) handle mass communication. No platform integrates guard operations with these school-specific systems: student behavioral incident documentation, lockdown drill performance tracking, custody alert management, or after-school event security coordination.
