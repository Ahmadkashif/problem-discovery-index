# Construction Site Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most construction site security operates on paper sign-in logs, radio check-ins, and minimal technology beyond a flashlight and phone.
**Target Buyer:** Guard firms providing overnight, weekend, and after-hours security for active construction sites.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Access logging, equipment theft reporting, and patrol verification are automatable; the deterrence and response function requires physical presence.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Construction site security protects high-value, unsecured environments — active job sites with incomplete fencing, no permanent access control, and millions of dollars in materials, tools, and heavy equipment sitting in the open. Theft is rampant: copper wire, power tools, heavy equipment, and building materials are high-value, easily resold targets. The guard works alone on a dark site with limited communication, managing contractor access during early morning hours and deterring theft overnight. Unlike commercial security where the building is locked and alarmed, construction sites are inherently open and vulnerable.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Basic security guard platforms handle scheduling and time tracking. Site-specific cameras and temporary alarm systems (MODS, Stealth Monitoring) provide electronic surveillance but don't integrate with guard operations. No platform combines physical guard patrol verification, site access control (managing which contractors are authorized at which hours), equipment/material inventory monitoring, and GC-facing reporting into a unified construction site security solution.
