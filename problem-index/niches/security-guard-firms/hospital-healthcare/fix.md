# De-escalation Outcome Tracking Gap

**Niche:** [[niches/security-guard-firms/hospital-healthcare/profile|Hospital & Healthcare Security]]
**Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Healthcare security invests heavily in de-escalation training but has no way to measure whether specific techniques actually reduce physical interventions in practice.
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Healthcare security teams spend $50,000-$200,000 annually on de-escalation training (CPI, Handle with Care, MOAB). These programs teach specific verbal and non-verbal techniques for managing agitated patients without physical intervention. But there's no outcome tracking: no one measures which techniques were attempted before a physical intervention, which techniques successfully de-escalated the situation, or whether training improvements correlate with reduced physical intervention rates. The security director can't prove the training investment works.

## Why It's Still Broken
De-escalation attempts happen in fast-moving, stressful situations — guards don't stop to document which techniques they used. Post-incident reports note "verbal de-escalation attempted, unsuccessful" without specifying which techniques were tried. There's no structured data collection framework that captures technique-level detail without burdening guards during a crisis. Training programs don't follow up on real-world application of their techniques.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A rapid post-incident debrief workflow (completed within 1 hour of the event) that captures: situation type (agitated patient, combative visitor, elopement), de-escalation techniques attempted (specific techniques from the training program — verbal reassurance, limit setting, active listening, environmental modification), duration of de-escalation attempt, and outcome (successful de-escalation, escalation to physical intervention, patient self-calmed). This data aggregates into technique effectiveness dashboards that inform training focus and demonstrate ROI to hospital administration.

## Who Feels the Pain
Security directors who can't justify $100,000+ annual de-escalation training budgets because they have no outcome data, and guards who receive training but get no feedback on whether their real-world application of techniques is effective.

## Impact If Fixed
Enables targeted training improvements (focus on techniques with lowest success rates), demonstrates training ROI to hospital administration (protecting the security contract), and identifies guards who need additional coaching — reducing overall physical intervention rates by 10-20%.
