# Hospital & Healthcare Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/security-guard-firms|Security Guard Firms]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Hospitals have badge access systems and cameras, but security guard operations (de-escalation tracking, patient safety checks, behavioral health holds) are managed separately with minimal integration.
**Target Buyer:** Security companies specializing in healthcare facilities with patient safety requirements, behavioral health units, and emergency department coverage.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Incident categorization, restraint documentation, and regulatory reporting follow strict protocols that are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Healthcare security guards face unique challenges: they interact with patients in crisis (psychiatric emergencies, substance abuse, combative patients), must comply with CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid) regulations on restraint use, and operate in HIPAA-protected environments where incident reports contain protected health information. The threat profile is distinct — workplace violence against healthcare workers is the fastest-growing category, and guards are often the first responders to patient aggression. Guards need de-escalation training beyond what commercial security requires, and incident documentation must meet Joint Commission and CMS standards.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Hospital badge access and camera systems are enterprise-grade (Lenel, CCURE, Genetec). Guard tour systems handle patrol verification. Incident reporting in healthcare settings often falls back to paper forms designed for nursing incident reports, not security-specific events. No platform integrates de-escalation intervention tracking, CMS-compliant restraint documentation, workplace violence trending, and guard-specific incident reporting in a healthcare context.
