# RV F&I Product Sales

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $7B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 16%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — F&I menus exist (IDS F&I, MaximTrak) but product selection, pricing, and presentation are heavily manual, and RV-specific products (extended service contracts covering coach systems, tire/wheel protection for towables) are poorly integrated.
**Target Buyer:** F&I manager at an RV dealership
**Automation Potential:** High — F&I product matching, pricing, and presentation follow structured rules based on vehicle type, buyer profile, and lender requirements — ideal for automation.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
RV F&I is significantly more complex than automotive F&I. The product menu includes: extended service contracts (with separate coverage for chassis and coach), GAP insurance (critical on depreciating $50K-$300K assets), tire and wheel protection (a $4,000-$8,000 concern on motorhomes with 6 tires at $400+ each), interior protection (relevant for units with $15K+ interiors), roadside assistance (must cover RV-specific towing at $500-$1,500 per incident), and campground membership programs. Each product has RV-specific pricing, coverage exclusions, and lender restrictions that differ from automotive equivalents. The F&I manager must present 6-10 products in a 45-60 minute session without overwhelming the buyer.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IDS F&I module and MaximTrak provide menu-based F&I presentation for RV dealers. But product matching (which products make sense for this specific unit and buyer) is done by the F&I manager's judgment, not by data. A first-time buyer purchasing a $28,000 travel trailer needs different products than a full-timer purchasing a $180,000 diesel pusher. The current tools present a static menu; they don't customize the presentation or prioritize products based on buyer profile, unit type, or historical attachment rates.
