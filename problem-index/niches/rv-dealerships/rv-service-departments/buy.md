# RV-Specific Digital Vehicle Inspection Templates

**Niche:** [[niches/rv-dealerships/rv-service-departments/profile|RV Service Departments]]
**Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** AutoVitals and Tekmetric provide excellent DVIs for cars, but they lack RV-specific inspection points — roof condition, slide-out operation, LP gas system, leveling jacks, and residential electrical.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
An RV service inspection covers 60-80 points across both automotive and residential systems: engine, transmission, brakes (automotive) PLUS roof membrane condition, slide-out alignment and seal integrity, LP gas pressure and leak testing, 12V and 120V electrical systems, fresh/grey/black water tank condition, awning operation, leveling system function, and appliance operation (residential). Auto-focused DVI tools cover the first 30 points but miss the next 30-50 RV-specific points entirely. Technicians inspect these RV systems but document them on paper checklists that don't get sent to customers, missing $2,000-$5,000 per inspection in recommended service.

## What Already Exists
AutoVitals and Tekmetric provide photo-rich DVIs with color-coded severity and digital delivery to customers — but their inspection templates are designed for passenger cars. IDS Service module handles basic RV work orders but has no DVI capability. No RV-specific DVI tool exists that covers both automotive and residential systems with photo documentation and digital customer delivery.

## The Customization Gap
RV-specific DVI templates that add 30-50 inspection points covering: roof membrane (photos of seams, sealant, skylights), slide-outs (operation video, seal condition, alignment measurement), LP gas (pressure test results, connection condition), water systems (pump operation, leak check, water heater condition, winterization status), electrical (battery health, converter/inverter function, shore power connections), and appliances (refrigerator cooling test, AC output temperature, furnace function). Each point includes photo/video capture and customer-facing explanations specific to RV systems.

## Target Customer
RV dealer service managers using automotive DVI tools (or paper checklists) who miss RV-specific recommended service worth $2,000-$5,000 per inspection.

## Impact If Solved
Increases per-inspection recommended service revenue by $2,000-$5,000 by documenting RV-specific issues with photos and clear explanations. For a service department doing 20 inspections per month, this represents $40K-$100K in annual recommended service revenue that was previously undocumented and unsold.
