# Dual-Warranty Claim Filing for Chassis and Coach Manufacturers

**Niche:** [[niches/rv-dealerships/rv-service-departments/profile|RV Service Departments]]
**Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool manages warranty claims for an RV that has two separate manufacturers (Ford for the chassis, Thor for the coach) with different portals, different claim formats, and different approval processes.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #compliance #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A Class C motorhome built on a Ford E-450 chassis has two separate warranty programs: Ford covers the engine, transmission, brakes, and chassis components; Thor (or Winnebago, Tiffin, etc.) covers the coach — the living area, appliances, slide-outs, electrical, plumbing, and body. When a customer brings in the RV with multiple issues (engine check light + leaking slide-out + AC not cooling), the service writer must file separate warranty claims through two completely different portals (Ford's OASIS system and Thor's dealer portal), with different documentation requirements, different labor time guides, and different parts ordering processes. A single RV visit can generate 4-6 separate warranty claims across two manufacturers, each taking 30-45 minutes to file.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The chassis warranty ecosystem (Ford, GM, Mercedes) is the automotive world — managed through auto dealer warranty portals. The coach warranty ecosystem is the RV world — managed through RV manufacturer portals. These two worlds have never been integrated because the chassis manufacturer and coach manufacturer are separate companies with separate systems. RV dealers sit at the intersection, dealing with both, but neither side has built tools for the RV dealer's dual-warranty reality.

## What to Build
A unified warranty claim management system that allows the service writer to document all warranty issues on a single work order, then automatically routes chassis-related items to the chassis manufacturer's portal and coach-related items to the coach manufacturer's portal, pre-filling each with the correct format, documentation, and claim codes. The system tracks approval status across both manufacturers in a single view, flags denied claims for appeal, and reconciles warranty payments from both sources.

## Target Customer
RV dealer service managers processing 30-80 warranty claims per month across 2-5 different chassis and 10-15 different coach manufacturers.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-claim filing time from 30-45 minutes to 10-15 minutes, recovering 15-30 hours per month of service writer time. Increases warranty claim approval rates by 15-20% through consistent, properly formatted documentation. Recovers $3,000-$8,000 per month in previously denied or unfiled warranty claims.
