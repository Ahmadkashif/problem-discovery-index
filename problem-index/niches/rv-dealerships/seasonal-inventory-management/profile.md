# Seasonal Inventory Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 9%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Dealers track inventory in DMS platforms but make seasonal stocking, pricing, and ordering decisions based on the GM's experience and "feel for the market."
**Target Buyer:** Dealer principal or general manager making inventory stocking and pricing decisions
**Automation Potential:** High — RV demand follows predictable seasonal patterns by region and RV type, making inventory optimization a data-driven problem currently solved with gut feel.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
RV sales are intensely seasonal — 60-70% of annual sales occur between March and August in northern states. A dealer must begin ordering spring inventory in September-November (6-month manufacturer lead times), price aggressively in August-September to clear aging summer stock, and manage floor plan interest costs ($200-$500/month per unit) on inventory that sits unsold during winter. The GM's seasonal inventory decisions — how many units to order, which floorplans, at what price to clear aging stock — determine 30-50% of annual profitability. These decisions are made with spreadsheets, manufacturer allocation pressure, and 20 years of experience.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DMS platforms (IDS, Lightspeed) track inventory age and floor plan costs. vAuto's iRecon and ProfitTime offer inventory management for auto dealers but don't handle RV-specific dynamics (seasonal demand curves, manufacturer allocation programs, consignment mix, floorplan interest on $30K-$300K units). No RV-specific inventory optimization tool exists that models seasonal demand, predicts aging risk, and recommends pricing adjustments.
