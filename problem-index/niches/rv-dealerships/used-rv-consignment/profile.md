# Used RV Consignment

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 27%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Consignment agreements are paper contracts, pricing relies on the sales manager's market knowledge, and consignor communication is ad-hoc.
**Target Buyer:** Dealer principal or used RV manager handling consignment inventory
**Automation Potential:** High — Consignment pricing, agreement management, price reduction schedules, and consignor communication are all rule-based and currently manual.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Used RV consignment creates a fundamentally different business model than dealer-owned inventory. The dealer doesn't own the unit — they display and sell it for a commission (typically 10-15% or a flat fee). This creates unique challenges: the consignor expects regular communication and has opinions about pricing, the dealer's margin is fixed (so they prioritize dealer-owned inventory in selling efforts), consignment agreements have legal requirements that vary by state, and pricing used RVs is exceptionally difficult because no two used RVs are alike (year/make/model only tells half the story — condition, mileage, options, modifications, and service history determine actual value). Used RV pricing is one of the most tacit-knowledge-intensive tasks in automotive retail.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DMS platforms (IDS, Lightspeed) track consignment inventory with stock numbers but don't manage consignment agreements, pricing analytics, or consignor communication. NADA Guides provides used RV values but the data is sparse and often 15-25% off actual market prices because used RV values vary dramatically by region, condition, and seasonal demand. No platform combines used RV market pricing, consignment agreement management, and consignor communication.
