# Consignors Calling Weekly for Updates They Never Receive

**Niche:** [[niches/rv-dealerships/used-rv-consignment/profile|Used RV Consignment]]
**Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Consignors leave their $40K-$150K RV on a dealer's lot and hear nothing for weeks — they call the dealer 2-3 times per week for updates that the dealer has no system to provide.
**Tags:** #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
A consignor has a $75,000 motorhome on a dealer's lot. They want to know: How many people have looked at it? Have there been any offers? Has the listing been updated with new photos? Is the dealer actually marketing it? The dealer has no answers because they don't track showings per unit, don't record inquiries unless they become leads in the CRM, and don't proactively communicate with consignors. The consignor calls every few days, speaks with whoever answers the phone (who knows nothing about their specific unit), gets a vague "we're working on it," and grows increasingly frustrated. After 90 days of silence, the consignor pulls their unit and badmouths the dealer to every RV forum and Facebook group.

## Why It's Still Broken
Dealers don't track activity per unit — the CRM tracks leads (potential buyers), not unit-level interest. A salesperson might show a consignment unit 5 times in a month, but none of those showings are logged unless the customer enters the CRM as a lead. The dealer genuinely has no data to share with the consignor. Proactive communication requires someone to manually call or text each consignor weekly — a task that falls to the bottom of the priority list when the sales team is busy with active deals.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated consignor communication system that: (1) logs showings per unit (salesperson taps "showed this unit" in the DMS or CRM), (2) tracks online listing views and inquiries per unit from RV Trader, Facebook Marketplace, and the dealer's website, (3) sends a weekly automated email/text to each consignor with activity metrics: "Your 2020 Tiffin Allegro had 145 online views, 3 inquiries, and 1 in-person showing this week. Current asking price: $72,500. Market avg for similar units: $69,000-$74,000." The consignor feels informed without calling.

## Who Feels the Pain
Consignors who entrust $40K-$150K assets to dealers and feel ignored, and dealer staff who spend 5-10 hours per week fielding consignor calls with no meaningful information to share.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces consignor calls by 70-80% (recovering 4-8 hours per week of staff time), prevents consignor attrition (keeping 20-30% more consignment units on the lot that would otherwise be pulled), and turns satisfied consignors into referral sources for additional consignment inventory.
