# Towable RV Segment

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $18B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Dealers use DMS platforms (IDS, Lightspeed) for inventory, but towable-specific workflows (tow vehicle matching, weight distribution guidance, lot organization for 200+ trailers) are manual.
**Target Buyer:** Dealer principal or sales manager at a dealership selling travel trailers, fifth wheels, toy haulers, and pop-up campers
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Tow vehicle matching, weight/capacity calculations, and inventory aging are automatable; the high-touch sales experience is not.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Towable RVs outsell motorized RVs 4:1 and represent 80%+ of units at most dealerships. The sales workflow for towables is fundamentally different from motorized: the buyer must own a compatible tow vehicle, understand weight ratings and tow capacity, and match their truck to the trailer's GVWR, hitch type, and tongue weight. A mismatch creates a dangerous towing situation and a liability for the dealer. Lot management for 200-400 towable units is a physical challenge — units can't be test-driven, they're arranged in tight rows, and showing a specific unit requires a tow vehicle to pull it out for inspection.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DMS platforms (IDS, Lightspeed EVO) manage inventory and F&I but treat towables like any other vehicle — they don't handle tow vehicle compatibility checking, weight rating calculations, or lot position management. RV-specific CRM tools (DealerSocket, VinSolutions) handle lead management but don't integrate tow vehicle data into the sales workflow. No tool helps the salesperson verify that the customer's truck can safely tow the trailer they're interested in.
