# Service Priority Scheduling for Full-Time Residents

**Niche:** [[niches/rv-dealerships/full-time-rv-lifestyle/profile|Full-Time RV Lifestyle Buyers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** RV dealer service scheduling treats all customers equally, but a full-timer whose RV is their only home needs same-day triage — waiting 3 weeks for an appointment means 3 weeks without a home.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
An RV dealer's service department has a 2-4 week backlog. When a recreational user calls for a water heater repair, waiting 3 weeks is inconvenient — they'll skip their next camping trip. When a full-timer calls for the same repair, waiting 3 weeks means 3 weeks without hot water in their only home. Full-timers often need emergency service: their AC fails in July in Arizona, their furnace quits in December in Colorado, or their water pump breaks leaving them without running water. These are housing emergencies, not RV inconveniences — but the service scheduler treats them identically.

## What Already Exists
RV dealer service scheduling tools (IDS Service module, Lightspeed Service) manage appointment booking and work order assignment. Some dealers offer "emergency" slots, but these are typically at the service manager's discretion and not systematically allocated.

## The Customization Gap
The service scheduling system needs a "full-time resident" flag that activates: (1) priority scheduling for habitability-critical systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, refrigeration), (2) reserved emergency slots (2-3 per week) specifically for full-timer housing emergencies, (3) expedited parts ordering for full-timer repairs (overnight shipping authorized), and (4) loaner/temporary housing coordination (local campground partnerships for overnight stays during multi-day repairs). The system should automatically identify full-time customers from their purchase record or account profile.

## Target Customer
RV dealer service managers at locations that service 50+ full-time residents, typically in sunbelt states and major RV corridor areas.

## Impact If Solved
Increases full-timer service satisfaction from 55-65% to 85-90%, driving $50K-$100K per year in additional service revenue from full-timer referrals and loyalty. Prevents full-timers from seeking service at competitors or independent shops, protecting $200-$500/year per customer in service revenue.
