# Full-Timer Configuration Advisor with 4-Season and Residential Spec Matching

**Niche:** [[niches/rv-dealerships/full-time-rv-lifestyle/profile|Full-Time RV Lifestyle Buyers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/rv-dealerships|RV Dealerships]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No dealer tool identifies which RVs in inventory are actually suitable for full-time living — rating units on insulation, appliance grade, electrical capacity, and daily-use durability.
**Tags:** #recommendation #tabular-ml #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A prospective full-timer walks onto the lot and asks "which of these can I live in year-round?" The salesperson gestures broadly at the Class A motorhomes and says "any of them." In reality, only 15-20% of RVs on a typical lot are genuinely suitable for full-time living — the rest have absorption refrigerators that struggle in heat, inadequate insulation for winter, undersized water heaters for daily showers, and recreational-grade furniture that will wear out in 18 months of daily use. Full-timers learn this the hard way: $100K+ into a motorhome that becomes uninhabitable in January when the underbelly freezes because the "4-season" marketing claim meant "we added a furnace vent to the underbelly."

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Manufacturers market nearly everything as "4-season capable" without standardized testing or ratings. The actual full-time suitability of an RV depends on: insulation R-value (rarely published), tank heating method, window type (single vs. dual pane), electrical system capacity (30A vs. 50A, inverter size, battery capacity), holding tank size for boondocking, and appliance grade (residential vs. RV-grade). This information is scattered across spec sheets, owner forums, and manufacturer technical documents — never aggregated into a full-time suitability score.

## What to Build
A full-time suitability scoring system that rates every RV in the dealer's inventory on dimensions critical to full-time living: (1) climate tolerance (insulation, tank heating, window type, furnace BTU), (2) residential comfort (appliance grade, shower size, storage volume, bed size), (3) self-sufficiency (electrical capacity, solar prep, water capacity, generator), and (4) durability (frame construction, furniture grade, roof material, slide-out mechanism). Each unit gets a full-time suitability score (A-F) with specific strengths and weaknesses explained in plain language.

## Target Customer
RV dealers in sunbelt states and major full-timer corridor areas (Florida, Texas, Arizona) where full-time buyers represent 15-25% of sales.

## Impact If Built
Increases full-timer close rates by 30-40% by building confidence that the unit is genuinely suitable. Reduces full-timer returns/exchanges (currently 15-20% within 12 months) by 50-60%. Each retained full-timer represents $80K-$200K in lifetime value through service, upgrades, and referrals.
