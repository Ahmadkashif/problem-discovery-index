# Location-Aware Session Programming

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/in-home-mobile-trainers/profile|In-Home Mobile Trainers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Trainerize and TrueCoach program exercises assuming gym equipment access, but mobile trainers must design sessions around whatever equipment they can carry in their car and whatever space the client's living room or garage provides.
**Tags:** #recommendation #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Mobile trainers program sessions under constraints that gym-based trainers never face: limited equipment (resistance bands, adjustable dumbbells, a TRX, maybe a kettlebell), variable space (some clients have a garage gym, others have a 10x10 living room), and no fixed machines. Programming for each client requires mentally filtering every exercise through two lenses: "can I do this with the equipment I'm bringing?" and "does this client have enough space?" When trainers carry different equipment subsets to different clients (heavy dumbbells for the client with a garage, bands-only for the apartment client), every session must be programmed against that specific equipment set.

## What Already Exists
Trainerize, TrueCoach, and TrainHeroic provide exercise libraries with thousands of movements, program templates, and client delivery. Their exercise databases include equipment tags (barbell, dumbbell, cable, bodyweight) but no way to filter programs by a specific portable equipment set. A trainer can search for "dumbbell exercises" but can't generate a complete push/pull/legs session using only "adjustable dumbbells up to 50 lbs, light/medium/heavy bands, TRX, and yoga mat" in a "10x12 foot space."

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is equipment-and-space-constrained programming: (1) client profiles that include available equipment and training space dimensions, (2) exercise filtering that shows only movements possible with that client's equipment set and space, (3) auto-generated session templates that build complete workouts from constrained equipment lists, (4) exercise substitution suggestions when a planned movement can't be done with available equipment, and (5) a "what to pack" list per client generated from the day's programming. This is a filter and constraint layer on existing exercise libraries.

## Target Customer
Mobile trainers managing 15+ regular clients with varying equipment access who spend 2-3 hours weekly programming sessions around equipment constraints.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-client programming time by 50% (saving 1-2 hours/week), eliminates the "I forgot the right equipment" moments that waste session time, and enables trainers to serve more clients with systematized programming.
