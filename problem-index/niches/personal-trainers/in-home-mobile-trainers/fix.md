# Travel Time Eating Into Billable Hours

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/in-home-mobile-trainers/profile|In-Home Mobile Trainers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Mobile trainers spend 25-35% of their working day driving between clients — unpaid time that caps their income at $50K-70K despite per-session rates of $75-150 — but they don't track travel costs per client and can't identify which clients are unprofitable.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A mobile trainer charging $100/session who drives 30 minutes each way to a client effectively earns $50/hour after accounting for unpaid travel. But most trainers don't calculate per-client profitability including travel time, fuel, and vehicle wear. They maintain unprofitable clients out of loyalty or because they don't have data showing that a client 25 miles away is earning them $35/hour while a client 5 miles away earns $90/hour. Without this visibility, trainers can't make rational decisions about pricing (adding distance surcharges), scheduling (only serving the distant client on days when nearby clients create a route), or client acquisition (targeting new clients in already-served neighborhoods).

## Why It's Still Broken
No personal training tool tracks travel time or calculates per-client profitability. Scheduling apps (Calendly, Acuity) don't know where clients are located. Payment apps (Venmo, Zelle) don't connect to session locations. The trainer would have to manually log drive times and fuel costs per session and run their own profitability analysis — a task that nobody does because the immediate priority is always "get to the next session." The data exists (GPS history, payment records) but isn't connected.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automatic profitability tracker that: (1) logs travel time between sessions using GPS (phone location history), (2) calculates per-client true hourly rate (session revenue / (session time + travel time)), (3) estimates fuel and vehicle costs per client based on distance, (4) ranks clients by profitability and flags unprofitable accounts, and (5) recommends actions for low-profitability clients (add travel surcharge, move to a different day for better routing, replace with a geographically closer prospect).

## Who Feels the Pain
Mobile trainers who work 10-hour days but only bill for 6-7 hours of sessions, and who can't understand why their income doesn't increase despite being fully booked.

## Impact If Fixed
Makes per-client profitability visible, enabling pricing and scheduling adjustments that can increase effective hourly earnings by 15-25% ($8K-15K annually) without adding sessions — simply by eliminating or repricing unprofitable client relationships.
