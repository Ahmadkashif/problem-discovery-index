# In-Home Mobile Trainers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 20%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Scheduling is often text-based, routing is not optimized, equipment inventory is mental, and billing is per-session Venmo/Zelle with no systematic invoicing.
**Target Buyer:** Mobile Trainer / Solo Operator
**Automation Potential:** High — Route optimization, equipment logistics, travel-time-aware scheduling, and automated invoicing are all rule-based workflows currently handled manually.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
In-home mobile trainers travel to clients' homes, offices, or parks to deliver sessions — a model that has grown 40% since COVID as clients discovered they prefer training at home. These trainers face unique operational challenges that gym-based trainers don't: route planning across 5-8 daily appointments with traffic-dependent travel times, portable equipment logistics (which bands, dumbbells, and mats to bring for which client), scheduling that accounts for travel time between locations, and invoicing that includes travel fees based on distance. Most mobile trainers manage all of this mentally or through text messages — no tool is designed for the mobile personal training workflow.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Calendly or Acuity handle appointment scheduling but don't account for travel time between locations. Google Maps handles individual routing but doesn't optimize multi-stop routes. Venmo and Zelle handle payment but don't generate invoices or track session-pack usage. Trainerize and TrueCoach handle programming but have no awareness of the mobile context (what equipment is available at each client's location). No integrated tool handles the mobile trainer's unique workflow: schedule with travel buffers, optimize routes, track per-client equipment needs, and invoice with distance-based pricing.
