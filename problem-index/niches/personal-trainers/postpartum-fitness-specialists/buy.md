# Client-Provider Communication Portal for Postpartum Care Teams

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/postpartum-fitness-specialists/profile|Postpartum Fitness Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Care coordination platforms like Healthie and Practice Better serve dietitians and therapists, but don't support the specific multi-provider communication that postpartum fitness requires — between trainer, OB/GYN, pelvic floor PT, and lactation consultant.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
Postpartum fitness training doesn't happen in isolation — the trainer needs to coordinate with the client's OB/GYN (for exercise clearance), pelvic floor physical therapist (for pelvic floor status and exercise permissions), and sometimes a lactation consultant (for energy/nutrition considerations). Currently, this coordination happens through the client: the trainer asks the client "what did your PT say about running?" and hopes the client relays accurately. Direct provider-to-provider communication requires HIPAA-compliant channels, client consent management, and a shared understanding of what information needs to flow where. The client becomes an unreliable relay node for critical medical information.

## What Already Exists
Healthie and Practice Better provide HIPAA-compliant practice management for health professionals with client portals and secure messaging. EHR systems (Epic, Cerner) handle medical provider communication but don't include fitness professionals. CareTeam and similar care coordination platforms serve post-acute medical care. None of these platforms include personal trainers as care team members or understand the specific information flows needed (physician clearance documents, pelvic floor assessment summaries, exercise restriction lists).

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is a lightweight care coordination layer for postpartum fitness: (1) HIPAA-compliant secure messaging between the trainer and the client's medical providers (with client consent on file), (2) standardized clearance request forms that the trainer sends to the OB/GYN and receives back digitally, (3) a shared exercise restriction list that the pelvic floor PT can update directly, (4) a client-facing portal showing their full care team and allowing them to manage consent for information sharing, and (5) session notes that the trainer can share with the PT showing exercise progression and client-reported symptoms.

## Target Customer
Postpartum fitness specialists who coordinate with 2-3 medical providers per client and currently rely on client-relayed verbal information for critical exercise decisions.

## Impact If Solved
Ensures trainers receive accurate, timely medical information for exercise programming — reducing the 10-15% of contraindication violations that occur due to miscommunication, while building credibility with medical providers who then refer more clients to the trainer.
