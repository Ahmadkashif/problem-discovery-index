## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Postpartum fitness specialists work with new mothers returning to exercise after childbirth — a population with specific physiological considerations that make generic training programs potentially harmful. Diastasis recti (abdominal separation), pelvic floor dysfunction, C-section recovery, hormonal changes affecting joint laxity, and breastfeeding-related energy demands all constrain exercise selection and progression. These trainers must track medical clearances, monitor symptoms that indicate too-rapid progression, and coordinate with physicians and pelvic floor physical therapists. The stakes are higher than general personal training: the wrong exercise at the wrong stage of recovery can cause lasting pelvic floor damage or worsen abdominal separation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Generic training platforms (Trainerize, TrueCoach) provide exercise delivery but no postpartum-specific features. Specialized prenatal/postpartum education programs (PROnatal, Birthfit, Girls Gone Strong) provide knowledge but not software tools. No platform manages the postpartum-specific workflow: tracking recovery milestones (weeks postpartum, clearance date, diastasis width), automatically excluding contraindicated exercises by recovery stage, or generating progression protocols that advance the client through evidence-based phases.
