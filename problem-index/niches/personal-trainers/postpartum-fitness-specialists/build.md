# Postpartum Recovery-Aware Exercise Programming

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/postpartum-fitness-specialists/profile|Postpartum Fitness Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No exercise programming platform understands postpartum recovery stages — automatically excluding contraindicated movements, progressing through evidence-based phases, and flagging symptoms that indicate regression.
**Tags:** #recommendation #tabular-ml #compliance #worker-facing #ai-platform

## The Problem
A postpartum fitness specialist must mentally track which exercises are safe for each client based on their recovery stage, delivery type, and specific conditions. A client 6 weeks post-vaginal-delivery with a 2-finger diastasis can do different exercises than a client 12 weeks post-C-section with pelvic floor hypertonia. The trainer carries a mental matrix of contraindications and progressions that they apply while programming each session — cross-referencing recovery week, diastasis width, pelvic floor status, physician clearances, and symptom reports against a library of exercises. One mistake (prescribing crunches to a client with active diastasis, or heavy deadlifts before C-section incision has fully healed) can cause measurable harm.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The evidence base for postpartum exercise programming is relatively new and evolving — clinical guidelines from ACOG, ACSM, and specialized organizations don't always agree on specific exercise timing. Encoding this knowledge requires expert clinical input that generic fitness tech companies don't have. The market (estimated 5,000-10,000 certified postpartum fitness specialists in the US) is small for standalone software but large enough for a specialized tool priced at $50-100/month.

## What to Build
A programming platform that: (1) intakes postpartum-specific client data (weeks postpartum, delivery type, diastasis width, pelvic floor assessment results, physician clearance status), (2) automatically categorizes the client into a recovery phase with evidence-based exercise permissions and restrictions, (3) filters the exercise library to show only phase-appropriate movements, (4) generates session templates from the permitted exercise pool, (5) prompts symptom tracking after each session (pain, leaking, pressure, bleeding), and (6) flags symptom patterns that indicate regression and recommends medical referral.

## Target Customer
Certified pre/postnatal fitness trainers managing 10-30 postpartum clients who currently rely on manual contraindication tracking and personal knowledge.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates the 70% of postpartum exercise errors caused by manual contraindication tracking (prescribing a stage-inappropriate exercise), reduces trainer liability exposure, and enables less-specialized trainers to safely serve postpartum clients — expanding the available trainer pool for a population that desperately needs qualified help.
