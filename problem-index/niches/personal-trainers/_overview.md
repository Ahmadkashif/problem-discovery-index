## Why These Niches

Online remote trainers and in-home mobile trainers represent the two dominant delivery models for independent personal training, each with completely different operational requirements — online coaches need content delivery and asynchronous client management, while mobile trainers need route optimization, portable equipment logistics, and location-based scheduling. Postpartum fitness specialists and adaptive/disability trainers serve populations whose specific physiological needs make generic training tools dangerous rather than merely inadequate — the wrong exercise programming can cause real harm. Contest prep coaches and corporate wellness trainers represent the low-digitized frontier: contest prep runs on spreadsheets and WhatsApp despite its precision-critical, data-heavy nature, and corporate wellness trainers operate with zero purpose-built business tools for managing B2B client relationships. Nutrition planning and session-pack billing are the two highest-volume administrative workflows consuming trainer time — both following rigid rules that should be automated but currently require 5-10 hours per week of manual work.
