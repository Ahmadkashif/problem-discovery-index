# Client Nutrition Planning

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Trainers calculate macros in spreadsheets, build meal plans in Google Docs, and send PDFs to clients. Nutrition coaching apps exist but don't generate meal plans from macro targets with the food preference constraints that real clients have.
**Target Buyer:** Trainer with Nutrition Certification
**Automation Potential:** High — Macro calculation, meal plan generation from preference constraints, grocery list creation, and nutritional adjustment based on progress are all algorithmically solvable but currently done manually.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Nutrition coaching is the highest-margin upsell for personal trainers: a $50-200/month add-on that requires 2-5 hours of manual work per client per month. Most trainers with nutrition certifications offer macro targets and meal plans, but the creation process is painstakingly manual: calculate TDEE and macro targets, then build a meal plan that hits those macros while respecting the client's food preferences, allergies, cooking skill, budget, meal prep time, and family size. A single custom meal plan takes 1-3 hours to create. Adjustments (the client lost 5 pounds, lower calories by 200) require rebuilding the plan. This manual intensity limits how many nutrition clients a trainer can serve and caps their income.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MyFitnessPal and MacroFactor handle client-side food logging. EatThisMuch generates meal plans from calorie targets but doesn't integrate with coaching platforms or handle the specific constraints trainers need (hit exact macro ratios, not just calories). Trainerize includes a basic meal planning feature, but it requires the trainer to manually select every meal — no automatic generation. Nutrition coaching platforms (Practice Better, Healthie) serve registered dietitians with clinical features, not personal trainers with simpler workflows.
