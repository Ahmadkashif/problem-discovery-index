# Functional Progress Metrics for Non-Standard Baselines

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/adaptive-disability-trainers/profile|Adaptive & Disability Trainers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Trainerize and TrueCoach track bench press 1RM and mile run time, but a wheelchair user's progress is measured in wheelchair push distance, transfer independence, and grip endurance — metrics these platforms don't support.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Standard fitness progress tracking measures (1RM lifts, running pace, body weight, body fat percentage) are meaningless or inapplicable for many adaptive fitness clients. A client with a spinal cord injury measures progress in wheelchair push speed and distance, upper body endurance, transfer independence (moving from wheelchair to car/bed), and pressure relief routine adherence. A client with cerebral palsy may track fine motor coordination improvements, gait stability scores, and functional task completion times. These functional metrics are the true indicators of life-improving progress, but no training platform includes them as trackable data fields.

## What Already Exists
Trainerize, TrueCoach, and TrainHeroic track standard fitness metrics (weight lifted, reps, sets, body measurements, cardio times). They offer custom fields, but these are unstructured notes rather than graphable data series. Physical therapy platforms (WebPT, SimplePractice) track functional outcomes but aren't designed for fitness programming. Paralympic and adaptive sport organizations (US Paralympics, Challenged Athletes Foundation) define sport-specific metrics but haven't digitized them for personal trainers.

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is a functional progress tracking layer: (1) pre-built metric templates for common disability categories (spinal cord injury, amputation, neurological conditions, intellectual disability) with the specific measurements that matter for each, (2) custom metric creation with configurable units and visualization, (3) functional assessment protocols (timed functional tasks, range of motion measurements, independence scales) built into the platform, (4) progress visualization that shows meaningful trends to the client and their care team, and (5) exportable progress reports in formats useful for physician appointments and insurance justification.

## Target Customer
Adaptive fitness trainers who need to demonstrate client progress to caregivers, physicians, and insurance companies using functional metrics rather than standard fitness benchmarks.

## Impact If Solved
Enables trainers to document and demonstrate functional improvements that justify continued training to clients, families, and insurers — directly supporting client retention and potentially enabling insurance reimbursement for adaptive fitness (a growing policy trend worth $50K-100K annually per trainer if covered).
