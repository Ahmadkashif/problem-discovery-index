# Contest Prep Coaches

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $600M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Contest prep is among the most data-intensive coaching niches (daily weigh-ins, macro tracking, depletion protocols, water manipulation) but runs almost entirely on Google Sheets, WhatsApp, and personal judgment with no purpose-built tools.
**Target Buyer:** Bodybuilding / Physique Coach
**Automation Potential:** High — Macro calculations, depletion schedules, water/sodium protocols, and reverse diet plans follow algorithmic rules that experienced coaches apply from memory but could be codified.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Contest prep coaches guide bodybuilding, physique, bikini, and figure competitors through 12-24 week preparation cycles culminating in a stage appearance. This is the most precision-critical form of personal training: coaches manipulate calories (often in 50-100 calorie increments), macronutrient ratios (to the gram), water intake (from 2 gallons to 2 cups over a peak week), sodium loading/depletion, and training volume on weekly or daily adjustment cycles. The data volume is enormous — daily weigh-ins, weekly progress photos, daily food logs, training logs, sleep data, and biofeedback metrics (mood, energy, hunger, digestion). Despite this data intensity, the entire workflow runs on Google Sheets shared via WhatsApp with the coach making manual adjustments based on pattern recognition.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MyFitnessPal or MacroFactor handles client-side food logging. Google Sheets handles the coach's macro tracking and adjustment history. WhatsApp handles check-in communication. Progress photos are sent via DM. No integrated platform handles the full contest prep workflow: collecting daily data from the client, presenting trend analysis to the coach, supporting the specific calculations involved (macro cycling, depletion protocols, peak week water/sodium manipulation), and documenting the prep for future reference.
