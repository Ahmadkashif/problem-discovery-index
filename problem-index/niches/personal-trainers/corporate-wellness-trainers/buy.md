# Corporate Wellness Outcome Reporting for HR

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/corporate-wellness-trainers/profile|Corporate Wellness Trainers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** SurveyMonkey and Google Forms collect participant feedback, but HR directors evaluating wellness program ROI need standardized outcome reports showing participation trends, satisfaction scores, and health metric changes — formatted for benefits committee presentations.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
When a corporate wellness program comes up for renewal, the HR director asks the trainer: "What were the outcomes?" The trainer scrambles to compile data from attendance spreadsheets, satisfaction survey results, and anecdotal feedback into a presentable format. The resulting PowerPoint deck looks amateurish next to the polished analytics dashboards that enterprise wellness platforms provide. More critically, the trainer doesn't collect the right data throughout the program — they have participation numbers but not engagement trends, satisfaction scores but not benchmarks, and no standardized metrics that HR can compare across vendors or present to the C-suite.

## What Already Exists
SurveyMonkey and Typeform handle feedback collection. Google Data Studio and Tableau handle data visualization. Enterprise wellness platforms (Virgin Pulse, Vitality) include polished analytics dashboards as a core feature. But no tool helps an independent trainer collect the specific data points that HR directors need for program justification and present them in the standard format that benefits committees expect — with industry benchmarks, trend analysis, and ROI estimates.

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is a wellness program outcome reporting template: (1) standardized pre/post program surveys based on CDC workplace health assessment frameworks, (2) automated participation analytics from attendance data (participation rate, engagement trend, departmental adoption), (3) satisfaction scoring with industry benchmarks ("your program scored 4.2/5.0 vs. industry average of 3.7"), (4) optional biometric tracking integration for programs that include health screenings, and (5) a quarterly report generator that produces a branded, professional PDF suitable for a benefits committee presentation.

## Target Customer
Corporate wellness trainers who struggle with program renewal conversations because they can't document outcomes in the format that HR decision-makers require.

## Impact If Solved
Increases contract renewal rate from 60-70% to 80-90% by providing the outcome documentation that HR needs to justify budget allocation — worth $20K-50K in annual revenue preservation per trainer, plus larger contract sizes from programs that can demonstrate measurable results.
