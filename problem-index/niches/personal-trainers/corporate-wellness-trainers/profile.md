# Corporate Wellness Trainers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Corporate wellness trainers manage B2B client relationships, multi-participant scheduling, and outcome reporting with the same consumer tools (Calendly, Venmo) used by solo trainers — with zero B2B infrastructure.
**Target Buyer:** Corporate Wellness Contractor
**Automation Potential:** High — Participant enrollment, session scheduling across corporate calendars, aggregate outcome reporting, and contract renewal documentation are all highly rule-based and currently manual.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Corporate wellness trainers sell fitness services to businesses rather than individuals — conducting on-site or virtual group fitness classes, lunch-and-learns, ergonomic assessments, and wellness challenges for company employees. This B2B model creates operational requirements that consumer training tools ignore: contracts with deliverable specifications, participant enrollment managed through HR departments, scheduling coordinated with corporate calendars and conference room bookings, aggregate reporting showing program outcomes (participation rates, health metric improvements) for HR review, and invoicing against purchase orders rather than personal credit cards.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Corporate wellness trainers use the same consumer tools as solo trainers: Calendly for scheduling, Google Sheets for participant tracking, Canva for proposals, and QuickBooks for invoicing. None of these tools understand the corporate wellness workflow: multi-participant sessions booked through HR, attendance tracking across departments, outcome reporting formatted for HR/benefits decision-makers, and contract management with auto-renewal tracking. Dedicated corporate wellness platforms (Virgin Pulse, WellSteps, Vitality) serve enterprise employers, not the independent trainers who deliver the actual programming.
