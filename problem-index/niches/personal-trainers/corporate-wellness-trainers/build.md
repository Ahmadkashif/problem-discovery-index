# B2B Wellness Program Management Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/corporate-wellness-trainers/profile|Corporate Wellness Trainers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform gives an independent corporate wellness trainer a professional portal to manage multiple company accounts — with branded program pages, participant enrollment, attendance tracking, and outcome reports that make a solo operator look like a wellness company.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
An independent corporate wellness trainer managing 3-8 company accounts operates a B2B business with consumer tools. They send proposals via email attachments, manage enrollment through Google Forms, track attendance in spreadsheets, send invoices through QuickBooks, and present outcomes through PowerPoint decks. The lack of professional infrastructure undermines their credibility: when an HR director evaluates their program alongside Virgin Pulse or WellSteps, the independent trainer looks like a freelancer, not a professional service provider. This perception gap limits contract size ($5K-20K vs. $50K-200K for platform-backed providers) and makes renewals harder to justify.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Corporate wellness platforms (Virgin Pulse, WellSteps, Vitality, Limeade) are built for enterprise employers — they cost $3-8 per employee per month and include app development, health assessments, incentive management, and insurance integration. An independent trainer delivering 2-3 weekly sessions to a 50-person company doesn't need this infrastructure. But no one has built the lightweight version: a professional portal that handles the B2B operational workflow without the enterprise overhead.

## What to Build
A B2B wellness program management portal that gives independent trainers: (1) branded program pages per client company with enrollment and scheduling, (2) participant self-enrollment linked to the company's HR approval, (3) session attendance tracking with department-level participation analytics, (4) aggregate outcome reporting (participation trends, satisfaction surveys, optional health metrics) formatted for HR review, (5) contract management with auto-renewal reminders and scope tracking, and (6) professional invoicing against purchase orders with usage documentation attached.

## Target Customer
Independent corporate wellness trainers managing 3-8 company accounts who want to professionalize their operations and compete for larger contracts.

## Impact If Built
Increases average contract size by 30-50% (from $8K to $12K annually per account) through professional presentation, reduces administrative overhead by 8-10 hours/month, and improves contract renewal rates by 20-30% through documented outcomes.
