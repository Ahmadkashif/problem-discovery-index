# No-Show and Late Cancel Fee Enforcement

**Niche:** [[niches/personal-trainers/session-pack-billing/profile|Session Pack Billing & Scheduling]]
**Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Calendly and Acuity collect credit cards for appointment deposits, but neither enforces personal training no-show policies — deducting a session from a pack, charging a late-cancel fee, or tracking repeat offenders.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
No-shows and late cancellations cost personal trainers $5K-15K/year in lost income. A trainer who holds a 6 PM slot for a client who cancels at 5:30 PM can't fill that slot — the hour is lost. Most trainers have policies (24-hour cancellation required, no-show forfeits session), but enforcing them is socially uncomfortable and operationally manual. The trainer must remember to deduct the session from the pack, send the client a message about the charge, and handle the awkward conversation. Many trainers waive the policy to avoid conflict, losing thousands annually. The absence of automated enforcement makes every no-show a personal confrontation.

## What Already Exists
Calendly and Acuity support cancellation policies with credit card holds, but they're designed for one-time appointment fees, not session-pack deductions. Mindbody and Vagaro enforce cancellation policies but within their full studio platform ($100-300/month). Square Appointments allows late-cancel charges but doesn't connect to session-pack balances. No lightweight tool combines scheduling, session-pack tracking, and policy enforcement in a way that removes the social friction from no-show consequences.

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is automated policy enforcement for session-pack trainers: (1) configurable cancellation policy (24-hour minimum, late-cancel deducts session, no-show deducts session + fee), (2) automatic enforcement when a client cancels or no-shows — the session is deducted from the pack without the trainer taking manual action, (3) automated client notification explaining the deduction per the agreed-upon policy, (4) a repeat-offender tracker that flags clients who no-show more than 2x per pack, and (5) an opt-in "fill my spot" feature that offers the canceled slot to other clients on a waitlist.

## Target Customer
Solo trainers losing $5K-15K/year to no-shows and late cancellations who avoid enforcing their own policies because the process is manual and socially awkward.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers $3K-10K annually in currently-lost session revenue through consistent policy enforcement, while removing the interpersonal friction by making enforcement automatic and policy-based rather than personal.
