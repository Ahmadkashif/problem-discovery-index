# Session Pack Billing & Scheduling

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/personal-trainers|Personal Trainers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most trainers use Calendly or Acuity for scheduling and Venmo/Zelle for payment, but session-pack tracking (how many sessions remain), expiration enforcement, and automated rebooking are manual.
**Target Buyer:** Solo Trainer / Small Studio Owner
**Automation Potential:** High — Session pack decrement, expiration tracking, auto-renewal prompts, no-show/late-cancel fee enforcement, and rebooking automation are all rule-based workflows currently managed in the trainer's memory.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Personal trainers primarily sell sessions in packs (10-session, 20-session, monthly unlimited) rather than individual sessions. This creates a billing and scheduling workflow distinct from both subscription businesses (where the charge is automatic) and appointment businesses (where each appointment is independently paid). The trainer must track: how many sessions remain in each client's pack, when the pack expires (most packs have 60-90 day expiration), when to prompt the client to purchase the next pack, how to handle no-shows and late cancellations (deduct a session or not?), and how to transition clients between pack types. This tracking is done mentally or in spreadsheets by most solo trainers.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Calendly and Acuity handle appointment scheduling. Venmo, Zelle, and Square handle payment. Mindbody and Vagaro handle both but are designed for studios with multiple trainers, not solo operators — the overhead of managing a full platform isn't justified for a single-trainer business. The gap is a lightweight session-pack management tool: purchase a 10-pack, each booked session decrements the count, the system alerts when 2 sessions remain, and auto-sends a repurchase link — without requiring the trainer to manage a full studio management platform.
