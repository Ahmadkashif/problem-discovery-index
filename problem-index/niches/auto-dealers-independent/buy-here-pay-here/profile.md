# Buy Here Pay Here (BHPH) Dealers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-dealers-independent|Independent Auto Dealers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-35B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~27%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — use BHPH-specific DMS tools (DealerCenter, Wayne Reaves) but collections, GPS tracking, and payment analytics are fragmented
**Target Buyer:** BHPH dealer principal or collections manager
**Automation Potential:** High — payment collection, delinquency prediction, and repossession workflow are all rule-driven and high-frequency

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
BHPH dealers are simultaneously a car dealership and a consumer finance company — they sell vehicles and carry the loan in-house, collecting weekly or bi-weekly payments directly from the customer. Their primary business risk is not selling cars but managing a loan portfolio of 200-1,000 active accounts with customers who have 500-600 credit scores. Collections is the core operational function: a single delinquent account can cost $3K-8K in lost principal plus the cost of repossession and remarketing. BHPH dealers also face unique regulatory exposure — state lending laws, GPS tracking disclosure requirements, and CFPB scrutiny on subprime auto lending practices.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DealerCenter, Wayne Reaves, and Dealer Management Software (ABCoA) handle BHPH-specific deal structuring and payment tracking. GPS devices from Spireon and PassTime provide vehicle location and starter-interrupt capability. But no tool provides predictive delinquency analytics (which accounts will miss next week's payment?), portfolio-level risk scoring, or optimized collection routing (which accounts should the collector call first for maximum recovery?).
