# Wholesale & Auction Flippers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-dealers-independent|Independent Auto Dealers]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — operate on gut feel, Manheim MMR lookups, and mental math at auction speed with minimal systematic tooling
**Target Buyer:** Wholesale operator or principal buyer at a high-volume independent
**Automation Potential:** Medium — acquisition decision support is data-rich but requires market intuition; arbitrage detection and transport logistics are highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wholesale flippers buy vehicles at auction and resell them — either to other dealers, at another auction, or at retail. Their margin per unit is thin ($300-1,500), so volume and speed are everything. A productive buyer evaluates 50-100 vehicles per auction day and purchases 5-15, making each buy decision in 30-90 seconds during the lane. The core competency is arbitrage detection: knowing that a vehicle priced at $12K at a Manheim lane in Atlanta can retail for $16K at their lot in Charlotte — a geographic arbitrage that requires knowledge of hyperlocal demand that no published guide captures.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Manheim Market Report (MMR) provides wholesale price benchmarks. ACV Auctions offers condition reports and digital bidding. vAuto and CarGurus provide retail market data. But no tool combines wholesale pricing, geographic arbitrage opportunities, transport cost, and reconditioning estimates into a single "profit potential" view per vehicle at auction speed. Buyers operate on accumulated market knowledge, which is impossible to transfer to new hires.
