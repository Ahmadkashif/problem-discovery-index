# Vehicle Reconditioning Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-dealers-independent|Independent Auto Dealers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most dealers track reconditioning via whiteboard, sticky notes, or a shared spreadsheet with no standardized workflow, cost tracking, or time measurement
**Target Buyer:** Recon manager, service director, or dealer principal at a dealer reconditioning 20+ units per month
**Automation Potential:** High — inspection, work assignment, parts ordering, vendor coordination, and cost tracking follow repeatable workflows with clear handoff points

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every used vehicle requires reconditioning before retail display: mechanical inspection and repair, body work, paint touch-up, interior cleaning, tire replacement, and detailing. At an independent dealer, this process involves 3-7 vendors (mechanic, body shop, detail shop, tire shop, glass shop, upholsterer, PDR specialist) and takes 5-15 business days — during which the vehicle accrues $35-50/day in floorplan interest and depreciation. The recon process is the single largest bottleneck between acquisition and revenue generation, yet it's managed with whiteboards, text messages, and memory at most independents.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Reconditioning-specific tools exist (ReconVelocity, Rapid Recon, ReconTRAC) but are designed for franchise dealers with dedicated recon centers and staff. Independent dealers use their own mechanics plus outside vendors, creating a coordination challenge that franchise-focused tools don't address. No tool handles the independent dealer's workflow: assigning work across internal and external vendors, tracking vehicle location (at the dealer, at the body shop, at the detail shop), monitoring cost accrual per vehicle in real time, and alerting when a vehicle exceeds its recon budget or timeline.
