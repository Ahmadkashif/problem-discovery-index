# Title & Registration Processing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-dealers-independent|Independent Auto Dealers]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (embedded operational cost)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most title work involves paper forms, in-person DMV visits, and manual tracking of title status across pending deals
**Target Buyer:** Title clerk, office manager, or dealer principal handling title processing
**Automation Potential:** High — title documentation, DMV submissions, lien releases, and registration filings follow strict procedural rules that vary by state

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every vehicle sale requires title transfer and registration — a process governed by state-specific rules that involve 5-15 documents per deal, DMV submissions (some states allow electronic, most require physical), lien release tracking for trade-ins, out-of-state title transfers for vehicles purchased from non-local sellers, and temporary tag management with expiration tracking. A title clerk at a 200-unit dealer processes 200+ title packages per month, each with its own timeline, required documents, and potential for delay. A missing signature, wrong VIN transcription, or expired temporary tag can result in fines, deal unwinding, and angry customers who can't register their vehicle.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DealerCenter and Frazer generate state-specific deal documents (buyer's orders, title applications). Electronic Lien and Title (ELT) systems exist in some states for digital title transfers. Tag agencies and title services handle submissions for a fee ($30-75 per deal). But no tool provides: end-to-end title status tracking across all pending deals, automated document completeness checking before submission, out-of-state title transfer workflow management, or temporary tag expiration monitoring with customer notification.
