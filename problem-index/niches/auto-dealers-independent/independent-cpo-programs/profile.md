# Independent CPO & Certified Programs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/auto-dealers-independent|Independent Auto Dealers]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — some independents use third-party certification programs (EasyCare, GWC Warranty) but lack the inspection, documentation, and marketing infrastructure that franchise CPO programs provide
**Target Buyer:** Dealer principal or used car manager building a certified brand at an independent lot
**Automation Potential:** Medium — inspection standardization, warranty administration, and certification documentation are structured processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Independent dealers increasingly want to compete with franchise CPO programs (Toyota Certified, Honda Certified) but lack the infrastructure. A franchise dealer certifies a vehicle against a manufacturer-defined 150+ point inspection, backs it with a manufacturer warranty, and markets the "Certified Pre-Owned" badge. Independent dealers have no equivalent system — they may inspect vehicles but with no standardized checklist, no branded warranty program customers trust, and no marketing framework that communicates "certified" credibly. This gap costs independents 5-10% in retail price per vehicle versus comparable franchise CPO inventory.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Third-party warranty providers (EasyCare, GWC, CARS Protection Plus) sell vehicle service contracts that independents can attach to vehicles. AutoCheck and Carfax provide vehicle history reports. But no tool provides: a standardized multi-point inspection workflow with photo documentation, a branded certification program the dealer can customize, automated warranty claim processing, and marketing materials that convey the certification credibly to online shoppers. Independents cobble this together manually, if at all.
