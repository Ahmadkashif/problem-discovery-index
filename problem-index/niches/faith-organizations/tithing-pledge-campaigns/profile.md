# Tithing & Pledge Campaigns

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — online giving platforms (Pushpay, Tithe.ly) handle transaction processing, but pledge management, campaign tracking, and stewardship follow-up remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Stewardship Director / Finance Committee Chair / Lead Pastor
**Automation Potential:** High — pledge reminders, giving trend analysis, campaign progress tracking, and year-end stewardship reporting are all rule-based and automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Tithing (regular percentage-based giving) and pledge campaigns (time-limited capital or mission commitments) are the financial engine of every congregation. US religious giving exceeds $130B/year, but giving rates have declined from 3.1% to 2.2% of income over the past 40 years. Churches run annual stewardship campaigns to encourage pledges, then spend the year tracking pledge fulfillment, sending reminders, and thanking donors — all manually. Unlike nonprofit fundraising (which uses Salesforce, Bloomerang, DonorPerfect), church giving has unique dynamics: it's theologically motivated, recurring (not event-driven), and deeply personal (pastors feel awkward "asking for money").

## Current Tools & Gaps
Pushpay, Tithe.ly, and Planning Center Giving handle digital giving transactions. Church giving platforms provide basic pledge tracking. However, none provide the full stewardship campaign lifecycle: pre-campaign communications, pledge card management (still paper at most churches), pledge fulfillment tracking with intelligent reminders, giving trend analysis per household, and stewardship reporting that frames giving data in ministry impact terms rather than financial terms.
