# Facility Rental Managers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most churches manage facility rentals via phone calls, paper contracts, and the church secretary's calendar; no standardized booking or payment system
**Target Buyer:** Church Administrator / Facilities Manager at churches with fellowship halls, gymnasiums, or campus-style facilities
**Automation Potential:** High — booking, contracts, invoicing, and calendar management are fully automatable; facility setup coordination is semi-automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Churches sit empty 5-6 days per week and increasingly rent their facilities to community groups, events, and organizations to generate revenue. A mid-size church with a fellowship hall, gymnasium, commercial kitchen, and outdoor space can generate $30K-$150K/year in rental income. But managing rentals is operationally complex: insurance verification, security deposits, setup/teardown coordination, key management, cleaning schedules, and conflict with church programming. Most churches handle this through the church secretary, who manages everything via phone, email, and a paper calendar — limiting capacity to 2-3 rentals per month when the facility could support 10-15.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Commercial venue rental platforms (Peerspace, Skedda, Tripleseat) exist but are designed for dedicated event venues, not shared-use church facilities where internal programming takes priority, volunteer teams handle setup, and the "rental manager" is a church secretary handling rentals alongside 15 other responsibilities. Church management tools (Planning Center, Breeze) have basic room scheduling but no rental-specific features — no contracts, no invoicing, no insurance verification, no deposit management.
