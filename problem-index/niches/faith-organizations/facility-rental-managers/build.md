# Church Facility Rental Marketplace

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/facility-rental-managers/profile|Facility Rental Managers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No marketplace exists where community groups, event planners, and organizations can discover and book church facilities — the second-largest inventory of community event space in America, almost entirely invisible online.
**Tags:** #ai-platform #automation #revenue-impact #data-integration

## The Problem
Churches collectively own more community event space than any organization except local government — fellowship halls, gymnasiums, commercial kitchens, classrooms, outdoor campuses — but this inventory is invisible to potential renters. Someone looking for a 200-person event space in their neighborhood would never think to call local churches, and churches don't list their spaces on Peerspace or EventBrite because those platforms don't understand church-specific constraints (Sunday mornings are blocked, the choir room has a piano that can't be moved, the kitchen requires a certified food handler). The result: churches sit empty and underutilized while community groups pay premium rates at hotels and conference centers.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Church facility rental is fragmented — each church sets its own policies, pricing, and availability independently. Aggregating this inventory requires onboarding 100,000+ individual churches, each with unique spaces, rules, and decision-making processes (often requiring board approval for new rental arrangements). The unit economics are challenging: average rental is $300-$1,500, making a marketplace commission model thin per transaction. Additionally, many churches are philosophically ambivalent about "renting" their sacred space and need the platform to frame it as community service, not commercial activity.

## What to Build
A marketplace where churches list their available spaces with photos, capacity, amenities, availability calendar (auto-synced with church programming), and rental policies. Community groups search by location, capacity, date, and amenities (kitchen, AV, parking). The platform handles booking requests, contract generation, payment processing, and insurance verification. Church-specific features: auto-block Sunday mornings and church events, require ministry approval for certain event types, and provide "community partner" pricing tiers.

## Target Customer
Church administrators at the 50,000+ churches with rentable facilities who currently rely on word-of-mouth and phone inquiries to fill their spaces.

## Impact If Built
Increases average church facility utilization from 15% to 35%, generating $20K-$80K in additional annual revenue per church, while making affordable event space accessible to community groups that currently can't afford commercial venues.
