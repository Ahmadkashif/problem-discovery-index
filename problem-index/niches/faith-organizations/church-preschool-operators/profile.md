# Church Preschool Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — church preschools use a mix of church management software (for the ministry side) and childcare tools (for the licensing side), but neither fits well; many use paper attendance and handwritten tuition records
**Target Buyer:** Preschool Director / Church Administrator responsible for preschool operations
**Automation Potential:** High — enrollment management, tuition billing, attendance tracking, and licensing compliance are all highly structured and automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
An estimated 50,000 churches in the US operate licensed or license-exempt preschool programs, making them the largest category of childcare providers in many states. These programs exist in a unique operational space: they are a ministry of the church (using church facilities, reporting to church leadership, serving families as outreach) but also a regulated childcare operation (subject to state licensing, background checks, teacher-to-child ratios, and health/safety inspections). Neither church management software nor childcare management software handles both sides — the preschool director toggles between Planning Center (for church families) and paper forms (for licensing compliance).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Dedicated childcare management platforms (Brightwheel, HiMama, Procare) handle enrollment, billing, attendance, and parent communication for standalone childcare centers. Church management tools (Planning Center, Breeze) handle membership and giving. But church preschools need both: tracking which preschool families are also church members, managing tuition as a separate revenue stream from tithes, and producing compliance reports for state licensing while also reporting to the church board. No tool bridges these two operational contexts.
