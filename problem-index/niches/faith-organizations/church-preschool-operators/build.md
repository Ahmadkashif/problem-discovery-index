# Church-Preschool Unified Management

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/church-preschool-operators/profile|Church Preschool Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform today unifies the ministry side (connecting preschool families to the church, tracking engagement beyond tuition) with the childcare operations side (licensing compliance, ratio tracking, developmental assessments) of church-run preschools.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #ai-platform

## The Problem
A church preschool director operates in two worlds: the church world (where the preschool is a ministry that should connect families to the congregation) and the childcare world (where the preschool is a licensed operation that must comply with state regulations). In the church world, the preschool is evaluated by how many families attend Sunday worship. In the childcare world, it's evaluated by licensing compliance, safety incident reports, and teacher qualifications. The director uses 2-4 separate systems for these overlapping responsibilities and spends hours each week reconciling information between them.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Church preschool operations fall between two established software categories — childcare management (Brightwheel, Procare) and church management (Planning Center, Breeze) — and neither vendor has an incentive to build into the other's territory. The market of 50,000 church preschools is split across denominations with different governance models, and the operational overlap (shared facilities, shared families, mixed funding) creates data model complexity that neither category's software architecture can easily accommodate.

## What to Build
A dual-mode management platform where preschool operations (enrollment, attendance, tuition, ratios, parent communication, developmental milestones) and church engagement (family membership status, Sunday attendance, small group participation, ministry involvement) share a unified family record. The director sees both lenses: "The Johnson family pays tuition and attends Sunday services" or "these 12 preschool families have never visited a church event — they're our outreach opportunity." Licensing reports and church board reports pull from the same data.

## Target Customer
Preschool directors at church programs with 30-150 enrolled children, currently managing operations across 2-4 disconnected systems. Priced at $75-$200/month.

## Impact If Built
Saves preschool directors 8-12 hours per week of cross-system data management, increases church engagement among preschool families by 25% through proactive outreach, and ensures licensing compliance through automated ratio tracking and documentation.
