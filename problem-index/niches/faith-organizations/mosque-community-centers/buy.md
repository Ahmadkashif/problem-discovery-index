# Zakat Collection & Distribution Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/mosque-community-centers/profile|Mosque Community Centers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Donation platforms like Stripe and Pushpay handle online giving but cannot distinguish between zakat, sadaqah, and fitrah — each with different Islamic jurisprudential rules about collection, calculation, and distribution to eligible recipients.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Mosques collect three distinct types of charitable giving: zakat (obligatory annual wealth tax, 2.5% of qualifying assets, distributed to 8 specific categories of recipients defined by the Quran), sadaqah (voluntary charity with no restrictions), and zakat al-fitr (per-person payment due before Eid al-Fitr). Each has different rules about who can give, how much, and who can receive. Most mosques dump all donations into one account and distribute informally, without tracking which funds are zakat-obligated (and therefore restricted in how they can be spent) versus unrestricted sadaqah. This creates both Islamic compliance concerns and IRS reporting complications.

## What Already Exists
Pushpay, Tithe.ly, and Stripe handle online donations with fund designation. LaunchGood provides Islamic crowdfunding. However, none understand the jurisprudential distinctions between giving categories — they treat "Zakat Fund" as just another donation category without enforcing that zakat funds can only be distributed to the 8 Quran-defined recipient categories, or that zakat al-fitr must be distributed before Eid prayer.

## The Customization Gap
The missing layer is a zakat-aware donation management system that: calculates individual zakat obligations (based on gold/silver nisab thresholds that fluctuate with market prices), tracks zakat vs. sadaqah vs. fitrah collection separately, enforces distribution rules (zakat only to eligible asnaf categories), generates donor receipts with Islamically correct categorization, and provides distribution reporting showing how zakat funds were allocated across recipient categories. The system must update nisab thresholds automatically based on current gold/silver prices.

## Target Customer
Mosque treasurers and zakat committees managing $100K-$1M+ in annual zakat collection who need both Islamic and IRS compliance in a single system.

## Impact If Solved
Ensures zakat funds are distributed correctly per Islamic jurisprudence, increases donor confidence (and therefore giving) by 15-20% through transparent reporting, and saves 5-10 hours/month of manual tracking and calculation during Ramadan peak giving season.
