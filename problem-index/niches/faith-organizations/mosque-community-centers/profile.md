# Mosque Community Centers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most mosques rely on WhatsApp groups, paper sign-ups, and manual cash collection; Islamic-specific software options are limited and often poorly maintained
**Target Buyer:** Imam / Board President / Executive Director at Islamic centers
**Automation Potential:** Medium — zakat calculation, prayer time scheduling, and membership management are automatable, but community governance (shura councils, community mediation) is inherently interpersonal

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The 3,000+ mosques in the US serve as both worship spaces and community centers — offering Friday prayers, daily salat, Quran classes, youth programs, food pantries, immigration assistance, and social gatherings. The operational model differs from churches: giving is less structured (zakat is an annual obligation calculated on wealth, not a weekly tithe), prayer times shift daily based on solar calculations, Ramadan creates an intense 30-day operational surge, and many mosques serve multilingual communities (Arabic, Urdu, Somali, Bengali). Existing church management software was designed around Christian worship patterns and doesn't accommodate Islamic practices.

## Current Tools & Gaps
A handful of mosque-specific tools exist (MasjidNow for prayer times, Masjidal for basic management) but they are limited in scope and development. Most mosques use generic tools — Square for donations, WhatsApp for communications, Google Calendar for events. Planning Center and Breeze have no concept of daily prayer times, zakat calculations, Ramadan programming, or Islamic calendar events. The gap is a comprehensive Islamic center management platform that handles the full operational model.
