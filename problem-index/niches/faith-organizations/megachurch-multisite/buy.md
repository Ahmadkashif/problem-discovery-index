# Multi-Site Content Distribution Pipeline

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/megachurch-multisite/profile|Megachurch Multi-Site]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Digital asset management tools like Dropbox and Frame.io handle file sharing for creative teams but lack the church-specific workflow of distributing sermon series packages (videos, graphics, notes, worship charts) to satellite campuses on a weekly production cycle.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Multi-site churches produce sermon content centrally and distribute it to satellite campuses — either as live video feeds or pre-recorded content packages. Each package includes sermon video, lower thirds graphics, worship setups (song charts, stems, click tracks), announcement slides, small group discussion guides, and social media assets. The creative team at the hub campus produces these assets on a Tuesday-to-Saturday cycle, and satellite campus tech directors need everything by Thursday for weekend rehearsal. Currently, distribution happens via shared Dropbox folders, email, and Slack, with assets scattered across 5-6 different tools and frequent version confusion.

## What Already Exists
Dropbox and Google Drive handle file sharing. Frame.io provides video review workflows. ProPresenter distributes worship slides. Planning Center Services manages service order and team assignments. However, none provide a unified content package for a multi-site worship experience — each tool handles one asset type, and the satellite campus tech director must pull from 5-6 sources to assemble a complete weekend.

## The Customization Gap
The missing product is a church-specific content distribution platform where the hub creative team publishes a weekly "campus package" containing all assets for the upcoming service, organized by service element (welcome, worship set 1, sermon, worship set 2, announcements, closing). Satellite campus directors receive a single notification, download one package, and have everything they need for weekend production — versioned, organized, and timestamped to match the service order.

## Target Customer
Creative directors and multi-site operations pastors at churches with 3-15 campuses who spend 5-10 hours per week assembling and distributing content packages.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces content distribution overhead from 8 hours to 1 hour per week, eliminates the 2-3 "wrong version" incidents per month that cause weekend service disruptions, and enables satellite campuses to rehearse with final content 24 hours earlier.
