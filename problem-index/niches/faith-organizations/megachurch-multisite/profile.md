# Megachurch Multi-Site

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 20%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — most use Planning Center, Church Community Builder, or Rock RMS, but multi-site coordination and campus-level analytics remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Executive Pastor / Operations Director at churches with 2,000+ weekly attendance
**Automation Potential:** Medium — centralized operations (finance, HR, communications) are automatable, but campus-specific pastoral care and ministry programming require local leadership

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Megachurches (2,000+ weekly attendance) and multi-site churches operate more like mid-sized nonprofits than traditional congregations — with paid staff of 30-200, annual budgets of $5M-$50M+, and the complexity of managing multiple physical campuses with shared branding but semi-autonomous programming. The operational challenges mirror franchise management: maintaining brand consistency across locations, allocating shared resources (creative teams, teaching content, technology), and tracking performance metrics per campus. These organizations outgrow standard church management software and need enterprise-grade operations tools with church-specific workflows.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Planning Center handles people, giving, and service planning. Rock RMS provides CRM and workflow automation. Pushpay/Church Community Builder handles mobile giving. However, multi-site-specific needs — cross-campus volunteer sharing, campus-level financial reporting, centralized content distribution to satellite campuses, and campus health scoring — require custom solutions that most churches build internally or manage in spreadsheets.
