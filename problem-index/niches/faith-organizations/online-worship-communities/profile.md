# Online Worship Communities

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 2%
**Digital Adoption:** High — these communities exist entirely online, but the tools they use (YouTube Live, Zoom, Facebook Groups) were not designed for worship and community building
**Target Buyer:** Digital Ministry Director / Online Campus Pastor
**Automation Potential:** Medium — content distribution and engagement tracking are automatable, but spiritual community building requires personal connection

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Post-COVID, thousands of churches launched "online campuses" and hundreds of digital-native churches emerged that have no physical building. These communities stream worship services, host small groups on Zoom, manage prayer requests via apps, and build community through chat and social platforms. The challenge is fundamentally different from in-person church: there's no physical connection, engagement is passive by default (someone can watch a stream without interacting), and the "front door" is a livestream that must compete with Netflix. Yet the tools are repurposed consumer platforms — YouTube for streaming, Facebook Groups for community, Venmo for giving — none designed for the unique needs of digital spiritual community.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Church Online Platform (Life.Church) provides a branded streaming experience with chat and prayer request features. Subsplash offers church apps with streaming. Zoom handles small groups. However, no platform provides the full online church experience: live worship with participatory elements (not just passive viewing), digital small group management with spiritual growth tracking, online giving integrated with the worship experience, and engagement analytics that distinguish between "watched 3 minutes and left" and "engaged for 60 minutes with chat interaction."
