# Simplified Giving Statement Generator

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/small-rural-congregations/profile|Small Rural Congregations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks and Excel can track donations, but generating IRS-compliant year-end giving statements for 50-100 donors is a 20-hour ordeal for volunteer treasurers who don't understand tax deductibility rules for non-cash gifts, building use, and designated funds.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Every January, small church treasurers must produce year-end giving statements for all donors — required by the IRS for tax-deductible contributions over $250. For a church receiving $150K in annual donations from 60-80 households, this means reconciling 12 months of offerings (cash, check, online, stock transfers, non-cash gifts), applying tax deductibility rules (are designated mission trip funds tax-deductible? Is the value of dinner at the fundraiser gala deductible?), and mailing individual statements. Volunteer treasurers — who are not accountants — spend 15-25 hours in January doing this, often making errors that could cause IRS compliance issues for both the church and the donor.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks tracks income by donor and category. Planning Center Giving and Breeze generate giving statements for their users. Free spreadsheet templates exist online. But QuickBooks requires accounting knowledge, church management platforms cost $50-$100/month (overkill for a church that only needs giving statements), and spreadsheet templates don't handle the nuances (partial deductibility for benefit events, non-cash gift valuation, quid pro quo contributions).

## The Customization Gap
The missing product is a standalone giving statement tool (not a full church management platform) that costs $20-$30/year (annual subscription timed to tax season), lets the treasurer enter donations from a simple list (date, donor, amount, method, fund), handles IRS-compliant language for different contribution types, and generates print-ready or email-ready statements in January. The tool should include built-in guidance: "This dinner ticket was $50 — the fair market value of the meal was $25 — the deductible amount is $25."

## Target Customer
Volunteer treasurers at 200,000+ small churches who currently produce giving statements using Excel or handwritten records.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces year-end giving statement preparation from 20 hours to 3 hours, eliminates IRS compliance risk from incorrect deductibility language, and gives donors accurate statements 2-3 weeks earlier — improving donor satisfaction and encouraging continued giving.
