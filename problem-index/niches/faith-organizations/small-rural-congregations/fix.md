# Treasurer Transition Knowledge Loss

**Niche:** [[niches/faith-organizations/small-rural-congregations/profile|Small Rural Congregations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/faith-organizations|Faith Organizations]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When a volunteer treasurer retires after 15 years, the church's financial institutional knowledge — budget history, designated fund balances, vendor relationships, and payroll procedures — walks out the door in a box of paper files.
**Tags:** #data-integration #quick-win #worker-facing #automation

## The Problem
Small church treasurers serve for 5-20 years, accumulating knowledge that exists nowhere else: which insurance company gives the best rates for small churches, how to file the annual clergy housing allowance designation, where the mortgage documents are stored, which designated fund was set up for the building roof and how much remains. When the treasurer steps down (retirement, health, relocation), the incoming treasurer inherits a box of files, a checkbook register, and maybe an Excel spreadsheet — with no context about why things are done the way they are. The transition takes 3-6 months of confused phone calls between old and new treasurers.

## Why It's Still Broken
Small churches have no documentation culture — the treasurer is the documentation. There's no motivation to document processes when the same person has done them for 15 years. Accounting software would help, but the outgoing treasurer often resists learning new tools in their final years of service, and the incoming treasurer inherits whatever system (or non-system) was in place. The knowledge gap is most acute around compliance tasks that happen annually (W-2s for the pastor, 1099s for contractors, property tax exemption renewals) and are easily forgotten by a new treasurer who has never done them.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A "church treasurer handbook" tool — a guided, fill-in-the-blank digital document specific to the church that captures: annual financial calendar (when to file what), account information (banks, insurance, vendors), designated fund purposes and balances, payroll procedures (housing allowance, SECA tax), and recurring financial obligations. The outgoing treasurer completes it as part of their transition, and the incoming treasurer has a searchable reference. Includes automated reminders for annual compliance tasks.

## Who Feels the Pain
Incoming volunteer treasurers who accept the role out of duty and spend 6 months in anxiety, worried they'll miss a tax filing or lose track of a designated fund, with no one to ask because the previous treasurer moved to Florida.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces treasurer transition period from 6 months to 6 weeks, prevents the 2-3 missed compliance deadlines (W-2 filing, insurance renewal, property tax exemption) that commonly occur during transitions, and makes the treasurer role less intimidating — increasing the pool of willing volunteers.
