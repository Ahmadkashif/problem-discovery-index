# Post-CAT Accounts Receivable Collection Crisis

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/storm-cat/profile|Storm & Catastrophe Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Restoration companies complete CAT work in 30-90 days but wait 6-18 months for carrier payment, creating cash flow crises that force profitable companies into borrowing or bankruptcy.
**Tags:** #time-series-forecasting #tabular-ml #revenue-impact #data-integration

## The Problem
After a CAT event, a restoration company may complete $5M-$20M in emergency services and restoration work within 90 days. Payment from carriers arrives in 6-18 months, after estimates are reviewed, supplements are negotiated, and claims are adjudicated. The company must pay labor (deployed crews from other states at premium rates), materials (purchased at disaster-zone prices), and equipment (rental costs during deployment) within 30-60 days — while waiting months for carrier reimbursement. This cash flow gap has bankrupted restoration companies after their most profitable years. Companies that survived Hurricane Ian in 2022 reported $10M+ in AR aging over 12 months.

## Why It's Still Broken
Carriers process CAT claims sequentially, and after a major event, claim adjuster bandwidth is overwhelmed. The restorer's invoices sit in a queue behind thousands of other claims. There's no priority mechanism for restorer invoices — a $200K restoration invoice is processed with the same urgency as a $5K personal property claim. Restorers can't accelerate payment without dedicated collections staff, which they don't maintain because CAT events are cyclical.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A post-CAT AR management system that categorizes outstanding invoices by carrier, aging bucket, supplement status, and collection probability. Automates carrier follow-up with status-appropriate communications (initial inquiry, escalation, demand letter, state insurance department complaint). Identifies invoices that are stalled due to specific blockers (missing documentation, pending supplement, adjuster assignment gap) and provides the specific resolution action for each. Integrates with factoring/financing providers to enable selective invoice financing for invoices with high collection probability but long expected timelines.

## Who Feels the Pain
Restoration company owners who fund $5M-$20M in post-CAT receivables from operating cash flow or lines of credit. Office managers who spend 40-60 hours per week on CAT AR follow-up calls for 6-12 months after each event.

## Impact If Fixed
Accelerates average CAT AR collection by 30-60 days. Reduces write-off rates from 5-8% to 1-2% through systematic follow-up. Provides cash flow visibility that enables more informed financing decisions and prevents the cash crises that bankrupt otherwise profitable companies.
