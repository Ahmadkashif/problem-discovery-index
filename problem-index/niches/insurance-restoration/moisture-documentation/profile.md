# Moisture Mapping & Documentation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most moisture mapping is done on paper floor plans with hand-drawn symbols and moisture meter readings written in pen. Digital moisture maps exist but require manual data entry from handheld meters.
**Target Buyer:** Restoration Technician / Project Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Moisture readings are objective numerical data points that follow physical drying models. The entire documentation workflow (reading, recording, mapping, trending, reporting) is automatable with connected sensors.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Moisture documentation is the evidentiary foundation of water damage mitigation claims. Every dollar of equipment rental and labor billed to the carrier must be justified by moisture readings showing that materials were wet and required drying. Without proper moisture documentation, carriers deny mitigation invoices — the second most common reason for water damage payment disputes after "excessive drying time." Yet the documentation process is absurdly manual: a technician walks through the property with a moisture meter, takes readings at 20-50 points, writes each reading on a paper floor plan, photographs the meter display at each point, and later transfers all data into a digital format for the drying report.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Protimeter, Tramex, and Delmhorst manufacture professional moisture meters ($200-$2,000 each). DASH (Drying Academy Smart Hub) provides a digital moisture documentation platform. Encircle handles general restoration documentation. The gap is in the data capture step — connecting moisture meter readings directly to digital floor plans without manual transcription, and auto-generating the drying curves, psychrometric calculations, and IICRC-compliant reports that carriers require.
