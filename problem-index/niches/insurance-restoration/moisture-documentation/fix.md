# Inconsistent Moisture Reading Protocols

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/moisture-documentation/profile|Moisture Mapping & Documentation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Different technicians take moisture readings at different locations, different heights, different times of day, and using different meter types — making readings across visits incomparable and drying curves unreliable.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #worker-facing

## The Problem
IICRC S500 requires moisture readings at consistent monitoring points throughout the drying process. In practice, technician A sets up 30 monitoring points on day 1, but technician B (covering the weekend shift) takes readings at only 20 of the 30 points — and not the same 20 technician A would prioritize. Readings at the same point vary based on meter type (pin versus pinless), probe depth (surface versus 1-inch penetration), reading location on the material (center of drywall panel versus near a stud), and time of day (morning readings in an unheated structure differ from afternoon readings). The result is drying curves with gaps, outliers, and inconsistencies that carriers use to challenge the drying timeline.

## Why It's Still Broken
Monitoring point locations are marked on paper floor plans with imprecise descriptions ("living room wall, 3 feet from corner"). When a different technician visits, they approximate the location and may read a different spot. There's no physical marking system that survives a multi-day drying project (tape falls off, chalk washes away, pen marks are covered by equipment). Reading protocols (which meter, which mode, which depth) are communicated verbally during crew handoffs and inconsistently applied.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A monitoring point management system that defines each point with specific coordinates (photograph of the exact location with measurement from reference points), specifies the reading protocol for that point (meter type, mode, depth), and ensures every technician takes readings at every defined point during every visit. The system uses visual waypoints (app displays the reference photo so the technician can match the exact location) and protocol reminders (meter mode, probe depth) at each point. Flags missing points and out-of-protocol readings immediately, not after the drying report is assembled.

## Who Feels the Pain
Project managers who discover documentation gaps when assembling drying reports — after the equipment has been picked up and re-reads are impossible. Technicians who are blamed for incomplete documentation when they weren't given clear monitoring point locations during shift handoffs.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces drying documentation gaps from 15-25% of monitoring points to under 3%. Eliminates carrier challenges based on inconsistent readings, saving $500-$2,000 per disputed job. Improves technician accountability and consistency, reducing drying report revision rate by 50%.
