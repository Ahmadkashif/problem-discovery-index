# Xactimate Supplement Negotiation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Supplements are written in Xactimate and submitted via email/carrier portal, but the comparison, justification, and tracking workflow is entirely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Supplement Specialist / Office Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — Supplement writing follows predictable patterns where carrier objections map to standard justification arguments, and estimate comparison is a mechanical data-matching task.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Insurance restoration companies lose 20-40% of their initial estimate to carrier reductions. Recovering this gap through supplements (additional documentation justifying the original scope or identifying additional damage) is a distinct skill that some companies handle in-house and others outsource to supplement specialists. The supplement process — comparing the carrier's revised estimate to the original, identifying every discrepancy, writing justification narratives for each disputed item, and tracking multi-round negotiations — is sufficiently complex and time-consuming that it has spawned an entire cottage industry of supplement writing services.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Xactimate is used to write supplement estimates. Carrier portals receive submissions. Email handles communication. Supplement writing services (ActionAble, Supplement Experts) outsource the work for 5-10% of recovered amount. No tool automates the core workflow: import two Xactimate files, auto-compare line by line, identify every discrepancy, and generate item-level justification narratives based on IICRC standards and Xactimate best practices.
