# Large-Loss Documentation and Carrier Reporting Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/commercial-industrial/profile|Commercial & Industrial Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Construction project management platforms handle scheduling and budgeting, but none produce the carrier-specific documentation packages (progress reports, photo documentation, cost tracking against estimate) required for large commercial insurance claims.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
Large commercial insurance claims ($500K-$10M) require structured reporting to the carrier throughout the project: weekly progress reports with photos mapped to affected areas, cost tracking against the approved estimate with variance explanations, change order documentation with carrier pre-approval, and milestone completion certifications. Carriers may have different reporting templates and frequency requirements. The restorer produces these reports manually — pulling data from their project management system, selecting and organizing photos, writing narrative summaries, and formatting documents to carrier specifications. A $5M commercial restoration generates 200-400 hours of reporting work over the project lifecycle.

## What Already Exists
Construction project management platforms (Procore, Buildertrend, CoConstruct) track schedules, budgets, and documents. Insurance claim documentation platforms (XactAnalysis) facilitate estimate submission and review. Neither auto-generates the structured progress reports that carriers require — reports that connect project management data (% complete, cost to date) with insurance claim data (estimate line items, approved supplements, remaining coverage).

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a reporting layer that maps project management data to insurance claim documentation requirements: auto-generates weekly progress reports by pulling schedule updates, budget data, and tagged photos from the project management platform; formats them to carrier-specific templates; tracks cost against the approved estimate line by line; and flags items approaching the approved amount that will require supplements. Must support multiple carrier formats simultaneously for the same project.

## Target Customer
Commercial restoration firms managing 5-15 active large-loss projects simultaneously, spending 10-20 hours per project per month on carrier reporting.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces carrier reporting time by 60-70% (from 15 hours to 5 hours per project per month). Improves carrier satisfaction through consistent, professional reporting. Reduces supplement delays by proactively identifying cost overruns before they exceed approved amounts.
