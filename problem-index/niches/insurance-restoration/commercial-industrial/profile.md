**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 13%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Large commercial restorers use project management platforms, but the specialized requirements of industrial restoration (hazmat compliance, business continuity coordination, large-loss documentation) are handled through manual processes.
**Target Buyer:** Commercial Restoration Firm Owner
**Automation Potential:** Medium — The restoration work requires specialized skills and equipment, but project coordination, compliance documentation, and carrier communication are automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial and industrial restoration handles losses at factories, warehouses, office buildings, hospitals, and retail spaces — where the restoration must accommodate ongoing business operations, specialized building systems (clean rooms, server rooms, manufacturing lines), and regulatory requirements (EPA, OSHA, hazmat). A single commercial loss can range from $500K to $50M. The work involves coordinating general contractors, specialty subcontractors (electrical, mechanical, roofing), environmental consultants, and business continuity planners. Most residential-focused restoration companies lack the project management capability, financial capacity, and specialized certifications to handle large commercial work.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Commercial restorers use construction project management tools (Procore, Buildertrend) adapted from general contracting, alongside Xactimate for insurance estimates. Large-loss documentation platforms (XactAnalysis, ClaimXperience) facilitate carrier communication. The gap is in business continuity coordination — no tool helps the restorer plan and execute restoration while the business continues operating, managing the interaction between restoration activities and business operations (noise restrictions during trading hours, air quality in occupied spaces, after-hours-only access requirements).
