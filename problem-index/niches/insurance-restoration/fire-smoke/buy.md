# Contents Pack-Out Inventory with Insurance-Ready Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/fire-smoke/profile|Fire & Smoke Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Inventory management tools exist, but none handle the insurance restoration-specific workflow of packing out contents, documenting pre-loss condition, tracking cleaning/restoration status, and generating carrier-approved inventory reports.
**Tags:** #cnn #object-detection #computer-vision #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
Fire restoration requires "pack-out" — removing all contents from the property for off-site cleaning, deodorization, or storage. Each item must be individually photographed, described, condition-assessed (salvageable, non-salvageable, questionable), packed in numbered boxes, and tracked through cleaning, storage, and eventual pack-back. A 3-bedroom home produces 50-100 boxes containing 1,000-3,000 items. The documentation must satisfy both the carrier (who wants to minimize contents claims) and the policyholder (who wants everything accounted for). Current pack-out documentation takes 2-3 days of labor per home.

## What Already Exists
Inventory management platforms (Sortly, inFlow) track items with barcodes and photos. Contents documentation tools (Encircle) provide mobile-first capture for restoration. Neither handles the full insurance pack-out lifecycle: pre-cleaning photo, condition assessment, cleaning method selection, post-cleaning photo, salvageability determination, and formatted reporting that meets carrier documentation standards for contents claims.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a pack-out workflow engine designed for insurance requirements: each item flows through a lifecycle (photograph → assess → pack → clean → re-photograph → determine salvageability → report) with documentation at each stage that satisfies carrier review. Must generate formatted reports showing before/after photos, cleaning methods applied, and salvageability determinations with rationale. Integration with Xactimate for contents claim line items.

## Target Customer
Restoration companies with contents divisions handling 10-30 fire loss pack-outs per year, each producing 1,000+ item inventories.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces pack-out documentation time by 40-50% (from 3 days to 1.5-2 days per home). Improves carrier approval rates for contents claims by providing standardized, complete documentation. Reduces disputes over "missing" items — full barcode tracking proves chain of custody from pack-out to pack-back.
