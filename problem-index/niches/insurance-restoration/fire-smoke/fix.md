# Scope Creep from Undiscovered Damage During Demolition

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/fire-smoke/profile|Fire & Smoke Restoration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Restorers begin work based on visible damage estimates, then discover additional damage behind walls and above ceilings during demolition — requiring supplements that carriers resist and that delay project completion by weeks.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Fire damage is rarely limited to what's visible. When a restorer opens walls, they find charred framing, melted wiring, damaged plumbing, and smoke-saturated insulation that wasn't in the original estimate. Each discovery requires stopping work, documenting the new damage with photos and measurements, writing a supplement estimate in Xactimate, submitting to the carrier, and waiting for approval before proceeding. A typical fire restoration job requires 3-5 supplements, each adding 1-3 weeks of delay while the carrier reviews. A $200K fire loss that should take 4 months takes 8-12 months because of supplement delays.

## Why It's Still Broken
Carriers designed the supplement process to prevent scope creep and fraud — they want to approve each additional cost before it's incurred. Restorers can't pre-scope hidden damage without destructive investigation, which the carrier won't authorize until the initial estimate is approved. This creates a sequential approval process where each discovery triggers a new cycle of documentation, submission, carrier review, and approval. There's no mechanism for carrier pre-authorization of a damage range ("we expect $50K-$80K in hidden damage based on fire type and structure age") that would allow work to continue within boundaries.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A predictive supplement scoping tool that, based on the initial damage assessment (fire origin, spread pattern, structure type, age), estimates the probability and cost range of hidden damage by category (electrical, plumbing, framing, insulation). This prediction, supported by data from hundreds of similar fire losses, can be presented to the carrier at the initial estimate stage to negotiate a "discovery allowance" — pre-authorized funding for expected hidden damage. The restorer proceeds with work and documents discoveries against the allowance, submitting a reconciliation supplement at project end rather than stopping work for each discovery.

## Who Feels the Pain
Restoration project managers who spend 20-30% of their time on supplement documentation and carrier negotiation. Policyholders who live in temporary housing for months longer than necessary because supplements delay construction progress.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average fire restoration project duration by 30-40% through fewer supplement delays. Recovers 5-10% of total project value that restorers write off when they proceed with work before supplement approval and the carrier later disputes the scope.
