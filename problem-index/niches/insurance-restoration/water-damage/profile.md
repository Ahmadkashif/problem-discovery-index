# Water Damage Mitigation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 26%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most restorers use DASH/Next Gear for job management and Xactimate for estimates, but moisture monitoring, drying documentation, and equipment tracking remain largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Restoration Company Owner
**Automation Potential:** High — Drying protocols follow IICRC S500 standards with measurable inputs (moisture readings, temperature, humidity) that can be automated with IoT sensors and decision engines.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Water damage mitigation is the highest-volume restoration category — a typical restoration company handles 200-500 water losses per year. The work follows a structured protocol: emergency water extraction, moisture mapping, equipment placement (dehumidifiers, air movers), daily moisture monitoring, and documentation until dry standard is achieved. Insurance carriers pay for equipment rental by the day, creating an economic tension: restorers want equipment on-site longer (more revenue), carriers want it removed sooner (less cost). The documentation burden is enormous — daily moisture readings across 20-50 monitoring points per job, equipment logs, psychrometric calculations, and photo documentation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
DASH (by Drying Academy), Next Gear Solutions, and MICA provide job management. Xactimate handles estimates. Moisture meters (Tramex, Protimeter) take readings manually. The gap is in automated moisture monitoring — no system continuously monitors moisture levels, auto-generates drying curves, determines when dry standard is achieved, and produces carrier-ready documentation without manual data entry.
