# Equipment Tracking and Utilization Optimizer

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/water-damage/profile|Water Damage Mitigation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Asset tracking platforms exist, but none handle the unique lifecycle of restoration drying equipment — deployment to job sites, daily runtime tracking, return and maintenance scheduling — integrated with per-job billing and carrier reimbursement.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A restoration company owns 200-1,000 pieces of drying equipment (dehumidifiers, air movers, air scrubbers) worth $500K-$2M. Equipment is deployed across 30-80 active job sites, moved between sites as drying is completed, and returned to the warehouse for maintenance. Carriers reimburse equipment rental at IICRC rates ($50-$200/day per unit). Restorers routinely lose 10-15% of equipment revenue because they can't track which equipment is on which site, whether equipment was billed for every day it was deployed, and when equipment should be removed (because the area dried days ago but nobody picked it up).

## What Already Exists
Asset tracking platforms (Asset Panda, GoCodes, Sortly) track equipment location via barcode or Bluetooth. Fleet management tools track vehicles. Neither integrates equipment deployment with job-specific billing — the restorer needs to know "Dehumidifier #47 was deployed to 123 Main St on March 1, ran for 8 days, should have been picked up on day 6 when that area dried, and should be billed to Claim #2024-0847 for 6 days at $75/day."

## The Customization Gap
The gap is connecting equipment tracking to job management and billing: each piece of equipment must be associated with a specific job, a specific affected area within that job, and billed at the carrier-approved rate for the documented drying period. The system should flag equipment that's been on-site longer than the drying documentation supports (revenue risk if the carrier disputes) and equipment that dried early (pickup opportunity to redeploy to another job).

## Target Customer
Restoration companies with $1M+ in annual equipment inventory managing 50+ simultaneous water damage jobs.

## Impact If Solved
Recovers 8-12% of lost equipment revenue through complete billing capture. Reduces equipment idle time by 20-30% through timely pickup and redeployment. For a company with $2M in annual equipment revenue, recovers $160K-$240K in previously unbilled equipment days.
