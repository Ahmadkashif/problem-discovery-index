# Multifamily & Property Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Property management companies use platforms like AppFolio and Yardi, but restoration coordination is handled through phone calls and email with no integration between property management and restoration workflows.
**Target Buyer:** Property Manager / Restoration Owner
**Automation Potential:** Medium — The restoration work itself is standard, but the coordination between property management, tenant communication, insurance carrier, and restoration company involves unique multi-party workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multifamily restoration (apartments, condos, senior living) involves unique challenges that single-family restorers aren't equipped for: multiple affected units from a single event (a 3rd-floor pipe burst affects 10 units below), tenant displacement coordination (temporary housing, access scheduling), property management company communication requirements (daily updates to portfolio managers), and complex insurance structures (property owner's policy, individual renter's insurance, liability coverage). A single water event in a 200-unit apartment building can generate $500K in restoration work across 15 units — but requires coordinating with 15 tenants' schedules, the property management company's maintenance team, and the building owner's insurance carrier simultaneously.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Restoration job management platforms (Next Gear, PSA) handle individual jobs. Property management platforms (AppFolio, Yardi, RealPage) handle building operations. Neither bridges the gap — the property manager submits a work order, the restoration company creates a job, and all coordination happens through phone and email. For a 15-unit water event, this means 15 separate phone conversations with tenants about access scheduling, daily email updates to the property manager, and carrier-specific documentation requirements — all managed manually.
