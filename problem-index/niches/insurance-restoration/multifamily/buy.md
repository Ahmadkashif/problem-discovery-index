# Tenant Communication and Access Scheduling Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/multifamily/profile|Multifamily & Property Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Scheduling and communication platforms exist, but none handle the specific requirements of coordinating restoration access across multiple apartment units — tenant availability windows, pet/child accommodations, noise restrictions, and sequential room-by-room scheduling.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Restoring 15 affected units in an apartment building requires scheduling crew access to each unit around tenant availability (most tenants work during the day and need evening/weekend access), building quiet hours (no demolition before 8 AM or after 6 PM), pet containment requirements (tenants must secure pets before crews enter), and sequential workflow dependencies (dry unit 301 before restoring unit 201 below). The restoration project manager coordinates this through phone calls to each tenant — scheduling, rescheduling when tenants cancel, and managing the cascading impact of one tenant's schedule change on the entire project sequence.

## What Already Exists
Appointment scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) handle one-to-one scheduling. Property management tenant communication tools (AppFolio portal, resident apps) send notices and collect responses. Field service scheduling platforms (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro) manage crew dispatch. None handle the multi-unit, multi-tenant coordination specific to restoration — where 15 tenants' schedules must be simultaneously optimized against crew availability, workflow sequencing, and building rules.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a multi-tenant scheduling optimizer that sends each affected tenant a digital access request (via text/email), collects their availability windows, optimizes the restoration schedule across all units to minimize crew idle time and project duration, and sends automated reminders with preparation instructions ("Please secure pets and remove items from kitchen counters before crew arrival at 9 AM"). Updates the schedule automatically when tenants reschedule, showing the cascade impact on other units.

## Target Customer
Restoration project managers handling 3-5 simultaneous multifamily events with 5-20 affected units each.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces tenant scheduling coordination time by 70% (from 3 hours to 45 minutes per event per week). Reduces project duration by 15-20% through optimized scheduling. Eliminates missed access appointments that cost $200-$500 per occurrence in wasted crew dispatch.
