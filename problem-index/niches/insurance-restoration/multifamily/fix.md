# Multi-Unit Insurance Allocation Confusion

**Niche:** [[niches/insurance-restoration/multifamily/profile|Multifamily & Property Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/insurance-restoration|Insurance Restoration]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When a single water event damages 15 apartment units, the restorer must split costs between the building owner's property policy and each affected tenant's renter's insurance — and no standard methodology exists for allocating shared costs like emergency response and common-area drying.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A pipe burst in a multifamily building triggers one emergency response but involves multiple insurance policies: the building owner's commercial property policy covers structural damage and common areas, each tenant's renter's insurance covers personal property, and the allocation of shared costs (emergency water extraction, building drying equipment, project management) between policies is ambiguous. The restorer writes one estimate for the total job but must allocate line items across policies for billing. There is no standard for how to allocate shared costs — some carriers accept a square-footage allocation, others demand a unit-by-unit breakdown, and some refuse to pay shared costs entirely ("that's the building owner's responsibility").

## Why It's Still Broken
Each carrier has different allocation expectations, and there's no industry standard. The restorer doesn't know which carriers cover which tenants until after the work is complete. Writing separate estimates for each policy is impractical during emergency response (the focus is on stopping water damage, not determining insurance allocation). By the time billing begins, the shared cost allocation question creates disputes between the building owner's carrier, tenant carriers, and the restorer — with each carrier arguing the cost belongs to another policy.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An allocation framework that defines standard methodologies for splitting multi-unit restoration costs: emergency services allocated by affected square footage, common-area work allocated to the building owner's policy, unit-specific work allocated to the respective renter's or building owner's policy, and shared equipment allocated proportionally. The framework produces a formatted allocation schedule that accompanies each carrier's invoice, with methodology documentation and supporting calculations. Pre-agreed allocation templates for common scenarios reduce post-work disputes.

## Who Feels the Pain
Restoration company billing staff who spend 10-20 hours per multi-unit event creating allocation breakdowns and negotiating with multiple carriers. Building owners whose insurance premiums increase when shared costs aren't properly allocated to responsible parties.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces multi-unit billing disputes by 50-60%, accelerating payment by 30-60 days. Recovers $2,000-$5,000 per multi-unit event in shared costs that are currently written off when no carrier accepts responsibility. Saves 10-20 hours of billing staff time per multi-unit event.
