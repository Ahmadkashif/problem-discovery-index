## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mold remediation sits at the intersection of restoration, environmental science, and insurance coverage disputes. Unlike water or fire damage, mold is often a secondary condition that develops after a water loss — and carriers frequently deny mold claims under policy exclusions. The work requires industrial hygienist assessments (pre- and post-remediation air sampling), containment construction, HEPA filtration, antimicrobial treatment, and clearance testing. Regulatory requirements vary by state — some states require licensed mold assessors and remediators, others have no regulations. The insurance documentation must prove that the mold resulted from a covered loss (sudden water damage, not long-term neglect) to overcome coverage exclusions.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No purpose-built mold remediation software exists. Remediators use generic job management tools (Jobber, ServiceTitan) designed for home services, not mold-specific workflows. Environmental testing labs provide results in PDF reports with no integration into the remediation workflow. Moisture mapping is done on paper floor plans. The entire niche operates on disconnected paper and PDF workflows.
