# Small-Balance Portfolio Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/collections-agencies|Collections Agencies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$1.5B in small-balance debt (under $500 per account) placed with or purchased by third-party agencies
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — small-balance portfolios are often worked with the same tools as large-balance accounts, but the economics do not justify per-account agent attention; digital-first collection channels are underutilized
**Target Buyer:** Portfolio buyer or owner-operator at agencies specializing in high-volume, low-balance debt
**Automation Potential:** High — the unit economics of small-balance collections require near-complete automation of debtor contact and payment processing to be profitable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small-balance debt (medical copays, utility arrears, parking tickets, gym memberships, subscription chargebacks) is unprofitable to collect through traditional agent-driven workflows. At $200-$500 per account, even a 5-minute phone call costs more than the likely recovery. These portfolios are purchased at 1-3 cents on the dollar and must be worked through automated channels — SMS, email, online payment portals, and automated voice — to achieve any margin. The regulatory environment is tightening: the CFPB's Regulation F imposes specific rules for electronic communications, and the major credit bureaus no longer report medical debts under $500.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Predictive dialers are designed for agent-assisted calls, not for autonomous digital-first collection. Payment portal software exists but is not optimized for debtor self-service on small balances (no streamlined dispute resolution, no settlement offer engine). SMS and email collection platforms (TrueAccord, now part of TransUnion) have emerged but are not available as white-label tools for independent agencies.
