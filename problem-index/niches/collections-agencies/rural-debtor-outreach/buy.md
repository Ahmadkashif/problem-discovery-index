# In-Person Payment Network for Non-Digital Debtors

**Niche:** [[niches/collections-agencies/rural-debtor-outreach/profile|Rural & Low-Connectivity Debtor Outreach]]
**Industry:** [[industries/collections-agencies|Collections Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Cash payment networks (PayNearMe, MoneyGram, Western Union) exist but are not integrated into collection platform payment workflows for debtors who cannot or will not pay online.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A significant portion of rural debtors lack reliable broadband access or prefer cash-based transactions. When an agent reaches a willing debtor who says "I can pay but I don't do online payments," the agent must manually provide payment mailing instructions and wait 7-14 days for a check — during which the debtor may change their mind. Cash payment networks exist at retail locations (Walmart, Dollar General, CVS) in most rural areas, but collection agencies have not integrated these networks into their payment workflow.

## What Already Exists
PayNearMe enables cash payments at 60,000+ retail locations. MoneyGram and Western Union offer similar capabilities. Bill payment kiosks are available at most grocery stores and pharmacies. Collection payment platforms (RevSpring, PaymentVision) support online payments and ACH. These two ecosystems — cash networks and collection platforms — are not connected.

## The Customization Gap
Integrating a cash payment network into the collection workflow requires generating unique payment barcodes or reference numbers per debtor, transmitting these via mail or SMS (since the debtor may not have email), confirming payment receipt in the collection platform in near-real-time, and handling partial payments and settlement amounts at the point of sale. The cash network's API must be integrated with the collection platform's payment posting and account closure workflow.

## Target Customer
Agencies working portfolios where 10-20% of debtors are in areas with limited broadband, or where the debtor demographic skews toward cash-preference populations (unbanked/underbanked, elderly, low-income).

## Impact If Solved
Converts 5-10% of "willing but unable to pay online" debtors into immediate payers. Reduces payment lag from 7-14 days (mail a check) to same-day (walk to the nearest Dollar General). For a 100,000-account portfolio, this could yield $500K-$1M in additional annual recoveries.
