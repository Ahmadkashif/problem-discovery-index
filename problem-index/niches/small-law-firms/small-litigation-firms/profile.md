# Small Litigation Firms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~27%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most use Clio or PracticePanther for case management, but discovery management, litigation hold tracking, and cost projection are handled in Excel spreadsheets and shared drives
**Target Buyer:** Managing partner at a 3-10 attorney civil litigation firm handling commercial disputes, employment claims, insurance defense, or collections
**Automation Potential:** High — litigation follows predictable phases (pleading, discovery, depositions, motions, trial prep, trial) with deadline-driven workflows that are ideal for automation but currently managed via manual calendar entries

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small litigation firms (3-10 attorneys) handle civil disputes where the workflow is fundamentally different from transactional practice. Litigation is deadline-intensive: court-imposed discovery cutoffs, deposition schedules, motion filing deadlines, and trial dates create cascading dependencies where missing one deadline can result in sanctions, default judgments, or malpractice claims. These firms carry 15-30 active cases per attorney across multiple courts and jurisdictions, each with different local rules, filing requirements, and e-filing systems. They leverage associates and paralegals more heavily than solos — the managing partner supervises work product rather than producing it directly — which creates workflow coordination challenges absent in solo practice. The business model is hourly billing with occasional contingency arrangements, and client sophistication varies from unsophisticated individual plaintiffs to corporate clients with outside counsel billing guidelines that dictate time entry format, staffing ratios, and budget caps.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Clio handles basic case management but lacks litigation-specific features: no built-in litigation hold management, no court-rule-aware deadline calculation, no discovery tracking (who produced what, what's outstanding, what's been reviewed). Dedicated litigation tools exist — Litify (Salesforce-based, targets mid-market), CaseFleet (timeline and fact management), Logikcull and Relativity (e-discovery) — but each solves one piece at enterprise pricing ($500-2,000/month per user). Small litigation firms need the functionality of these enterprise tools at a price point appropriate for a 5-attorney firm. The critical gap is litigation budget management: these firms often work under client-imposed budgets or fee arrangements that require tracking costs against case value, and no affordable tool projects costs by litigation phase.
