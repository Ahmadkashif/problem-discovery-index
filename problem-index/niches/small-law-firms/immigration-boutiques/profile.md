# Immigration Boutiques

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most small immigration firms use INSZoom or Docketwise for immigration case tracking but rely on manual systems for the small-firm business operations (billing, profitability analysis, client acquisition) that determine whether the practice survives
**Target Buyer:** Small immigration firm owner (1-3 attorneys) managing 100-200+ active cases per attorney with heavy paralegal leverage
**Automation Potential:** High — immigration case processing follows well-defined government workflows with predictable milestones, but the business operations layer (flat-fee profitability tracking, paralegal utilization, case type portfolio optimization) is entirely unautomated

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Immigration boutiques sit at the intersection of two complexity domains: immigration law (covered in detail in the immigration-law industry analysis) and small law firm operations. The operational challenges compound: these firms typically charge flat fees ($3K-15K per case depending on type), carry 100-200+ active cases per attorney with 2-4 paralegals per attorney, serve a client base that is largely non-English-speaking, and face case timelines measured in months to years (not weeks like litigation). The flat-fee model means every hour of unexpected work (RFE responses, additional family member petitions, changes in case strategy) directly erodes profitability — but no firm tracks actual time against flat fees because "we don't bill hourly." The result is that immigration boutique owners have no idea which case types are profitable and which are money-losers. A firm might discover that asylum cases consume 3x the expected paralegal hours only after taking on 20 of them. The small-firm perspective — how to run a profitable, sustainable immigration practice — is distinct from the immigration-law-specific workflow challenges.

## Current Tools & Gaps
INSZoom and Docketwise handle immigration-specific case management: form preparation, deadline tracking, government processing time estimates, and client portals. They are excellent at the immigration workflow but completely ignore the business of running a small firm. Clio handles billing and practice management but doesn't understand immigration-specific workflows. No tool bridges the gap: tracking actual time and cost per case type against flat-fee revenue to identify which case categories are profitable, measuring paralegal utilization and quality (RFE rate as a proxy for petition quality), or optimizing the firm's case type portfolio for maximum profitability.
