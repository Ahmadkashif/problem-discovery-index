# Family Law Practices

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~13%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most family law attorneys use generic practice management (Clio, MyCase) with no family-law-specific tooling for financial disclosure calculations, parenting plan drafting, or child support worksheets
**Target Buyer:** Family law attorney or small family law firm (1-4 attorneys) handling divorce, custody, child support, and domestic violence cases
**Automation Potential:** High — financial disclosure assembly, child support calculations, and parenting plan scheduling involve repetitive, rule-based work that consumes 30-40% of attorney time per case

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Family law is the most emotionally intensive area of legal practice. Clients are going through the most stressful event of their lives (divorce, custody disputes, domestic violence) and communicate with their attorney at a volume and intensity unmatched by any other practice area. A family law attorney can receive 50+ emails and texts per week from a single client during an active contested divorce. The legal work itself combines financial complexity (asset division, retirement account valuation, business valuations, tax implications of settlement structures) with highly variable court procedures (each county may have its own required forms, parenting plan templates, and financial disclosure formats). Child support calculations follow state-specific formulas that incorporate income, custody percentages, health insurance costs, and childcare expenses — each state uses a different formula. The combination of extreme client emotional demands, financial calculation complexity, and jurisdiction-specific procedural variation creates a niche that generic practice management tools fundamentally cannot serve.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Clio and MyCase handle case management but have zero family-law-specific features. A few specialized tools exist: FamilyLawSoftware.com offers financial calculation tools for divorce, and SupportPay tracks child support payments. State bar associations provide child support calculator spreadsheets that are clunky and error-prone. No single platform combines financial disclosure preparation, child support calculation, parenting plan drafting, and client communication management in a way that matches the family law workflow. The largest gap is client communication management: family law attorneys spend 10-15 hours per week managing client emotions via email and text, with no tool to triage urgent messages from anxious noise.
