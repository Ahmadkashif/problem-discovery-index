# General Practice Solo Attorneys

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $35-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~37%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most solos use Clio or MyCase for basic case management but rely on memory, sticky notes, and Outlook calendar for time tracking, client communication, and deadline management
**Target Buyer:** Solo attorney handling multiple practice areas — family law, real estate closings, simple wills, minor criminal matters — who is simultaneously the lawyer, office manager, billing department, and receptionist
**Automation Potential:** Very High — the solo attorney has zero slack in their schedule, meaning any tool that eliminates administrative tasks directly converts to billable hours or personal time recovered

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Approximately 360,000 solo practitioners make up the largest segment of the US legal market. The defining characteristic is radical generalism combined with extreme resource constraints. A solo general practitioner might draft a will at 9am, appear in family court at 11am, review a real estate contract at 2pm, and handle a misdemeanor arraignment at 4pm — then spend 7pm to midnight reconstructing time entries, drafting invoices, and responding to client emails. They have no paralegal, no associate, no office manager. Practice management software like Clio is designed for firms with at least 2-3 attorneys and assumes role separation (timekeeper vs. billing administrator vs. calendar manager) that doesn't exist for solos. The result is that 25-35% of billable time goes uncaptured because the attorney is too busy doing work to record doing work. Buying decisions are intensely personal, budget-sensitive ($50-150/month ceiling for software), and driven by "does this save me time THIS WEEK" rather than long-term ROI analysis.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Clio dominates with ~35% market share among solos who use any practice management tool, but an estimated 40% of solos still rely on manual systems (Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, paper files). PracticePanther and MyCase compete on simplicity. LawPay handles credit card payments with trust account compliance. Calendly or Acuity handle scheduling. The critical gap is time capture: existing tools require the attorney to stop working and manually enter time, which solos skip when they're in court, on calls, or drafting documents. Ambient time capture — automatically detecting and categorizing legal work from calendar events, email activity, document editing, and phone calls — would recover the 25-35% of billable time that currently evaporates.
