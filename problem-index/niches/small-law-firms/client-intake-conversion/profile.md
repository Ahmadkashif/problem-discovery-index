# Client Intake & Lead Conversion

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B (embedded in practice operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~2-3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — chatbots and answering services exist but legal intake qualification is still human-dependent
**Target Buyer:** Attorney (who is also the intake department), receptionist, or virtual receptionist service
**Automation Potential:** Very High — intake qualification follows structured decision trees (practice area match, jurisdiction, statute of limitations, ability to pay) that are fully automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
When a potential client calls a small law firm, the firm has a 5-minute window to qualify them, capture their information, and schedule a consultation. 40-60% of leads are lost because: the phone goes to voicemail (attorney is in court), the receptionist can't do legal intake qualification, or the follow-up is delayed by hours/days. For PI firms on contingency, each lost qualified lead represents $5,000-$50,000 in potential fees. For hourly firms, it's a $2,000-$10,000 engagement. Small firms spend $2,000-$5,000/month on marketing but can't track which sources generate retained clients vs. tire-kickers.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Virtual receptionist services (Smith.ai, Ruby, LEX Reception) answer calls and take messages but don't do legal intake qualification — they can't assess whether the caller has a viable case. Chatbot tools (Drift, Intercom) handle web leads but aren't trained on legal intake criteria. Clio Grow offers basic intake forms but no real-time lead qualification or source attribution. No tool connects marketing spend → lead source → intake qualification → retained client → case outcome.
