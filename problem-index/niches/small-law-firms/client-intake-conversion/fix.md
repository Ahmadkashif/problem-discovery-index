# Lead Source Attribution and Marketing ROI

**Niche:** [[niches/small-law-firms/client-intake-conversion/profile|Client Intake & Lead Conversion]]
**Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Small firms stop wasting half their marketing budget on channels that generate calls but not retained clients — by finally connecting ad spend to actual case outcomes.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #decision-trees #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #cross-validation #descriptive-statistics #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Small law firms spend $2,000-$10,000/month on marketing across Google Ads, Avvo, FindLaw, social media, and local directories. They know how many calls each source generates (call tracking numbers), but they cannot track which sources produce leads that become retained clients that generate revenue. A source that produces 50 calls but zero retained clients is worse than a source that produces 5 calls and 3 retained clients. Without this attribution, firms allocate budget based on call volume rather than client value — systematically overspending on high-call, low-conversion channels.

## Why It's Still Broken
The attribution chain is broken: marketing platforms track clicks and calls, but the practice management system tracks matters and revenue. Nothing connects them. When a client calls from a Google Ad, gets qualified, retains the firm, and the case settles 18 months later, there's no system that connects the $15 click to the $15,000 fee. Building this requires integrating call tracking (CallRail), CRM/intake (Clio Grow), practice management (Clio), and billing — four separate systems with no native integration. Small firms don't have the technical capacity to build this integration.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An integrated marketing attribution pipeline that tracks: ad source → call/inquiry → intake qualification outcome → consultation → retained/not retained → matter opened → fees collected. Dashboard shows: cost per retained client by source, average case value by source, and ROI by marketing channel. The firm sees: "Google Ads costs $3,000/month and produced 4 retained clients worth $45,000 in fees. Avvo costs $1,500/month and produced 1 retained client worth $3,000 in fees." Budget reallocation becomes obvious.

## Who Feels the Pain
The firm owner/managing partner who signs marketing checks monthly with no ability to evaluate return. The marketing agency that can't demonstrate ROI to justify their fees.

## Impact If Fixed
Reallocating marketing budget from low-ROI to high-ROI channels typically saves 30-50% of marketing spend or doubles retained client count at the same budget. For a firm spending $5,000/month on marketing, this is $18,000-$30,000/year in savings or equivalent revenue growth.
