# Automated Collection Workflow for Clio/LawPay

**Niche:** [[niches/small-law-firms/billing-collections-management/profile|Billing & Collections Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/small-law-firms|Small Law Firms]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Clio generates invoices and LawPay processes payments, but nobody follows up when the invoice goes unpaid — the gap is an automated collection workflow that turns outstanding invoices into collected revenue without awkward attorney-client conversations.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
Small firm invoices go unpaid at alarming rates — 15-20% of billed time is never collected. The attorney sends an invoice through Clio, the client doesn't pay, and the attorney faces an uncomfortable choice: chase the client (damaging the relationship), write it off (losing revenue), or send to collections (nuclear option). Most choose passive-aggressive inaction — the invoice sits in AR aging while the attorney hopes the client pays eventually. There is no automated escalation path between "invoice sent" and "send to collections."

## What Already Exists
Clio generates and sends invoices with payment links. LawPay processes credit card and ACH payments with bar-compliant trust/operating separation. Clio shows AR aging reports. LawPay has basic payment reminders. Neither provides a structured collection workflow with escalation logic.

## The Customization Gap
Legal collections require sensitivity that generic AR automation doesn't have: you can't threaten a current client, you can't withdraw from a case for non-payment without court permission in many jurisdictions, and you must maintain client relationship quality. The customization is a legal-appropriate collection escalation sequence: friendly reminder (day 15) → firmer reminder with payment plan offer (day 30) → attorney notification with recommended action (day 45) → engagement letter modification or payment plan formalization (day 60). Each step respects the attorney-client relationship while systematically pursuing payment.

## Target Customer
Solo attorneys and small firms with $20,000-$100,000+ in outstanding AR who currently have no collection process beyond manually re-sending invoices.

## Impact If Solved
Collects 40-60% of currently written-off receivables. For a firm with $50,000 in outstanding AR, that's $20,000-$30,000 recovered. Ongoing: reduces AR aging from 90+ days to 30-45 days average.
