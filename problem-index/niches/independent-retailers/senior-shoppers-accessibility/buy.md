# Loyalty Program Adapted for Non-Digital Senior Customers

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-retailers/senior-shoppers-accessibility/profile|Accessibility-Focused Retail for Senior Shoppers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-retailers|Independent Retailers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Digital loyalty programs (Square Loyalty, Shopify loyaltyapps) require smartphone apps or email signups — senior customers want loyalty rewards but not app downloads, creating a gap that physical punch cards fill poorly.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Customer loyalty programs drive repeat visits and increased basket size — a 5% increase in retention can increase profits by 25-95%. Every major POS platform offers a digital loyalty module. But for retailers where 30-50% of customers are 65+, digital loyalty programs fail: seniors are less likely to download an app (30% of adults 65+ don't own a smartphone), less likely to use a phone-based points system at checkout, and less likely to engage with email marketing. The retailer either excludes their most loyal customers from their loyalty program or reverts to paper punch cards, which provide zero data and no ability to personalize offers.

## What Already Exists
Square Loyalty ($45/month) tracks points via phone number lookup at checkout. Shopify loyalty apps (Smile.io, LoyaltyLion) are app-based. FiveStars uses a physical card with a kiosk-based scan. These systems collect customer data through digital touchpoints — phone numbers, email addresses, or app installations — and deliver rewards through digital channels.

## The Customization Gap
Senior-accessible loyalty needs: (1) a physical loyalty card (not an app) that the cashier scans or swipes at checkout — the customer carries it in their wallet next to their grocery list, (2) phone number lookup as a backup (for customers who forget their card), (3) rewards communicated through the customer's preferred channel — printed on the receipt ("You have $5 in rewards — ask to apply at your next visit"), announced verbally by the cashier, or mailed as a physical coupon, (4) simplified reward structure (every $100 spent earns a $5 reward, not "earn 2 points per dollar on select categories with double points on Tuesdays"), and (5) purchase history tracking that enables the store to notice when a regular stops coming in ("Mrs. Chen hasn't visited in 3 weeks — call to check on her"). The loyalty system serves both a business function (retention) and a community function (welfare checking for isolated seniors).

## Target Customer
Independent retailers in high-senior-population communities whose current loyalty programs exclude 30-50% of their customer base because they're digital-only.

## Impact If Solved
Extends loyalty program participation from 50-60% of customers (digital-only) to 85-90% (physical card + digital). Increases average transaction frequency by 10-15% for enrolled senior customers. The "regular customer not seen" alert provides a community welfare function that strengthens the store's role as a community anchor.
