# Accessibility-Focused Retail for Senior Shoppers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/independent-retailers|Independent Retailers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-50B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — retailers in high-senior-population areas often have older customers who prefer phone ordering, delivery, and personal service, but the store's systems don't support these interaction modes
**Target Buyer:** Retailer in a community with 25%+ population aged 65+, serving customers who value accessibility, personal service, and non-digital purchasing options
**Automation Potential:** Medium — customer service automation must preserve the personal touch that seniors value while reducing the operational burden of phone orders, delivery coordination, and assisted shopping

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Communities with high senior populations (retirement communities, aging rural towns, 55+ planned communities) have retail needs that mainstream independent retail doesn't address: (1) customers who prefer phone ordering and home delivery over in-store shopping, (2) accessibility requirements (larger signage, wider aisles, seating, hearing-accessible checkout), (3) high loyalty but declining mobility (regulars who shopped for 20 years but now can't drive to the store), (4) medication and health product integration (pharmacies closing in rural areas push health product demand to general retail), and (5) social function (the store visit is the week's social highlight for isolated seniors). Retailers serving this demographic adapt informally — the owner takes phone orders, a staff member delivers groceries in their car — but these adaptations are unstructured, unsustainable, and unprofitable.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Instacart and DoorDash handle delivery for chain retail but don't serve independent stores in smaller communities. POS systems don't support phone-order workflows. No platform helps independent retailers manage the phone-order-to-delivery pipeline that senior customers need. Customer loyalty programs are digital (apps, emails) but senior customers prefer paper, phone, or in-person interaction.
