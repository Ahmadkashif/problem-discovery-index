# Commercial Lines Comparative Rater Adaptation

**Niche:** [[niches/independent-insurance-agents/commercial-lines-agencies/profile|Commercial Lines Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/independent-insurance-agents|Independent Insurance Agents]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Personal lines comparative raters (EZLynx, TurboRater) work well for auto and home but cover fewer than 20% of commercial lines classes — the adaptation gap is enormous.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Comparative raters have solved personal lines quoting: a producer enters client data once and gets side-by-side quotes from 10+ carriers in minutes. For commercial lines — where premiums are 5-10x larger and the agency earns higher commissions — this capability barely exists. Producers must log into each carrier portal individually, re-enter ACORD data, and wait days for quotes to come back.

## What Already Exists
EZLynx, TurboRater, and ITC provide real-time comparative rating for personal auto and homeowners. Emerging platforms like Bold Penguin and Tarmika are attempting commercial lines quoting for simpler classes (BOP, small commercial auto, workers' comp for low-hazard classes). These tools handle maybe 15-20% of commercial lines classes and fewer than half of carriers.

## The Customization Gap
Commercial lines classes are far more heterogeneous than personal lines: a restaurant's GL quote requires liquor liability endorsement logic, a contractor needs wrap-up program analysis, and a manufacturer needs product liability with recall coverage. Each class requires industry-specific supplemental applications, unique underwriting questions, and carrier-specific appetite rules. The rater must handle class-specific form variations, not just policy-level data.

## Target Customer
Agency principals at commercial-focused agencies spending 200+ hours per month on manual quoting across carrier portals.

## Impact If Solved
Cutting commercial lines quoting time by 60-70% across all classes would free 100-150 producer-hours per month at a mid-size agency, directly translating to $200K-$400K in additional new business capacity per year.
