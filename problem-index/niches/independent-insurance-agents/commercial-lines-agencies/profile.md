# Commercial Lines Agencies

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/independent-insurance-agents|Independent Insurance Agents]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** ~$90B in commercial premiums placed through independent agents annually
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~60%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — AMS systems are universal but commercial lines quoting remains portal-by-portal with minimal API integration; comparative raters cover fewer than 20% of commercial classes
**Target Buyer:** Agency principal or commercial lines department manager at a 5-30 person agency
**Automation Potential:** High — carrier appetite matching, submission preparation, and ACORD form population are rule-heavy workflows with clear automation targets

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial lines agencies derive the majority of their revenue from business insurance — GL, BOP, workers' comp, commercial auto, professional liability — where placement requires matching risk profiles against 15-30 carrier appetite guides. Unlike personal lines, there is no universal comparative rater; each submission is manually prepared and sent to individual carrier portals. The producer's tacit knowledge of which underwriter at which carrier is writing which class in which geography is the agency's primary competitive asset, making this niche uniquely dependent on experienced talent and uniquely vulnerable to producer turnover.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Applied Epic and Vertafore AMS360 manage policy data, but commercial lines quoting tools (Bold Penguin, Tarmika) cover only a fraction of classes and carriers. The critical gap is intelligent carrier-risk matching: no tool ingests a risk profile and recommends the top 3-5 carriers most likely to quote competitively. Submission preparation still requires re-entering the same ACORD data into each carrier's proprietary portal.
