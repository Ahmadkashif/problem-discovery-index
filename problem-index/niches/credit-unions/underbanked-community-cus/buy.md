# Small-Dollar Lending Platform for CUs

**Niche:** [[niches/credit-unions/underbanked-community-cus/profile|Underbanked Community CUs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Small-dollar lending platforms exist in fintech but aren't designed for CU integration — underbanked-serving CUs need a payday-alternative lending product that works within their core banking platform and regulatory framework.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Underbanked members' most urgent need is small-dollar liquidity — $200-$1,000 loans to cover emergencies, car repairs, or rent shortfalls. CU LOS platforms are designed for $5,000+ loans with full underwriting: credit pull, income verification, debt-to-income analysis. Processing a $500 emergency loan through the standard LOS costs the CU $150-$300 in staff time and cannot be done profitably at 18% APR. As a result, most CUs either don't offer small-dollar loans (sending members to payday lenders) or offer them as an unprofitable public service with minimal process — creating compliance risk.

## What Already Exists
QCash provides an automated small-dollar lending platform for CUs but has limited integration with smaller core platforms. Oportun offers small-dollar consumer lending but as a fintech lender, not a CU product. NCUA has issued guidance encouraging CU payday alternatives (PALs I and PALs II) with specific program rules. Fintech lending platforms (Earnin, Dave, Brigit) provide cash advance products but aren't CU products — they compete with CUs for the same members.

## The Customization Gap
CUs need a small-dollar lending module that: (1) integrates with their core platform (Symitar, KeyStone, CU*Answers) as a native product; (2) automates PALs I or PALs II compliance (28% APR cap, $200 application fee cap, 1-6 month terms, minimum membership requirements); (3) enables 5-minute application-to-disbursement for loans under $1,000 using account behavior and membership tenure instead of full underwriting; (4) manages repayment through automatic payroll deposit allocation; (5) reports to credit bureaus to help members build credit history. The gap is CU-native integration and PALs compliance — features fintech small-dollar lenders don't need and don't build.

## Target Customer
CEOs and VP of Lending at CUs serving underbanked communities who want to offer payday-alternative lending at scale without the per-loan processing cost that makes small-dollar CU loans unprofitable.

## Impact If Solved
Makes small-dollar CU lending operationally sustainable at 500+ loans per year per institution. Diverts member demand from 300%+ APR payday lenders to 28% APR CU products, saving members $200-$1,000 per loan in interest. Builds credit history for thin-file members through bureau reporting — creating a pathway from no-score to scoreable that enables future access to larger CU products.
