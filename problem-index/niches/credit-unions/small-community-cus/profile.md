# Small Community CUs (<$500M Assets)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800B in combined assets across ~3,500 institutions
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~65% of institutions, ~35% of assets
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Core banking platforms (Symitar, KeyStone) are deployed but advanced analytics, digital lending, and member engagement tools are unaffordable or underutilized; most lack any in-house data science or marketing automation capability
**Target Buyer:** CEO or VP of Lending at a community CU with $50M-$500M in assets and 10-80 employees
**Automation Potential:** High — Lending decisions, member outreach, and compliance processes follow clear rules but are executed manually due to limited staff

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small community CUs constitute the majority of the industry by institution count but face a technology gap that larger CUs don't experience. They can't afford dedicated analytics staff, custom LOS configurations, or enterprise digital banking platforms. Their competitive advantage — deep member relationships and community knowledge — is concentrated in 2-5 veteran employees whose judgment can't be replaced when they retire. These CUs originate loans based on personal knowledge of the member and community, a process that works at 5,000 members but breaks at 15,000 as the loan officer can no longer know every applicant personally.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Core platforms (Symitar, KeyStone, CU*Answers) handle basic banking operations. LOS tools (MeridianLink, CU Direct) manage loan workflows but decision intelligence is minimal — loan officers still manually evaluate most applications against credit policy. Digital banking (NCR, Alkami) provides member-facing access but most small CUs use basic online banking without engagement features. Analytics is typically a quarterly spreadsheet from the core platform, not real-time dashboards.
