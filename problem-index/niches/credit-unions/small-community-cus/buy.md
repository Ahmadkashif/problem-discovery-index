# Affordable Loan Decisioning Intelligence

**Niche:** [[niches/credit-unions/small-community-cus/profile|Small Community CUs (<$500M Assets)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Enterprise loan decisioning platforms exist but are priced for $1B+ CUs — small community CUs need the same credit risk intelligence at a price point that fits a $200M institution.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #binary-classification #tabular-ml #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
When a member applies for a $15,000 personal loan, a small CU loan officer reviews the credit report, checks DTI, verifies employment, and makes a judgment call. There's no risk scoring model specific to the CU's member base, no automated policy check against the CU's credit guidelines, and no data-driven recommendation on rate or terms. The loan officer either approves based on experience or escalates to a supervisor. This process works at 50 loans/month but becomes a bottleneck at 200 loans/month as the CU grows — and quality degrades when the experienced loan officer is unavailable.

## What Already Exists
Enterprise decisioning platforms (FICO Decision Management, Zest AI, Upstart) provide ML-based credit decisioning but require $100K+ annual contracts, dedicated implementation staff, and minimum loan volumes that small CUs can't meet. CU-specific tools like CU Direct and Open Lending provide indirect auto lending decisioning but don't cover personal loans, credit cards, or HELOCs. CUNA Mutual provides some risk analytics, but they're portfolio-level, not application-level.

## The Customization Gap
Small CUs need a decisioning tool that: (1) works with their existing LOS (MeridianLink or CU Direct) without ripping and replacing; (2) builds risk models from the CU's own loan performance history (not generic bank models); (3) incorporates CU-specific credit policy rules (max DTI by product, minimum membership tenure requirements, employer list policies); (4) prices at $1,000-3,000/month, not $100K/year; and (5) provides explainable risk scores that satisfy NCUA fair lending review — not black-box ML. The gap is affordability and CU-specificity, not capability.

## Target Customer
VP of Lending at community CUs with $100M-$500M in assets originating 100-500 loans per month who want data-driven decisioning but can't justify enterprise platform costs.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces manual loan review time by 30-50% through automated policy checks and risk scoring. Decreases default rates by 10-20% on unsecured lending by surfacing risk signals the loan officer may miss. Enables consistent decisioning regardless of which loan officer is working — removing the quality variance between experienced and junior staff.
