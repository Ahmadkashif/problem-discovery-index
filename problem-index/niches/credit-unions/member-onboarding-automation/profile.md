# Member Onboarding & Cross-Sell Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-2B in embedded operational cost across CU industry
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting operational function affecting 100% of CUs
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Account opening is partially digitized at larger CUs (Alkami, Q2 digital platforms) but onboarding journeys (first 90 days) are unstructured; cross-sell during teller interactions is untargeted; most CUs have no systematic member engagement workflow after initial account opening
**Target Buyer:** VP of Marketing or Digital Banking Manager
**Automation Potential:** High — New member onboarding follows a predictable lifecycle with clear trigger points for product recommendations; cross-sell during transactions can be automated with propensity models

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Credit unions acquire new members at significant cost ($200-$500 per member through marketing, branch operations, and onboarding staff time) but lose 15-25% within the first year because the post-opening experience is passive — the member opens a share account and hears nothing until a marketing mailer arrives 6 months later. Meanwhile, members with only a single product relationship (share account only) have a 60-70% attrition rate within 3 years, while members with 3+ products have less than 5% attrition. The first 90 days after account opening is the critical window for building product depth, but most CUs have no structured onboarding journey and no data-driven cross-sell capability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Digital banking platforms (Alkami, Q2, NCR) handle account opening and provide limited marketing messaging capabilities. CRM platforms (Salesforce Financial Services Cloud) are deployed at some larger CUs for campaign management. Email marketing tools (Constant Contact, CU-specific platforms like CUProdigy) send bulk communications. None of these provide a personalized, trigger-based onboarding journey that adapts based on member behavior during the first 90 days, and none integrate real-time cross-sell recommendations into teller or digital banking interactions.
