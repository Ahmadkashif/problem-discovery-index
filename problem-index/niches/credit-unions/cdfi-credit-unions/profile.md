# CDFI-Designated Credit Unions

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $30-50B in combined assets across ~350 CDFI-certified CUs
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7% of institutions, ~2% of assets
**Digital Adoption:** Low — CDFI CUs serve low-income communities with limited technology budgets; CDFI Fund reporting requirements are managed in spreadsheets; mission-driven lending analytics are absent from standard CU platforms
**Target Buyer:** CEO or Chief Lending Officer at a CDFI-certified credit union
**Automation Potential:** Medium — CDFI Fund reporting, community impact measurement, and mission-driven lending assessment are highly automatable but require specialized data models not found in standard CU technology

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
CDFI (Community Development Financial Institution) certified credit unions have committed to serving low-income and underserved communities, receiving federal funding through the CDFI Fund in exchange for documented community impact. They operate under dual regulatory frameworks — NCUA for CU operations plus CDFI Fund for mission compliance — and must demonstrate that their lending activity targets specific geographies and populations. This creates unique data and reporting requirements that standard CU technology doesn't address: lending volume in designated target markets, impact measurement by income level and geography, and compliance with CDFI Fund award terms. These CUs are typically small ($50M-$500M in assets) and resource-constrained.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard CU core platforms handle banking operations but don't model CDFI target market definitions or impact metrics. CDFI Fund reporting (Transaction Level Report, Institution Level Report) requires manual data assembly from core banking exports. Community impact measurement — demonstrating that loans went to target populations and produced positive outcomes — is done through manual geocoding and census tract analysis. The CDFI Fund's AMIS (Award Management Information System) requires specific data formats that no CU core platform exports natively.
