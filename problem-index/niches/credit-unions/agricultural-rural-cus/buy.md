# FSA Guarantee Program Documentation Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/credit-unions/agricultural-rural-cus/profile|Agricultural & Rural CUs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** USDA Farm Service Agency loan guarantee programs reduce ag lending risk by 90% — but the application documentation is so burdensome that many CUs don't participate, leaving billions in guaranteed lending capacity unused.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
FSA guaranteed loans allow CUs to originate ag loans with a 90% federal guarantee on losses — transforming a risky agricultural lending portfolio into near-risk-free lending. But the FSA guarantee application requires extensive documentation: farm business plan, balance sheet, cash flow projections, environmental review, credit analysis narrative, and multiple FSA-specific forms. A single guarantee application takes 15-25 hours of staff time to assemble. Small rural CUs that could most benefit from the risk reduction often don't participate because they can't absorb the documentation burden with 2-3 lending staff.

## What Already Exists
FSA provides forms and instructions on its website. Farm financial analysis tools (FINPACK, AgPlan) generate some required financial statements. Document management platforms handle general file organization. Agricultural lending consultants offer FSA documentation services at $2,000-$5,000 per application. No integrated tool automates the assembly of FSA guarantee applications from CU lending data.

## The Customization Gap
An FSA guarantee automation tool needs to: (1) pull borrower financial data from the CU's core platform and LOS; (2) generate FSA-required financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow projection) in FSA-accepted formats; (3) pre-populate FSA forms (FSA-2211, FSA-2254, environmental review) from CU lending file data; (4) generate the credit analysis narrative using borrower data and CU credit policy language; (5) assemble the complete application package in the order FSA offices expect. The customization is FSA-specific form mapping and financial statement formatting — a well-defined problem once someone builds the template library.

## Target Customer
Ag lending staff at rural CUs who want to use FSA guarantee programs to reduce portfolio risk but currently avoid them due to documentation burden, or who outsource preparation at $2,000-$5,000 per application.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces FSA guarantee application preparation from 15-25 hours to 3-5 hours per loan. Enables small CUs to access FSA guarantees at scale — a 90% risk reduction on ag lending that transforms the portfolio risk profile. Increases CU willingness to make ag loans that would otherwise be declined as too risky without the guarantee.
