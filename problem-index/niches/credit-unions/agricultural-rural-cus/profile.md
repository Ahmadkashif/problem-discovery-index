# Agricultural & Rural CUs

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-60B in combined assets across ~400 agricultural and rural-chartered CUs
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8% of institutions, ~3% of assets
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Broadband limitations constrain digital banking adoption; ag lending requires crop/livestock collateral valuation expertise that no CU platform models; seasonal cash flow patterns break standard credit models
**Target Buyer:** CEO or ag lending specialist at a rural CU serving farming communities
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Ag loan underwriting and seasonal cash flow analysis follow domain-specific patterns that are automatable but require agricultural knowledge absent from standard CU tools

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Agricultural credit unions serve farming and ranching communities where the lending dynamics are fundamentally different from consumer lending. Ag loans are secured by non-standard collateral (crops, livestock, equipment, land), have seasonal cash flow that makes standard DTI calculations meaningless (a farmer may have zero income for 8 months and $500K in September), and depend on market variables (commodity prices, weather, subsidy payments) that standard credit models don't incorporate. Loan officers at ag CUs develop deep expertise in crop economics, land valuation, and farm financial analysis — tacit knowledge that is especially concentrated and hard to replace in rural communities.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard CU core platforms handle basic banking but don't model ag loan structures (operating lines tied to crop cycles, equipment loans with seasonal payment schedules, farmland mortgages). Farm Service Agency (FSA) loan guarantee programs have specific documentation requirements that CU LOS platforms don't automate. Farm financial analysis tools (FINPACK, AgPlan) are standalone and don't integrate with CU lending workflows. The gap is widest in cash flow analysis — standard debt-to-income ratios are meaningless for seasonal agricultural income, yet CU LOS platforms enforce them as standard fields.
