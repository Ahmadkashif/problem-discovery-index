# Transient Member Relationship Continuity

**Niche:** [[niches/credit-unions/military-federal-cus/profile|Military & Federal Employee CUs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/credit-unions|Credit Unions]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Military CU members relocate every 2-3 years, breaking the branch relationship that is the CU's competitive advantage — a member who worked with the same loan officer for 3 years starts over with a stranger at the new duty station.
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #worker-facing #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Credit unions compete on member relationships, but military member relationships reset every PCS cycle. A loan officer at Fort Liberty has built a 3-year relationship with a service member — knows their financial goals, family situation, and lending history in context. When that member PCS to Camp Pendleton, the receiving branch or digital banking channel has no context. The member starts over: re-explaining their financial situation, rebuilding trust, and often receiving generic product recommendations that ignore 3 years of relationship history. This relationship discontinuity is the primary driver of military CU member attrition — members who relocate to areas with competing military CU branches (Navy Federal, USAA) switch institutions because there's no relationship equity to keep them.

## Why It's Still Broken
CU core platforms store transaction and account data that transfers between branches, but the relationship context — the loan officer's notes about the member's goals, the financial planning conversations, the understanding of the member's risk tolerance and life plans — is stored nowhere. CRM platforms exist but military CUs rarely use them for member-level relationship documentation. Even when they do, the receiving branch staff don't review CRM records before the first interaction with a transferred member. There's no "relationship handoff" process built into the PCS workflow.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A member relationship profile that travels with the member across branch and digital touchpoints: (1) structured relationship notes from previous interactions (financial goals, life events, preferences, risk tolerance); (2) product usage history with context (why they chose this mortgage product, what drove the auto loan decision); (3) a "member brief" generated for the receiving branch staff before the first post-PCS interaction — summarizing the member's relationship history, current product holdings, and likely upcoming needs; (4) proactive notification to receiving branch: "SSG Rodriguez is arriving at your installation next month — 3-year member, active mortgage, auto loan maturing in 6 months, expressed interest in investment accounts."

## Who Feels the Pain
Service members who must rebuild their CU relationship from scratch every 2-3 years despite being loyal members, and branch staff at receiving installations who serve transferred members with zero context about their relationship history.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces military member attrition at PCS by 15-25% — currently 10-15% of members who relocate switch to a competitor CU. For a CU with 50,000 members and 8,000 PCS events per year, retaining 1,200-2,000 additional members represents $600K-$1M in annual lifetime value preservation. Creates the differentiated member experience that justifies choosing a smaller military CU over Navy Federal or USAA.
