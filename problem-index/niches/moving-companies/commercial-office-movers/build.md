# Commercial Move Project Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/commercial-office-movers/profile|Commercial Office Movers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No purpose-built platform exists for managing multi-phase commercial office relocations from survey through post-move verification.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation #ai-platform

## The Problem
A commercial office move for a 200-person company involves 2,000-5,000 individual items across 3-8 floors, coordinated over 4-12 weeks with multiple stakeholders (facilities, IT, HR, department heads). The move project manager juggles floor-plan mapping, item tagging assignments, phase scheduling (which departments move which nights), elevator reservation, loading dock booking, IT disconnect/reconnect timelines, and client communication — all in disconnected spreadsheets, email chains, and whiteboard schedules. When phases slip or items are misrouted, the mover absorbs overtime costs and risks losing the client's future business.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Residential moving software vendors (SmartMoving, MoveitPro) built for truck-and-crew dispatch, not multi-week project management. Generic PM tools (Asana, Monday.com) lack moving-specific concepts like item tagging schemas, floor-plan-based routing, and phased overnight scheduling. The commercial moving market is smaller than residential (~$3-4B vs. ~$14B), so software vendors default to the larger segment. Building this requires deep understanding of commercial move workflows that few software teams have.

## What to Build
A vertical project management platform for commercial moves that includes: floor-plan upload with drag-and-drop item placement, barcode/QR tag generation and assignment, phase scheduling with dependency management (IT must disconnect before furniture moves), crew and elevator/dock resource allocation per phase, real-time progress tracking during move nights via mobile scan-and-confirm, and automated client status reports. Integration with the mover's existing CRM and dispatch system for crew scheduling.

## Target Customer
Commercial moving company owners and project managers handling 20-100 office relocations per year, typically $50K-500K per move.

## Impact If Built
Reduces project management overhead by 40-60% per commercial move, cuts post-move item misplacement from 3-5% to under 0.5%, and provides the structured reporting that wins repeat corporate contracts. A 500-company addressable market at $1,000-3,000/month represents a $6-18M ARR opportunity.
