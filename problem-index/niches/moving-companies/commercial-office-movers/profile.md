# Commercial Office Movers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Larger commercial movers use project management tools (Monday.com, Smartsheet) adapted from construction, but no purpose-built software exists for multi-phase office relocations.
**Target Buyer:** Operations manager or project lead at a moving company specializing in corporate relocations, office buildouts, and multi-floor commercial moves
**Automation Potential:** High — commercial moves follow repeatable project phases (survey, tagging, disconnect, move, reconnect, verify) that map well to workflow automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial office moves are project-managed endeavors, not truck-and-muscle jobs. A 200-person office relocation involves furniture inventorying across floors, IT equipment disconnect/reconnect coordination with the client's IT team, phased move scheduling (often nights and weekends to minimize business disruption), and post-move verification that every tagged item reached its correct destination. The sales cycle is 3-6 months, quotes involve walk-throughs with facilities managers and IT directors, and a single botched corporate move can generate six-figure liability claims. The customer is a procurement or facilities team evaluating vendors on project management capability, not just price per pound.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Commercial movers adapt generic project management tools or use homegrown spreadsheets to track item tagging, floor plans, and phase schedules. No platform combines asset tagging, floor-plan mapping, IT coordination checklists, and phased scheduling into a single commercial-move-specific workflow. Post-move verification (did item #4,237 reach desk B-14 on floor 3?) is done by walking the destination floor with a clipboard.
