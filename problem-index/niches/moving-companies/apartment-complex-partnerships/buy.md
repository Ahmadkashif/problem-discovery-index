# Building Access and Move-In/Move-Out Scheduling

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/apartment-complex-partnerships/profile|Apartment Complex & Property Management Partnerships]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Building access scheduling tools exist for deliveries and contractors but don't handle the unique constraints of residential moves — elevator holds, loading dock time blocks, floor protection requirements, and insurance certificate verification.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Apartment buildings with elevators and loading docks require movers to reserve access in advance — typically a 4-hour elevator hold and a loading dock slot. The mover must also provide proof of insurance (COI) meeting the building's minimums, agree to floor protection requirements, and sometimes pay a refundable deposit. Currently, the mover's dispatcher calls the building management office, gets put on hold, negotiates a time slot, faxes or emails the COI, and hopes the reservation is confirmed. On move day, conflicts arise when two movers are booked for the same elevator or the COI was never processed, causing delays that cost crews idle time.

## What Already Exists
Building access and visitor management platforms (Building Engines, ButterflyMX, Envoy) handle contractor and delivery scheduling for commercial buildings. Calendar-based reservation systems exist for shared amenities in residential buildings. However, none handle the specific workflow of a residential move: multi-hour elevator holds, loading dock reservations paired with freight elevator access, COI validation against building-specific insurance minimums, and deposit tracking.

## The Customization Gap
The system needs move-specific reservation types (elevator hold + loading dock as a paired resource, not independent bookings), COI auto-validation that checks the mover's insurance against the building's requirements and flags gaps, floor protection acknowledgment workflows, and integration with both the property manager's building calendar and the mover's dispatch system so the reservation auto-populates on the move schedule. The mover needs a single view of all building reservations across their daily moves, not separate logins to each building's system.

## Target Customer
Dispatchers at moving companies operating in urban markets where 60-80% of moves involve buildings with elevator/dock access requirements, and property managers who currently process move reservations via phone calls and email.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates the 30-60 minutes per move dispatchers spend calling buildings to arrange access, reduces move-day access conflicts from 15-20% to under 3%, and prevents the $500-1,500 in crew idle time that each access conflict causes.
