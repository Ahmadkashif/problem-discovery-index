# Property Manager-to-Mover Referral Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/apartment-complex-partnerships/profile|Apartment Complex & Property Management Partnerships]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform exists that embeds a preferred mover's booking flow directly into the tenant onboarding process managed by property management software.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When a tenant signs a lease, the property manager's onboarding checklist includes setting up utilities, renter's insurance, and moving logistics. The "moving" step is currently a verbal recommendation or a flyer — there is no digital handoff that connects the tenant to the preferred mover with pre-populated move details (unit number, floor, elevator availability, move-in date, building rules). The mover has no visibility into upcoming move-ins until the tenant calls (if they call at all). Result: the mover captures only 30-40% of available referrals, and the property manager has no way to track whether their preferred vendor is delivering a good tenant experience.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Property management software companies (AppFolio, Buildium) view moving as a one-time event outside their core lease-management loop and haven't built vendor marketplace features. Moving software companies build for the mover's workflow, not the property manager's. The two-sided marketplace problem — you need both property managers and movers on the platform — creates a chicken-and-egg challenge that neither side has been willing to anchor.

## What to Build
A lightweight integration layer that plugs into property management platforms (via API or embeddable widget) and surfaces during tenant onboarding. The tenant sees their preferred mover with a pre-filled booking form (address, unit, floor, elevator policy, move-in window). The mover receives a qualified lead with all building-specific details attached. The property manager gets a dashboard showing referral conversion rates, tenant satisfaction scores, and upcoming move volume. Revenue model: per-booking fee or monthly SaaS to the mover.

## Target Customer
Moving company owners who want to build a B2B referral channel with property management companies controlling 200-5,000 units, replacing the current business-card-and-flyer referral model.

## Impact If Built
Increases referral capture rate from 30-40% to 70-80%, giving a mover partnered with 10 apartment complexes (2,000 units, ~25% annual turnover) an additional 150-200 booked moves per year at $800-1,500 average revenue per move — $120K-300K in incremental annual revenue per mover.
