# Moving-Specific Claims Management Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/damage-claims-documentation/profile|Damage Claims Documentation & Resolution]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Insurance claims management platforms handle auto and property claims but don't understand moving-specific liability structures, valuation options, or interstate carrier regulations.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #compliance #text-classification #nlp

## The Problem
When a damage claim comes in, the claims handler must determine: what valuation protection the customer selected (released value at $0.60/lb or full-value protection), whether the move was interstate (FMCSA-regulated) or intrastate (state-regulated), whether the mover was the carrier or an agent booking through a van line (affecting liability splits), the item's depreciated value vs. repair cost, and whether a third-party repair is viable. Each determination requires referencing the bill of lading, valuation addendum, and applicable regulations. This process takes 2-4 hours per claim and is error-prone — a misapplied liability calculation can result in overpayment or a regulatory complaint.

## What Already Exists
Claims management platforms (ClaimXperience, Snapsheet, CCC Intelligent Solutions) provide structured claims intake, damage assessment workflows, and settlement calculators for auto insurance and property insurance. These platforms handle high volume efficiently but their liability models, valuation formulas, and regulatory frameworks are built for insurance, not moving.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs a moving-specific liability engine: released value vs. full-value protection calculation, carrier vs. agent liability splits for van line moves, FMCSA vs. state-level regulatory rule application, and depreciation schedules for household goods (not vehicles or structures). Claim intake must pull customer selection from the bill of lading and valuation addendum. Settlement calculations must follow the arbitration rules of the American Moving & Storage Association (AMSA) dispute resolution program. Customer communication templates must reference moving-specific terms and regulatory rights.

## Target Customer
Claims managers at mid-to-large moving companies processing 20-100 claims per month, and van line agents who handle claims on behalf of their carrier.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces per-claim processing time from 2-4 hours to 30-45 minutes, eliminates liability calculation errors that cause 10-15% of claims to be overpaid, and cuts average resolution time from 45-60 days to 15-20 days — directly reducing the negative review window.
