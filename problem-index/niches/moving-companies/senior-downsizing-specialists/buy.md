# Multi-Destination Inventory Sorting and Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/senior-downsizing-specialists/profile|Senior Downsizing Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Inventory management tools exist, but none handle the "keep, donate, sell, dispose" sorting workflow that is the core of senior downsizing.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
A 3,000 sqft family home contains 500-2,000 individual items that must be categorized: keep and move to the new home, donate to specific charities, sell at estate sale, or dispose/recycle. The sorting process takes multiple sessions over weeks, with the senior and family making emotional decisions about each item. The move manager tracks dispositions on sticky notes, color-coded stickers on items, and a clipboard inventory sheet. When the estate sale company arrives, they need the "sell" list. When Goodwill comes for pickup, they need the "donate" pile. When the movers arrive, they need to know what's going and what's staying. Miscommunication means a cherished heirloom goes to Goodwill or an intended donation ends up on the truck.

## What Already Exists
Home inventory apps (Sortly, Encircle, Magicplan) catalog items with photos but don't support multi-destination sorting or disposition tracking. Estate sale software (EstateSales.NET, MaxSold) manages the sale process but not the upstream sorting. Moving inventory tools track what goes on the truck but not what goes elsewhere.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs: (1) photo-based item cataloging with disposition tagging (keep/donate/sell/dispose), (2) multi-stakeholder decision workflow (family members can review items and vote on disposition remotely via shared gallery), (3) filtered export by disposition (generate the estate sale list, the donation list, the moving inventory separately), and (4) status tracking (is the family's decision final? has the item been physically sorted? has it been picked up/moved?). The family collaboration feature is the key gap — adult children in 3 different states need to see photos and make decisions without being physically present.

## Target Customer
Senior downsizing specialists who process 500-2,000 items per project and currently track dispositions with sticky notes and paper lists.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces sorting documentation time by 40-60% (from 4-8 hours to 2-3 hours per project). Prevents disposition errors (items going to wrong destinations) that cause $500-$2,000 in family distress and service recovery costs per incident. Enables remote family participation that accelerates decision-making by 2-3 weeks per project.
