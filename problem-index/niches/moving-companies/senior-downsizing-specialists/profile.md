# Senior Downsizing Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most operate on referrals from senior living facilities and estate attorneys; no CRM, no estimation tool, and heavy reliance on personal relationships
**Target Buyer:** Owner or manager specializing in 55+ moves: downsizing from family home to senior living, estate cleanouts, and assisted-living transitions
**Automation Potential:** Medium — The emotional and sorting components resist automation, but inventory cataloging, donation coordination, and estate sale integration are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Senior downsizing is fundamentally different from standard residential moving: the customer is often emotionally overwhelmed (leaving a 30-year family home), the scope includes not just moving but sorting (keep, donate, sell, dispose of a lifetime of possessions), and the decision-maker may not be the senior themselves but an adult child or power of attorney. The move may involve coordination with estate sale companies, donation pickup services, junk removal, and senior living facilities — all requiring scheduling around the senior's capacity and the family's availability. Standard moving companies handle the physical move but not the upstream sorting and downstream coordination that seniors need.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No CRM is designed for the senior downsizing workflow: the sales process takes 2-6 months (vs. 1-2 weeks for standard moves), involves multiple family decision-makers, and includes services beyond moving (sorting, packing, donation coordination, estate sale referral). Standard moving CRMs (SmartMoving) track the move, not the 3-month relationship leading up to it. Inventory tools don't distinguish between "keep and move," "donate," "sell at estate sale," and "dispose" — categories that are central to the senior downsizing process.
