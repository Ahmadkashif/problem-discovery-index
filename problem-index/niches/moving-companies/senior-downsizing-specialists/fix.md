# Family Decision-Maker Coordination Bottleneck

**Niche:** [[niches/moving-companies/senior-downsizing-specialists/profile|Senior Downsizing Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Three adult children in three states need to agree on 200 items before the move can proceed — and the project stalls for weeks waiting for decisions that happen over group text chains.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #worker-facing

## The Problem
Senior downsizing projects typically involve 2-5 family decision-makers: the senior, adult children, and possibly a power of attorney or elder care coordinator. Major decisions (what to keep, what to sell, what to donate) require consensus among parties who may be geographically dispersed and emotionally charged. The move manager sends photos of items via text or email, asks for decisions, and waits. One sibling responds immediately, another responds in 3 days, and the third doesn't respond until called. A project that should take 8 weeks stretches to 14 because 3 weeks are lost to pending family decisions. The move manager has no tool to track which decisions are pending, who hasn't responded, and which items are blocking the project timeline.

## Why It's Still Broken
The decision-making process is inherently emotional and interpersonal — no software can resolve a sibling dispute over who gets mom's jewelry box. But the logistics of collecting and tracking decisions can be systematized. Currently, the move manager uses a personal text chain or email thread, which becomes unmanageable when discussing 50+ items with 3+ family members. Decisions get buried in the thread, reversals aren't tracked, and the move manager spends 2-3 hours/week just chasing responses.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A simple decision-tracking portal shared with family members: item photos with description, disposition options (keep for [family member], donate, sell, dispose), a voting/approval mechanism, and a "pending decisions" dashboard showing which items are blocking project progress. Automated reminders to family members who haven't responded within 3 days. A decision log showing who decided what and when, preventing the "I never agreed to donate that" disputes that arise after the fact. Delivered via mobile web link — no app download required.

## Who Feels the Pain
The move manager who has called the daughter in Portland three times this week to ask about the dining room set, and the senior who is anxious because the move date is approaching but half the house hasn't been sorted because the family can't coordinate.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces average project timeline by 2-4 weeks through faster family decision collection. Saves the move manager 2-3 hours/week per project in decision-chasing. Prevents project delays that cost $500-$1,500 each in re-scheduling fees and rushed service provider bookings.
