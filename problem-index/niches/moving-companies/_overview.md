# Niche Analysis — Moving Companies

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]

## Niche Selection

Applied four-axis niche discovery: Market Share, Digital Adoption, Underserved Audience, Automation Potential.

| # | Niche | Category | Est. Market Size | Digital Adoption | Target Buyer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Long-Distance Interstate Movers | High Market Share | $8-10B | Medium | Owner/GM managing DOT-regulated interstate operations |

| 3 | Senior Downsizing Specialists | Low Digitized | $2-3B | Low | Owner/manager specializing in 55+ downsizing, estate, and assisted-living transitions |

| 8 | Move Estimation & Crew Scheduling Optimization | Highly Automatable | $1-2B (embedded) | Low-Medium | Estimator or dispatcher allocating crews and trucks to daily moves |

## Why These Niches

Moving companies fragment along distance (local vs. interstate), customer type (residential vs. commercial vs. military), specialization (general vs. senior vs. high-value), and operational function (estimation, dispatch, claims). These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (interstate and local residential), the two most digitally neglected specializations (senior downsizing and military PCS), the two most underserved customer segments (commercial office moves and property management partnerships), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (damage claims and estimation/scheduling). Excluded: piano/specialty item movers (too narrow), storage-only operations (different business model), and international relocation (distinct regulatory universe).
