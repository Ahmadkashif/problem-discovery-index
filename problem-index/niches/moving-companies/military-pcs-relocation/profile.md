**Parent Industry:** [[industries/moving-companies|Moving Companies]]

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — TSPs use GovTrack/DPS portals for booking but manage internal operations (packing inventories, weight tickets, claims) on paper forms and spreadsheets.
**Target Buyer:** TSP (Transportation Service Provider) owner or operations manager handling government-contracted military moves
**Automation Potential:** High — strict regulatory format requirements and repetitive documentation workflows make this highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Military PCS (Permanent Change of Station) moves operate under a completely different commercial framework than civilian residential moves. The customer is the Department of Defense, not the service member; pricing follows tariff schedules and weight-based formulas rather than competitive bidding; and every move must comply with the Defense Personal Property Program (DP3) rules governing documentation, weight verification, and claims adjudication. TSPs face unique challenges: shipment must be accepted within 24-48 hours of tender, storage-in-transit (SIT) has strict 90-day federal limits, and performance is scored via the Best Value Score (BVS) system that directly determines future business allocation. A TSP with declining BVS scores can lose 30-50% of their volume in a single cycle.

## Current Tools & Gaps
The DoD's DPS (Defense Personal Property System) and its successor MilMove handle booking and tracking, but TSPs must manually bridge between government portals and their own dispatch, inventory, and billing systems. No commercial moving software (SmartMoving, MoveitPro) has built DP3-compliant modules for weight ticket management, government billing codes, or BVS score tracking. TSPs cobble together Excel trackers for their performance metrics and manually enter data into multiple disconnected systems.
