# POS System with Live Plant Inventory Awareness

**Niche:** [[niches/greenhouse-horticulture/small-greenhouse-nurseries/profile|Small Greenhouse Nurseries]]
**Industry:** [[industries/greenhouse-horticulture|Greenhouse Horticulture]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic POS systems (Square, Clover) track transactions but have no concept of living inventory that changes condition daily — a flat of petunias that was premium-grade last week is now overgrown and should be discounted, but the POS shows the same price and the same SKU.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
A nursery's inventory is alive and deteriorating. Unlike retail merchandise that sits at constant quality until sold, plants overgrow their pots, become root-bound, stretch from inadequate light, or develop pest damage — all of which reduce their market value over time. The owner mentally tracks which benches have plants past their prime and marks them down with handwritten signs. The POS system has no awareness of this lifecycle: the same "4-inch petunia" SKU represents a freshly potted plant and a leggy, root-bound specimen that should be half-price. This disconnect means markdowns happen late (plants look bad before someone remembers to discount them) or not at all (dead inventory occupies bench space until it is thrown away).

## What Already Exists
Square, Clover, and Shopify POS handle transactions, basic inventory counts, and customer management. Nursery-specific POS systems (GardenWare, Bonsai Software) add plant category taxonomies and seasonal pricing but still treat inventory as static units. None incorporate a time-based quality model that automatically triggers markdown alerts based on pot-up date and expected shelf life.

## The Customization Gap
A nursery-adapted POS needs: (1) lot-based inventory tracking where each crop lot has a pot-up date and expected retail shelf life (petunias: 3 weeks, perennials: 6 weeks, shrubs: 12 weeks), (2) automatic markdown alerts when a lot exceeds its prime retail window ("Bench 4 petunias are 4 weeks old — recommend 30% markdown or move to compost"), (3) seasonal pricing rules that adjust automatically (spring premium pricing, mid-summer clearance, fall mum season), and (4) landscaper account management with volume pricing, delivery scheduling, and pre-book tracking. Must integrate with Square/Clover hardware since nurseries will not replace their card readers.

## Target Customer
Small nursery owner-operators currently using generic POS systems who experience 10-20% inventory shrink from unsold plants that deteriorate past saleable condition. Approximately 10,000 US nursery retail operations.

## Impact If Solved
Inventory shrink from late markdowns and missed quality degradation reduced by 30-50%, saving $5K-$15K annually. Average markdown price improves (marking down at 3 weeks instead of 5 weeks captures higher remaining value). Customer satisfaction improves — shoppers see fresh, well-priced plants rather than leggy leftovers at full price.
