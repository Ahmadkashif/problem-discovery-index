# Small Greenhouse Nurseries

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/greenhouse-horticulture|Greenhouse Horticulture]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — owner-operators at small nurseries (< 2 acres under glass/poly) typically manage with paper records, mental note-keeping, and basic POS systems; climate control is manual (opening vents, turning on heaters) rather than computer-controlled
**Target Buyer:** Owner-Operator at a retail or small wholesale greenhouse nursery
**Automation Potential:** Medium — crop planning, customer management, and basic climate monitoring are automatable at affordable price points, but the business model is diverse (retail + wholesale + landscaping) making standardized tools difficult

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Small greenhouse nurseries (under 2 acres of controlled environment) represent the majority of US greenhouse operations by count but a minority of revenue. These are typically family-owned businesses combining retail garden center sales, small-batch wholesale to landscapers and garden centers, and sometimes landscape installation services. The owner is simultaneously the grower, salesperson, bookkeeper, and delivery driver. Product diversity is extreme — a small nursery may grow 200-500 varieties across annuals, perennials, herbs, vegetables, and houseplants — making the crop management challenge qualitatively different from a large wholesale grower focused on 20-30 high-volume items. Climate control is often manual (propane heaters with thermostats, roll-up sidewalls, shade cloth pulled by hand) rather than computer-managed.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Small nurseries use generic POS systems (Square, Clover) for retail sales and QuickBooks for bookkeeping. Specialized nursery POS (Bonsai, GardenWare) exist but lack crop planning features. No affordable tool combines crop planning (what to grow, when to plant, when it will be ready), retail inventory (what is available for sale, what stage is it in), and customer management (landscaper accounts, special orders, spring booking) in a platform designed for the scale and budget of a 1-acre nursery.
