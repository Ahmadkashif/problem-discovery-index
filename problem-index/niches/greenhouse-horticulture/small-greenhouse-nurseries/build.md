# Unified Crop Planning and Retail Inventory for Small Nurseries

**Niche:** [[niches/greenhouse-horticulture/small-greenhouse-nurseries/profile|Small Greenhouse Nurseries]]
**Industry:** [[industries/greenhouse-horticulture|Greenhouse Horticulture]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product connects the growing side (what is planted, when it will be ready, how much bench space it occupies) with the selling side (what is available for sale, which landscaper accounts need spring deliveries, what was popular last year) for a small nursery growing 200-500 varieties.
**Tags:** #recommendation #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A small nursery owner decides in January what to plant for the spring season — 200-500 varieties in quantities ranging from 50 flats to 2,000 plugs. These decisions are based on what sold last year (remembered imprecisely), what the landscape accounts have pre-booked (communicated via phone and scrawled notes), what the plug suppliers have available (catalogs arriving in October-November), and how much bench space is available (estimated by walking the greenhouse). By April, the owner discovers they grew too much of the slow sellers and too little of the popular items — a universal problem at small nurseries because the crop planning and sales data live in separate systems (or no system at all).

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The small nursery market is fragmented (10,000+ US operations), low average revenue ($200K-$1M/year), and operationally diverse (each nursery has a unique mix of retail, wholesale, and landscape). No single crop planning tool can accommodate the variety diversity (200-500 items vs. 20-30 in wholesale) at a price point the owner can justify ($30-$50/month vs. $300-$800/month for wholesale tools). The POS and crop planning must share data in a way that generic tools (Square + a spreadsheet) cannot.

## What to Build
A mobile-first platform with three integrated modules: (1) a crop planning tool where the owner enters varieties, quantities, planting dates, and pot sizes — with pre-loaded production schedules for common crops (8-week petunia, 12-week tomato, 6-week herb) and plug supplier catalog integration, (2) a retail/wholesale inventory module that automatically tracks what is available for sale based on crop readiness dates and sales transactions, and (3) a year-over-year recommendation engine that compares this year's plan to last year's sales data and flags mismatches ("you grew 500 flats of impatiens last year and sold 480, but only planned 300 this year"). Designed for a nursery owner with 30 minutes/week to manage the system.

## Target Customer
Owner-operators at small greenhouse nurseries (0.25-2 acres under cover) doing $200K-$1M in annual sales. Approximately 8,000-12,000 US operations at this scale. Price point: $30-$50/month.

## Impact If Built
Overproduction waste reduced by 20-30%, saving $10K-$30K annually in materials and lost bench space. Underproduction losses (missed sales from popular items) reduced by 15-25%, capturing $15K-$50K in additional revenue. Spring season planning time reduced from 40-60 hours of catalog browsing and spreadsheet work to 10-15 hours of reviewing system-generated recommendations.
