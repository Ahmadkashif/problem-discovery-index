# Urban & Vertical Farms

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/greenhouse-horticulture|Greenhouse Horticulture]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — vertical farms are tech-forward by nature (LED lighting, automated nutrient delivery, IoT sensors), but the software layer is immature relative to the hardware sophistication
**Target Buyer:** Farm Manager / Operations Director at an indoor vertical farm or urban greenhouse
**Automation Potential:** High — fully controlled environments generate dense data streams, but most facilities lack the analytics to optimize energy-to-yield ratios and labor deployment

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Urban and vertical farms represent the newest segment of controlled-environment agriculture, with purpose-built indoor facilities using stacked growing layers under LED lighting. These operations face fundamentally different economics than traditional greenhouses: energy costs run 30-50% of production cost (vs. 20-30% in greenhouses) because all light is artificial, capital costs are 3-5x higher per square foot, and the crop mix is constrained to leafy greens and herbs that grow fast enough to justify the infrastructure cost. The promise — year-round local production, zero pesticide use, 95% less water — has attracted $4B+ in venture capital, but most vertical farms have struggled to achieve profitability. The operational challenge is optimizing the energy-to-yield ratio: every photon costs money, and the grower must maximize grams of product per kWh consumed.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Vertical farm operators use custom-built control systems or platforms like Agrilution, InFarm, and 80 Acres' proprietary software. General greenhouse climate computers (Priva, Ridder) do not support vertical farm configurations (multi-tier, all-LED, sealed environment). Harvest automation companies (Iron Ox, Bowery) have built integrated hardware-software platforms but these are proprietary. Independent vertical farms lack access to the analytics and optimization tools that the well-funded players have built in-house.
