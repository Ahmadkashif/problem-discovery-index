**Automation Potential:** High — yield prediction, harvest scheduling, and climate-crop optimization are data-rich problems with clear ML applications

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Controlled-environment vegetable production — primarily greenhouse-grown tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, and leafy greens — is a capital-intensive business with facility costs of $50-$150/sq ft and operating costs dominated by energy (20-30%) and labor (25-35%). Large operators (Mastronardi/SUNSET, Village Farms, AppHarvest, Revol Greens) run 50-200 acre facilities with sophisticated climate and fertigation control, but yield optimization — maximizing grams of produce per square meter per year — still depends on the head grower's experience with crop steering. Crop steering is the practice of adjusting climate, irrigation, and pruning strategies to shift plant energy between vegetative growth and generative (fruit) production. This requires reading subtle plant signals (internode length, stem thickness, leaf color, truss load) to determine whether the plant is "in balance" — a tacit skill that the best growers develop over decades of observing plant response.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Priva and Ridder climate computers provide rule-based environmental control. Grodan and Meteor Systems provide substrate sensor platforms. Harvest robots (AppHarvest/Root AI, MetoMotion) handle picking in some facilities. But crop steering — the highest-value decision process — has no software support. The grower walks rows, examines plants, and makes intuitive adjustments to irrigation strategy, pruning aggressiveness, and climate setpoints based on what the plant is telling them.
