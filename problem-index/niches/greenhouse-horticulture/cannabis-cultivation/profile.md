# Cannabis Cultivation Facilities

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/greenhouse-horticulture|Greenhouse Horticulture]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B (wholesale cultivation segment)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most cannabis cultivators use seed-to-sale compliance tracking (Metrc, BioTrack) mandated by regulators, but operational growing tools are minimal; climate control, nutrition, and pest management are managed with spreadsheets and grower intuition
**Target Buyer:** Cultivation Director / Head Grower at a licensed cannabis production facility
**Automation Potential:** High — environmental control, fertigation, and phenotype-to-yield prediction are data-rich problems, but regulatory fragmentation across state markets limits scalable solutions

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cannabis cultivation operates under a regulatory framework unlike any other segment of greenhouse horticulture. State-mandated seed-to-sale tracking (Metrc in 20+ states, BioTrack, Leaf Data Systems) creates a compliance burden that consumes 15-25% of operational labor. Beyond compliance, the cultivation itself is intensely data-dependent: cannabis cultivators track 50-100 environmental and nutritional parameters per grow room, with small deviations in photoperiod, temperature DIF, VPD (vapor pressure deficit), and fertigation EC creating measurable differences in cannabinoid profiles and yield. Despite this data intensity, most facilities manage cultivation with spreadsheets, whiteboards, and the head grower's personal notebook. The industry's rapid growth (from zero to $30B+ in legal sales in 15 years) has outpaced the development of purpose-built cultivation management tools.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Seed-to-sale compliance platforms (Metrc, BioTrack, Leaf Data Systems) are mandated by regulators but serve compliance needs, not cultivation management. Cannabis-specific ERP platforms (Dutchie, Treez, Canix) focus on retail and distribution, not growing. Cultivation management tools (Trym, Grownetics, Aroya) exist but market penetration is under 20%, and most focus on environmental monitoring without analytics. No tool integrates environmental data, fertigation data, strain-specific performance history, and compliance tracking into a unified cultivation management platform.
