# HVAC Diagnostic AI from Symptom + Measurement Data

**Niche:** [[niches/hvac-contractors/diagnostic-troubleshooting/profile|Diagnostic Troubleshooting]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A cross-manufacturer diagnostic AI that takes symptom descriptions, system specifications, and field measurements (superheat, subcooling, pressures, amperage, temperature splits) and returns ranked probable diagnoses with step-by-step verification procedures — encoding the pattern recognition that expert HVAC technicians develop over 10,000+ service calls into a tool that any technician can use on any brand of equipment.

## The Problem
HVAC diagnostic troubleshooting is the industry's highest-value tacit knowledge problem. An experienced technician integrates 8-15 data points simultaneously to reach a diagnosis: customer complaint (no cooling, weak cooling, short cycling, strange noise, water leak, ice formation, high bills), system type (split system, heat pump, package unit, ductless mini-split), system age, refrigerant type (R-22, R-410A, R-32, R-454B), operating conditions (ambient temperature, indoor temperature, humidity), and measured parameters (suction pressure, discharge pressure, superheat, subcooling, compressor amperage vs. RLA, supply-return temperature split, static pressure). The diagnosis is a multiclass classification problem with ~30 common fault categories (low charge, overcharge, restricted metering device, failed compressor valve, dirty evaporator coil, dirty condenser coil, failed fan motor, failed capacitor, faulty reversing valve on heat pumps, duct leakage, undersized ductwork, etc.) and another ~50 uncommon faults. Expert technicians classify correctly 85-95% of the time. Junior technicians classify correctly 55-70% of the time. The 20-30% gap costs $5-8B annually in unnecessary repairs, repeat visits, and customer churn across the industry.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The defining barrier is training data. To build a diagnostic classifier, you need thousands of labeled examples: {system spec, symptoms, measurements} → {verified root cause}. This data does not exist in structured form anywhere. Service invoices record the repair performed ("replaced TXV") but not the diagnostic inputs (measurements, symptoms in structured form). The diagnosis that led to the repair is in the technician's head, not in the service record. Building the training dataset requires instrumenting the diagnostic process — having technicians record their measurements, symptoms, and diagnoses in structured form during actual service calls. This is a workflow change that slows technicians down by 3-5 minutes per call, and adoption requires either strong management mandate or a tool that provides immediate value to the technician (not just future training data collection). Additionally, HVAC systems span dozens of manufacturers with different normal operating parameters — a Lennox XC21 heat pump has different superheat/subcooling targets than a Carrier Infinity 26, and the diagnostic model must account for manufacturer-specific variations.

## What to Build
A three-phase product. Phase 1 (rule-based MVP, 3 months): A structured diagnostic workflow app where the technician enters system type, symptoms, and measurements, and a rule-based engine (encoding HVAC diagnostic trees from RSES, ACCA, and manufacturer service manuals) returns ranked probable causes with verification steps. This provides immediate value and begins collecting structured diagnostic data. Phase 2 (ML-enhanced, 12 months): After 5,000-10,000 diagnosed service calls with structured input data, train a gradient-boosted multiclass classifier and a Bayesian network that jointly predict root cause from the input features. The Bayesian network handles the causal reasoning (low refrigerant causes high superheat AND low subcooling, but a restricted metering device causes high superheat AND high subcooling), while the gradient-boosted model handles the statistical patterns that experts recognize but can't articulate ("something about the way the pressures are moving suggests a compressor valve problem"). Phase 3 (continuous learning): Every confirmed diagnosis feeds back into the training set. Disagreements between model prediction and technician diagnosis are flagged for expert review, creating a continuously improving system. The model's confidence calibration improves with each thousand diagnosed calls.

## Target Customer
HVAC service companies with 5-50 technicians, especially those with a mix of experienced and junior technicians where the diagnostic quality gap creates revenue and reputation risk. Also: HVAC training programs (trade schools, apprenticeship programs, manufacturer training centers) that need a diagnostic reasoning tool for instruction.

## Impact If Built
Closes the diagnostic accuracy gap between junior and senior technicians from 20-30% to under 5%, adding $100K-300K in annual revenue per junior technician through eliminated misdiagnosis costs and improved first-call fix rates. Reduces average diagnostic time by 30-50% for complex faults where even experienced technicians currently spend 20-45 minutes on differential diagnosis. Creates the first cross-manufacturer diagnostic knowledge base that improves with every service call across the entire user base.
