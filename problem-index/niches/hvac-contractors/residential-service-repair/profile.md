# Residential Service & Repair

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-45B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35-39%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — ServiceTitan is the dominant platform for shops with 5+ technicians, Housecall Pro and Jobber serve smaller operators. Flat-rate pricebooks (Coolfront, National Comfort Institute) are standard. Most shops still rely on experienced technicians' memory for diagnosis rather than any digital decision support.
**Target Buyer:** Residential HVAC service company owner (typically a former lead technician who started the business)
**Automation Potential:** High — service calls follow repeatable symptom-to-diagnosis patterns, flat-rate pricing is structured, and the seasonal demand cycle (AC in summer, heating in winter) creates predictable capacity planning needs

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential HVAC service is a dispatch-driven emergency business: a homeowner's AC stops cooling in July or furnace stops heating in January, and a technician drives out, diagnoses the failure, prices the repair from a flat-rate book, and either fixes it on-site or recommends a system replacement. The revenue model has two tiers — service/repair (average ticket $300-800) and system replacement (average ticket $6,000-15,000). The conversion from repair call to replacement sale depends entirely on the technician's ability to assess the system's remaining useful life, efficiency degradation, and refrigerant costs (R-22 phase-out makes older systems economically unviable to repair). Experienced technicians listen to compressor sounds, measure superheat/subcooling readings, check amperage draws, and integrate dozens of data points to determine whether a system is worth repairing — a judgment call that junior technicians consistently get wrong in both directions (recommending unnecessary replacements that destroy trust, or repairing systems that will fail again in 60 days). The industry also faces EPA 608 refrigerant handling compliance: every technician must be certified, every pound of refrigerant must be tracked, and violations carry fines up to $44,539 per day.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ServiceTitan dominates mid-market residential HVAC ($400+/month per tech) with dispatching, pricebook, invoicing, and membership agreement tracking. Coolfront provides a mobile-optimized flat-rate pricing app. FieldEdge integrates with QuickBooks for smaller shops. All handle scheduling, pricing, and invoicing — none help technicians diagnose problems. No tool connects symptom descriptions, measured readings (pressures, temperatures, amperage), and system specifications to suggest probable root causes. Diagnosis is entirely dependent on technician experience, and the knowledge gap between a 15-year veteran and a 2-year apprentice is the single largest constraint on service quality and revenue per call.
