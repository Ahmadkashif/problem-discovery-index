# Refrigeration Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10-13%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most refrigeration companies use generic field service software (ServiceTitan, FieldEdge) that doesn't understand refrigeration-specific equipment types, temperature logging requirements, or health department compliance. Refrigeration monitoring hardware exists (Emerson Copeland, Danfoss) but is disconnected from service management.
**Target Buyer:** Refrigeration service company owner serving restaurants, grocery stores, convenience stores, and cold storage facilities
**Automation Potential:** High — refrigeration equipment generates continuous temperature and pressure data that can predict failures before they cause food spoilage, but this data is rarely connected to service dispatch

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Refrigeration service is HVAC's high-stakes cousin. When a restaurant's walk-in cooler fails, the owner faces $5,000-50,000 in spoiled inventory within hours — not the discomfort of a warm house but a financial emergency. Refrigeration technicians service walk-in coolers, walk-in freezers, reach-in refrigeration units, ice machines, and beer cooler systems in restaurants, grocery stores, convenience stores, commercial kitchens, and cold storage facilities. The equipment runs 24/7/365 (unlike residential HVAC which cycles), operates at much lower temperatures (down to -20°F for freezers), and uses different refrigerants and system architectures than comfort cooling. The critical differentiator: refrigeration failures have regulatory consequences. Health departments require temperature logs for food safety (HACCP compliance), and a failed cooler that raises food above 41°F triggers mandatory food disposal. The technician must not only fix the equipment but also determine whether the food is still safe — a judgment call with legal liability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ServiceTitan and FieldEdge handle dispatch and invoicing but have no refrigeration-specific functionality — no equipment database for walk-in coolers vs. reach-in units, no temperature logging integration, no health department reporting templates. Emerson's Copeland digital platform and Danfoss's Alsense provide remote monitoring for refrigeration equipment but are manufacturer-specific and don't integrate with service management. IoT temperature monitoring systems (ComplianceMate, SensorPush) track temperatures for HACCP compliance but don't connect to service dispatch — they alert the restaurant manager, who then calls the refrigeration company manually. No platform connects equipment monitoring data to predictive service dispatch.
