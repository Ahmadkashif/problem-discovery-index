# Maintenance Agreement Renewal Prediction and Upsell Engine

**Niche:** [[niches/hvac-contractors/maintenance-agreement-management/profile|Maintenance Agreement Management]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A system that analyzes agreement customer behavior — service usage, repair frequency, tune-up attendance, equipment age, response to past communications — and predicts renewal probability, optimal renewal timing, and upsell readiness for every agreement in the portfolio, replacing the one-size-fits-all renewal email with targeted retention and growth actions.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #revenue-impact #data-integration #logistic-regression

## The Problem
HVAC maintenance agreements have a typical annual renewal rate of 70-80%, meaning 20-30% of agreement customers cancel each year. For a company with 1,000 agreements at $250/year average, that's $50K-75K in lost recurring revenue annually — plus the downstream impact on repair revenue and system replacement sales from those customers. The cancellation is rarely dramatic: the renewal email goes out, the customer doesn't respond, a follow-up call goes to voicemail, and the agreement lapses. The HVAC company treats all 1,000 renewals the same way — same email, same timing, same message — but the renewal risk varies enormously. A customer who used both seasonal tune-ups, called for a repair using their discount, and has a 10-year-old system (approaching replacement age) has a 95% renewal probability and is also an upsell candidate for a premium agreement with parts coverage. A customer who skipped both tune-ups, has a 2-year-old system under manufacturer warranty, and has never called for a repair has a 40% renewal probability and needs a different retention approach — perhaps a reminder of the benefits they're not using, or a downgrade to a lower-priced plan rather than outright cancellation.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Two barriers. First, the data is inside ServiceTitan/FieldEdge, and accessing it at the resolution needed for prediction requires API integration and data modeling that HVAC companies can't do themselves. The prediction requires features from multiple data sources: agreement details (tier, price, start date, renewal history), service history (tune-up attendance, repair calls, parts replaced, invoices paid), customer communication history (email opens, call logs), and equipment data (system age, type, remaining useful life). Second, the HVAC industry doesn't think about agreement management as a data science problem — it's handled by an office manager or CSR who sends renewal letters and makes follow-up calls using the same script for every customer. The concept of renewal risk scoring and targeted retention interventions is borrowed from SaaS churn prediction, a field that most HVAC companies have never encountered.

## What to Build
A maintenance agreement intelligence platform with three capabilities. Renewal prediction: a gradient-boosted binary classifier trained on historical agreement renewal/cancellation outcomes, predicting renewal probability for each active agreement based on 15-25 features (agreement tenure, tier, tune-up attendance rate, repair usage frequency, equipment age, customer lifetime value, days since last interaction, email engagement, payment promptness, time to renewal). The model produces a risk score for each agreement, enabling the office manager to prioritize retention efforts on the 200 at-risk agreements rather than treating all 1,000 the same. Upsell readiness scoring: a secondary model identifying agreement customers most likely to upgrade — based on equipment age (approaching replacement), repair frequency (would benefit from parts coverage), and customer spend trajectory (increasing engagement signals higher willingness-to-pay). Optimal timing: time-series analysis identifying the best renewal contact window — how many days before expiration, which day of week, which communication channel — personalized per customer segment.

## Target Customer
Residential HVAC companies with 300-5,000 maintenance agreements, generating $75K-1.5M in annual agreement revenue, using ServiceTitan or FieldEdge for agreement management. These companies have enough data for meaningful prediction (300+ renewal decisions per year) and enough revenue at stake to justify a $200-500/month subscription.

## Impact If Built
Increases agreement renewal rate from 75% to 85-90% through targeted retention interventions, recovering $25K-75K in annual recurring revenue per company. Identifies 15-25% of agreement customers who are ready for upsell, generating $30K-100K in incremental agreement revenue through tier upgrades. Creates a data-driven agreement management culture that compounds annually as the retained customer base grows.
