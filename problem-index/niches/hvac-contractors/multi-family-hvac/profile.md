# Multi-Family HVAC

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7-9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Property management platforms (AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi) handle work orders and tenant communication but have no HVAC equipment intelligence. HVAC contractors serving multi-family use ServiceTitan for dispatch but lose the property-level context once the service call is complete.
**Target Buyer:** Property management company maintenance director, or HVAC contractor specializing in multi-family service contracts
**Automation Potential:** High — multi-family HVAC is repetitive by design (same equipment across hundreds of units), making equipment lifecycle prediction, replacement budgeting, and complaint pattern analysis highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multi-family HVAC operates at the intersection of property management and HVAC service, and neither industry's tools serve it well. A 200-unit apartment complex has 200 individual HVAC systems (PTACs, split systems, or fan coil units), each with its own age, maintenance history, and remaining useful life. The property manager receives tenant comfort complaints ("my unit is too hot," "the AC makes a noise"), dispatches the HVAC contractor, and pays per service call. The HVAC contractor fixes the immediate problem and invoices — but neither party maintains a portfolio-level view of equipment health. The property manager doesn't know that Building C's units all have 15-year-old PTACs that are costing $800/year each in repairs and should be replaced as a batch at $2,500/unit rather than repaired one at a time. The HVAC contractor doesn't proactively recommend batch replacements because they profit from individual service calls. The disconnect wastes $100K-500K per year in unnecessary repairs on equipment that has exceeded its economic useful life, and creates tenant satisfaction problems from recurring complaints on units with chronically failing HVAC.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Property management platforms (AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi) track work orders by unit but don't track HVAC equipment — the work order says "HVAC not working, Unit 302" but doesn't record the equipment model, age, refrigerant type, or maintenance history. The HVAC contractor uses ServiceTitan, which tracks service calls by customer address but doesn't distinguish between 200 individual unit-level equipment records at the same address. Neither system produces the portfolio-level analysis that property managers need: which units have the oldest equipment, which have the highest repair frequency, which should be replaced this budget cycle, and what the 5-year capital expenditure forecast looks like for HVAC across the entire portfolio.
