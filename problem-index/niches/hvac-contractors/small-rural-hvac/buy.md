# ServiceTitan Too Expensive for Small Rural Shops

**Niche:** [[niches/hvac-contractors/small-rural-hvac/profile|Small & Rural HVAC]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** ServiceTitan costs $400+/month per technician and is designed for 10+ tech operations — small rural HVAC shops with 1-3 technicians need a field service platform at $50-100/month total that handles scheduling, invoicing, and basic customer management without the enterprise complexity.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
A 2-person HVAC shop in rural Montana doing $500K in annual revenue cannot justify $800-1,200/month for ServiceTitan. The owner-operator runs 3-5 service calls per day, handles his own dispatching (often from memory), writes invoices on paper or in QuickBooks, and manages a maintenance agreement list in a spreadsheet. The tools that dominate the HVAC industry — ServiceTitan ($400+/month per tech), FieldEdge ($300+/month per tech) — are priced for businesses with 5-25 technicians where the per-tech cost is spread across a larger revenue base. Housecall Pro and Jobber are cheaper ($49-149/month) but still designed for multi-tech operations with features (dispatching boards, GPS fleet tracking, marketing automation) that a 1-2 person shop doesn't need and finds confusing. The rural owner-operator wants three things: (1) a way to schedule and track service calls on his phone, (2) a way to generate professional invoices and collect payment on-site, and (3) a way to manage maintenance agreements (who's due for a tune-up this month). Everything else is overhead.

## What Already Exists
ServiceTitan is the market leader for residential HVAC but prohibitively expensive for small shops. Housecall Pro ($49-199/month) and Jobber ($49-149/month) are more affordable but still multi-tech-focused. Invoice apps (Square Invoices, Wave) handle billing but not scheduling. Google Calendar handles scheduling but not billing. QuickBooks handles accounting but requires manual data entry after every job. No tool is designed specifically for the 1-3 person trades shop that does 3-8 jobs per day and needs a simple all-in-one tool.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is simplicity and price, not features. A small rural HVAC shop needs: (1) A mobile-first daily schedule — today's jobs with customer name, address, equipment history, and one-tap navigation; (2) On-site invoicing with payment collection (card reader or tap-to-pay); (3) A maintenance agreement tracker — list of agreement customers, when each is due for service, automatic reminders; (4) Basic customer history — what equipment does this customer have, when was it last serviced, what was done. These four features exist in every field service platform, but they're buried under dispatching dashboards, marketing modules, and reporting suites designed for 15-person offices. The product opportunity is a stripped-down, $50/month tool that does these four things well and nothing else.

## Target Customer
HVAC shops with 1-3 technicians and $200K-800K in annual revenue, currently using paper/QuickBooks/Google Calendar, located in rural and small-town markets where the customer base is 500-2,000 accounts within a 50-mile radius.

## Impact If Solved
Saves 5-8 hours per week of administrative time (scheduling, invoicing, agreement tracking) currently done manually, equivalent to adding half a day of billable service per week ($500-800/week in recovered revenue). Reduces missed maintenance agreement appointments — which currently cost rural shops 10-20% of agreement renewal rates because customers don't get reminded and let agreements lapse.
