# Indoor Air Quality

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hvac-contractors|HVAC Contractors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7-9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — IAQ testing equipment is digital (particle counters, CO2 monitors, VOC sensors) but the assessment-to-recommendation workflow is entirely manual. No platform connects IAQ test results to remediation recommendations. Reports are generated in Word/Excel from scratch for each client.
**Target Buyer:** IAQ testing and remediation company owner, or HVAC contractor expanding into IAQ services post-COVID
**Automation Potential:** High — IAQ assessment follows a structured data-in, recommendation-out pattern that maps directly to a rule-based or ML-driven recommendation engine

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Indoor air quality became a mainstream concern during COVID-19 and has sustained post-pandemic momentum. IAQ companies test for particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10), carbon dioxide (CO2 — proxy for ventilation adequacy), volatile organic compounds (VOCs), humidity, mold spore counts, radon, and carbon monoxide in residential and commercial spaces. The testing is straightforward — deploy sensors, collect data over 24-72 hours, retrieve. The value creation happens in the assessment: interpreting results against EPA guidelines, ASHRAE 62.1/62.2 ventilation standards, and WHO recommendations, then recommending specific remediation measures — improved filtration (MERV-13+ filters, HEPA filtration), UV-C germicidal treatment, ERV/HRV installation, duct sealing, humidity control, or source elimination. Experienced IAQ consultants synthesize test results, building characteristics, occupant complaints, and their knowledge of available remediation technologies into a prioritized recommendation plan. Newer practitioners collect the data but struggle with the interpretation-to-recommendation step, often over-recommending expensive solutions or missing the root cause entirely.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IAQ testing instruments (TSI particle counters, Kanomax multi-gas analyzers, Temtop monitors) collect data accurately but export it as CSV files or proprietary formats with no analytical layer. Report generation is manual — an IAQ consultant creates a Word document with test results, photos, and recommendations for each project. No platform ingests test data and generates standardized IAQ assessment reports with evidence-based remediation recommendations. The closest tools are energy audit platforms (TREAT, AkitaBox) that handle some ventilation analysis but don't cover the full IAQ testing spectrum or recommendation workflow.
