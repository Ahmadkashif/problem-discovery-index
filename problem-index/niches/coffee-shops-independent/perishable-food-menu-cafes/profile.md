# Perishable Inventory & Food-Menu Cafes

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B (food-forward segment)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Use POS for sales tracking but manage perishable ordering, prep quantities, and waste through gut feel and whiteboards
**Target Buyer:** Owner or kitchen manager at a cafe with a significant food menu (sandwiches, salads, baked goods) alongside coffee
**Automation Potential:** High — Daily ordering, prep quantity, and waste decisions are rule-heavy and data-rich but entirely manual

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Cafes with food menus (not just pastries from an outside bakery, but in-house prep of sandwiches, salads, breakfast items, baked goods) face a fundamentally different inventory challenge than drink-only shops. Coffee beans last weeks; a turkey avocado sandwich has a 24-hour window. These owners make 20-40 individual prep quantity decisions every morning ("how many croissants to bake, how many sandwiches to build") based on gut feel, and each wrong decision either wastes food (15-25% waste rate) or loses a sale. The decisions are highly automatable because the inputs (POS history, day of week, weather, local events) and the cost functions (waste cost vs. stockout cost) are well-defined.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MarketMan and BlueCart handle ingredient ordering for restaurants but are overbuilt for a cafe's simpler supply chain. No tool specifically handles the daily "how many of each prep item to make" decision for a cafe food menu. POS systems track what sold but don't connect backward to prep quantities or forward to ordering recommendations. Waste tracking is almost always manual — a clipboard in the kitchen at best.
