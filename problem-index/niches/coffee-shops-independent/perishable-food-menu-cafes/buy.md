# Cafe-Scale Ingredient Ordering Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/coffee-shops-independent/perishable-food-menu-cafes/profile|Perishable Inventory & Food-Menu Cafes]]
**Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Restaurant inventory systems exist but are overbuilt for a cafe ordering from 3 suppliers with 30 ingredients — the setup alone takes longer than a year of manual ordering.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A food-forward cafe orders from 3-5 suppliers (broadline distributor, local bakery, dairy, produce vendor, specialty items) 2-3 times per week. Each order takes 15-30 minutes of checking stock, estimating needs for the next 2-3 days, and placing the order by phone, text, or vendor portal. Over a week, this is 2-4 hours of ordering time — repetitive, error-prone, and done under time pressure (the morning order must be placed by 10am for next-day delivery). Mistakes mean either emergency grocery store runs (3x cost) or waste from over-ordering.

## What Already Exists
MarketMan, BlueCart, and Parsley offer restaurant ingredient ordering with par-level management and vendor integration. These tools are designed for full-service restaurants with 50-200 ingredients, dedicated kitchen managers, and multiple prep stations. Setup requires entering complete recipes, defining par levels for every ingredient, and connecting vendor EDI systems — a 10-20 hour configuration process that a cafe owner with 30 ingredients and no IT support won't complete.

## The Customization Gap
A cafe needs a dramatically simplified version: 30-50 ingredients (not 200), 3-5 vendors (not 15), and par-level recommendations derived from POS sales data (not manual configuration). The system should auto-suggest orders based on "you sold 45 turkey sandwiches this week, you have 2 lbs of turkey left, your next delivery is Thursday — order 10 lbs." Setup should take under 30 minutes, not 10 hours. Integration with cafe-scale suppliers (who use text messages, not EDI) is essential.

## Target Customer
Cafe owners spending 2-4 hours/week on ingredient ordering across multiple suppliers, currently tracking stock mentally or on a whiteboard.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces ordering time from 2-4 hours/week to 30 minutes. Prevents 1-2 emergency grocery runs per week ($50-$150 each in premium pricing and lost time). Cuts ingredient waste by 10-15% through par-level discipline.
