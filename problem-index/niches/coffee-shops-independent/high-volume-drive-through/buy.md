# Drive-Through Queue Management

**Niche:** [[niches/coffee-shops-independent/high-volume-drive-through/profile|High-Volume Drive-Through Independents]]
**Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QSR queue management systems exist but are designed for burger chains, not independent coffee shops with different menu complexity and seasonal drink builds.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
When the drive-through line hits 8+ cars, an experienced owner knows to switch to a limited "rush menu" (no blended drinks, no food), deploy a runner with an iPad to pre-take orders, and shift a barista from lobby to drive-through. These interventions are ad hoc and depend on the owner being present. No system triggers these escalation protocols automatically based on real-time queue state.

## What Already Exists
QSR chains use systems like Xenial, Olo, and proprietary queue management tools that trigger menu simplification, kitchen alerts, and staffing redeployment based on queue length. HME's ZOOM Nitro provides real-time queue monitoring. All of these are designed for multi-location chains with standardized menus, lane configurations, and corporate IT support.

## The Customization Gap
An independent coffee drive-through has a different menu structure (espresso drinks take 30-90 seconds to build vs. 5-second burger assembly), different queue geometry (single lane, no dual-point ordering), and no corporate IT team. The system needs to integrate with Square/Toast POS, handle drink-build-time variability (a quad-shot oat milk latte takes 3x longer than a drip coffee), and trigger owner-configurable escalation rules (limit blended drinks when queue > 6 cars) rather than chain-standardized protocols.

## Target Customer
Drive-through independent shop owners doing 300+ cars/day who lose 5-15 customers daily to drive-away during peak congestion.

## Impact If Solved
Captures 5-15 daily drive-aways worth $5-$8 each, adding $10K-$40K in annual revenue. Reduces peak-hour customer complaints about wait times by 30-50%.
