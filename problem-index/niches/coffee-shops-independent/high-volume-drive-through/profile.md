# High-Volume Drive-Through Independents

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Use POS systems with drive-through timers but lack queue analytics, speed-of-service optimization, and predictive staffing tools
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator focused on throughput, speed, and morning rush capture
**Automation Potential:** High — Every second of drive-through time is measurable and optimizable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Drive-through independents compete directly against Starbucks and Dutch Bros on speed and convenience, not ambiance. Their unit economics are entirely different from sit-down cafes: revenue per square foot is 2-3x higher but success depends on throughput measured in cars-per-hour during the 6:30-8:30am window. A 15-second delay per car during peak means 8-10 lost customers per hour. These shops need speed-of-service analytics, queue management, and predictive staffing tools — none of which exist for independent operators (chains build proprietary systems).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Square and Toast provide basic drive-through POS capability but lack queue time tracking, speed-of-service analytics, or throughput optimization. Drive-through timer hardware (HME, Delphi) exists but is designed for chain QSR and priced at $3,000-$8,000 per lane — overkill for a single-location independent. No tool correlates staffing levels with throughput metrics for independents.
