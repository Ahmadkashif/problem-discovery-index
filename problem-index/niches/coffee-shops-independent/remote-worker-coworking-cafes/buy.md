# Time-Based WiFi and Table Management

**Niche:** [[niches/coffee-shops-independent/remote-worker-coworking-cafes/profile|Remote-Worker & Coworking Cafes]]
**Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Captive WiFi portals and table management systems exist separately — neither handles time-based workspace access tied to POS purchase requirements.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
An owner wants to offer free WiFi for the first 2 hours after a purchase, then require an additional purchase or a small hourly fee — a simple policy that captures the value of workspace usage without being hostile. Implementing this requires connecting the WiFi captive portal to the POS system so that purchase verification gates internet access, and no off-the-shelf solution does this for a single-location cafe. The owner either offers unlimited free WiFi (subsidizing 6-hour laptop campers) or posts passive-aggressive signs about purchase minimums that damage the brand.

## What Already Exists
Captive WiFi portals (Purple WiFi, Yelp WiFi, Zenreach) gate access behind email capture or social media check-ins but have no POS integration for time-based purchase verification. Restaurant table management systems (Resy, OpenTable) manage reservations but don't model dwell time or workspace-style occupancy. Coworking platforms (Optix) handle time-based access but require membership models unsuited for casual cafe visitors.

## The Customization Gap
The solution needs to connect Square/Toast POS purchase events to WiFi access duration — "Customer A bought a latte at 9:15am, grant 2 hours of WiFi, prompt for additional purchase at 11:15am." This requires a lightweight POS-to-WiFi integration that no current vendor provides for independent cafes. The system also needs to handle exceptions gracefully (group meetings where one person buys for the table, regular customers who should get extended access automatically).

## Target Customer
Cafe owners offering free WiFi who want to monetize workspace usage without switching to a formal coworking membership model.

## Impact If Solved
Converts 20-30% of long-dwell remote workers into second-purchase customers, adding $500-$1,500/month in incremental revenue while maintaining the welcoming atmosphere that attracts workspace seekers in the first place.
