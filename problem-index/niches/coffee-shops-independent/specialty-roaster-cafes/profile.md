# Specialty Roaster-Cafes

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/coffee-shops-independent|Independent Coffee Shops]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25-30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Use specialty roasting software (Cropster, RoastLog) for production but lack integration between roasting, retail POS, and wholesale channels
**Target Buyer:** Owner-roaster managing both production roasting and retail cafe operations
**Automation Potential:** High — Roast profiling, green bean inventory, wholesale order management, and retail demand all generate data that is never connected

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Roaster-cafes operate two businesses under one roof: a production roasting operation with green bean sourcing, roast profiling, and quality control, and a retail cafe with baristas, food, and customer service. The owner-roaster splits time between the roasting room and the floor, and the two sides rarely share data — roasting software doesn't know what's selling at the retail counter, and the POS doesn't know what's in the roaster queue. This dual-business complexity creates unique inventory, scheduling, and quality control problems that pure retail coffee shops don't face. Wholesale accounts (restaurants, grocery, subscription) add a third revenue stream with different fulfillment cadences.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Cropster and RoastLog handle roast profiling and green bean inventory but don't integrate with Square/Toast POS for demand-driven roast scheduling. Wholesale order management is typically a spreadsheet or email chain. No tool connects retail velocity data to roasting schedules to green bean purchasing — the owner does this mentally, and when they're sick or on vacation, the system breaks.
