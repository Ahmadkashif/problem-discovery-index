## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Allergen-free food manufacturers — producing certified gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free, or "free-from-top-8" products — operate under a higher standard than manufacturers that simply label allergens. They must demonstrate the absence of allergens, not just declare their presence. This requires: dedicated production lines or validated cleaning procedures between allergen and allergen-free runs, environmental monitoring (swab testing for allergen residues), supplier allergen management programs (verifying that incoming ingredients are truly free from cross-contact), and third-party certification audits (GFCO for gluten-free, FARE for various allergens). The margin for error is zero — a child with severe peanut allergy consuming a product labeled "peanut-free" that contains peanut residue from cross-contact is a medical emergency and a liability catastrophe.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SafetyChain and Alchemy provide food safety software with allergen modules. ERP allergen tracking flags ingredients containing major allergens. No platform models the cross-contact risk in sufficient detail for allergen-free certification: assessing whether a shared facility, shared supplier, or shared transportation chain could introduce trace allergen contamination into an allergen-free product — the probabilistic risk assessment that allergen-free manufacturers must perform for every ingredient and every production scenario.
