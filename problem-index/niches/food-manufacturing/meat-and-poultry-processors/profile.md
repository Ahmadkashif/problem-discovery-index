**Target Buyer:** Plant manager or QA director at USDA-inspected meat and poultry processing facilities
**Automation Potential:** High — carcass grading, trim yield optimization, and USDA inspection documentation are high-value automation targets

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Meat and poultry processors operate under USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS) continuous inspection — a federal inspector is physically present in the plant during all production hours, unlike FDA-regulated food manufacturers who receive periodic inspections. This continuous oversight creates unique documentation requirements: pre-operational sanitation checks verified by the inspector, HACCP monitoring records reviewed by the inspector, and NR (Non-compliance Record) responses documented and submitted within regulatory timeframes. Additionally, meat processing involves carcass yield optimization — maximizing the value extracted from each animal through grading, cutting, and trim decisions that experienced butchers and cutters make based on years of visual assessment.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CSB-System, Cimcorp, and JBS's proprietary systems provide meat processing ERP. USDA inspectors use PHIS (Public Health Information System) for documentation. Vision grading systems (VBP, E+V) exist for automated carcass grading at large packing houses. Mid-size processors (200-500 employees) lack affordable grading and yield optimization tools — their cutters make trim decisions visually, and yield variability between cutters can be 3-5% on the same raw material.
