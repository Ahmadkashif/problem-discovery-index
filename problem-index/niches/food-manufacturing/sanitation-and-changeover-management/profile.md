# Sanitation & Changeover Management

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-manufacturing|Food Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-15B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~1-2% (embedded labor and chemical cost across all food plants)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — sanitation SOPs exist on paper, cleaning verification is manual visual inspection, and changeover scheduling is based on experience rather than data
**Target Buyer:** Sanitation supervisor or plant manager at food manufacturing plants with multiple product lines on shared equipment
**Automation Potential:** High — sanitation verification, changeover scheduling, and cleaning chemical optimization follow deterministic procedures that are currently executed and verified manually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Sanitation and changeover management is a horizontal function across all food manufacturing — every plant that runs multiple products on shared equipment must clean between product runs to prevent cross-contamination, allergen carryover, and microbial buildup. Sanitation labor represents 10-15% of total plant labor cost. Changeover time (cleaning + line setup between products) is the largest source of planned downtime, consuming 15-25% of available production time. The sanitation process follows documented CIP (Clean-in-Place) and COP (Clean-out-of-Place) procedures, but verification of cleaning effectiveness is predominantly visual inspection supplemented by ATP swab testing — a judgment call that experienced sanitation supervisors make reliably but that varies with shift, worker, and inspection rigor.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Sanitation chemical suppliers (Diversey, Ecolab) provide cleaning chemistry and some digital monitoring (CIP system monitoring, chemical concentration verification). MES platforms log CIP cycle parameters (time, temperature, concentration). No platform optimizes the changeover schedule across products to minimize total sanitation time (which products to run in which sequence to reduce the number of full allergen cleans), verifies cleaning effectiveness through objective measurement rather than visual inspection, or tracks sanitation worker performance to identify training gaps.
