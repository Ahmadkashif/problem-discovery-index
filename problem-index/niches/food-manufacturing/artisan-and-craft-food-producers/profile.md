# Artisan & Craft Food Producers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-manufacturing|Food Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~2-3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most operate with paper batch records, handwritten HACCP logs, and QuickBooks invoicing with no MES or production tracking
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator or head of production at small-batch food companies with 5-50 employees
**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — batch record keeping, ingredient traceability, and nutrition labeling are automatable but production processes are inherently manual and artisan

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Artisan and craft food producers — small-batch cheese makers, artisan bread bakeries, craft chocolate manufacturers, specialty sauce producers, small-batch fermentation companies — produce premium products where the production process is the brand story. These companies operate with 5-50 employees, produce in small batches (50-500 units per run), and sell through farmers markets, specialty retailers, and DTC e-commerce. They face the same FDA FSMA and state health department requirements as large manufacturers but lack the staff, systems, and expertise to manage compliance efficiently. The founder is typically a food artisan who started a business, not a food scientist who designed a production system — and the compliance burden threatens to consume the creative energy that makes the product special.

## Current Tools & Gaps
QuickBooks for invoicing. Paper batch records. Handwritten HACCP logs. Spreadsheets for ingredient inventory. No tool manages the artisan food production workflow: batch formulation tracking, ingredient traceability (which lot of cream went into which batch of cheese), nutrition label generation, and FDA compliance documentation — at a price and complexity appropriate for a 10-person cheese-making operation.
