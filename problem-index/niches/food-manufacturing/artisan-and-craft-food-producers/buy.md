# Simplified Nutrition Labeling for Small-Batch Products

**Niche:** [[niches/food-manufacturing/artisan-and-craft-food-producers/profile|Artisan & Craft Food Producers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-manufacturing|Food Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Nutrition labeling software like Genesis R&D and ESHA Research generates FDA-compliant Nutrition Facts panels, but they're designed for food scientists at large companies — a $200-$400/month subscription with a learning curve measured in weeks, not hours, that a small-batch hot sauce maker can't justify or navigate.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
FDA requires Nutrition Facts labels on all packaged food products. Generating a compliant label requires: entering each ingredient with its nutritional composition, specifying the recipe in terms of weight or volume, selecting the appropriate serving size, and generating a label that meets 21 CFR 101 formatting requirements. Large food companies have food scientists and Genesis R&D subscriptions to handle this. Small artisan producers either: pay a lab $300-$500 per product for nutritional analysis, use free online calculators that may not be FDA-compliant, or skip the requirement and hope for the best. When a producer with 20 SKUs needs to update labels (recipe change, regulatory update, new format), the cost and effort multiply.

## What Already Exists
Genesis R&D (ESHA Research) and Nutritionist Pro are the standard platforms for food nutrition analysis and label generation. MenuCalc and ReciPal offer lower-cost alternatives for restaurants and small food businesses. The professional tools (Genesis, Nutritionist Pro) are priced and designed for food science teams at large companies. The simplified tools (ReciPal, MenuCalc) handle basic label generation but may lack the precision and regulatory currency that FDA compliance requires.

## The Customization Gap
A small-producer nutrition labeling tool needs: (1) simplified ingredient entry — search a comprehensive nutrient database (USDA FoodData Central) by common ingredient names, not by scientific nomenclature; (2) recipe-to-label workflow — enter the recipe as the producer actually makes it (cups, tablespoons, pounds), not in grams per 100g; (3) FDA-compliant label output — formatted per 21 CFR 101, including allergen declarations, with support for the current and updated Nutrition Facts formats; (4) multi-product management at an affordable price — $10-$20/month flat rate, not $200+/month or $300/product for lab analysis.

## Target Customer
Artisan food producers with 5-50 SKUs who currently pay for lab analysis or use non-compliant calculators for nutrition labeling.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces nutrition label cost from $300-$500 per product (lab analysis) to $10-$20/month for unlimited products. Enables label updates within minutes when recipes change, rather than requiring new lab analysis. Ensures FDA compliance for producers who are currently guessing. For a producer with 20 SKUs, replacing lab analysis with software saves $6,000-$10,000 upfront and enables responsive label management.
