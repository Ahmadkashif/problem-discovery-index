# HR for Skilled Trades & Field Workforces

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — workers don't have company email addresses, don't sit at desks, and often don't speak English as a first language — standard HRIS tools fail at the last mile of employee interaction
**Target Buyer:** HR consultant serving general contractors, HVAC companies, plumbing firms, landscaping companies, and manufacturers with 20-200 field employees
**Automation Potential:** Medium — compliance requirements are the same as any employer, but delivery mechanisms must adapt to a workforce that interacts via text message, not email, and may not have reliable internet access during work hours

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
HR for skilled trades companies has unique challenges that standard HR tools don't address: workers don't have company email addresses (communication must be via text or WhatsApp), they work at dispersed job sites with limited connectivity, many are Spanish-speaking with limited English literacy, turnover is 40-60% annually (making onboarding a constant process rather than an occasional event), and safety compliance (OSHA) creates HR obligations beyond what office-based employers face. The HR consultant serving these companies must deliver handbooks in Spanish, collect I-9 documents from workers who may not have smartphones, manage drug testing and background check workflows adapted for high-volume hiring, and handle workers' comp claims at a rate 5-10x higher than office environments.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard HRIS platforms (BambooHR, Gusto) assume email-based communication and desk-based employees. Construction-specific HR tools (Arcoro, eBacon, LCPtracker) handle certified payroll and prevailing wage compliance but not general HR administration. Workforce management apps (Connecteam, Sling) handle scheduling and time tracking for field workers but don't connect to HR compliance workflows. No platform bridges the gap between field workforce management and HR administration for companies with 20-200 trade workers.
