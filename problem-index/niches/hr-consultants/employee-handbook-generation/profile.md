# Employee Handbook Generation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-2B (embedded in consulting project fees)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — SixFifty and Blissbook exist but most consultants still build handbooks in Word from firm templates
**Target Buyer:** HR consultant who generates 20-50 employee handbooks per year for new and existing clients
**Automation Potential:** High — handbooks are 80% templated content adapted to state-specific requirements, company policies, and industry norms, following deterministic rules for which provisions are required

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Employee handbook creation is one of the most common deliverables an HR consultant produces. Every new client engagement starts with a handbook, and existing clients need annual updates to reflect legal changes. A handbook is 40-80 pages covering 30-40 policy areas: at-will employment, EEO, anti-harassment, leave policies (FMLA, state-specific), attendance, PTO, dress code, technology use, social media, conflict of interest, discipline, and termination. Roughly 80% of handbook content follows standard templates adapted for the client's state(s), industry, and preferences. The remaining 20% requires genuine customization — unique PTO structures, industry-specific safety policies, cultural-fit language. Despite the template-heavy nature, most consultants spend 15-25 hours per handbook because they manually assemble provisions, cross-reference state requirements, and ensure internal consistency.

## Current Tools & Gaps
SixFifty ($500-800 per handbook) generates state-specific handbooks through an interview-style questionnaire. Blissbook ($50-150/month per client) provides digital handbook hosting and acknowledgment tracking. Neither supports the multi-client consultant workflow: SixFifty generates one handbook at a time with no cross-client template management, and Blissbook hosts handbooks but doesn't generate them. Most consultants still work from Word templates maintained in their firm's shared drive, manually inserting or removing state-specific provisions.
