# Mosey-Style State Registration with Ongoing Compliance Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/hr-consultants/remote-workforce-compliance/profile|Remote Workforce Compliance]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Mosey handles initial state registrations (tax accounts, unemployment insurance) but stops there — the HR consultant needs ongoing compliance tracking that continues after registration: required postings maintained, leave policies updated, handbook provisions current, and annual reporting filed.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
State registration is a one-time task; compliance is ongoing. Mosey and similar services register the company with state tax and unemployment agencies, but they don't manage the continuing obligations: updating workplace postings when laws change, adding state-specific handbook provisions when the company expands into new states, filing annual reports (state unemployment tax returns, workers' comp audits), and adjusting leave policies when state legislation changes. The HR consultant handles all of this ongoing compliance work manually, typically for 10-30 clients simultaneously — each in a different set of states.

## What Already Exists
Mosey ($200-400/month) automates state registration and provides a compliance calendar for registered obligations. Rippling can handle multi-state payroll and some compliance, but it is an HRIS platform, not a consultant tool. SixFifty generates state-specific employment documents. Mineral provides legal updates. None integrate registration, ongoing compliance management, and multi-client portfolio tracking.

## The Customization Gap
The consultant needs a post-registration compliance layer: (1) automated posting compliance — when a state updates its required workplace postings, the system generates the updated electronic posting and pushes it to the client, (2) handbook maintenance — when a state changes its leave, meal/break, or pay transparency requirements, the system flags affected handbook sections with recommended language, (3) annual compliance calendar — state-specific filings (unemployment tax returns, workers' comp audits, EEO-1) with auto-generated preparation checklists, and (4) multi-client view — which clients have postings out of date, which need handbook updates for new state laws, which have upcoming filing deadlines. This ongoing compliance management is what the consultant delivers; the tool should support it, not just the initial registration.

## Target Customer
HR consultants who use Mosey or similar services for state registration but still manage ongoing compliance manually through spreadsheets and memory.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces ongoing multi-state compliance management from 2-4 hours per client per month to 30-60 minutes. Ensures posting and handbook compliance stay current between annual reviews, preventing the accumulation of 5-10 compliance gaps per client per year that create enforcement exposure.
