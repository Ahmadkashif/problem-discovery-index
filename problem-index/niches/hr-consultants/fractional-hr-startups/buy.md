# Startup HR Playbook Templates for Gusto/Rippling

**Niche:** [[niches/hr-consultants/fractional-hr-startups/profile|Fractional HR for Startups]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Gusto and Rippling provide the HRIS platform, but each startup requires the same foundational HR setup (handbook, policies, onboarding checklist, review cycle) — the fractional HR leader rebuilds this from scratch for every new client because no startup-stage-specific template library exists within these platforms.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a fractional HR leader onboards a new startup client, they must build the HR foundation: employee handbook (state-specific), offer letter templates, I-9 and benefits enrollment workflows, performance review framework, PTO policy, expense policy, and required compliance postings. This setup takes 20-40 hours per client. The fractional HR leader has done it dozens of times and could recite the checklist from memory, but each HRIS platform (Gusto, Rippling) requires different configuration steps, and each state adds different policy requirements. The content is 80% identical across startups of the same stage and state composition, but no pre-built template library exists that is both HRIS-native and startup-stage-appropriate.

## What Already Exists
Gusto and Rippling both offer onboarding workflows and document templates, but they're generic — designed for any company, not specifically for a Series A startup with 15 employees in California and New York. SixFifty generates state-specific handbooks but doesn't integrate with HRIS onboarding workflows. SHRM provides policy templates but they're written for established companies, not startups with unlimited PTO and equity compensation.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a startup-stage-appropriate template library that integrates with major HRIS platforms: (1) stage-specific templates (pre-seed: minimal handbook with at-will statement and harassment policy only; Series A: full handbook with equity section; Series B: performance review framework and promotion ladder), (2) state-specific policy modules that auto-configure based on where the startup's employees are located, (3) one-click deployment into the client's Gusto or Rippling instance rather than manual PDF uploads, and (4) annual update notifications when state laws change policies that were deployed. The fractional HR leader selects "Series A, employees in CA/NY/TX" and gets a deployable HR infrastructure in 2 hours instead of 20.

## Target Customer
Fractional HR leaders who onboard 4-8 new startup clients per year and currently spend 20-40 hours per client on foundational HR setup.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces new client HR setup from 20-40 hours to 3-5 hours. For a fractional HR leader onboarding 6 new clients per year, this saves 90-210 hours annually — time redirected to strategic advisory work that justifies higher billing rates.
