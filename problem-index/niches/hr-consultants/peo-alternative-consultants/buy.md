# Benefits Administration Platform Adapted for Multi-Client HR Consultants

**Niche:** [[niches/hr-consultants/peo-alternative-consultants/profile|PEO Alternative Consultants]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Benefits administration platforms like Employee Navigator and Ease exist but assume a single employer or benefits broker — HR consultants managing benefits for 30 clients need a multi-tenant view with consultant-level controls that these platforms don't provide.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
An HR consultant managing benefits for 30 clients must administer open enrollment, qualifying life events, COBRA notices, and carrier data feeds for each client separately. Benefits platforms (Employee Navigator, Ease, PlanSource) provide per-company administration portals, but the consultant must log into each client's instance separately, with no cross-client view. During open enrollment season (October-December), the consultant manages 20-30 concurrent enrollment windows across different carriers, different plan designs, and different employee communication schedules — all tracked in spreadsheets because no benefits platform provides a consultant-level portfolio view.

## What Already Exists
Employee Navigator ($4-6/employee/month) and Ease ($3-5/employee/month) are well-built benefits administration platforms that handle enrollment, carrier feeds, COBRA, and compliance reporting. They support benefits broker access, but the broker model assumes the broker is managing plan selection and quoting, not day-to-day benefits administration. PlanSource and Businessolver target larger employers (500+). All are designed for per-company administration.

## The Customization Gap
HR consultants need: (1) a portfolio dashboard showing open enrollment status across all clients (which clients have completed enrollment, which have pending elections, which have data feed errors), (2) batch communication tools (send enrollment reminders to all employees across all clients in a single workflow, with client-specific branding), (3) consultant-level reporting (total lives under management, carrier distribution across portfolio, compliance status per client), and (4) pricing that reflects the consultant model ($100-200/month for the practice plus per-employee fees) rather than per-company flat rates. The underlying benefits administration functionality is identical — the gap is the multi-client orchestration layer.

## Target Customer
PEO-alternative HR consultants administering benefits for 15+ client companies, currently managing each client's enrollment in a separate platform instance with no unified view.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces open enrollment management overhead by 40-50% during the October-December peak. Catches missing enrollments and data feed errors across the portfolio that currently slip through, preventing the "employee went to the doctor and their insurance wasn't active" disasters that damage client relationships.
