# PEO Alternative Consultants

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hr-consultants|HR Consultants]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — these consultants replicate PEO services manually because no integrated platform exists for the non-co-employment model
**Target Buyer:** HR consulting firm principal offering "PEO-level services without PEO co-employment" to SMBs with 15-100 employees
**Automation Potential:** High — the services (payroll, benefits, compliance, HR admin) are identical to PEO offerings but delivered without the integrated platform PEOs provide

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Professional Employer Organizations (PEOs) like TriNet, ADP TotalSource, and Insperity provide comprehensive HR outsourcing through co-employment — the PEO becomes the employer of record for tax, benefits, and compliance purposes. Many SMBs want PEO-level services (payroll, benefits, compliance, workers' comp) but resist co-employment due to: loss of employer identity, restrictions on hiring/firing autonomy, difficulty switching PEOs if dissatisfied, and concerns about the PEO's financial stability. HR consultants fill this gap by manually assembling the same services without co-employment: they set up the client on a standalone payroll provider (Gusto, Paychex), broker benefits through an independent agency, manage compliance, and administer HR processes. The problem is that without the PEO's integrated platform, every service is a separate vendor, a separate login, and a separate data silo.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PEOs provide a single integrated platform because they control the employment relationship. PEO-alternative consultants must stitch together: payroll (Gusto/Paychex), benefits administration (Employee Navigator, Ease), compliance monitoring (Mineral), HR document management (BambooHR), workers' comp (standalone policy), and reporting — using 5-7 separate systems with no integration layer. Each client requires configuration across all systems, and the consultant spends significant time on data re-entry and cross-system reconciliation.
