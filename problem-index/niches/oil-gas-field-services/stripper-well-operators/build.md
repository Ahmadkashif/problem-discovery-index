# Mobile-First Stripper Well Operations Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/oil-gas-field-services/stripper-well-operators/profile|Stripper Well Operators]]
**Industry:** [[industries/oil-gas-field-services|Oil & Gas Field Services]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No operations platform is designed for the one-person stripper well operator who manages 10-100 wells with a smartphone, a truck, and a pocket notebook.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A stripper well operator drives 50-200 miles per day visiting 15-30 wells. At each well, they: check the pump unit (is it running?), gauge the tank battery (how many barrels since last visit?), check for leaks and spills, adjust equipment if needed, and record observations in a pocket notebook. Back at the kitchen table that evening, they transfer notebook entries into a ledger, calculate daily production, and figure out which wells need attention tomorrow. Their "operations platform" is a combination of a spiral notebook, a wall calendar, a Rolodex of service company phone numbers, and 30 years of experience. When the operator is incapacitated (illness, injury), nobody else can run the lease because all operational knowledge lives in their head and notebook.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The stripper well market has 100,000+ operators but most are one-person or family operations with $0-$200/month technology budgets. Enterprise oilfield software vendors cannot profitably serve accounts this small. The feature requirements are also different from enterprise software: a stripper well operator does not need multi-user permissions, approval workflows, or ERP integration — they need a fast way to record what they see at each well, track production, schedule maintenance, and comply with state reporting requirements. The simplicity of the need contrasts with the complexity of existing solutions.

## What to Build
A smartphone app that is the stripper well operator's digital notebook: GPS auto-detects which well the operator is visiting, a one-tap gauge reading entry records current tank level (the app calculates production since last visit from tank capacity and level change), a quick-capture form logs pump status, observations, and maintenance performed, a photo function documents conditions for reference, and a calendar view shows upcoming maintenance, state reporting deadlines, and service company appointments. All data syncs to a simple web dashboard where the operator can review production trends, generate state reports, and share access with a backup operator. Pricing: $50-$100/month for unlimited wells.

## Target Customer
Independent stripper well operators managing 10-100 wells who currently track everything on paper and want to digitize without adopting enterprise-scale software.

## Impact If Built
Saves 5-10 hours per week of manual record-keeping (production logs, maintenance tracking, reporting), provides the first-ever digital production history for wells that have never been monitored, and creates an operational continuity plan (a backup person can access the app and run the lease if the primary operator is unavailable).
