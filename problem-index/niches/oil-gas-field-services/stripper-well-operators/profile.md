# Stripper Well Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/oil-gas-field-services|Oil & Gas Field Services]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-15B (annual production value)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Stripper wells represent ~10% of US oil production from ~380,000 wells — the highest well count of any segment
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most stripper well operators have no SCADA, no telemetry, and no digital production tracking; pumpers visit wells physically and record production on paper
**Target Buyer:** Independent operator / pumper / lease operator
**Automation Potential:** Medium — The economics of a 5 bbl/day well cannot justify $10K+ SCADA installations, but $50-200/month IoT monitoring and mobile-first tools could transform operations

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Stripper wells produce fewer than 15 barrels of oil equivalent per day — marginal economics where every dollar of operating cost matters. The US has approximately 380,000 stripper wells producing ~770,000 bbl/day, operated primarily by independent operators (often one person managing 10-100 wells) and small family companies. These operators are completely underserved by oilfield technology: SCADA installations cost $5K-$15K per well (more than a stripper well's monthly revenue), field service management platforms assume company-scale operations, and AI/ML solutions target operators with 500+ wells. Stripper well operators track production on paper, perform maintenance reactively, and manage their leases with trucks, wrenches, and decades of experience.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Enterprise oilfield software (Enbase, OFS Portal, FieldFX) targets operators with 50+ employees and monthly budgets for enterprise licenses. SCADA vendors (ABB, Weatherford) require per-well hardware installations that cost more than a stripper well's annual margin. Low-cost IoT sensors (Ambyint, Flowmore, Wellsite Report) are emerging but have limited adoption due to connectivity challenges in remote locations. No tool is designed for the one-person operator managing 50 wells with a phone, a truck, and a pocket notebook.
