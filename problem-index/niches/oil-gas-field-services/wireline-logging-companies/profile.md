## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wireline logging companies (Schlumberger, Baker Hughes, and 200+ independent service companies) lower sensor packages into wellbores to measure rock and fluid properties: resistivity, porosity, density, sonic velocity, natural gamma, and specialty measurements. The raw data (LAS files with dozens of curves) is interpreted by petrophysicists who identify productive zones, calculate water saturation, estimate permeability, and recommend completion intervals. This interpretation is a high-expertise, high-value service — a petrophysicist's zone recommendation determines where $1-10M in completion investment is applied. Yet log interpretation remains an artisanal, manual process: each petrophysicist develops their own interpretation style, parameter selections, and basin-specific knowledge over years of practice.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Techlog (Schlumberger), Interactive Petrophysics (LR Senergy), and Geolog (Paradigm/Emerson) are the standard interpretation platforms. They display log data and provide calculation modules, but the actual interpretation — identifying formation tops, selecting cutoffs, choosing interpretation models — is entirely manual. No tool automates the initial interpretation to provide a starting point that the petrophysicist refines. Quality varies widely between petrophysicists, and a second opinion often changes the zone recommendation.
