# Commercial Office & Retail

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/property-management|Property Management]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12-15B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Yardi Voyager and MRI Software handle commercial PM. But lease abstracting (extracting obligations from PDF leases), CAM reconciliation, and tenant improvement coordination are manually intensive processes that no platform automates well.
**Target Buyer:** Commercial property manager or lease administrator managing office, retail, or industrial properties
**Automation Potential:** High — lease abstracting is an NLP task, CAM reconciliation is a calculation automation problem, and tenant obligation tracking is a workflow management opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial property management operates under fundamentally different lease structures than residential. Commercial leases are 20-100 page documents with unique terms: base rent escalations (fixed %, CPI-linked, or market resets), Common Area Maintenance (CAM) obligations, real estate tax pass-throughs, insurance requirements, tenant improvement allowances, renewal options, expansion rights, co-tenancy clauses, and exclusive use provisions. Each tenant's lease has different terms, different calculation methods, and different obligation triggers. A 20-tenant retail center has 20 unique lease agreements, each requiring individual CAM calculations, tax reconciliations, and obligation tracking. This complexity is managed by lease administrators using PDF leases and Excel spreadsheets, with Yardi handling the accounting but not the lease interpretation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Yardi Voyager and MRI Software handle commercial property accounting, rent billing, and financial reporting. Lease abstracting — extracting the key terms from a 50-page PDF lease into a structured database — is done manually by lease administrators at $50-100/hour, taking 2-4 hours per lease. CAM reconciliation — calculating each tenant's share of common area expenses and issuing annual reconciliation statements — is done in Excel with formulas built per-tenant because each lease has different CAM definitions and calculation methods.
