# Self-Managing Landlords

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/property-management|Property Management]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — self-managing landlords with 1-10 properties use Venmo/Zelle for rent, phone for maintenance, and gut feel for tenant screening. AppFolio and Buildium are too complex and expensive for 3 properties.
**Target Buyer:** Individual landlord managing 1-10 rental properties themselves, without a professional property manager
**Automation Potential:** High — the core workflows (screening, lease, rent collection, maintenance, accounting) are standard and could be simplified into a lightweight tool

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Self-managing landlords represent 70%+ of rental property owners by count but are served by no purpose-built software. A typical self-managing landlord: owns 1-5 properties (often starting with a house they moved out of and kept as a rental), collects rent via Venmo or personal check, handles maintenance by calling their "guy" (a handyman whose number is in their phone), screens tenants by gut feel during a showing ("they seemed nice"), and does accounting with a folder of receipts at tax time. They don't use AppFolio ($1.40/unit/month minimum, $280/month for 200 units — but 3 units at $1.40 = $4.20/month, which AppFolio won't even sell) or Buildium ($52/month minimum — more than the hassle of 3 properties justifies). The pain points are real: inconsistent tenant screening (exposing them to fair housing violations), missed rent collection, untracked expenses (losing tax deductions), and informal maintenance that creates liability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Avail (now Apartments.com for Landlords) and TurboTenant offer free or low-cost basic tools (listing, screening, lease). RentRedi offers mobile-first management. But adoption is low — most self-managing landlords don't know these tools exist or find the setup process more work than their current informal approach. The gap is a tool that requires zero configuration and works entirely from the phone, handling the 5 essential workflows in under 2 minutes each.
