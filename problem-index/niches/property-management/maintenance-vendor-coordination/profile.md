# Maintenance & Vendor Coordination

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/property-management|Property Management]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-8B (embedded across all PM segments)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting function — maintenance coordination is the most time-consuming daily task for property managers across all segments
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — AppFolio and Yardi handle work order creation and tracking. Vendor matching, dispatch optimization, and invoice verification are manual processes.
**Target Buyer:** Property manager or maintenance coordinator spending 30-50% of their day on maintenance coordination
**Automation Potential:** Very High — work order triage is a text classification task, vendor matching is a recommendation problem, and invoice verification is a rules-based automation opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Maintenance coordination consumes more property manager time than any other function: receiving tenant requests, classifying urgency, identifying the appropriate trade, selecting a vendor, dispatching, tracking completion, and verifying invoices. A 500-unit property management company processes 400-800 maintenance work orders per month. The maintenance coordinator reads each request, decides who to send, coordinates scheduling with the tenant and vendor, follows up when the vendor doesn't show or doesn't resolve the issue, receives the invoice, verifies charges against the scope, and processes payment. This end-to-end workflow takes 15-30 minutes per work order, with 60% of that time spent on vendor selection, dispatch, and follow-up. No tool automates the intelligence layer: which vendor to send based on the specific issue, vendor availability, vendor pricing, and vendor performance history.

## Current Tools & Gaps
AppFolio and Yardi handle work order lifecycle management — creation, assignment, completion, and payment. They store vendor records and work order history. But vendor selection is manual: the coordinator chooses from a list without data-driven recommendation. Dispatch is manual: the coordinator calls or texts the vendor. Follow-up is manual: the coordinator checks whether the vendor completed the work. Invoice verification is manual: the coordinator reviews vendor charges against the expected scope.
