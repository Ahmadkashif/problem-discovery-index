# Grant Budget Tracking for Multi-Funder Film Projects

**Niche:** [[niches/video-production-smb/nonprofit-documentary-producers/profile|Nonprofit Documentary Producers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/video-production-smb|Video Production SMB]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks tracks expenses, but a documentary funded by 3 grants needs to allocate each expense to the correct funder, track spending against each grant's budget categories, and generate funder-specific financial reports — none of which QuickBooks models.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A documentary with a $200K budget may be funded by 3 grants ($80K from Ford, $50K from a state arts council, $30K from a private foundation) plus $40K in individual donations. Each funder has different allowable expense categories, different budget line items, and different reporting requirements. Ford may allow 20% for "administrative overhead" while the state arts council caps it at 10%. Equipment rental may be allowable under one grant but not another. The producer must: allocate every expense to the correct funder, ensure no funder's allocation exceeds the approved budget in any category, generate interim and final financial reports in each funder's required format, and reconcile all allocations at project close. This is currently done in a custom Excel workbook with 15+ tabs — one per funder, plus an allocation tab, a reconciliation tab, and a cash flow tab.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks and Xero handle general bookkeeping. Nonprofit-specific accounting tools (Aplos, Sage Intacct for Nonprofits) handle fund accounting with restricted/unrestricted fund tracking. Movie Magic Budgeting handles film production budgets. But none combine: multi-funder grant allocation (splitting a single expense across 3 funders based on a predetermined ratio), funder-specific budget category mapping (Ford calls it "production" but the state calls it "direct costs"), grant period tracking (expenses must fall within the grant's fiscal year), and film industry budget line items (AICP or independent film budget formats) in a single tool.

## The Customization Gap
A documentary grant budget tool needs to: (1) set up multiple funding sources with their approved budgets, allowable categories, and reporting periods, (2) import expenses from QuickBooks/bank feed and allocate each to one or more funders with split percentages, (3) flag expenses that would exceed a funder's approved budget in any category, (4) track spending velocity against the grant timeline (alert when 50% of the grant period has passed but only 30% of funds have been spent — indicating underspending that may jeopardize the grant), (5) generate funder-specific interim and final financial reports in each funder's template format, and (6) produce an overall project P&L showing all funding sources and total spending. Priced for documentary budgets ($50–$100/month), not enterprise nonprofits.

## Target Customer
Documentary producers managing projects with 2+ funding sources totaling $50K–$500K.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces financial reporting time from 15–20 hours per funder report to 3–5 hours. Eliminates the 5–10% of expenses that are misallocated (charged to a funder that doesn't allow that category), preventing grant audit findings that can require repayment. Provides real-time budget visibility that prevents the "we ran out of grant money with 30% of the project remaining" crisis that affects 20% of multi-funder documentaries.
