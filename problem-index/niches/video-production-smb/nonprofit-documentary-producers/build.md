# Documentary Impact Measurement and Funder Reporting Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/video-production-smb/nonprofit-documentary-producers/profile|Nonprofit Documentary Producers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/video-production-smb|Video Production SMB]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform tracks documentary film impact — screening attendance, audience surveys, media mentions, policy changes, and behavioral outcomes — in a structured format that satisfies funders' demands for evidence that their $100K grant made a difference.
**Tags:** #data-integration #nlp #automation #revenue-impact #ai-platform

## The Problem
Funders (Ford Foundation, MacArthur, Sundance Institute, Corporation for Public Broadcasting) increasingly require impact data from documentary grants: How many people saw the film? Did attitudes change? Was there policy or legislative action? Did the target audience take the desired action? Producers track impact informally: saving news articles, counting screening attendees on legal pads, collecting anecdotal feedback, and writing narrative impact reports months after the screening tour ends. The reports are subjective, inconsistent, and impossible for funders to compare across grantees. Yet the producer's ability to secure future grants depends heavily on demonstrating impact from past projects — a circular problem when no measurement infrastructure exists.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Documentary impact is genuinely hard to measure: the causal chain from "person watches film" to "person changes behavior or policy changes" is long, nonlinear, and influenced by dozens of confounding factors. The documentary field has debated impact methodology for 15 years without converging on a standard. The market (3,000–5,000 documentary producers in the U.S.) is small, grant-dependent, and can't afford SaaS subscriptions. And producers are skeptical of quantifying impact — fearing that it reduces storytelling to "metrics that miss the point."

## What to Build
An impact tracking dashboard where: (1) the producer logs screenings (venue, date, attendance, audience type — community, policy, educational), (2) post-screening audience surveys are collected digitally (tablet at the venue or QR code to phone) with standardized questions adaptable per project, (3) media mentions are auto-tracked via Google Alerts integration with sentiment classification, (4) policy/legislative actions are logged with documentation links, (5) partner organization activities (curriculum adoptions, campaign actions) are recorded, and (6) funder-specific impact reports are auto-generated matching each funder's reporting template. Over time, the platform aggregates anonymized data across projects, enabling field-level insights ("documentaries screened at policy briefings generate 3x more policy action than public screenings"). Priced at $50–$150/month with a free tier for projects under $50K.

## Target Customer
Documentary producers and impact producers at nonprofits who need to demonstrate grant outcomes to funders.

## Impact If Built
Transforms impact reporting from 20–40 hours of narrative writing per funder report to 3–5 hours of data review and customization. Strengthens future grant applications with structured impact evidence — producers who demonstrate impact receive 40–60% larger subsequent grants. Creates a field-level evidence base that helps all documentary producers make the case for impact filmmaking funding.
