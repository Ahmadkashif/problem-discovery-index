**Target Buyer:** Producer / Director
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Grant reporting, impact tracking, and consent management are structured processes; creative and ethical decision-making remain human.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Nonprofit documentary producers create impact films, advocacy videos, and fundraising content for nonprofits, foundations, and social enterprises. Their business model combines fee-for-service production ($10K–$200K per project) with grant-funded independent documentaries. The unique constraints: projects involve vulnerable populations (refugees, domestic violence survivors, incarcerated persons) requiring rigorous consent protocols; funders demand impact measurement ("did the film change policy or behavior?"); budgets are tight and tied to grant periods with strict expenditure reporting; and distribution targets impact (screenings, policy briefings, educational use) rather than commercial revenue. The production company must navigate both the creative demands of compelling storytelling and the ethical demands of responsible representation.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Production uses standard NLE tools (Premiere, DaVinci Resolve). Distribution uses Vimeo for screening links and Film Freeway for festival submissions. But the nonprofit-specific workflows are unserved: no tool tracks participant consent (who consented to what use, with what restrictions, in what languages); no tool manages grant-funded production budgets with funder-specific reporting requirements; no tool measures documentary impact (screening attendance, media coverage, policy changes, audience behavior shifts) in a way that satisfies funders. These workflows run on Word documents, email, and hope.
