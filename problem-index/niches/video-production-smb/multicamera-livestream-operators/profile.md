## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multicamera livestream operators produce live video for corporate events, conferences, worship services, sports events, and concerts — managing 3–8 cameras, audio mixing, graphics/lower thirds, screen sharing, and streaming to 1–5 platforms simultaneously. The technical complexity rivals broadcast television but operates with 2–5 person crews instead of 30. Every livestream is a live performance with no second takes: a missed camera switch, an audio dropout, or a stream disconnect is visible to hundreds or thousands of viewers in real-time. The business combines equipment-intensive production (cameras, switchers, encoders, cables, monitors) with high-stakes event execution, creating operational challenges at both ends — equipment logistics before the event and technical execution during.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Switching uses ATEM (Blackmagic), TriCaster (Vizrt), or OBS. Streaming uses Restream, Switchboard, or platform-native tools. Graphics use ProPresenter, CasparCG, or Singular.live. But the operational layer — equipment checklist management, venue load-in planning, crew scheduling, stream health monitoring, and post-event VOD processing — runs on paper checklists, WhatsApp groups, and institutional knowledge. No tool connects the pre-event logistics (what equipment goes where, with what settings) to the live execution (camera switching, graphics triggering) to the post-event workflow (VOD editing, highlight extraction, client delivery).
