## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Drone aerial production companies provide aerial photography, videography, mapping, and inspection services for real estate, construction, events, agriculture, and infrastructure clients. The business is uniquely equipment-intensive and regulation-heavy: every commercial flight requires Part 107 certification, airspace awareness (controlled vs. uncontrolled, TFRs, NOTAMs), weather minimums, and in some cases LAANC authorization for flights near airports. Operators must log every flight for insurance and regulatory purposes. The combination of FAA compliance, equipment management (batteries, sensors, aircraft maintenance), and client service makes this one of the most operationally complex segments of video production.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Flight planning uses DJI Fly or Litchi. Airspace checking uses AirMap or B4UFLY. Weather monitoring uses standard apps. Production uses standard NLE tools. But no single platform connects: flight scheduling (which drone, which pilot, what time, what location), regulatory compliance (airspace authorization, NOTAM check, weather check, pilot certification status), flight logging (takeoff/landing times, battery cycles, location, purpose), equipment tracking (aircraft hours, battery charge cycles, sensor calibration status), and client delivery. Each function uses a separate app or spreadsheet, and the pilot-owner is the integration layer.
