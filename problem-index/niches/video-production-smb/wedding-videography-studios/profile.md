# Wedding Videography Studios

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/video-production-smb|Video Production SMB]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 22%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Editing uses professional NLEs (Premiere, Final Cut), but client management, booking, and delivery are handled with email, Instagram DMs, and Google Drive links.
**Target Buyer:** Studio Owner / Lead Videographer
**Automation Potential:** High — Client intake, contract generation, timeline questionnaires, highlight reel assembly, and delivery workflows are highly repetitive across weddings.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Wedding videography studios (1–5 person operations) shoot 20–60 weddings per year at $2,500–$10,000 per event. The business model is seasonal (80% of bookings in May–October), relationship-driven (referrals from planners and venues), and emotionally high-stakes (the couple watches the wedding video more than any other content they'll ever own). The production workflow is standardized — ceremony, vows, first dance, speeches, reception — but each wedding generates 200–500 GB of footage that must be culled, edited, color-graded, and delivered in 4–12 weeks. The bottleneck is always post-production: a single wedding takes 30–60 hours to edit, and the peak season creates a backlog that pushes delivery dates into the following year.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Adobe Premiere Pro and Final Cut Pro handle editing. HoneyBook and Dubsado handle client management (contracts, invoices, questionnaires). Pixieset and SmartSlides handle gallery delivery for photographers but not video delivery. The critical gap: no tool helps with the most time-consuming post-production tasks — rough-cut assembly from hundreds of clips, syncing multi-camera footage with audio, selecting the best moments from each segment, and generating a highlight reel. Editors watch hours of footage to find 5 minutes of highlights, a process that AI could dramatically accelerate.
