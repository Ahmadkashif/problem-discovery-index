# Real Estate Media Producers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/video-production-smb|Video Production SMB]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 11%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Drones and gimbals are standard, but scheduling, delivery, and MLS-formatted output are manual workflows.
**Target Buyer:** Owner-Operator / Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Listing photo/video shoots are highly standardized, turnaround expectations are tight (24–48 hours), and output formats are prescribed by MLS standards.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Real estate media producers create listing photos, video walkthroughs, drone aerials, 3D tours (Matterport), and floor plans for real estate agents. The business model is high-volume, low-margin: $200–$500 per listing, 5–15 shoots per day, 24–48 hour delivery expected. The producer's competitive advantage is turnaround speed and consistency, not creative artistry. Operations resemble a logistics business more than a creative studio: scheduling shoots at properties, dispatching photographers/videographers, processing media through a standardized editing pipeline, and delivering MLS-ready assets to the agent. Most operators handle scheduling via text message, process media overnight, and deliver through Dropbox or WeTransfer links.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Photography uses Canon/Sony mirrorless cameras with HDR bracketing. Video uses gimbals (DJI RS series) and drones (DJI Mini/Air). Editing uses Lightroom (batch photo processing) and Premiere (video). Some operators use scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) but most rely on text messages because agents book last-minute. No tool manages the end-to-end real estate media workflow: agent booking → photographer dispatch → shoot → media processing → QC → delivery → invoicing. Each step is a separate manual process, and scaling past 10 shoots per day requires hiring an operations coordinator.
