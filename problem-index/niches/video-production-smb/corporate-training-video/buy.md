# SCORM/xAPI Packaging for LMS Delivery

**Niche:** [[niches/video-production-smb/corporate-training-video/profile|Corporate Training Video]]
**Industry:** [[industries/video-production-smb|Video Production SMB]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Articulate and Adobe Captivate create SCORM packages, but they're designed for e-learning course authors — a video production company needs to wrap its finished videos in SCORM/xAPI compliance without learning a full authoring tool.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
When a video production company delivers training videos to a corporate client, the videos must be uploaded to the client's LMS (Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors, Workday Learning, etc.). LMS platforms require content in SCORM or xAPI format to track completion, quiz scores, and learner progress. A raw MP4 video file can be uploaded but doesn't communicate completion data back to the LMS — the client can't prove that employees watched the required training. Creating a SCORM package requires wrapping the video in an HTML player with JavaScript tracking code and packaging it as a ZIP file that meets SCORM 1.2, SCORM 2004, or xAPI specifications. Most video production companies don't know how to create SCORM packages and outsource it or deliver raw files that the client's L&D team must package.

## What Already Exists
Articulate Storyline, Adobe Captivate, and iSpring Suite create SCORM-compliant e-learning courses with interactive elements, quizzes, and branching. These are powerful authoring tools designed for instructional designers — not video editors. A video production company that needs to wrap a 10-minute MP4 in a SCORM package with a completion tracking marker and a 5-question quiz doesn't need a $1,300/year authoring tool with a 40-hour learning curve. Simple SCORM wrappers exist (SCORM Cloud, Rustici's tools) but are developer-oriented and not designed for non-technical video editors.

## The Customization Gap
A SCORM packaging tool for video producers needs to: (1) accept an MP4 video file and generate a SCORM 1.2/2004/xAPI package with zero coding, (2) auto-insert completion tracking (mark complete when 90% of video is watched), (3) allow optional knowledge-check questions (multiple choice, true/false) to be added after the video, (4) generate the package in the specific format the client's LMS requires (different LMS platforms have different SCORM implementation quirks), (5) preview the packaged content in a test LMS environment before delivery, and (6) support branding customization (client logo on the player, progress bar styling). The entire process — from MP4 to SCORM package — should take 10 minutes, not 2 hours.

## Target Customer
Video production companies delivering training content to corporate clients whose L&D departments require SCORM/xAPI packaging for LMS upload.

## Impact If Solved
Enables video companies to deliver LMS-ready packages directly, eliminating the client's need to involve their L&D team for packaging — differentiating the production company from competitors who deliver raw files. Adds $500–$2,000 per project in packaging/delivery services revenue. Reduces delivery-to-LMS-upload time from 1–2 weeks (client's internal queue) to same-day.
