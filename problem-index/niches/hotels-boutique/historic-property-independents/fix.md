# Modern Guest Expectations in Pre-Modern Buildings

**Niche:** [[niches/hotels-boutique/historic-property-independents/profile|Historic Property Independents]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Guests expect high-speed WiFi, USB charging, smart locks, and quiet HVAC — but historic buildings have thick walls, limited wiring, no ductwork, and heritage restrictions on modifications.
**Tags:** #data-integration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
The most common complaints at historic properties are modern infrastructure gaps: slow WiFi (stone/plaster walls block signals), inadequate outlets (original wiring cannot be expanded without wall demolition), temperature control issues (radiators or window units instead of central HVAC), and noise transmission (original hardwood floors and thin interior walls). The guest chose the property for its character but rates it against the infrastructure standard of a Marriott. The owner faces a paradox: modernizing destroys the character; not modernizing destroys the reviews.

## Why It's Still Broken
WiFi mesh systems, portable HVAC, and wireless charging solutions exist but are not packaged or deployed with historic building constraints in mind. Each property solves these problems independently through expensive trial-and-error: the owner hires a generic IT company to install WiFi that fails in the 200-year-old stone wing, buys portable ACs that overwhelm the electrical panel, and adds power strips that create fire hazards in rooms with original knob-and-tube wiring.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A "historic building tech retrofit" consulting product (or productized service) that assesses the property's infrastructure constraints and deploys a tested configuration: WiFi access points positioned for stone/plaster/brick wall penetration, discrete USB/wireless charging solutions that do not require wall modifications, portable heating/cooling solutions rated for limited electrical capacity, and soundproofing treatments compatible with heritage requirements. Packaged as a per-room retrofit with predictable pricing.

## Who Feels the Pain
The front desk agent who fields 5-10 WiFi complaints per day and has no solution beyond "try moving closer to the hallway," and the owner who watches review scores drop because of infrastructure that cannot be conventionally upgraded.

## Impact If Fixed
Resolving the top 3 infrastructure complaints (WiFi, temperature, outlets) improves review scores by 0.2-0.4 points — a shift that increases OTA conversion rates by 5-10%, worth $30K-$80K in annual revenue for a 25-room property.
