# Historic Property Independents

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — legacy PMS common; historic building constraints (thick walls, limited wiring) make modern tech deployment difficult
**Target Buyer:** Owner of a renovated historic inn, heritage hotel, or landmark building conversion with 10-40 rooms
**Automation Potential:** Medium — unique room configurations and building constraints make standardization harder but maintenance and guest experience benefit from digitization

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Historic property independents operate in buildings designed before WiFi, HVAC zoning, modern plumbing standards, and ADA requirements. Each room is architecturally unique — different sizes, layouts, views, and quirks — making standardized room-type pricing inappropriate. Maintenance is perpetual and specialized: plaster walls, original hardwood, antique fixtures, and heritage-protected exteriors require craftspeople, not generic repair techs. These properties attract a specific guest who values character and history over consistency — but that guest's expectations are shaped by chain-hotel technology (mobile check-in, smart locks, high-speed WiFi) that historic buildings struggle to deliver.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Many use legacy PMS systems (even paper in some cases) because modern cloud PMS onboarding assumes standardized room types and modern building infrastructure. WiFi is unreliable through stone or plaster walls. Smart locks require door frame modifications that heritage regulations may prohibit. Energy management systems assume modern HVAC — not radiator heating and window units.
