# Heritage-Compatible Building Maintenance Management

**Niche:** [[niches/hotels-boutique/historic-property-independents/profile|Historic Property Independents]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CMMS tools like Limble and UpKeep manage modern building maintenance but have no concept of heritage-protected materials, specialized craftspeople requirements, or the regulatory approval process for exterior modifications.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
A historic inn's maintenance needs are fundamentally different from a modern hotel: repointing brick mortar requires a mason who uses lime-based mortar (not Portland cement), replacing a window in a heritage-listed building requires municipal historic commission approval (4-8 weeks), and refinishing original hardwood uses different products and techniques than modern flooring. The maintenance manager tracks these specialized requirements in their head and a paper notebook — when they leave, the knowledge walks out the door.

## What Already Exists
Limble, UpKeep, and Fiix provide CMMS (computerized maintenance management systems) with work order tracking, preventive maintenance schedules, and vendor management. These are designed for modern buildings with standardized systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) and commercially available replacement parts.

## The Customization Gap
The tool must handle heritage-specific maintenance: materials inventory (which mortar type, which paint formulation, which wood species for each element), approved vendor lists (specialized craftspeople with heritage experience), regulatory workflows (historic commission approval required before work begins on exterior elements), and preservation-grade preventive maintenance schedules (annual inspections of original features with specialized care protocols).

## Target Customer
Owners and maintenance managers of heritage-listed or architecturally significant properties who need to track specialized maintenance requirements and approved craftspeople across unique building elements.

## Impact If Solved
Preventing a single heritage violation (using wrong materials, modifying protected elements without approval) avoids $5K-$50K in fines and mandatory restoration costs. Extending the life of original building elements through proper maintenance preserves the property's character — its primary competitive advantage.
