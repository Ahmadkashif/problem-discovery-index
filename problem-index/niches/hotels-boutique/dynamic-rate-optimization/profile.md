# Dynamic Rate Optimization

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B (embedded — revenue opportunity from pricing optimization)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded revenue function affecting all boutique hotels
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — 90%+ of boutique hotels set rates manually or with basic spreadsheet rules; fewer than 5% use automated revenue management
**Target Buyer:** GM or owner who sets room rates 2-3 times per week based on competitor checks and occupancy, without a dedicated revenue manager
**Automation Potential:** High — rate-setting is a data-driven decision with clear inputs (demand signals) and outputs (price recommendations), currently performed manually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Rate optimization is the single highest-leverage business function for a boutique hotel — a $10 improvement in ADR on 14,600 annual room-nights adds $146K in revenue with zero incremental cost. Yet 95% of boutique hotels set rates manually: the GM checks Booking.com, sees competitor rates, adjusts their price once or twice a week, and hopes they are in the right range. Chain hotels reprice 3-5 times daily using IDeaS or Duetto. The independent is flying blind — underpricing on demand nights (selling out too early), overpricing on soft nights (sitting empty), and missing every demand signal between their infrequent manual checks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
IDeaS and Duetto provide enterprise revenue management but at $15K-$25K/year with multi-property contracts. Atomize and RoomPriceGenie offer lighter-weight alternatives but still require configuration and monitoring. Most boutique GMs have tried none of these — they set rates in their PMS manually and consider their gut feel sufficient until they see how much chain competitors capture on high-demand nights.
