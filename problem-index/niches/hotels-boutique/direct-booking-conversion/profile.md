# Direct Booking Conversion Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B (embedded — OTA commissions paid by boutique hotels)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded cost affecting 95%+ of boutique hotels
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most have a website but direct booking conversion optimization is near-zero
**Target Buyer:** GM or owner losing 60-70% of bookings to OTA channels and paying 15-25% commission per booking
**Automation Potential:** High — booking funnel optimization, rate parity, and guest communication are rule-based processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
OTA dependency is the defining financial challenge for independent hotels. Booking.com and Expedia capture 60-70% of bookings and charge 15-25% commission — $100K-$500K annually for a typical boutique property. Chain hotels solve this with loyalty programs (Marriott Bonvoy, Hilton Honors) that drive 50%+ of bookings direct. Independents have no equivalent: their website is a brochure, their booking engine is a basic widget, and they have no guest database for direct marketing. Shifting even 15-20% of OTA bookings to direct channels would add $30K-$100K in annual profit.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Booking engines (SynXis, Triptease, Bookassist) provide website booking widgets. Channel managers (SiteMinder, Lodgify) maintain rate parity. But no tool provides the full direct-booking conversion stack: website conversion optimization, Google Hotel Ads management, meta-search bid strategy, email capture and nurturing, loyalty/return-guest incentives, and rate parity enforcement — integrated and designed for a single property without a dedicated digital marketing team.
