# Guest Experience Personalization

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-7B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded competitive need affecting all boutique hotels
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — guest preferences tracked in staff memory and PMS notes; no systematic personalization system
**Target Buyer:** GM seeking to differentiate through personalized service that chains cannot replicate at scale
**Automation Potential:** Medium — personalization requires data capture and delivery, both of which are systematizable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Boutique hotels' primary competitive advantage over chains is personalized service — the GM who remembers a guest's name, the front desk that stocks the preferred pillow type, the bartender who has the guest's favorite cocktail ready. But this personalization is stored in individual staff members' memories and is lost with every turnover. A boutique hotel with 40% annual front-desk turnover loses its guest knowledge every 2.5 years. The chain alternative — CRM-driven personalization through Marriott Bonvoy or Hilton Honors — uses enterprise technology the independent cannot afford.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PMS systems have a guest notes field, but notes are unstructured, inconsistently entered, and not surfaced at the right moment (the front desk agent does not check notes before greeting a returning guest). CRM tools (Revinate, Cendyn) offer guest profiling but target chain and management company portfolios. No affordable tool captures, structures, and surfaces guest preferences at the moment of service for a single-property independent.
