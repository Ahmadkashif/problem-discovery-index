# Housekeeping Workflow Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B (embedded — housekeeping labor cost)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded operations cost representing 15-20% of total hotel operating expenses
**Digital Adoption:** Low — room assignment sheets printed each morning; cleaning status updated verbally or on paper checklists; no real-time tracking
**Target Buyer:** Housekeeping supervisor managing 8-15 room attendants at a boutique hotel with 20-60 rooms
**Automation Potential:** High — room assignment, cleaning sequence, supply management, and quality inspection are all rule-based processes currently managed on paper

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Housekeeping is the single largest labor cost center in a hotel (15-20% of operating expenses) and the most operationally manual. Every morning, the housekeeping supervisor prints a room list from the PMS, manually assigns rooms to attendants based on their location guess of checkout timing, and hands out paper sheets. When checkouts happen early or late, the plan is immediately wrong — attendants wait for rooms that are still occupied or miss rooms that checked out early. Status updates happen when the attendant walks back to the supervisor's desk or radios in. Quality inspection happens room-by-room with a clipboard. None of this is digitized.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Optii, Flexkeeping, and Housekeeping apps built into PMS platforms (Mews, Cloudbeds) offer digital room assignment and status tracking. But adoption at boutique hotels is under 10% — the setup requires mapping every room, configuring cleaning times by room type, and training staff on mobile apps, which the supervisor perceives as adding complexity rather than removing it. No tool provides the dynamic re-sequencing that actually solves the core problem (static plans becoming stale as checkout times vary).
