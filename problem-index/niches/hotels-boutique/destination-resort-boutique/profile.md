# Destination Resort Boutiques

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hotels-boutique|Boutique Hotels]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — PMS adopted but revenue management is seasonal-intuition-based; guest experience tools focused on concierge rather than data-driven personalization
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator of a boutique property in a resort/vacation market (coastal, mountain, wine country, national park adjacent)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — extreme seasonality creates forecasting challenges but also clear patterns; ancillary revenue (spa, F&B, experiences) adds optimization dimensions

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Destination resort boutiques face extreme seasonality (90%+ occupancy in peak, 30% in shoulder, sometimes closed in off-season), heavy reliance on ancillary revenue (spa, restaurant, guided experiences can represent 30-50% of total revenue), and a guest profile seeking experiences rather than rooms. Unlike urban boutiques competing on rate, resort boutiques compete on the total experience — making ancillary revenue optimization and pre-arrival engagement more important than room rate intelligence. The long booking lead time (60-120 days for resort vs. 7-14 days for urban) creates a different revenue management challenge: pricing decisions made months in advance, with limited ability to adjust.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PMS systems handle reservations. Spa management tools (Booker, Mindbody) operate separately from the PMS. Restaurant POS is another silo. No tool provides a unified guest revenue view across room, spa, F&B, and experiences — making it impossible to calculate true guest lifetime value or optimize the total stay yield.
