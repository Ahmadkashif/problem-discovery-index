# Arrangement Conference Workflow

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** Cross-segment (applies to all funeral homes conducting in-person arrangements)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting operational function
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Most directors use paper forms or legacy software during arrangements; the conference itself is largely undocumented.
**Target Buyer:** Funeral director conducting 2-5 arrangement conferences per week
**Automation Potential:** High — Information capture, document generation, pricing calculation, and vendor order initiation are all automatable without compromising the human-centered nature of the meeting.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The arrangement conference is the central operational moment in funeral service: a 60-90 minute meeting where a grieving family makes 30+ decisions (service type, casket, flowers, music, readings, pallbearers, obituary, reception, transportation) while the funeral director captures every detail, calculates costs, ensures FTC compliance, and maintains empathetic composure. It's simultaneously a sales meeting, a logistical planning session, and a pastoral care moment. The information captured during this meeting drives every downstream action — but it's typically recorded on paper forms that must be manually transcribed into case management systems afterward.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some funeral homes use Passare or HMIS on a laptop during the arrangement conference, but the software interfaces are dated and require the director to type while maintaining eye contact and emotional presence. Most directors take handwritten notes and enter them into the system later — a 30-60 minute transcription task per case. No tool is designed for the specific UX challenge of the arrangement conference: capturing detailed structured data while the user (the funeral director) needs to focus primarily on the humans in the room.
