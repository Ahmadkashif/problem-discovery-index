# Smart Pricing Calculator with FTC Compliance

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/arrangement-conference/profile|Arrangement Conference Workflow]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Pricing and quoting tools exist for service businesses but none enforce FTC Funeral Rule itemization requirements while helping directors build service packages during the arrangement conference.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
During the arrangement conference, the funeral director builds a service package with the family — selecting individual items (casket, vault, flowers, transportation, services) that must be presented as itemized prices per FTC Funeral Rule. Currently, the director refers to a printed General Price List, manually calculates the total, and may inadvertently bundle items in ways that violate itemization requirements. The family sees a hand-calculated number at the end of a 90-minute emotional meeting — not an itemized statement they can review at their own pace. This creates both compliance risk and family trust concerns.

## What Already Exists
QuoteWerks, PandaDoc, and other CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote) tools handle service package building and quoting for professional services. Funeral management systems (Passare, HMIS) have pricing modules. But generic CPQ tools don't enforce FTC Funeral Rule requirements (mandatory itemization, required price disclosures for specific items like embalming), and legacy funeral systems present pricing in interfaces too clunky to use in front of a family during an emotional meeting.

## The Customization Gap
The pricing tool needs to enforce FTC Funeral Rule requirements automatically: embalming must have a separate line item with required disclosure language, alternative containers must be offered when no casket is selected, package prices must break down into itemized components. The interface must be family-friendly — clear, respectful, and reviewable — not a dense spreadsheet. It should generate a professional itemized statement that serves as both the family's receipt and the funeral home's FTC compliance documentation.

## Target Customer
Any funeral home conducting in-person arrangements, especially homes that have received FTC mystery shopper visits or operate in states with active enforcement.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates FTC compliance risk during the most scrutinized interaction (the arrangement conference), provides families with transparent itemized pricing that builds trust, and saves 10-15 minutes per conference in manual pricing calculation.
