# Pre-Need Sales Pipeline CRM

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/pre-need-planning/profile|Pre-Need Planning Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No CRM exists for the pre-need funeral sales cycle — a consultative, mortality-sensitive pipeline that spans initial community outreach through in-home presentation to contract signing and decades-long relationship maintenance.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #ai-agent #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Pre-need sales counselors generate leads through community events (senior center presentations, church groups, veterans' organizations), direct mail, and website inquiries. They track these leads in notebooks, spreadsheets, or their heads. Follow-up is inconsistent — a counselor may have 50 warm leads but no system to remind them who needs a second call, who attended the seminar but didn't book a consultation, or who started the process but stalled at the funding decision. The result: conversion rates of 10-15% that could be 25-35% with systematic follow-up.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The pre-need funeral market is too small for major CRM vendors to target ($3B across 20,000+ funeral homes), and the sales cycle is uniquely sensitive — tone-deaf automation (e.g., "Don't forget to plan your funeral!") would be offensive. Building a CRM for this market requires understanding the emotional dynamics of mortality planning, the regulatory constraints on pre-need sales practices (which vary by state), and the financial products involved (insurance policies, trust funds).

## What to Build
A CRM designed for pre-need counselors with: lead capture from community events (QR code sign-in sheets), a pipeline view with stages (awareness → interest → consultation → presentation → contract → funded), automated but compassionate follow-up sequences (templates reviewed by grief counselors), tracking of funding status (insurance application submitted, trust established), and long-term relationship maintenance (annual check-in reminders, birthday cards, plan update reviews). The tone throughout must be warm and respectful — not sales-aggressive.

## Target Customer
Pre-need sales counselors or funeral home owners with a pre-need program generating 50+ leads per quarter but converting less than 20%, typically at homes handling 150-400 at-need cases per year.

## Impact If Built
Increases pre-need conversion rate from 10-15% to 25-35%, adding $150,000-500,000 in funded pre-need contracts per year for a mid-size funeral home, while ensuring no lead falls through the cracks.
