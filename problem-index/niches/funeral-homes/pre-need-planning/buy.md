# Digital Pre-Need Contract & Funding Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/pre-need-planning/profile|Pre-Need Planning Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Digital contract and insurance platforms exist but none handle the funeral pre-need workflow: service selection tied to current GPL pricing, price-lock guarantees, state-specific pre-need trust regulations, and integration with insurance carriers.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Pre-need contracts are complex documents: they specify the services and merchandise the client has selected (at current GPL prices), establish a funding mechanism (insurance policy or trust fund), guarantee that the funeral home will provide those services at the contracted price regardless of future cost increases, and comply with state-specific regulations governing pre-need sales (cooling-off periods, cancellation refund requirements, trust fund management rules). Currently, these contracts are generated on paper forms or outdated software, signed in person, and filed physically. Funding documentation (insurance applications, trust deposits) is handled separately with no digital link to the contract.

## What Already Exists
DocuSign and Adobe Sign handle digital contract signing. Homesteaders Life and other pre-need insurance providers have their own application portals. Funeral home management systems (Passare, HMIS) can store pre-need records. But none of these connect the full workflow: service selection from the funeral home's current GPL → contract generation with price-lock calculations → insurance or trust funding → state-compliant documentation → long-term record management.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs to pull from the funeral home's current GPL to build the contract, calculate the price-lock guarantee (total funded amount), generate state-compliant contract language (which varies significantly — some states require specific cancellation disclosures, revocability provisions, or trust fund depositary requirements), integrate with pre-need insurance carriers for policy application submission, and maintain a long-term digital record that can be retrieved decades later when the death occurs.

## Target Customer
Funeral homes selling 30+ pre-need contracts per year that currently manage the process across paper forms, insurance carrier portals, and filing cabinets — losing efficiency and compliance confidence at every handoff.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces contract processing time from 2-3 hours to 30-45 minutes, eliminates compliance gaps in state-specific contract language, and ensures contracts are retrievable and complete when the at-need case arrives — sometimes 10-20 years after signing.
