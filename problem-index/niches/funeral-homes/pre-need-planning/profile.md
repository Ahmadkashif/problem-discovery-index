# Pre-Need Planning Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~13%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Pre-need sales are conducted in-person with paper contracts, trust fund or insurance documentation is manual, and follow-up on leads is inconsistent.
**Target Buyer:** Pre-need sales counselor or funeral home owner building a pre-need program
**Automation Potential:** High — Lead nurturing, contract generation, trust/insurance administration, and price-lock tracking are all rule-based.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pre-need funeral planning — where individuals arrange and fund their funeral in advance — is a distinct sales and administrative function within funeral homes. It involves a consultative sales process (often in the client's home), complex financial instruments (pre-need insurance policies or trust funds), state-specific regulations governing pre-need contracts, and a follow-up timeline that can span decades (the client may not die for 20+ years). The operational requirements are closer to insurance sales than to funeral service delivery, yet the same funeral home staff typically handle both.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Homesteaders Life Company and other pre-need insurance providers offer basic contract management, but the sales pipeline (lead generation, follow-up, conversion tracking) is managed manually — sticky notes, spreadsheets, or memory. No CRM is tailored to the pre-need funeral sales cycle, which involves sensitive conversations about mortality, financial planning, and family dynamics. Generic CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce) require extensive customization that small funeral homes can't implement.
