# Public-Assistance Funeral Benefit Navigator

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/low-income-public-assistance/profile|Low-Income & Public-Assistance Funerals]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool helps families or funeral directors identify all available funeral assistance programs — federal, state, county, nonprofit, and veterans — for a specific decedent's situation, leaving thousands of dollars in benefits unclaimed.
**Tags:** #ai-agent #nlp #workflow-orchestration #compliance

## The Problem
A low-income family facing a $3,000-8,000 funeral bill may qualify for multiple assistance programs simultaneously: Social Security lump-sum death benefit ($255), state Medicaid burial benefit ($1,500-5,000), county indigent burial assistance, FEMA funeral assistance (if death was COVID or disaster-related), veterans' burial benefits (if the deceased served), and local charitable funds. But no single source lists all available programs for a given jurisdiction, and eligibility rules are complex. Families often claim only one benefit (or none), leaving thousands uncollected. Funeral directors who want to help must navigate each program individually.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Funeral assistance programs are administered by different levels of government (federal, state, county) with no coordination between them. Eligibility rules, application processes, documentation requirements, and reimbursement amounts vary widely. Maintaining a current database requires monitoring legislative changes, county budget decisions, and nonprofit fund availability — a maintenance burden that no one has taken on. The affected population (low-income families) has no purchasing power to fund the tool, and funeral homes that serve them are already operating at minimal margins.

## What to Build
A benefit navigator that takes decedent information (state, county, age, veteran status, Medicaid status, cause of death, family income) and returns a ranked list of all applicable funeral assistance programs with eligibility likelihood, documentation requirements, application deadlines, and expected reimbursement amounts. The system should generate pre-filled applications where possible and track submitted claims through to payment. Could be accessed by families directly or by funeral directors during the arrangement conference.

## Target Customer
Funeral directors handling 20-50+ public-assistance cases per year who currently spend 2-4 hours per case navigating benefit systems, and low-income families who lack the knowledge to identify available assistance.

## Impact If Built
Increases average benefit recovery per case by $1,000-3,000 through identifying programs families didn't know existed, reduces funeral director administrative time by 60-70% per public-assistance case, and helps families avoid the financial devastation of an unfunded funeral.
