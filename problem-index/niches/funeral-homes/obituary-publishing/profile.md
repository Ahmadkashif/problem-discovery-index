# Obituary & Death Notice Publishing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** Cross-segment (applies to virtually every funeral case)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting service function
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Online tribute pages exist (Legacy.com, funeral home websites) but newspaper obituary placement is still manual, and obituary writing is a time-consuming human task.
**Target Buyer:** Funeral home staff responsible for obituary writing and placement
**Automation Potential:** High — Obituary drafting from biographical data, newspaper placement, and multi-platform publishing are highly automatable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Every funeral generates an obituary — it's both a notification to the community and a biographical tribute to the deceased. Writing and placing the obituary is one of the most time-consuming tasks per case: gathering biographical details from the family, drafting the text in the family's preferred style and tone, getting family approval (often requiring 2-3 revision rounds), placing the obituary in local newspapers (each with different submission formats, word-count pricing, and deadlines), and publishing online across funeral home websites, Legacy.com, and social media. A single obituary can consume 2-3 hours of staff time.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Legacy.com dominates online obituary publishing but doesn't help with newspaper placement. Newspapers have their own submission portals with inconsistent formats. Some case management systems generate basic obituary templates, but they produce stilted, formulaic text that families reject. No tool combines AI-assisted drafting (from biographical data already in the case file), family approval workflow, and multi-channel publishing (newspaper, website, social media, Legacy.com) in a single workflow.
