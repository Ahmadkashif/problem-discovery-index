# Multi-Newspaper Submission System

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/obituary-publishing/profile|Obituary & Death Notice Publishing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** PR distribution platforms like Cision and PR Newswire handle multi-outlet publishing but don't support funeral-specific requirements — per-word newspaper pricing, death notice vs. obituary formats, photo specifications, and service detail insertions.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Families often request obituary placement in multiple newspapers — the local paper where the deceased lived, the paper in their hometown, and sometimes a major metro paper. Each newspaper has different: submission formats (email, web portal, phone), word-count pricing ($5-15 per word for paid obituaries), photo specifications (resolution, format, color vs. B&W), deadlines (48 hours before print date, next-day for some), and formatting rules (whether service details are included in the text or as a separate box). The funeral home staff reformats and resubmits the same obituary for each paper independently.

## What Already Exists
Cision and PR Newswire distribute press releases to multiple media outlets simultaneously. Legacy.com handles online obituary publishing. Some regional newspaper chains have consolidated submission portals. But none of these solve the funeral home's specific problem: submitting the same obituary, reformatted per each newspaper's specifications, with accurate word-count pricing estimates, photo formatting, and deadline tracking — all from a single submission.

## The Customization Gap
The system needs a database of newspaper obituary submission requirements (format, pricing, deadlines, photo specs, portal URLs), the ability to reformat a single approved obituary text to each paper's specifications, word-count-based pricing estimates so the family can approve costs before submission, submission tracking (confirmed received, confirmed for print date), and clipping archival (saving published versions for the family).

## Target Customer
Funeral homes in metro or multi-community areas where families commonly request placement in 2-4 newspapers per obituary, with staff spending 30-60 minutes per paper on submission logistics.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces multi-newspaper submission from 1-2 hours to 15-20 minutes per case, provides families with accurate cost estimates before submission, and ensures no publication deadline is missed.
