# Obituary Revision Cycle Overhead

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/obituary-publishing/profile|Obituary & Death Notice Publishing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Obituary approval requires 2-4 revision rounds between the funeral home and family — conducted via phone calls, emails, and text photos of marked-up printouts — delaying publication and consuming staff time.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
After the funeral director drafts the obituary, the family must review and approve it. This sounds simple but typically involves: emailing a draft to the family contact, who forwards it to 3-5 family members for input, who provide conflicting edits via phone calls and text messages back to the funeral home contact, who relays them to the funeral home staff. The staff member incorporates changes, sends a revised draft, and the cycle repeats 2-3 times. Common issues: a sibling was omitted from the survivor list, a middle name is wrong, the family disagrees about whether to include the cause of death, someone wants a Bible verse added. Each round takes 2-4 hours of wall-clock time and 15-30 minutes of staff effort.

## Why It's Still Broken
The revision process uses general-purpose communication tools (email, phone, text) rather than a purpose-built approval workflow. There's no single shared document where all family members can see the current draft, make suggestions, and reach consensus. The funeral home staff member serves as a human relay between family members who are communicating about the obituary through separate channels. The pressure of publication deadlines (newspaper print schedules) adds urgency that intensifies the chaotic communication.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A shared obituary review interface (web-based, accessible via link — no account creation) where the funeral home publishes the draft and all designated family members can view, comment, and suggest changes in one place. The funeral home sees all suggestions consolidated, makes one round of edits, and publishes the final version for family sign-off with a single "approve" button. Track changes and comment threading replace the phone-email-text relay. A deadline countdown shows when the final version must be locked for newspaper submission.

## Who Feels the Pain
Funeral home staff members who spend 1-2 hours per case managing obituary revision communications across multiple channels, and families who feel the process is disorganized during an already stressful time.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces revision cycles from 3-4 rounds to 1-2, cuts staff time per obituary from 2-3 hours to 45-60 minutes, and ensures newspaper deadlines are met by making the approval timeline visible to all parties.
