# AI-Assisted Obituary Drafting & Publishing Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/obituary-publishing/profile|Obituary & Death Notice Publishing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool generates a warm, personalized obituary draft from the biographical data already captured during the arrangement conference, then publishes it across newspaper, web, and social channels from one workflow.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #ai-agent #automation

## The Problem
Obituary writing is an underappreciated time sink: the funeral home staff member (often the director) must synthesize 30+ biographical data points collected during the arrangement conference into a flowing narrative that honors the deceased's life, matches the family's preferred tone (formal, celebratory, humorous, religious), and includes all required information (survivors, service details, memorial donation instructions). Current templates produce lifeless text ("John was born on... He is survived by..."). Families expect something that reads like a tribute, not a form letter.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Pre-LLM, generating natural-sounding text from structured data required either templates (which sound robotic) or human writers (which doesn't scale). LLMs now make it possible to generate warm, personalized obituary drafts from biographical data — but the funeral industry hasn't adopted AI writing tools because: (1) the emotional stakes are extreme (a bad obituary is unforgivable), (2) the tool must be integrated into the funeral workflow (not a separate ChatGPT session), and (3) families must approve every word, requiring a collaborative editing interface.

## What to Build
An obituary platform that pulls biographical data from the case management system (or from the arrangement conference notes), generates a draft in the family's preferred tone (selectable: traditional, celebratory, faith-centered, brief notice), presents it through a family-facing review interface (like Google Docs commenting), and upon approval publishes to: the funeral home website, Legacy.com, selected newspapers (formatted per their specs), and social media. The AI draft saves 30-45 minutes of writing; the review interface eliminates 2-3 email/phone revision rounds.

## Target Customer
Funeral homes handling 100+ cases/year where obituary writing and placement consumes 2-3 hours per case — roughly 200-300 staff hours per year dedicated to this single task.

## Impact If Built
Reduces obituary turnaround from 24-48 hours (with revision cycles) to 2-4 hours, saves 1-2 hours of staff time per case, produces higher-quality tributes than formulaic templates, and reduces family frustration with revision cycles.
