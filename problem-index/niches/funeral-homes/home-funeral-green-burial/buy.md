# Green Burial Site Management System

**One-liner:** Cemetery management software like CIMS and CemSites exists for traditional cemeteries but doesn't handle green burial's unique requirements — GPS-based plot marking without headstones, conservation easement tracking, and biodegradation-compliant inventory management.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #computer-vision #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Green burial grounds don't use traditional headstones or grid-based plot maps — they use GPS coordinates, native plantings, and natural markers to identify burial locations within conservation landscapes. Traditional cemetery software assumes rows, sections, and monument records. A green burial operator needs to track: GPS coordinates for each burial, depth and orientation, shroud/container type (must be biodegradable), no-embalming verification, native plant restoration progress over the plot, and conservation easement compliance for the land itself.

## What Already Exists
CIMS (Cemetery Information Management System) and CemSites are the standard cemetery management platforms. They handle plot inventory, sales, interment records, and monument tracking. They assume a grid-based cemetery with defined sections, lots, and headstones. The green burial use case — GPS-only plot identification on conservation land with no permanent markers — doesn't fit their data model.

## The Customization Gap
The system needs GPS-based plot mapping overlaid on satellite imagery of the conservation site, with burial records attached to coordinates rather than grid references. It must track biodegradable container verification (no metal, no concrete vault), no-embalming attestation, depth compliance, and post-burial native planting records. Conservation easement compliance reporting (land use restrictions, ecological monitoring) should be integrated so the burial ground can demonstrate it's meeting its conservation obligations alongside its burial function.

## Target Customer
Green burial cemetery operators (approximately 350 certified green burial sites in the US) managing 20-200+ burials per year on conservation land, currently using paper maps and spreadsheets.

## Impact If Solved
Provides green burial operators with professional management tools comparable to traditional cemeteries, ensures every burial meets green burial certification standards, and simplifies conservation easement reporting — reducing administrative burden by 50% and enabling growth in a segment limited by manual operations.
