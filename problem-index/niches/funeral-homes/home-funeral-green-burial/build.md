# Home Funeral Legal & Care Guide Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/home-funeral-green-burial/profile|Home Funeral & Green Burial Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No digital platform guides families through the jurisdiction-specific legal requirements, body care procedures, and paperwork needed to conduct a home funeral — information that currently requires hiring a death doula or extensive self-research.
**Tags:** #compliance #ai-agent #nlp #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A family in Oregon that wants to care for their loved one's body at home and hold a home vigil before a green burial must navigate: state laws on who can file the death certificate, whether a funeral director must be involved in transportation, body cooling requirements and timeline, disposition permit procedures, and county-specific registration processes. This information is scattered across state statutes, county clerk websites, and death care advocacy blogs. Families discover this in the immediate aftermath of a death — the worst possible time to do legal research.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The home funeral movement is small and grassroots, with no industry association or trade group to fund software development. Death doulas and home funeral guides are typically individual practitioners with limited tech capacity. The legal complexity is high (50 states with different laws, plus county-level variation), and the emotional sensitivity of the topic makes it difficult to productize without feeling transactional. Building this requires partnership with the death positive movement, not just technical capability.

## What to Build
A jurisdiction-aware digital guide that asks the family their state and county, then provides a step-by-step checklist: legal requirements for home care of the body, body cooling and preservation procedures (dry ice, cooling blankets), timeline constraints, death certificate filing process, transportation rules, and disposition permit procedures. Include video guides for body care, templates for required paperwork, and a directory of death doulas and home funeral guides for families who want professional support.

## Target Customer
Families choosing home funerals (estimated 5-10% of deaths in progressive states), death doulas guiding 10-30 families per year, and funeral homes that want to offer home funeral support as a service option.

## Impact If Built
Enables families to navigate the home funeral process without costly professional guidance ($500-2,000 for a death doula), reduces legal compliance errors that can delay or complicate disposition, and makes home funerals accessible to families without connections to the death positive community.
