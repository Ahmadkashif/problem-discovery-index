## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Home funerals (where the family cares for the body at home rather than at a funeral home) and green/natural burials (no embalming, biodegradable containers, conservation land) are the fastest-growing alternative death care movements. They are driven by environmental values, desire for family involvement, and cost concerns. But the legal landscape is complex: home funeral legality varies by state (some require funeral director involvement, some don't), green burial sites have specific requirements (no embalming, depth specifications, GPS plot marking instead of headstones), and families need education on body care, cooling, and legal paperwork that funeral directors typically handle.

## Current Tools & Gaps
This segment has essentially no purpose-built software. Death doulas use Google Docs and Facebook groups. Green burial cemeteries manage plots with paper maps and spreadsheets. Families navigating home funerals rely on books ("Final Rights" by Joshua Slocum) and nonprofit hotlines. There is no digital platform that guides a family through the legal requirements, body care procedures, and paperwork for a home funeral in their specific jurisdiction.
