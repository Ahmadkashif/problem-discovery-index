# FTC Funeral Rule Compliance Gaps

**Niche:** [[niches/funeral-homes/traditional-full-service/profile|Traditional Full-Service Funerals]]
**Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Funeral homes risk $10,000+ FTC fines because their General Price Lists are outdated, inconsistently presented, and don't comply with itemization requirements — problems that are entirely preventable with proper tooling.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #nlp #quick-win

## The Problem
The FTC Funeral Rule requires funeral homes to provide an itemized General Price List (GPL) to every family during the arrangement conference, offer prices by phone on request, and avoid bundling services in ways that obscure individual costs. Compliance seems simple but fails in practice: GPLs aren't updated when prices change, phone staff aren't trained to quote prices accurately, and arrangement conference practices drift from the scripted process over time. FTC mystery shopper audits result in violations at 25-30% of homes tested.

## Why It's Still Broken
GPL management is typically a once-a-year update printed by the office manager. Between updates, prices may change for caskets, vault suppliers, or cremation fees — but the GPL isn't reprinted. Phone staff rotate and don't always know they must provide prices when asked. The funeral director conducting arrangements may present packages rather than itemized pricing, or may not physically hand the GPL to the family. There's no systematic check that ensures compliance happens consistently at every interaction.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A digital GPL system that's always current — when any item price changes, the GPL updates automatically across all formats (printed, web, phone reference). Phone staff see a "price quote mode" screen that shows exactly what they must disclose. The arrangement conference workflow includes a compliance checkpoint: the system confirms the GPL was presented and logs the confirmation. Quarterly self-audits flag common violation patterns before the FTC's mystery shopper finds them.

## Who Feels the Pain
Funeral home owners who face $10,000-50,000 in FTC fines per violation, reputational damage from public enforcement actions, and the stress of knowing their compliance is inconsistent but not knowing where the gaps are.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates FTC Funeral Rule violation risk ($10,000-50,000 per violation), ensures pricing transparency that builds family trust, and removes the anxiety of audit exposure from funeral home owners.
