# Traditional Full-Service Funerals

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/funeral-homes|Funeral Homes]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $12.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~55%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most use funeral-specific software (FrontRunner, Passare, HMIS) for case management, but many processes remain paper-based and the software is dated.
**Target Buyer:** Funeral home owner or managing director at a family-owned, 100-300 case/year operation
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Arrangement paperwork, vendor coordination, and scheduling are automatable; pastoral care and family interaction are not.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Traditional full-service funerals involve a complex multi-day workflow: first call (body retrieval), arrangement conference with the family, coordination with cemetery/church/caterer/florist/newspaper, embalming and preparation, visitation, service, procession, and committal. Each case touches 8-15 vendors, generates 20-30 documents, and must comply with FTC Funeral Rule pricing requirements. The emotional intensity of working with grieving families makes every interaction high-stakes — there is no "redo" on a funeral.

## Current Tools & Gaps
FrontRunner, Passare, and HMIS are the dominant funeral home management systems. They handle case management, accounting, and some document generation, but vendor coordination (florist orders, church scheduling, cemetery plots) is still done by phone. The software interfaces are 10-15 years behind modern UX standards, and integration with external services (payment processing, online tributes, livestreaming) requires manual bridging.
