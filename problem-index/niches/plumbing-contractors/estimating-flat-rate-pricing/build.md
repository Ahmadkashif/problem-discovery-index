# Dynamic Flat-Rate Pricebook with Market Intelligence

**Niche:** [[niches/plumbing-contractors/estimating-flat-rate-pricing/profile|Estimating & Flat-Rate Pricing]]
**Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A flat-rate pricebook that automatically adjusts task prices based on real-time material costs, local market comparison data, and the company's own close rate analytics — replacing static spreadsheet pricing with a dynamic system that keeps the company competitive and profitable without manual updates.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #optimization-fundamentals #revenue-impact #automation

## The Problem
A residential plumbing company's flat-rate pricebook is the most important document in the business — it determines every dollar of revenue. Yet it's built and maintained manually. The typical pricebook journey: owner buys a template ($2,000-5,000), spends 100-200 hours customizing it for local labor rates and material costs, loads it into ServiceTitan, and then forgets about it for 6-18 months. During that time, copper prices increase 15%, PVC fitting costs jump 20%, and a competitor opens across town with aggressive pricing. The pricebook is now simultaneously unprofitable on material-heavy tasks and uncompetitive on labor-heavy tasks. The owner doesn't know which tasks are losing money because no analytics connect the pricebook to actual job costing data (what did materials actually cost on this job vs. what the pricebook assumed?). Nor does the owner know which tasks are priced too high — the only signal is a declining close rate that they notice months later.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Dynamic pricing requires three data feeds that don't exist in one place: (1) real-time material cost data — supply house pricing varies by distributor, account, and volume; there's no public API for plumbing material costs, and prices change weekly for volatile commodities like copper, (2) local market comparison — there's no Zillow for plumbing pricing; knowing what competitors charge for a water heater install requires calling them or surveying customers, and (3) close rate analytics per task category — ServiceTitan tracks proposals and accepted jobs, but connecting "the customer declined the $4,200 tankless water heater install" to "our price is 15% above market" requires analysis nobody does. Building the material cost feed requires partnerships with or scraping of major distributors (Ferguson, Hajoca, Winsupply). Building the market comparison requires aggregating pricing data across customers. Both are cold-start problems.

## What to Build
A pricebook management platform with three components: (1) a material cost engine that ingests pricing from distributor APIs, catalogs, and manual entries, auto-updating the material component of every task in the pricebook when input costs change — when copper couplings increase by $0.50, every task containing copper couplings is repriced automatically, (2) a close rate optimizer that analyzes the company's own job data (proposals presented, accepted, rejected, by task type) and identifies tasks where close rates are below target (suggesting overpricing) or significantly above average (suggesting underpricing and leaving money on the table), using a gradient-boosted model to predict close probability at different price points, and (3) a market intelligence layer that aggregates anonymized pricing data across platform customers to show how each task's price compares to local market averages. The platform integrates with ServiceTitan and FieldEdge via API and presents a dashboard where the service manager reviews recommended price changes weekly. Revenue model: $149-299/month, positioned as "your pricebook never goes stale again."

## Target Customer
Residential plumbing service companies with 3-20 technicians using flat-rate pricing, whose pricebook hasn't been updated in 6+ months, losing money on material-heavy tasks due to cost increases and losing close rate on labor-heavy tasks due to competitive pressure — but lacking the time or data to fix either systematically.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates pricebook staleness — the #1 profitability leak in residential service plumbing. A 10-tech operation with $2M revenue losing 3-5% margin to outdated material pricing recovers $60K-100K annually. Close rate optimization — moving tasks from 50% acceptance to 60% by adjusting prices to market — adds another $100K-200K in annual revenue. Combined, the platform pays for itself 50-100x.
