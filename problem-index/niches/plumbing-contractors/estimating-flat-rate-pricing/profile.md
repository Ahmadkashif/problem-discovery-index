# Estimating & Flat-Rate Pricing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (embedded across all residential service plumbing)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Touches all residential service segments
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — ServiceTitan and FieldEdge include static pricebook modules, but building and maintaining a competitive, profitable flat-rate pricebook with 500-2,000 tasks remains a manual spreadsheet exercise
**Target Buyer:** Service manager or owner building and maintaining a flat-rate pricebook for residential plumbing service, trying to set prices that are competitive enough to close jobs and profitable enough to cover overhead
**Automation Potential:** Very High — pricebook management involves tracking material costs, labor time estimates, overhead allocation, and competitive positioning — all data-driven tasks that are currently done manually

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential service plumbing has largely moved from time-and-materials pricing to flat-rate pricing — the plumber quotes a fixed price for the task before starting work, and the homeowner knows the cost upfront. This model requires a "pricebook" — a master list of 500-2,000 tasks (replace kitchen faucet, install water heater, clear main drain, etc.) each with a fixed price calculated from: estimated labor time, material cost, truck stock allocation, overhead per hour, and profit margin. Building this pricebook from scratch takes 200-400 hours. Maintaining it requires quarterly updates as material costs change (copper pipe prices fluctuated 40% in 2021-2023). And optimizing it requires data that most plumbing companies don't collect — which tasks have high close rates at current prices, which have low close rates (priced too high), and where is the company leaving money on the table (priced too low for the local market). ServiceTitan includes a pricebook module, but it's a static database — prices don't adjust when copper prices spike, and there's no competitive intelligence.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ServiceTitan's pricebook is the most widely used — it allows companies to create tasks with labor hours, material costs, and markup, then present flat-rate pricing options to homeowners on a tablet. But the pricebook is only as good as its inputs, which are manually maintained. When a copper coupling goes from $2.50 to $4.00, every task that includes copper fittings needs a price update — a manual process across hundreds of tasks. Flat Rate Plus and Callahan Roach sell pre-built pricebooks ($2,000-5,000 one-time) but these are static snapshots that are outdated within a year. No product dynamically adjusts flat-rate prices based on current material costs, close rate data, or local market comparison.
