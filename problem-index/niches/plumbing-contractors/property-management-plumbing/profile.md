# Property Management Plumbing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — property management companies track work orders in Yardi, AppFolio, or Buildium, but these systems have no plumbing equipment lifecycle knowledge; plumbing contractors serving these accounts use basic FSM or paper
**Target Buyer:** Property management company maintenance director or in-house maintenance supervisor managing plumbing across a portfolio of 50-500+ residential or commercial units
**Automation Potential:** High — equipment failure prediction, maintenance scheduling, and compliance tracking across a portfolio of properties are well-suited to data-driven automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Property management plumbing is recurring, predictable, and compliance-driven — the opposite of residential emergency service. A property management company or HOA with 200 residential units needs: annual water heater inspections, quarterly backflow preventer testing (mandated by local water authorities), periodic drain cleaning on shared sewer laterals, emergency response for tenant-reported leaks and backups, and fixture replacement in unit turns. The plumbing contractor serving these accounts operates under service contracts with defined SLAs (emergency response within 2 hours, non-emergency within 24 hours) and invoices monthly against a portfolio, not per-call. The key differentiator from residential service: the property manager cares about total cost of ownership across the portfolio, not individual job pricing. They want to know: "How much will plumbing cost me this year across all 200 units, and what can I do to reduce it?" No plumbing contractor or software tool provides this portfolio-level view.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Property management software (Yardi, AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager) handles work order creation, tenant communication, and vendor payment. These systems know that "Unit 302 submitted a work order for a leaky faucet" but don't know that Unit 302's water heater was installed in 2012 and has a 10-year expected lifespan, or that the building's backflow preventers are due for annual testing in 6 weeks. The plumbing contractor tracks their side of the relationship in ServiceTitan or on paper, but neither party has a shared view of equipment age, maintenance history, and upcoming needs across the portfolio. Preventive maintenance is scheduled manually by the property manager's maintenance coordinator, who maintains a spreadsheet of equipment and due dates that becomes outdated within weeks.
