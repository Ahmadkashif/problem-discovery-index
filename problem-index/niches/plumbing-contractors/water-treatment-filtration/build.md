# Water Quality Test Interpreter and Treatment System Recommender

**Niche:** [[niches/plumbing-contractors/water-treatment-filtration/profile|Water Treatment & Filtration]]
**Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A system that ingests raw water test results, interprets contaminant interactions and treatment sequencing requirements, and outputs a complete treatment system recommendation with equipment sizing, installation specs, and estimated annual operating costs — replacing the water chemistry expertise that takes 5-10 years to develop.
**Tags:** #decision-trees #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #tacit-knowledge-ml #revenue-impact #automation

## The Problem
When a water treatment contractor receives water test results — typically 15-40 parameters including hardness, iron, manganese, pH, TDS, nitrates, bacteria, sulfur, and various heavy metals — they must: (1) identify which parameters exceed EPA limits or customer comfort thresholds, (2) determine contaminant interactions (iron fouls softener resin if not removed first; low pH corrodes copper downstream of softeners; tannins interfere with iron filter media), (3) design a treatment train in the correct sequence (sediment filter → iron filter → softener → acid neutralizer → RO for drinking water → UV for bacteria), (4) size each component for the household's peak flow rate and daily water usage, and (5) quote the system with equipment, installation labor, and annual maintenance costs. Experienced water treatment specialists perform this analysis in 10-15 minutes from memory. General plumbers adding water treatment services get it wrong 30-40% of the time — usually by missing contaminant interactions that cause premature equipment failure or inadequate treatment.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Water treatment system design is rule-based at its core — contaminant X at level Y requires treatment Z — but the rules have hundreds of interaction effects that aren't documented in any single reference. Equipment manufacturer literature covers their own products but doesn't address treatment sequencing, competitive alternatives, or the chemistry of multi-contaminant scenarios. The tacit knowledge of experienced water treatment specialists lives in mental decision trees refined over thousands of installations: "well water in this region typically has iron + manganese + low pH — you need a birm filter before the softener, but only if the pH is above 6.8; below that, chemical injection is required." Capturing these decision trees requires interviewing experienced practitioners, codifying their branching logic, and validating against real-world water chemistry scenarios. The market is also fragmented — thousands of small contractors, no dominant buyer — making customer acquisition expensive relative to deal size.

## What to Build
A web and mobile application where the contractor enters water test results (manual entry or photo-OCR of lab reports) and the system outputs: (1) a plain-English interpretation of water quality issues ranked by health risk and aesthetic impact, (2) a recommended treatment train with equipment sequence, explaining why order matters, (3) equipment options across 3-4 major manufacturers with model numbers, sizing for the customer's flow rate and usage, and estimated equipment cost, (4) installation requirements (electrical, drain, space), and (5) annual operating cost (filter replacements, salt, electricity, maintenance visits). The recommendation engine uses a decision-tree model that codifies contaminant interactions and treatment sequencing rules, with gradient-boosted refinement trained on contractor-reported outcomes (did the recommended system achieve target water quality at 6 and 12 months?). A customer-facing report is auto-generated for presentation at the kitchen table. Pricing at $79-149/month positions it as a fractional water chemistry expert.

## Target Customer
Plumbing contractors adding water treatment services who lack deep water chemistry expertise, and dedicated water treatment companies whose junior technicians design systems inconsistently because the knowledge lives only in the owner's head.

## Impact If Built
Enables any plumber to perform expert-level water treatment system design, expanding the addressable workforce for water treatment services by 10x. Reduces system design errors that cause callbacks from 30-40% (for general plumbers) to under 10%. For a water treatment contractor installing 200-400 systems per year, eliminating redesign callbacks at $500-1,500 each saves $30K-100K annually. The customer-facing report also increases close rates by 15-25% compared to verbal recommendations.
