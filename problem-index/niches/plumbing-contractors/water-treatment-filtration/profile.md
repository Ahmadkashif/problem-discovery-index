# Water Treatment & Filtration

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most water treatment contractors use paper-based water test interpretation guides from equipment manufacturers, with scheduling handled by basic tools or paper calendars; no purpose-built software exists for residential/commercial water treatment
**Target Buyer:** Water treatment contractor or plumber adding water treatment services, serving residential well water and municipal water customers with filtration, softening, and purification systems
**Automation Potential:** High — water test interpretation follows deterministic chemistry rules, treatment system sizing uses manufacturer tables, and filter replacement scheduling is a simple recurring-service automation opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Water treatment plumbing sits at the intersection of plumbing and chemistry. The workflow: test the customer's water (either in-house with test strips and photometers, or via a lab), interpret results against EPA standards and customer preferences, recommend a treatment system (water softener, iron filter, sediment filter, reverse osmosis, UV disinfection, acid neutralizer, or a combination), size the system for the household's water usage and flow rate, install it, and then maintain it with recurring filter replacements, salt delivery, and annual service visits. The business model is attractive because it generates recurring revenue — a customer who buys a $3,000 water softener becomes a $300-500/year maintenance customer for the life of the system. The challenge is that water chemistry interpretation requires knowledge most plumbers don't have. An experienced water treatment specialist reads a water test and instantly knows that 8 gpg hardness + 3 ppm iron + 6.2 pH means a softener alone won't work — the low pH will corrode copper pipes and the iron will foul a standard softener, requiring an iron filter upstream and an acid neutralizer. A general plumber with the same test results installs a softener and gets a callback in 6 months.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Water treatment equipment manufacturers (Kinetico, Culligan, WaterBoss, Pentair) provide sizing calculators for their own product lines, but these are single-manufacturer tools that don't compare across brands or suggest multi-stage treatment trains. Water testing labs (National Testing Laboratories, Tap Score) provide test results with basic pass/fail flagging against EPA limits but don't recommend specific treatment systems or account for the interaction between contaminants (iron + low pH + hardness require a specific treatment sequence). No software product exists that takes raw water test results as input and outputs a complete treatment system recommendation with sizing, installation requirements, equipment options across manufacturers, and estimated operating costs.
