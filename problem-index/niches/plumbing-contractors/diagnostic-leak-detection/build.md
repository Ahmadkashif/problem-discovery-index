# Leak Detection Diagnostic Decision Support System

**Niche:** [[niches/plumbing-contractors/diagnostic-leak-detection/profile|Diagnostic Leak Detection]]
**Industry:** [[industries/plumbing-contractors|Plumbing Contractors]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A structured diagnostic workflow that guides a plumber through systematic leak detection testing based on symptoms, building type, pipe material, and equipment readings — encoding 15+ years of tacit sensory expertise into a decision tree that any licensed plumber can follow to locate leaks accurately on the first attempt.

## The Problem
Finding a hidden leak is the hardest diagnostic task in plumbing. An experienced leak detection specialist develops a systematic mental model over 10-15 years: they start with the symptom presentation (high water bill with no visible leak, water stain on ceiling, warm spot on slab, sound of running water in walls), form hypotheses about leak type and location, then run a sequence of tests to narrow down the location. The test sequence itself is tacit knowledge — an expert knows to start with a static pressure test to confirm a supply-side leak exists, then isolate hot from cold to identify the leaking line, then use acoustic equipment to pinpoint the location on the slab, then confirm with thermal imaging. A junior plumber skips steps, starts with the wrong equipment, or misinterprets readings. The consequences are expensive: cutting a test hole in the wrong location costs $500-1,500 in drywall repair and damages customer trust. Experienced leak detection specialists charge $250-500 just for the diagnosis because the skill is rare.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Leak detection integrates multiple sensory modalities — sound (acoustic equipment), temperature (thermal camera), pressure (gauges), and visual pattern recognition (stains, cracks, condensation). Building a diagnostic decision support system requires: (1) codifying the expert's mental model into a branching decision tree (if symptom X + test result Y, then check Z), (2) integrating data from multiple equipment types (acoustic amplitude at frequency, thermal gradient direction, pressure decay rate), and (3) handling the enormous variability in building construction (slab-on-grade vs. crawlspace vs. basement, copper vs. PEX vs. CPVC vs. polybutylene, 1960s construction vs. 2020s). The data collection challenge is severe: documenting an expert's diagnostic reasoning requires recording their narration during actual leak detection calls, which few experts are willing to do. The labeling challenge is that the "correct" diagnosis is only confirmed when the leak is exposed — a destructive verification that can't be repeated for training purposes.

## What to Build
A tablet-based diagnostic workflow application with two layers: (1) a structured decision tree that guides the plumber through a systematic diagnostic sequence — starting with symptom intake (what does the customer report?), building assessment (construction type, pipe material, age), and then a branching test protocol where each test result narrows the possible leak types and locations, and (2) equipment integration modules that capture and interpret data from acoustic sensors (audio spectral analysis to distinguish supply leak sounds from drain sounds, with a CNN classifier trained on labeled leak audio recordings), thermal cameras (FLIR SDK integration for automated heat plume direction analysis), and pressure gauges (pressure decay curve analysis to estimate leak severity and pipe section isolation). The system produces a ranked list of probable leak locations with confidence scores and recommended verification approach. Training data comes from partnering with 5-10 experienced leak detection specialists who record diagnostic sessions with narration, building a labeled dataset of symptom → test sequence → confirmed location examples. Revenue model: $199-399/month for leak detection specialists or $99/month for general plumbing companies, plus per-diagnosis fees for casual users.

## Target Customer
Plumbing companies that want to offer leak detection services but lack a specialist-level diagnostician, and established leak detection companies that need to train junior technicians to perform expert-level diagnoses without 10-15 years of on-the-job learning.

## Impact If Built
Reduces leak detection misdiagnosis from 20-30% (junior technicians) to under 5% by providing structured diagnostic guidance. Enables general plumbing companies to offer premium leak detection services ($250-500 per diagnosis) without hiring a dedicated specialist. Reduces property damage from exploratory test holes by guiding verification to the highest-probability location first. For a plumbing company adding leak detection services at 5-10 calls per week, this generates $65K-130K in new annual revenue from a service line that was previously inaccessible without expert hiring.
