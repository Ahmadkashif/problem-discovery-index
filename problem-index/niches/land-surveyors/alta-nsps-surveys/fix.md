# Table A Item Omissions Causing Rejection and Rework

**Niche:** [[niches/land-surveyors/alta-nsps-surveys/profile|ALTA/NSPS Surveys]]
**Industry:** [[industries/land-surveyors|Land Surveyors]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** 15-20% of ALTA surveys are rejected by title companies because the surveyor missed a Table A item or used outdated certification language — each rejection costs 2-3 hours of rework and delays the real estate closing.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #quick-win #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
The ALTA/NSPS standard has 20 Table A optional items, each with specific requirements. A title company selects items based on the transaction's needs — item 6(b) for building setbacks, item 11(a) for utilities, item 19 for professional liability insurance. The surveyor must address every selected item on the plat. But the Table A selection arrives via email (often as a checked list in a PDF), gets transcribed into the firm's project file, and is referenced during drafting. Items get missed — the surveyor forgets to show parking counts (item 18), uses pre-2021 certification language, or misinterprets which sub-item was selected (11(a) vs. 11(b)). The title company reviews the plat, finds the omission, and sends it back.

## Why It's Still Broken
The workflow is fragmented: the Table A selection exists in an email, the survey work happens in the field and CAD, and the QA check is a manual visual review. There is no digital checklist that connects the client's Table A selections to specific deliverable requirements and tracks whether each requirement has been fulfilled. Most firms rely on institutional memory — the senior surveyor "knows" what each Table A item requires. When that surveyor is out sick or on vacation, the error rate spikes. The 2021 standard update changed several Table A items, and many firms are still using pre-2021 workflows.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A digital Table A compliance tracker that: (1) imports the client's Table A selections at project initiation; (2) expands each selection into specific deliverable requirements (item 6(b) = show setback lines from all boundary lines to all buildings and structures); (3) creates a checklist for the field crew (what to measure) and the drafter (what to show); (4) tracks completion status for each requirement; (5) blocks delivery until all items are marked complete. The tracker is integrated into the firm's CAD or project management workflow so compliance is tracked continuously, not checked after the fact.

## Who Feels the Pain
Survey firm owners who absorb the rework cost (estimated $200-400 per rejection in unbillable labor), title companies whose closings are delayed by survey rework, and the drafting technicians who redo work because of upstream communication failures.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces ALTA rejection rate from 15-20% to under 5%. Saves 2-3 hours of rework per rejected survey. For a firm producing 20 ALTA surveys per month, that's 6-12 hours of recovered billable time monthly. Eliminates the most common cause of closing delays attributable to the survey.
