# Construction Staking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/land-surveyors|Land Surveyors]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~16%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Machine control (Trimble, Topcon) automates grading equipment, but the surveyor-to-contractor communication workflow is still paper stakes, painted marks, and verbal instructions.
**Target Buyer:** Construction Surveyor / Site Superintendent / General Contractor
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — Stake-out calculations are mathematical, and the communication between design engineer, surveyor, and contractor can be digitized.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Construction staking translates engineering design into physical markers on the ground — the surveyor takes the architect's plans and places stakes, hubs, and marks that tell the contractor exactly where to dig, pour, build, and grade. This is a high-frequency, time-sensitive service: a surveyor may visit the same site 10-20 times during a project, staking foundations, utilities, curbs, and finish grade at each construction phase. The audience is underserved because the end user (the contractor operating heavy equipment) needs real-time spatial information but gets batch-processed deliverables — stakes placed yesterday that may have been knocked over, buried, or misinterpreted today. The gap between the surveyor's precision and the contractor's need for "show me where to dig right now" is the core underserved problem.

## Current Tools & Gaps
GPS machine control (Trimble Earthworks, Topcon 3D-MC, Leica iCON) allows grading equipment to follow design surfaces automatically, reducing staking needs for mass grading. But building layout, utility staking, and finish work still require physical stakes. Surveyors use data collectors (Trimble TSC5, Leica CS20) and robotic total stations for staking. Communication with contractors is paper-based: cut/fill sheets printed on-site, stakes marked with spray paint, and verbal explanations. No digital system connects the survey stake to the contractor's understanding of what it means.
