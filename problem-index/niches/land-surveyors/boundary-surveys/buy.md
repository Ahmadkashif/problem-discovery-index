**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
After completing fieldwork and establishing boundary opinions, the surveyor must produce a boundary survey plat — a legal document that includes the drawn boundary, monument descriptions, bearings and distances, area calculations, a written legal description, basis of bearing statement, certification, and jurisdiction-specific notes (flood zone, zoning, easements). CAD software handles the geometry, but every text element — the legal description, monument notation, notes, and certification — is typed manually. A single boundary survey plat takes 2-4 hours of office work, and 60% of that time is text composition, not drafting.

## What Already Exists
AutoCAD (Autodesk), Carlson Survey (Carlson Software), and MicroSurvey are the dominant CAD platforms for surveying. They handle point management, line work, and geometric calculations well. Carlson Survey has some template functionality for plat sheets. SurvPC and FieldGenius handle field-to-office data transfer. None auto-generate the text-heavy components of a boundary plat.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to auto-generate: legal descriptions from the drawn boundary (metes and bounds format, matching the jurisdiction's conventions), monument descriptions from field notes, basis of bearing statements, area calculations in required units, and certification language matching the state board's requirements. Each state has different plat formatting requirements — some require specific font sizes, margin widths, seal placement, and note language. The generated text must be legally precise — a misdescribed bearing or monument notation creates a title defect.

## Target Customer
Survey firms of all sizes producing boundary survey plats. The pain is universal — every boundary survey requires this manual text work.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces plat production time from 2-4 hours to 30-60 minutes by auto-generating 80% of the text content. Reduces errors in legal descriptions that create title defects — estimated 5-8% of manually written legal descriptions contain errors that require correction.
