# Electronic Plan Review Adapted for Subdivision Plat Specifics

**Niche:** [[niches/land-surveyors/plat-subdivision-review/profile|Plat & Subdivision Review]]
**Industry:** [[industries/land-surveyors|Land Surveyors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Electronic plan review platforms (ProjectDox, Bluebeam Studio) handle submission workflow beautifully but treat a subdivision plat the same as an architectural drawing — no survey-specific checklist, no geometric validation, no ordinance-linked review.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Municipalities are adopting electronic plan review platforms to replace paper submission and review. These platforms digitize the workflow — submit electronically, route to reviewers, annotate digitally, track revisions — but they don't add intelligence to the review. A subdivision plat submitted through ProjectDox still requires the reviewer to manually check every ordinance requirement. The platform knows it's a "subdivision plat" document type but doesn't know what that means — it can't distinguish a lot dimension from a street label, can't check lot areas against zoning requirements, and can't verify that the surveyor's certification language matches the county's requirements.

## What Already Exists
ProjectDox (Avolve Software) is the dominant electronic plan review platform for municipalities. Bluebeam Studio provides collaborative PDF markup. ePlans and CityView offer similar submission and routing workflows. Some jurisdictions use OpenGov or Accela for development permitting with plan review modules. All handle document workflow; none handle plat-specific content review.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs a subdivision plat review module that: (1) provides a dynamic checklist linked to the municipality's subdivision ordinance (not a static PDF checklist, but one that adjusts based on zoning district, plat type, and project characteristics); (2) enables reviewers to mark each requirement as pass/fail/not-applicable with annotation directly on the plat; (3) tracks which requirements were failed and whether revisions addressed them (preventing the "fixed one deficiency, introduced another" problem); (4) generates a standardized review letter from the completed checklist; (5) maintains review history across submission cycles. The checklist must be configurable per jurisdiction without custom development.

## Target Customer
Municipal planning and engineering departments with electronic plan review platforms already deployed. Platform vendors (ProjectDox, Bluebeam) as a vertical integration partner.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces review time by 30-40% through structured checklists that prevent reviewers from re-checking requirements already confirmed in prior cycles. Standardizes review quality across multiple reviewers in the same department. Reduces revision cycles by 25-35% through clear, structured deficiency communication.
