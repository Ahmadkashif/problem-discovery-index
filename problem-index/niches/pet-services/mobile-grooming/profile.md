# Mobile Pet Grooming

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most mobile groomers manage bookings via phone/text, have no online presence beyond a Facebook page, and track nothing about their routes or client preferences.
**Target Buyer:** Solo mobile groomer or owner of a 2-5 van fleet
**Automation Potential:** High — Route planning, appointment scheduling, and client preference tracking are all highly automatable for a mobile service business.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mobile groomers bring a fully-equipped van to the client's home — eliminating the pet's stress of travel and kennel waiting. The business model is fundamentally different from salon grooming: higher per-appointment revenue ($60-120 vs. $40-80), geographic route dependency (the van must follow an efficient path), and one-on-one service (no assembly-line bathing). Mobile groomers face unique challenges: van maintenance and equipment management, weather-dependent scheduling, limited water supply per day, and the need to manage appointments across a geographic area without double-booking or creating impossible routes.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most mobile groomers use phone calls and text messages for booking, with a paper appointment book or Google Calendar. No tool designed for mobile grooming handles the intersection of scheduling, route optimization, and grooming-specific client profiles (breed, coat type, temperament, preferred groom style, medical notes). Generic service business scheduling (Jobber, Housecall Pro) doesn't understand grooming appointments, and salon grooming software (Gingr, PetExec) doesn't handle mobile route logistics.
