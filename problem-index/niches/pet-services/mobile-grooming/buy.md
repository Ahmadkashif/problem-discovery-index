# Mobile Grooming Client Profile System

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/mobile-grooming/profile|Mobile Pet Grooming]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CRM tools exist but none maintain the grooming-specific pet profile that mobile groomers need — breed, coat type, preferred cut style, temperament, medical flags, and photos of the desired groom outcome.
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #quick-win #automation

## The Problem
Mobile groomers develop detailed knowledge of each client's pet over repeated visits: how Bella likes her ears trimmed, that Max gets anxious during nail clipping and needs a break, that the Schnauzer at 42 Oak Street has a skin condition that requires specific shampoo. This knowledge lives in the groomer's memory. When a groomer is sick and sends a substitute, the sub has no access to this information. When the groomer takes on 80+ recurring clients, they can't remember every detail. Clients notice when their dog comes back with the wrong ear trim, and consistency is the top driver of retention.

## What Already Exists
HubSpot, Jobber, and even salon grooming software (Gingr) maintain client profiles. But these are people profiles, not pet profiles — or if they support pets, they capture breed and name but not the grooming-specific details that matter: coat condition photos from the last visit, preferred blade length, scissor cut vs. clipper cut, specific areas the pet is sensitive about, dematting history, and before/after photos that show the groomer what the client expects.

## The Customization Gap
The profile system needs per-pet grooming records: coat type and condition, preferred groom style (with reference photos), behavioral notes (anxiety triggers, handling preferences), medical notes (skin conditions, ear infections, lumps to be careful around), and a photo history showing the groom result each visit. When a substitute groomer picks up the route, they see each pet's profile and can deliver consistent results. The system should also flag when a pet is due for a groom based on their recurring schedule and breed-typical grooming frequency.

## Target Customer
Mobile groomers with 50+ recurring clients who rely on memory for pet-specific preferences, experiencing 2-3 complaints per month about inconsistent groom results — each complaint risking a $1,200-2,400/year client relationship.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces groom inconsistency complaints by 60-70%, enables seamless substitute coverage without quality loss, and automates recurring booking reminders that recover 3-5 lapsed clients per month.
