# Medication Administration Tracking Gaps

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/boarding-daycare-facilities/profile|Pet Boarding & Daycare Facilities]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Boarding facilities track medication schedules on whiteboards and paper, leading to missed doses, double-dosing, and liability exposure when staff shift changes create handoff gaps.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A boarding facility with 40 dogs may have 10-15 on medications — insulin injections, anxiety meds, joint supplements, ear drops, eye ointments — each with specific timing, dosage, and administration instructions. This is tracked on a whiteboard or paper chart, checked off by the staff member who administers the medication. But shift changes at 2pm mean the morning staff's checks may not be visible to the afternoon staff. A missed dose of anxiety medication results in a stressed dog; a missed insulin injection is a veterinary emergency. Facilities absorb the liability of medication errors with no digital verification trail.

## Why It's Still Broken
The whiteboard system "works" most of the time — medication errors are infrequent enough that facilities don't prioritize digital tracking. But when errors happen, they're serious: a double dose, a missed dose, or the wrong medication given to the wrong dog. Existing facility management software has medication tracking features, but they're buried in the interface and require navigating away from the operational workflow. Staff in the medication room are juggling 10 dogs' pills and don't want to open a laptop to log each one.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A medication-specific mobile interface (phone or tablet mounted in the medication room) that shows only the medications due now, with photos of each dog and specific instructions. Staff tap to confirm administration, and the system logs the confirmation with timestamp and staff ID. Missed medication alerts escalate: first to the staff member, then to the shift supervisor, then to the manager. The system prevents double-dosing by showing "already administered at 8:15am by Sarah." Shift handoff includes a medication status summary showing what's been given and what's pending.

## Who Feels the Pain
Boarding staff responsible for medication administration who carry the stress of managing 10-15 medication schedules from memory, and facility owners who bear the liability when errors occur.

## Impact If Fixed
Eliminates medication administration errors (reducing liability exposure worth $5,000-50,000 per incident), provides parents with verified medication confirmations, and reduces staff anxiety about medication responsibility — a leading cause of boarding staff turnover.
