# Branded Parent Communication Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/boarding-daycare-facilities/profile|Pet Boarding & Daycare Facilities]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Photo-sharing and messaging platforms exist but none are purpose-built for pet boarding — where parents want daily photos, activity reports, feeding confirmations, and medication logs delivered automatically in a branded experience.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Pet parents dropping off their dog for boarding or daycare want continuous reassurance: photos of their dog playing, confirmation that meals and medications were given, and activity reports. Facilities handle this by having staff take individual phone photos and text them to parents — a process that consumes 1-2 hours of staff time per day and produces inconsistent results (some dogs get 5 photos, others get none). Parents who don't receive updates text the facility asking "How's Buddy doing?" — interrupting operations for a reassurance check.

## What Already Exists
Gingr and PetExec have report card features. DogVacay (now Rover) offered photo updates through its platform. SnapSend and Moment Garden handle photo sharing for childcare centers. But pet facility-specific needs differ: photos need to be linked to the specific pet's profile (not just a general group photo), feeding and medication logs should auto-populate the update, and the experience should be branded to the facility (not a third-party platform) to build the facility's brand loyalty.

## The Customization Gap
The portal needs to integrate with the facility's existing management system to pull pet profiles, feeding schedules, and medication logs into a parent-facing daily report. Staff should be able to take a group photo and tag individual dogs (AI-assisted dog recognition from check-in photos), which auto-distributes the photo to each tagged dog's parent portal. Feeding and medication confirmations entered by staff during the day auto-appear in the report. Parents see a timeline: "10:30am - Buddy played in Group B" → "12:00pm - Lunch served" → "2:15pm - Afternoon nap."

## Target Customer
Boarding and daycare facilities handling 30+ pets per day that currently spend 1-2 staff hours daily on manual photo/update communication and still receive "How's my dog?" texts.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 1-2 hours of daily staff time on manual updates, reduces parent anxiety check-in calls/texts by 70-80%, and differentiates the facility in a market where parent communication quality is the #1 driver of online reviews and referrals.
