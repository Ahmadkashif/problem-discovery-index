# Pet Boarding & Daycare Facilities

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~50%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Facility management software exists (Gingr, PetExec) but many single-location facilities still use spreadsheets and paper.
**Target Buyer:** Owner/operator of a boarding kennel or pet daycare facility with 20-100+ animals per day
**Automation Potential:** High — Reservations, check-in/out, feeding schedules, medication tracking, and parent updates are all systematizable.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pet boarding and daycare facilities manage a complex daily operation: intake assessments (temperament, vaccination verification, special needs), group assignment (matching dogs by size, energy level, and play style), feeding schedules (each pet may have different food, amounts, and timing), medication administration, facility cleaning rotations, staff-to-pet ratios, and real-time parent communication (photos, webcam access, behavior reports). The business is seasonal (holiday peaks require 2-3x normal capacity) and high-trust (pet parents are leaving their "family member" in someone else's care).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Gingr, PetExec, and Kennel Connection offer facility management with reservations, check-in, and basic reporting. These tools handle the administrative layer but don't address the operational floor: group assignment is done by staff judgment, feeding/medication is tracked on whiteboards, and real-time parent communication (the photo and update stream parents crave) is manual — staff taking phone photos and texting them individually.
