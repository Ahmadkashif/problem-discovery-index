# Veterinary Communication Bridge

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/medical-senior-sitting/profile|Specialized Pet Sitting (Medical/Senior)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Telehealth platforms exist for veterinary consultations but don't connect pet sitters to the pet's established veterinarian for real-time guidance during medical pet sitting engagements.
**Tags:** #data-integration #ai-agent #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
When a medical pet sitter encounters a concerning symptom — the diabetic cat is lethargic after insulin, the post-surgical dog's incision looks red — they call the pet owner, who calls the vet, who calls the sitter back (if they can reach everyone in this chain). The vet has no context about what the sitter has observed, the sitter has no direct relationship with the vet, and the owner is a stressed intermediary in a different time zone. Critical decisions are delayed by this communication triangle while the pet's condition may be deteriorating.

## What Already Exists
Vetster and PetDesk offer veterinary telehealth and communication platforms. These connect pet owners to veterinarians for virtual consultations. But they don't accommodate the pet sitter as a temporary care proxy — someone who is physically with the pet and needs to communicate directly with the pet's established vet, not a random telehealth vet unfamiliar with the pet's history.

## The Customization Gap
The platform needs a "temporary care proxy" role: the pet owner authorizes the sitter to communicate directly with the pet's vet during the sitting period. The sitter can send symptom observations (photos, video, vital signs) directly to the vet, who has the pet's medical history. The vet can respond with guidance specific to the pet's condition. When the sitting ends, the proxy access expires. This requires integration with veterinary practice management systems (or at minimum, the ability for vets to access the pet's records during the exchange).

## Target Customer
Medical pet sitters handling 3-8 engagements per month who currently rely on a phone-tag triangle (sitter → owner → vet → sitter) that delays critical decisions by 30-120 minutes.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces emergency communication time from 30-120 minutes to 5-15 minutes, enables faster veterinary decisions that improve pet outcomes, and gives both sitters and owners peace of mind that professional veterinary guidance is immediately accessible.
