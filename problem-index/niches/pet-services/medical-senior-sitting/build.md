# Medical Pet Care Protocol Manager

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/medical-senior-sitting/profile|Specialized Pet Sitting (Medical/Senior)]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool manages the full care protocol for a medically complex pet — medication schedules with dosage tracking, symptom monitoring checklists, emergency escalation procedures, and veterinary-grade care logs.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
A pet sitter caring for a diabetic cat must: administer insulin injections at precise times, monitor food intake (insulin dose depends on whether the cat ate), check blood glucose if the owner provides a monitor, watch for hypoglycemia signs (lethargy, trembling, seizures), and know when to call the emergency vet vs. when to call the owner. This protocol is communicated via a long text message, a handwritten note on the counter, or a verbal walkthrough during the meet-and-greet. If the sitter forgets a step or encounters a scenario not covered in the instructions, they're on their own.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The medical pet sitting market is small and fragmented — sitters are solo operators, not businesses buying SaaS products. The care protocols are pet-specific (every diabetic pet has different insulin types, doses, and schedules), making it impossible to create a one-size-fits-all checklist. Building this requires structured templates for common conditions (diabetes, seizure disorders, renal disease, post-surgical care) that can be customized per pet, with the veterinary knowledge to make the templates clinically useful.

## What to Build
A care protocol builder where the pet owner (or veterinarian) creates a structured medical care plan for their pet: medications with exact dosing schedules, feeding requirements, monitoring checklists, symptom escalation tiers (green/yellow/red with specific actions for each), and emergency contacts in priority order. The sitter receives this as an interactive checklist during the sitting, confirming each action with timestamps. Deviations from the protocol (missed feeding, abnormal symptom observation) trigger automatic notifications to the owner.

## Target Customer
Pet sitters specializing in medical/senior care who handle 3-8 sitting engagements per month, each requiring complex care protocols that take 30-60 minutes to communicate and are still incomplete.

## Impact If Built
Reduces care instruction communication time from 30-60 minutes to 10 minutes (protocol is built once and reused), eliminates missed care steps through interactive checklists, and provides pet owners with a verified care log that dramatically reduces anxiety during their absence.
