# Dog Walking & Daily Pet Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Platforms like Rover and Wag dominate discovery, but independent walkers manage operations via text and Venmo.
**Target Buyer:** Solo dog walker or owner of a 2-5 person walking company
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Route optimization, scheduling, and client updates are automatable; the walking itself and pet rapport are not.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Dog walking is the entry point for most pet service businesses: low startup cost, immediate demand, and repeat revenue. But the economics are challenging — walkers typically charge $15-25 per 30-minute walk, must travel between clients (unpaid time), and handle 4-8 walks per day. Independent walkers compete with Rover and Wag on discovery but prefer to move clients off-platform to avoid 15-20% commission. The operational challenge is managing a growing book of recurring clients across a geographic area with scheduling changes, weather cancellations, and key management.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Rover and Wag handle discovery and booking but take 15-20% commission. Time To Pet and PetPocketbook offer independent walker management tools (scheduling, invoicing, GPS tracking). But most solo walkers still manage via text messages, Google Calendar, and Venmo — the existing tools feel overbuilt for a one-person operation doing 4-8 walks per day.
