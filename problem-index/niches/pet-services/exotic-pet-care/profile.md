# Exotic & Non-Dog/Cat Pet Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $520M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — No mainstream pet service platform supports reptiles, birds, rabbits, fish, or small mammals; owners rely on specialized forums and word of mouth.
**Target Buyer:** Owners of reptiles, birds, rabbits, guinea pigs, ferrets, fish, or other non-dog/cat pets needing care during travel
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Husbandry automation (lighting, temperature, humidity) is highly automatable; handling-intensive species require skilled human care.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The 20+ million US households with exotic pets face a near-total absence of professional pet care services. Rover lists "other pets" as a category but virtually no sitters have experience with reptile husbandry (UVB lighting cycles, temperature gradients, humidity requirements), bird care (flock socialization, wing clipping awareness, specialized diets), or aquarium maintenance (water chemistry, feeding schedules, filtration). An inexperienced sitter who forgets to maintain a bearded dragon's heat lamp or overfeeds a fish tank can cause death in days. The knowledge required is species-specific and can't be generalized from dog/cat experience.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Rover allows listing "exotic" pets but provides no sitter verification for exotic care competency. Facebook groups (e.g., "Reptile Sitters of [State]") exist but are small, unvetted, and geographically sparse. No platform matches exotic pet owners with species-experienced caregivers, provides species-specific care instruction templates, or offers the husbandry monitoring tools (temperature, humidity, lighting) that would allow remote oversight of an automated setup.
