# Lost Health Observation Data

**Niche:** [[niches/pet-services/health-behavior-documentation/profile|Pet Health & Behavior Documentation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pet-services|Pet Services]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Pet service providers notice health and behavior changes daily but have no way to record and share these observations — valuable data that could prevent emergencies disappears into memory.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A pet sitter notices that the elderly cat is drinking more water than usual. A dog walker notices that a normally-social dog has started hiding during drop-off. A daycare handler notices that a dog's coat is thinning. These observations are mentioned to the owner in passing ("Oh by the way, Bella seemed thirsty today") or not at all — and the owner doesn't connect the dots because each individual observation seems minor. Two months later, the cat is diagnosed with kidney disease, the dog with anxiety, and the daycare dog with a thyroid condition. The early warning signs were observed but never captured.

## Why It's Still Broken
Pet service providers aren't trained to document observations — they're trained to provide care. Documentation feels like "extra work" unrelated to their job. Even providers who want to document have no standard framework for what's worth noting vs. what's normal variation. And the communication channel to the owner (a text message) doesn't distinguish between "heads up, something might be off" and "Bella had a great walk!" — health observations get buried in the daily update stream and never reach the veterinarian.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A flagging system within the daily update where the provider can mark an observation as "health note" with one tap — separating it from the routine update. Health notes accumulate in a pet health timeline that the owner can review and optionally share with their vet. The system provides simple guidance on what's worth flagging: "changes from this pet's baseline in energy, appetite, water intake, mobility, behavior, bathroom habits, or physical appearance." Monthly health summaries aggregate flagged observations for the owner.

## Who Feels the Pain
Pet owners who learn about their pet's health issue too late and realize their walker or sitter had been observing early signs for weeks without a structured way to communicate them, and providers who feel helpless when a pet they saw daily develops a preventable condition.

## Impact If Fixed
Creates a structured communication channel for health observations between providers and owners, enables earlier veterinary intervention (estimated 2-6 weeks earlier diagnosis for chronic conditions), and positions pet service providers as health partners — increasing perceived value and retention.
