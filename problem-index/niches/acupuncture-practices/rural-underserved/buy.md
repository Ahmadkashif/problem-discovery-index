# Telehealth Platform Adapted for Acupuncture Follow-Up

**Niche:** [[niches/acupuncture-practices/rural-underserved/profile|Rural & Underserved-Area Acupuncturists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** General telehealth platforms handle video visits but don't support acupuncture-specific follow-up workflows — acupoint self-care instruction, herbal formula adjustments, or treatment plan progress tracking.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Rural patients drive 30-60 minutes for acupuncture appointments. Many treatments require 6-12 weekly sessions for chronic conditions. Drop-off rates are 40-50% after visit 3 because the travel burden becomes unsustainable. Telehealth could handle follow-up check-ins, self-care instruction, and herbal formula adjustments — but acupuncture practitioners cannot do needle treatments remotely, creating an awkward fit with standard telehealth platforms designed for consultation-based medicine.

## What Already Exists
Doxy.me, SimplePractice Telehealth, and Jane App's built-in video offer HIPAA-compliant video visits. These work for talk-based consultations but provide no acupuncture-specific features: no acupoint instruction tools (showing patients where to apply acupressure at home), no visual tongue/pulse assessment aids, no herbal formula management, and no structured treatment progress tracking between in-person needling sessions.

## The Customization Gap
The telehealth platform needs acupuncture overlays: (1) an interactive body map for guiding patients through at-home acupressure between visits, (2) photo-based tongue assessment tools (patient takes a tongue photo, practitioner evaluates TCM patterns), (3) herbal formula review and adjustment workflow, and (4) treatment plan milestone tracking that distinguishes between "needs in-person needling" and "can be handled remotely." This converts every-other-visit to telehealth, halving the patient's travel burden.

## Target Customer
Rural acupuncturists losing patients to travel fatigue who could retain 30-40% more patients through hybrid in-person/telehealth treatment plans.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces patient drop-off by 25-35%, increases treatment plan completion rates, and adds $15,000-30,000/year in telehealth visit revenue per practitioner.
