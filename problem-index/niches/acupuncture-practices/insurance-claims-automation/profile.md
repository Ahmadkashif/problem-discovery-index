# Insurance Eligibility & Claims Processing

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $900M (billing volume)
**Share of Parent Industry:** 11% (of total revenue processed through insurance)
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Claims are submitted electronically but eligibility verification and denial management remain largely manual.
**Target Buyer:** Billing manager, clinic owner, or outsourced billing service
**Automation Potential:** High — Eligibility checks, claim scrubbing, and denial follow-up are rule-based workflows with high repetition rates.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Acupuncture insurance billing is disproportionately manual compared to other healthcare specialties. The combination of wildly inconsistent payer rules, state-by-state mandate variations, and acupuncture-specific code limitations (narrow CPT code range, visit caps, unusual modifier requirements) means that generic claim scrubbing tools miss 20-30% of acupuncture-specific denial triggers. Every eligibility check requires verifying not just "does this patient have coverage" but "does this plan cover acupuncture, for this diagnosis, with what visit limit, in this state." This verification takes 10-20 minutes per patient per payer.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Clearinghouses like Availity, Trizetto, and Office Ally handle electronic claim submission. Eligibility verification tools like Pverify and Availity provide basic coverage checks. None provide acupuncture-specific eligibility detail: visit limits, covered CPT codes, diagnosis requirements, or state mandate applicability. The biller must call the payer directly for this information — a 15-30 minute phone call per patient.
