# Solo Community Acupuncture Practitioners

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $650M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most use paper charts, Google Calendar, and Venmo/Square for payments.
**Target Buyer:** Solo practitioner (owner-operator)
**Automation Potential:** High — Administrative tasks consume 30-40% of a solo practitioner's week and follow repeatable patterns.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Community acupuncture operates on a sliding-scale, high-volume model — treating 6-10 patients per hour in a shared room at $15-40 per session instead of the private-room model at $80-150. This makes it financially viable for lower-income patients but leaves practitioners with almost no administrative margin. A solo community acupuncturist earning $60,000-80,000/year cannot justify $300/month EHR software designed for private-room practices billing insurance. Most run their entire practice on Google Calendar, a paper sign-in sheet, and a Square reader. Clinical documentation is minimal or non-existent.

## Current Tools & Gaps
POCA (People's Organization of Community Acupuncture) provides some community resources, but no shared technology platform. Some practitioners use Acuity Scheduling or Square Appointments for booking. Very few use any EHR at all. The gap is total: no affordable, community-acupuncture-specific system exists that handles sliding-scale billing, group room scheduling, basic SOAP documentation, and patient tracking in a single tool under $50/month.
