# Traditional Chinese Medicine Herbalists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $480M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Herbal formulation knowledge lives in textbooks, notebooks, and practitioner memory; almost no digital tools exist for formula management.
**Target Buyer:** TCM practitioner who compounds or prescribes custom herbal formulas
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Formula compounding follows pattern-based rules but requires deep TCM diagnostic knowledge.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
TCM herbalists don't just do acupuncture — they diagnose using traditional pattern differentiation (tongue, pulse, symptom constellations) and prescribe custom herbal formulas from a pharmacopoeia of 300-400 commonly used herbs. Each formula is modified per patient based on their constitutional pattern, concurrent medications, and seasonal factors. This knowledge is almost entirely analog: practitioners reference Bensky's Materia Medica, handwritten formula notebooks passed down from mentors, and personal clinical experience. There is no digital formula management system that understands TCM diagnostic logic.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Some practitioners use spreadsheets to track herb inventory and pricing. A few Chinese-language software products exist (e.g., TCM-SMART) but are poorly localized for English-speaking practitioners. No Western EHR integrates herbal formula management. The gap spans formula creation, herb-drug interaction checking, inventory tracking, and patient formula history.
