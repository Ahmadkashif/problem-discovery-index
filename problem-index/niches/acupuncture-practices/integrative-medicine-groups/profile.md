## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Integrative medicine groups combine acupuncture with chiropractic, naturopathy, massage therapy, and sometimes conventional primary care under one roof. The challenge isn't any single modality — it's coordinating treatment plans across practitioners who use different clinical frameworks. An acupuncturist thinking in terms of qi stagnation and a chiropractor thinking in terms of spinal subluxation may be treating the same patient's lower back pain with no shared clinical language in their notes. This creates care fragmentation that generic multi-provider EHRs don't address.

## Current Tools & Gaps
These practices typically use multi-provider platforms like ChiroTouch, Jane App, or Practice Fusion. While these support multiple practitioner types, they don't provide cross-modality treatment plan views, shared outcome tracking, or integrated progress notes that translate between clinical frameworks. Referral coordination between in-house providers is often done verbally or via sticky notes.
