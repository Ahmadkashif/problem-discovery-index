# EHR Documentation Templates for VA Acupuncture Standards

**Niche:** [[niches/acupuncture-practices/veterans-military/profile|Veterans & Military Acupuncture Providers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Acupuncture EHRs provide SOAP note templates for commercial insurance but lack VA-specific documentation standards for Battlefield Acupuncture protocols and Community Care outcome reporting.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
VA requires specific documentation elements that commercial payers don't: standardized pain outcome measures (DVPRS — Defense and Veterans Pain Rating Scale), functional status assessments, Battlefield Acupuncture protocol adherence notes, and treatment progress reporting tied to VA treatment plan goals. Acupuncture EHRs like Jane App and Unified Practice have no VA-specific templates, forcing practitioners to manually add required elements to every note — or risk audit findings and payment clawbacks.

## What Already Exists
Jane App, Unified Practice, and DrChrono all offer customizable SOAP note templates for acupuncture. VA-specific EHRs (CPRS/Cerner) are used by VA staff but not available to community providers. Some practitioners have created their own Word document templates, but these don't integrate with EHR workflows and require double-entry.

## The Customization Gap
The EHR's SOAP note template needs VA-specific fields: DVPRS pain scores (pre/post treatment), BFA protocol checkboxes (which ear points, which phase of protocol), functional outcome measures aligned with VA treatment plan goals, and auto-generated progress summaries formatted for VA review. These templates should auto-populate from the practitioner's clinical notes and output both the clinical chart note and a VA-compliant progress report simultaneously.

## Target Customer
VA-contracted acupuncturists who currently spend 10-15 extra minutes per VA patient visit on documentation compliance.

## Impact If Solved
Saves 5-10 minutes per VA visit (1-3 hours/day for a practitioner seeing 10-15 VA patients daily), eliminates documentation-related audit risks, and produces standardized outcome data that strengthens re-authorization requests.
