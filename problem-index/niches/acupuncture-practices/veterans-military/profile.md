# Veterans & Military Acupuncture Providers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/acupuncture-practices|Acupuncture Practices]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $410M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — VA-contracted providers use VA systems for some documentation but manage their own practice operations separately.
**Target Buyer:** Acupuncturist contracted with VA Community Care or DoD programs
**Automation Potential:** Medium — VA billing and documentation compliance are highly rule-based but the rules are uniquely complex.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The VA has dramatically expanded acupuncture coverage since 2017 through Community Care referrals, Battlefield Acupuncture (BFA) protocols, and the MISSION Act. This created a growing market of private acupuncturists treating veterans under VA contracts. However, VA billing follows completely different rules than commercial insurance — different authorization workflows, different documentation requirements, different payment timelines (often 60-90 days), and different outcome reporting expectations. Most acupuncturists who accept VA patients lose money on their first year of VA billing because the administrative overhead is so different from commercial practice.

## Current Tools & Gaps
VA-contracted providers must use VA's referral authorization system for Community Care, then document and bill through their own practice management system. No EHR designed for acupuncture understands VA-specific requirements: the authorization-before-treatment workflow, VA-specific documentation standards, VA fee schedule lookups, or the triennial recredentialing process. Providers cobble together VA portal access, their own EHR, and manual tracking spreadsheets.
