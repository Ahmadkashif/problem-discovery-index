# End-to-End PA Automation Agent with Clinical Data Collection and Alternative Suggestion

**Niche:** [[niches/pharmacy-independents/prior-authorization-automation/profile|Prior Authorization Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pharmacy-independents|Independent Pharmacies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** An AI agent that handles the entire prior authorization workflow — detecting PA requirements at adjudication, auto-requesting clinical data from prescribers, pre-filling PA forms, suggesting formulary alternatives, submitting to PBMs, and tracking outcomes — reducing the 15-30 minute manual process to pharmacist verification and approval.

## The Problem
Prior authorization consumes 1.5-7.5 hours of daily staff time at a busy independent pharmacy, generates zero revenue, and directly causes 30% of affected prescriptions to be abandoned by patients. The bottleneck is not PA submission (CoverMyMeds handles that) but the pre-submission work: determining why PA is required, obtaining clinical justification from the prescriber, and identifying whether a formulary-preferred alternative could bypass PA entirely. The pharmacy technician calls the prescriber's office, waits on hold, explains what clinical information is needed, and often must call back multiple times because the office doesn't have the information ready. Meanwhile, the patient waits — sometimes days — for a medication they need, and 30% give up entirely.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
End-to-end PA automation requires solving three distinct problems simultaneously: (1) clinical data collection — the PA form requires diagnosis codes, prior medication trials, lab results, and clinical notes that reside in the prescriber's EHR, not the pharmacy's PMS. Accessing this data requires either direct EHR integration (fragmented across Epic, Cerner, Athena, DrChrono, and dozens of others) or an automated communication workflow with the prescriber's office, (2) formulary alternative identification — determining which medications on the patient's PBM formulary are clinically appropriate substitutes requires clinical knowledge (is this switch therapeutically equivalent?) combined with real-time formulary data (which drugs are preferred on this specific plan?), and (3) PA form pre-fill — each PBM's PA form has different questions, and the clinical information must be mapped to the correct form fields. CoverMyMeds solves (3) partially but hasn't tackled (1) or (2) because they require clinical AI capabilities and EHR integration that are outside their core platform.

## What to Build
An AI-powered PA automation agent that: (1) detects PA requirements at the moment of claim rejection and identifies the specific PA trigger (step therapy, formulary exclusion, quantity limit, age restriction), (2) queries the PBM's formulary to identify covered alternatives that would not require PA, presenting the pharmacist with clinically appropriate substitution options ranked by patient copay and therapeutic equivalence, (3) if PA is needed, generates a structured clinical data request to the prescriber — sent electronically via the prescriber's EHR patient portal, e-prescribing network, or secure fax — specifying exactly what information is needed in a format the prescriber's staff can complete in 2 minutes, (4) receives the clinical response, uses a language model to extract relevant data points, and pre-fills the PBM's PA form, (5) presents the completed PA to the pharmacist for review and one-click submission, and (6) tracks PA status with automated escalation (resubmit if no response in 24 hours, alert pharmacist if denied). The pharmacist's role shifts from doing the PA work to reviewing and approving agent-completed PAs — a 2-3 minute task instead of 15-30 minutes.

## Target Customer
Independent pharmacies handling 5+ PAs per day (most pharmacies filling 150+ Rx/day), where PA processing consumes 1.5-7.5 hours of staff time daily and causes measurable patient abandonment.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-PA staff time from 15-30 minutes to 2-3 minutes, saving 1-6 hours of daily labor per pharmacy. Reduces patient abandonment on PA-required prescriptions from 30% to under 10% by accelerating turnaround from 48-72 hours to under 12 hours. Identifies formulary alternatives that bypass PA entirely for an estimated 20-35% of PA-triggering prescriptions, eliminating the PA workflow altogether for those claims.
