# DIR Fee Exposure Prediction and Performance Metric Optimization Engine

**Niche:** [[niches/pharmacy-independents/dir-fee-pbm-optimization/profile|DIR Fee & PBM Optimization]]
**Industry:** [[industries/pharmacy-independents|Independent Pharmacies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A prediction engine that estimates DIR fee liability per prescription at point of sale and identifies which patient adherence interventions would deliver the highest dollar reduction in total DIR exposure — turning an opaque retroactive clawback into a manageable, forecastable cost.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #optimization-fundamentals #revenue-impact #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
DIR fees are calculated using formulas that combine pharmacy performance on quality metrics (medication adherence measured by PDC — proportion of days covered, generic dispensing rate, medication therapy management completion) with PBM-specific thresholds and weightings that are not fully disclosed. A pharmacy's DIR fee rate can range from 2% to 15% of total reimbursement depending on performance tier. Experienced pharmacy owners develop an intuition for DIR exposure — they know which PBM contracts are most punitive, which patient populations drag down adherence scores, and which months are critical for metric improvement — but this knowledge takes 3-5 years of painful financial surprises to develop. New pharmacy owners or locum pharmacists cannot replicate this judgment. The financial stakes are massive: a pharmacy filling 250 Rx/day with an average reimbursement of $12 generates ~$1.1M in annual reimbursement. A 3% DIR fee rate vs. a 9% DIR fee rate is the difference between $33,000 and $99,000 in annual clawbacks — often the difference between profitability and closure.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
DIR fee prediction requires data from multiple sources that don't naturally connect: (1) per-claim adjudication data from the PMS, (2) current and historical performance metric standings from PBM portals, (3) historical DIR assessment amounts from PSAO or PBM remittance files, (4) PBM contract terms from the PSAO (which may not share full contract details with member pharmacies), and (5) patient-level adherence data to model the impact of targeted interventions. The PBM's exact calculation methodology is proprietary, meaning the model must be trained on observed input-output pairs (performance metrics in → DIR fee assessment out) rather than a known formula. Each PBM has different thresholds and weightings, requiring per-PBM model training. The data volume per pharmacy is limited (4-8 DIR assessment periods per year), requiring pooling across pharmacies for model training — which raises data sharing concerns among independent owners.

## What to Build
A SaaS platform that: (1) ingests adjudication data from the PMS, performance metric data from PBM portals, and historical DIR assessment files, (2) trains per-PBM gradient-boosted models predicting DIR fee rate based on current and projected performance metrics, (3) estimates per-prescription DIR fee exposure at point of sale, flagging claims likely to be underwater after DIR, (4) identifies the highest-ROI adherence interventions — which patients, on which medications, for which PBM, would improve PDC enough to cross a DIR performance tier threshold — and quantifies the dollar impact, (5) projects DIR fee liability for the current assessment period based on year-to-date dispensing and metric trajectory. The system captures the tacit DIR knowledge of experienced pharmacy owners — which PBM contracts are most punitive, which patient behaviors drive metric degradation, which interventions actually move the needle — and encodes it in a model accessible to any pharmacy from day one. A pharmacy cooperative model for training data (anonymized metrics and outcomes pooled across participating pharmacies) solves the per-pharmacy data scarcity problem.

## Target Customer
Independent pharmacy owners with $50,000+ in annual DIR fee exposure who currently have no forward-looking visibility into their DIR liability and make dispensing decisions without understanding the net-of-DIR margin on each prescription.

## Impact If Built
Gives pharmacies predictive visibility into DIR fee exposure, enabling targeted interventions that reduce DIR fees by 15-30% ($8,000-30,000/year per pharmacy). Identifies underwater prescriptions at point of sale, enabling proactive decisions about therapeutic alternatives or patient adherence outreach. Captures the DIR management expertise that experienced owners develop over years and makes it available immediately to new pharmacy operators.
