## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Compounding pharmacies prepare custom-formulated medications — dermatology creams, hormone replacement therapy, pain management compounds, pediatric suspensions, veterinary compounds — that cannot be purchased commercially. The workflow is fundamentally different from retail dispensing: the pharmacist develops or follows a master formulation record, sources bulk pharmaceutical ingredients from specialty suppliers, compounds the preparation in a controlled environment, assigns beyond-use dating based on stability data, and in many cases performs potency testing. Regulatory burden is severe: USP 795 governs non-sterile compounding, USP 797 governs sterile compounding (IV preparations, eye drops), and USP 800 governs hazardous drug handling. Compliance requires documented cleaning logs, environmental monitoring data, personnel competency assessments, and ingredient certificates of analysis — all of which most pharmacies maintain on paper. The margin profile is different too: compounded preparations carry 40-60% gross margins compared to 2-5% on retail generics, but the labor intensity per preparation is 5-10x higher.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Standard PMS systems (PioneerRx, QS/1) handle the dispensing and billing side but have minimal compounding-specific functionality. PK Software and Compounding Today offer formulation databases but don't integrate with the PMS for billing or inventory. PCCA (Professional Compounding Centers of America) provides formulation resources and bulk ingredient sourcing but their software tools are reference materials, not workflow management. The gap is an end-to-end compounding workflow platform: master formulation records linked to ingredient lots linked to batch records linked to quality testing results linked to beyond-use dating — all generating USP-compliant documentation automatically. Currently, a pharmacist compounds a preparation, then spends 10-15 minutes completing paper batch records, manually cross-referencing ingredient lot numbers, and filing documentation.
