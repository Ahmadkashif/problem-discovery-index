# Live Podcast Tour Operations Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/live-event-podcast-producers/profile|Live Event Podcast Producers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the full lifecycle of a podcast tour — venue booking, technical rider management, ticket sales coordination, travel logistics, night-of production, and post-event content processing — in a single workflow.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #data-integration #ai-platform

## The Problem
A podcast network touring a popular show through 15 cities must manage: venue research and booking (capacity, sound system quality, recording-friendly acoustics), contract negotiation (revenue split vs. flat rental), technical rider communication (microphone requirements, recording feeds, video capture), ticket sales across multiple platforms (venue box office, Eventbrite, direct sales), travel and accommodation for 2–5 talent, merchandise inventory for each city, night-of production coordination (sound check, recording engineer, audience management), and post-event content processing (editing 15 live recordings into publishable episodes). The tour producer manages all of this in a combination of email, shared Google Docs, and a master spreadsheet with 300+ rows. When the same show tours again next year, the producer rebuilds everything from scratch because last year's information is scattered across inboxes.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Tour management software exists for music (Master Tour, Bandsintown Backstage) but models the music touring workflow: one performance per night, standardized technical riders, merch at the door. Podcast touring is different: the "performance" is a conversation that must be recorded at broadcast quality, audience participation must be mic'd and managed, the venue's sound system may not meet recording standards (requiring portable equipment), and the output is both a live experience and a distributed media product. The podcast touring market is small (200–500 shows tour nationally per year) and hasn't attracted the tool investment that music touring has.

## What to Build
A tour management platform tailored for podcast production: (1) venue database with podcast-relevant attributes (acoustics rating, recording feed availability, audience mic infrastructure, previous podcast shows hosted), (2) technical rider templates for podcast recording (microphone counts, recording feeds, monitor requirements, video camera positions), (3) tour schedule with multi-city timeline, travel bookings, and load-in/sound check windows, (4) ticket sales aggregation across venues and third-party platforms, (5) night-of-show checklist with role assignments (host, recording engineer, audience manager, merch), (6) post-show content pipeline that tracks each recording from raw capture through editing to publication, and (7) year-over-year tour data retention for repeat shows.

## Target Customer
Event producers and operations directors at podcast networks touring 2+ shows per year across 5+ cities.

## Impact If Built
Reduces tour planning time from 80 hours to 25 hours per tour. Eliminates the 15% of shows with audio quality issues caused by venue-specific problems that could have been identified in advance. Enables the producer to manage 3–4 concurrent tours instead of 1–2, doubling the network's live event revenue without proportional staff increase.
