# Live Event Podcast Producers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $250M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Audio production is professional, but venue logistics, ticket sales, and live-to-recorded production workflows are managed with spreadsheets and email.
**Target Buyer:** Event Producer / Operations Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Venue booking, ticket sales coordination, live recording logistics, and post-event content processing are repetitive workflows with clear automation pathways.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Live event podcast producers create in-person experiences for podcast audiences — live tapings, comedy shows, panel discussions, and immersive experiences at venues ranging from 100-seat comedy clubs to 2,000-seat theaters. The business model combines ticket revenue ($20–$75 per ticket), sponsorship, merchandise, and the recorded episode (which generates additional advertising revenue when published). The operational challenge is managing two parallel production streams: the in-person event (venue, tickets, lighting, sound, hospitality) and the audio/video recording (capture quality, audience mic management, editing for distribution). Most podcast networks treat live events as marketing (low priority) rather than as a revenue line (high potential), leaving the operational infrastructure underdeveloped.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Eventbrite handles ticketing. Venues provide basic sound. Recording uses portable rigs (Zoom recorders, mobile studios). But the unique challenges of live podcast production — managing audience participation mics, capturing clean audio in non-studio environments, editing a 90-minute live show into a 45-minute podcast episode, coordinating multi-city tours — have no purpose-built tools. The producer manages venue contracts, technical riders, travel logistics, and post-production timelines in email and shared documents.
