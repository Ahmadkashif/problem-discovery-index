# Event Ticketing with Podcast Audience Integration

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/live-event-podcast-producers/profile|Live Event Podcast Producers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Eventbrite sells tickets, but it doesn't connect ticket purchasers to the podcast's subscriber list — leaving the producer unable to promote the recorded episode to attendees or invite past attendees to future shows.
**Tags:** #data-integration #recommendation #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A live podcast show sells 500 tickets. The attendees are the show's most engaged fans — they paid $40 and traveled to see a show they listen to for free. After the event, the producer has no way to: (1) email attendees when the recorded episode is published, (2) send early access to the live episode recording, (3) promote future events in the same city to people who attended last time, or (4) cross-promote other shows in the network to proven live-event buyers. Eventbrite captures email addresses, but they sit in Eventbrite's database — the producer must export a CSV and manually import it to their email platform, segmented by city and show, for every event. Most producers don't bother, losing their most valuable audience segment.

## What Already Exists
Eventbrite, Dice, and Universe handle event ticketing with email capture. Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign handle email marketing with segmentation. But the integration between ticketing and podcast audience management is manual: export from Eventbrite, format the CSV, import to the email platform, create segments by city and show, build automation sequences. Zapier can connect the two, but configuring Zapier for multi-show, multi-city tour events requires custom workflows per event. No ticketing platform understands that a ticket purchaser is also a podcast listener with a subscriber profile.

## The Customization Gap
A podcast-integrated ticketing system needs to: (1) automatically sync ticket purchasers to the podcast's email platform with tags for city, show, and event date, (2) trigger a post-event email sequence (thank you → recorded episode link → next show in this city → cross-show promotion), (3) create a "live event attendee" segment that receives exclusive content and early access to future tickets, (4) provide a pre-event email to ticket holders with venue logistics, parking, and what to expect, (5) integrate with the podcast hosting platform so the attendee list can be used to measure podcast-to-ticket conversion (did podcast listeners convert to ticket buyers from the on-air promotion?), and (6) maintain a unified attendee database across all events for multi-tour retargeting.

## Target Customer
Event producers and marketing directors at podcast networks running 10+ live events per year who want to maximize the lifetime value of live event attendees.

## Impact If Solved
Post-event email sequences increase recorded episode downloads for live episodes by 30–50% (attendees share the episode they were part of). Retargeting past attendees achieves 2–3x higher ticket conversion rates than cold promotion, filling 20–30% of seats for return shows from the existing database. Cross-show promotion to live attendees converts 5–10% into listeners of other network shows — worth $5–$15 per new subscriber in lifetime ad revenue.
