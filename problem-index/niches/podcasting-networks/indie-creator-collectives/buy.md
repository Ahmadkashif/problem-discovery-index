# Cross-Promotion Scheduler with Attribution Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/indie-creator-collectives/profile|Indie Creator Collectives]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Podcast promotion tools like Overcast Ads and Podbean's cross-promotion feature connect shows for ad swaps, but they don't track whether the promotion actually drove subscribers — leaving collectives guessing about the value of their most important growth tactic.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #causal-inference #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Cross-promotion — mentioning or running a promo spot for another collective member's show — is the primary growth mechanism for indie podcast collectives. But it's managed informally: "Hey, can you mention my show this week?" with no coordination to ensure fair reciprocity, no tracking of whether the mention drove actual subscribers, and no optimization of which shows promote which (a comedy show's audience may not convert to a political show, but might convert to a storytelling show). Over time, members with larger audiences feel they're giving more than they get, while members with smaller audiences feel excluded from promotion opportunities.

## What Already Exists
Overcast Ads allows podcast-to-podcast advertising within the Overcast app. Podbean has a cross-promotion feature for shows on its platform. Podcast Addict has cross-promotion tools. Chartable's SmartPromos provides attribution tracking for podcast promo swaps. But these tools are platform-specific (only work within one app or hosting platform) and don't coordinate collective-wide promotion strategy. Chartable's SmartPromos provides attribution but requires manual setup per campaign and costs $100+/month per show — prohibitive for a 15-show collective.

## The Customization Gap
A collective cross-promotion tool needs to: (1) maintain a promotion calendar showing who is promoting whom in which episodes, ensuring equitable reciprocity over time, (2) auto-generate promotion spots (pre-recorded trailer swaps or host-read script suggestions) for each show pair, (3) track attribution — when Show A promotes Show B, how many new subscribers does Show B gain from Show A's audience? — using prefix URL tracking or unique promo codes, (4) recommend optimal promotion pairings based on audience overlap analysis (promote shows whose audiences are adjacent but not overlapping), and (5) provide a reciprocity dashboard showing each member's promotional balance (hours of promotion given vs. received, adjusted for audience size). The key insight: attribution data transforms cross-promotion from a favor-based system into a value-based system.

## Target Customer
Collective organizers and member creators in podcast collectives with 5+ shows seeking structured, data-driven cross-promotion.

## Impact If Solved
Data-driven promotion pairing increases subscriber conversion rates from cross-promotion by 40–60% (promoting to adjacent audiences vs. random). Equitable scheduling prevents the burnout and resentment that cause 30% of collectives to lose members annually. Enables smaller shows in the collective to demonstrate their promotional value, maintaining their place in the group.
