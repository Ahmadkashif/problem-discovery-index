# Cooperative Podcast Network Management Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/indie-creator-collectives/profile|Indie Creator Collectives]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform provides the shared infrastructure a podcast collective needs — unified analytics, collective-level ad inventory, cross-promotion coordination, and equitable revenue distribution — without requiring creators to give up show ownership.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #ai-platform #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A podcast collective of 12 creators has a combined audience of 200,000 monthly listeners — enough to attract meaningful sponsorship deals ($15K–$50K per quarter). But operationalizing this requires: aggregating download data from 8 different hosting platforms, calculating each show's share of the collective audience, coordinating ad placement across 12 independent production schedules, collecting audio ad spots from each creator, tracking delivery, and splitting revenue. The collective organizer does all of this in their spare time using spreadsheets, email, and goodwill. When disagreements arise about revenue splits or promotional obligations, there's no data to arbitrate — just feelings and accusations. Collectives frequently dissolve over these disputes.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Podcast network platforms (Megaphone, Art19) assume centralized ownership and control — one organization manages all shows. They don't model the cooperative structure where each creator is an independent entity with their own hosting, their own audience, and their own editorial autonomy. Building for collectives means building for 200–500 organizations (the estimated number of podcast collectives with 5+ members), each with 5–20 member shows — a fragmented market with tiny per-customer revenue. And the cooperative governance model (equal voice, transparent decisions, equitable splits) requires features that commercial platforms have no reason to build.

## What to Build
A cooperative management platform where: (1) each member connects their hosting platform via API, contributing their analytics to a collective dashboard without changing their hosting provider, (2) the dashboard shows collective-level audience (total, demographics, overlap) and per-member contribution, (3) a sponsorship management module lists collective-level ad inventory (total available impressions across all shows), manages sponsor campaigns (which shows run which ads in which episodes), and tracks delivery, (4) revenue distribution calculates each member's share based on configurable rules (equal split, audience-weighted, impression-delivered, or hybrid), (5) cross-promotion scheduling coordinates who promotes whom, when, with tracking of promotional impact on subscriber growth, and (6) governance tools (voting, policy documentation, agreement templates) maintain the cooperative structure. Price: $10–$20 per member show per month.

## Target Customer
Collective organizers managing 5–20 member podcasts who want to professionalize their operations without incorporating as a traditional network.

## Impact If Built
Enables collectives to capture $20K–$100K in annual sponsorship revenue that currently goes to formal networks because sponsors can't buy across an uncoordinated collective. Reduces organizer overhead from 15–20 hours/week to 3–5 hours/week. Prevents collective dissolution by providing transparent data for revenue splits and promotional obligations.
