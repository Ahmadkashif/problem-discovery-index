# Hyperlocal Podcast Ad Marketplace for Community Businesses

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/local-news-podcast-studios/profile|Local News Podcast Studios]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform connects local businesses (restaurants, dentists, car dealers) with local news podcasters for ad placement — the way Google Ads connects businesses with local search intent.
**Tags:** #recommendation #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #ai-platform

## The Problem
A local news podcaster in a metro area of 200,000 people has 5,000–15,000 regular listeners — a meaningful audience for local businesses but invisible to national ad networks (which require 10,000+ downloads per episode minimum). The podcaster must sell ads directly: cold-calling local businesses, explaining what a podcast is, proposing pricing they've invented, recording the ad themselves, and chasing payment via invoice. Most local podcasters attempt this, sell 1–3 sponsors, and give up because the sales effort exceeds the revenue. Meanwhile, local businesses are spending $5K–$50K per year on Facebook ads, Google ads, and remnant radio — they're willing to spend on audio advertising but have no way to find or evaluate local podcasts.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
National podcast ad networks (Spotify Ad Network, iHeart) serve national advertisers buying at scale — they can't economically service a $500/month local restaurant campaign. Local media ad platforms (Borrell, local media agencies) focus on traditional channels (radio, newspaper, digital display). The transaction size for a local podcast ad is tiny ($200–$1,000 per month), making manual sales uneconomical for both the podcaster and any intermediary. The business model only works with a marketplace approach: aggregate hundreds of local podcasters and thousands of local businesses, automate the matching and transaction, and take a percentage.

## What to Build
A two-sided marketplace where: (1) local podcasters list their shows with audience demographics, geographic concentration, and episode schedule, (2) local businesses browse podcasts serving their area, select a show, choose a package (30-second pre-roll, 60-second mid-roll, sponsored segment), and upload a script or request host-read, (3) the platform handles dynamic ad insertion, payment processing, and basic attribution (promo code tracking), (4) pricing is standardized by market size and show performance so neither party negotiates from scratch, and (5) a recommendation engine matches businesses with podcasts based on audience demographics and content alignment. The podcaster focuses on content; the platform handles sales.

## Target Customer
Local news and community podcasters with 2,000–25,000 downloads per episode seeking advertising revenue, and local businesses with $500–$5,000/month ad budgets.

## Impact If Built
Enables local podcasters to generate $1,000–$5,000/month in ad revenue without direct sales effort — making local news podcasting financially sustainable. Gives local businesses access to a new engaged audience at CPMs ($15–$25) competitive with local radio ($10–$20) and far below Facebook ads ($30–$50 effective CPM for local targeting).
