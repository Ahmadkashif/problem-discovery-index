# Local News Podcast Studios

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $200M
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Audio production tools are adequate, but distribution, local ad sales, and audience measurement for hyperlocal content lag far behind national shows.
**Target Buyer:** Station Manager / News Director / Independent Journalist
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Local ad sales, event calendar integration, and community engagement tracking are automatable; editorial judgment is inherently human.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Local news podcast studios produce content about a specific city, county, or metro area — city council coverage, local crime reporting, neighborhood development stories, and community interest features. They range from spin-offs of legacy media (newspaper or radio station podcasts) to independent journalist-entrepreneurs covering beats that traditional media has abandoned. Their unique challenge: the audience is geographically constrained (50,000–500,000 potential listeners), making national ad networks irrelevant (a listener in Portland doesn't need a Portland restaurant ad, but a listener everywhere else doesn't either). Monetization requires local ad sales — approaching the same businesses that buy newspaper ads and radio spots — with the same relationship-driven sales process but without the established sales infrastructure.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Production uses standard tools (Audacity, GarageBand, Descript). Hosting platforms (Buzzsprout, Anchor/Spotify for Podcasters) handle distribution. But local podcast-specific needs are unmet: no tool helps a local news podcaster sell advertising to local businesses at scale, no platform provides hyperlocal audience measurement (how many listeners are in the coverage area vs. elsewhere), and no system connects local event calendars to content planning. The fundamental gap: local podcasting has the content model of a newspaper but the business infrastructure of a hobby.
