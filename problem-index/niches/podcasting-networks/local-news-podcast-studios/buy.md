# Local Audience Measurement and Geographic Analytics

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/local-news-podcast-studios/profile|Local News Podcast Studios]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Chartable and Podtrac measure total downloads, but a local news podcaster needs to know what percentage of their 8,000 listeners are actually in the coverage area — the only metric that matters to local advertisers.
**Tags:** #tabular-ml #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A local news podcaster covering Austin, TX has 8,000 downloads per episode. A local car dealer asks "how many of those listeners are in Austin?" The podcaster has no idea. Podcast analytics platforms report total downloads by country and sometimes by state, but not by metro area. Apple Podcasts provides city-level data but only for Apple listeners (40–50% of the audience). Spotify provides city-level data for Spotify listeners (20–30%). The remaining 20–30% (other apps, web players) have no geographic attribution. The podcaster either guesses ("probably 80% are local") or loses the advertiser because they can't prove local reach.

## What Already Exists
Chartable (now Spotify), Podtrac, and OP3 measure aggregate downloads with basic geographic breakdowns (country, state). Apple Podcasts Connect and Spotify for Podcasters provide platform-specific city-level data. Prefix URL services add attribution tracking. But none of these tools: (1) combine data from multiple platforms into a unified geographic view, (2) provide metro-area-level audience measurement that matches how local advertisers think about markets (DMA regions), or (3) estimate total local audience by combining platform-specific data with statistical modeling for the unknown segment.

## The Customization Gap
Local audience measurement needs to: (1) aggregate geographic data from Apple Podcasts, Spotify, and hosting-platform IP-based geolocation into a single dashboard, (2) map listeners to DMA (Designated Market Area) regions — the standard geographic unit used by local advertisers, (3) use statistical modeling to estimate the geographic distribution of listeners on platforms that don't provide city-level data, (4) present a "local listener count" metric that the podcaster can confidently share with advertisers, and (5) trend local vs. non-local audience over time to show growth. The key insight: local podcasters don't need better total analytics — they need geographic analytics that prove local relevance.

## Target Customer
Local news and community podcasters seeking local advertising revenue who need to prove geographic audience concentration to potential sponsors.

## Impact If Solved
Enables local podcasters to confidently pitch local advertisers with verified geographic audience data — converting 30–50% more sales conversations into deals. Increases average local ad deal size by 20–40% when the podcaster can prove 60–80% local audience concentration (vs. the unverifiable "I think most listeners are local").
