# Podcast Production Project Management for Agency Workflow

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/branded-podcast-agencies/profile|Branded Podcast Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Asana and Monday.com handle generic project management but don't model the podcast production pipeline — pre-production (research, scripting, booking), production (recording, editing, mixing), and post-production (client review, revisions, distribution) — with audio-specific deliverables and approval gates.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
A branded podcast agency producing 5 client shows simultaneously manages 20–30 episodes in various production stages. Each episode follows a pipeline: topic research → scripting → client script approval → guest booking → recording → rough edit → sound design/mixing → client audio review → revisions → final master → metadata preparation → distribution → promotion. That's 13 stages per episode, each with different team members (researcher, writer, producer, editor, mixer, account manager), different deliverables (document, audio file, metadata sheet), and different client approval gates. Tracking this in Asana requires creating custom templates, configuring audio file attachments, and building client portal views — a 40-hour setup per show that the agency reinvents for every new client.

## What Already Exists
Asana, Monday.com, and ClickUp handle project management with custom workflows, templates, and client-facing views. Frame.io handles video review with timestamped annotations. But no tool combines task management with audio review — the critical workflow where a client listens to a rough edit and provides timestamped feedback. The agency emails an audio file, the client writes feedback in a separate document referencing timestamps, the producer translates feedback into editing tasks — a 3-step process that introduces errors and delays.

## The Customization Gap
A podcast production PM tool needs to: (1) pre-built pipeline templates for podcast production (interview show, narrative show, hybrid) with standard stage durations, (2) audio review built into the platform — clients listen and leave timestamped comments directly on the audio waveform, (3) automatic task creation from client feedback (client comment at 14:32 → editing task "remove section at 14:32"), (4) client portal showing their show's status without exposing internal production details, (5) resource allocation across shows (which editor has capacity this week?), and (6) profitability tracking per client per episode (actual hours vs. budgeted hours). The audio review feature is the differentiator — it's what makes generic PM tools inadequate for podcast agencies.

## Target Customer
Operations managers and executive producers at branded podcast agencies managing 3+ simultaneous client shows.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces production pipeline overhead by 25–30% through standardized templates and integrated audio review. Eliminates the 2–3 day delay per revision cycle caused by email-based audio feedback (client feedback goes directly into the editing workflow). Improves profitability tracking, preventing the 10–15% of client relationships that become unprofitable due to unbilled revision cycles.
