**Target Buyer:** Network Head / Content Director / Executive Producer
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Research aggregation, fact verification, and audience engagement analysis have clear automation targets; editorial judgment remains human.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
True crime is the single largest podcast genre by listenership, accounting for approximately 20% of all podcast consumption. True crime networks (Audiochuck, Exactly Right, Parcast/Spotify) operate differently from other podcast networks because their content requires investigative research — court records, police reports, witness interviews, geographic data, and timeline reconstruction. Production cycles are longer (weeks per episode vs. days for interview shows), legal review is mandatory (defamation risk), and source management is critical (protecting confidential sources, tracking document provenance). The audience is intensely engaged, creating both opportunity (high ad CPMs, $25–$35) and risk (harassment of subjects, misinformation amplification).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Production uses standard tools: Pro Tools/Logic for audio, Descript for editing, Riverside/SquadCast for remote recording. Hosting platforms (Megaphone, Simplecast) handle distribution. But the research workflow — the most time-intensive part of true crime production — has no purpose-built tools. Producers use Google Docs for case files, personal folders for documents, and memory for cross-episode fact consistency. No tool helps a producer answer "did we already cover this aspect of the case in Episode 12?" or "does this new source contradict what we said in Season 2?"
