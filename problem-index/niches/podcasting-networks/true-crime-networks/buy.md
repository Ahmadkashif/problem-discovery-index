# Legal Review Workflow for True Crime Content

**Niche:** [[niches/podcasting-networks/true-crime-networks/profile|True Crime Networks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Contract management tools like Ironclad handle legal workflows, but no tool manages the pre-publication legal review specific to true crime — flagging defamation risk, identifying living subjects who need notification, and tracking consent documentation.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #compliance #automation

## The Problem
True crime podcasts routinely name suspects, victims, witnesses, and law enforcement officers — any of whom may sue for defamation, invasion of privacy, or intentional infliction of emotional distress. Every episode script requires legal review before recording. The network's media attorney reads the script, flags risky statements (unproven allegations, characterizations of living persons, details about minors), and either clears the language or requests revisions. This review creates a 3–7 day bottleneck in the production schedule. When a network produces 5–10 shows simultaneously, the attorney is reviewing 20+ scripts per month, each requiring 2–4 hours of careful reading.

## What Already Exists
Legal review tools (Ironclad, Juro) manage contract workflows. Media law firms use document review platforms (Relativity, Everlaw) for litigation. But none focus on pre-publication content review for audio media. Newspaper and magazine fact-checking tools exist but are designed for text articles with inline citations, not for audio scripts that will be performed and may deviate from the written page. No tool helps a media attorney quickly identify the specific legal risks in a true crime script: living persons named, unproven allegations, sealed court records referenced, minor victims identified, or law enforcement criticism that might trigger qualified immunity issues.

## The Customization Gap
A true crime legal review tool needs to: (1) parse scripts to auto-flag living persons mentioned by name (cross-referenced against a "known entities" database for the show), (2) highlight statements about living persons that constitute allegations of criminal conduct or moral failing (the core defamation trigger), (3) flag references to minors, sealed proceedings, or ongoing investigations, (4) track which statements have prior legal clearance (cleared in Episode 3) vs. which are new claims requiring review, (5) document the attorney's clearance decision for each flagged item (approved, revised, removed) as a liability record, and (6) estimate review priority based on risk level so the attorney triages multiple scripts efficiently.

## Target Customer
Media attorneys and executive producers at true crime networks producing 3+ shows simultaneously, each requiring pre-publication legal review.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces attorney review time from 3 hours to 1 hour per script through automated pre-flagging. Shortens the legal review bottleneck from 5 days to 2 days per episode. Prevents the $50K–$500K litigation costs from a single defamation or privacy claim by ensuring systematic review of every statement about a living person.
