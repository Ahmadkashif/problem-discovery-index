# Podcast Ad Sales Houses

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/podcasting-networks|Podcasting Networks]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 30%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Programmatic insertion tools exist, but campaign management, inventory forecasting, and advertiser reporting remain heavily manual.
**Target Buyer:** VP Sales / Ad Operations Director
**Automation Potential:** High — Inventory management, campaign trafficking, insertion verification, and advertiser reporting are rule-heavy, high-volume workflows.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Podcast ad sales houses (Midroll/Stitcher, AdvertiseCast, Gumball, Podcorn) sell advertising inventory across networks of podcasts, connecting advertisers with shows that match their target audience. They manage the full ad lifecycle: inventory forecasting, campaign proposals, IO (insertion order) processing, creative trafficking, dynamic insertion scheduling, delivery verification, and performance reporting. A mid-size ad sales house manages 200–1,000 shows, processes 50–200 campaigns per month, and handles thousands of individual ad insertions. The operational complexity rivals a digital ad exchange, but the tools are far less sophisticated — most operations run on spreadsheets and email.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Dynamic ad insertion platforms (Megaphone, AdsWizz, Triton) handle the technical insertion of ads into podcast episodes. CRM tools (Salesforce, HubSpot) manage advertiser relationships. But the operational layer between "campaign sold" and "campaign delivered and reported" is manual: an ad ops coordinator receives an IO via email, enters the campaign details into the insertion platform, sets targeting rules (show, episode, geographic, time-based), monitors delivery pacing, pulls delivery reports from one system, matches them against the IO in another, and compiles advertiser reports in PowerPoint. Each step involves manual data entry between disconnected systems.
