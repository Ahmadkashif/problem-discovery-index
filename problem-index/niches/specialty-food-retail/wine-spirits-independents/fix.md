# Distributor Rep Ordering Chaos and Allocation Management

**Niche:** [[niches/specialty-food-retail/wine-spirits-independents/profile|Wine & Spirits Independents]]
**Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Wine shop owners meet with 5-15 distributor reps per week who pitch 20-50 wines each, creating an overwhelming ordering process with no way to compare across distributors or track allocation commitments.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A wine shop owner's week includes 5-15 meetings with distributor sales reps, each presenting a portfolio of 20-50 wines with sample tastings, pricing, and availability. The owner must: taste samples (or review notes), compare options across distributors (three distributors might offer similar Pinot Noirs at different price points), decide what to buy within the shop's budget and shelf capacity, place orders through each distributor's individual system, and track allocated wines (high-demand wines like certain Burgundies or Napa Cabs are allocated — the shop gets a limited quantity, and missing the order window means missing the vintage entirely). This process consumes 15-20 hours/week and results in impulsive buying during rep visits, incomplete price comparison, and missed allocation deadlines.

## Why It's Still Broken
SevenFifty provides a distributor product search and ordering platform, but it covers only a fraction of distributors and doesn't handle allocation tracking or cross-distributor comparison. Each distributor has their own ordering platform (or just uses email/text), and none are interoperable. The three-tier system creates information asymmetry — distributors know their competitors' pricing but retailers don't, making comparison shopping difficult. Allocation management is relationship-driven and informal — the distributor rep verbally offers an allocation, and the owner must remember to confirm before the deadline.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A buying management tool that: centralizes all product offerings received from distributor reps (with pricing, availability, and vintage info), enables side-by-side comparison across distributors for similar products, tracks allocation offers with confirmation deadlines and auto-reminders, maintains a buying budget tracker that shows remaining monthly allocation across categories (reds, whites, spirits), and generates orders per distributor from the consolidated buying session. The key insight: instead of making buying decisions during each rep visit, the owner reviews all options together and makes comparative decisions weekly.

## Who Feels the Pain
Wine shop owners and buyers who feel overwhelmed by the volume of distributor presentations, make buying decisions under time pressure during rep visits, and discover they've over-bought in one category while under-buying in another — only to realize they missed an important allocation deadline because it was communicated verbally 3 weeks ago.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces buying decision time by 5-10 hours/week through consolidated comparison instead of rep-by-rep evaluation. Prevents missed allocation windows (each missed allocation represents $500-5,000 in lost margin on high-demand wines). Improves category balance by enabling budget-aware buying across distributors rather than impulsive rep-meeting purchases.
