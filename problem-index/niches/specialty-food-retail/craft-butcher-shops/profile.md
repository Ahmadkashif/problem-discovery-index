# Craft Butcher & Charcuterie Shops

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $20-25B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~13%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most butcher shops use a basic POS and paper-based cutting schedules with no yield tracking, production planning, or digital ordering
**Target Buyer:** Butcher / shop owner / head cutter
**Automation Potential:** Medium — cutting schedules, yield optimization, and wholesale order management follow structured workflows but require craft-specific knowledge

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Craft butcher shops perform fabrication — breaking whole or primal cuts into retail portions, sausages, charcuterie, and prepared items. The core business challenge is yield optimization: a beef chuck primal costs $X per pound wholesale, but the retail revenue depends on how the butcher breaks it — which portions become premium steaks, which become ground beef, which become stew meat, and which become waste. An experienced butcher maximizes value per carcass through cutting decisions that are tacit and learned over years. The shop must also balance retail counter demand (unpredictable) with wholesale/restaurant orders (committed) and charcuterie production (multi-day cure schedules).

## Current Tools & Gaps
POS systems (Square, Clover) handle retail transactions. No tool provides: yield tracking by primal cut (how much revenue was generated per dollar of wholesale purchase?), production planning for charcuterie (which items need to go into cure today to be ready for Saturday's market?), wholesale order management for restaurant accounts, or cutting schedule optimization that balances retail demand, wholesale commitments, and staffing.
