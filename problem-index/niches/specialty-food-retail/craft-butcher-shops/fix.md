# Wholesale Restaurant Order Management

**Niche:** [[niches/specialty-food-retail/craft-butcher-shops/profile|Craft Butcher & Charcuterie Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Craft butcher shops serving 10-30 restaurant accounts manage wholesale orders via text messages, phone calls, and sticky notes — losing orders, delivering wrong cuts, and spending hours on order coordination instead of cutting.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A craft butcher shop with 20 restaurant accounts receives wholesale orders via text ("need 40 lbs chicken thigh boneless skinless for Thursday"), phone call ("same as last week but add 10 lbs pork belly"), email, and occasionally fax. The owner or office manager transcribes these into a spreadsheet or paper list, consolidates them into a cutting schedule, invoices after delivery, and follows up on payment. With 20 accounts ordering 2-3x/week, that's 50+ order transactions per week managed through personal text message threads. Orders get lost, quantities get transposed, and delivery days get confused — each error damages a restaurant relationship that took months to build.

## Why It's Still Broken
Wholesale ordering platforms (BlueCart, Choco, Cut+Dry) target the restaurant side — helping restaurants order from distributors. They don't serve the supplier side, especially a small butcher shop that needs to receive orders, consolidate them into production plans, and manage delivery routes. The order volume (50-100 orders/week) is too low for enterprise order management systems and too high for manual text management. The butcher is stuck in a gap between "too small for wholesale software" and "too big for sticky notes."

## What a Fix Looks Like
A simple ordering portal where restaurant accounts place orders from the butcher shop's product list (with current availability and pricing), orders are automatically consolidated into a daily cutting/production list, delivery routes are organized by day and geography, and invoices are generated automatically at delivery. The ordering interface must be as easy as texting (restaurants won't adopt complex portals) — a mobile-friendly web form or WhatsApp-based ordering bot would meet the adoption bar. The butcher sees a single dashboard: today's wholesale orders, what to cut, who to deliver to.

## Who Feels the Pain
Shop owners who spend 1-2 hours daily managing wholesale orders via text and phone, and restaurant buyers who experience order errors that disrupt their prep schedule.

## Impact If Fixed
Recovers 5-10 hours/week of owner time from order management. Eliminates order errors (estimated at 5-8% of wholesale orders currently result in wrong item, wrong quantity, or missed delivery). Improves restaurant retention — a butcher shop with reliable ordering and delivery keeps accounts for years; one with frequent errors loses them in months. Wholesale typically represents 30-50% of a craft butcher shop's revenue.
