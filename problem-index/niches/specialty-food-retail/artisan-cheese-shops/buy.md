# Cheesemonger Product Knowledge Base

**Niche:** [[niches/specialty-food-retail/artisan-cheese-shops/profile|Artisan Cheese Shops]]
**Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Wiki and knowledge base tools exist, but none are structured for the specific information cheesemongers need at the counter — provenance, flavor notes, pairings, storage, and cutting instructions per cheese, searchable in 5 seconds while a customer waits.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #recommendation #worker-facing

## The Problem
A customer asks: "What's a good substitute for Gruyere that melts well for fondue?" The cheesemonger must search their memory for cheeses currently in the case that are Alpine-style, have similar melt characteristics, and are in stock. A veteran cheesemonger answers instantly from experience; a new hire freezes. Product knowledge is the competitive moat of a cheese shop, but it lives entirely in staff memory — no searchable, structured reference exists for the 200+ cheeses in the case at any time, cross-referenced by flavor profile, pairing, melting behavior, milk type, origin, and current availability.

## What Already Exists
Notion, Confluence, and general wiki tools allow building knowledge bases. Cheese databases exist online (cheese.com, Murray's Cheese descriptions). CRM tools can store product notes. Some POS systems allow item descriptions.

## The Customization Gap
Generic knowledge tools aren't structured for counter-speed retrieval by a cheesemonger's specific query patterns: "What do I have that pairs with Barolo?" (requires filtering by current inventory + wine pairing data), "Customer is lactose-sensitive — what's safe?" (requires filtering by milk type + aging process that reduces lactose), "What's similar to Manchego but from France?" (requires flavor profile + origin cross-reference). The tool needs cheese-specific data fields (milk type, rennet, aging, rind type, texture, flavor descriptors, pairing affinities) populated for every product in the case, searchable by natural language or filter, and updated as inventory changes.

## Target Customer
Cheese shop owners who invest heavily in staff training but lose product knowledge every time an employee leaves, and who want to accelerate new hire ramp-up from 6 months to 6 weeks.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces new cheesemonger training time from 3-6 months to 4-8 weeks for baseline competency. Enables confident customer interactions for junior staff, maintaining the knowledge-intensive customer experience that drives cheese shop loyalty. Preserves institutional product knowledge that currently exists only in individual employee memory.
