# Artisan Cheese Shops

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most cheese shops operate on handwritten inventory sheets, email/phone ordering from 30-50 creameries, and rely on cheesemonger memory for product knowledge
**Target Buyer:** Cheesemonger / shop owner / cheese buyer
**Automation Potential:** Medium — vendor ordering and ripeness tracking are structured processes, but product knowledge transfer and sensory assessment resist simple automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Artisan cheese shops are knowledge-intensive retail operations where product expertise is the primary competitive advantage. A cheesemonger must know the provenance, flavor profile, optimal ripeness window, pairing suggestions, and proper cutting technique for 100-300 cheeses in the case at any time. This knowledge takes 2-5 years to develop, and losing an experienced cheesemonger is devastating to the business. Inventory management is uniquely complex: each wheel or piece has a different arrival date, different ripeness trajectory, and different optimal selling window. A wheel of Comte that's perfect today will be past peak in 10 days, while the Parmigiano that arrived today won't hit peak for 3 weeks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Square or Clover POS handle transactions. No cheese-specific inventory tool exists. Ordering from 30-50 small creameries and distributors happens via email, phone, and text with no centralized order management. Product knowledge lives in the cheesemonger's head and in handwritten tasting notes. No tool tracks ripeness state, optimal selling windows, or pairing recommendations in a structured, searchable format.
