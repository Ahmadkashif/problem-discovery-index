# Farmers Market Vendors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/specialty-food-retail|Specialty Food Retail]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~3%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most vendors use cash boxes or Square readers with no inventory tracking, pre-order management, or multi-market scheduling
**Target Buyer:** Farm-stand operator / small producer / market vendor
**Automation Potential:** Medium — pre-order management, inventory allocation across markets, and customer communication are automatable but vendor operations are highly seasonal and variable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Farmers market vendors sell at 2-5 different markets per week, each at a different location with different customer demographics and different sales volumes. They must decide how to allocate limited perishable inventory across markets (send more bread to the Saturday urban market, more produce to the Wednesday suburban market), manage pre-orders (customers who want to reserve items before market day), and handle all of this from a truck or tent with no fixed infrastructure. Their technology needs are fundamentally different from a brick-and-mortar shop: everything must be mobile, offline-capable, and operable with dirty hands in outdoor conditions.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Square provides mobile payment processing. Barn2Door offers an online store/pre-order platform for farms but is designed for farm-to-consumer delivery, not multi-market vendor operations. Harvie provides CSA management but not market-day operations. No tool handles: multi-market inventory allocation planning, market-day sales tracking by location, pre-order management with market pickup, and customer communication across markets.
