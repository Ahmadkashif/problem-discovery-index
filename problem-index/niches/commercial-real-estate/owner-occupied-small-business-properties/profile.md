# Owner-Occupied Small Business Properties

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6-9B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12-15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — buyers are small business owners using Zillow/LoopNet casually, brokers serving this segment use general-purpose tools, SBA loan process is paper-heavy
**Target Buyer:** Business owner buying their own building / buyer's broker specializing in owner-user sales
**Automation Potential:** Medium — SBA 504/7(a) loan pre-qualification, property-business fit scoring, and closing coordination are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Owner-occupied commercial properties — where a small business buys the building it operates from — represent a fundamentally different transaction type from investment sales. The buyer is not an investor evaluating cap rate and NOI; they're a business owner evaluating whether the building serves their operational needs (loading dock for their distribution business, retail frontage for their restaurant, ADA compliance for their medical practice). Financing is typically SBA 504 or 7(a) loans with different documentation requirements, lower down payments, and longer timelines than conventional commercial mortgages. The broker must understand both the real estate and the business operations to match properties to owner-users — a combination skill set that investment sales and leasing brokers rarely possess.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Owner-user buyers search LoopNet, Zillow commercial, and CREXi without guidance on SBA eligibility, operational fit, or true occupancy cost. Brokers serving this segment use general CRMs (not CRE-specific) and manage SBA loan documentation manually. No tool combines property search with SBA pre-qualification screening and operational fit scoring for small business buyers.
