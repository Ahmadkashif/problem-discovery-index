# SBA-Integrated Property Search for Business Owners

**Niche:** [[niches/commercial-real-estate/owner-occupied-small-business-properties/profile|Owner-Occupied Small Business Properties]]
**Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No property search platform combines SBA 504/7(a) loan pre-qualification with commercial property listings — so a business owner searching for their own building can't see which properties they can actually afford to buy with SBA financing until after they engage a lender, adding weeks to the search process.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A restaurant owner looking to buy a building for their business needs to know: which listed properties fit my operational requirements (commercial kitchen, parking, ADA, signage), which are SBA-eligible (owner-occupancy requirements, property condition standards, environmental clearance), and what's my monthly cost with SBA 504 financing at 10% down? Currently, the owner searches LoopNet, finds properties, then separately contacts an SBA lender to check eligibility and get pre-qualified — a process that adds 3-6 weeks to the search and eliminates 40-60% of properties that don't meet SBA criteria.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
SBA eligibility is complex: 504 loans require 51%+ owner occupancy, specific property condition standards, and business eligibility screening. 7(a) loans have different criteria. Integrating SBA pre-qualification logic into a property search requires both CRE listing data and SBA lending expertise — two industries that don't typically collaborate on product development. CRE tech companies build for investors; SBA lenders build loan origination tools, not property search.

## What to Build
A property search platform for business owners that layers SBA eligibility screening onto commercial listings: input business type, revenue, and down payment capacity; output filtered property listings with estimated monthly SBA 504 and 7(a) payment amounts, SBA eligibility flags, and operational fit scores based on property characteristics vs. business requirements. Integration with SBA preferred lenders for streamlined pre-qualification.

## Target Customer
Small business owners actively searching for owner-occupied commercial space — approximately 200,000-300,000 active searches annually. Monetization through SBA lender referral fees ($1,000-$3,000 per closed loan) and broker subscription ($200-$400/month for buyer's brokers specializing in owner-user transactions).

## Impact If Built
Business owners identify SBA-eligible properties on day 1 instead of week 4. Property search-to-purchase timeline compresses from 6-12 months to 3-6 months. SBA lenders get pre-qualified leads instead of cold inquiries — improving close rates by 30-40%.
