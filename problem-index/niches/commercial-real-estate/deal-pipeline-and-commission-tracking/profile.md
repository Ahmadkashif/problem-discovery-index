# Deal Pipeline & Commission Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B (embedded)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~4-6% (embedded operational cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — larger firms use Buildout or ClientLook, but commission split calculations, draw reconciliation, and pipeline forecasting remain Excel-based at most boutique firms
**Target Buyer:** Brokerage operations manager, CFO, or managing broker responsible for commission accounting and pipeline forecasting
**Automation Potential:** High — commission split calculations, draw reconciliation, pipeline stage tracking, and revenue forecasting are rule-based and highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
CRE commission structures are uniquely complex: split percentages vary by deal type (leasing vs. investment sales), seniority tier (graduating splits at production thresholds), co-brokerage arrangements (splits between listing and selling broker, often at different firms), draw reconciliation (brokers receive monthly draws against future commissions), and override calculations (managing broker takes a percentage off the top). A 20-broker firm processes 100-200 commission calculations per year, each with different split structures, co-brokerage deductions, and draw balances. Errors in commission calculations are the number-one source of broker-management disputes.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ClientLook and Buildout track deals through pipeline stages but don't calculate commissions. QuickBooks handles accounting but has no CRE commission module. Brokermint and RealNex provide some commission tracking but are designed for residential real estate, where commission structures are simpler (percentage of sale price, fixed split). No tool handles graduating CRE splits, co-brokerage calculations with different firms, and draw reconciliation in a single platform.
