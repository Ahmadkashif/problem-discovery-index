# Co-Brokerage Commission Split Reconciliation

**Niche:** [[niches/commercial-real-estate/deal-pipeline-and-commission-tracking/profile|Deal Pipeline & Commission Tracking]]
**Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When two brokerage firms split a commission on a co-brokered deal, the reconciliation process — verifying the gross commission, confirming the split percentage, deducting referral fees, and tracking payment from the closing attorney — takes 2-4 weeks of back-and-forth emails and produces payment disputes in 10-15% of co-brokered transactions.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
In a co-brokered CRE transaction, the listing broker's firm and the buyer/tenant broker's firm split the total commission according to a co-brokerage agreement signed at deal initiation. At closing, the commission is disbursed from the closing attorney's trust account — but the disbursement instructions must be verified by both firms, and discrepancies between the co-brokerage agreement, the closing statement, and each firm's internal commission calculation create disputes. Common issues: the co-brokerage agreement specifies a split on the "gross commission" but one firm calculates it on the net-of-referral-fee amount; the closing statement applies the commission to a different sale price than expected (due to credits or adjustments); or the split percentage was verbally modified during negotiations but the co-brokerage agreement wasn't updated.

## Why It's Still Broken
Co-brokerage agreements are typically one-page documents signed at deal inception and filed in a drawer until closing — 6-18 months later. No system tracks the co-brokerage agreement alongside the deal through its lifecycle. Commission disbursement at closing is handled by the closing attorney's office, which receives conflicting instructions from the two firms. The volume is too low per firm (10-30 co-brokered deals/year) to justify a purpose-built system, but the aggregate industry cost is significant.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A co-brokerage commission reconciliation tool: both firms enter the co-brokerage terms at deal inception into a shared record; the system tracks the deal through closing; at closing, it calculates the split based on the actual closing statement figures and generates verified disbursement instructions for the closing attorney. Both firms see the same calculation before closing, eliminating post-closing disputes.

## Who Feels the Pain
Brokerage operations managers and accounting staff who spend 3-5 hours per co-brokered closing reconciling commission splits, and managing brokers who mediate commission disputes with co-brokers.

## Impact If Fixed
Commission reconciliation per co-brokered deal drops from 3-5 hours to 30 minutes. Co-brokerage disputes drop from 10-15% to under 3% of transactions. Payment receipt accelerates from 2-4 weeks post-closing to 3-5 business days when disbursement instructions are pre-verified.
