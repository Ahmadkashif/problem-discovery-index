# Lease Administration Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (services)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6-8% (embedded operational cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — large firms use Yardi/MRI for lease tracking, but mid-market firms and landlords with 5-50 leases manage in spreadsheets
**Target Buyer:** Lease administrator, property manager, or asset manager responsible for tracking lease obligations across a commercial portfolio
**Automation Potential:** High — critical date tracking, CAM reconciliation, rent escalation calculations, and lease renewal notifications are rule-based and highly automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Lease administration is the back-office function of tracking every obligation in every commercial lease across a property portfolio: rent escalation dates, CAM reconciliation deadlines, option exercise windows, insurance certificate renewals, tenant improvement allowance drawdowns, and percentage rent calculations. For a property manager overseeing 200 leases, missing a single option exercise deadline can cost the landlord a below-market lease that could have been renegotiated. The work is entirely rule-based — every obligation is specified in the lease document — but the rules are different for every lease, and the volume of critical dates across a portfolio overwhelms manual tracking.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Yardi Voyager and MRI Software provide institutional-grade lease administration for portfolios with 500+ leases. LeaseHawk and LeaseQuery provide ASC 842 lease accounting compliance. For mid-market landlords and property managers with 20-200 leases, these tools are over-engineered and over-priced. Most mid-market operators track critical dates in Excel, missing 5-15% of deadlines annually.
