**Market Size:** $12-15B

**Digital Adoption:** Medium — CoStar used for comps, but site selection modeling, zoning verification, and environmental due diligence are largely manual
**Target Buyer:** Industrial brokerage team lead or principal at a boutique industrial-focused firm
**Automation Potential:** Medium — site selection scoring, environmental risk screening, and zoning compatibility checks are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Industrial and warehouse brokerage has exploded since 2020 driven by e-commerce logistics demand. The workflow differs fundamentally from office or retail: deals are driven by clear-height specifications, dock-door counts, truck court dimensions, power capacity, and proximity to interstate access and last-mile delivery zones. Site selection involves matching these physical specifications to tenant requirements — a task that experienced industrial brokers perform through memorized building inventories but that doesn't scale as markets expand. Environmental contamination risk (Phase I/II ESA history) adds a due diligence layer absent in office and retail.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CoStar provides industrial listing data. CBRE and JLL have proprietary site selection tools for institutional clients. No affordable tool combines building specification matching (clear height, dock doors, power), zoning verification against municipal code, environmental risk screening from public records, and logistics proximity scoring for boutique industrial brokerages serving mid-market tenants and investors.
