# Environmental Risk Screening from Public Records

**Niche:** [[niches/commercial-real-estate/industrial-warehouse-specialists/profile|Industrial & Warehouse Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Environmental database search tools (EDR, GeoSearch) provide raw data dumps of contamination records near a property, but don't translate findings into deal-relevant risk assessments that an industrial broker can use to advise a buyer before committing to a Phase I ESA.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #automation #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
Every industrial property transaction requires environmental due diligence. Before spending $3,000-$5,000 on a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment, brokers and buyers need a preliminary risk screening: are there known contamination sites within 1 mile, is the subject property on any EPA or state cleanup lists, and what's the historical land use? Experienced industrial brokers recognize high-risk indicators from property history (former dry cleaners, gas stations, manufacturing plants on adjacent parcels) but junior brokers miss these signals.

## What Already Exists
Environmental Data Resources (EDR) and GeoSearch provide database searches of EPA, state, and tribal environmental records for any address. These tools return raw record counts and lists of nearby contamination sites — often 20-50 entries that require manual interpretation by an environmental consultant to assess actual risk to the subject property.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is interpretation: translating a raw database report (nearby sites, contamination types, cleanup status, distance, and groundwater flow direction) into a deal-relevant risk tier (low/medium/high) with specific concerns flagged. An industrial CRE-specific layer would parse EDR/GeoSearch outputs, apply distance and contaminant-type weighting, flag recognized environmental patterns (upgradient contamination plumes, petroleum release sites within vapor intrusion distance), and produce a one-page risk summary the broker can share with a buyer before ordering the full Phase I.

## Target Customer
Industrial brokers and mid-market investors evaluating 3-10 industrial acquisitions per year, each requiring environmental screening — approximately 5,000 active industrial investment buyers nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces preliminary environmental screening from 2-4 hours of manual EDR report interpretation to a 15-minute automated risk summary. Prevents $3,000-$5,000 Phase I expenditures on properties with obvious environmental red flags — saving buyers $10,000-$20,000 per year in avoided due diligence costs on deals they would have killed after Phase I results.
