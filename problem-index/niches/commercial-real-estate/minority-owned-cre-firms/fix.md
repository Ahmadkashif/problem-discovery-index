# Off-Market Deal Discovery from Public Records

**Niche:** [[niches/commercial-real-estate/minority-owned-cre-firms/profile|Minority-Owned CRE Firms]]
**Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Minority brokers excluded from the referral networks that generate off-market deal flow need a systematic alternative — property owners likely to sell identified from public signals (tax delinquency, estate filings, code violations, maturing loans) that indicate motivation before the property hits the open market.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #automation #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
In commercial real estate, 40-60% of transactions occur off-market — the property never appears on CoStar or LoopNet. Off-market deals are sourced through broker-to-broker referral networks, direct owner relationships, and repeat client business. Minority brokers who are underrepresented in these networks miss the majority of deal flow, regardless of their market knowledge. They're left competing for the 40-60% of deals that do hit the open market, where they face competition from every broker with a CoStar subscription.

## Why It's Still Broken
The referral networks that generate off-market deal flow are social structures built over decades — they can't be disrupted by technology directly. The alternative is systematic prospecting: identifying properties whose owners are likely to sell from public data signals. This data exists (tax delinquency records, probate filings, code violation databases, CMBS loan maturity schedules) but is scattered across county, state, and federal databases with no aggregation or scoring tool purpose-built for CRE prospecting.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A seller motivation scoring tool that aggregates public signals for commercial properties in the broker's target market: tax delinquency status, probate/estate filings involving the property, code violation history, CMBS loan maturity within 24 months, and ownership duration (>15 years suggests aging owner). Each property gets a motivation score; the broker receives a ranked list of properties most likely to transact within 12 months, with owner contact information from county records. Updated monthly as new filings occur.

## Who Feels the Pain
Minority CRE brokers and developers who lack established referral networks — approximately 5,000-8,000 professionals nationally who generate below-market deal volume despite market competency because of network access gaps.

## Impact If Fixed
Systematic off-market prospecting from public data generates 3-5 additional listing opportunities per year for brokers who currently rely on open-market competition. Each additional listing at a $1M property generates $30,000-$50,000 in commission — transformative for a minority broker building their practice.
