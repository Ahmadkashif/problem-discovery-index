# Multi-Tenant Office Brokerages

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/commercial-real-estate|Commercial Real Estate]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $18-22B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35-40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — CoStar subscriptions are standard, Buildout or ClientLook used for deal management, but analytics and tenant prospecting remain manual
**Target Buyer:** Managing broker or office leasing director at a boutique-to-mid-size brokerage (5-30 brokers)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — tenant prospecting, lease renewal tracking, and market report generation are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multi-tenant office brokerages focus on leasing and investment sales of office buildings with multiple tenants — a fundamentally different workflow from single-tenant net lease or industrial. The core competency is tenant mix optimization (which tenant types complement each other, which anchor tenants attract smaller tenants) and lease staggering (avoiding a cluster of expirations that creates vacancy risk). Brokers in this niche maintain deep submarket knowledge: vacancy rates by building class, asking rents per floor, and tenant migration patterns between competing buildings within the same submarket. This knowledge is highly local and perishable — a broker who stops transacting in a submarket for 12 months loses their information advantage.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CoStar provides market data and listings; Buildout handles marketing materials and deal tracking. VTS provides institutional-grade leasing and asset management but is priced for landlords with 1M+ sq ft portfolios. No affordable tool combines tenant prospecting (which companies are growing and need space), lease expiration tracking across competing buildings, and submarket analytics into a single workflow for boutique office brokerages.
