# Police Rotation & Impound Operators

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/towing-companies|Towing Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~30%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Use Towbook or TOPS for dispatch, separate impound lot software (TowLien, AutoIMS), but lack integrated dispatch-to-impound workflow
**Target Buyer:** Owner managing police rotation contracts, impound lots, and the compliance-heavy lien/auction pipeline
**Automation Potential:** High — Lien processing, title searches, notification requirements, and auction timelines are highly rule-based and vary by jurisdiction

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Police rotation towing generates high-volume, guaranteed call flow (police calls are non-negotiable — someone must tow the car), but comes with intensive compliance requirements: impound lot management, lien filing deadlines, registered owner notifications, abandoned vehicle declarations, and auction procedures. Each state (and often each county) has different rules for lien amounts, notification timelines, and auction rights. A missed notification deadline means the company can't auction the vehicle and eats storage costs on a car worth less than the storage bill. This is simultaneously the highest-volume revenue source and the highest-compliance-burden business function.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TOPS handles impound lot inventory and basic lien tracking. TowLien offers lien processing services. AutoIMS manages lot inventory. None integrate with dispatch systems (Towbook) to create a seamless flow from "police call received" to "vehicle impounded" to "lien filed" to "vehicle auctioned." The dispatcher, lot attendant, and office manager each use different systems with manual handoffs between them.
