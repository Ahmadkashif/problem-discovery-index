# Roadside-Only Tire & Battery Services

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/towing-companies|Towing Companies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most operators are solo mobile technicians with a van, a phone, and no software beyond a Square reader
**Target Buyer:** Mobile roadside service operator handling tire changes, jump starts, lockouts, and fuel delivery without tow capability
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Job routing, parts inventory, and pricing are simple but completely unmanaged

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Not every roadside call needs a tow truck. Flat tires, dead batteries, lockouts, and fuel delivery are high-frequency, low-complexity services that can be handled by a technician in a van rather than a $100,000 tow truck. These operators often start as side businesses for off-duty tow drivers or auto mechanics, running solo with a van full of common batteries and tire repair supplies. They undercut tow companies on price (a tire change costs $50-$80 vs. a tow to a shop for $150+) but struggle with the same dispatch and routing challenges at a fraction of the revenue per call. Motor clubs increasingly route these calls to dedicated roadside service providers rather than full tow trucks, creating growth opportunity for operators who can respond faster and cheaper.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most roadside-only operators use personal phones and Square for payment. No dispatch software is designed for a solo mobile operator handling 8-15 short-duration calls per day. Inventory management for batteries and tire repair supplies is done by looking in the van. No tool helps with route optimization across a day of scattered short calls with variable timing.
