# Motor Club Billing & Claims Reconciliation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/towing-companies|Towing Companies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M-1.2B (services)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8% (services, but affects 60% of operators)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Most billing is done by manually entering call details into each motor club's portal, then reconciling payments against bank deposits weeks later
**Target Buyer:** Billing manager or owner handling 100-500 motor club calls per month across 3-5 different programs
**Automation Potential:** High — Each motor club has defined rate structures, documentation requirements, and payment terms; billing is pure data matching with known rules

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
A towing company participating in 3-5 motor club programs (AAA, Agero, Allstate Motor Club, GEICO roadside, State Farm) bills each one differently: different rate structures (per-mile, flat fee, zone-based), different documentation requirements (some need photos, some need VIN, some need odometer readings), different payment timelines (15-60 days), and different dispute processes. The billing manager submits 100-500 claims per month across these programs, manually entering data into each portal. When payments arrive, they must be reconciled against submitted claims to identify underpayments, denied claims, and missing payments. This reconciliation is a spreadsheet exercise that takes 10-20 hours/month and still misses $2,000-$5,000 in unbilled or under-billed services.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Towbook generates invoices but doesn't submit to motor club portals automatically. Motor club portals accept claim submissions but each has a different interface and format. QuickBooks tracks payments received but can't match them to specific motor club claims. No tool reconciles "we submitted 187 claims to Agero this month; they paid 172; 8 were denied for documentation issues; 7 are still pending past their 30-day payment term."
