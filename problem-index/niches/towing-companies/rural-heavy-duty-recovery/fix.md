# Insurance Claim Disputes on Complex Recovery Jobs

**Niche:** [[niches/towing-companies/rural-heavy-duty-recovery/profile|Rural & Highway Heavy-Duty Recovery]]
**Industry:** [[industries/towing-companies|Towing Companies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Insurance companies dispute 30-40% of heavy-duty recovery invoices above $5,000 — the operator has the right charges but lacks the documentation to prove it.
**Tags:** #computer-vision #ocr #automation #compliance #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A heavy-duty recovery operator submits a $22,000 invoice for an overturned tanker recovery. The trucking company's insurance carrier disputes $8,000 of the charges: "We don't see evidence that environmental cleanup was necessary. The boom truck hours seem excessive. Traffic control charges are not supported." The operator knows every charge was legitimate — they spent 8 hours on scene in freezing rain — but their documentation is a handwritten invoice and 3 blurry phone photos. Without timestamped evidence of equipment deployment, scene conditions, and cleanup necessity, the operator negotiates down and accepts $16,000 — losing $6,000 on a single job. This happens on 3-5 jobs per year.

## Why It's Still Broken
The operator is focused on the recovery, not documentation. At 2am on a rainy highway with cars flying past, taking systematic photos and logging equipment hours feels like a luxury. By the time they write the invoice the next day, details are fuzzy: was the boom truck running for 6 hours or 5? Did they deploy the air cushion for 45 minutes or an hour? The insurance company knows operators can't prove their charges and uses this systematically to negotiate invoices down by 20-35%.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A scene documentation workflow triggered when the operator arrives: GPS-verified arrival timestamp, prompted photo sequence (overall scene, vehicle position, equipment deployed, environmental conditions, cleanup progress, completion), automatic equipment timers (start/stop on the app when each piece of equipment is deployed), and a scene conditions log (weather, road type, hazmat presence). All timestamped and GPS-tagged. The resulting documentation package is attached to the invoice as an evidence file that makes insurance disputes untenable — every line item has time-stamped, geo-tagged photographic support.

## Who Feels the Pain
The recovery operator who just completed a physically grueling, dangerous, 10-hour job in the middle of the night and now has to fight with an insurance adjuster for 2 months to get paid for work they actually performed.

## Impact If Fixed
Recovers $15,000-$60,000/year per operator in disputed charges that are currently negotiated away. Reduces dispute resolution time from 60-90 days to 7-14 days by providing unimpeachable documentation at invoice submission.
