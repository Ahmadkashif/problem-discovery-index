# Motor Club Rotation Fleets

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/towing-companies|Towing Companies]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~40%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Use Towbook or TOPS for dispatch, connected to motor club portals (AAA, Agero), but lack performance analytics and SLA optimization tools
**Target Buyer:** Owner or dispatcher managing motor club relationships that represent 40-60% of call volume
**Automation Potential:** High — Motor club SLA compliance, rotation performance tracking, and billing are all rule-bound and measurable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Motor club rotation is the bread and butter of mid-size towing companies (5-15 trucks). AAA, Agero (serving Allstate, GEICO, etc.), and other motor clubs assign calls to towing companies on a rotation basis, but maintain strict performance standards: ETA compliance (arrive within 30-45 minutes), customer satisfaction scores, proper documentation, and billing accuracy. Companies that fall below thresholds lose their rotation position — which can mean losing 40-60% of monthly call volume overnight. This creates a high-stakes optimization problem: which calls to prioritize, which drivers to assign to motor club calls, and how to maintain SLA compliance while also serving higher-margin private calls.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Towbook handles dispatch and records but doesn't analyze motor club performance trends or predict which calls are at risk of SLA violation. Motor club portals (AAA's portal, Agero's provider hub) show historical performance but in arrears — by the time you see the scorecard, the damage is done. No tool provides real-time SLA risk assessment: "This call will miss the 30-minute window if you don't dispatch in the next 3 minutes."
