# Residential Interior Painting

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/painting-contractors|Painting Contractors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-17B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~37%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — PaintScout adoption growing among mid-size shops, but most painters still estimate with a tape measure, notepad, and memory. Scheduling is text messages and a wall calendar. Invoicing split between QuickBooks and handwritten receipts.
**Target Buyer:** Interior painting company owner (typically a painter who started their own business after 5-10 years in the field)
**Automation Potential:** High — room measurement follows geometric rules, color matching is a pattern recognition task, and surface prep assessment is a visual classification problem that experienced painters do by touch and sight

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Interior residential painting is a high-frequency, moderate-ticket business: a typical interior repaint runs $3,000-8,000 for a full house, $800-2,000 per room, with the average company completing 100-300 projects per year. The workflow is deceptively complex beneath the apparent simplicity. An experienced estimator walks into a room and within 30 seconds has mentally calculated paintable square footage (subtracting doors, windows, closets), assessed surface condition (nail pops, texture damage, water stains requiring primer), identified trim/door/ceiling scope, and estimated prep time — which varies 2-5x depending on wall condition, existing paint quality, and color change darkness. This estimating intuition is pure tacit knowledge: the difference between a profitable $4,200 job and an unprofitable $3,500 bid is whether the estimator correctly assessed that the textured walls with dark red paint would require three coats of primer plus two finish coats instead of the standard two-coat application. Color consultation is the second major differentiator — painters who offer design guidance close at 40-60% higher rates than those who just paint whatever the customer picks.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PaintScout is the leading estimating tool for painting contractors, handling rate-per-square-foot calculations with customizable pricing for different surface types and difficulty levels. However, PaintScout requires manual measurement input — the painter still has to measure every wall, count every window, and assess every surface by hand. Jobber handles scheduling and invoicing but has no painting-specific functionality. CRM tools like Markate combine estimating with marketing but lack depth in either. No tool addresses the core bottleneck: converting a room walkthrough into an accurate estimate without the 15-20 minutes of manual measurement and mental calculation that experienced estimators perform. Benjamin Moore and Sherwin-Williams offer color visualizer apps but these are consumer-facing and disconnected from the contractor's estimating and project management workflow.
