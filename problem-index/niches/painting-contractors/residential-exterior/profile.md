# Residential Exterior Painting

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/painting-contractors|Painting Contractors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — exterior estimating is almost entirely manual site visits with ladders, binoculars, and notepads. Drone adoption is emerging but rare. Scheduling is driven by weather windows, managed via weather apps and gut feel rather than any planning software.
**Target Buyer:** Exterior painting contractor or full-service residential painting company owner
**Automation Potential:** High — exterior surface condition assessment is a visual pattern recognition task, weather window scheduling is a forecasting problem, and material estimation from exterior photos is geometrically tractable with aerial imagery

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Exterior residential painting is fundamentally different from interior work: it is weather-dependent, prep-intensive, and requires working at heights. A typical exterior repaint runs $5,000-15,000 for a single-family home, with prep work (pressure washing, scraping, caulking, priming bare wood) consuming 40-60% of total labor hours. The critical judgment call on every exterior project is assessing prep scope — an experienced estimator looks at the siding, trim, and fascia and estimates how much surface is peeling, how much caulk has failed, whether the substrate is sound or rotted, and how many coats of primer bare wood requires. This assessment directly determines whether a job is profitable: underestimating prep by 20% on a $10,000 job turns a $3,000 profit into a $500 loss. The second major differentiator is weather dependency: exterior painting requires dry conditions (no rain for 4-6 hours after application), moderate temperatures (50-90°F), and low humidity (<85%). A crew scheduled for exterior work that gets rained out loses $1,500-3,000 in unbillable labor for the day.

## Current Tools & Gaps
PaintScout handles exterior estimating calculations but requires manual surface condition assessment and measurement. Drone-based measurement services exist (EagleView, Hover) for roofing but are not optimized for painting — they measure roof planes, not paintable siding and trim surfaces. Weather apps provide forecasts but no painting-specific scheduling intelligence — a 30% rain chance at 4 PM may or may not affect a crew that finishes spraying at 2 PM. No tool combines exterior condition assessment, painting-specific measurement, weather-optimized scheduling, and material calculation into a single workflow.
