## Profile
**Market Size:** $5-7B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~14%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — industrial coating contractors rely on paper-based batch records, hand-written surface prep logs, and manufacturer spec sheets in three-ring binders. QC documentation is photos emailed to the project engineer with manual mil thickness readings on paper forms.
**Target Buyer:** Industrial coatings contractor owner or QC manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium — coating specification compliance is a structured documentation problem, surface prep verification is a visual classification task, and coating failure prediction is a pattern recognition opportunity

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Industrial and specialty coatings — epoxy floors, polyurethane tank linings, intumescent fireproofing, zinc-rich primers for structural steel, polyurea waterproofing — operate under a completely different paradigm than decorative painting. Every application is governed by detailed coating specifications (typically SSPC/NACE standards) that prescribe surface preparation methods (abrasive blast to SSPC-SP 10 Near White, anchor profile 2-3 mils), environmental conditions during application (surface temp minimum 5°F above dew point, relative humidity below 85%), wet film thickness per coat, dry film thickness per coat, and inter-coat recoat windows. Failure to meet any parameter can cause catastrophic coating failure — a tank lining applied at too-low temperature may delaminate within months, requiring a $200K+ re-application. The work is high-stakes, highly regulated, and demands meticulous documentation that proves compliance with every specification requirement.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Generic project management tools (Procore, BuilderTrend) track schedules and budgets but know nothing about coating specifications. SSPC publishes standards, but compliance verification is manual — an inspector records surface profile readings on paper, logs environmental conditions from a sling psychrometer, and photographs each stage. No integrated system connects specification requirements, environmental monitoring, surface prep verification, and coating application documentation into a single auditable trail. Coating manufacturers provide product data sheets but these are PDFs consumed by humans, not structured data consumed by software.
