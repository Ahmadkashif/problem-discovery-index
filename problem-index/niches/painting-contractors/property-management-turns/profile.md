# Property Management Turns

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/painting-contractors|Painting Contractors]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — turn painting is managed by phone call from the property manager, verbal scope confirmation on-site, and a text message when the unit is done. No scheduling software, no standardized scoping, no quality tracking across units.
**Target Buyer:** Painting contractor specializing in apartment/rental unit turnover work, or property management company's maintenance director
**Automation Potential:** High — unit turns follow repeatable patterns (standard colors, standard scope, standard unit layouts), making them ideal for batch scheduling optimization and standardized quality verification

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Property management turn painting is a volume business with razor-thin margins. When a tenant moves out of an apartment, the unit must be turned (cleaned, repaired, painted) within 3-7 days before the next tenant moves in. Every day the unit sits vacant costs the property owner $50-150 in lost rent. The painting scope is typically standardized: the same 2-3 colors (landlord white walls, semi-gloss white trim, accent color in bedrooms), the same products, and the same unit layouts across a complex. A painting contractor handling turns for a 500-unit apartment complex may paint 150-200 units per year, with each unit following a nearly identical scope. The decision that consumes the most time and creates the most disputes is the touch-up vs. full repaint assessment: property managers want the cheapest option (touch-up at $200-400), but a unit with scuff marks, nail holes, and 3 previous touch-up layers often looks worse after touch-up than before because the touch-up paint doesn't match the aged existing paint. Experienced turn painters can assess this in 30 seconds; inexperienced ones either over-recommend (full repaint at $800-1,200 when touch-up would suffice) or under-recommend (touch-up that looks terrible and generates a callback).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Property management software (AppFolio, Buildium) tracks unit turn status but doesn't manage painting scope or scheduling. Painting estimating tools (PaintScout) are designed for custom residential work, not standardized unit turns. No tool handles the batch scheduling optimization that turn painting requires: 8 units moving out this week, 3 painters available, 2 units need full repaint (2 days each), 6 units need touch-up (half day each) — what's the optimal assignment to minimize total turn time? This scheduling is done manually by the painting foreman each morning.
