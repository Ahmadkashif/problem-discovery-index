## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — commercial painting companies use Procore or generic PM software for project tracking but painting-specific workflows (after-hours scheduling, occupied space coordination, specification compliance) are managed via email, spreadsheets, and phone calls with building managers.
**Target Buyer:** Commercial painting company owner or project manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — project sequencing in occupied buildings is a constraint optimization problem, and specification compliance tracking is a structured documentation task

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial painting operates under fundamentally different constraints than residential work. Projects happen in occupied spaces — offices, retail stores, hospitals, schools — requiring after-hours or weekend work to avoid disrupting tenants. The scope is specified by architects or facility managers in detailed paint specifications (CSI Division 09 90 00) that prescribe exact products, surface prep standards, mil thickness, and environmental conditions. Commercial painters bid competitively against other contractors using these specs, with margins of 8-15% versus the 30-45% margins in residential work. The scheduling complexity is the primary differentiator: painting the common areas of a 15-story office building requires coordinating with building management for elevator access, HVAC scheduling (shutdown required during spray application), tenant notification, security access, and freight elevator booking — all while sequencing the work floor-by-floor to minimize disruption and ensure proper cure times between coats.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Procore handles project management but is not painting-specific — it doesn't understand coating specifications, mil thickness requirements, or the constraint that you cannot paint a conference room on Tuesday if the adjacent office is occupied on Wednesday (VOC off-gassing). Painting-specific tools like PaintScout are residential-focused and don't handle commercial spec interpretation or multi-phase project scheduling. Commercial painters typically manage building coordination through a combination of email chains with building managers, shared Google Calendars for floor access schedules, and paper spec binders on the job site.
