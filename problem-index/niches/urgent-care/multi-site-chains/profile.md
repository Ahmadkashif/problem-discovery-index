# Multi-Site Urgent Care Chains

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $10-12B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~42%
**Digital Adoption:** Med-High — Experity or Athenahealth deployed per site, but cross-site analytics and centralized operational tooling are manual or nonexistent
**Target Buyer:** VP Operations or COO at 5-50+ location chain (CityMD, GoHealth, MedExpress, PatientFirst)
**Automation Potential:** High — multi-location operational standardization, cross-site staffing allocation, and centralized quality metric tracking are all rule-heavy with clear decision logic

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Multi-site urgent care chains face problems that single-site operators never encounter: normalizing operational metrics across 10-50 locations with different patient mixes, provider pools, and local competition; floating providers between sites based on real-time demand; maintaining brand-consistent patient experience across geographically dispersed clinics; and centralizing quality and compliance reporting for payer contracts and accreditation. Experity handles per-site clinical workflow well, but its reporting is site-siloed — a VP Operations managing 30 locations cannot see a cross-site comparison of door-to-door time, patients per provider hour, or X-ray utilization without exporting and merging spreadsheets from every location.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Experity dominates UC-specific EHR/PM but was architected as a per-clinic system. Cross-site reporting requires manual data pulls or custom BI integrations that most chains build internally with fragile Excel-and-SQL pipelines. Athenahealth offers multi-location views but lacks UC-specific metrics (door-to-door time, left-without-being-seen rate, X-ray turnaround). General BI tools (Tableau, Power BI) require dedicated analysts to build UC dashboards from scratch. No turnkey product gives a chain operator a real-time cross-location operational command center.
