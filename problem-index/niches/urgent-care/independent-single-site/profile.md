# Independent Single-Site Urgent Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most run Experity or Athenahealth but use basic features only; owner-physicians lack time to configure or optimize
**Target Buyer:** Owner-physician or practice manager at standalone urgent care
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — business intelligence, marketing ROI tracking, and payer mix optimization are all automatable but currently invisible to solo operators

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
The owner-physician at an independent urgent care is simultaneously the lead clinician, business manager, HR department, and marketing director. They run their business blind — they know how many patients walked in today but cannot easily answer: What is my daily P&L? Which payer mix trends are shifting? Is my Google Ads spend actually driving walk-ins, or are those patients coming from the apartment complex across the street? They compete against chains with dedicated marketing teams, centralized purchasing, and operational playbooks, armed only with clinical skill and a general-purpose EHR. The technology gap is not feature depth — it is operational intelligence packaged for someone who has 30 minutes per day to manage the business side.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Experity works for clinical workflow and billing but its analytics are designed for operational staff, not owner-physicians with no time to build custom reports. Athenahealth provides broader practice management but is over-featured for a single site — the owner pays for capabilities they never configure. QuickBooks handles accounting but is disconnected from clinical volume data. Google Analytics tracks website visits but cannot connect ad clicks to actual patient walk-ins. No product gives the independent UC owner a "business cockpit" that synthesizes clinical volume, financial performance, and marketing attribution into a daily 5-minute briefing.
