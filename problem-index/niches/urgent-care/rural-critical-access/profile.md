# Rural & Critical Access Urgent Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~8%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many still use paper-heavy workflows or basic EHRs not configured for UC throughput; internet connectivity can be unreliable
**Target Buyer:** Administrator at rural UC or critical access ED alternative, often affiliated with a rural hospital or community health center
**Automation Potential:** Medium — triage-to-transfer decision support and telemedicine-augmented care have high impact but face infrastructure barriers

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Rural urgent cares may be the only walk-in medical facility within a 30+ mile radius, making them a de facto emergency department for communities without one. They handle higher-acuity patients than suburban UCs because patients have no nearby alternative — a chest pain patient in rural Kansas goes to the UC, not a 45-minute-away ED. This creates a distinct clinical challenge: providers (often NPs or PAs because rural areas cannot recruit physicians) must make transfer-or-treat decisions with limited diagnostics (often no CT, limited lab panels, no on-site specialist). The technology they need is fundamentally different from urban UC tools — they need decision support for "when do I put this patient in an ambulance" rather than "how do I move patients through faster."

## Current Tools & Gaps
Experity assumes urban/suburban UC density and throughput optimization — its workflow is designed for 30-60 patients per day, not the 8-15 that characterize many rural sites. Critical access hospital EHRs (CPSI/TruBridge, Evident) are inpatient-focused and poorly suited for walk-in workflow. Telemedicine platforms (Amwell, Teladoc) provide specialist consults but do not integrate with UC workflow for real-time clinical decision support. Transfer coordination is still primarily phone and fax — calling multiple hospitals to find an accepting physician and available bed.
