# After-Hours & Weekend-Only Urgent Care

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** Underserved

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1-1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — many use the same EHR as a daytime practice but without workflow optimization for the distinct after-hours operating model
**Target Buyer:** Owner of evening/weekend-only UC, or daytime practice owner operating extended hours as a separate business unit
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — PCP handoff communication, moonlighter scheduling, and follow-up care coordination are all high-value automation targets

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
After-hours and weekend-only urgent cares operate in a fundamentally different context than standard UCs. They exist because primary care offices close at 5pm and ERs are overcrowded — their value proposition is filling the gap between these two options. This creates distinct challenges: their entire provider workforce is moonlighters (physicians, NPs, PAs working after-hours shifts alongside their primary jobs), making staffing inherently fragile — one provider cancellation can close the clinic for the night. Their patients cannot follow up with their PCP until Monday morning at earliest, creating a care coordination gap for conditions that need 24-48 hour re-evaluation. They see higher-acuity presentations than daytime UCs because patients who would normally go to their PCP have waited all day for symptoms to worsen. And their liability exposure is elevated because they are covering the gap when patients' regular providers are unavailable.

## Current Tools & Gaps
EHR systems (Experity, Athenahealth) make no distinction between daytime and after-hours UC workflows. There is no built-in PCP notification system — after-hours providers document the visit in the EHR, but the patient's PCP receives no automatic notification and may not learn about the visit until the patient mentions it weeks later. Moonlighter scheduling is managed through group texts, spreadsheets, or platforms like ShiftAdmin that were designed for hospital shift scheduling, not small UC operations. No tool addresses the specific follow-up gap: the after-hours provider who sees a patient Friday night and wants to ensure re-evaluation Monday morning has no reliable way to close that loop.
