# Employer On-Site Clinics

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5-2.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~7%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — many use paper-based OSHA logs, occupational health platforms disconnected from UC workflow, and employer-specific billing systems that don't integrate
**Target Buyer:** Employer health benefits director, occupational health company (Concentra, CareHere, Marathon Health), or on-site clinic manager
**Automation Potential:** High — OSHA injury reporting, drug testing workflows, return-to-work assessments, and employer-direct billing are all process-heavy and error-prone

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Employer on-site and near-site clinics operate in a fundamentally different model from walk-in urgent care. The patient is an employee; the payer is the employer (not an insurance company); the visit types include workers' comp injuries, OSHA-reportable incidents, drug/alcohol testing, DOT physicals, return-to-work assessments, and employer-funded primary care — a hybrid scope that no standard UC platform handles natively. Billing is employer-direct (invoiced monthly, not claims-based), compliance requirements are OSHA-driven (not CMS-driven), and outcomes reporting goes to the employer's HR and safety teams, not to the patient's PCP. The clinical workflow, billing model, compliance framework, and reporting requirements are all distinct from retail urgent care.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Occupational health platforms (Net Health, OccuMed, Agility) handle OSHA tracking and workers' comp but lack urgent care clinical workflow (triage, walk-in management, procedure documentation). UC EHRs (Experity) handle clinical workflow but have no OSHA reporting, employer-direct billing, or return-to-work assessment modules. CareHere and Marathon Health have built proprietary platforms for their own clinic networks but these are not available to independent operators. The result is dual-system operation: one for clinical documentation and another for occupational health compliance, with manual data bridging between them.
