## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Pediatric urgent care is clinically and operationally distinct from adult UC in every dimension. Clinically: vital sign normal ranges vary by age (a heart rate of 140 is alarming in an adult, normal in a 1-year-old); medication dosing is weight-based (requiring calculation at every prescription); differential diagnoses differ (a febrile infant under 90 days requires a sepsis workup that would be unnecessary in an adult with the same fever); and procedures differ (pediatric IV access, age-appropriate splinting, behavioral management of frightened children). Operationally: the parent is the decision-maker and communicator, not the patient; CHIP and Medicaid reimbursement rates are lower; liability profiles differ (missed meningitis in a child carries extreme malpractice risk); and the environment must be child-friendly. No UC-specific EHR addresses these differences — providers must mentally apply pediatric clinical logic on top of adult-defaulting tools.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Experity's clinical templates, vital sign alerts, and dosing references default to adult parameters. Pediatric-specific EHRs (PCC, Office Practicum) were designed for primary care scheduling and well-child visits, not UC walk-in workflow with triage, rapid throughput, and same-day discharge. PM Pediatrics built a proprietary internal system but it is not available commercially. No platform provides age-normed vital sign alerting, weight-based dosing guardrails, pediatric-specific triage protocols, or parent-facing communication workflows out of the box for the UC setting.
