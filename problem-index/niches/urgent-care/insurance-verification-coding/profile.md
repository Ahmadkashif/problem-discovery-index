# Insurance Verification & Coding

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/urgent-care|Urgent Care]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $0.5-1B embedded (touches all UC segments)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Cross-cutting revenue cycle function
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — eligibility checking tools exist (Availity, Waystar) but are not integrated into the walk-in speed required by UC workflow, and coding remains a manual provider activity
**Target Buyer:** Front desk lead, billing manager, or revenue cycle director
**Automation Potential:** Very High — insurance eligibility verification and E&M coding are both structured, rule-based processes that currently consume significant staff and provider time with high error rates

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Urgent care's walk-in model creates two revenue cycle problems that scheduled-care settings do not face. First, insurance verification must happen at the point of arrival — the patient is standing at the front desk with no prior appointment, and the staff must verify coverage, co-pay, and deductible status in real time while the patient waits and other patients arrive behind them. There is no pre-visit eligibility check because there was no scheduled visit. Second, coding must happen at or near discharge — the provider documents the visit and selects the E&M level (99201-99215 or 99281-99285, depending on whether the UC bills under office or ED codes), and this decision directly determines reimbursement. Providers consistently under-code to avoid audit risk, systematically leaving 15-20% of legitimate revenue on the table.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Eligibility verification tools (Availity, Waystar, Experian Health) perform real-time eligibility checks but require manual data entry and return results in 30-90 seconds per patient — too slow for the UC walk-in pace where 3-4 patients may arrive simultaneously. Coding assistance tools (3M CodeAssist, Optum360) suggest CPT/ICD codes from documentation but are designed for post-visit coding review, not real-time UC workflow. Experity has built-in eligibility checking and coding suggestion, but front desk staff report eligibility checks frequently time out or return incomplete data, and providers find coding suggestions unreliable enough that they ignore them.
