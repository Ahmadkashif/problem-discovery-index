# FSVP Compliance Documentation for Customs Brokers

**Niche:** [[niches/customs-brokers/fda-regulated-food-cosmetics/profile|FDA-Regulated Food & Cosmetics Importers]]
**Industry:** [[industries/customs-brokers|Customs Brokers]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Food safety compliance platforms (ReposiTrak, TraceGains) manage FSVP for food manufacturers and retailers, but they're not designed for customs brokers who need to verify that their importer clients have valid FSVP documentation before filing FDA-regulated entries.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
FSVP (Foreign Supplier Verification Program) requires the importer of record to maintain documentation proving their foreign food suppliers meet US food safety standards: hazard analysis, supplier verification activities (audits, testing), and corrective action records. FDA inspects FSVP compliance by showing up at the importer's location and requesting records. But many small food importers don't understand FSVP obligations — they import food from overseas without supplier verification documentation. When FDA inspects, the importer has no records, receives a warning letter, and faces potential import refusal. The customs broker, who is in the best position to advise the importer about FSVP requirements at the time of first entry, typically doesn't address FSVP because it's outside the traditional brokerage scope.

## What Already Exists
ReposiTrak and TraceGains provide FSVP compliance management for food companies with internal compliance teams. These are designed for food manufacturers and retailers, not for customs brokers or their small importer clients who have no food safety expertise.

## The Customization Gap
A broker-facing FSVP compliance tool needs: (1) a simplified FSVP checklist triggered when a broker files a food entry for a new importer or new foreign supplier; (2) guidance for the importer on what documentation they need to maintain (in plain language, not regulatory jargon); (3) a document repository where the importer stores their FSVP records, accessible to the broker and FDA upon request; (4) FSVP expiration alerts (supplier audits must be refreshed periodically based on hazard analysis).

## Target Customer
Customs brokers serving small food importers ($500K-$10M annual food import value) who lack internal food safety compliance staff.

## Impact If Solved
Preventing a single FDA FSVP warning letter or import refusal saves the importer $10K-$100K in remediation costs, legal fees, and lost shipments. The broker who proactively addresses FSVP generates advisory fee revenue ($2K-$5K per client) and strengthens the client relationship.
