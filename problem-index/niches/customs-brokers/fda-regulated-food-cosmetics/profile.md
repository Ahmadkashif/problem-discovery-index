# FDA-Regulated Food & Cosmetics Importers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/customs-brokers|Customs Brokers]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $500-700M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~13%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — FDA prior notice filing is electronic (through PNSI or ACE), but FSVP (Foreign Supplier Verification Program) compliance, VQIP application, and FDA detention/refusal response are managed through email and paper records
**Target Buyer:** FDA prior notice specialist or food import compliance officer at customs brokerages or importers
**Automation Potential:** Medium — prior notice filing is automatable, but FDA admissibility analysis requires understanding product-specific regulations that change frequently

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Food, dietary supplement, and cosmetic imports require FDA clearance in addition to CBP clearance — a parallel regulatory process with its own filing requirements, inspection protocols, and detention/refusal authorities. FDA requires Prior Notice (PN) before food arrives at the US port, maintains a complex admissibility framework (some ingredients are banned, some require specific labeling, some trigger automatic detention from specific countries), and enforces FSVP — the importer's obligation to verify that foreign suppliers meet US food safety standards. The FSVP compliance burden is significant: the importer must maintain supplier audit records, hazard analyses, and corrective action documentation for every foreign food supplier. Customs brokers serving food importers need FDA-specific expertise that general customs knowledge doesn't provide.

## Current Tools & Gaps
FDA's PNSI (Prior Notice System Interface) handles electronic prior notice filing. ACE integrates FDA filing with CBP entry. ReposiTrak and TraceGains provide food safety compliance management. The gap is in connecting FDA compliance (prior notice, FSVP, detention/refusal status) with the customs clearance workflow — so the broker knows before filing whether the product, the manufacturer, or the country is flagged for FDA detention.
