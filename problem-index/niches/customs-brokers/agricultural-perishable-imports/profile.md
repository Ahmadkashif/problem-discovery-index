# Agricultural & Perishable Imports

## Profile
**Market Size:** $800M-1.2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~20%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — perishable import brokers use ACE for filing but USDA/APHIS coordination, fumigation scheduling, and cold treatment documentation are managed through phone calls, faxes, and separate government portals
**Target Buyer:** Perishable entry specialist or USDA-licensed customs broker
**Automation Potential:** Medium — USDA/APHIS admissibility verification and phytosanitary document management are automatable, but the regulatory complexity and time sensitivity of perishable imports limit full automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Agricultural and perishable imports (fresh produce, cut flowers, nuts, seeds, live plants) require clearance through both CBP and USDA/APHIS — a dual-agency process that adds complexity, time, and cost to every entry. USDA requires phytosanitary certificates from the origin country, may require cold treatment or fumigation before release, and maintains a complex admissibility matrix (not all products are admissible from all countries — Mexican avocados are admissible, Ecuadorian avocados are not). The time pressure is extreme: a container of roses from Colombia that sits at the port for 2 extra days waiting for USDA clearance loses 30-40% of its shelf life and value. Perishable import brokers develop deep expertise in USDA admissibility rules, port-specific USDA staffing patterns, and which fumigation facilities process fastest.

## Current Tools & Gaps
ACE handles entry filing. USDA's ACIS (Automated Commercial Import System) handles USDA entry and inspection tracking. These systems don't communicate seamlessly — the broker files in ACE and separately tracks USDA clearance in ACIS or by phone. No tool provides an integrated CBP + USDA clearance workflow with admissibility pre-verification and USDA inspection scheduling coordination.
