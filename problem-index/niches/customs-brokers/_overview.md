## Why These Niches

Customs brokerage fragments along commodity type (consumer goods vs. agricultural vs. industrial), regulatory complexity (standard entry vs. FDA-regulated vs. USDA-regulated), importer sophistication (Fortune 500 with compliance teams vs. small e-commerce sellers with no import knowledge), and operational function (classification vs. entry filing vs. compliance advisory). These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (high-volume consumer goods and agricultural/perishable imports), two digitally neglected populations (small e-commerce importers with no customs knowledge and maquiladora operations with dual-country complexity), two underserved audiences (FDA-regulated importers navigating complex admissibility rules and artisan/fair-trade importers unfamiliar with customs requirements), and two highest-ROI automation targets (HTS classification workflow and ACE entry filing). Excluded: large freight forwarder integrated brokerage (different business model), military/government imports (specialized), and temporary imports/ATA Carnets (niche within niche).
