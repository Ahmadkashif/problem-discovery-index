# Foot Traffic Data for Food Truck Scale

**Niche:** [[niches/food-trucks/location-revenue-intelligence/profile|Location Revenue Intelligence]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Foot traffic intelligence platforms sell to retail chains and real estate developers for $10K-$50K/year — food truck operators need the same data at a $30/month price point and a mobile-first interface.
**Tags:** #regression #tabular-ml #data-integration #revenue-impact

## The Problem
Foot traffic data — how many people walk past a given location at a given time — is the strongest predictor of food truck revenue at that spot. This data exists: Placer.ai, SafeGraph (via Dewey), and Gravy Analytics sell aggregated mobile location data that shows foot traffic density by hour, day, and location. But these platforms are designed for enterprise customers (retailers, real estate firms) with $10K-$50K annual contracts, complex dashboards, and API-first delivery. A food truck operator needs "how busy will this corner be at lunch today?" — not a data warehouse.

## What Already Exists
Placer.ai provides foot traffic analytics for retail locations. SafeGraph (via Dewey) offers point-of-interest visit data. Google Popular Times shows historical busyness patterns for listed businesses. All require enterprise pricing, technical sophistication, or only cover listed businesses (not arbitrary street corners where trucks park).

## The Customization Gap
Food truck operators need a mobile-first interface that shows foot traffic for their specific candidate locations (which may be parking lots, street corners, or park entrances — not listed businesses) at a $20-$40/month price point. The system must translate raw foot traffic counts into revenue estimates calibrated to the operator's own sales history at each location. It needs to handle arbitrary GPS coordinates, not just business addresses, and show hourly patterns for the upcoming day.

## Target Customer
Operators who currently rely on gut feel for location selection and would use foot traffic data if it were affordable and simple — representing 80%+ of the ~35,000 active food trucks in the US.

## Impact If Solved
Even modest location improvement (10-15% better daily location choices) adds $3K-$8K in annual revenue per truck. At scale across 10,000+ trucks, this is a $30M-$80M addressable market at $30-$50/month per truck.
