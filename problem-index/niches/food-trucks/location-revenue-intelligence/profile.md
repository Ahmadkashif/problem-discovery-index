# Location Revenue Intelligence

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $300-400M (embedded value across all food truck operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22% (revenue attributable to location selection quality)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — location decisions are made by gut feel, word-of-mouth, and personal experience with no data tools
**Target Buyer:** Operator choosing daily parking locations based on years of accumulated intuition rather than data
**Automation Potential:** High — location-revenue correlation is strong, features are measurable (weather, foot traffic, events, day-of-week), and the decision is made daily

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Location selection is the single highest-leverage decision a food truck operator makes: the same truck, same menu, same operator can gross $200 or $2,000 depending on where they park. This is fundamentally a prediction problem — experienced operators build a mental model that integrates weather, events, day-of-week, competitor positions, and foot traffic into a location choice, but this model takes 2-3 years to build and is non-transferable. The niche is distinct because it is purely a data and prediction problem, not an operational or regulatory one.

## Current Tools & Gaps
No food-truck-specific location intelligence product exists. Operators use Google Maps for navigation, Waze for traffic, and social media to check competitor positions. Some check event calendars manually. Foot traffic data (Placer.ai, SafeGraph) is available but not packaged for food truck operators. The gap is a prediction layer that combines these signals into a daily recommendation.
