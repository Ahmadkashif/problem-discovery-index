# Prep Waste & Inventory Optimization

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $150-200M (embedded cost across food truck operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~12% (operators where waste exceeds 15% of food cost)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — prep quantities decided by gut feel, waste tracked by dumpster volume rather than weight or dollar value
**Target Buyer:** Owner-operator losing 10-20% of food purchases to over-prep, spoilage, and menu mismatch
**Automation Potential:** High — prep decisions follow demand patterns that are learnable from POS and weather data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Food trucks cannot store surplus food the way a restaurant can. There is no walk-in cooler, no next-day lunch service to absorb yesterday's over-prep, and no catering arm to redirect excess inventory. What is prepped and not sold goes in the trash at the end of the shift. The constraint is physical: truck refrigeration holds 1-2 days of inventory at most, and many perishable items (sliced vegetables, marinated proteins, fresh sauces) have a 4-8 hour service life once prepped. This makes prep accuracy the single highest-leverage variable in food truck profitability after location selection.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Restaurant inventory tools (MarketMan, BlueCart) are designed for fixed kitchens with stable daily volume and weekly ordering cycles. They do not account for the food truck reality: variable location, variable demand, no storage buffer, and prep done at a commissary 3-6 hours before service begins (when demand is still uncertain). No tool connects POS data to prep planning specifically for the mobile food context.
