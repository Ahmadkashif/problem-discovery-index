# Niche Analysis — Food Trucks

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]

## Niche Selection

Applied four-axis niche discovery: Market Share, Digital Adoption, Underserved Audience, Automation Potential.

| # | Niche | Category | Est. Market Size | Digital Adoption | Target Buyer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Festival & Event Circuit Trucks | High Market Share | $500-600M | Medium | Operator running 80-120 event days/year on the festival circuit |
| 2 | Weekday Office Lunch Route Trucks | High Market Share | $400-500M | Medium | Operator serving 3-5 office park/business district locations on a weekly rotation |
| 3 | Late-Night Bar District Trucks | Low Digitized | $150-200M | Low | Operator serving 10 PM-3 AM outside bars and entertainment venues |
| 4 | Rural & Underserved Area Trucks | Low Digitized | $100-150M | Low | Operator serving small towns, food deserts, or areas without restaurant options |
| 5 | Multi-Jurisdiction Permitting Operations | Underserved Audience | $200-300M (embedded) | Low | Operator working across 3-8 cities/counties with different permit requirements |
| 6 | Prep Waste & Inventory Optimization | Underserved Audience | $150-200M (embedded) | Low | Owner-operator losing 10-20% of food purchases to over-prep waste |
| 7 | Location Revenue Intelligence | Highly Automatable | $300-400M (embedded) | Low | Operator choosing daily parking locations based on years of gut feel |
| 8 | Owner-Operator Admin Automation | Highly Automatable | $250-350M (embedded) | Low | Solo operator spending 15-20 hours/week on non-cooking tasks |

## Why These Niches

Food trucks fragment along operating model (event circuit vs. fixed route vs. late-night), geography (urban vs. rural vs. mobile), and business function (location selection vs. permitting vs. operations). These 8 niches cover the two largest revenue segments (festival circuit and office lunch routes, together representing 60% of food truck revenue), the two most digitally neglected segments (late-night operators with no analytics and rural trucks with no tech infrastructure), the two most underserved populations (multi-jurisdiction operators drowning in permit paperwork and waste-plagued operators with no inventory tools), and the two highest-ROI automation targets (location intelligence and administrative automation, both rule-heavy and currently manual). Excluded: corporate catering trucks (covered under catering), commissary-based ghost kitchen trucks (different model), and ice cream/dessert trucks (distinct seasonal pattern).
