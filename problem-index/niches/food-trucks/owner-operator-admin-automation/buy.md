# Food Truck Bookkeeping Automation

**Niche:** [[niches/food-trucks/owner-operator-admin-automation/profile|Owner-Operator Admin Automation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks and Wave handle bookkeeping for any small business — but they do not auto-categorize food truck expenses, calculate per-event P&L, or handle the cash-heavy, multi-location, commissary-based cost structure unique to mobile food.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win #tabular-ml

## The Problem
Food truck bookkeeping is uniquely complex for a micro-business: revenue comes from card and cash across multiple locations, food costs are split between wholesale suppliers and retail emergency purchases, commissary rent is a fixed cost, fuel is both a personal and business expense, event vendor fees vary by event, and health permit fees vary by jurisdiction. Most operators stuff receipts in a bag and hand them to a tax preparer once a year — resulting in missed deductions, inaccurate food cost tracking, and quarterly tax surprises.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks Self-Employed and Wave offer free or low-cost bookkeeping for small businesses. They handle bank feed categorization, expense tracking, and basic reporting. Bench and Pilot offer bookkeeping-as-a-service. All are general-purpose and require the operator to manually categorize food truck expenses into the right accounts.

## The Customization Gap
A food-truck-specific bookkeeping layer needs pre-built categories (food cost by supplier, commissary rent, event vendor fees by event, fuel by route, permit fees by jurisdiction), auto-categorization rules trained on food truck transactions ("Sysco = food cost, Shell = fuel, City of Dallas = permit fee"), per-event P&L calculation (matching event-day revenue to event-specific costs), and food cost percentage tracking as a real-time KPI. Integration with Square POS should auto-import revenue split by location and payment type (cash vs. card).

## Target Customer
Solo operators who currently do no bookkeeping during the year and face a chaotic, expensive tax season — or who pay $200-$400/month for a general bookkeeper who does not understand food truck economics.

## Impact If Solved
Automated food truck bookkeeping saves 3-5 hours per week of receipt sorting and manual entry, catches $2K-$5K in missed tax deductions annually, and provides real-time food cost percentage tracking that directly informs pricing and prep decisions.
