# Owner-Operator Admin Automation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $250-350M (embedded time cost across food truck operations)
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18% (administrative burden as share of operator value)
**Digital Adoption:** Low — bookkeeping in shoeboxes, scheduling via text message, social media done ad hoc between prep and service
**Target Buyer:** Solo operator spending 15-20 hours per week on non-cooking tasks: bookkeeping, social media, scheduling, tax prep, permit management
**Automation Potential:** High — most admin tasks are repetitive, rule-based, and low-judgment, making them ideal automation candidates

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Food truck owner-operators are the most compressed small business role in food service: one person serves as chef, driver, cashier, marketer, bookkeeper, permit manager, and maintenance coordinator. Unlike a restaurant owner who delegates bookkeeping to a manager and social media to a staffer, the food truck operator does everything. The 15-20 hours per week spent on admin tasks directly compete with sleep, family time, and the cooking work that generates revenue. This is not a technology adoption problem — it is a bandwidth problem. Operators do not lack tools; they lack the hours to use them.

## Current Tools & Gaps
QuickBooks or Wave for basic bookkeeping (if used at all — many use shoeboxes of receipts). Instagram/TikTok for marketing (posted inconsistently). Text messages for scheduling staff and communicating with event organizers. Google Sheets or paper for menu planning. No integrated system connects these workflows, and no product understands that the operator has 10 minutes between prep and service to handle all admin work.
