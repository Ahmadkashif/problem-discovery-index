# Festival & Event Circuit Trucks

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $500-600M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Square POS universal, social media active, but event selection, pricing, and inventory planning are manual
**Target Buyer:** Operator running 80-120 event days per year on the festival, fair, and concert circuit
**Automation Potential:** Medium — events are high-variance but patterns exist across event types, sizes, and demographics

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Festival circuit trucks are a different business from daily-route trucks: revenue is concentrated in 80-120 high-intensity days rather than 250 moderate days. A single festival can generate $5K-$20K in revenue over a weekend — or $500 if the event is poorly attended, the weather is bad, or the operator mispriced. Event selection is the highest-leverage decision: choosing which festivals to apply for, what vendor fee to pay ($500-$5,000 per event), and how much inventory to bring. Experienced circuit operators develop tacit knowledge about which events are worth the fee and travel, but this knowledge takes 3-5 seasons to build.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Square POS tracks sales and basic analytics. Event listing sites (Roaming Hunger, FoodTruckEmpire) list upcoming events. But no tool helps operators evaluate event ROI before committing — estimating expected revenue against vendor fees, travel costs, and inventory investment. Post-event P&L is calculated manually, if at all. Event application and scheduling are managed through email and spreadsheets.
