# Automated Permit Renewal & Deadline Management

**Niche:** [[niches/food-trucks/multi-jurisdiction-permitting/profile|Multi-Jurisdiction Permitting Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Calendar and task management tools can track deadlines — but none understand that a food truck in the DFW metro has 22 permits across 6 cities with different renewal windows, fee amounts, and inspection prerequisites.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #compliance #quick-win

## The Problem
A multi-jurisdiction operator holds 15-25 active permits at any time, each with different renewal dates (annual, biannual, quarterly), inspection prerequisites (must pass health inspection before renewal), and fee amounts ($25-$500 each). Missing a single renewal triggers a lapsed-permit violation — a $200-$1,000 fine or an on-site shutdown. Operators currently track renewals with a mix of Google Calendar reminders, sticky notes, and memory. One missed reminder during a busy festival season means a lapse.

## What Already Exists
Project management tools (Asana, Trello), calendar apps (Google Calendar), and compliance platforms (Avalara for tax, Gusto for employment). License management tools like Harbor Compliance and LicenseLogic serve large enterprises. None are designed for food truck permitting complexity, where a single operator has 20+ permits across multiple municipal systems.

## The Customization Gap
The system needs to understand permit dependency chains (inspection must pass before renewal can be submitted), jurisdiction-specific lead times (Dallas processes in 3 days, Fort Worth takes 3 weeks), fee tracking with payment confirmation, and document management (storing copies of each permit for roadside verification). Auto-generated renewal checklists per jurisdiction — "Your Arlington health permit expires March 15, inspection required by Feb 15, fee $175, submit here" — transform a chaotic process into a guided workflow.

## Target Customer
Multi-jurisdiction operators managing 15-25 permits who spend 3-5 hours per month on renewal tracking and have been fined at least once for a lapsed permit.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminating lapsed-permit fines ($1K-$3K/year) and reducing renewal administration from 5 hours to 1 hour per month. The larger impact is preventing a shutdown during a peak revenue day — a single mid-service shutdown at a festival can cost $2K-$5K in lost sales.
