# Customer Pre-Order & Arrival Coordination

**Niche:** [[niches/food-trucks/weekday-office-lunch-route/profile|Weekday Office Lunch Route Trucks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Restaurant pre-order apps exist everywhere — but none handle a kitchen that moves between locations, has a 3-hour window, and serves a different customer base at each stop.
**Tags:** #recommendation #automation #quick-win #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Office workers have a 30-60 minute lunch window and will not wait 15 minutes in a food truck line. Pre-ordering solves this, but route trucks present a unique challenge: the truck is at Location A on Monday, Location B on Tuesday, and Location C on Wednesday. Customers at each location need to know the truck is coming, place orders ahead of arrival, and receive a pickup time — all coordinated around the truck's travel schedule between stops.

## What Already Exists
Toast, Square Online, and ChowNow offer pre-ordering and online ordering for restaurants. DoorDash and Uber Eats handle delivery. Roaming Hunger lists food truck locations. These platforms assume a fixed address or handle delivery logistics — they do not support a business that changes location daily and needs per-stop order windows tied to an arrival schedule.

## The Customization Gap
The system needs location-aware order windows: "Pre-order for Tuesday at Meridian Office Park, truck arrives 11:15 AM, pickup window 11:30-12:30." It must auto-adjust if the truck is running late (GPS-based ETA updates), cap orders per stop based on prep capacity and service time, and let recurring customers at each stop save favorites. Push notifications must be location-specific ("Your Tuesday truck is 10 minutes away — your order is confirmed").

## Target Customer
Route operators with 100+ regular customers across 3-5 stops who lose sales to long wait times and unpredictable arrival schedules.

## Impact If Solved
Pre-ordering can increase per-stop revenue 20-35% by reducing line abandonment and enabling accurate prep. For a truck grossing $1,200/day across stops, that is an additional $60K-$100K annually.
