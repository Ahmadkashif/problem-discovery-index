# Weekday Office Lunch Route Trucks

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $400-500M
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~28%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — POS and social media adopted, but route planning, demand forecasting, and customer pre-ordering are manual
**Target Buyer:** Operator serving 3-5 office park or business district locations on a weekly rotation
**Automation Potential:** High — routes are repetitive, demand patterns are day-of-week stable, and customer bases are recurring

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Office lunch route trucks operate the most predictable food truck model: the same 3-5 locations on the same days each week, serving the same customer base of office workers between 11 AM and 2 PM. This predictability creates a massive optimization opportunity — route trucks can forecast demand by location and day-of-week far more accurately than event or late-night trucks. However, the niche faces a specific threat: return-to-office policy changes can wipe out a stop overnight when a company shifts to hybrid or fully remote, and operators have no early warning system.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Square/Clover POS handles payments. Google Maps or Waze handles routing. Some operators use Instagram to announce daily locations. No tool integrates customer pre-ordering (to reduce wait times and improve prep accuracy), monitors office occupancy trends at each stop, or optimizes route ordering to minimize drive time while maximizing revenue per location.
