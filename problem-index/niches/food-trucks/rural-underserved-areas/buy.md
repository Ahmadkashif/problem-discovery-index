# SMS-Based Pre-Order & Arrival Alerts

**Niche:** [[niches/food-trucks/rural-underserved-areas/profile|Rural & Underserved Area Trucks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-trucks|Food Trucks]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Pre-order apps assume smartphones and data connections — rural food truck customers often have flip phones, 2G signal, and no app store, making SMS the only reliable channel.
**Tags:** #automation #quick-win #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Rural food truck customers are often elderly residents, farmworkers, or small-town populations with limited smartphone adoption. They rely on the truck arriving on schedule and having their preferred items available. When the truck is delayed, diverted, or out of stock, customers drive 10-20 minutes to the stop location and find nothing — eroding the trust that sustains the entire route. Operators want to notify customers and take pre-orders, but Instagram and app-based ordering do not reach this demographic.

## What Already Exists
Twilio-based SMS marketing tools (SimpleTexting, SlickText) handle bulk messaging. Pre-order platforms (Toast, Square Online) support online ordering. Both are designed for smartphone users with internet access and app capability. Neither handles two-way SMS ordering ("Reply 1 for BBQ plate, 2 for chicken, 3 for sides") or integrates SMS communication with route scheduling.

## The Customization Gap
The system must support keyword-based SMS ordering ("Text LUNCH to 55555 to see today's menu and reply with your order"), two-way confirmation, arrival ETA alerts based on the truck's GPS position, and simple text-based menu updates. It needs to handle customers with flip phones who cannot click links, view images, or install apps. Order aggregation per stop must feed back into prep planning so the operator knows how many orders are waiting before arriving.

## Target Customer
Rural operators serving 5-10 stops per route with 10-30 regular customers per stop, most of whom do not use smartphone apps.

## Impact If Solved
Pre-orders via SMS reduce no-shows (customers arriving when the truck is not there) by 60-70% and increase per-stop revenue 15-25% by ensuring customers come when the truck arrives. Operators save 1-2 hours per day on phone calls and Facebook posts currently used for scheduling communication.
