# Post-Surfside Structural Inspection Compliance Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/high-rise-condo-associations/profile|High-Rise Condo Associations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** After the 2021 Surfside condo collapse, Florida and other states enacted mandatory structural inspection requirements for aging high-rise condos — creating a new compliance obligation (milestone inspections, structural integrity reserve studies, remediation tracking) that no HOA management platform supports, forcing managers to track compliance through emails and spreadsheets.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Florida's SB 4-D (2022) requires condos 30+ years old (25+ years in coastal zones) to complete milestone structural inspections and fully fund structural reserves — with no more reserve fund waiver votes. Other states (California, Colorado, Maryland) are enacting similar legislation. A management company overseeing 20 high-rise condos must track: which buildings require milestone inspections and by what deadline, structural inspection status and findings, remediation plan tracking for identified deficiencies, structural integrity reserve study completion and funding adequacy, and annual reporting to the state. Missing a compliance deadline can result in fines, building condemnation orders, and board member personal liability.

## Why It's Still Broken
Post-Surfside legislation is new (2022-2025), and HOA management platforms haven't added structural inspection compliance modules. Each state has different requirements (Florida's milestone inspection is different from California's SB 326 balcony inspection). Management companies are tracking compliance in spreadsheets created by their compliance officers — one-off solutions that don't scale, don't send automated reminders, and don't connect structural findings to reserve funding requirements.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A structural compliance tracking module that: inventories each managed building's age, location, and applicable structural inspection requirements by state/municipality; generates a compliance calendar with inspection deadlines; tracks inspection status (not started, scheduled, completed) with findings categorization (no deficiencies, minor, major, critical); links inspection findings to remediation work orders with completion tracking; and connects structural reserve funding requirements to the association's financial management. Integration with Vantaca/AppFolio HOA for financial tracking.

## Who Feels the Pain
Community managers at management companies with high-rise condo portfolios in states with structural inspection mandates — approximately 5,000-8,000 buildings nationally (growing as more states enact legislation).

## Impact If Fixed
Compliance deadline management drops from manual calendar tracking to automated alerts. Missing a milestone inspection deadline (which can trigger building condemnation) is prevented. Board members have clear visibility into structural compliance status — reducing personal liability anxiety that is driving board member resignations across Florida.
