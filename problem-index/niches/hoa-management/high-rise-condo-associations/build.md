# Building Systems Lifecycle Management for Condo Associations

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/high-rise-condo-associations/profile|High-Rise Condo Associations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the complete lifecycle of high-rise condo building systems — from installation through maintenance scheduling to replacement planning and reserve funding — leaving building engineers and condo managers to track $2-5M in critical infrastructure through spreadsheets and institutional memory.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
A 30-story condo building has 150+ building system components with different maintenance schedules and replacement timelines: 3 elevators (annual inspection, 25-year replacement at $150K each), HVAC chiller (quarterly maintenance, 20-year replacement at $300K), fire pump (annual testing, 15-year replacement at $80K), roof membrane (annual inspection, 20-year replacement at $500K), facade waterproofing (5-year inspection, 30-year replacement at $1M+), and dozens more. The building engineer maintains these schedules mentally and in spreadsheets. When the engineer leaves, the institutional knowledge goes with them — the new engineer discovers deferred maintenance and upcoming replacement needs through emergency failures rather than proactive tracking.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Facility management systems (CMMS platforms like eMaint, Hippo, Fiix) are designed for commercial and industrial buildings managed by professional facility management companies. Condo associations need a simpler, board-friendly interface that connects maintenance scheduling to reserve funding (the financial planning that pays for replacements). CMMS platforms don't integrate with HOA financial management, and HOA platforms don't handle building systems maintenance. The gap is the integration layer between physical building management and condo financial planning.

## What to Build
A building systems lifecycle platform that: maintains a component inventory with installation date, expected useful life, replacement cost, and maintenance schedule; auto-generates maintenance work orders based on schedules; tracks actual vs. scheduled maintenance with deferred maintenance alerts; connects component replacement timelines to the reserve study (showing which components will need replacement in which years and whether reserve funding is adequate); and provides board-friendly dashboards showing building condition and funding adequacy.

## Target Customer
Condo association managers and building engineers at high-rise buildings with 100+ units — approximately 15,000-20,000 buildings nationally. Average contract value: $200-$500/month.

## Impact If Built
Deferred maintenance is detected before failure — each prevented emergency repair saves $10,000-$50,000 vs. planned replacement. Reserve funding adequacy is continuously monitored — preventing special assessments that surprise unit owners. Building engineer transition risk drops dramatically because institutional knowledge is in the system, not in one person's head.
