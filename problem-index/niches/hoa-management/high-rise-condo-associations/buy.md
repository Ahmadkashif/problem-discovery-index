# Condo-Specific Reserve Component Tracking from CMMS

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/high-rise-condo-associations/profile|High-Rise Condo Associations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CMMS platforms like eMaint and Hippo track maintenance work orders for commercial buildings, but condo associations need reserve component tracking linked to financial planning — knowing that the elevator replacement in 2030 will cost $450K and whether current reserve contributions will cover it — a financial integration that no CMMS provides.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
A condo association's reserve study identifies 80-120 components with different remaining useful lives and replacement costs. The building engineer performs maintenance that extends or shortens component life. But the reserve study is a static PDF updated every 3-5 years — it doesn't reflect the actual condition of components based on recent maintenance history. The elevator reserve study shows 10 years remaining useful life, but the engineer knows the motor is struggling and it needs replacement in 5 years. This disconnect between maintenance reality and financial planning produces underfunded reserves — the #1 cause of special assessments that devastate unit owner finances.

## What Already Exists
eMaint, Hippo, and Fiix provide CMMS functionality (work order management, preventive maintenance scheduling, asset tracking). Reserve study firms (Association Reserves, Reserve Advisors) produce PDF reserve studies with 30-year funding projections. HOA management platforms (Vantaca, AppFolio HOA) manage financial reporting and assessment collection. None connect: maintenance activity in the CMMS → updated component condition → adjusted remaining useful life → updated reserve funding requirement.

## The Customization Gap
A condo reserve component tracking tool needs to: (1) import the reserve study's component inventory as a baseline; (2) link each component to maintenance work orders in the CMMS (or built-in maintenance tracking); (3) allow the building engineer to update component condition assessments that adjust remaining useful life; (4) automatically recalculate reserve funding adequacy when component life estimates change; (5) alert the board and manager when a component's revised replacement timeline creates a funding shortfall.

## Target Customer
Condo association managers and boards at buildings with $1M+ in reserve funds — approximately 15,000-20,000 buildings nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Reserve funding accuracy improves from static 3-5 year snapshots to continuously updated projections. Special assessment surprises decrease because component replacement timelines are adjusted in real-time based on maintenance reality. Board decision-making improves — they see the financial impact of deferring maintenance vs. performing it on schedule.
