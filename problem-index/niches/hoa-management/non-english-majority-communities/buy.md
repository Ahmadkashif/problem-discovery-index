# Bilingual Resident Communication Portal

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/non-english-majority-communities/profile|Non-English Majority Communities]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** TownSq and AppFolio HOA provide resident portals with communication features, board meeting notices, and document libraries — entirely in English — making them unusable for 50-80% of residents in non-English majority communities.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A community manager posts a board meeting notice on the resident portal: "Annual Meeting — January 15, 7:00 PM, Clubhouse. Agenda: 2027 budget approval, reserve study update, board election." For English-speaking residents, this is straightforward. For the 60% of residents in this community who speak Spanish as their primary language, the portal notice is meaningless — they may not attend the meeting, may not vote on the budget, and may not run for the board. The manager must manually translate every portal post, email, and notice — or rely on bilingual board members to informally translate during meetings. Every communication becomes a dual workload.

## What Already Exists
TownSq, AppFolio HOA, and Vantaca provide English-language resident portals with communication, payment, and document features. Google Translate browser extension can auto-translate web content but produces inconsistent results and requires the resident to know how to activate it. No HOA portal provides built-in multilingual content presentation.

## The Customization Gap
A bilingual resident portal needs: (1) automatic translation of all manager-posted content into the community's primary non-English language (LLM-powered, with HOA-specific terminology handling); (2) resident language preference settings that control the portal display language; (3) bilingual document viewing (English and translated versions of uploaded documents); (4) translated notification templates for standard communications (meeting notices, assessment reminders, maintenance alerts); (5) bilingual inquiry submission and response (resident writes in their language, manager receives in English, responds in English, resident receives in their language).

## Target Customer
Management companies serving non-English majority communities — approximately 5,000-8,000 communities where portal adoption is currently suppressed by language barriers.

## Impact If Solved
Resident portal adoption in non-English majority communities increases from 20-30% to 60-70% (matching English-dominant community adoption rates). Board meeting attendance increases 30-40% when notices are in the residents' language. Assessment payment timeliness improves because payment reminders are understood.
