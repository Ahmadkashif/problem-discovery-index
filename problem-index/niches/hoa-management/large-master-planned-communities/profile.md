# Large Master-Planned Communities

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~30-35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — Vantaca or AppFolio HOA for management, resident portals for payments and communications, but amenity management, common area maintenance scheduling, and architectural review are often manual
**Target Buyer:** Community association manager (CAM) or regional director at a professional management company overseeing master-planned communities with 500-5,000+ units
**Automation Potential:** High — architectural review workflow, amenity reservation management, common area maintenance scheduling, and resident communication are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Master-planned communities are large, amenity-rich developments with multiple sub-associations, shared common areas (pools, clubhouses, parks, fitness centers, trails), and complex governance structures (a master association plus 3-10 sub-associations, each with separate budgets and boards). The management complexity scales with size: a 2,000-unit master-planned community has 4-6 sub-association boards (each meeting monthly), a master association board, shared amenity scheduling across sub-associations, architectural review for all exterior modifications, and 3-5 full-time staff plus vendors for common area maintenance. The community manager juggles multiple governance bodies, each with different CC&Rs, budgets, and enforcement standards.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Vantaca and AppFolio HOA handle financial management, work orders, and resident portals. No platform manages the master-planned-specific workflows: multi-association governance coordination (scheduling and managing 5-8 board meetings per month), architectural review committee (ARC) workflow with application tracking and compliance verification, and shared amenity management across sub-associations with usage allocation and cost sharing.
