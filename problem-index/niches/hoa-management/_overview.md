## Why These Niches

HOA management fragments along community type (master-planned subdivision vs. high-rise condo vs. age-restricted), management model (professionally managed vs. self-managed volunteer boards), resident demographics (English-speaking vs. non-English majority, mixed residential-commercial), and operational function (enforcement workflows vs. capital reserve planning). These 8 niches cover the two largest professionally-managed community types (master-planned communities with complex amenity management and high-rise condos with building systems), the two most digitally neglected segments (self-managed small HOAs run on spreadsheets and age-restricted communities with low tech adoption), two underserved populations (non-English majority communities lacking translated governance tools and mixed-use associations needing commercial-residential hybrid management), and two high-volume operational workflows where rule-based automation has clear ROI (CC&R violation enforcement and reserve study capital planning). Excluded: timeshare associations (distinct regulatory framework), commercial-only property owners associations (overlap with CRE), and national management company corporate operations (different buyer).
