# Simplified Assessment Collection for Small HOAs

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/self-managed-small-hoas/profile|Self-Managed Small HOAs]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Payment platforms like Zelle, Venmo, and even QuickBooks Online handle money transfers, but none are designed for HOA assessment collection — tracking who has paid their quarterly assessment, sending automated late notices, generating delinquency reports for board meetings, and depositing to a community bank account rather than a personal one.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A volunteer treasurer at a 40-unit HOA collects $200/quarter assessments by check. Each quarter: mail 40 invoices, receive 30-35 checks over 3 weeks, deposit checks at the bank, manually record each payment in Excel, identify the 5-10 units that haven't paid by the due date, mail late notices, and follow up by phone. The treasurer spends 4-8 hours per quarter on assessment collection alone — the single largest time sink in self-managed HOA governance. Some communities have shifted to Zelle or Venmo, but these don't provide: payment tracking by unit, automatic late notice generation, delinquency reporting, or community (non-personal) bank account integration.

## What Already Exists
Zelle, Venmo, and PayPal handle peer-to-peer payments. QuickBooks Online handles invoicing and payment tracking. PayHOA provides online payment for HOAs. All require setup complexity beyond what most volunteer treasurers will complete, and none provide HOA-specific features (assessment tracking by unit, late notice automation, delinquency reporting formatted for board meetings).

## The Customization Gap
A small-HOA payment tool needs to: (1) send quarterly assessment invoices to all unit owners by email/text with a one-click payment link; (2) accept ACH, credit card, or check payments and record automatically to the unit's ledger; (3) send automated late notices at 15 and 30 days past due; (4) generate a delinquency report formatted for board meeting review; (5) integrate with the community's bank account (not a personal account). Setup must take under 15 minutes — enter unit addresses and owner emails, set assessment amount, connect bank account.

## Target Customer
Volunteer treasurers at self-managed HOAs — approximately 150,000-200,000 communities nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Assessment collection time drops from 4-8 hours per quarter to 30-60 minutes. Late payment rates decrease 20-30% because online payment removes friction (no stamp, no check writing). Cash handling (holding a stack of checks from neighbors) is eliminated — reducing the interpersonal awkwardness and fraud risk inherent in volunteer-managed community finances.
