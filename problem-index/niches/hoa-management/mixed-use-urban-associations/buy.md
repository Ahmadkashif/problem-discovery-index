# Dual Assessment Structure Management

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/mixed-use-urban-associations/profile|Mixed-Use Urban Associations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** HOA management platforms like Vantaca and AppFolio HOA model a single assessment structure per community, but mixed-use associations often have separate residential and commercial assessment rates, different assessment schedules, and different delinquency procedures — requiring the manager to maintain two parallel financial tracking systems within a single association.
**Tags:** #automation #data-integration #compliance

## The Problem
In a mixed-use association, commercial units may be assessed differently than residential: higher per-sq-ft rate (reflecting higher common area usage), monthly billing (vs. quarterly for residential), different late fee and collection procedures (commercial leases have different enforcement mechanisms than residential), and separate budget line items for commercial-only expenses (loading dock maintenance, commercial signage). The HOA management platform treats all units identically — the manager must manually override assessment calculations for commercial units, track commercial delinquency separately, and generate split financial reports for the board (commercial operations vs. residential operations).

## What Already Exists
Vantaca and AppFolio HOA manage assessment billing, collections, and financial reporting for residential associations. Yardi and AppFolio Property Manager manage commercial tenant billing. No platform handles both in a single association with dual assessment structures.

## The Customization Gap
A mixed-use association management tool needs: (1) unit-type classification (residential vs. commercial) with different assessment rates per type; (2) configurable billing schedules by unit type (monthly for commercial, quarterly for residential); (3) separate delinquency workflows by unit type (commercial follows lease enforcement; residential follows CC&R collection procedure); (4) split financial reporting (commercial operations, residential operations, shared operations); (5) budget modeling with dual assessment rate scenarios.

## Target Customer
Managers at mixed-use associations with 5+ commercial units — approximately 8,000-12,000 associations nationally.

## Impact If Solved
Manual override and parallel tracking time drops from 3-5 hours per month to near zero. Financial reporting accuracy improves (no more combining commercial and residential financials that obscure the true cost allocation). Budget season preparation drops from 8-12 hours to 3-4 hours because dual assessment scenarios generate automatically.
