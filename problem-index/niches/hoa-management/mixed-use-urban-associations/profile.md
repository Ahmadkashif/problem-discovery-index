# Mixed-Use Urban Associations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $600M-1B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~6-8%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — residential management tools used for the residential component, but commercial tenant coordination, shared utility allocation, and dual-governance structures are managed manually
**Target Buyer:** Association manager at a mixed-use development (ground-floor retail/commercial with residential condos or townhomes above)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — commercial-residential cost allocation, dual-use common area management, and commercial tenant coordination are automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Mixed-use associations manage developments with both residential units and commercial spaces — typically ground-floor retail or office with residential condos above. This creates unique challenges absent from purely residential HOAs: dual assessment structures (commercial owners pay different rates than residential), shared utility allocation (HVAC, water, electricity serving both commercial and residential with different usage patterns), parking management (commercial customers, residential residents, and commercial tenant employees competing for the same garage), noise and hours-of-operation conflicts between residential and commercial uses, and different governance interests (commercial owners want foot traffic and signage; residential owners want quiet and parking).

## Current Tools & Gaps
HOA management platforms model residential associations only. Commercial property management platforms (Yardi, AppFolio Property Manager) handle commercial tenants but not residential association governance. No platform manages the intersection: dual assessment structures, commercial-residential cost allocation for shared building systems, and governance that balances commercial and residential board member interests.
