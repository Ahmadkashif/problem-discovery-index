# Senior Community Activities and Social Programming Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/hoa-management/age-restricted-senior-communities/profile|Age-Restricted Senior Communities]]
**Industry:** [[industries/hoa-management|HOA Management]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the social programming workflow unique to active adult communities — scheduling activities (card games, fitness classes, day trips, holiday events), tracking participation to identify isolated residents, managing volunteer activity leaders, and coordinating transportation — the programming that residents cite as the primary reason for choosing age-restricted living.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing

## The Problem
A 300-unit active adult community offers 30-50 weekly activities: fitness classes, card groups, art classes, social hours, day trips, guest speakers, holiday events. The activities director (or community manager doubling as activities director) manages: scheduling and room/venue booking, instructor/volunteer coordination, resident sign-ups and waitlists, transportation for day trips, supply purchasing, and monthly activity calendar printing. This work takes 15-25 hours per week and is managed through a combination of paper sign-up sheets at the clubhouse, a printed monthly calendar, and personal phone calls to volunteers and instructors. More importantly, activity participation data could identify socially isolated residents — seniors who stop attending activities are at higher risk for health decline — but no one tracks participation systematically.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Recreation center management software (RecDesk, PerfectMind) serves municipal recreation departments and YMCAs — too complex and expensive for a single community's activities program. Community association platforms focus on governance, not social programming. The market (15,000-20,000 age-restricted communities nationally) is too small for general-purpose recreation software to customize, and the senior demographic requires a high-accessibility, low-complexity interface.

## What to Build
An activities management platform for senior communities: scheduling with room/venue booking, volunteer coordinator assignment, resident sign-up via multiple channels (online, phone-in, clubhouse kiosk), printed monthly calendar generation, day-trip transportation coordination, and — critically — participation tracking that flags residents whose attendance drops significantly (potential isolation indicator). The interface must be accessible: large text, simple navigation, phone-call sign-up option for residents who don't use computers.

## Target Customer
Activities directors and community managers at 55+ age-restricted communities with active social programming — approximately 10,000-15,000 communities nationally. Average contract value: $100-$300/month.

## Impact If Built
Activities coordination time drops from 15-25 hours/week to 5-10 hours. Participation tracking identifies isolated residents before health crises — potentially preventing 2-3 emergency situations per year per community through early social engagement intervention. Resident satisfaction with community programming improves through more organized scheduling and waitlist management.
