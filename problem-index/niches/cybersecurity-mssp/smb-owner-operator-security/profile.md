# SMB Owner-Operator Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cybersecurity-mssp|Cybersecurity MSSP]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 10%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — SMB owner-operators (sub-100 employees) rely on consumer-grade security (Windows Defender, free antivirus) and their MSP's basic monitoring; dedicated MSSP services are priced out of reach.
**Target Buyer:** MSSP founders targeting the SMB market, and MSPs looking to add security monitoring to their managed services offering.
**Automation Potential:** High — SMB security monitoring is less complex than enterprise (smaller attack surface, simpler networks), enabling highly standardized, automated service delivery.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
There are 30 million small businesses in the US, and fewer than 5% have any form of professional security monitoring. A 25-person accounting firm, a 50-person manufacturer, or a 10-person law firm faces the same ransomware and BEC (business email compromise) threats as enterprise companies, but with no security staff, no SIEM, and no incident response plan. When attacked, they call their MSP (if they have one), who is typically not equipped for security incident response. The economic reality is brutal: SMB security monitoring must cost $200-$1,500/month to be viable, a price point that no traditional MSSP can serve profitably with manual SOC operations.

## Current Tools & Gaps
MSPs like Datto, ConnectWise, and Kaseya provide basic endpoint protection and patch management but do not offer true security monitoring (alert triage, threat hunting, incident response). Enterprise MSSP services start at $5K-$15K/month. Some "SMB MSSP" offerings have emerged (Huntress, SentinelOne Vigilance, Arctic Wolf) but focus primarily on endpoint detection, not holistic security monitoring (email security, identity protection, cloud security, compliance). No comprehensive SMB MSSP service covers the full threat landscape at sub-$1,500/month pricing.
