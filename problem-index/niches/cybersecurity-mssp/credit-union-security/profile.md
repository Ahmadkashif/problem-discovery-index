# Credit Union & Community Bank Security

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/cybersecurity-mssp|Cybersecurity MSSP]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Credit unions use core banking platforms (Symitar, Corelation) with basic security features, but external security monitoring, vulnerability management, and NCUA/FFIEC compliance evidence collection are handled manually or outsourced piecemeal.
**Target Buyer:** MSSP account managers and sales teams targeting credit unions with $50M-$5B in assets that lack dedicated security staff.
**Automation Potential:** High — NCUA and FFIEC compliance requirements are well-defined, and credit union security monitoring follows predictable patterns around core banking, online banking, and card processing systems.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Credit unions face the same cybersecurity threats as large banks but operate with a fraction of the security budget. A $500M credit union might have one IT manager and no dedicated security staff. Yet they are subject to NCUA examination requirements (including cybersecurity assessments), FFIEC guidance (CAT — Cybersecurity Assessment Tool), and state-specific regulatory requirements. They process card transactions (PCI DSS compliance), offer online banking (web application security), and manage member PII (data protection). Their core banking systems (Symitar/PowerOn, Corelation KeyStone) are unique to the credit union industry, requiring MSSP analysts who understand these platforms. The MSSP service offering must be affordable on credit union budgets ($2K-$10K/month, not $50K+/month enterprise pricing).

## Current Tools & Gaps
Large financial institution MSSPs (Secureworks, Trustwave) price their services for banks with $50K+/month security budgets. Credit union service organizations (CUSOs) offer some shared security services but typically provide point solutions (phishing training, vulnerability scanning) rather than comprehensive monitoring. No MSSP offers a bundled credit union security service that covers SIEM monitoring, vulnerability management, NCUA/FFIEC compliance evidence, and incident response at credit union price points.
