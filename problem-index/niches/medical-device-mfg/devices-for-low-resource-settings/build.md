# Multi-Jurisdiction Regulatory Pathway Planner

**Niche:** [[niches/medical-device-mfg/devices-for-low-resource-settings/profile|Devices for Low-Resource Settings]]
**Industry:** [[industries/medical-device-mfg|Medical Device Manufacturing]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool maps the regulatory requirements across WHO prequalification, FDA, CE marking, and the 50+ national regulatory authorities in low- and middle-income countries — forcing global health device companies to hire country-specific regulatory consultants at $10-30K per country.
**Tags:** #large-language-models #text-generation #compliance #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A device intended for use in rural clinics in Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Latin America may need WHO prequalification (for procurement through UN agencies), CE marking (many African countries accept CE), FDA clearance (for US-based grant funding requirements), and national registration in 10-30 individual countries (each with its own requirements, fees, and timelines). No single regulatory professional understands all these jurisdictions. Companies hire country-specific consultants at $10-30K per country, with no way to identify overlapping requirements, shared documentation, or the optimal sequencing of submissions across jurisdictions.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The regulatory landscape in low- and middle-income countries is fragmented, poorly documented, and changes frequently. Many national regulatory authorities do not have English-language guidance documents. The WHO prequalification process has been formalized only recently (2017 for medical devices). The target market (global health device companies) is small and grant-funded, limiting willingness to pay for commercial software.

## What to Build
A regulatory planning platform that maps device characteristics to registration requirements across WHO PQ, FDA, CE, and 50+ national authorities. It identifies overlapping requirements (tests, documentation, clinical data that satisfy multiple jurisdictions simultaneously), recommends optimal submission sequencing (which jurisdictions to file first to create precedent for others), estimates total cost and timeline, and generates jurisdiction-specific documentation checklists. Updated from regulatory intelligence feeds and user-contributed updates from in-country regulatory consultants.

## Target Customer
Regulatory affairs leads at global health device companies, NGOs managing device development programs (PATH, Clinton Health Access Initiative), and regulatory consulting firms serving global health clients. Approximately 100-200 organizations. Average contract value: $15-30K/year.

## Impact If Built
Regulatory planning time drops from 6-12 months to 4-8 weeks. Submission sequencing optimization saves 20-40% of total regulatory cost by identifying shared documentation across jurisdictions. Devices reach patients in low-resource settings 6-12 months faster.
