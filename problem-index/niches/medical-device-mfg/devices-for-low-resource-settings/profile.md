# Devices for Low-Resource Settings

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-device-mfg|Medical Device Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2-4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~1.5%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — design tools are modern but manufacturing often occurs in partner facilities in emerging markets with minimal digital infrastructure
**Target Buyer:** Product Development Lead or CEO at global health device companies (10-100 employees)
**Automation Potential:** Low — the challenge is design-for-manufacturability and regulatory navigation, not production automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Manufacturers designing devices for low-resource settings (rural clinics, community health workers, field hospitals in low- and middle-income countries) face constraints that invert normal device design priorities: the device must function without reliable electricity, clean water, or trained biomedical technicians; consumable supply chains are unreliable; the device must be manufactured at a cost point 5-20x lower than US-equivalent devices; and the regulatory pathway spans multiple jurisdictions (FDA for US sales, WHO prequalification, national regulatory authorities in target countries). This unique constraint set means that design decisions, manufacturing processes, and regulatory strategies all differ fundamentally from standard US medical device development.

## Current Tools & Gaps
CAD (SolidWorks, Fusion 360), basic ERP, and standard QMS tools are used. PATH, USAID, and the Gates Foundation fund many of these devices through grants. No platform specifically helps design for low-resource constraints (electricity independence, heat/humidity tolerance, minimal maintenance), navigate multi-jurisdiction regulatory pathways (WHO PQ + national authorities), or plan manufacturing transfers to facilities in emerging markets.
