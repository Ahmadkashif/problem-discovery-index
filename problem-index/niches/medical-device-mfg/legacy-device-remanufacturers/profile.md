# Legacy Device Remanufacturers

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/medical-device-mfg|Medical Device Manufacturing]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~2%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most remanufacturers operate with paper-based inspection records, manual disassembly documentation, and spreadsheet-based inventory tracking
**Target Buyer:** Owner or Operations Manager at remanufacturing facilities (10-100 employees)
**Automation Potential:** Medium — incoming device assessment, refurbishment documentation, and equivalency testing are partially automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Medical device remanufacturers (refurbishing endoscopes, surgical instruments, imaging equipment, and electrosurgical devices for resale or hospital reprocessing programs) occupy a regulatory gray zone that intensified after FDA's 2020 remanufacturing guidance. They must demonstrate that the remanufactured device meets original performance specifications, document every refurbishment step with traceability to the original device, and manage an incoming inventory of used devices with unpredictable condition. Unlike new-device manufacturers who control inputs, remanufacturers receive devices in unknown states — corrosion, wear, previous unauthorized repair — and must assess each unit individually. This per-unit assessment is the critical bottleneck and depends entirely on technician expertise.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most remanufacturers use paper inspection forms, Excel for inventory and work orders, and basic accounting software. The largest (Stryker Sustainability Solutions, Innovative Health) have custom internal systems. No commercial platform addresses the remanufacturing workflow: incoming device condition assessment, refurbishment procedure routing based on condition, component replacement tracking with OEM-equivalent part validation, and functional testing to original specifications.
