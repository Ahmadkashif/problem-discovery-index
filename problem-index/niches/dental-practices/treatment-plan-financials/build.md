# One-Click Treatment Plan Financial Package Generator

**Niche:** [[niches/dental-practices/treatment-plan-financials/profile|Treatment Plan Financial Presentation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** Input the treatment plan, automatically calculate insurance estimated coverage per procedure, patient total responsibility, monthly payment plan options at 3-4 term lengths, CareCredit/Sunbit pre-qualification estimate, and output a branded patient-facing financial comparison — because nothing generates this complete financial picture today.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #data-integration #revenue-impact #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Treatment acceptance in dentistry runs at approximately 50% — half of clinically necessary treatment goes unperformed. Research consistently shows that the primary barrier is not clinical reluctance but financial uncertainty. When a patient hears "you need a crown" and is told "your estimated cost is around $500-800 depending on your insurance," the vagueness creates anxiety and the default response is "I need to think about it." Patients who say "I need to think about it" return less than 20% of the time. The treatment coordinator who can present a clear, specific financial package — "Your insurance covers $620, you owe $380, and you can pay that as $64/month for 6 months with no interest" — gets 70-80% acceptance. But generating that specific financial package requires combining data from 3-4 disconnected sources (PMS treatment plan, insurance benefit verification, practice fee schedule, CareCredit/Sunbit terms) and performing 5-10 calculations. No tool assembles this automatically. The quality of financial presentation is therefore entirely dependent on the treatment coordinator's individual skill, creating massive variance across practices and even across staff within the same practice.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The data required for a complete financial package spans systems that don't talk to each other. Insurance benefit data comes from payer portals or eligibility verification tools (Vyne Dental, DentalXChange). Practice fee schedules live in the PMS. Third-party financing terms require API integration with CareCredit (Synchrony), Sunbit, Proceed Finance, and others — each with their own pre-qualification API, terms structure, and approval logic. In-house payment plan terms are practice-specific policies stored nowhere digitally. Combining these data sources into a single patient-facing document requires integrations that cross vendor boundaries — CareCredit doesn't integrate with Dentrix's treatment plan module, and Dentrix doesn't know about Sunbit's terms. Building the integration layer is a partnership challenge as much as an engineering challenge. Additionally, estimated insurance coverage is just that — an estimate — and presenting it as definitive creates liability if the actual adjudication differs.

## What to Build
A financial package generator that plugs into the practice's PMS (Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental) and, triggered by the treatment coordinator clicking a single button on a treatment plan, performs: (1) insurance coverage estimation per procedure (pulling benefit data from the verification system or benefit intelligence API, applying coverage percentages, deductible, annual maximum remaining, and frequency limitations), (2) patient responsibility calculation (total out-of-pocket after insurance estimate, with a clearly labeled confidence range), (3) payment option comparison — pay-in-full today (with practice-specific discount if offered), in-house payment plan at 2-3 term lengths with monthly amounts, CareCredit terms at 0%, 6-month, and 12-month promotional periods with monthly amounts, and Sunbit terms with monthly amounts, (4) pre-qualification check for third-party financing (soft credit pull via CareCredit/Sunbit API with no patient credit impact), and (5) output as a branded, patient-facing PDF or digital presentation (tablet-viewable in the operatory) with clear visual comparison of payment options. A gradient-boosted model trained on historical treatment acceptance data (features: procedure type, patient age, insurance type, out-of-pocket amount, financing presented vs. not, day of week, treatment coordinator) can recommend the presentation format most likely to achieve acceptance for this specific patient and procedure.

## Target Customer
Dental practices of any size experiencing treatment acceptance rates below 60%, particularly practices without a dedicated treatment coordinator where the dentist or dental assistant handles financial presentation ad hoc.

## Impact If Built
Increases treatment acceptance from 50% to 65-75% by removing financial ambiguity from the decision. For a practice diagnosing $1.5M in treatment annually, a 15-percentage-point increase in acceptance adds $225K in annual production. The tool pays for itself if it converts one additional crown per month.
