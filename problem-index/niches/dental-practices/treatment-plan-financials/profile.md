# Treatment Plan Financial Presentation

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $3-5B (embedded in dental practice operations across all practices)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Touches every practice that presents treatment plans — the function exists in all ~200,000 dental offices but is performed systematically in perhaps 30% of them
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — treatment plan estimates are generated in the PMS but the financial presentation to patients is typically verbal (treatment coordinator explains costs), supplemented by paper printouts or informal calculations
**Target Buyer:** Treatment coordinator, office manager, or the dentist themselves in smaller practices where no dedicated treatment coordinator exists
**Automation Potential:** Very High — the inputs (treatment plan, insurance benefits, fee schedule, financing terms) are all structured data, and the output (a patient-facing financial package showing insurance coverage, out-of-pocket cost, and payment plan options) is a templated document — but no tool assembles the complete picture automatically

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
After a dentist diagnoses a treatment need and presents the clinical recommendation, someone must answer the patient's real question: "How much is this going to cost me, and how can I afford it?" This financial presentation determines treatment acceptance — and the industry average is only ~50%, meaning half of diagnosed treatment goes unperformed. The financial presentation requires combining: insurance benefit data (estimated coverage per procedure, remaining annual maximum, applicable limitations), practice fee schedule data (the practice's charges), patient responsibility calculation (coinsurance, deductible, amount exceeding annual maximum), and financing options (CareCredit terms at various durations, Sunbit approval estimate, in-house payment plan terms). Currently, the treatment coordinator does this math mentally or on a calculator, writes numbers on a printed treatment plan, and verbally explains the options. The quality of this financial communication varies wildly by staff skill — an experienced treatment coordinator can present a $5,000 treatment plan and get acceptance; an inexperienced one presenting the same plan loses the patient.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Dental PMS systems generate treatment plan estimates that show insurance estimated coverage and patient estimated responsibility per procedure. CareCredit and Sunbit offer financing but require separate applications disconnected from the treatment plan. Dental Intelligence and other analytics tools track case acceptance rates but don't help with the presentation itself. No tool generates a complete patient-facing financial package that combines: "Here's what your insurance covers, here's your out-of-pocket total, and here are 3 ways to pay for the out-of-pocket amount (pay today with 5% discount, 6-month payment plan at $X/month, or CareCredit at 0% for 12 months at $Y/month)." This complete view is assembled ad hoc by treatment coordinators using mental math and verbal presentation.
