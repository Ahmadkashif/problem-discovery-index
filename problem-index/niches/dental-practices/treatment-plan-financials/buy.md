# Unified Insurance + Financing Patient Financial View

**Niche:** [[niches/dental-practices/treatment-plan-financials/profile|Treatment Plan Financial Presentation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** CareCredit and Sunbit offer patient financing but are separate applications disconnected from the treatment plan — dental PMS systems generate insurance estimates but don't include financing options — the gap is a unified view combining both into one patient-friendly presentation.
**Tags:** #data-integration #workflow-orchestration #automation #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
A patient sitting in the dental chair looking at a treatment plan sees a number — their estimated out-of-pocket cost. If they can afford it, they say yes. If they can't, they say "I'll think about it." The treatment coordinator then says "We also offer CareCredit" — but applying for CareCredit is a separate process: the patient fills out a different form, on a different tablet, and finds out minutes later whether they're approved and at what terms. If CareCredit declines, the coordinator might mention Sunbit — another application, another tablet, another wait. The patient experiences the financial side of dental care as a fragmented, multi-application process that feels like buying a used car. The practice experiences it as a conversion funnel with drop-off at every step: 50% accept the treatment plan, 60% of decliners are offered financing, 40% of those offered financing apply, 70% of applicants are approved. The cascading funnel means only 8-10% of patients who initially declined end up accepting via financing.

## What Already Exists
CareCredit (Synchrony Health) is the dominant dental patient financing platform — accepted at 250,000+ healthcare providers, with an established patient application flow and promotional financing terms (0% APR for 6-24 months). Sunbit is a newer point-of-sale financing option with higher approval rates (85%+ vs. CareCredit's ~60%) but higher APR for patients with lower credit. Proceed Finance, LendingClub Patient Solutions, and others serve similar roles. On the PMS side, Dentrix, Eaglesoft, and Open Dental generate treatment plan estimates showing insurance coverage and patient responsibility. These two worlds — insurance estimates and financing options — never appear on the same screen.

## The Customization Gap
The gap is a presentation layer that unifies the PMS treatment plan estimate with financing options in a single patient-facing view. Instead of "your out-of-pocket is $1,200" followed by a separate financing conversation, the patient sees: "Your insurance covers $800. Your responsibility is $1,200. Here's how you can pay: Option A — pay today for $1,140 (5% courtesy discount). Option B — our in-house plan at $200/month for 6 months. Option C — CareCredit at $100/month for 12 months at 0% APR. Option D — Sunbit at $58/month for 24 months." The presentation should include a soft pre-qualification for CareCredit and Sunbit (using their APIs) so the patient sees only the options they are likely to qualify for — not options that will result in a declined application. This unified view turns the financial presentation from a multi-step negotiation into a one-screen comparison, mimicking the e-commerce checkout experience patients are accustomed to in every other purchasing context.

## Target Customer
Dental practices already using CareCredit or Sunbit but presenting them as separate, after-the-fact financing options rather than integrated components of the financial presentation — particularly practices where treatment coordinators report that patients "shut down" when they hear the out-of-pocket number before financing is mentioned.

## Impact If Solved
Increases financing utilization from 10-15% of eligible patients to 30-40% by presenting options proactively rather than reactively. Converts an additional 15-20% of treatment plan decliners into acceptances. For a practice with $500K in unaccepted treatment annually, converting 15% represents $75K in recovered production.
