# Dental Labs & Denturists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~5-6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — CAD/CAM adoption (exocad, 3Shape Dental System) is growing but only ~25% of cases arrive as digital impressions; production tracking software exists but dentist-facing communication remains phone/fax-based
**Target Buyer:** Lab owner or production manager at a commercial dental laboratory (typically 5-50 technicians)
**Automation Potential:** High — case intake, shade communication, production tracking, and dentist communication are structured workflows dominated by manual handoffs and subjective interpretation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Dental laboratories fabricate custom prosthetics — crowns, bridges, dentures, implant abutments, orthodontic appliances — on prescription from dentists. The workflow is two-sided: receive a case from a dentist (impression, shade, bite record, written Rx), fabricate the restoration, and ship it back. This two-sided communication is the fundamental bottleneck. Dentists send physical impression trays in boxes via courier, shade photos taken with phone cameras under inconsistent operatory lighting, and handwritten or faxed Rx forms with ambiguous instructions ("make it match A2 but a little warmer"). The lab interprets these inputs, fabricates the restoration, and ships it back — only to have 8-12% of cases returned for remakes because the shade didn't match, the fit was off, or the dentist's intent wasn't captured correctly. Digital impressions from intraoral scanners (iTero, 3Shape TRIOS) are growing but still represent only ~25% of case volume. The lab's production floor uses CAD/CAM for design but manual processes for case management.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Lab management software (LabStar, Evident/DDX, Maestro3D) handles internal production tracking — case stages, technician assignments, due dates, invoicing. But these systems stop at the lab door. The dentist-facing communication is phone calls ("Is my case ready?"), paper Rx forms, and physical impression boxes. No system connects the dentist's case submission to the lab's production pipeline to the dentist's delivery expectation in a single digital workflow. For digital cases, STL files arrive via email or portal, but analog cases (75% of volume) have no digital touchpoint between submission and delivery.
