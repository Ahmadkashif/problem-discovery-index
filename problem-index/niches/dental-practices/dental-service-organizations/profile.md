# DSOs (Dental Service Organizations)

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $35-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~22%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — DSOs run enterprise PMS (Denticon/Planet DDS) and have IT teams, but cross-location analytics and M&A integration tooling remain immature
**Target Buyer:** VP Operations, CTO, or Chief Dental Officer at a multi-location dental group (10-500+ locations)
**Automation Potential:** Very High — standardizing clinical protocols, benchmarking provider performance, and integrating acquired practices are all structured problems with massive operational leverage

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
DSOs (Aspen Dental, Pacific Dental Services, Heartland Dental, and hundreds of smaller PE-backed groups) aggregate dental practices under corporate management. Their problems are fundamentally different from single-location practices: standardizing clinical protocols across 50+ providers who were trained differently, tracking provider productivity and quality metrics at scale, centralizing procurement to capture supply chain savings, managing cross-location patient scheduling (a patient who moves or travels should be seen at the nearest location with access to their records), and absorbing acquired practices onto a unified technology platform. The M&A integration challenge alone — converting an acquired practice from Dentrix to Denticon, migrating patient records, re-credentialing providers, and harmonizing fee schedules — typically takes 6-12 months per acquisition. DSOs are increasingly PE-backed, meaning the buyer cares about EBITDA optimization metrics that don't exist in single-practice software.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Denticon (Planet DDS) is the dominant enterprise dental PMS, designed for multi-location management. CareStack is a newer cloud-native alternative. These handle multi-location scheduling and centralized billing but lack real-time operational dashboards — DSO executives rely on monthly Excel reports built by analysts pulling data from Denticon's reporting module. Provider performance benchmarking requires custom SQL queries or expensive consulting engagements. M&A integration has no dedicated tooling — each acquisition is a manual project managed via spreadsheets and Asana boards. Supply chain management uses generic procurement tools (Coupa, SAP Ariba) that don't understand dental-specific ordering patterns.
