# Pediatric Dental Growth & Development Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/dental-practices/pediatric-dental/profile|Pediatric Dental Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A system that tracks eruption patterns, mixed dentition development, and interceptive orthodontic timing across visits — alerting the pediatric dentist when timing-sensitive interventions like space maintainers or early orthodontic referral should be considered based on the child's developmental stage.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #feature-engineering #evaluation-metrics #cross-validation #tacit-knowledge-ml #decision-trees

## The Problem
Pediatric dentistry is time-sensitive in ways that general dentistry is not. A premature loss of a primary molar at age 5 requires a space maintainer within weeks — delay and the adjacent teeth drift, closing the space and creating a malocclusion that requires years of orthodontics to correct. Early orthodontic evaluation should happen at age 7, but the referral window is narrow and depends on the child's specific developmental stage, not just their calendar age. Serial extraction protocols depend on tracking eruption sequence across 4-6 visits over 2-3 years. Experienced pediatric dentists carry this developmental timeline in their heads — they glance at a panoramic radiograph and know which teeth should be erupting when, whether the sequence is normal, and whether intervention is needed now or can wait. This is tacit clinical knowledge built over thousands of patient-years of observation that no dental PMS captures, tracks, or alerts on. A junior associate or locum covering the practice has no access to this pattern recognition and may miss critical intervention windows.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Dental PMS systems model teeth as static objects — present, missing, restored, or decayed. They don't model developmental state: which primary teeth have exfoliated, which permanent teeth have erupted, what stage of root development the unerupted teeth are in (visible on panoramic X-ray), or whether the eruption sequence deviates from normal. Building a developmental tracker requires: (1) a tooth-level developmental stage model (not just present/absent but erupting/partially erupted/fully erupted/root complete), (2) age-normed eruption timelines calibrated by population data, (3) longitudinal tracking across visits (recording developmental state at each visit to detect deviations from expected progression), and (4) clinical decision rules for when to alert about intervention windows. This is a small-market specialty problem — there are ~7,000 pediatric dental practices in the US — which discourages general-purpose PMS vendors from building it.

## What to Build
A developmental tracking module that integrates with the practice's existing PMS and panoramic imaging system. At each visit, the dentist records the developmental state of the dentition (assisted by a CNN that reads panoramic radiographs and auto-detects eruption stages of visible teeth). The system maintains a longitudinal developmental record per patient, compares it against age-normed eruption timelines (using gradient-boosted regression to model expected eruption timing given age, sex, ethnicity, and prior eruption history), and generates alerts when: (1) a tooth is significantly delayed in eruption (potential impaction or pathology), (2) a primary tooth is lost prematurely and a space maintainer should be placed before space closure begins, (3) the child reaches the developmental stage where interceptive orthodontic evaluation is indicated, or (4) a serial extraction sequence needs its next step. The alerts go to the treating dentist before the patient's next scheduled visit, enabling proactive scheduling of time-sensitive interventions rather than reactive discovery at a routine cleaning.

## Target Customer
Pediatric dental practices (2-5 providers) treating 2,000-5,000 active patients, where tracking developmental milestones across thousands of children at different stages is cognitively overwhelming for even experienced practitioners.

## Impact If Built
Catches missed intervention windows that currently slip through in 5-10% of cases — space maintainers not placed in time, orthodontic referrals delayed by 1-2 years, impacted teeth discovered late. Each missed space maintainer can result in $4,000-8,000 in future orthodontic costs for the patient. For the practice, proactive developmental tracking differentiates the practice clinically and generates interceptive treatment revenue ($30K-80K/year in space maintainer and early ortho procedures that would otherwise be missed).
