# General Dentistry Single-Location Practices

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/dental-practices|Dental Practices]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $75-80B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~48%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — most practices run Dentrix or Eaglesoft but use only 30-40% of the features; reporting, analytics, and workflow automation are largely untouched
**Target Buyer:** Practice owner-dentist who is simultaneously the lead clinician, HR department, and business strategist
**Automation Potential:** High — the owner-dentist has no time for administrative optimization, meaning any tool that auto-surfaces business intelligence or automates front-desk workflows delivers immediate ROI

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Solo or 2-3 dentist practices performing preventive, restorative, and basic prosthetic dentistry make up roughly 120,000 of the ~200,000 dental offices in the US. The defining characteristic is that the owner IS the dentist — they spend 32-36 hours per week in the operatory and have 4-6 hours for everything else: hiring, billing disputes, marketing, equipment maintenance, and strategic planning. The smallest practices lack a dedicated front-office manager, meaning the dentist's spouse or a single receptionist handles scheduling, insurance verification, and collections. Buying decisions are personal and budget-conscious — there is no IT department or procurement committee. These practices are deeply loyal to their PMS (Dentrix/Eaglesoft), resistant to switching, and skeptical of new software that requires training time they don't have.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Dentrix (Henry Schein) and Eaglesoft (Patterson) dominate with ~60% combined market share. Open Dental is the open-source alternative with a growing user base. These systems handle charting, scheduling, and basic billing, but their reporting is rudimentary — generating a "production by procedure code" report requires navigating 5 screens and exporting to Excel. Practice analytics tools like Dental Intelligence and Practice by Numbers exist but cost $300-500/month and require separate logins and data exports. Patient communication tools (Weave, RevenueWell, Lighthouse 360) handle recalls and reminders but don't integrate deeply with clinical workflows. The gap is a unified intelligence layer that sits on top of the existing PMS without requiring a rip-and-replace.
