# Vertical Marketing Playbook Platform with Industry Benchmarks

**Niche:** [[niches/marketing-agencies-smb/industry-vertical-agencies/profile|Niche Industry-Vertical Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform captures a vertical agency's accumulated playbooks — proven campaign templates, content calendars, benchmark KPIs, and strategic frameworks — in a structured, reusable format that scales beyond the agency owner's personal knowledge.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #recommendation #tacit-knowledge-ml

## The Problem
A dental marketing agency with 50 clients and 10 years of experience has developed a "playbook" that lives in the owner's head: new patient acquisition campaigns should target within a 7-mile radius, Google Ads cost-per-lead for implants runs $75-$150 but for general dentistry it is $15-$35, the best-converting blog topics are "how much do dental implants cost" and "emergency dentist near me," and the ideal content calendar posts 2 blogs and 4 social posts per week with a mix of educational and promotional content. This playbook was built through trial-and-error across 50 clients, but it exists as informal knowledge, scattered notes, and pattern recognition that the owner applies intuitively. When the agency hires a new strategist, they cannot access this accumulated wisdom — they must either shadow the owner for 6 months or reinvent the playbook through their own trial-and-error.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Playbook platforms exist for sales (Gong, Chorus) and customer success (Gainsight), but marketing agencies operate differently: their playbooks are specific to the intersection of marketing channel, industry vertical, and client segment. A "dental marketing playbook" is different from a "personal injury law marketing playbook" across every dimension — targeting, creative, compliance rules, seasonal patterns, and benchmark metrics. Building a platform requires a flexible framework that agencies can populate with their own vertical-specific knowledge, benchmarked against aggregated performance data from other agencies serving the same vertical (if available).

## What to Build
A playbook platform where the agency captures and structures its domain knowledge: proven campaign templates by objective (new patient acquisition, retention, referral), content topic databases with historical performance data, industry benchmark KPIs by client size (cost-per-lead, conversion rate, ROAS, lifetime value), seasonal campaign calendars, compliance checklists, and strategic decision frameworks ("when to recommend Google Ads vs. Meta vs. content marketing based on client maturity and budget"). The platform provides new team members with a structured onboarding path through the playbook and suggests playbook-informed campaign strategies for new client engagements.

## Target Customer
Vertical marketing agency owners with 20-60 clients and 5-15 employees who want to scale their strategic capability beyond the founding team's personal knowledge.

## Impact If Built
Reduces new strategist ramp time from 6 months to 6 weeks, enables the agency to serve more clients at consistent quality without the owner being the bottleneck, and creates a defensible knowledge asset that increases the agency's valuation.
