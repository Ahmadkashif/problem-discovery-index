# Industry-Specific Compliance Checker for Marketing Content

**Niche:** [[niches/marketing-agencies-smb/industry-vertical-agencies/profile|Niche Industry-Vertical Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Content compliance tools exist for pharma and financial services but not for the SMB verticals where marketing agencies operate — healthcare practices, law firms, and home services contractors each have advertising rules that agencies must follow.
**Tags:** #bert #text-classification #nlp #compliance #automation

## The Problem
Healthcare marketing must comply with HIPAA (no patient testimonials with identifiable health information without consent), state medical board advertising rules (cannot guarantee outcomes), and ADA guidelines (dental advertising restrictions vary by state). Legal marketing must comply with state bar advertising rules (some states require disclaimer language, some prohibit specific claims about case results). Even home services marketing has rules: some states require contractor license numbers in advertising. A vertical agency must know these rules for each state where their clients operate and check every piece of content — blog posts, ads, social media posts, website pages — for compliance before publication. This review is done by senior staff from memory, creating a bottleneck and a risk of violations.

## What Already Exists
Veeva Vault PromoMats handles pharmaceutical marketing compliance. Grammarly and Writer offer brand consistency checking. Generic AI content detectors check for various issues. None of these understand the specific advertising rules that apply to dental practices, law firms, or home service contractors at the state level.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs a rules engine populated with industry-specific and state-specific advertising regulations: for healthcare — which states require specific disclaimer language, which claims about outcomes are prohibited, what constitutes an impermissible patient testimonial; for legal — state bar advertising rules including required disclosures, prohibited superlatives ("the best lawyer"), and case result communication requirements; for home services — contractor license display requirements. Content submitted for review should be checked against the applicable rules for the client's industry and operating states, with flagged violations and suggested corrections.

## Target Customer
Content managers and senior strategists at vertical marketing agencies who review 50-200 pieces of content per month for industry-specific compliance, spending 5-10 hours per week on manual compliance review.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces compliance review time by 60-70%, catches violations that manual review misses (especially state-specific rules the reviewer may not know), and prevents the $5K-$50K penalties and reputational damage from advertising compliance violations.
