# Project Scoping, SOW Generation, and Profitability Tracking

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4-6B (embedded cost across all agencies)
**Share of Parent Industry:** Embedded function — represents 15-20% of agency owner and operations manager time
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — most agencies scope projects in Google Docs, estimate hours from memory, and discover profitability (or lack thereof) only at project close
**Target Buyer:** Agency owner or operations manager tracking profitability across 15-40 active client engagements
**Automation Potential:** High — scoping, estimation, SOW generation, and real-time profitability tracking follow patterns that can be learned from historical project data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Project scoping and profitability management is where agencies either make or lose money. Scoping determines the SOW, which sets client expectations and defines the revenue ceiling. If the scope underestimates effort (which happens 30-40% of the time because estimates come from memory, not data), the project runs over budget, the agency eats the overage, and the client relationship suffers when the agency tries to bill for scope creep. Most agencies discover profitability issues post-hoc: a project closes, the owner reviews time logs, and discovers the team spent 60 hours on a project scoped for 35. By then, the revenue is lost and the lesson is repeated on the next similar project because the estimating knowledge never makes it back into the scoping process.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Google Docs and PandaDoc generate SOWs and proposals. Monday.com, Asana, and ClickUp track project tasks. Harvest and Toggl track time. The gap is the feedback loop: no tool connects historical project data (scoped hours vs. actual hours, by deliverable type) to the scoping process for new projects. The agency owner scopes "a website redesign" at 80 hours from memory, even though the last three website redesigns took 110-130 hours — because that historical data is buried in time tracking exports, not surfaced at scoping time.
