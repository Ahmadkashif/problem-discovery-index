# Agency-Specific SOW Generator with Scope Boundary Templates

**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Proposal and contract tools exist but none ship with agency-specific SOW templates that include clear scope boundaries, revision limits, and out-of-scope definitions — the clauses that prevent profit-killing scope creep.
**Tags:** #llm #text-generation #nlp #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Agency SOWs are typically written in Google Docs by the agency owner or account manager, using a previous SOW as a starting template and modifying it for the new client. The resulting SOW often includes vague deliverable descriptions ("website design and development"), ambiguous revision policies ("reasonable revisions included"), and no explicit definition of what is out of scope. When the client requests a 5th round of homepage revisions or asks to add a blog section that was not discussed, the agency has no contractual basis to push back. The scope creep happens not because the client is unreasonable, but because the SOW did not define boundaries clearly enough.

## What Already Exists
PandaDoc, Proposify, and Better Proposals offer proposal and SOW template builders with e-signature. These are document generation tools with some template functionality. HoneyBook and Dubsado offer combined proposal/contract/invoicing for creative businesses.

## The Customization Gap
Agency SOWs need pre-built, legally reviewed scope boundary clauses specific to common agency deliverables: for website projects (number of design concepts, revision rounds per page, content responsibility, third-party integrations scope), for monthly retainers (included hours, rollover policy, scope of included services vs. add-ons), for content projects (pieces per month, revision rounds, client content input deadlines, publishing responsibility). The SOW should auto-generate out-of-scope sections based on the in-scope deliverables selected ("the following are NOT included unless separately scoped and priced: blog development, e-commerce functionality, custom integrations, ongoing hosting management"). Each clause should be derived from the agency's historical scope creep patterns.

## Target Customer
Agency owners writing 3-10 SOWs per month who have experienced scope creep on 30-40% of projects due to vague scope definitions.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces scope creep incidents by 40-60% through clear, pre-defined boundaries, enabling the agency to maintain project margins and have productive conversations about change orders rather than passive scope absorption.
