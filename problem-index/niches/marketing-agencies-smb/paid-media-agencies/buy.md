# Automated Budget Pacing and Overspend Prevention

**Niche:** [[niches/marketing-agencies-smb/paid-media-agencies/profile|Paid Media (PPC/Social Ads) Focused Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Ad platform budget controls exist but operate per-campaign, not at the client-level monthly budget that agencies are accountable for — leading to overspend that the agency must eat or explain.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #revenue-impact #quick-win

## The Problem
An agency manages a client with a $15,000/month ad budget split across 5 Google Ads campaigns and 3 Meta campaigns. Each platform has its own daily budget setting, but the agency is accountable for the total monthly spend across all platforms. If Google Ads campaigns overspend by 10% and Meta underspends by 5%, the net overspend is $750 — and the client's contract says $15,000/month. The agency must either absorb the overspend, explain it to the client, or manually monitor and adjust daily budgets across 8 campaigns on 2 platforms throughout the month. Budget pacing is one of the most common sources of client complaints ("you overspent by $2,000 this month") and agency margin erosion.

## What Already Exists
Google Ads has monthly spend caps and daily budget limits. Meta has campaign spending limits. Optmyzr and other optimization tools offer budget pacing dashboards. These tools monitor pacing on a per-platform basis but do not provide cross-platform budget management against a single client-level monthly target.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to manage a single client-level monthly budget that spans across all platforms and campaigns. When total spend across all platforms is pacing ahead of schedule (by day 15, 60% of the monthly budget is spent instead of the expected 50%), the system should automatically reduce daily budgets proportionally across campaigns and platforms to bring pacing back on track. Conversely, when pacing is behind (underperforming campaigns), the system should redistribute budget to better-performing campaigns to maximize spend efficiency. The agency should be able to set pacing rules per client: "never exceed monthly budget by more than 3%" and "prioritize Google branded campaigns for budget protection."

## Target Customer
PPC specialists and account managers at agencies managing 20-60 client budgets across multiple platforms, where budget pacing errors affect 15-25% of clients each month.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates budget overspend incidents that cost the agency $500-$2,000 per client per month in client credits or margin erosion, and reduces the 5-10 hours per week that specialists spend on manual budget monitoring and adjustment.
