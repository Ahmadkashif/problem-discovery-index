# Marketing Readiness Assessment and Growth-Stage Playbook

**Niche:** [[niches/marketing-agencies-smb/diverse-owned-business-agencies/profile|Minority and Women-Owned Business Marketing Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool assesses a first-time business owner's marketing readiness and prescribes a growth-stage-appropriate marketing plan — most marketing tools assume the client already knows what SEO and paid ads are.
**Tags:** #recommendation #tabular-ml #automation #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
When a first-generation entrepreneur approaches a marketing agency, they often need to start with fundamentals that typical clients already have in place: a professional website, Google Business Profile, basic brand identity, social media presence, and an understanding of what "leads" and "conversions" mean. Agencies serving this population must assess each client's current marketing maturity — not just their budget — to recommend the right starting point. A business without a website does not need Google Ads; a business without a Google Business Profile does not need an SEO strategy. But agencies lack a structured assessment framework, so they either prescribe their standard package (which is often too advanced for the client) or customize every engagement from scratch (which is not scalable).

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Marketing tools are designed for businesses that already have a digital presence — they assume a website, tracking pixels, and some digital footprint exist. The pre-digital-marketing stage (establishing foundational presence) is considered "too basic" for sophisticated marketing tools to address. The client segment (early-stage diverse entrepreneurs) is also budget-constrained, making it less attractive for SaaS vendors. Building a growth-stage assessment requires defining marketing maturity stages, mapping appropriate activities to each stage, and creating a progression framework — essentially, encoding the experience-based judgment that agency owners develop serving dozens of early-stage businesses.

## What to Build
A marketing readiness assessment that evaluates a business across 8-10 dimensions (website presence and quality, Google Business Profile status, social media presence, brand consistency, review profile, local visibility, customer data capture, and competitive positioning) and places the business on a growth-stage maturity scale (Stage 1: Foundation, Stage 2: Visibility, Stage 3: Lead Generation, Stage 4: Optimization). Each stage has a prescribed action plan with specific activities, recommended tools, budget ranges, and expected timelines. The agency uses this assessment during the sales process to set expectations and during engagement to track progress.

## Target Customer
Agency owners serving 20-50 early-stage diverse entrepreneurs who need a scalable framework for assessing client readiness and prescribing appropriate marketing activities.

## Impact If Built
Reduces sales cycle time by 30-40% by setting clear expectations during the assessment, improves client retention by 20-30% by matching service delivery to actual readiness (avoiding the "we bought ads but don't have a website" mismatch), and enables the agency to serve 2x more clients at lower price points with a structured framework.
