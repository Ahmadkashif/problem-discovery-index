# Client Content Approval Workflow with Structured Feedback

**Niche:** [[niches/marketing-agencies-smb/content-inbound-agencies/profile|Content & Inbound Marketing Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/marketing-agencies-smb|SMB Marketing Agencies]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Document collaboration tools handle comments and suggestions, but none enforce a structured approval workflow that captures client feedback in actionable categories rather than vague margin notes.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #worker-facing #quick-win

## The Problem
Content agencies send drafts to clients via Google Docs or shared links. The client leaves comments: "this doesn't sound like us," "can we change the tone here?", "I'm not sure about this section," or simply "needs work." These comments lack specificity — is the issue factual accuracy, brand voice, technical depth, or personal preference? The writer must interpret vague feedback, make changes they hope satisfy the client, resubmit, and repeat. 30-40% of content pieces go through 3+ revision rounds because the initial feedback was not actionable, each round consuming 45-60 minutes of writer time and adding 3-5 days to the production timeline.

## What Already Exists
Google Docs provides inline commenting and suggestion mode. GatherContent and Content Snare offer structured content collection and approval workflows. Monday.com and Asana track approval status. Ziflow and ProofHQ handle creative proofing with structured markup. None of these are designed specifically for written content revision workflows with feedback categorization.

## The Customization Gap
The tool needs to replace open-ended commenting with structured feedback: when a client reviews content, they are guided to categorize each piece of feedback (factual correction, tone/voice adjustment, add missing information, remove content, restructure flow, personal preference). Each category triggers different actions: factual corrections are highest priority and must be addressed; personal preferences are lowest priority and may be discussed. The system tracks feedback patterns over time ("this client gives tone feedback on 80% of first drafts — pre-check voice alignment before submission") and provides the writer with a structured revision checklist rather than a thread of ambiguous comments.

## Target Customer
Content managers at agencies producing 50-200 pieces per month who spend 10-15 hours per week managing client review cycles and interpreting feedback.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces average revision rounds from 3 to 1.5 per piece by making feedback actionable from the first review. Saves 8-12 hours per week in content manager time spent interpreting and relaying client feedback.
