# National Certification Bodies

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $6B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 23%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — most have online exam registration and CE tracking, but exam development, credential verification, and recertification workflows involve significant manual processing
**Target Buyer:** Executive Director / Certification Director at organizations offering professional credentials
**Automation Potential:** High — exam administration, CE credit tracking, recertification reminders, and credential verification are all rule-based processes

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Certification bodies (PMI, CompTIA, SHRM, AHLA, NATE, etc.) generate revenue through exam fees, annual credential maintenance fees, and continuing education program accreditation. They maintain the integrity of professional credentials that employers rely on for hiring decisions. The operational model centers on the certification lifecycle: candidate eligibility review, exam development and administration, credential issuance, continuing education tracking, and periodic recertification. This lifecycle is distinct from membership-based associations because the relationship is credential-centric rather than membership-centric — a certificant may never attend an event or read the newsletter but must maintain their credential.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Exam administration platforms (Prometric, Pearson VUE, PSI) handle testing logistics. AMS platforms (iMIS, Fonteva, YourMembership) track member/certificant records. LMS platforms (Absorb, Docebo) deliver education. However, the end-to-end certification lifecycle — from application through recertification — crosses multiple systems with manual handoffs. Credential verification (employers checking if someone's certification is current) is often a phone call or a clunky web lookup. No single platform manages the full certification lifecycle with the rigor these organizations need.
