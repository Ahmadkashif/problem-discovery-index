# Construction Trade Groups

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — most construction trade groups manage apprenticeship rosters, safety certifications, and job boards via spreadsheets and paper files; members are field workers who primarily use phones
**Target Buyer:** Executive Director / Apprenticeship Coordinator at local/regional construction trade associations
**Automation Potential:** Medium — apprenticeship hour tracking, safety certification monitoring, and workforce availability matching are automatable; labor relations and advocacy require human judgment

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Construction trade associations (local NECA chapters, ABC chapters, AGC affiliates, plumbers' associations, IBEW-affiliated groups) serve contractors and skilled trades workers. Their primary operational functions are unique to the construction industry: managing apprenticeship programs (tracking 4-8 year apprenticeships with required classroom hours and on-the-job hours), monitoring member safety certifications (OSHA 10/30, confined space, fall protection — which expire and must be renewed), providing workforce availability information (which journeymen are available for dispatch), and advocating for industry-specific regulatory issues (prevailing wage, licensing requirements). These functions have no parallel in generic association management.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Construction trade groups use generic AMS platforms (MemberClicks, Wild Apricot) for basic membership, but these tools know nothing about apprenticeship hour tracking, safety certification expiration, or workforce dispatch. Apprenticeship management is handled in custom Access databases or state-mandated systems. Safety certification tracking is done in spreadsheets. Workforce availability is communicated via phone calls. The gap is a construction-trade-specific association platform that handles all three alongside standard membership functions.
