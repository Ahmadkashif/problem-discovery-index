# Safety Certification Expiration Monitor

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/construction-trade-groups/profile|Construction Trade Groups]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Compliance tracking tools like iAuditor and SafetyCulture monitor jobsite safety conditions but don't track individual worker certification expirations across OSHA 10/30, First Aid/CPR, confined space, fall protection, and trade-specific licenses.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
Construction workers carry 5-10 safety certifications that expire on different schedules: OSHA 10 (no expiration but many employers require refresher every 5 years), OSHA 30 (similar), First Aid/CPR (2 years), confined space (annual), fall protection (3 years), trade-specific licenses (varies by state). A trade association managing 500 members must track 2,500-5,000 individual certifications with different expiration dates. Currently this is done in spreadsheets — the association sends annual reminders ("check your certifications") but cannot proactively identify which specific members need which specific renewal. A member showing up to a jobsite with an expired confined space certification can shut down the entire project.

## What Already Exists
iAuditor/SafetyCulture handles jobsite inspections and audits. BIS Safety Software tracks safety training records for large contractors. Construction-specific HR platforms (ExakTime, HCSS) manage time and compliance for individual companies. However, none serve the trade association use case — tracking certifications across multiple employer members' workers, providing a cross-employer view of workforce certification health, and enabling the association to schedule group renewal classes when enough members need the same certification.

## The Customization Gap
The missing product is a certification monitoring system designed for trade associations: each member company uploads worker certification records (or workers upload directly via phone photo of certification card), the system tracks expiration dates across all certification types, sends proactive renewal reminders 90/60/30 days before expiration, and aggregates demand for renewal training ("23 members need OSHA 30 renewal in Q3 — schedule a class"). The association can also verify worker certifications when contractors request workforce referrals.

## Target Customer
Trade association administrators managing certification records for 200-1,000 member workers across 50-200 contractor members.

## Impact If Solved
Prevents jobsite shutdowns from expired certifications ($5K-$50K per incident), enables associations to proactively schedule group training (increasing non-dues revenue by $20K-$50K/year), and positions the association as the authoritative source of workforce certification data.
