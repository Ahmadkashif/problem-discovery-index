# Workforce Availability Telephone Tag

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/construction-trade-groups/profile|Construction Trade Groups]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When a contractor needs 3 journeyman electricians for a project starting Monday, they call the trade association, which calls available workers one by one — a process that takes hours or days and often fails because the best workers are already committed.
**Tags:** #recommendation #automation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Construction trade associations serve as informal dispatch centers — contractor members call looking for available skilled workers, and the association coordinator checks their mental list (or paper list) of available journeymen, then calls them one by one to gauge interest and availability. This "telephone tag" process takes 2-4 hours per request, during which the contractor is waiting to staff their job. The coordinator can only handle 3-5 dispatch requests per day, limiting the association's value as a workforce resource. Workers who are available but not top-of-mind get overlooked, while the same reliable workers get called first every time, leading to inequitable distribution of work.

## Why It's Still Broken
There is no digital system where workers can post their availability and contractors can search. The workforce is a mix of union and non-union workers with different dispatch norms. Workers don't update availability proactively because there's no easy way to do it. The coordinator's "who's available" knowledge is entirely in their head — when they go on vacation, dispatch stops. Previous attempts at online job boards failed because they required workers to log in to a website, which field workers won't do.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A text-message-based workforce availability system: workers text their status ("available starting Monday" or "booked through 3/20"), and the system maintains a real-time availability board. When a contractor needs workers, the coordinator searches by trade, certification, and availability, then sends a group text to matching workers. First to respond gets the referral. Workers can also set standing availability (e.g., "always available Fridays") via a simple text command. No app download, no login, no website — just SMS.

## Who Feels the Pain
Trade association coordinators who spend 3-4 hours per day on the phone playing matchmaker between contractors and workers, and skilled workers who miss opportunities because the coordinator didn't think to call them.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces workforce matching from 3 hours to 15 minutes per request, increases the coordinator's capacity from 5 to 20 dispatch requests per day, distributes work more equitably across the available workforce, and positions the association as the go-to workforce resource for its contractor members.
