# Unified Conference Lifecycle Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/conference-driven-associations/profile|Conference-Driven Associations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the full 12-month conference lifecycle — from call-for-proposals and speaker management through exhibit sales, sponsorship fulfillment, attendee engagement, and post-conference reporting — in a single system with a unified data model.
**Tags:** #ai-platform #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #automation

## The Problem
An association conference director uses 5-8 disconnected tools: OpenWater for abstract submissions, Cvent for registration and housing, a CRM for exhibit booth sales, a spreadsheet for sponsorship tracking, Whova for the mobile app, WordPress for the website, and Mailchimp for communications. When a speaker changes their session title, the director updates it in OpenWater, Cvent, Whova, the website, and the printed program. When a sponsor upgrades their package, the director updates the CRM, the exhibit floor plan tool, the website, the app, and the sponsorship fulfillment tracker. A single data change touches 4-6 systems, and the risk of inconsistency is constant.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Conference management spans too many functional domains (CRM, event registration, content management, mobile app, exhibit management) for any single vendor to build well. Cvent expanded from registration into more functions but remains registration-centric. Exhibit management companies (A2Z, Freeman) focus on the expo floor. Abstract management tools (OpenWater) focus on submissions. Building a truly unified platform requires deep expertise in all these domains simultaneously — a combination no founding team typically has.

## What to Build
A single-platform conference management system where: speakers submit and are managed through the proposal → review → acceptance → scheduling → delivery pipeline, exhibitors purchase and manage booths through the inquiry → contract → floor plan → logistics pipeline, sponsors purchase packages with automated deliverable tracking, attendees register and build personalized schedules, and the conference team manages all of this from a single dashboard with one data model. When a session changes, it updates everywhere simultaneously.

## Target Customer
Conference directors at associations where the annual conference generates $1M-$20M in revenue and uses 5+ disconnected tools to manage operations.

## Impact If Built
Eliminates the 15-20 hours per week conference staff spend synchronizing data across tools, prevents the 50+ data inconsistencies per conference that cause attendee complaints and sponsor dissatisfaction, and provides post-conference analytics that currently require manual data assembly from 5+ sources.
