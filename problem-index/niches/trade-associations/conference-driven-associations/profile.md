# Conference-Driven Associations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 19%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium-High — associations use event management platforms (Cvent, Whova) for registration and logistics but lack tools for the full conference lifecycle: speaker management, exhibit sales, sponsorship fulfillment, and attendee ROI measurement
**Target Buyer:** VP of Events / Conference Director at associations where conferences generate 40-70% of total revenue
**Automation Potential:** High — speaker management, session scheduling, exhibit booth assignment, and attendee matchmaking are all constraint-satisfaction problems well suited for automation

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
For many associations, the annual conference IS the business — generating 40-70% of total revenue through registration fees, exhibit booth sales, and sponsorships. A mid-size association conference (2,000-10,000 attendees) manages 200-500 session proposals, 50-200 speakers, 100-400 exhibit booths, and 20-50 sponsors. The conference team operates on a 12-month cycle with overlapping years (closing out this year's conference while opening registration for next year's). The operational complexity rivals that of a mid-size event production company, but the conference team is typically 3-8 people using tools designed for simpler events.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Cvent handles registration, housing, and logistics. Whova and Attendify provide mobile event apps. A2Z/Map Your Show manages exhibit floor plans. Abstract Scorecard or OpenWater handle session submissions. However, each tool handles one phase of the conference lifecycle, and data doesn't flow between them. The speaker who submitted an abstract in OpenWater must be manually entered into Cvent for registration, then manually added to Whova for the app schedule, then manually listed on the website. A single speaker change ripples through 4 systems.
