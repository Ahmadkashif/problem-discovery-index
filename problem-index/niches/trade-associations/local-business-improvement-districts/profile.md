**Automation Potential:** Medium — assessment billing, property database management, and performance reporting are automatable; community engagement and government relations require personal effort

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) are quasi-governmental entities where commercial property owners within a defined geographic area pay special assessments to fund services beyond what the city provides — street cleaning, security, beautification, marketing, and events. There are 1,200+ BIDs in the US managing $800M+ in annual assessments. BIDs are operationally distinct from chambers of commerce and trade associations: their funding is mandatory (authorized by local government), their membership is property-based (not voluntary), and their accountability is to both property owners and local government. No software exists for this specific organizational type.

## Current Tools & Gaps
BIDs use QuickBooks for financial management, Excel for assessment calculations, basic websites for public communication, and paper surveys for stakeholder engagement. Chamber management software (GrowthZone) doesn't fit because BIDs aren't membership organizations — they're assessment districts. Municipal software doesn't fit because BIDs aren't government agencies. The gap is complete — there is no BID-specific management platform, and BIDs cobble together generic tools for every function.
