## Why These Niches

Chambers of commerce and certification bodies represent the core revenue engines of the association world — chambers through membership dues and local influence, certification bodies through examination fees and credential maintenance. Construction trade groups and healthcare professional societies are digitally underserved relative to their economic significance: construction groups still coordinate apprenticeship programs and safety certifications via paper, while medical societies manage CME credits through outdated systems. BIDs and conference-dependent associations represent underserved segments — BIDs operate more like quasi-governmental entities than typical associations, while conference-heavy associations need event technology that goes far beyond generic AMS features. Advocacy/PAC work and continuing education delivery are highly automatable rule-based workflows. Excluded: large national associations with dedicated IT departments (AMA, ABA), union locals (different governance model), and social clubs (different purpose).
