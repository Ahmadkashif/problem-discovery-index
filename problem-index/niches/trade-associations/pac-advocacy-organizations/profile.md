# PAC & Advocacy Organizations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 8%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — advocacy platforms (Quorum, FiscalNote) serve large national organizations, but state and local advocacy is largely manual; grassroots campaign tools are underused
**Target Buyer:** Government Affairs Director / Lobbyist / PAC Treasurer
**Automation Potential:** High — legislative tracking, grassroots action alerts, PAC contribution compliance, and advocacy impact reporting are all rule-based and automatable

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Many trade associations maintain Political Action Committees (PACs) and run advocacy programs to influence legislation affecting their industry. The advocacy function involves: tracking legislation across federal and state legislatures, analyzing bill text for industry impact, mobilizing members for grassroots action (calls, emails, testimony), managing PAC contributions with FEC/state compliance, and reporting advocacy impact to the board. This function operates on legislative calendars (not the association's fiscal year), requires real-time response to bill introductions and committee hearings, and faces strict legal compliance requirements for PAC contributions and lobbying disclosure.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Quorum and FiscalNote provide legislative tracking, stakeholder management, and grassroots advocacy for large organizations ($20K-$100K+/year). Phone2Action (now Capitol Canary) handles grassroots campaigns. PAC compliance is managed through FEC filing software (NGP VAN, Aristotle) or manually. However, state-level trade associations and local advocacy groups cannot afford enterprise advocacy platforms and manage legislative tracking via Google Alerts, grassroots campaigns via email blasts, and PAC compliance in spreadsheets.
