# PAC Contribution Compliance Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/pac-advocacy-organizations/profile|PAC & Advocacy Organizations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** PAC treasurers at trade associations track contributions and disbursements in spreadsheets, risking FEC or state campaign finance violations because contribution limits, reporting deadlines, and disclosure requirements are complex, state-specific, and frequently updated.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #data-integration #quick-win

## The Problem
A trade association PAC collects voluntary contributions from members and disburses them to endorsed candidates. Federal PACs must comply with FEC rules (contribution limits, reporting schedules, disclosure requirements), and state PACs face 50 different sets of state campaign finance laws. A PAC treasurer at a state trade association must know: contribution limits per election per candidate (which vary by state and change with elections), reporting deadlines (monthly, quarterly, or per-transaction depending on state and election proximity), and disclosure thresholds. Most PAC treasurers are volunteer board members or the association's finance director, not campaign finance specialists. Errors can result in fines, FEC/state enforcement actions, and reputational damage.

## Why It's Still Broken
Campaign finance compliance software (NGP VAN, Aristotle) is designed for candidate campaigns and large PACs, not for the 5,000+ small trade association PACs that make $10K-$200K in annual contributions. These tools cost $5K-$25K/year and require training that a volunteer PAC treasurer won't complete. Small PACs track contributions in Excel and file reports manually on state filing portals — a process that takes 5-10 hours per reporting period and is error-prone.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A lightweight PAC compliance tool ($50-$150/month) that: tracks contributions by donor and candidate with automatic limit checking ("this donor has given $450 of their $500 limit to this candidate"), generates filing-ready reports in the format required by the applicable state or FEC, sends deadline reminders for upcoming reporting periods, and flags compliance issues in real time ("this contribution would exceed the per-election limit — do not accept"). The tool must be configurable for state-specific rules and usable by a non-specialist in 30 minutes.

## Who Feels the Pain
Volunteer PAC treasurers who dread reporting season, spend weekends assembling contribution records from email and bank statements, and worry constantly about making a compliance mistake that triggers an enforcement action.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces per-filing-period reporting from 8 hours to 1 hour, prevents contribution limit violations that average $2K-$10K in FEC fines, and makes the PAC treasurer role manageable enough that associations can actually find volunteers willing to serve.
