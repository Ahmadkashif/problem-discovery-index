# Member ROI Attribution Dashboard

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/state-level-chambers-of-commerce/profile|State-Level Chambers of Commerce]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool today can show a chamber member the dollar value they've received from membership — referrals, event connections, advocacy wins, and visibility — making renewal conversations a faith-based ask rather than a data-driven discussion.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #revenue-impact #ai-platform

## The Problem
When a chamber member asks "What do I get for my $2,000 dues?", the membership director has no quantified answer. They can recite benefits (networking events, directory listing, advocacy, health insurance access) but cannot attribute specific value: "You received 14 referrals through the directory, your event connections led to 3 introductions, and our advocacy on the zoning issue saved your industry $2M in compliance costs." This inability to demonstrate ROI is the #1 reason for non-renewal — members who can't see value won't renew, even if value exists.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
ROI attribution requires connecting data across multiple systems: website analytics (who clicked the member's directory listing), event systems (who did the member meet), referral tracking (which members referred business to each other), and advocacy outcomes (which legislative wins benefited which industries). No single AMS captures all these touchpoints, and building the attribution model requires defining "value" in ways that vary by member type (a restaurant values referrals; a law firm values networking; a manufacturer values advocacy).

## What to Build
A member ROI dashboard that aggregates engagement data from the AMS, website, event platform, and referral program, then calculates an estimated dollar value of membership by member type. For each member, the system shows: directory exposure (clicks, referral inquiries), event connections (attendees at the same events), advocacy impact (legislative actions that benefited their industry, prorated to dues level), and program utilization (discounts used, resources accessed). The chamber can share this dashboard with each member before renewal, making the ROI conversation data-driven.

## Target Customer
Membership directors at chambers with 500+ members and $500K+ in annual dues revenue who face 15-25% non-renewal rates.

## Impact If Built
Reduces non-renewal rate from 20% to 12% by enabling data-driven renewal conversations, increases dues revenue by $100K-$500K per year per mid-sized chamber, and identifies underengaged members 6 months before renewal for proactive outreach.
