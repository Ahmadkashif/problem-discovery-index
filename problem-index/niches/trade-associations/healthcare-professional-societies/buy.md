# Clinical Guideline Version Management

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/healthcare-professional-societies/profile|Healthcare Professional Societies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Document management tools like SharePoint and Confluence handle version control but cannot manage the clinical guideline development workflow — systematic evidence review, expert panel consensus, public comment periods, and multi-year update cycles with transparent methodology documentation.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #data-integration #nlp

## The Problem
Healthcare professional societies develop clinical practice guidelines that define standard-of-care for their specialty. A single guideline takes 18-36 months to develop, involving: systematic literature review, evidence grading, expert panel deliberation, draft writing, public comment period, revision, board approval, and publication. Once published, guidelines must be updated every 3-5 years. Societies managing 10-30 active guidelines track the development process in shared drives and email, with the managing editor maintaining a master timeline in Excel. When a panel member asks "What was the evidence basis for recommendation 3.2 in the 2019 version?", the answer requires 30 minutes of folder archaeology.

## What Already Exists
SharePoint and Confluence provide document versioning. GRADEpro supports evidence grading. Covidence manages systematic reviews. However, none handle the end-to-end guideline lifecycle: from evidence review through panel deliberation to public comment to final publication, with full version history showing how each recommendation evolved across versions and what evidence drove each change.

## The Customization Gap
The missing tool is a guideline lifecycle management system that: tracks the evidence basis for each recommendation (linked to the systematic review), manages expert panel deliberations (voting records, minority opinions, conflict-of-interest recusals), facilitates public comment collection and response, maintains version history showing exactly what changed between guideline editions and why, and publishes guidelines in multiple formats (PDF, web, mobile, EHR integration). Must comply with AGREE II guideline development standards.

## Target Customer
Guideline committee chairs and managing editors at medical specialty societies maintaining 5-30 active clinical guidelines with 3-5 year update cycles.

## Impact If Solved
Reduces guideline development cycle time by 6 months (from 30 months to 24 months), ensures transparent methodology documentation that protects against legal challenges, and enables rapid guideline updates when new evidence emerges (COVID demonstrated this need acutely).
