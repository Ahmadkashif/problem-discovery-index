# CME Commercial Support Compliance

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/healthcare-professional-societies/profile|Healthcare Professional Societies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Societies accepting commercial support (pharmaceutical/device company funding) for CME activities must maintain strict content independence and financial documentation per ACCME Standards for Integrity — currently tracked in spreadsheets that are audit nightmares.
**Tags:** #compliance #automation #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a pharmaceutical company provides $50,000 to support a CME symposium, ACCME requires that: the supporter has no influence on content, speaker selection, or educational methods; all financial relationships are disclosed; the support is documented in a written agreement with specific terms; and the society maintains a firewall between the funding source and the educational planning committee. Societies manage these requirements through email exchanges, Word document agreements, and spreadsheet tracking. During ACCME accreditation reviews (every 4-6 years), auditors request documentation for every commercially supported activity — and the CME office spends weeks assembling paper trails from email archives and shared drives.

## Why It's Still Broken
Commercial support compliance involves multiple stakeholders (industry relations staff, CME planners, faculty, legal) with different documentation responsibilities, and no single system coordinates them. The written agreement template is a Word document that gets customized per supporter and stored inconsistently. Financial reconciliation (did the supporter pay what they committed? were unused funds returned?) is tracked in QuickBooks, not connected to the CME activity record. The firewall documentation (proving the supporter didn't influence content) exists only as negative evidence — the absence of communication, which is impossible to document proactively.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A commercial support compliance module that: generates standardized support agreements from templates with ACCME-required terms, tracks agreement execution and payment milestones, documents the content independence firewall (who communicated with whom, what the planning committee decided independently), collects and manages faculty financial disclosure forms with conflict-of-interest review, and produces audit-ready compliance packages per activity. During ACCME review, the society can pull a complete compliance dossier for any activity in minutes, not weeks.

## Who Feels the Pain
CME directors who live in fear of ACCME accreditation reviews, spending 200+ hours preparing documentation that proves compliance for the past 4 years of commercially supported activities.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces ACCME audit preparation from 200 hours to 20 hours, eliminates the risk of accreditation findings from incomplete documentation (which can result in probation or loss of accreditation), and enables societies to accept more commercial support with confidence in their compliance infrastructure.
