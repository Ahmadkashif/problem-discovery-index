# CME Activity Lifecycle Manager

**Niche:** [[niches/trade-associations/healthcare-professional-societies/profile|Healthcare Professional Societies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/trade-associations|Trade Associations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform manages the full CME activity lifecycle — from educational needs assessment through content development, ACCME accreditation filing, delivery, evaluation, and credit reporting — in a single system that ensures compliance at every step.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #compliance #automation #data-integration

## The Problem
Developing an ACCME-accredited CME activity involves 12-15 discrete steps: conduct a needs assessment, identify practice gaps, define learning objectives, select faculty (with conflict-of-interest disclosures), secure commercial support (with independence requirements), develop content (with peer review), submit accreditation application, deliver the activity, collect evaluations, assess outcomes, report credits to state boards, and file a completion report with ACCME. Each step has specific ACCME requirements, and failure at any step can result in loss of accreditation. Societies manage this process in spreadsheets, Word documents, and email chains — with the CME director carrying the compliance knowledge in their head.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
ACCME accreditation standards are complex and change periodically. Building a compliant lifecycle manager requires deep understanding of ACCME criteria (Standards for Integrity and Independence, Activity Reporting requirements, PARS reporting), plus integration with credit reporting systems (state medical boards, specialty boards, ANCC for nurses). The market is niche — only 2,000 accredited CME providers in the US — and most are nonprofits with limited technology budgets. LMS vendors focused on content delivery, not compliance workflow.

## What to Build
A CME lifecycle platform that guides the planning committee through each phase: structured needs assessment templates, learning objective builders aligned to ACCME competency domains, automated conflict-of-interest disclosure collection and management, commercial support agreement tracking with independence firewalls, content review workflow, accreditation application generation, evaluation collection and outcomes assessment reporting, and automated credit reporting to PARS and state boards. Each step includes ACCME compliance checkpoints that prevent the team from advancing until requirements are met.

## Target Customer
CME directors at medical and healthcare professional societies managing 20-200 accredited activities per year, currently coordinating compliance across 5+ disconnected tools.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-activity administrative overhead from 25 hours to 8 hours, eliminates the accreditation compliance risk that comes from manual process management, and enables societies to scale their CME program from 30 activities/year to 80+ without adding staff.
