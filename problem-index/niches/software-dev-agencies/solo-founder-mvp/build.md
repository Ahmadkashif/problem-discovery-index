# Scope Agreement Visualization for Non-Technical Founders

**Niche:** [[niches/software-dev-agencies/solo-founder-mvp/profile|Solo-Founder MVP Agencies]]
**Industry:** [[industries/software-dev-agencies|Software Dev Agencies]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No product helps MVP agencies and their non-technical clients achieve shared understanding of what will be built, what will not be built, and what happens when the budget runs out mid-feature.
**Tags:** #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #revenue-impact

## The Problem
The number one source of conflict between MVP agencies and solo founders is scope misunderstanding. The founder describes their vision, the agency writes a proposal with feature bullet points, both parties sign, and development begins. Three weeks later, the founder says "I assumed the user profile would include social login, photo upload, and privacy settings" — features the agency categorized as a single "user profile" line item. The agency says "that's 3 separate features, each a week of work." The relationship deteriorates. This pattern repeats on 30-40% of MVP engagements because text-based proposals cannot convey scope boundaries to non-technical clients.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The problem seems trivial — "just write better proposals." But the challenge is deeper: non-technical founders cannot evaluate feature complexity from text descriptions. They do not understand that "search functionality" could mean a simple text filter (2 hours) or a full-text search with filters, autocomplete, and relevance ranking (3 weeks). The tool must translate technical scope into visual, interactive representations that founders can engage with and approve at a granularity that prevents surprises. This requires combining UX design principles with project scoping methodology — a niche intersection.

## What to Build
An interactive scope visualization tool where the agency maps each feature as a visual card with: a mockup or wireframe showing what the user will see, explicit inclusion/exclusion notes ("includes email login, does NOT include social login"), effort estimate displayed as relative size (small/medium/large), and dependencies. The founder reviews and approves each card, clicking through to see exactly what is included. Changes are tracked and priced. The tool generates a visual scope agreement that replaces the text-based proposal — the founder can look at the approved cards at any time during development to confirm alignment.

## Target Customer
MVP agencies running 2-8 person teams that deliver 10-30 projects per year in the $15K-$75K range, where scope disputes are the primary source of client dissatisfaction.

## Impact If Built
Reduces scope disputes by 60-70%, converting 30-40% dispute rate to 10-15%. Saves 15-20 hours per project in scope negotiation and change order processing. Improves client referral rates by 25-30% through clearer expectations.
