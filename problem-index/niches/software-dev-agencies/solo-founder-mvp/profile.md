# Solo-Founder MVP Agencies

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/software-dev-agencies|Software Dev Agencies]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 3%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — These small agencies use modern stacks (Next.js, Supabase, Vercel) but lack project management, client communication, and scoping tools designed for 2-8 person teams building MVPs in 4-12 week cycles.
**Target Buyer:** Boutique agency owners running 2-8 person teams focused on building MVPs, prototypes, and v1 products for solo founders, indie hackers, and early-stage startups.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — Scope documentation, contract generation, and handoff processes follow predictable patterns but vary significantly per project.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Solo-founder MVP agencies operate at the opposite end of the spectrum from enterprise dev shops. Projects are 4-12 weeks, budgets are $10K-$75K, clients are non-technical founders who cannot evaluate technical decisions, and the "requirements document" is often a Figma sketch and a 30-minute call. The agency must translate vague founder vision into buildable scope, manage expectations when the budget cannot support the vision, and deliver a working product that the founder can use to raise funding or acquire first users. The failure mode is not project overruns — it is scope disagreement: the founder expected X, the agency built Y, and neither realized the mismatch until delivery.

## Current Tools & Gaps
These agencies use Notion or Linear for project management, Figma for design, and GitHub for code. But enterprise project management tools (JIRA, Monday.com) are overkill for 4-week sprints with 3 developers. Proposal tools (PandaDoc, Proposify) are designed for agencies selling $100K+ engagements, not $25K MVPs. No tool addresses the unique workflow of MVP agencies: rapid scoping sessions, budget-constrained feature prioritization, founder education on technical tradeoffs, and structured handoff documentation when the founder takes the product in-house.
