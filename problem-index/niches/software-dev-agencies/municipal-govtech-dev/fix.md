# Government Payment Terms Cash Flow Crunch

**Niche:** [[niches/software-dev-agencies/municipal-govtech-dev/profile|Municipal & GovTech Development]]
**Industry:** [[industries/software-dev-agencies|Software Dev Agencies]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Government clients pay in 60-120 days while agency developers expect biweekly paychecks, creating a cash flow gap that forces small govtech agencies to turn down contracts they would otherwise win.
**Tags:** #revenue-impact #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
A 10-person dev agency wins a $600K municipal contract with net-90 payment terms and milestone-based billing. The agency invoices $100K at the first milestone (month 3), but receives payment at month 6. Meanwhile, the agency has spent $250K in payroll, benefits, and overhead for 6 months of work. The cash flow gap is $250K — potentially fatal for a small agency. Many govtech agencies have gone bankrupt not because they were unprofitable, but because payment timing mismatches consumed their working capital. Others simply refuse to bid on government work, leaving the market to large firms with treasury departments.

## Why It's Still Broken
Government payment terms are set by statute or policy and are not negotiable. Municipalities process payments through centralized finance departments with approval chains that add weeks to already-long payment cycles. Invoice disputes (the government PM questions a line item, the finance department requires additional documentation) can add months. Traditional factoring services charge 3-5% of invoice value — on already-thin agency margins (15-25%), this eats the profit. Government contracts often prohibit assignment of receivables, limiting traditional financing options.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A govtech-specific receivables financing platform that: (1) advances 80-90% of approved government invoices within 48 hours (government credit risk is extremely low, enabling lower discount rates than commercial factoring), (2) handles the complexity of government payment processing (understanding warrant systems, central payment offices, and government fiscal year constraints), (3) offers rates below traditional factoring (1-2% vs. 3-5%) because government default risk is near zero, and (4) integrates with government procurement portals to verify contract status and approval workflows, reducing underwriting costs.

## Who Feels the Pain
Owners of 5-25 person dev agencies who want to serve government clients but cannot absorb 90-120 day payment delays, and project managers at municipal agencies who lose strong vendor bids because small firms cannot afford the payment terms.

## Impact If Fixed
Enables small dev agencies to compete for $500K-$5M government contracts previously accessible only to large firms. At 1-2% financing cost vs. 3-5% factoring, saves $5K-$15K per $500K contract in financing costs. Expands the govtech vendor pool, increasing competition and reducing costs for municipalities.
