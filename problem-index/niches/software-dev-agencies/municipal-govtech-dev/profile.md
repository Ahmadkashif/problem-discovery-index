# Municipal & GovTech Development

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/software-dev-agencies|Software Dev Agencies]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $4B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 6%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Municipal clients use paper-based procurement, RFP evaluation committees with scoring spreadsheets, and payment terms of 60-120 days; agencies serving this market lack tools designed for government procurement cycles.
**Target Buyer:** Project managers and owners at dev agencies building citizen portals, permitting systems, utility billing platforms, and public records tools for city, county, and state governments.
**Automation Potential:** Medium — RFP response workflows and government reporting requirements are highly structured, but the procurement relationship management is inherently human-driven.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Dev agencies serving municipal government clients operate in a fundamentally different commercial environment than those serving enterprise or startup clients. Sales cycles are 6-18 months driven by formal RFP processes. Contracts are governed by public procurement law, requiring detailed documentation of decision rationale, often with mandatory subcontracting to minority/women-owned businesses (M/WBE). Payment terms are 60-120 days, straining agency cash flow. Requirements change slowly (annual budget cycles), but when they change, they are driven by political priorities, not user feedback. Agencies must navigate public records laws (any email to a government client may be subject to FOIA), accessibility requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA mandatory), and government-specific security frameworks (FedRAMP, StateRAMP).

## Current Tools & Gaps
GovWin and Bloomberg Government track government contract opportunities but do not help agencies respond to RFPs or manage govtech project delivery. Standard project management tools (JIRA, Asana) do not model RFP response workflows, M/WBE subcontracting requirements, or government acceptance testing processes. Govtech-specific agencies typically maintain internal templates for RFP responses, compliance checklists, and government communication protocols, with no shared tooling ecosystem.
