# Irrigation Specialists

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $8-10B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~9%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Smart controllers (Rachio, Hunter Hydrawise) have digitized the endpoint but the contractor workflow — design, install, audit, and management of commercial irrigation systems — remains paper-based. Most irrigation contractors hand-draw system diagrams and calculate hydraulics manually.
**Target Buyer:** Irrigation contractor or water management company owner, often a landscaper who specialized in irrigation and split off as a standalone business
**Automation Potential:** High — irrigation optimization is fundamentally a data problem (weather + soil moisture + plant water needs + system hydraulics), and smart controller hardware has created the sensor layer that optimization software can sit on top of

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Irrigation specialists occupy a technically demanding sub-segment of landscaping: designing sprinkler systems with proper hydraulic calculations (GPM, PSI, pipe sizing), installing controllers and valve manifolds, conducting water audits for commercial properties (measuring distribution uniformity and application rates), and increasingly integrating smart controllers that adjust watering schedules based on weather data. The niche is bifurcated: residential irrigation install-and-repair is a commoditized service ($2K-8K per new system install), while commercial irrigation management — auditing and optimizing water usage for HOAs, office parks, and municipal properties — is a recurring revenue opportunity with increasing regulatory pressure. Water conservation mandates in drought-prone states (California, Texas, Colorado, Arizona) now require documented water audits and smart controller adoption on commercial properties, creating compliance obligations that most irrigation contractors track with paper logs and spreadsheets.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Smart controllers (Rachio Pro, Hunter Hydrawise, Weathermatic) optimize watering schedules for individual properties based on weather and zone configuration, but they're property-management tools, not contractor-management tools — they don't help an irrigation contractor manage 50-200 commercial properties with different controllers, different compliance requirements, and different audit schedules. Rain Bird's IQ Central and Weathermatic's SmartLink offer centralized multi-site controller management but focus on controller programming, not on compliance documentation or water usage analytics. No tool connects soil moisture sensor data, weather data, plant water requirements by species, and irrigation system performance metrics into a unified optimization and compliance documentation platform for the contractor managing dozens of commercial properties.
