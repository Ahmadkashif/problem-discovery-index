# Smart Irrigation Optimization from Weather + Soil + Plant Data

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/irrigation-specialists/profile|Irrigation Specialists]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A platform that fuses weather forecasts, soil moisture sensor readings, plant water requirements by species, and irrigation system performance data to generate zone-by-zone watering schedules that reduce water consumption 30-50% while maintaining plant health — replacing the irrigation contractor's manual schedule adjustments with data-driven optimization across their entire portfolio.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #tacit-knowledge-ml #automation #compliance #data-integration

## The Problem
Irrigation contractors managing 50-200 commercial properties typically visit each site 4-6 times per year to manually adjust controller schedules based on seasonal conditions. An experienced irrigation tech walks a property, feels the soil, looks at the turf color, checks for runoff patterns, and adjusts run times by zone — a process that takes 30-60 minutes per property and relies entirely on the tech's tacit judgment about how much water this specific soil-plant-exposure combination needs right now. Between visits, the controller runs a fixed schedule that doesn't respond to actual conditions: it waters on schedule through a week of rain, under-waters during a heat wave, and over-waters shade zones that dry 40% slower than sun zones. Smart controllers like Rachio adjust for weather at the property level but apply uniform adjustments across all zones, ignoring the reality that Zone 3 (full sun, clay soil, warm-season turf) has completely different water needs than Zone 7 (shade, sandy soil, fern beds) on the same property. The experienced irrigation tech's ability to "read" a landscape and set zone-specific schedules is tacit knowledge that takes 5-10 years to develop and cannot be codified into static rules.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
True zone-level irrigation optimization requires fusing four data streams that currently live in separate systems: hyperlocal weather data (temperature, precipitation, evapotranspiration, wind), soil moisture data (from sensors that most properties don't have), plant water requirements (from horticultural databases that use different species naming conventions than irrigation zone maps), and irrigation system performance data (application rates, distribution uniformity, head spacing, nozzle GPM). The soil moisture sensor installation cost ($200-500 per zone) has historically made zone-level monitoring uneconomic for most properties. Plant identification per zone requires a site survey that maps actual plantings to irrigation zones — a manual process that changes as plants are added, removed, or mature. The optimization model must account for soil type (clay holds moisture longer than sand), slope (runoff risk), root depth by plant type (turf vs. shrubs vs. trees), and microclimate effects (buildings create wind tunnels and rain shadows) — all factors that experienced irrigation techs internalize but that resist simple parametric modeling.

## What to Build
A multi-property irrigation management platform for irrigation contractors. For each managed property: zone-level configuration captures plant types, soil type, exposure, and slope per zone (initially via tech survey, eventually via satellite imagery + plant identification). Low-cost soil moisture sensors ($50-100/zone, declining) are deployed on representative zones. The platform ingests weather data (OpenWeather, Tomorrow.io), soil moisture readings, and controller runtime logs via smart controller APIs (Rachio Pro, Hunter Hydrawise, Weathermatic). A gradient-boosted model predicts soil moisture depletion per zone as a function of weather, soil type, plant water demand, and irrigation application — essentially modeling what an experienced irrigation tech intuits when they "read" the landscape. The optimizer generates zone-by-zone watering schedules pushed directly to smart controllers, adjusting daily for forecast conditions. A portfolio dashboard shows water usage, savings vs. baseline, compliance status per property, and alerts for zones showing stress (soil moisture below threshold) or waste (soil moisture above field capacity after irrigation).

## Target Customer
Irrigation contractors and water management companies managing 30-200+ commercial properties with smart controllers, operating in water-regulated markets (California, Texas, Colorado, Arizona, Florida) where water conservation compliance creates both obligation and selling opportunity.

## Impact If Built
Reduces commercial irrigation water consumption by 30-50% across managed portfolios (documented savings support contractor marketing and client retention). Replaces 4-6 manual site visits per property per year with continuous automated optimization, saving 2-4 hours per property per year in tech time — worth $100K-300K in labor savings for a 100-property portfolio. Generates compliance documentation automatically for water authority audits.
