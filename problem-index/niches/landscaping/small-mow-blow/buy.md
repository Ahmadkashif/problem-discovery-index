# Jobber Lite for Solo Operators

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/small-mow-blow/profile|Small Mow & Blow]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Jobber is the most recommended software for small landscapers, but its Core plan at $60/month includes crew management, team scheduling, and multi-user features that a solo operator will never use — while missing the one thing they need most: a 15-minute setup that imports their existing client list and generates optimized routes from day one.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win #worker-facing

## The Problem
When a solo landscaper asks "what software should I use?" in any landscaping Facebook group, the top answer is Jobber. They sign up, see the 30-minute onboarding wizard asking about team size, service areas, tax settings, and notification preferences, and abandon the setup because they have 30 clients to mow tomorrow. Those who complete setup encounter a dashboard designed for a multi-employee operation: a dispatch board showing crew assignments, a calendar with color-coded team views, and a client database that requires manual entry of every client's name, address, phone, email, service frequency, and billing preferences. The features a solo operator actually uses — recurring job scheduling, invoicing, and a map of today's stops — are buried inside an interface built for the 10-employee company that Jobber's pricing model prefers.

## What Already Exists
Jobber's Core plan ($60/month) handles scheduling, invoicing, quoting, and client management with a mobile app for field use. It includes GPS job tracking, automated payment reminders, and client communication tools. The Grow plan ($120/month) adds lead management and review automation. Both plans include features a solo operator will never touch: team scheduling, crew dispatching, work request assignment, and employee GPS tracking.

## The Customization Gap
Three adaptations would make Jobber usable for solo operators. First, instant onboarding: a "Solo Mode" setup that imports the operator's phone contacts, uses address geocoding to place them on a map, lets the operator tag each with service frequency (weekly/biweekly), and generates a weekly schedule with optimized routes — total setup time under 15 minutes, not 2-3 hours. Second, simplified mobile interface: a daily view that shows only today's route in order with navigation, a "complete" button per stop, and an end-of-day invoice summary — no dispatch board, no team calendar, no crew assignment views. Third, aggressive pricing: a $20-30/month solo tier with the 3-4 features solo operators actually use, removing the features they don't. Jobber's current cheapest plan ($60/month) represents 0.8-1.5% of a solo operator's monthly revenue — viable but psychologically difficult when the operator was previously paying $0 for text messages and Venmo.

## Target Customer
Solo landscapers who have tried Jobber and abandoned it during onboarding, and solo landscapers who haven't tried any business software because they perceive it as too complex and expensive for their 20-40 client operation.

## Impact If Solved
Captures the estimated 200,000+ solo landscaping operators in the US who currently use no business software, representing a $50-70M annual revenue opportunity at $20-30/month. For the operator, reduces weekly business management overhead from 3-5 hours to under 1 hour and professionalizes their client communication — which directly impacts client retention rates.
