# Landscape Design & Install

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $15-20B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~17%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — Design software (Vectorworks, SketchUp, Realtime Landscaping Architect) is used by larger design-build firms, but most small-to-mid firms still present hand-drawn plans or basic 2D layouts. Estimating is disconnected from design in nearly every workflow.
**Target Buyer:** Landscape design-build firm owner (typically a landscape architect or experienced designer who runs crews)
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — property measurement, plant selection based on site conditions, material takeoff from design plans, and visualization can all be automated or AI-assisted

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Landscape design-install is project-based, not recurring — a homeowner pays $15K-150K+ for a complete landscape transformation including grading, hardscape (patios, retaining walls, walkways), planting, irrigation, and lighting. The sales cycle depends heavily on the client's ability to visualize the finished product, which is why design-build firms that produce high-quality 3D renderings close at 40-60% compared to 15-25% for firms presenting 2D plans or verbal descriptions. The design-to-estimate gap is the core workflow bottleneck: a designer spends 4-8 hours creating a landscape plan, then an estimator spends another 4-8 hours manually counting plants, calculating material quantities, pricing hardscape materials, and estimating labor — and the two processes are entirely disconnected. A design change (swapping a plant species, resizing a patio) requires a full re-estimate. Plant availability adds chaos — designers specify plants that nurseries may not have in stock, forcing substitutions that change pricing, spacing, and aesthetic intent.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Vectorworks Landmark and Land F/X are professional landscape CAD tools used by landscape architects, but they're complex and expensive ($3,000+/year). SketchUp with landscape plugins provides 3D modeling but no plant database integration. Realtime Landscaping Architect targets the consumer/small-firm market with template-based design. LMN and Aspire handle estimating but don't connect to any design tool — the designer creates a plan and the estimator manually counts every item from the drawing. No tool auto-generates material takeoffs from a landscape design, and no tool checks plant availability against local nursery inventory in real time.
