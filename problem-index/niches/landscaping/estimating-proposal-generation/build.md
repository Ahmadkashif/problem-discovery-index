# AI Property Measurement and Estimate from Satellite Imagery

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/estimating-proposal-generation/profile|Estimating & Proposal Generation]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A platform that takes a property address, automatically identifies and measures all landscape surfaces (lawn, beds, hardscape, hedge lines) from satellite and aerial imagery, calculates a service estimate using company-specific rates, and generates a branded proposal — reducing the 1-3 hour estimate process to 10 minutes without a site visit.
**Tags:** #semantic-segmentation #tacit-knowledge-ml #automation #revenue-impact #cnns #gradient-boosting

## The Problem
Every landscaping proposal starts with measurement: how many square feet of lawn, how many linear feet of edging, how many square feet of planting beds, how many linear feet of hedge, how many trees. For residential maintenance, the owner or salesperson drives to the property (15-30 minutes each way), walks the property with a measuring wheel (15-30 minutes), returns to the office, enters measurements into a calculator or spreadsheet, applies hourly rates, and generates a quote — 1-3 hours total for a job that might be $150-300/month. At that proposal-to-revenue ratio, companies can profitably produce only 3-5 estimates per day, creating a bottleneck that limits growth. Experienced estimators develop a tacit ability to look at a property and estimate service time without formal measurement — they've seen enough 8,000 sq ft lawns to visually identify one, and they know a property with 6 mature trees takes 10 minutes longer than one with 2. This tacit estimation skill takes 2-3 years to develop and is one of the reasons experienced salespeople generate higher close rates — their estimates are more accurate, so their pricing is more competitive without sacrificing margin.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Go iLawn demonstrates that satellite-based property measurement works for landscaping — users manually trace property boundaries and surface types on aerial imagery. The automation gap is in surface classification: automatically identifying which areas are mowed turf, which are planting beds, which are hardscape (driveway, patio, walkway), which are structures, and which are unmaintained (wooded areas, natural grass). This is a semantic segmentation problem on aerial imagery with challenging edge cases: a brick patio surrounded by lawn looks similar to a mulch bed from above, a gravel path is hard to distinguish from a gravel driveway, and tree canopy occludes the surfaces beneath it. Resolution matters — consumer satellite imagery (Google Maps, 30-50cm/pixel) is adequate for gross area measurement but insufficient for distinguishing edge types (concrete curb vs. landscape timber) that affect edging time estimates. The translation from measurement to estimate is also non-trivial: two 8,000 sq ft lawns may have very different service times based on obstacle density, slope, gate access, and shape complexity — factors that experienced estimators account for intuitively but that resist simple formula-based calculation.

## What to Build
An end-to-end estimation platform. Input: property address. The system pulls high-resolution aerial imagery (Nearmap, EagleView, or Google Aerial at 7-15cm/pixel for supported areas), runs a CNN-based semantic segmentation model to classify surfaces (mowed turf, planting beds, hardscape, structures, trees, hedge lines, fences), and calculates measurements for each surface type. A gradient-boosted regression model predicts service time from the measurements plus property characteristics (shape complexity, obstacle count, slope estimated from terrain data, gate access presence) — capturing the experienced estimator's tacit adjustment factors. The system applies the company's rate card (hourly crew cost, equipment allocation, overhead margin) to generate a service estimate. A proposal generator produces a branded PDF with aerial property photo, annotated measurement overlay, service descriptions, and pricing — ready for email delivery. The estimator reviews the auto-generated proposal in 5-10 minutes, makes any adjustments, and sends. For properties where aerial imagery is insufficient (heavy tree canopy, recent construction), the system flags for site visit rather than producing an inaccurate estimate.

## Target Customer
Residential and light-commercial landscaping companies with 3-20 employees that produce 10-30 estimates per week, where the estimation bottleneck limits their ability to grow the client base and where 15-25% of site visit estimates result in no-close (wasted trip).

## Impact If Built
Reduces average estimate time from 1-3 hours to 10-15 minutes, enabling 3-5x more estimates per day without additional staff. Eliminates the site visit for 60-70% of standard residential maintenance estimates, saving 30-60 minutes per estimate in drive time. Improves close rate by 10-15% through professional branded proposals with aerial imagery — differentiating from competitors who email a two-line text quote.
