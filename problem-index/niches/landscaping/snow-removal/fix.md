# Per-Event Documentation for Billing and Liability

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/snow-removal/profile|Snow Removal]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** Snow contractors' defense against slip-and-fall lawsuits and billing disputes depends on per-event documentation proving when they arrived, what they did, and what conditions existed — but this evidence is scattered across truck GPS logs, phone photos, and crew memory, often irretrievable when needed 2-3 years later in litigation.
**Tags:** #automation #compliance #data-integration #worker-facing

## The Problem
Slip-and-fall lawsuits on commercial properties are the existential risk of snow removal: a single claim can produce $500K-2M in damages, and the snow contractor is typically the first defendant named. The contractor's defense rests entirely on documenting that they performed service according to contract terms — arrival time, departure time, service performed (plowing, salting, or both), material quantities applied, and site conditions at departure. Today, this documentation consists of: truck GPS tracks (proving the truck was on-site but not what it did), timestamped photos taken on the plow operator's personal phone (showing cleared conditions but only if the operator remembered to take them and hasn't changed phones in the 2-3 years before the lawsuit reaches discovery), and the operator's memory (unreliable after thousands of similar events). Billing disputes are the daily version of the same problem: a property manager claims the contractor billed for a push that didn't happen, or disputes the accumulation tier, and the contractor has no verifiable evidence beyond "we were there." Per-push contracts generate 30-60 billable events per season per property — tracking and documenting each one manually is a full-time administrative job that most snow contractors handle with a part-time bookkeeper and wishful thinking.

## Why It's Still Broken
The documentation must be created during the event — at 3am in a snowstorm — by plow operators whose priority is clearing lots quickly, not taking photos and logging data. Any system that requires more than 10 seconds of operator interaction per property will be bypassed under the time pressure of a multi-property storm route. Automatic GPS geofence logging captures arrival and departure but doesn't document service quality or conditions — a truck idling in a parking lot for 5 minutes to take a phone call is indistinguishable from 5 minutes of plowing in the GPS record. Photo evidence requires the operator to exit the truck cab in the cold, walk to a vantage point, and take clear photos in dark, snowy conditions — a process most operators skip after the first few properties in a long storm night. The storage and retrieval problem is equally critical: even when photos exist, they're on personal phones that get replaced every 2-3 years, stored in camera rolls with thousands of unrelated photos, with no metadata linking them to specific properties, contracts, or storm events.

## What a Fix Looks Like
A dashcam-plus-app system purpose-built for snow documentation. A wide-angle dashcam mounted on each plow truck automatically captures timestamped, GPS-tagged video of every property visit — arrival conditions, plowing in progress, and cleared conditions at departure. The app, running on a truck-mounted tablet, auto-detects property entry via GPS geofence, prompts the operator for a one-tap service confirmation ("Plow only" / "Plow + Salt" / "Salt only"), and logs salt quantity from a connected spreader sensor or manual entry. At departure, the system captures a 10-second "completion clip" from the dashcam. All evidence is automatically uploaded to cloud storage and indexed by property, date, event, and operator — creating a searchable, defensible service record that persists regardless of phone changes, employee turnover, or time elapsed. For billing, each event generates an invoice-ready record with timestamp, service type, material quantities, and weather data (official accumulation from nearest weather station) — eliminating manual event logging and accumulation-tier disputes.

## Who Feels the Pain
Plow operators asked to document service in brutal conditions when their priority is finishing the route, company owners who discover critical documentation gaps only when served with a lawsuit 2 years after the event, and administrative staff who spend 20-30 hours per month reconciling service records with invoices because the raw data is incomplete and disorganized.

## Impact If Fixed
Creates litigation-defensible service records automatically, reducing slip-and-fall claim exposure by providing the timestamp-plus-video evidence that settles 70-80% of claims before trial. Reduces per-storm administrative documentation time from 4-8 hours to under 30 minutes. Eliminates billing disputes on per-push contracts by linking every invoice line item to weather-verified, GPS-verified, photo/video-verified service evidence.
