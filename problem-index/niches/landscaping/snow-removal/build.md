# Snow Event Prediction and Crew Pre-Positioning

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/snow-removal/profile|Snow Removal]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A platform that combines hyperlocal weather forecasting with contract-specific trigger thresholds and crew availability to pre-position equipment and notify crews 12-24 hours before snow events — replacing the frantic 3am group-text scramble with planned, staged responses that reduce response times and overtime costs.
**Tags:** #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #tacit-knowledge-ml #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
Snow removal operations run on reaction: the owner checks the weather forecast at 10pm, decides whether to call crews at 3am, and dispatches via group text to whoever responds. Experienced snow contractors develop an intuitive sense of which weather patterns produce real accumulation versus which fizzle — a "2-4 inches" forecast from the National Weather Service can mean 1 inch in the north side of the metro and 5 inches in the south due to elevation, lake effect, or urban heat island patterns. This microclimate judgment is tacit knowledge built over 10-20 winters and determines whether the contractor pre-positions equipment (moving plows to commercial lots the evening before) or waits until accumulation hits trigger depth. Getting it wrong in either direction is expensive: pre-positioning for a storm that doesn't materialize wastes $2K-5K in crew standby costs, while failing to pre-position for a real storm means missing contract response time SLAs (typically 2-4 hours post-trigger) and paying premium overtime rates to scramble crews. The decision also varies by contract: per-push contracts incentivize waiting (every push costs money), while per-season contracts incentivize early response (fixed revenue, lower liability risk). Balancing these competing incentives across 30-80 commercial contracts during a developing storm is a cognitive load that experienced contractors manage through intuition and decades of local weather pattern knowledge.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Hyperlocal snow accumulation forecasting is a harder problem than general weather prediction. Official NWS forecasts cover county-level zones that can span 20+ miles with dramatically different accumulation outcomes. Commercial weather APIs (Tomorrow.io, DTN) offer better spatial resolution but still produce 2-4 inch uncertainty bands that don't help a contractor decide between "deploy at midnight" and "wait until 6am." The contractor's local microclimate knowledge — knowing that the office park on the hill gets 30% more accumulation than the shopping center in the valley 3 miles away — is exactly the kind of tacit pattern recognition that a model could learn from historical data but that no weather service captures. Building the prediction model requires historical accumulation data at the individual property level, which no contractor systematically records — they know it snowed "a lot" or "not much" but didn't measure accumulation at each site. The crew pre-positioning optimization requires solving a stochastic scheduling problem where both the event timing and the event magnitude are uncertain, subject to contract-specific trigger thresholds and response time requirements.

## What to Build
A snow operations planning platform with three layers. First, hyperlocal accumulation prediction: ingest commercial weather API forecasts (Tomorrow.io or DTN), augment with historical accumulation data collected from contractors' per-event service records (measuring actual cleared snow depth as a training signal), and train a gradient-boosted model to predict accumulation at the individual property level based on location, elevation, proximity to water bodies, and historical over/under-prediction patterns for that site. Second, contract-aware dispatch planning: for each predicted accumulation range, determine which contracts trigger service (2" threshold contracts vs. 4" threshold contracts), calculate optimal crew deployment timing to meet response time SLAs across the portfolio, and generate pre-positioning recommendations ("move Truck 3's plow to the Main Street depot by 10pm for north-side priority properties"). Third, crew notification and confirmation: automated crew alerts 12-24 hours before predicted events with shift assignments, equipment assignments, and route sequences — with confirmation tracking so the contractor knows at 8pm who's confirmed for the 3am callout instead of discovering at 3am that two operators aren't answering their phones.

## Target Customer
Snow removal contractors and landscaping companies with snow overlay managing 20-80+ commercial contracts in northern US and Canadian markets, where missed response times trigger contract penalties and slip-and-fall exposure.

## Impact If Built
Reduces missed response time SLAs by 50-70% through proactive pre-positioning rather than reactive scrambling. Cuts crew standby costs by 20-30% by improving storm prediction accuracy at the portfolio level — deploying only when and where accumulation will actually trigger contracts. Eliminates the 3am "who's available?" group text scramble that causes crew burnout and seasonal employee attrition.
