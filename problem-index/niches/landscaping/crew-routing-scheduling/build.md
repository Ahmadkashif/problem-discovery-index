# Dynamic Daily Route Optimizer

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/crew-routing-scheduling/profile|Crew Routing & Scheduling]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A constrained vehicle routing optimizer purpose-built for landscaping that assigns crews to properties, sequences stops to minimize drive time, respects equipment and access constraints, and re-optimizes in real time when weather, cancellations, or crew absences disrupt the plan — replacing the operations manager's 30-60 minute daily planning ritual with a 2-minute review-and-dispatch workflow.
**Tags:** #tacit-knowledge-ml #gradient-boosting #time-series-forecasting #automation #workflow-orchestration

## The Problem
A landscaping company with 6 crews and 400 properties schedules each property weekly or biweekly, producing 80-120 property visits per day distributed across crews. The operations manager assigns these visits manually each morning: mentally grouping properties by geography, matching crew capabilities to property requirements (Crew 1 has the 72" mower for large commercial lots, Crew 4 specializes in detail work for high-end residential), accounting for property-specific constraints (the apartment complex requires parking lot access before 8am, the corner lot on Elm requires the crew to park on the side street), and sequencing each crew's stops to minimize driving. This process takes 30-60 minutes of focused attention, produces routes that are 20-40% longer than optimal (based on GPS studies of actual vs. optimal landscaping routes), and creates a single point of failure — when the ops manager is sick, on vacation, or quits, nobody else knows how to build efficient routes. The tacit knowledge is layered: the ops manager doesn't just know geography, they know that Property #47 takes 15 minutes longer after rain because the backyard floods, that the HOA on Maple Drive has a 4pm noise ordinance, and that the fastest route through the Westside neighborhood uses the service entrance behind the strip mall. None of this is written down.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The algorithmic foundation (vehicle routing with time windows) is well-solved — OR-Tools, OptaPlanner, and commercial solvers handle the math efficiently. What's missing is the constraint modeling layer specific to landscaping. Generic routing tools model stops as points with time windows; landscaping routing requires modeling: (1) crew-equipment-property matching (which crew has the equipment this property requires), (2) variable service duration by property, season, and weather conditions (a property that takes 25 minutes in July takes 45 minutes during fall leaf cleanup), (3) property-specific access constraints (gate codes, parking rules, noise ordinances, client preferences), (4) crew skill matching (some properties require certified pesticide applicators or irrigation technicians), and (5) dynamic replanning when rain, crew absences, or emergency callouts disrupt the daily plan mid-route. Building accurate time-per-property predictions requires 6-12 months of historical GPS and job completion data per property, which most companies don't have in structured form. The training data for the tacit constraints (property access rules, neighborhood navigation tricks) exists only in the ops manager's head and must be explicitly elicited and encoded.

## What to Build
A routing optimization platform that starts with structured property data import (address, lot size, service level, equipment requirements, access constraints — entered once during onboarding). GPS tracking of crew vehicles provides historical time-per-property and drive-time data, building a gradient-boosted regression model that predicts service duration per property adjusted for day of week, season, recent precipitation, and crew assignment. The optimizer runs the constrained vehicle routing problem nightly, generating next-day route assignments that minimize total portfolio drive time while respecting all constraints. Each morning, the ops manager reviews the proposed routes (displayed as maps with stop sequences and predicted crew-day durations), makes adjustments if needed, and dispatches with one tap — a 2-minute workflow replacing the 30-60 minute manual planning session. During the day, real-time re-optimization handles disruptions: a cancellation text from a client removes the stop and re-sequences the remaining route, a crew member calling in sick redistributes their properties across other crews, and a rain alert triggers the cascade rescheduling engine (see Fix card). Over time, the model learns the ops manager's adjustment patterns — which crew they reassign first, which properties they prioritize when routes are disrupted — encoding the tacit decision-making into the optimization.

## Target Customer
Landscaping companies with 3-15 crews and 150-800 properties on recurring service, where the operations manager spends 30-60 minutes daily on route planning and route quality directly impacts crew productivity and profitability.

## Impact If Built
Reduces daily route planning time from 30-60 minutes to under 5 minutes. Decreases total portfolio drive time by 20-35% based on optimization modeling of typical landscaping route structures — saving 30-60 minutes per crew per day, equivalent to 1-3 additional properties per crew per day without additional labor cost. Eliminates single-point-of-failure risk on routing knowledge.
