# Residential Maintenance

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $35-40B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~35%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Jobber adoption is common in the 5-15 crew range, but most scheduling is still whiteboard-based for smaller operators. Route optimization is almost entirely manual, done by an operations manager who "knows" which neighborhoods cluster well.
**Target Buyer:** Residential lawn care company owner (typically started as a solo mower, now manages 3-10 crews)
**Automation Potential:** High — weekly recurring service on fixed routes with predictable time-per-property creates ideal conditions for route optimization, crew productivity tracking, and automated client communication

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Residential maintenance is the bread-and-butter of landscaping: weekly mowing, edging, and blowing for 200-800 residential properties on recurring monthly contracts ($150-400/month per property depending on lot size and services). The revenue model is subscription-based, which makes it predictable but margin-thin — the difference between a profitable and unprofitable company is often crew efficiency measured in minutes-per-property. An experienced operations manager builds routes by intuition over years, clustering properties by neighborhood, accounting for gate access delays, equipment requirements per lot size, and drive time between stops. This route knowledge is tacit — when the ops manager leaves, the company loses months of efficiency while someone else rebuilds the routes through trial and error. Seasonal add-ons (fertilization, aeration, leaf cleanup, holiday lighting) create upsell opportunities that most companies pursue haphazardly through mass emails rather than targeted recommendations based on property characteristics and client spending history.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Jobber ($60-250/month) handles scheduling, invoicing, and basic CRM but has no route optimization — the map view shows pins but doesn't sequence them. LMN provides estimating and time tracking but is built for design-build, not recurring maintenance. ServiceTitan launched a landscaping vertical but pricing ($400+/month per tech) prices out most maintenance companies. Route optimization tools like OptimoRoute exist generically but don't understand landscaping-specific constraints: crew-of-three with a specific truck-and-trailer setup, properties that require a 60-inch mower vs. only a 36-inch walk-behind, properties where the backyard gate is locked and requires a client text to open. No tool connects route efficiency data to per-property profitability — companies don't know which properties are money-losers until they do a manual analysis.
