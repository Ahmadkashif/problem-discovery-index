# SLA Compliance Monitoring with Photo Documentation

**Niche:** [[niches/landscaping/commercial-grounds/profile|Commercial Grounds]]
**Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** A system that automatically verifies SLA compliance on commercial grounds contracts by matching timestamped, geotagged crew photos against contract-specific service standards — replacing the account manager's weekly drive-by inspection with continuous, auditable documentation.
**Tags:** #tacit-knowledge-ml #automation #compliance #revenue-impact #cnns #gradient-boosting

## The Problem
Commercial landscaping contracts specify service levels in granular detail: "Turf mowed to 3-3.5 inches weekly April-October, biweekly November-March. Edging along all hardscape surfaces within 24 hours of each mowing. Seasonal color rotations installed by March 15, June 1, September 15, and November 15. Debris and litter removed from all common areas within 4 hours of crew arrival." Compliance with these SLAs determines contract renewal — and the most common reason commercial landscapers lose contracts is not poor work but inability to prove consistent work. Today, an account manager drives the property 1-2 times per week, takes phone photos of problem areas, and reports verbally to the property manager. There is no structured record linking each SLA clause to evidence of compliance across the contract term. When a property manager claims "the edging has been poor for months" at renewal time, the landscaper has no data to confirm or refute the claim. An experienced account manager develops a tacit sense of which properties are "at risk" based on the tone of property manager communications, the frequency of complaints, and visual cues from drive-bys — but this judgment doesn't scale and doesn't transfer when account managers turn over.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
SLA compliance verification requires connecting three data streams that currently live in separate systems: contract terms (in PDF or Word documents, rarely structured), service execution data (in Aspire or similar as dispatch records), and visual evidence (in crew phone photo galleries). The computer vision challenge is specific: a CNN needs to distinguish between "properly edged sidewalk" and "improperly edged sidewalk" in outdoor photos with variable lighting, angles, and seasonal conditions — a task that even experienced account managers disagree on 15-20% of the time. Training data is scarce because no company has been systematically photographing and labeling SLA compliance states. The business model challenge is also real: the platform needs to serve both the landscaper (who wants to prove compliance) and potentially the property manager (who wants to verify compliance), creating a two-sided value proposition that's harder to sell.

## What to Build
A mobile-first compliance documentation platform. Crews are prompted to take 3-5 photos at each property visit following a contract-specific shot list (front entrance, parking lot edges, seasonal color beds, turf close-up). Photos are geotagged and timestamped automatically. A CNN model trained on labeled landscape maintenance photos classifies each photo by SLA category (mowing quality, edging quality, debris status, seasonal color status) and flags potential non-compliance for account manager review before photos are submitted to the compliance record. Over time, the model learns property-specific standards — what "acceptable edging" looks like at this specific property, not generically. A dashboard aggregates compliance scores per SLA clause per property over time, generating contract renewal documentation automatically: "Property X — 96% mowing SLA compliance, 91% edging compliance, 100% seasonal color compliance over 24 months." A gradient-boosted model predicts contract renewal risk based on compliance score trends, complaint frequency, property manager communication sentiment, and contract pricing relative to market rates.

## Target Customer
Commercial landscape maintenance companies with 10-50+ commercial contracts generating $1-20M in annual revenue, where each contract represents $50K-500K/year and losing a single contract creates a material revenue gap.

## Impact If Built
Increases contract renewal rates from the industry average of 70-80% to 85-95% by providing irrefutable compliance documentation, worth $200K-1M in retained revenue for a mid-size commercial landscaper. Reduces account manager windshield time on drive-by inspections by 60-70%, freeing 10-15 hours per week for client relationship management and new business development.
