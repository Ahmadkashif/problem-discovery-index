# Commercial Grounds

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/landscaping|Landscaping]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $25-30B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Aspire and BOSS LM serve the upper market, but most mid-size commercial grounds companies still track SLA compliance manually with paper checklists and weekly drive-by inspections by account managers.
**Target Buyer:** Commercial grounds maintenance company owner or operations director managing multi-year contracts with HOAs, office parks, retail centers, and municipal properties
**Automation Potential:** High — SLA compliance is measurable and photo-documentable, contract renewal risk follows predictable patterns, and service verification can be automated with GPS and timestamped photo evidence

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Commercial grounds maintenance operates on multi-year contracts (typically 2-3 years) with SLA clauses specifying mowing frequency, edging standards, seasonal color rotations, debris removal timelines, and snow response triggers. The revenue per account is 10-50x residential ($2K-25K/month per property), but the sales cycle is 3-6 months with competitive bidding, and losing a single contract can represent 10-20% of annual revenue. The core challenge is proving compliance — property managers at HOAs and commercial portfolios evaluate landscapers primarily on whether they consistently meet the contracted service standards. Today, compliance is "proven" through weekly drive-by inspections by an account manager who takes photos on their phone, files them nowhere searchable, and hopes the property manager doesn't dispute a service claim. When contract renewal season arrives, companies have no structured documentation to demonstrate their track record, and property managers default to rebidding because they can't distinguish between vendors on anything other than price.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Aspire ($1,000+/month) is the only platform built specifically for commercial landscape operations, handling estimating, scheduling, job costing, and invoicing. BOSS LM and Landscape Management Network (LMN) cover similar ground at lower price points. All three track whether a crew was dispatched to a property but none verify whether the SLA was actually met — a crew that mows but forgets to edge the sidewalks still shows as "completed." Photo documentation exists in crew members' phone galleries, unsearchable and unlinked to contract SLA terms. No tool scores compliance at the SLA-clause level or predicts contract renewal risk based on service history patterns.
