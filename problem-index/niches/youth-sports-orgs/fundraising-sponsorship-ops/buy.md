# Per-Family Fundraising Credit Tracker

**Niche:** [[niches/youth-sports-orgs/fundraising-sponsorship-ops/profile|Fundraising & Sponsorship Operations]]
**Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** QuickBooks tracks team-level fundraising income, but can't allocate per-family fundraising credits — the Smith family sold $400 in candy bars and earned $160 toward their season fees — without hours of manual spreadsheet reconciliation.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
Many youth sports teams offer "earn your way" fundraising: families participate in fundraising activities, and a percentage of what they raise is credited toward their season fees. The treasurer must track every family's fundraising contribution across 3-8 fundraisers per season, calculate the credit percentage (typically 40-60% of revenue raised), apply credits against each family's balance, and communicate updated balances. With 15 families and 5 fundraisers, this creates 75+ individual credit transactions per season — all tracked manually in a spreadsheet. The most common dispute in youth sports team finances is "how much fundraising credit did our family earn?" — and the treasurer often can't answer precisely.

## What Already Exists
QuickBooks and Wave track organizational income and expenses. Snap! Raise handles online fundraising with individual participant pages. Team Snap allows payment collection. But none connect fundraiser results to per-family credit allocation within a team financial system. QuickBooks treats all fundraising revenue as team income — it has no concept of "this portion was raised by Family A and should reduce their balance." Snap! Raise tracks per-participant fundraising but doesn't connect to team billing. The credit allocation step is always manual.

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is a fundraising-to-billing credit pipeline: (1) per-family fundraising contribution tracking across all fundraiser types (product sales, online campaigns, events), (2) configurable credit calculation (flat percentage, tiered, or capped), (3) automatic application of earned credits against each family's team fee balance, (4) family-facing portal showing fundraising activity, credits earned, and remaining balance, (5) real-time fundraiser leaderboard to encourage participation, and (6) end-of-season report showing total fundraising by family with credit allocations.

## Target Customer
Team treasurers managing per-family fundraising credits across 12-20 families and 3-8 fundraisers per season who currently reconcile credits in spreadsheets.

## Impact If Solved
Eliminates 3-5 hours of manual credit reconciliation per fundraiser (saving 15-25 hours per season), reduces family financial disputes by 70-80% through transparent tracking, and increases fundraising participation by 20-30% through gamified family-level dashboards.
