# Fundraising & Sponsorship Operations

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Category:** Low Digitized

## Profile
**Market Size:** $2.5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 12%
**Digital Adoption:** Low — Fundraising runs on cash envelopes, handwritten order forms, and personal Venmo requests. Sponsorship deals are handshake agreements with no tracking, fulfillment verification, or renewal pipeline.
**Target Buyer:** Team Treasurer / Booster Club President
**Automation Potential:** High — Fundraiser tracking, donor receipting, sponsor fulfillment verification, and financial reporting are all rule-based workflows currently done manually with paper and spreadsheets.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Youth sports fundraising is a $2.5B+ industry operating on infrastructure from the 1980s. Every travel team, rec league, and booster club runs 3-8 fundraisers per year (candy bar sales, car washes, restaurant nights, silent auctions, online campaigns) plus solicits 5-20 local business sponsors. The team treasurer (a volunteer parent) tracks fundraising revenue in a spreadsheet, allocates credits to families based on participation, manages sponsor deliverables (banner display at games, logo on jerseys, social media mentions), and generates financial reports for the organization's board. This $20K-100K per team per year flows through one person's personal spreadsheet with minimal oversight, creating both operational burden and accountability risk.

## Current Tools & Gaps
GoFundMe and Snap! Raise handle online fundraising campaigns. Square and PayPal handle payment collection. QuickBooks handles accounting for larger organizations. But no tool manages the complete youth sports fundraising lifecycle: tracking per-family fundraising participation and credit allocation, managing sponsor contracts and fulfillment deliverables, reconciling cash and digital payments across multiple simultaneous fundraisers, and generating the financial transparency reports that build trust with families who are collectively investing $50K-100K.
