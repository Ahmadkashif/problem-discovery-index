# Weekly Practice Plan Generator for Volunteer Coaches

**Niche:** [[niches/youth-sports-orgs/volunteer-coach-networks/profile|Volunteer Coach Networks]]
**Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No tool generates a complete, age-appropriate, time-blocked practice plan for a volunteer coach who knows nothing about the sport — telling them exactly what to do for 90 minutes with 14 kids, including setup instructions, coaching cues, and transitions.
**Tags:** #recommendation #llm #automation #ai-platform #worker-facing

## The Problem
A first-time volunteer soccer coach with practice Thursday at 5:30 PM needs to know: what drills to run, in what order, for how long, with what equipment, what to say during each drill, and how to transition between activities. They need this at a level of detail that a professional coach would find insulting — but volunteer coaches aren't professional coaches. They're parents who need a script. Currently, they Google "youth soccer practice drills," watch 3-4 YouTube videos, scribble notes on paper, and improvise. The result is disorganized practices that frustrate kids, waste time, and make the coach feel incompetent — the #1 driver of volunteer coach dropout.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
Coaching education organizations (NAYS, PCA, US Soccer) focus on teaching coaching philosophy and generic skill development, not generating specific practice plans. Drill databases (SportsSessionPlanner, CoachUp) provide individual drills but not complete sequenced practice plans with warm-up, skill focus, game application, and cool-down. Building a practice plan generator requires sport-specific curriculum knowledge (what skills should 8-year-olds learn in week 4 vs. week 1?), age-appropriate drill selection, time-blocking across a session, and enough detail that a non-expert can execute. This cross-disciplinary content requires input from coaching education experts that tech companies don't typically access.

## What to Build
A practice plan generator that: (1) asks the coach for sport, age group, number of players, available equipment, and practice duration, (2) generates a complete time-blocked practice plan with warm-up, 2-3 skill drills, a game-application activity, and cool-down, (3) includes setup diagrams, coaching cues (what to say), common mistakes to watch for, and modifications for different skill levels, (4) progresses through a season-long curriculum (week 1: dribbling, week 2: passing, etc.) so skills build logically, (5) allows the coach to swap individual drills within the plan, and (6) includes a "print and bring" PDF format.

## Target Customer
First-time and returning volunteer coaches (2.5M annually) who want to provide a good experience for kids but don't know how to plan an effective practice.

## Impact If Built
Transforms the volunteer coaching experience from stressful improvisation to confident execution, reducing first-year volunteer coach dropout from 40% to 15-20% and directly improving the youth sports experience for millions of children whose development depends on their volunteer coach's preparation quality.
