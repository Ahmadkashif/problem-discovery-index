## Why These Niches

Travel tournament teams and rec league operators represent the two dominant organizational models in youth sports — high-commitment, competitive, family-funded travel teams vs. community-based, volunteer-run recreational leagues — with fundamentally different operational needs and price sensitivities. Volunteer coach networks and special needs adaptive leagues serve populations systematically underserved by youth sports technology: volunteers who receive zero tooling or training support, and families of children with disabilities who find that every registration form, scheduling system, and communication tool assumes able-bodied, neurotypical participants. Multi-sport complexes and fundraising operations represent the digitization frontier: complexes managing 10-20 fields/courts with overlapping schedules and tournaments using whiteboards and spreadsheets, and fundraising operations running on cash envelopes and handwritten spreadsheets despite handling $20K-100K per team per year. Field scheduling and registration/draft systems are the two highest-volume, most rule-constrained administrative workflows in youth sports — consuming hundreds of volunteer hours annually that could be automated.
