# Tournament Logistics and Travel Coordination Platform

**Niche:** [[niches/youth-sports-orgs/travel-tournament-teams/profile|Travel Tournament Teams]]
**Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Build (Greenfield Opportunity)
**One-liner:** No platform helps a travel team manager compare tournament options, register across multiple tournament platforms, coordinate hotel blocks, and manage travel logistics for 15-20 families attending 20+ tournaments per season.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #data-integration #ai-agent #quick-win

## The Problem
A travel team manager spends 5-10 hours per tournament on logistics: researching tournament options (comparing entry fees, locations, competition levels, field quality reviews), registering on the tournament's specific platform (every tournament uses a different registration system), negotiating hotel block rates, polling families on availability, coordinating carpools, distributing schedules, and managing the inevitable changes (bracket updates, rain delays, field changes). With 20+ tournaments per season, this represents 100-200 hours of unpaid volunteer work. Tournament selection alone requires comparing 50+ options across multiple websites with no standardized format.

## Why Nobody Has Built This
The tournament landscape is fragmented: thousands of tournament operators use dozens of different registration platforms (Exposure Events, TourneyMachine, BrainyBird, custom websites). There's no aggregated database of tournaments by sport, age group, location, and competitive level. Hotel block coordination requires B2B relationships with hotels that a software platform would need to establish market by market. The buyer (volunteer team manager) has zero budget — the product must be funded through tournament operator fees, hotel commissions, or team subscription.

## What to Build
A tournament lifecycle platform that: (1) aggregates tournament listings by sport, age group, region, and competitive level from major registration platforms, (2) enables side-by-side comparison (entry fee, distance, past reviews, competition quality), (3) manages registration across different tournament platforms from a single interface, (4) coordinates hotel blocks with negotiated group rates, (5) polls families on availability and attendance, (6) auto-distributes schedules and bracket updates as they're published, and (7) tracks per-family tournament costs.

## Target Customer
Travel team managers (volunteer parents) coordinating logistics for 12-20 family teams attending 15-25 tournaments per season.

## Impact If Built
Reduces per-tournament logistics time from 5-10 hours to 1-2 hours, saving 80-160 hours per season of volunteer time — the single biggest factor in volunteer burnout and the reason most team managers quit after 1-2 seasons.
