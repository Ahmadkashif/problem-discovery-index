# Travel Tournament Teams

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $5B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 25%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Most travel teams use TeamSnap or SportsEngine for basic communication and scheduling, but tournament logistics, travel coordination, and per-family financial management are handled through group texts, Venmo requests, and spreadsheets.
**Target Buyer:** Club Director / Team Manager
**Automation Potential:** Medium-High — Tournament registration, travel logistics, team financial accounts, and schedule coordination are all rule-based but currently manual.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Travel tournament teams (travel baseball, club soccer, AAU basketball, etc.) represent the premium end of youth sports — families paying $2,000-10,000+ per year for competitive play that includes weekly practices, weekend tournaments (often out of town), private coaching, uniforms, and equipment. The organizational complexity is significant: a single team may attend 15-25 tournaments per season, each requiring registration, hotel blocks, travel coordination, meal planning, and scheduling. The team manager (usually a volunteer parent) handles logistics for 12-20 families simultaneously. Financial management is particularly complex: families owe different amounts based on participation, payment plans, tournament add-ons, and fundraising credits.

## Current Tools & Gaps
TeamSnap and SportsEngine handle basic team communication, scheduling, and roster management. SportsConnect and Blue Star Sports serve league-level administration. But tournament logistics — comparing tournament options, managing registrations across multiple tournament platforms, coordinating hotel blocks, tracking per-family tournament fees, and managing travel logistics — remain entirely manual. The team manager becomes a part-time travel agent and accountant with zero purpose-built tools.
