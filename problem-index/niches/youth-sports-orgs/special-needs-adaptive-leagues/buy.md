# Adaptive Registration with Accommodation Intake

**Niche:** [[niches/youth-sports-orgs/special-needs-adaptive-leagues/profile|Special Needs Adaptive Leagues]]
**Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** LeagueApps and SportsEngine registration forms collect jersey size and emergency contacts, but not mobility aid type, communication method, sensory sensitivities, seizure protocol, or one-on-one support requirements — the information adaptive programs actually need.
**Tags:** #data-integration #compliance #automation #quick-win

## The Problem
When a family registers their child for an adaptive sports program, the standard registration form asks for information that is mostly irrelevant (prior sports experience, skill level) while missing information that is critical (type of wheelchair, communication method, seizure action plan, sensory triggers that cause meltdowns, bathroom assistance needs). Programs supplement with paper intake forms that families fill out separately, creating two disconnected records. The digital registration record goes into SportsEngine; the paper accommodation form goes into a binder. When the program coordinator needs to know if a participant requires a seizure action plan, they search through a physical binder at the field.

## What Already Exists
LeagueApps, SportsEngine, and Blue Star Sports provide online registration with custom form fields. Technically, a league can add custom questions about accommodations. But custom fields are unstructured text boxes — "describe your child's needs" — with no categorization, no searchability, and no way to generate actionable reports (e.g., "which participants need wheelchair-accessible field access?"). School IEP/504 accommodation tracking platforms (SpedTrack, Frontline) understand structured accommodation data but aren't designed for sports programs.

## The Customization Gap
The needed adaptation is an adaptive-sports-specific registration layer: (1) structured accommodation intake fields organized by category (mobility, communication, sensory, behavioral, medical, toileting), (2) dropdown selections for common accommodations rather than free-text (wheelchair type, AAC device type, sensory sensitivity triggers), (3) secure upload for medical action plans (seizure, allergy, behavioral intervention), (4) summary report generation for coaches and buddies showing each participant's essential needs at a glance, (5) accommodation-based grouping tools for coordinators (show all participants needing wheelchair access for field assignment), and (6) parent-accessible portal to update accommodation information throughout the season.

## Target Customer
Adaptive sports program directors who currently collect critical accommodation data through paper forms disconnected from their digital registration system.

## Impact If Solved
Consolidates participant information into a single searchable record, reducing game-day accommodation errors by 50-70% and saving 2-3 hours per event that coordinators currently spend cross-referencing paper binders — while improving safety for participants whose medical and behavioral needs must be known by every adult on the field.
