# Registration & Draft Systems

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Category:** Highly Automatable

## Profile
**Market Size:** $1.8B
**Share of Parent Industry:** 9%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — Online registration is common but the downstream workflows it triggers — waitlist management, age verification, team placement, skill evaluation data collection, and draft preparation — remain manual.
**Target Buyer:** League Registrar / Commissioner
**Automation Potential:** High — Registration validation, age/division placement, waitlist management, registration-fee payment plans, and draft-data preparation are all highly rule-based and currently handled through manual review of each registration.

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Registration is the front door of youth sports — and it creates cascading administrative work that consumes the first 4-6 weeks of every season. A rec league accepting 500 registrations must: validate each player's age for correct division placement, verify residency (many leagues have geographic boundaries), process payments (including financial aid applications), manage waitlists when divisions fill, collect evaluation data for draft preparation, communicate registration status to families, and prepare the final roster data for team formation. Each step is partially automated at best: online forms collect data, but a volunteer registrar manually reviews every registration for exceptions (age waivers, address discrepancies, special requests).

## Current Tools & Gaps
LeagueApps, SportsEngine, and Blue Star Sports handle online registration with payment processing. Some leagues use Google Forms or JotForm for simpler registration. These platforms collect data effectively but don't automate the downstream workflows: age verification requires manual birthdate checking against division cutoff rules (which vary by sport and league), residency verification requires cross-referencing addresses against league boundaries, waitlist management requires manual promotion when spots open, and draft preparation requires exporting data to a separate spreadsheet for the commissioner to build teams.
