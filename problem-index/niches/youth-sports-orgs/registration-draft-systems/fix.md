# Waitlist Promotion and Roster Finalization Delays

**Niche:** [[niches/youth-sports-orgs/registration-draft-systems/profile|Registration & Draft Systems]]
**Industry:** [[industries/youth-sports-orgs|Youth Sports Organizations]]
**Type:** Fix (Pain Point)
**One-liner:** When a registered player withdraws, promoting the next waitlisted player is a manual process — email the family, wait for confirmation, process payment, update the roster — delaying roster finalization by 3-7 days per withdrawal during the critical pre-season window.
**Tags:** #automation #workflow-orchestration #quick-win

## The Problem
Rec leagues fill divisions to capacity and maintain waitlists of 10-50 families per division. When a registered player withdraws (family moves, child changes sport, financial hardship), the registrar must: update the roster, identify the next waitlisted player, email the family, wait for a response (which may take 1-5 days), process their payment, add them to the roster, and notify the coach. If the first waitlisted family doesn't respond or declines, the registrar moves to the next family and repeats. This chain can take 1-2 weeks per withdrawal, and with 5-15 withdrawals per division per season, the roster is never truly final until weeks into the season. Late additions disrupt team formation, confuse coaches, and frustrate families who've been on the waitlist for weeks without updates.

## Why It's Still Broken
Registration platforms maintain waitlists but don't automate the promotion workflow. When a spot opens, the platform notifies the registrar (or doesn't — the registrar discovers it during manual review), and all subsequent steps are manual. The family on the waitlist has no visibility into their position, no estimated timeline, and no way to indicate whether they're still interested. Some families have registered for a different league in the meantime, so the first 2-3 waitlist contacts may decline — but the registrar doesn't know this until they email each one and wait.

## What a Fix Looks Like
An automated waitlist promotion system that: (1) detects when a roster spot opens (withdrawal, non-payment removal), (2) automatically contacts the next waitlisted family via email and text with a time-limited acceptance window (48 hours), (3) if accepted, processes payment automatically and adds the player to the roster, (4) if declined or expired, automatically advances to the next family, (5) provides waitlisted families a real-time position indicator and estimated promotion date, and (6) notifies the division coach of roster changes in real-time.

## Who Feels the Pain
League registrars who spend 10-20 hours per season on manual waitlist promotion and roster adjustments, and waitlisted families who wait weeks with no communication only to discover a spot opened and was filled before they were notified.

## Impact If Fixed
Reduces waitlist promotion time from 3-7 days to 48 hours per withdrawal, saves 10-20 hours of registrar time per season, and fills 95%+ of open spots (vs. 75-80% currently, where waitlisted families who weren't contacted promptly have already made other plans).
