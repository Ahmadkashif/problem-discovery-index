# Specialty Produce Distributors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-distributors|Food Distributors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $40-60B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~15%
**Digital Adoption:** Low-Medium — produce distributors use basic ERP and accounting systems but the buying side is almost entirely phone-based with real-time negotiation; the extreme perishability (3-5 day shelf life on fresh-cut, 7-10 days on whole produce) makes standard inventory tools inadequate
**Target Buyer:** Owner / Head Buyer at a specialty or regional produce distribution company
**Automation Potential:** High — produce quality assessment, demand-supply matching, and dynamic pricing are high-frequency decisions currently made by experienced buyers using tacit market knowledge

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Produce distribution operates on a fundamentally different rhythm than broadline distribution: shelf lives are 3-10 days rather than weeks or months, prices fluctuate daily based on growing region weather and harvest volumes, and quality varies dramatically within a single SKU (a case of strawberries could be premium or barely sellable depending on when it was picked and how it was handled in transit). The produce buyer is the most critical role — they commit to purchasing 50-200 items daily from growers, shippers, and terminal market wholesalers at negotiated spot prices, then sell at marked-up spot prices to restaurant and retail customers. An experienced produce buyer carries a mental model of supply-demand dynamics across 200+ items, 20+ growing regions, and 50+ suppliers — tacit market knowledge that directly determines the company's profitability.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Produce-specific ERP systems (Produce Pro, Edible Software, Produce Business Solutions) handle lot tracking and catch-weight invoicing. USDA Market News provides daily terminal market price reports. But the buyer's decision process — which items to buy, from which supplier, at what price, in what quantity — is entirely manual, based on phone calls and years of market experience. No tool aggregates real-time supply signals (growing region weather, harvest reports, transportation delays) with demand signals (customer orders, seasonal patterns) to support the buyer's daily purchasing decisions.
