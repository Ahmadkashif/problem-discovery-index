# Catch-Weight Inventory ERP with Yield Tracking

**Niche:** [[niches/food-distributors/protein-commodity-distributors/profile|Protein & Commodity Distributors]]
**Industry:** [[industries/food-distributors|Food Distributors]]
**Type:** Buy & Customize (Vertical Adaptation)
**One-liner:** Generic food distribution ERPs handle per-unit inventory, but protein operations need catch-weight inventory (every case has a different weight and price), yield tracking (how many steaks did we get from this loin?), and USDA lot traceability — a combination that generic ERP cannot model.
**Tags:** #data-integration #automation #compliance #revenue-impact

## The Problem
A protein distributor purchasing a case of beef strip loins receives cases ranging from 55 to 75 pounds, each priced per pound. When the cutting room breaks these loins into steaks, portion cuts, trim, and fat, the yield varies by 2-5% depending on the animal and the cutter's skill. The ERP must track: (1) inbound inventory by weight (not units), (2) per-pound cost by lot, (3) cutting yield (how many pounds of saleable product from each pound of raw material), (4) outbound inventory by weight at per-pound pricing, and (5) full lot traceability from inbound case through cutting to outbound delivery for USDA recall compliance. Generic food distribution ERPs handle per-unit inventory and fixed pricing; protein-specific ERPs exist (JAMIS, CSB-System) but are expensive ($50K-$200K implementation) and designed for large processors, not mid-market distributors.

## What Already Exists
JAMIS and CSB-System provide comprehensive protein processing ERP with catch-weight, yield tracking, and USDA compliance. JustFood ERP offers a mid-market option. These systems cost $50K-$200K to implement with $20K-$50K annual maintenance, putting them out of reach for protein distributors under $50M in revenue. SAP Business One and NetSuite can be configured for catch-weight with custom development, but the yield tracking and cutting room management modules do not exist natively.

## The Customization Gap
A mid-market protein ERP needs: (1) catch-weight inventory management native to every transaction (purchasing, receiving, cutting, selling — all per-pound with automatic weight-based pricing), (2) cutting yield tracking by product, lot, and cutter (which cutter achieves the best yield on strip steaks? which supplier's loins yield more steaks per pound?), (3) USDA lot traceability with 24-hour recall capability (trace any finished product back to the source case and forward to every customer who received product from that lot), and (4) implementation cost under $20K for distributors doing $10M-$50M in protein revenue.

## Target Customer
Operations managers at protein distributors doing $10M-$100M in revenue with in-house cutting operations. Approximately 1,500-2,500 US operations at this scale.

## Impact If Solved
Yield tracking visibility enables a 1-2% improvement in cutting yield through cutter training and product allocation, worth $100K-$500K annually on a $50M protein operation. Catch-weight accuracy reduces invoicing disputes by 60-80%, recovering $30K-$100K in underbilled product. USDA recall compliance achieved in hours rather than days, reducing the risk of expanded recalls that can cost $500K-$5M.
