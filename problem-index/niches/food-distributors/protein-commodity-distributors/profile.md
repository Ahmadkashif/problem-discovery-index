# Protein & Commodity Distributors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-distributors|Food Distributors]]
**Category:** Underserved Audience

## Profile
**Market Size:** $50-70B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~18%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — protein distributors use ERP and catch-weight inventory systems, but commodity price hedging and protein market intelligence remain manual, phone-based activities
**Target Buyer:** Buyer / Trader at a protein-focused distributor (beef, pork, poultry, seafood)
**Automation Potential:** High — catch-weight inventory management, commodity price hedging, and protein yield optimization are data-rich problems poorly served by generic distribution tools

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Protein distributors deal with a product category fundamentally different from dry goods or produce: catch-weight pricing (every case weighs differently, so invoicing is per-pound rather than per-unit), commodity price volatility (beef primals can swing 20-30% in 2-3 weeks), yield variation (a beef loin produces different numbers of steaks depending on animal size and fat cover), and strict USDA inspection and traceability requirements. The buyer at a protein distributor is essentially a commodity trader — purchasing boxed beef, pork primals, or frozen seafood containers at prices that change daily, then selling at a markup that must cover handling, cutting, portioning, and delivery. A 1% improvement in buy timing or cut yield on a $100M protein operation is worth $1M.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Protein-specific ERPs (JAMIS, CSB-System, JustFood) handle catch-weight inventory and USDA traceability. Commodity price data comes from USDA Livestock Mandatory Price Reporting and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange. But the buyer's decision process — when to buy, how much to buy, which suppliers offer the best current value, and how to hedge against price drops — is managed with spreadsheets, Bloomberg terminals, and phone calls to packers. No platform integrates commodity market intelligence with the distributor's inventory position and customer demand forecast to support the buyer's trading decisions.
