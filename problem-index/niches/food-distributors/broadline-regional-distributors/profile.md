# Broadline Regional Distributors

**Parent Industry:** [[industries/food-distributors|Food Distributors]]
**Category:** High Market Share

## Profile
**Market Size:** $150-180B
**Share of Parent Industry:** ~50%
**Digital Adoption:** Medium — ERP systems (SAP B1, NetSuite, Produce Pro) and WMS platforms are standard, but demand forecasting and dynamic pricing are spreadsheet-based or absent
**Target Buyer:** VP Operations / General Manager at a regional broadline distributor ($50M-$500M revenue)
**Automation Potential:** High — demand forecasting, pricing optimization, and customer churn detection are data-rich problems solvable with existing ERP data

## What Makes This a Distinct Niche
Broadline regional distributors carry 3,000-10,000 SKUs across all temperature zones (frozen, refrigerated, dry) and serve a diverse customer base: independent restaurants, hospitals, schools, catering companies, and institutional accounts. These mid-market distributors ($50M-$500M) compete against Sysco and US Foods on service quality and relationship depth rather than price. Their operational challenge is complexity management: 5,000+ SKUs with different shelf lives, temperature requirements, and supplier terms, delivered to 500-3,000 customers on tight morning delivery windows. The buyer/merchandiser team makes daily purchase decisions across hundreds of suppliers, the sales team manages customer relationships that determine whether an account stays or defects, and the warehouse runs a multi-temperature picking operation where a single error on a $400 seafood order destroys margin.

## Current Tools & Gaps
Most regional broadline distributors run SAP Business One, NetSuite, or industry-specific ERP (Edible Software, Produce Pro) paired with a WMS (Manhattan Associates, HighJump). Route optimization uses Descartes or Roadnet. CRM adoption is inconsistent — many sales teams still track accounts in personal notebooks. Demand forecasting, where it exists, uses trailing averages inside the ERP with no external signal integration. No platform provides the unified analytics (demand forecasting + pricing + churn detection + route optimization) that national distributors build in-house.
