# Niche Discovery — How to Execute

## Step 1: Map the Revenue Landscape

DO this first for every industry. ASK: "Where does the money concentrate?"

- IDENTIFY the 2-3 segments that account for 60-80% of revenue
- PICK the 2 most distinct as your **High Market Share** (🔵) niches
- IF you can't quantify market share, ASK: "Who are the largest buyers/operators? What service lines dominate?"
- Example: In dental → DSOs with 10+ locations vs. high-revenue specialty practices (ortho, implant)

**IF your niche is vague like "Large Practices" → STOP. Make it specific: "Multi-location DSOs with centralized billing."**

## Step 2: Find the Digitally Neglected

ASK: "Where is technology adoption lowest in this industry?"

- LOOK for geographic isolation (rural operators)
- LOOK for demographic barriers (non-English-speaking operators)
- LOOK for regulatory complexity that discourages tech adoption
- LOOK for sub-segments still running on paper, spreadsheets, or phone calls
- PICK the 2 most distinct as your **Low Digitized** (🟠) niches
- Example: In home health → Medicaid waiver programs are far less digitized than Medicare skilled nursing

**IF you catch yourself naming a segment that's "Low Digitized" just because it's small → STOP. Small ≠ neglected. The segment must genuinely lack appropriate tools.**

## Step 3: Identify Underserved Audiences

ASK: "Who is poorly served by existing solutions in this industry?"

- CHECK for language barriers (Spanish-speaking patients, non-English providers)
- CHECK for age demographics (pediatric vs. geriatric)
- CHECK for economic segments (low-income, uninsured, underbanked)
- CHECK for special populations (veterans, tribal communities, immigrant communities)
- PICK the 2 most distinct as your **Underserved Audience** (🟣) niches
- Example: In behavioral health → autism spectrum services for non-verbal adolescents

**IF the underserved audience is just "everyone who can't afford the premium tool" → STOP. That's a pricing problem, not an underserved niche. Find a population with structurally different needs.**

## Step 4: Find the Automation Goldmines

ASK: "Where are rule-heavy, high-volume workflows begging for automation?"

- LOOK for repetitive data entry or form-filling
- LOOK for rules-based decision trees currently done manually
- LOOK for high-volume document processing
- LOOK for scheduling/routing optimization
- PICK the 2 most distinct as your **Highly Automatable** (⚡) niches
- Example: In medical billing → prior authorization workflows involve 20+ manual steps per request

## Step 5: Name the Niches

- USE descriptive, specific names as the niche ID (slug)
- GOOD: `medicare-skilled-nursing`, `rural-solo-practices`, `spanish-speaking-patient-services`
- BAD: `segment-a`, `type-1`, `other`

## Step 6: Write the Problem Files

For each niche, write 3 problem files:

### Build (Greenfield)
ASK: "What product would I build from scratch if I were starting a company in this niche?"
- The answer MUST be something that genuinely doesn't exist — not a feature request
- IF your "build" is really just "add feature X to existing product Y" → STOP. That's a Buy problem.

### Buy (Vertical Adaptation)
ASK: "What horizontal SaaS tool does this niche already use, and what industry-specific gap makes them curse at it weekly?"
- NAME the actual generic tool
- NAME the specific gap
- IF you can't name both → STOP and research harder

### Fix (Pain Point)
ASK: "What process is so broken that workers have built manual workarounds, and those workarounds themselves create new problems?"
- EXPLAIN the root structural issue, not just the symptom
- IF your fix is "automate this manual thing" without explaining WHY it's still manual → STOP. Explain the structural reason.

## Failure Modes

- **IF you catch yourself writing niches that are too broad** → split by buyer persona, not just size
- **IF two niches overlap** → ask "would these two niches buy the same product?" If yes, merge and find a different segment
- **IF build/buy/fix problems repeat industry-level pains** → zoom in. What's different about THIS niche that creates a UNIQUE problem?
- **IF all 8 niches feel generic** → you haven't understood the industry deeply enough. Re-read the industry profile and problem cards before continuing
